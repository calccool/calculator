Online sliding work schedule calculator: calculation of shifts 2/2, 3/3, 1/3

Planning personal time during shift work is not an easy task. Our sliding graph calculator created for those whose work cycle is not tied to the standard five-day week. Whether you work in security, manufacturing or retail, the tool will help you instantly visualize your schedule for the month ahead or check a specific date (for example, whether your shift falls on a holiday).

The program takes into account the mathematical cyclical nature of your work, allowing you to avoid manual calculation errors in a paper calendar.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on an algorithm for finding the remainder of dividing the difference in dates by the total duration of the cycle.

The following formula is used to determine the status of the day:

$$D_{rem} = (T_{target} — T_{start}) \pmod{C}$$

Where:

  • $T_{target}$ — target date (in days);
  • $T_{start}$ - date of the very first shift;
  • $C$ — total cycle duration (sum of working days and weekends);
  • $D_{rem}$ is the index of the current day in the cycle.

If the resulting $D_{rem}$ value is less than the number of working days in the cycle, the day is considered workers, otherwise - on days off.

How to use the calculator

  1. Set up the loop: Select a ready-made scheme or enter your own values ​​in the “Custom option” mode.
  2. Specify a starting point: Select the date of your very first shift (past or future). This is the “anchor” from which an endless mathematical cycle will begin.
  3. Select period: Select the month you want to analyze.
  4. Activate salary accounting: If necessary, turn on the “Hours and Salary Accounting” switch, enter the rate and duration of the shift.
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will instantly generate a calendar and display summary data.

This tool is indispensable for pre-planning vacations, part-time jobs, and family events. Thanks to the high accuracy of the algorithm, you can look into your schedule years into the future by simply changing the target month.
