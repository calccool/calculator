Insulation calculator: Calculation of the number of packages and volume (m³) for walls, floors and roofs

An error in insulation calculations leads to two problems: either you will not have enough material in the middle of work and will have to pay for re-delivery, or you will be left with extra packaging that is difficult to return to the store. Our calculator solves this problem by calculating the exact amount of material for insulating facades, frame walls, joist floors and pitched roofs.

The algorithm supports the calculation of any sheet and roll materials:

  • Mineral (basalt) wool;
  • Glass wool;
  • Extruded polystyrene foam (XPS, Penoplex);
  • Polystyrene foam (EPS).

How to use the calculator

To get the correct estimate, follow a simple algorithm:

  1. Geometry: Enter the total area of ​​the insulated surface. Don't forget to indicate the area of ​​"deductions" (windows, doors, vents) to avoid paying for air.
  2. Layer thickness: Specify the desired total thickness of insulation. For example, for the walls of a frame house in the middle zone, 150–200 mm is usually required.
  3. Frame accounting: This is a unique feature. If you insulate a wooden frame or floor along joists, the racks take up space. Activate the “Subtract the volume of frame posts” checkbox so that the algorithm automatically reduces the volume of purchased insulation (on average, savings are 5-7%).
  4. Material parameters: Enter the dimensions of one board (length, width, thickness) and the number of sheets in one package. This data is always indicated on the manufacturer's website.

Calculation formulas

We use a transparent mathematical model. You can check the calculations manually using the following formulas.

Calculation of net area

First, the effective insulation area ( $S_{net}$ ) is determined:

$$S_{net} = S_{total} — S_{excl}$$

Where:

  • $S_{total}$ - total area of ​​walls or floors;
  • $S_{excl}$ - area of ​​openings (windows, doors).

Calculation of required volume

The volume of insulation required ( $V_{req}$ ) depends on the number of layers. If the required thickness ( $T_{target}$ ) is greater than the thickness of one sheet ( $T_{mat}$ ), the material is laid in several layers:

$$N_{layers} = \lceil \frac{T_{target}}{T_{mat}} \rceil$$

$$V_{req} = S_{net} \times (N_{layers} \times T_{mat})$$

If the frame accounting option is enabled, the final volume is reduced by the factor $K_{frame}$ (usually 0.93):

$$V_{final} = V_{req} \times K_{frame}$$

Calculation of the number of packages

To find out how many packs to buy, we calculate the volume of one pack ( $V_{pack}$ ) and divide the total required volume by the volume of the pack, rounded up:

$$V_{pack} = L_{mat} \times W_{mat} \times T_{mat} \times Count_{in\_pack}$$

$$Packs = \lceil \frac{V_{final}}{V_{pack}} \rceil$$

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How much insulation should I take?

For simple rectangular walls, there is enough margin, which is obtained by rounding the number of packages to a whole number. For complex roofs with a large number of undercuts (valleys, ridges), it is recommended to manually increase the calculated area by 10-15% by offcuts.

How to convert packages into cubes?

Our calculator does this automatically. In the results you will see the line “Total Volume”. This value is useful for ordering transport, since insulation is a bulky but light load.

Do we need to take into account seams and cold bridges?

When calculating volume - no. Modern insulation materials are installed with overlapping joints (spaced apart) to avoid cold bridges. This does not change the total volume of material, only the laying pattern changes.
