Aquarium soil volume calculator taking into account slope

Correct calculation of the amount of substrate is the foundation of a healthy ecosystem and beautiful design. Aquascaping rarely uses an even layer; Most often, a slope is created from the front glass to the back to visually deepen the perspective and give room to the roots of large plants. Our calculator takes these nuances into account, helping you buy exactly as many bags as you need.

Why is soil slope important?

Creating a “slide” or slope is not only an aesthetic technique. This solution has practical purposes:

  • Visual volume: The slope creates the illusion of infinity, making even a narrow aquarium visually deep.
  • Plant health: Long-stemmed plants in the background have a strong root system and require a layer of 7–10 cm, while ground cover plants in the front need 3 cm.
  • Garbage collection: Thanks to the natural slope, fish waste products gradually accumulate at the front wall, from where they are easier to remove with a siphon.

Formula for calculating soil volume

To calculate the volume of the substrate in the presence of a slope, the formula for the arithmetic average height of the layers is used.

$$V = \frac{L \times W \times \frac{H_f + H_b}{2}}{1000}$$

Where:

  • $V$ - final volume of soil in liters;
  • $L$ - aquarium bottom length (cm);
  • $W$ — width (depth) of the bottom (cm);
  • $H_f$ — layer height at the front glass (cm);
  • $H_b$ - layer height at the rear wall (cm).

To calculate the weight ( $M$ ), the volume result is multiplied by the bulk density of the selected material:

$$M = V \times \rho$$

where $\rho$ is the density coefficient (kg/l).

Soil density reference table

Different types of substrate have different weights for the same volume. This is critical when purchasing, since many manufacturers indicate the weight on the packaging in kilograms, not liters.

Recommendations for choosing layer thickness

If you don’t know what values ​​to enter into the calculator, use the standard “golden” rules of aquarium keeping:

  1. Nano aquariums: Front - 2 cm, back - 5 cm.
  2. Classic herbalists: Front - 3-4 cm, back - 8-10 cm.
  3. Aquariums with large stones (Iwagumi): At the back wall, the layer can reach 15-20 cm to create an expressive relief.

How to use the calculator

To get accurate results, follow three simple steps:

  • Enter the internal dimensions of the bottom of your aquarium (subtract the thickness of the glass from the external dimensions).
  • Specify the desired layer height at the front (at the sight glass) and at the back.
  • Select the type of soil from the list so that the calculator will automatically calculate the weight and required quantity of standard 5-liter or 5-kg packages.
