Calculator Corrected Calcium (by Albumin)

Albumin calcium correction calculator

When interpreting a calcium blood test, it is important to consider the level of albumin, the main plasma protein. The fact is that about 40-45% of calcium in the blood is bound to albumin and is biologically inactive. Only ionized (free) calcium performs physiological functions.

If the patient has a low albumin level ( hypoalbuminemia ), then the level general calcium in the analysis will be falsely low, although the level of active (ionized) calcium may be normal. In such cases, it is necessary to use the correction formula.

Why is correction needed?

Without correction, a false diagnosis can be made hypocalcemia and prescribe unnecessary treatment with calcium supplements, which can lead to dangerous hypercalcemia.

This calculator uses Payne's formula (Payne's formula), which is the gold standard in clinical practice for assessing a patient's true calcium status.

Calculation formula

The following equation is used for calculation (all values ​​are expressed in mg/dL):

$$Ca_{corr} = Ca_{total} + 0.8 \times (4 — Alb)$$

Where:

  • $Ca_{corr}$ —corrected calcium (mg/dL).
  • $Ca_{total}$ —measured total calcium (mg/dL).
  • $Alb$ —measured albumin (g/dL).
  • $4$ —average normal albumin level (g/dL).

Our calculator automatically converts units (mmol/L, g/L), so you don't have to do it manually.

Interpretation of results

Normal values ​​for corrected calcium are usually in the range:

  • 2.20 – 2.65 mmol/l
  • 8.8 – 10.6 mg/dl

If the result is below this range, it may indicate true hypocalcemia. If higher, it indicates hypercalcemia.
