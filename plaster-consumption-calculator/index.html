Flow calculation formula

To understand the algorithm of the calculator, we provide the basic formula used by professional estimators. The final mass of the mixture ( $M_{total}$ ) is calculated as follows:

$$M_{total} = (S_{walls} — S_{openings}) \times T \times C \times K_{loss}$$

Where:

  • $S_{walls}$ - total wall area (Perimeter × Height).
  • $S_{openings}$ - area of ​​windows and doors (subtracted area).
  • $T$ - average layer thickness in mm.
  • $C$ - standard mixture consumption (kg per 1 m² with a layer of 1 mm).
  • $K_{loss}$ - safety factor (usually 1.05–1.10).

How to correctly determine the layer thickness?

This is the most difficult parameter for beginners. If you plaster “beacons”, the thickness of the layer will not be the same everywhere due to the curvature of the walls.

  1. Hang the wall: Use a laser level or plumb line.
  2. Find 3 points: The most protruding point of the wall (minimum layer, usually 6 mm under the beacon) and the deepest depression.
  3. Calculate the average: $$T_{avg} = \frac{T_{min} + T_{max} + T_{mid}}{3}$$ It is this value ( $T_{avg}$ ) that must be entered in the “Average layer thickness” field.

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

  1. Measurements: Measure the perimeter of the room (the sum of the lengths of all the walls) and the height of the ceiling.
  2. Deductions: Calculate the total area of ​​windows and doors. It's not worth paying for air plastering.
  3. Blend selection: Select a specific material type from the list. If you have not decided on the brand, choose “Gypsum Basic” for living rooms or “Cement” for bathrooms.
  4. Stock: We recommend leaving 5% (Minimum) reserve for “shrinkage”, residues in the bucket and hose (for machine application). For beginners it is better to choose 10%.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

  • How long does it take for gypsum plaster to dry? On average 7 days at normal humidity and layer thickness up to 20 mm. Each additional centimeter adds 3-5 days of drying.
  • Can gypsum plaster be used in a bathroom? Not recommended. Gypsum is hygroscopic (absorbs moisture). For bathrooms, it is better to use cement-based mixtures.
  • What is a "bark beetle"? This is decorative plaster with pebbles. Its consumption is fixed and depends on the grain size, and not on the thickness of the application.
