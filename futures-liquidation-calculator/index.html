Futures liquidation price calculator (Margin Call) online

In trading crypto derivatives and futures liquidation price - this is a critical point, upon reaching which your loss on a position becomes equal (or almost equal) to the collateral (margin). To protect the exchange's capital from going negative, the system forcibly closes your transaction. This process is also called Margin Call.

This tool is designed for traders who use Isolated Margin. It allows you not only to find out the exit point, but also to simulate scenarios for “saving” a position by adding margin.

Basic terms and parameters

To make an accurate forecast, it is necessary to understand the key variables used in our algorithm:

Liquidation calculation formulas

Our calculator uses mathematically verified models for two types of transactions. It is based on a balance between the position value, collateral and the required reserve (Maintenance Margin).

For Long positions (Buy)

The liquidation price is calculated using the formula:

$$P_{liq} = \frac{V_{pos} — M_{total} + M_{maint}}{Q}$$

For Short positions (Sell)

For bearish play, the formula is mirrored:

$$P_{liq} = \frac{V_{pos} + M_{total} — M_{maint}}{Q}$$

Where:

  • $V_{pos}$ — Total position cost (Entry price $\times$ Quantity).
  • $M_{total}$ — Total margin (Initial + Added).
  • $M_{maint}$ — Maintenance margin in monetary terms (Position value $\times$ % MMR).
  • $Q$ — Number of asset units (contracts).

How to use the calculator

  1. Select the direction of the transaction: Switch tab to Long (Buy) if you expect growth, or Short (Sale), if you are waiting for a fall.
  2. Enter your login details: Specify the order execution price and the number of assets.
  3. Set the shoulder: Use the quick selection buttons (10x, 20x, 50x, 100x) or enter the value manually.
  4. Analysis of the current situation (Optional): Enter the current market price to have the visual indicator calculate your current PNL (profit/loss) and threat level.
  5. Advanced settings: If you have already “added” margin to a position, activate the settings checkbox and indicate the amount and MMR rate of your exchange.

Why is this important for risk management?

Using a calculator before opening a trade helps you avoid common beginner mistakes:

  • Distance control: You see in advance what percentage of price movement against you is acceptable.
  • Leverage optimization: A visual risk indicator will show you how dangerous it is to use leverage above 20x when volatility is high.
  • ROE and PNL: The calculator instantly calculates return on capital, which allows you to set reasonable profit goals.

Important tip: Always consider trading fees and possible slippage. We recommend leaving a margin of 0.5–1% of the calculated liquidation price to place a protective Stop-Loss order.
