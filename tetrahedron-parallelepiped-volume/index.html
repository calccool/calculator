Calculator for tetrahedron and parallelepiped volume using vertex coordinates

Calculating the geometric characteristics of spatial figures is a classic problem in analytical geometry and 3D modeling. Our online calculator allows you to instantly find tetrahedron volume and built on the same vectors parallelepiped, knowing only the coordinates of four vertices ( $A, B, C, D$ ).

The tool not only gives the final answer, but also calculates intermediate parameters: vector coordinates, base area and height of the pyramid.

Operating principle and formulas

The calculations are based on the vector method. Given the coordinates of four points in space, we can construct three vectors coming from one vertex (for example, from the point $A$ ):

  • $\vec{AB} = \{x_2-x_1; y_2-y_1; z_2-z_1\}$
  • $\vec{AC} = \{x_3-x_1; y_3-y_1; z_3-z_1\}$
  • $\vec{AD} = \{x_4-x_1; y_4-y_1; z_4-z_1\}$

1. Volume of a parallelepiped

The geometric meaning of the mixed product of three vectors is the volume of a parallelepiped built on these vectors. Algebraically, it is calculated as the modulus of the determinant of a third-order matrix composed of the coordinates of these vectors:

$$V_{par} = |\vec{AB} \cdot (\vec{AC} \times \vec{AD})| = \left| \det \begin{pmatrix} x_{AB} & y_{AB} & z_{AB} \\ x_{AC} & y_{AC} & z_{AC} \\ x_{AD} & y_{AD} & z_{AD} \end{pmatrix} \right|$$

2. Volume of a tetrahedron

A tetrahedron (or triangular pyramid) occupies exactly one-sixth of the volume of the corresponding parallelepiped. Therefore the formula looks like this:

$$V_{tetr} = \frac{1}{6} \cdot V_{par}$$

Additional features of the calculator

Unlike simple analogues, this script solves the problem in a comprehensive manner, providing the data necessary for coursework and engineering calculations:

  • Footprint ( $S_{ABC}$ ): Calculated as half the modulus of the cross product of the vectors $\vec{AB}$ and $\vec{AC}$ .
  • Height ( $h$ ): The calculator finds the length of the height dropped from the top of $D$ to the bottom of $ABC$ . This is done through the inverse formula for the volume of the pyramid: $$h = \frac{3 \cdot V_{tetr}}{S_{base}}$$

Instructions for use

  1. Enter the coordinates of the four vertices ( $x, y, z$ ) in the appropriate fields.
  2. The points can be specified in any order, but to correctly determine the height, the system considers the triangle $ABC$ as the base, and the point $D$ as the vertex.
  3. Click the button "Calculate volume".
  4. In the results block you will see the volumes of both figures, the area of ​​the base and the height.
