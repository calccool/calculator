Formulas for calculating cube roots

The cube root of $$( x )$$ is denoted as $$∛x$$ or $$( x^{1/3} ).$$ Basic formula: $$[ y = \sqrt[3]{x} = x^{1/3} ]$$

To check the correctness of calculations: $$x(\sqrt[3]{x})^3$$

Examples:

  • ∛8 = 2
  • ∛27 = 3
  • ∛(-125) = -5

Frequently asked questions

What is a cube root?

This is the number that, when raised to the third power, gives the original value. For example, the cube root of 27 is 3 because 3³ = 27.

Is it possible to take the cube root of a negative number?

Yes, the cube root of negative numbers exists and is also negative: ∛(-8) = -2.

Why is precision adjustment needed?

For scientific and engineering calculations, it is important to control rounding. The calculator allows you to set from 0 to 12 decimal places.
