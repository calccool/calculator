Calculator for aerated concrete blocks and glue: accurate calculation of walls, partitions and cost

Construction of a house made of aerated concrete (gas silicate) requires high precision in procurement planning. An error in calculations can lead to either crew downtime due to the lack of a couple of pallets, or to overpaying for excess material, which then has nowhere to put it. Our online calculator for aerated concrete blocks solves this problem by allowing you to create an accurate estimate for a home box in a minute.

The main advantage of this tool is separate calculation of external load-bearing walls and internal partitions. They are usually built from blocks of different thicknesses (for example, 375 mm for the external contour and 100 mm for the rooms), and they cannot be mixed into one pile.

Calculation algorithm: how it works

The calculator uses geometric formulas to calculate the net volume of masonry, automatically subtracting “voids” (windows, doors, arches) and adding the necessary technological reserve.

Basic formulas used in the calculation:

  1. Net wall area ( $S_{net}$ ): $$S_{net} = (P \times H) — S_{open}$$ where $P$ is the perimeter of the walls, $H$ is the height of the walls, $S_{open}$ is the area of ​​door and window openings.
  2. Masonry volume ( $V$ ): $$V = S_{net} \times T \times K_{waste}$$ where $T$ is the thickness of the block, $K_{waste}$ is the safety factor for trimming (usually 1.05, that is, 5%).
  3. Number of blocks in pieces ( $N$ ): $$N = \frac{V}{L_{block} \times H_{block} \times T_{block}}$$ where $L, H, T$ are the dimensions of one block (standard 600×250×T).

Adhesive consumption for thin joint masonry

Unlike cement mortar, special adhesive for cellular concrete is applied in a layer of only 1–3 mm. This minimizes cold bridges. On average, the standard consumption of dry mixture is 25 kg per 1 m³ masonry (with ideal block geometry). The calculator takes into account the total volume of aerated concrete (external + internal) and converts it into the number of bags.

Useful tips for using the calculator

  • Trimming margin: Do not enter 0%. When transporting, unloading and sawing blocks, some of the material will inevitably end up in scraps or scraps. Recommended value - 5% for simple walls and 7-10% for houses with complex geometry (bay windows, complex corners).
  • Accounting for armored belt: If you plan to pour the armored belt on top of the walls, subtract its height from the total height of the walls ( $H$ ), otherwise you will buy an extra row of blocks.
  • Glue: Please note that glue consumption may increase if the geometry of the blocks is broken or you use it to cover chips.
  • Pallets: The number of pallets is calculated approximately, since the volume of the pallet depends on the manufacturer (most often from 1.44 to 1.8 m³).
