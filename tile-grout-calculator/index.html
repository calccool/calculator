How to calculate how much grout is needed for tiles?

The final stage of laying tiles is jointing (grouting). An error in calculations here is costly: buy little - you will have to look for the same batch number (tone) so that the color does not differ; buy a lot - you will be left with unnecessary bags that have an expiration date.

Our online grout calculator allows you to determine the exact weight of the required material in seconds, taking into account the geometry of your cladding and the density of the specific mixture.

What determines material consumption?

Fugue consumption is not constant (like paint consumption, for example). It directly depends on four parameters:

  1. Tile dimensions (Length × Width × Thickness): The smaller the tile (for example, mosaic), the more joints per 1 m², and the higher the consumption. Conversely, for large-format porcelain tiles (600×600 mm) the consumption will be minimal. The thickness of the tile determines the depth of the joint.
  2. Seam width: A narrow seam (1-1.5 mm) requires several times less material than a designer wide seam (5-10 mm).
  3. Laying area: The total footage of walls or floors.
  4. Mix density: Different types of grout have different specific gravity.

Flow calculation formula

The calculator uses the classic construction formula to calculate the mass of grout ( $M$ ) per 1 square meter:

$$Consumption (kg/m^2) = \frac{L + W}{L \times W} \times H \times S \times D$$

Where:

  • L — Tile length (mm)
  • W — Tile width (mm)
  • H — Tile thickness (mm)
  • S — Seam width (mm)
  • D — Mixture density (kg/dm³)

Grout density: Cement vs Epoxy

One of the unique features of our tool is the material type accounting. Depending on the composition, the density (specific gravity) changes:

  • Cement mixtures (standard): Density approx. 1.8 kg/dm³. The most popular and budget option.
  • Light cement / Epoxy: Density approx. 1.6 kg/dm³. Epoxy grout (“two-component”) is lighter, but durable and resistant to chemicals.
  • Polyurethane / Finished: Density approx. 1.5 – 1.7 kg/dm³.

Why do you need a 10% reserve?

We recommend that you always use the field "Stock" in the calculator. There are several reasons for material loss:

  • The grout remains on the walls of the bucket and on the tool (trowel).
  • When washing (cleaning tiles), some of the material is washed out of the seams.
  • Irregularities in the ends of the tiles or the “walking” thickness of the seam increase the actual volume.
  • Human factor (material fell on the floor).

Enough reserve for large tiles 5-10%, for small mosaics it is better to lay 10-15%.
