ROE Calculator: Return on Equity and DuPont Analysis

Return on Equity (ROE) is a key financial indicator for any investor and business owner. It demonstrates how effectively a company's management uses shareholders' money to generate net profit.

Our online calculator allows you not only to calculate the final percentage, but also to conduct an in-depth analysis of the reasons for the success or failure of the company using the DuPont method.

How to calculate ROE: Two formulas

Depending on the available data and analysis goals, our tool offers two calculation modes.

1. Classic formula

This method is used for quick assessment. It shows the “bottom line” - how many cents of profit each dollar invested by the owners brought.

$$ROE = \left( \frac{\text{Чистая прибыль (Net Income)}}{\text{Собственный капитал (Shareholders’ Equity)}} \right) \times 100\%$$

Advice: To improve accuracy, use the switch “Enter medium?” in the calculator. This will allow you to take into account the change in capital (beginning of the year + end of the year) / 2, which smoothes out the effect of depositing/withdrawing funds during the year.

2. DuPont three-factor model (DuPont Analysis)

If a company's ROE has fallen or increased, it is important to understand Why. The DuPont model breaks down profitability into three drivers:

$$ROE = \text{Маржа} \times \text{Оборачиваемость} \times \text{Рычаг}$$

Where:

  • Net Profit Margin: Shows operational efficiency (cost control and pricing).
  • Asset Turnover: Shows how quickly assets generate revenue.
  • Financial Leverage: Shows the degree of debt of the company.

Why use DuPont analysis?

Let's imagine two companies with the same ROE of 20%. It would seem that they work equally well. But Dupont analysis can show a different picture:

  • Company A: Reached 20% due to high markup (effective brand) and lack of debt. This is a reliable business.
  • Company B: It has a low margin, but reached 20% due to the giant leverage (high leverage). This is a risky business - any increase in rates could bankrupt it.
