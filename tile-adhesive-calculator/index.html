Tile adhesive consumption calculator: Online calculation based on the area and size of the trowel tooth

Accurate calculation of materials is the first step to saving budget when renovating. Our tile adhesive consumption calculator allows you to determine with professional accuracy how many bags of the mixture will be needed for tiling walls or floors, taking into account not only the area, but also the specifics of the tool used and the tile itself.

Calculation formula

Many beginners mistakenly believe that glue consumption is a constant value. It actually depends on three variables. Our algorithm uses the following physical formula:

$$M = S \times H \times \rho \times k$$

Where:

  • $M$ - final mass of glue (kg).
  • $S$ - laying area ( $м^2$ ).
  • $H$ - thickness of the adhesive layer (mm), which depends on the size of the tile and the trowel tooth.
  • $\rho$ - specific consumption of the mixture (kg/m² at a layer of 1 mm). Each manufacturer has its own (usually from 1.1 to 1.6 kg).
  • $k$ - safety factor (usually 1.1, that is, +10% for trimming and unevenness).

How to use the calculator?

  1. Select brand of mixture: If you haven't decided yet, leave it at "Universal (Standard)". If the brand is not in the list, select “Custom consumption” and look at the numbers on the back of the bag (usually the consumption is written per 1 mm of layer).
  2. Specify area: Enter the total square footage of the walls or floor.
  3. Select tile size: This is critical. The system itself will set the recommended layer thickness. For perfectly smooth walls the layer can be thinner, for curved walls it can be thicker.
  4. Consider the stock: We recommend leaving 10% for trimming, rejects and scraps in the bucket.
  5. Price: Enter the price of one bag to get a ready estimate.

Factors that increase consumption

Even the most accurate calculation may diverge from the fact if the following nuances are not taken into account:

  • Uneven base: If you plan to level the walls with glue (which is not recommended, but is practiced), the consumption may double.
  • Spatula angle: The sharper the angle, the lower the height of the ridge and the thinner the layer.
  • Application to tiles: When laying porcelain tiles or working outdoors, glue is often applied both to the base and to the tile itself, which adds ~10-15% to the consumption.
