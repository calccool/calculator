Tank volume calculator: calculate liters and cubes online

Determining the exact capacity of a tank is a critical task in both home and industrial settings. Our tool allows you to instantly calculate how many liters of water, fuel or other liquid will fit in the container, as well as find out the current balance if the tank is only partially filled.

This calculator will be useful in the following situations:

  • For car owners: calculation of the volume of the fuel tank or expansion tank.
  • In construction: determination of the volume of septic tanks, water storage tanks and concrete mixers.
  • In gardening: calculation of watering tanks and barrels of fertilizers.
  • In industry: accounting of fuels and lubricants in horizontal and vertical tanks.

How to use an online calculator?

The interface is divided into three tabs depending on the shape of your container. To get the result, follow three simple steps:

  1. Select tank shape: Rectangular (box), Vertical cylinder or Horizontal cylinder.
  2. Enter dimensions: Enter the length, width, height or diameter in centimeters (cm).
  3. Calculate filling level (optional): Turn on the “Calculate liquid volume” function to find out how many liters are currently poured into the tank.

Supported container types

  • Rectangular tanks: classic tanks, pools, containers.
  • Vertical cylinders: barrels, standing tanks, silos.
  • Horizontal cylinders: recumbent tanks, fuel tanks underground.

Calculation methods and formulas

The calculator's mathematical algorithm is based on classical stereometry formulas. All calculations are made in cubic centimeters ( $см^3$ ), after which they are converted to generally accepted units of measurement.

Rectangular tank

The simplest type of calculation. The volume is found as the product of three sides:

$$V = L \cdot W \cdot H$$

Where:

  • $L$ - length;
  • $W$ - width;
  • $H$ - height.

Vertical cylinder

For cylindrical containers, the key parameter is the radius ( $R$ ), which is equal to half the diameter ( $D/2$ ):

$$V = \pi \cdot R^2 \cdot H$$

Horizontal cylinder (difficult case)

Calculating the partial volume in a lying barrel is the most difficult task, since the cross-sectional area of ​​the liquid changes nonlinearly. We use the circle segment formula:

$$V_{fill} = (R^2 \cdot \arccos(\frac{R-h}{R}) — (R-h) \cdot \sqrt{2Rh — h^2}) \cdot L$$

Where:

  • $h$ - liquid level height;
  • $L$ - cylinder length.

Practical advice on measuring

To make the calculation as accurate as possible, follow these recommendations:

  • Consider the wall thickness: If you are measuring the outside of the tank, subtract the thickness of the material from each measurement (length, width, diameter).
  • Form error: Many plastic tanks have ribs or rounded corners. The calculator calculates the “ideal” geometric figure, so the actual volume can be 2-5% less than the calculated one.
  • Temperature expansion: Remember that the volume of fuel and lubricants (gasoline, diesel) varies depending on the ambient temperature.
