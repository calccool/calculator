Basic formulas and cube parameters

A cube (or regular hexahedron) is a regular polyhedron, each face of which is a square. All edges of a cube are equal, which simplifies the calculation of its characteristics. Below are the key formulas used in our algorithm.

Geometric characteristics

For a cube with edge length $a$ the following relations are valid:

  • Cube volume ( $V$ ): The space occupied by a figure. $$V = a^3$$
  • Total Surface Area ( $S$ ): The sum of the areas of all six faces. $$S = 6 \cdot a^2$$
  • Face diagonal ( $d_{face}$ ): A line segment connecting opposite vertices of one face. $$d_{face} = a \cdot \sqrt{2}$$
  • Spatial diagonal of the cube ( $d$ ): A line segment connecting two opposite vertices of a figure, passing through the center. $$d = a \cdot \sqrt{3}$$

Inscribed and circumscribed spheres

In 3D modeling and design problems, knowledge of the parameters of the spheres associated with the cube is often required:

  1. Inscribed sphere radius ( $r$ ): The sphere touches the centers of all faces of the cube. $$r = \frac{a}{2}$$
  2. Circumscribed sphere radius ( $R$ ): The sphere passes through all the vertices of the cube. $$R = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{a \cdot \sqrt{3}}{2}$$

Calculation of mass and density of materials

Our calculator is equipped with a function for calculating the physical mass of a cube. This is useful when designing foundations, tanks or machine parts. The mass is calculated using the formula:

$$m = V \cdot \rho$$

Where $V$ is the volume (in cubic meters), and $\rho$ is the density of the material (kg/m³). The tool has built-in reference densities for popular materials:

  • Water: 1000 kg/m³
  • Wood (pine): ~600 kg/m³
  • Concrete: 2400 kg/m³
  • Aluminum: 2700 kg/m³
  • Steel: 7850 kg/m³
  • Gold: 19300 kg/m³

Instructions for use

The calculator works in several modes, adapting to your initial data:

  1. Direct calculation: Select “Cube Side” from the drop-down list, enter a value, and the system will calculate all other parameters.
  2. Reverse calculation: If you only know, for example, the volume (liter) of the tank, select “Volume (V)” and enter the value. The calculator will determine how long the walls should be.
  3. Weight rating: Select a material from the list to find out the mass of an object of given dimensions.

Important: When calculating mass, make sure to enter dimensions in meters, or take into account the scale of the result, since the density of materials is indicated in kg/m³.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How to find the side of a cube knowing its volume?

To do this, you need to take the cube root of the volume. Reverse calculation formula: $a = \sqrt[3]{V}$ . Our calculator does this automatically when you select the “Volume” mode.

How are the diagonal of a cube and its edge related?

The diagonal is always larger than the edge by $\sqrt{3}$ times (about 1.73 times). If the side is 10 cm, then the diagonal will be 17.32 cm.

What units of measurement does the calculator work in?

The tool is mathematical and works with any units. If you enter the length in centimeters, the area will be in cm² and the volume will be in cm³. The exception is the calculation of mass, where SI units (meters and kilograms) are used.
