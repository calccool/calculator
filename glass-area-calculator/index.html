Glazing area calculator: calculation of the ratio of windows to floor area according to GOST

Natural lighting directly affects human biorhythms, performance and eye health. When designing a house, it is not enough just to choose beautiful windows - it is necessary to maintain a balance between light transmission and thermal characteristics of the building.

Calculating the glazing area in relation to the floor area allows you to:

  • Comply with the law: Compliance with SNiP 2.08.01-89 and current GOSTs.
  • Optimize energy consumption: Avoid excessive heat loss in winter and overheating (greenhouse effect) in summer.
  • Provide comfort: Find the “golden mean” between cozy twilight and blinding light.

Formula for calculating the ratio of windows to floor

Mathematically, the calculation is the determination of the coefficient $K_{glass}$ , which shows how much of the horizontal surface (floor) is covered by the luminous flux from vertical or inclined openings.

The formula looks like this:

$$K_{glass} = \frac{\sum (W_{width} \cdot W_{height} \cdot N)}{F_{length} \cdot F_{width}} \cdot 100\%$$

Where:

  • $W_{width}$ and $W_{height}$ - width and height of the window opening;
  • $N$ - total number of windows in the room;
  • $F_{length}$ and $F_{width}$ - room dimensions (length and width).

Consequences of violating insolation standards

Deviation from the recommended values ​​in any direction creates technical and operational problems:

  • Lack of glazing (less than 10-12%):
    • Constant need for artificial light.
    • Risk of developing depression and vision impairment.
    • Visual reduction of space.
  • Excess glazing (more than 20-25%):
    • Heat loss: The window is the “weakest” element in the thermal circuit of the house. Even a premium multifunctional double-glazed window loses more heat than an insulated wall.
    • Overheat: In summer, panoramic windows turn the room into a greenhouse, requiring powerful air conditioning systems.
    • Privacy: Windows that are too large require complex shading solutions (raft blinds, blackout curtains).
