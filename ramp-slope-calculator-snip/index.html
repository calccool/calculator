Disabled ramp slope calculator according to SNiP: Online calculation and verification of standards

Designing an accessible environment requires strict adherence to geometric parameters. An error of a few centimeters can make the structure unsuitable for operation or lead to refusal to accept the object by government agencies. This calculator allows you to calculate the slope in three quantities: percentage, degrees and ratio (1:X), as well as determine the minimum permissible march length for a given lifting height.

Basic regulatory requirements (SP 59.13330.2020)

According to the current set of rules “Accessibility of buildings and structures for people with limited mobility,” the maximum permissible slope depends on the lifting height and type of structure.

Formulas for calculating tilt parameters

The calculator is based on three mathematical dependencies. We use trigonometric functions and linear dimensional relationships to ensure accuracy.

1. Calculation of slope as a percentage

The slope ( $S$ ) is defined as the ratio of the rise height ( $H$ ) to the length of the horizontal projection of the flight ( $L$ ):

$$S = \left( \frac{H}{L \cdot 100} \right) \cdot 100\%$$

Note: in the formula, $H$ is taken in centimeters, and $L$ is converted to similar units.

2. Determination of the angle of inclination in degrees

To calculate the angle ( $\alpha$ ) the arctangent function is used:

$$\alpha = \arctan\left( \frac{H}{L \cdot 100} \right) \cdot \frac{180}{\pi}$$

3. Calculation of the minimum length

If you know the required slope (for example, standard 5%), the minimum length is calculated as follows:

$$L_{min} = \frac{H}{S_{target}}$$

Why correct calculation is critical

Exceeding the angle of inclination above 8% makes independent lifting of a wheelchair user almost impossible due to the high risk of tipping back. A slope that is too steep is equally dangerous: the braking systems of wheelchairs may not be able to cope with the load.

Key design rules according to SNiP:

  • Horizontal platforms: Mandatory at the beginning and end of each march. The size of the site must be at least 1.5 × 1.5 meters.
  • Handrails: Must be continuous, double (at a height of 0.7 m and 0.9 m) and extend beyond the inclined part by 30 cm.
  • Sides: Along the edges of the flight, protective edges with a height of at least 5 cm are required to prevent the wheel from slipping.
  • Coating: The surface must be hard, non-slip and contrasting with the surrounding area.

How to use an online calculator

  1. Select mode: “Check parameters” (if you have a ready-made project) or “Calculate length” (if you are just planning construction).
  2. Enter lift height: Indicate the total height of the obstacle (porch, threshold) in centimeters.
  3. Specify length: To check, enter the length of the existing run in meters.
  4. Analyze the visualization: An interactive scale and diagram will instantly show whether your slope falls within the “green zone” of safety.
