Paint thickness decoder: Online decoding of thickness gauge readings in microns

When purchasing a car, checking the body with a thickness gauge is a mandatory step. However, the numbers on the device screen (microns) themselves say little without reference to the car brand and a specific body element. Our online paint thickness decoder allows you to instantly analyze the received data and obtain a technical conclusion about the condition of the coating.

The calculator takes into account that the factory paint thickness is not a static value. It varies depending on the country of manufacture and the class of the vehicle. In addition, internal elements (pillars, thresholds, openings) always have a thinner layer of paint coating than external panels.

How to use the decryption calculator

To get accurate results, follow three simple steps:

  1. Select measurement area: “Exterior Elements” for doors, fenders and hood, or “Openings” for interior body parts.
  2. Specify the car class: Select your region of origin (Asia, Europe or Premium) as color standards vary.
  3. Enter the value in microns: Enter the number that your device showed.

Formula for calculating deviations from the norm

To determine coverage status, the calculator calculates the delta (deviation) between the actual measurement and the factory-set range. Mathematically it looks like this:

$$\Delta T = T_{fact} — T_{norm\_max}$$

Where:

  • $T_{fact}$ - current thickness gauge reading;
  • $T_{norm\_max}$ is the upper limit of the factory standard for the selected class.

If the $\Delta T$ result is positive and exceeds the norm by more than 50–100 µm, this indicates the presence of a secondary color.

What do the decryption results mean?

Our tool doesn’t just produce a number, but visualizes the structure of the layers that may be under the device’s sensor.

Main coverage statuses:

  • Too thin / Polishing: Readings below 70 microns (for external panels). This may indicate excessive abrasive polishing, when the protective layer of varnish is almost removed.
  • Factory color: Ideally within the manufacturer's range. The body is in original paint, no repair work has been carried out.
  • Secondary color (Cosmetics): Readings in the range of 180–300 microns. This is usually the result of removing scratches or chips without using putty.
  • Putty (Repair after an accident): Values ​​from 300 to 1000 µm. An unambiguous sign of leveling the surface with special compounds after damage to the metal.
  • Thick Putty / Welding: Readings over 1000–1500 microns. Critical level, indicating a serious accident or replacement of a body part (“quarter”) with subsequent masking of the seams.

Features of checking internal racks and openings

It is important to remember that the interior parts of the body are painted at the factory with a minimal amount of varnish (or no varnish at all). If you see 110 microns on the outer door, this is the norm, but if you see the same 110 microns on the central post in the lock area, this is a cause for serious concern, since the norm there rarely exceeds 80 microns. Our calculator automatically switches sensitivity thresholds when selecting the “Openings and internal studs” mode.
