Online I-beam (I-beam) weight calculator - GOST 8239 and 26020

An I-beam (I-beam) is the main load-bearing element in the construction of metal structures, having an H-shape in cross-section. When designing, purchasing metal or logistics, it is critical to know the exact weight of rolled products. Our calculator allows you to instantly convert length to weight And mass in length, and also calculate the surface area to be painted.

Supported standards (GOST)

Unlike simple calculators, our tool takes into account the geometric differences between old and new rental standards. The weight of the “20th beam” may vary depending on the type of profile.

1. GOST 8239-89 (With a slope of the internal edges of the shelves)

Classic hot-rolled I-beam. Feature - the inner edges of the shelves have an inclination of 6-12%. This gives the profile additional rigidity, but complicates bolted connections (oblique washers are required).

  • Application: Crane beams, floors of industrial buildings.
  • Marking: Just a number (eg 10, 18, 30) indicating the height in centimeters.

2. GOST 26020-83 (With parallel edges of shelves)

Modern standard. The shelves have a constant thickness, which simplifies installation. Depending on the size ratio, beams are divided into series:

  • B (Normal): Universal beams (eg 20B1). Used in bendable elements.
  • W (Wide flange): The width of the shelf is close to the height of the profile (for example, 25Ш1). They have increased stability and often do not require additional connections.
  • K (Column): The most powerful and heaviest (eg 30K1). The height is equal to the width. Used as vertical supports.

Calculation formulas

The theoretical mass of an I-beam is calculated based on its cross-sectional area and steel density ( $\rho \approx 7850 \text{ кг/м}^3$ ).

Mass formula:

$$M = L \times m_{spec}$$

Length formula:

$$L = \frac{M}{m_{spec}}$$

Where:

  • $M$ - total batch weight (kg);
  • $L$ - total length (m);
  • $m_{spec}$ is the specific weight of one linear meter (kg/m), which is taken from GOST tables.

Calculation of painting area

When drawing up estimates, they often forget to take into account the cost of anti-corrosion treatment. Our calculator automatically calculates the surface area of ​​the beam ( $S_{paint}$ ) that needs to be covered with primer or paint.

$$S_{paint} = P \times L$$

Where $P$ is the section perimeter (m²/m). For example, for a beam 20B1 painting area is approximately 0.63 m² for each linear meter of length.

Instructions for use

  1. Select mode: “Calculate weight” (if you know the length) or “Calculate length” (if you know the weight, for example, when purchasing).
  2. Specify standard: Select GOST 8239 (slope) or GOST 26020 (parallel edges).
  3. Select series (only for GOST 26020): B, Sh or K.
  4. Enter your profile number: Select the size you need from the drop-down list.
  5. Enter value: Length in meters or mass in kilograms.
  6. Get the result: The calculator will show the final weight/length, reference weight per meter, number of meters per ton and area to be painted.
