Ecowool calculator: Accurate calculation of consumption, number of bags and cost of insulation

Ecowool (cellulose insulation) is fundamentally different from slab materials (mineral wool or polystyrene foam). It cannot be calculated simply “by quadrature”. The main nuance is packing density, which varies depending on the angle of inclination of the structure.

If you blow an insufficiently dense layer into the walls, over time the material will shrink and cold bridges will appear. If you overcompact the floor, you will overpay for extra material. Our calculator uses algorithms based on manufacturers' process sheets to eliminate these errors.

How to use the tool

The calculation is carried out in three stages, covering the entire thermal circuit of the building:

  1. Packaging customization: Indicate the weight of one bag (standard is 15 kg, but packages of 12, 20 or 25 kg are also available) and the price if you want to receive a financial estimate.
  2. Entering dimensions: Fill in the fields for area ( $S$ ) and required layer thickness ( $h$ ) for each type of structure:
    • Horizon: Floors, attic floors.
    • Incline: Roof slopes, attics.
    • Vertical: Walls, partitions.
  3. Result: The system automatically summarizes the data and gives the exact number of bags, total tonnage and volume in cubic meters.

Density standards and calculation formulas

The calculator is based on the physical formula for calculating mass through volume and bulk density:

$$M = V \times \rho_{constr}$$

Where:

  • $M$ - final weight of ecowool (kg).
  • $V$ - volume of insulated space ( $V = S \times h$ ), m³.
  • $\rho_{constr}$ - recommended mounting density for a specific design.

Calculation of the number of packages

After calculating the total mass, the calculator determines the required amount of container:

$$N_{bags} = \lceil \frac{M_{total}}{W_{bag}} \rceil$$

Where $W_{bag}$ is the weight of one bag that you specified in the settings. The result is always rounded up to the nearest whole number to ensure that there is enough material.

Installation features that affect consumption

When planning your purchase, consider two factors:

  • Installation method: This calculation is relevant for dry blowing (mechanized or manual with a mixer). With the wet-glue method (spraying on open walls), the consumption may vary slightly due to the presence of a binder component and trimming of excess.
  • "Fuzzing": Ecowool in transport condition is compressed 3-5 times. It must be fluffed before installation. Do not try to fill the material directly from the briquette - this will lead to overconsumption and deterioration of the thermal insulation properties.
