What are your expenses?

The final cost of servicing a broker is the sum of three components:

  • Turnover commission: The percentage charged on each purchase or sale of an asset.
  • Subscription fee: Fixed amount per month (debited only if there are transactions).
  • Exchange fee: The Moscow Exchange commission, which by default is 0,015% from the transaction amount (unless otherwise provided by the tariff).

Mathematical formula for calculation

To understand the logic of the calculator, here is a formula that calculates the total load on the portfolio:

$$S_{total} = (V \cdot \frac{P_{broker}}{100}) + M + (V \cdot \frac{P_{moex}}{100})$$

Where:

  • $S_{total}$ - total expenses for the month;
  • $V$ — total trade turnover (sum of all purchases and sales);
  • $P_{broker}$ - broker's interest rate;
  • $M$ - fixed monthly fee;
  • $P_{moex}$ - Moscow Exchange rate (0.015%).

Which strategy to choose: Investor or Trader?

The choice of tariff directly depends on your monthly turnover.

Tariff "Investor"

Suitable for those who buy assets rarely (for example, once a month when replenishing their account) and hold them for a long time. There is no subscription fee, but the percentage per transaction is higher.

Break even: If your turnover is less than 100,000 - 150,000 rubles per month, this option is usually more profitable.

Tariff "Trader"

Designed for active players. Low commission (0.03% - 0.05%) compensates for the presence of a fixed payment.

Advantage: With multimillion-dollar turnover, savings on commissions can be 10-20 times higher than the cost of the subscription fee itself.
