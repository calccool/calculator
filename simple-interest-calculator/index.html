Simple interest calculator: Calculate income on deposits and loans online

Simple interest is a fundamental mechanism in finance used in calculating the return on short-term bank deposits, bonds (coupon yield), or in estimating overpayments on fixed-rate loans. Unlike compound interest, where profit is “inflated” onto profit, the simple method involves accruing income solely on the original amount (the body of the deposit or debt).

Formula for calculating simple interest

The mathematical model underlying the calculations is known throughout the world as Simple Interest Formula. It is linear and predictable.

The basic equation looks like this:

I = P × R × T

Where variables mean:

  • I (Interest) — the amount of accrued interest (your income or overpayment).
  • P (Principal) — initial amount (deposit or loan body).
  • R (Rate) — annual interest rate (in decimal form, for example, 0.05 for 5%).
  • T (Time) — period of placement of funds in years.

If you want to calculate the total amount (A) that you will receive in your hands along with the return of the deposit, the formula takes the form:

A = P + I or A = P(1 + RT).

Comparison: Simple vs Compound Interest

The main mistake of beginners is to confuse these two concepts. Simple interest is beneficial for short distances or when you withdraw profits monthly. Compound interest (capitalization) works for long-term capital growth.

Usage example
