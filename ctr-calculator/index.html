CTR calculator: Online calculation of click-through rate and advertising effectiveness

CTR (Click-Through Rate) is a key performance indicator in internet marketing, reflecting the “clickability” of an advertisement, banner or snippet in search results. Our tool allows you not only to calculate the classic CTR, but also to predict traffic or evaluate performance using financial metrics (CPM and CPC).

CTR calculation formula

Click-through rate is calculated as the ratio of the number of clicks to the number of impressions, expressed as a percentage.

The mathematical formula looks like this:

$$CTR = \left( \frac{\text{Клики}}{\text{Показы}} \right) \times 100\%$$

Where:

  • Clicks — the number of times users clicked on your ad.
  • Impressions — the number of times the ad was shown to users.

Example:

If your ad was shown 1000 times and 25 people clicked on it, the calculation would be:

$$CTR = \left( \frac{25}{1000} \right) \times 100\% = 2.5\%$$

Why is it important to track CTR?

CTR is more than just statistics. This is a metric that directly affects the cost of your advertising.

  • Impact on cost per click: Advertising systems (Google Ads, Yandex.Direct, Facebook) encourage ads with high CTR, reducing their cost per click (CPC).
  • Quality indicator: A high CTR signals to the algorithms that your ad is relevant and useful to your audience.
  • Creative diagnostics: A low CTR is the first signal that the headline is not catchy or the image is poorly chosen.

5 Ways to Increase CTR

If our calculator shows poor results, try the following optimization methods:

  1. Work with headers: Use numbers, questions, and strong verbs (e.g., “Find out,” “Get,” “Save”).
  2. Add a call to action (CTA): A button or phrase “Click”, “More details”, “Buy” increases click-through rate by 10-20%.
  3. Use ad extensions: In contextual advertising, adding sitelinks, keywords, and a business card makes the ad appear larger.
  4. Segment your audience: Show ads only to those who are truly interested in them. Narrow your targeting.
  5. Test creatives: Always run A/B testing on different images and texts.
