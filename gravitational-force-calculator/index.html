Calculator of gravity and the Law of Universal Gravitation (Online calculation)

Gravity is the fundamental force that keeps planets in orbit, creates tides, and prevents us from flying into outer space. Our online gravity calculator allows you to instantly calculate the force of attraction between any two objects in the Universe, be it atoms or galaxies.

The tool is based on Isaac Newton's classical law, discovered in 1687. It reads:

“All bodies attract each other with a force directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.”

Gravity formula

For manual calculation the following equation is used:

$$F = G \cdot \frac{m_1 \cdot m_2}{r^2}$$

Where:

  • F — The force of gravitational attraction (in Newtons, N).
  • G — Gravitational constant ( $6.67430 \times 10^{-11} \text{ м}^3 \cdot \text{кг}^{-1} \cdot \text{с}^{-2}$ ).
  • m₁ And m₂ — Masses of the first and second objects (in kilograms, kg).
  • r — Distance between the centers of mass of objects (in meters, m).

Calculator features

Unlike simple solutions, our tool works in three modes and solves inverse dynamics problems:

  1. Search for Power (F): Classic problem. Enter masses and distances - find out with what force bodies attract.
  2. Search Mass (m): Knowing the force of gravity and distance, you can calculate the mass of an unknown celestial body.
  3. Search Distance (r): Allows you to find out in what orbit a planet must be in order to experience a certain attraction.

Additional options

In addition to the basic force, the calculator automatically calculates:

  • Gravity acceleration (g): At what speed will the object fall onto the surface of the first body (relevant for calculating gravity on other planets).
  • Orbital speed (v): The first escape velocity required to maintain a stable circular orbit.

Why does weight change on different planets?

Your weight is essentially the gravitational force with which the planet is pulling you towards it. Because the planets have different masses and radii, the strength of gravity (and your weight) will vary.

Using this calculator, you can find out the acceleration due to gravity ( $g$ ) at any height.

  • On the surface of the Earth $g \approx 9.8 \text{ м/с}^2$ .
  • At the altitude of the ISS (400 km), gravity is still strong - about $8.7 \text{ м/с}^2$ , and weightlessness occurs only due to the constant free fall of the station in orbit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the atmosphere affect gravity calculations?

No. The law of universal gravitation depends only on mass and distance. Air resistance is a separate force.

Why is the gravitational constant G used?

This is a proportionality coefficient that “adjusts” the formula to our Universe. Without it, the result would simply be kilograms times meters, not actual force in Newtons. The value of G is extremely small, so gravity is noticeable only for massive objects.

How to calculate the distance between centers?

If you are standing on the surface of a planet, the distance $r$ is the radius of that planet, not zero.
