Online collinearity calculator: Checking whether three points belong to the same line

In analytical geometry, computer graphics and navigation, the problem often arises of determining collinear whether given points. Our tool allows you to instantly check this condition, visualize the location of points on the coordinate plane and obtain related geometric parameters.

What is collinearity?

Three or more points are called collinear (from lat. collineus - “located on the same line”) if they lie on the same straight line. If this condition is not met, the three points form the vertices of a triangle.

Mathematical justification: How do we count?

There are several ways to test the collinearity of the three points $A(x_1, y_1)$ , $B(x_2, y_2)$ and $C(x_3, y_3)$ . Our algorithm uses area (determinant) method, since it is the most reliable and avoids division by zero errors (unlike the slope method).

Collinearity condition:

Points lie on the same straight line if the area of ​​the triangle formed by them is zero. Mathematically, this is expressed through the determinant of the matrix:

$$\det = x_1(y_2 — y_3) + x_2(y_3 — y_1) + x_3(y_1 — y_2)$$

  • If $\det = 0$ , then the points collinear.
  • If $\det \neq 0$ , then the points form a triangle, and the area ( $Area$ ) is calculated as:

$$Area = \frac{1}{2} | x_1(y_2 — y_3) + x_2(y_3 — y_1) + x_3(y_1 — y_2) |$$

Alternative Method: Through Slopes

To understand the essence, the check can be performed through the equality of the angular coefficients (slopes) of the segments. If the slope of the segment $AB$ is equal to the slope of $AC$ , the points lie on the same straight line:

$$\frac{y_2 — y_1}{x_2 — x_1} = \frac{y_3 — y_1}{x_3 — x_1}$$

Note: This method is difficult to calculate if $x_2 = x_1$ (vertical line), so we use the more general area method.
