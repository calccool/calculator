Circle (bar) weight calculator: online calculation of rolled metal weight

A metal circle (rod) is one of the most popular types of long products, which is used in construction, mechanical engineering and turning. Our online calculator allows you to instantly determine the theoretical weight of a steel circle, as well as rolled products from non-ferrous metals, without having to look for values ​​in bulky GOST tables.

How is the weight of a metal rod calculated?

The calculator is based on the classic geometric formula for calculating the mass of a cylinder. To find the weight, you need to calculate the volume of the product and multiply it by the density of the material.

First, the cross-sectional area of ​​the circle ( $S$ ) is determined through its diameter ( $d$ ):

$$S = \frac{\pi \cdot d^2}{4}$$

Then the total theoretical weight ( $M$ ) of rolled metal of a given length ( $L$ ) is calculated taking into account the density of the alloy ( $\rho$ ):

$$M = S \cdot L \cdot \rho$$

The program automatically converts millimeters to meters and produces highly accurate results, eliminating manual calculation errors.

Benefits of using our calculator

The tool is designed keeping in mind the needs of engineers, estimators and logistics specialists.

  • Work with any diameters: Unlike static tables according to GOST 2590-2006, where the pitch is indicated only for standard sizes (for example, 10 mm, 12 mm, 16 mm), the calculator calculates the weight of a rod of absolutely any non-standard or calibrated section (for example, 41.5 mm).
  • Two-way calculation: The tool operates in two modes. You can find out the weight by a known length (to order a truck with the required carrying capacity) or find out the length by weight (to check how many meters there are in a ton of metal that arrived at the warehouse).
  • Logistics assessment: The automatic calculation of “length per ton” is critical for inventory control and bulk purchasing.
  • Multimateriality: In one click you can switch the calculation from standard black steel to copper or lightweight aluminum.

Why do we need a theoretical calculation of mass?

Knowing the exact weight of rolled metal is necessary at all stages of production. Structural engineers need this data to collect loads when designing structures. Suppliers use weight for costing and budgeting, and turners and millers use weight to estimate the weight of workpieces before machining.

It is important to remember that the actual weight of rolled metal may have slight deviations from the calculated theoretical weight. This is due to the errors allowed by GOST during rolling: plus or minus tolerances on diameter, as well as possible ovality of the rod.
