Online calculator for calculating indoor and outdoor illumination: Standards, Lumens and Watts

Proper lighting is not only a matter of comfort, but also eye health, as well as energy efficiency. A lack of light causes fatigue and decreased concentration, while too much light leads to unnecessary electricity costs.

Our universal illumination calculator (Lux Master) allows you to perform engineering calculations both for interiors (apartments, offices) and for open spaces (parking lots, courtyards), taking into account the geometry of the room, the reflection coefficients of the walls and the type of lamps used.

Calculation method: Physics of light

The calculator is based on the luminous flux utilization coefficient method. This is a professional approach that takes into account that some of the light is absorbed by the walls, floor and ceiling, and some is lost due to the geometry of the room.

The basic formula for determining the required total luminous flux ( $F_{total}$ ) is as follows:

$$F_{total} = \frac{E \times S \times k}{\eta}$$

Where:

  • $E$ - required illumination in Lux (lx) according to the standards;
  • $S$ - room area ( $м^2$ );
  • $k$ — safety factor (takes into account lamp aging and dust);
  • $\eta$ (or $U$ ) - luminous flux utilization factor (depends on the room index $K$ ).

Room index and geometry

One of the most important parameters that simple calculators often ignore is the geometry of the room. Narrow rooms with high ceilings “eat up” more light than wide halls. To adjust calculations it is used Room index ( $K$ ):

$$K = \frac{S}{h \times (A + B)}$$

Where $h$ is the estimated height of the suspension (distance from the lamp to the working surface), $A$ and $B$ are the length and width of the room.

Effect of finishing on the number of lamps

Light walls reflect up to 80% of the light that hits them, returning it to the room, while dark wallpaper or brickwork can absorb up to 70-80% of the light flux.

Our calculator applies the following correction factors depending on the selected type of finish:

  • Light colors (White/Beige): High reflectivity. A minimum number of lamps is required.
  • Midtones (Grey/Pastel): Standard calculation.
  • Dark colors (Black/Dark Wood): Low reflection. It will be necessary to increase the lighting power by 30-50%.

Street mode and parking

When calculating street lighting ( Outdoor Mode ) the algorithm is changing. There are no walls or ceilings outside that could reflect light. All light that does not reach the target surface goes into the sky or is scattered.

For street calculations we use:

  1. Reduced utilization factor (UF).
  2. Increased safety factor ( $k=1.5 — 1.7$ ) due to aggressive environment (dust, moisture, contamination of lampshades).

Instructions for use

  1. Mode: Choose where the calculation is made - indoors or outdoors.
  2. Dimensions: Specify the length and width of the zone.
  3. Height: For indoors, indicate the height of the ceiling; for outdoors, indicate the height of the lamppost. Important: the calculation is carried out for the “working plane” (usually 0.8m from the floor for tables).
  4. Walls: Please indicate the predominant trim color. If the room is colorful, choose Mid Tones.
  5. Lamp type: Select the planned type of lamps. This will allow the calculator to convert Lumens into a specific number of light bulbs (pieces).

The result will show not only how many lamps you need to buy, but also the approximate electrical power of the entire system, which will help you choose the right cable cross-section and circuit breaker.
