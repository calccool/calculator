Calculator of the date of entry into legal force of a court decision

Determining the moment when a judicial act becomes binding is a critical task for the lawyer and litigant. It is from this date that the deadline for presenting a writ of execution or filing a cassation appeal begins.

Our calculator automates complex rules for calculating procedural deadlines, taking into account:

  • Type of legal proceedings (civil, arbitration, administrative);
  • Date of production of the reasoned (full) decision;
  • Rules for postponing deadlines if their end falls on a non-working day.

Rules for calculating deadlines by law

According to the legislation of the Russian Federation, a court decision comes into force after the expiration of the period for appeal, if it has not been appealed. This period begins the day after the decision is made in final form.

Date calculation formula

Mathematically, the calculation of the effective date ( $D_{force}$ ) can be represented as follows:

$$D_{force} = (D_{start} + T_{appeal}) + 1 \text{ день}$$

Where:

  • $D_{start}$ - date of adoption of the decision in final form;
  • $T_{appeal}$ - the period for appeal established by law (month or days).
  • If $(D_{start} + T_{appeal})$ falls on a weekend, that day will be moved to the following Monday.

Important nuances when calculating

Date of acceptance in final form

Do not confuse the date of announcement of the operative part (“stand up, the trial is coming...”) and the date of production of the full text of the decision. The appeal period is always counted from the date of production of the reasoned decision. The judge is required to indicate this date in the text of the decision itself.

Last day rule

If the last day of the period falls on a non-working day (Saturday, Sunday or holiday), the end day of the term is considered to be the next working day. Our calculator does this automatically using the functionweekendToggle.

Submission via mail

Remember the 23:59 rule. If you sent a complaint through a Russian Post office before midnight of the last day of the deadline, the deadline is considered met, even if the letter arrives in court a week later. To confirm this fact, be sure to keep an inventory of the investment and a cash receipt with the RPO.
