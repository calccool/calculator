Methods and formulas for calculating shifts

To determine the final indicators, the calculator uses a mathematical algorithm based on cyclic repetitions.

Calculation of time indicators

First, the total duration of the period in days $T_{total}$ and the length of one full cycle $C$ are determined:

$$C = D_{work} + D_{rest}$$

Where:

  • $D_{work}$ - number of days on site;
  • $D_{rest}$ — number of days of inter-shift rest.

Next, the number of complete $N$ cycles and the remaining days that fall at the end of the selected planning horizon are calculated. This allows you to accurately determine the total number of $W_{total}$ work shifts for 6 or 12 months.

Calculation of financial results

If the “Schedule + Income” mode is selected, the system applies the formula:

$$I = W_{total} \times S$$

Where:

  • $I$ - projected income before taxes;
  • $S$ - pay rate for one shift per day.

Peculiarities of accounting between shifts

It is important to understand that, according to the law, the length of service that gives the right to early retirement may include not only days of direct work at the site, but also time spent on the road, as well as periods of rest between shifts.

Our calculator highlights days on site as the main indicator for financial planning, since it is on them that the size of the “net” salary most often depends.
