Types of compounds and formulas

It is important to remember the main rule of electronics: capacitors are counted the opposite way compared to resistors.

1. Parallel connection

This is the easiest and most common way to increase the overall capacity. With this connection, the plates of all capacitors are connected together, which is equivalent to increasing the area of ​​​​the plates of one large capacitor.

Peculiarities:

  • Capacity increases.
  • The voltage on all elements is the same.

Formula:

$$C_{total} = C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + \dots + C_n$$

2. Serial connection

It is used less frequently, usually in cases where it is necessary to increase the operating voltage of the circuit (breakdown voltage). The total capacity will always be less, than the value of the smallest capacitor in the chain.

Peculiarities:

  • Capacity decreases.
  • The charge ( $Q$ ) on all capacitors is the same.
  • The voltage is distributed inversely proportional to the capacitances.

Formula:

$$\frac{1}{C_{total}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3} + \dots + \frac{1}{C_n}$$

Electric Charge and Energy

If you specify the voltage of the power supply ( $V$ ), our calculator will automatically calculate the physical parameters accumulated in the capacitor bank:

  • Electric Charge ( $Q$ ): Measured in Coulombs (C). Shows the amount of electricity accumulated on the plates. $$Q = C \times V$$
  • Field Energy ( $E$ ): Measured in Joules (J). Shows the potential energy stored in the capacitor. $$E = \frac{C \times V^2}{2}$$
