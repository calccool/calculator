VAT calculation formulas

To understand the principles of pricing, it is useful to know the mathematical basis. The following variables are used in the calculations: total amount with tax ( $S_{total}$ ), amount without tax ( $S_{base}$ ), amount of the tax itself ( $V$ ) and percentage rate ( $R$ ).

How to allocate VAT (if the amount already includes tax)

First, the base cost of the product is calculated excluding tax:

$$S_{base} = \frac{S_{total}}{1 + \frac{R}{100}}$$

Then the amount of the tax itself is determined:

$$V = S_{total} — S_{base}$$

How to calculate VAT (if only the net value is known)

The tax amount is calculated by multiplying the base by the interest rate:

$$V = S_{base} \cdot \frac{R}{100}$$

The total cost for the final buyer will be:

$$S_{total} = S_{base} + V$$
