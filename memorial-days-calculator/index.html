The meaning of memorable dates in Orthodoxy

In Orthodox theology, each time period after death has a deep symbolic meaning. The calculation is carried out according to a strict formula, where the day of death is always taken as the first day, regardless of the hour of death.

Third day

It is believed that the soul spends the first two days on earth, visiting places that are memorable to it. On the third day (tertina), the soul ascends for the first time to worship God.

Ninth day

From the 4th to the 9th day, the soul is shown the heavenly abodes. На девятый день (девятины) происходит второе поклонение Творцу. On this day, relatives pray that the deceased will be canonized.

Fortieth day (Fortieth day)

The most important milestone. According to the teaching, until the 40th day the soul goes through “ordeals” and sees hell. On the 40th day, a decision is made about the location of the soul until the Last Judgment. Prayers on this day are considered the most effective.

Anniversary

This is the “birthday” of a person in eternity. The family comes together to reaffirm their love and eternal memory of their loved one.

Differences in Calculations: Orthodoxy vs Catholicism

Despite common roots, methods for calculating funeral dates in different branches of Christianity have their own characteristics.

Mathematical formula for calculation

To understand how the algorithm of our service works, you can use a simple mathematical model. Since the day of death is counted first, the formula for finding the target date is as follows:

$$D_{target} = D_{death} + (n — 1)$$

Where:

  • $D_{target}$ — the desired date of commemoration;
  • $D_{death}$ — calendar date of death;
  • $n$ - serial number of the memorial day (for example, 9 or 40).

If the “After Sunset” option is activated, 1 day is automatically added to the base date of death of $D_{death}$ in accordance with liturgical tradition, where a new day begins with Vespers.

Features of the church calendar

It is important to remember that if the memorial day falls during Lent or Bright Week (the week after Easter), the church charter may postpone public commemorations to the next weekend or Radonitsa.
