Self-leveling floor calculator: Accurate calculation of mixture consumption and cost

Calculation of the amount of self-leveling mixture

Self-leveling flooring (self-leveling) is an ideal solution for finishing leveling the surface before laying laminate, tiles or parquet. However, the main mistake during repairs is incorrect calculation of materials. A shortage of just one bag can stop work and disrupt the filling technology, and excess is wasted money.

Our online self-leveling floor calculator will help you accurately determine the required volume of dry mixture and plan the repair budget.

Flow calculation formula

The principle of operation of the calculator is based on the physical formula for the volume and density of the mixture. The following equation is used to determine the total material weight ( $TotalWeight$ ):

$$TotalWeight = S \times H \times C$$

Where:

  • $S$ — Room area ($m^2$).
  • $H$ — Layer thickness fills ( $mm$ ). This is a critical parameter. If the floor is uneven, it is recommended to take average layer thickness based on laser level measurements.
  • $C$ — Mixture consumption ( $kg/m^2/mm$ ). This is the number of kilograms of dry mixture required to cover 1 square meter with a layer of 1 millimeter.

How to use the calculator?

  1. Select a product. If you have not yet decided on a brand, use the average value (1.5 - 1.6 kg/m²/mm) for a preliminary assessment.
  2. Enter room parameters. Enter the area in square meters.
  3. Specify the layer thickness. It is important to remember that each product has an acceptable thickness range (eg 2 to 100mm). Do not go beyond the manufacturer's recommendations.
  4. Bag weight. Standard packaging is 20 or 25 kg, but bags of 30 kg are also available. Check the packaging.
  5. Stock We highly recommend adding 5-10% stock. Some of the solution may remain on the walls of the bucket, tools, or the actual differences in floor height will be slightly greater than when measured.

Calculation of the number of bags

To find out how many packs to buy at the store, the calculator uses a rounding up formula:

$$Bags = \lceil \frac{TotalWeight}{BagWeight} \rceil$$

This ensures that you definitely have enough material to fill the floor in one go, without the formation of seams or cracks.
