Crushed stone calculator: calculate weight, volume and cost online

An error in the calculations of bulk materials means either an idle crew due to a lack of a couple of cubes, or an overpayment for an extra 20-ton dump truck and its delivery. Crushed stone has specific properties: it shrinks when compacted and has different bulk densities depending on the type of stone and the size of the “grain”.

Our online tool allows you to instantly calculate the parameters for a foundation pad, road or parking lot, taking into account all the physical and mechanical nuances of the material.

Formulas used in the calculator

To obtain the most accurate result, we use a system of two basic equations.

Net volume calculation

The first step is to calculate the geometric volume of space that needs to be filled:

$$V_{base} = L \cdot W \cdot \frac{H}{100}$$

Where:

  • $L$ — platform length (m);
  • $W$ — platform width (m);
  • $H$ - layer thickness (cm).

Calculation of the final mass taking into account compaction

To find out how many tons of material to order, a formula is used that takes into account the compaction coefficient and bulk density:

$$M = V_{base} \cdot K_{comp} \cdot \rho$$

Where:

  • $K_{comp}$ - compaction coefficient (usually from 1.1 to 1.3 );
  • $\rho$ — bulk density of the material ( $т/м^3$ ).

Effect of fraction on weight

The larger the stones, the more voids are formed between them in the car body. Therefore, when calculating mass, we adjust the density depending on the selected grain size:

  • Fine (5-20 mm): Maximum tight laying.
  • Medium (20-40 mm): Standard for most construction work (density reduction by 2% ).
  • Large (40-70 mm): For backfilling and bases (density reduction by 4% ).

Compaction factor (tamping)

When ordering crushed stone, it is important to remember that the volume of material in the back of a dump truck will be greater than the volume of the same crushed stone after a vibrating plate or roller has passed over it.

  1. K=1.10: Used for backfilling garden paths or backfilling without intense mechanical impact.
  2. K=1.15 - 1.25: Standard for parking passenger cars and cushions for the foundations of private houses.
  3. K=1.30: Maximum compaction with heavy rollers during the construction of federal highways and industrial facilities.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

To receive an estimate, follow these steps:

  • Select mode: “Calculation by area” if you know the dimensions of the site, or “Exact volume” if the cubic capacity is already known.
  • Specify parameters: Enter the length, width and desired layer thickness in centimeters.
  • Select material: Specify the type of crushed stone and its fraction - this is critical for determining the weight.
  • Consider tamping: Activate the “Taking into account compaction factor” switch to create the required margin.
  • Add price: If you know the supplier's cost per ton, enter it to calculate your overall budget.

After pressing the button "Calculate" the system will give you the final volume in cubes, weight in tons, and even visualize the number of 20-ton vehicles that will be required for delivery.
