What is the difference between Markup and Margin?

Understanding the difference is critical for financial planning:

  1. Markup is the percentage that you add to the cost of goods to obtain the selling price. It shows how much you have “winded up” from above.
  2. Margin is the share of profit in final price goods. It shows how many cents of profit are contained in each dollar (or ruble) earned.

Important: The margin can never exceed 100% (since the profit cannot be greater than the entire price), while the markup can be 200% or 1000%.

Calculation formulas

For manual calculation, use the following mathematical models, where $P$ is the selling price and $C$ is the cost:

1. Calculation of Markup

The markup shows the ratio of gross profit to cost:

$$\text{Markup (\%)} = \frac{P — C}{C} \times 100$$

To find the selling price ( $P$ ) through the desired markup:

$$P = C \times (1 + \frac{\text{Markup}}{100})$$

2. Calculation of Margin

Margin shows the ratio of gross profit to selling price:

$$\text{Margin (\%)} = \frac{P — C}{P} \times 100$$

To find the selling price ( $P$ ) via the desired margin:

$$P = \frac{C}{1 — (\frac{\text{Margin}}{100})}$$
