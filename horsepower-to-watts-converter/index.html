Horsepower to Watts Converter (hp to W): Online calculator and formulas

Power is one of the key characteristics of any engine or electrical appliance, but confusion often arises due to the use of different measurement standards. In the automotive industry, it is common to talk about “horsepower,” while in electrical engineering and physics the SI standard is watt.

Our online converter allows you to instantly and accurately convert between these units, taking into account the specifics of different types of horsepower.

Why are there several types of horsepower?

The main difficulty in translation is that “horsepower” is not a single standardized quantity throughout the world. Depending on the country and area of ​​application (automobiles, electric motors, boilers), the value is 1 hp. in Watts will be different.

Our calculator supports three main standards:

  1. Mechanical (Imperial) HP [hp(I)]: Standard adopted in the USA and UK. Often used in English-language technical documentation.
  2. Metric HP [hp(M)/PS]: Standard used in Europe, Russia and most CIS countries. It is these “horses” that are indicated on the title of your car.
  3. Electric HP [hp(E)]: A specialized unit used to mark the power of electric motors in the US industry.

Conversion formulas

For accurate manual translation, you need to know the exact coefficient for each type. Below are the formulas that our algorithm uses.

1. Mechanical horsepower

Defined as the power required to lift a 550-pound load to a height of 1 foot in 1 second.

$$1 \text{ hp (I)} = 745.699872 \text{ Вт}$$

Conversion formula:

$$P_{\text{(Вт)}} = P_{\text{(hp)}} \times 745.7$$

2. Metric horsepower (Pferdestärke)

Defined as the power required to uniformly lift a 75 kg load to a height of 1 meter in 1 second.

$$1 \text{ hp (M)} \approx 735.49875 \text{ Вт}$$

Conversion formula:

$$P_{\text{(Вт)}} = P_{\text{(hp)}} \times 735.5$$

3. Electric horsepower

Used to express the mechanical power output of electric motors.

$$1 \text{ hp (E)} \equiv 746 \text{ Вт}$$

How to use the converter

The tool works in double-sided mode. This means you can translate as hp → W, so W → hp.

  1. Enter value: Enter the number in the first field.
  2. Select type: If you are converting the power of a European or Russian car, select "Metric". For American technology - “Mechanical”. For electric motors - “Electric”.
  3. Direction: Press the arrow button (⇄) if you want to change the translation direction (for example, find out how many horsepower is in 1000 Watts).
  4. Result: The calculator will instantly show the value in Watts and duplicate it in Kilowatts (kW) for convenience.

Interesting fact

The term "horsepower" was coined by Scottish engineer James Watt in the late 18th century. He needed to clearly demonstrate the advantage of his steam engines over the human draft power of horses, which were used in the mines to pump water. Since then, the tradition of measuring engine power in “horses” has continued, although Watt (W) is a more accurate and scientific unit of measurement.
