Notary fee calculator for real estate transactions

Registration of rights to real estate through a notary is the highest degree of legal protection of a transaction. However, the issue of cost often causes confusion, since the final receipt consists of two disparate values: the federal tariff (state duty) and the regional component (USTKH).

This calculator allows you to instantly determine the financial burden on the parties to the transaction, based on Fundamentals of Russian legislation on notaries and current rates Tax Code.

How is the total calculated?

The total cost of notary services ( $S_{total}$ ) is calculated using the following formula:

$$S_{total} = T + R$$

Where:

  • $T$ - notary fee (state fee) established at the federal level;
  • $R$ - regional tariff for legal and technical services (LTP).

Types of notarization

Depending on the composition of participants and the property, the law distinguishes two categories of transactions, each of which has its own calculation method.

1. Mandatory ID

Applies to transactions that by law cannot be concluded in simple written form (for example, the sale of shares or real estate owned by minors).

For such cases, the tariff ( $T$ ) is:

$$T = P \times 0.005$$

Provided: $300 \le T \le 20\,000$ rub.

(Where $P$ is the contract amount or cadastral value).

2. Voluntary certificate

Selected by the parties at will to provide additional security. Here the calculation is more complicated and depends on the degree of relationship.

Why the amount may differ in different regions (UPTH)

In addition to the strictly fixed state duty, there is payment for “legal and technical services.” This is the job of a notary:

  • Legal analysis of provided documents;
  • Drawing up a draft agreement;
  • Making copies and scanning;
  • Consulting the parties.

The size of the UPTK is established annually by the regional notary chamber. For example, in Moscow this figure for real estate transactions can be about 8,000 rubles, while in other regions of the Russian Federation it can vary from 5,000 to 12,000 rubles. When is a notary required?

Despite the possibility of registering many transactions through the MFC, the law identifies situations where a trip to a notary is inevitable:

  • Transactions with shares: Sale of a share in the right of common ownership to an outsider.
  • Transactions involving children: Any alienation of property belonging to a minor or person under guardianship.
  • Rent agreements: Lifetime maintenance with dependents.
  • Division of property of spouses: Marriage contracts and agreements on the division of joint property.
