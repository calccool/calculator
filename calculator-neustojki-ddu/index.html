Penalty calculator for DDU: online calculation of arrears under 214-FZ

If the developer violated the deadlines for transferring the property specified in the equity participation agreement (DDU), Law No. 214-FZ gives you the right to demand monetary compensation. Our online calculator allows you to instantly calculate not only the basic amount of the penalty, but also additional payments that the courts award in favor of shareholders.

For correct calculation you will need three basic values:

  • Apartment cost, fixed in the contract.
  • Overdue period (number of days from the date following the key transfer deadline).
  • The current refinancing rate (key rate) of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation.

Formula for calculating penalties according to 214-FZ

The calculation is regulated by Part 2 of Article 6 of Federal Law No. 214-FZ. The amount of the payment directly depends on who the shareholder is: an individual or a legal entity.

For citizens (individuals), the calculation is made according to the formula:

$$P = C \times D \times \frac{R}{100} \times \frac{1}{150}$$

Where:

  • $P$ - total amount of the penalty;
  • $C$ - price of the object under the contract;
  • $D$ - number of days overdue;
  • $R$ is the key rate of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation on the day of fulfillment of the obligation.

For legal entities, the formula remains the same, but the coefficient is $1/300$ instead of $1/150$ , which makes the payout half as much.

What additional charges can be collected from the developer?

The penalty alone is often not enough to cover all of the shareholder's losses. Our calculator supports an advanced calculation mode, including:

  1. Consumer penalty (50%): According to the Consumer Rights Protection Law (CPL), if the developer refuses to pay the penalty in pre-trial order, the court will collect a fine from him in the amount of half of the entire awarded amount.
  2. Moral damage: A fixed amount for the experiences suffered (usually courts award from 5,000 to 30,000 rubles).
  3. Compensation for losses: If you had to rent housing due to delay, you have the right to demand compensation for rental expenses (if you have an agreement and receipts).

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

To get the most accurate result, follow this algorithm:

Determination of the period of delay

The first day of delay is considered to be the day following the date of transfer of the apartment specified in the DDU. For example, if the contract states the date “no later than March 31, 2026,” then the penalty starts counting from April 1, 2026. The end date is the day of signing the transfer and acceptance certificate.

Selecting a key rate

According to established judicial practice, calculations are most often made at the Central Bank rate in effect on the day of actual fulfillment of the obligation (signing of an act or issuance of a court decision). In the calculator you can select the current current rate or enter it manually.

Accounting for moral damages and rent

If you plan to file a lawsuit, be sure to toggle the “Take into account moral damages and damages” switch. This will allow you to formulate the full “claim price”, which you will indicate in the statement of claim.

Important nuances and Article 333 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation

It is worth remembering that developers often petition in court to reduce the amount of the penalty, citing Article 333 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (disproportion between the fine and the consequences of the violation). However, the presence of a competent calculation, supported by graphs and detail (as in our tool), significantly increases the chances of maintaining the maximum payout amount.

Advice: Before going to court, be sure to send the pre-trial claim to the developer in a valuable letter with an inventory of the investment. This is a prerequisite for receiving that same 50% fine.
