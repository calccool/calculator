Ceiling plasterboard calculator: Accurate calculation of profiles, gypsum boards and components

Ceiling repairs require precision. An error in calculations of just 10% can lead to either crew downtime due to a shortage of a couple of profiles, or to overpaying for extra sheets of drywall, which then have nowhere to put. Our ceiling plasterboard calculator developed on the basis of Knauf technological maps for single-level metal frames (P-113). It allows you to instantly receive a ready-made estimate of materials taking into account the geometry of your room.

What does this tool calculate?

Unlike simple area converters, our algorithm details the estimate down to every screw. The calculation is made for three main groups of materials:

  1. Sheathing (gypsum plasterboard): Determination of the number of sheets of standard sizes (2500x1200 or 3000x1200 mm) with the ability to select the number of layers (important for sound insulation).
  2. Metal frame: Calculation of guide (UD-27) and ceiling (CD-60) profiles required to create a rigid sheathing.
  3. Fasteners and components: Automatic counting of “crabs”, straight hangers, dowels and all types of screws (TN, LN).

Calculation method and formulas

For those who are accustomed to double-checking estimates manually, we present the basic formulas included in the algorithm of our calculator.

1. Calculation of plasterboard sheets ( $N_{гкл}$ ):

$$N_{гкл} = \frac{S_{помещения} \times K_{слоев} \times K_{запаса}}{S_{листа}}$$

Where $K_{запаса}$ is the trimming coefficient (usually 1.05 - 1.10).

2. Calculation of the guide profile UD-27 ( $N_{ud}$ ):

The profile is mounted around the perimeter of the room.

$$N_{ud} = \frac{(L + W) \times 2}{3}$$

Where $L$ is the length, $W$ is the width of the room, and 3 is the standard profile length in meters.

3. Calculation of ceiling profile CD-60 ( $N_{cd}$ ):

The average flow coefficient for a frame cell of 400×400 mm or 600×600 mm is used.

$$N_{cd} = \frac{S_{помещения} \times 2.9}{3}$$

5 common mistakes when calculating the ceiling

Using our online calculator will help you avoid typical beginner problems:

  • Ignoring sheet layout. If the room is 2.6m long and the sheet is 2.5m, you will have to buy an additional sheet for a 10 cm strip. Our “Trimming margin” parameter eliminates this risk.
  • Forgotten "Bedbugs". Small self-tapping screws for fastening profiles together are often forgotten to be included in the estimate, and a lot of them are required (for each hanger and crab).
  • Saving on profile. An attempt to increase the profile pitch from 400 mm to 600 mm or 800 mm often leads to the ceiling sagging under its own weight. The calculator calculates according to the reliability standard.
  • Unaccounted for primer and putty. To finish the seams, special mixtures (such as Fugenfüller) and reinforcing tape are required, which are also included in our final report.
