How to make money with bank money: Mathematics of government bonds

The “Credit Carousel” strategy using domestic government loan bonds (OVGZ/OVDP) is one of the most popular methods of interest rate arbitrage in Ukraine. The idea is simple: you use the interest-free (grace) period on a credit card to buy government bonds, receive a coupon income, and then repay the loan before the bank charges interest.

Our calculator helps you instantly determine the profitability of a trade, taking into account hidden commissions and spreads that often kill all profits.

Formula for calculating profitability (Calculation Logic)

To understand whether it is worth opening a trade, we use the following calculation model. Unlike deposits, timing down to the day is critical here.

Basic net profit formula:

$$NetProfit = GrossIncome — TotalCommissions$$

Where:

  • GrossIncome is the accumulated coupon income during the holding period.
  • TotalCommissions — the amount of commissions for entry (purchase) and exit (sale/redemption).

Calculation of Gross Income:

$$GrossIncome = P \times \frac{R}{100} \times \frac{D}{365}$$

  • $P$ (Principal) — Amount of invested credit funds.
  • $R$ (Annual Rate) — Annual interest rate on the bond (YTM).
  • $D$ (Days) — The number of days the bond is held (must be less than the grace period).

Main risks: MCC codes and Quasi-cache

The biggest mistake newbies make is ignoring MCC code (Merchant Category Code) operations. Banks classify the purchase of securities in different ways:

  1. MCC 6211 (Securities): Ideal option. Most banks consider this a trading transaction, maintaining a grace period.
  2. MCC 6051 (Quasi-Cache/Transfers): "Red Zone". The bank can remove the 4% commission immediately and start charging interest on the loan from the first day, canceling the grace period.

Important: Before making a large transaction, always make a test transaction of a minimum amount to check how your bank classifies the transaction.

How to use the calculator

  1. Investment amount: Please indicate the available credit limit that you are willing to invest.
  2. Government bond yield: Enter the current rate of return (YTM). In 2025, current rates vary between 15-18%.
  3. Retention period: Enter the number of days. Recommendation: bet 3-5 days less than your grace period in order to have time to withdraw funds (T+1 settlements) and pay off the debt.
  4. Commissions: If your bank charges a percentage for transferring credit funds, be sure to indicate this in the “Commission (Input)” field.
