Calculator for the ordinal number of the day in the year

The ordinal number of a day in a year is a number from 1 to 366 that indicates the position of a specific date from the beginning of the calendar cycle. This parameter is widely used in programming (ISO 8601 format), logistics, financial planning and statistics. Our tool allows you to instantly perform two types of operations:

  • Convert date to number: By entering a specific date and month, you get its strict index in the year.
  • Search date by number: By specifying a number (for example, 256), you will find out what calendar day it falls on in a particular year.

Mathematical model and calculation formulas

The calculator is based on an algorithm for checking the year for a leap year and summing up the number of days in the months preceding the desired one.

The following logical formula is used to determine the year type:

A year ( $Y$ ) is considered a leap year if the following condition is met:

$$(Y \mod 400 = 0) \lor ((Y \mod 4 = 0) \land (Y \mod 100 \neq 0))$$

The day's serial number ( $N$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$N = \sum_{i=1}^{M-1} D_i + d$$

Where:

  • $M$ — serial number of the current month;
  • $D_i$ — number of days in the $i$ th month;
  • $d$ is the date of the current month.

Table of distribution of days by month

Depending on whether the year is a common year or a leap year, the distribution of ordinal numbers changes after February 28th.

Special meaning: 256th day of the year

One of the most popular queries is to search for the 256th day. This number was not chosen at random: $2^8$ (the number of values ​​that can be expressed using one eight-bit byte).

  • IN normal year this day falls on September 13.
  • IN leap year - on September 12 It is on this day that a professional holiday is officially celebrated - Programmer's Day.

Main functions of our calculator

The tool is designed with advanced user experience (UX) in mind and provides the following data:

  1. Progress indicator: a visual pie chart shows what percentage of the year is already behind you.
  2. Week number: calculation of the ordinal week from the beginning of the year for accurate time management.
  3. Season Definition: automatic identification of the current season (winter, spring, summer, autumn).
  4. Days to go counter: you immediately see how much time is left until the celebration of the next New Year.

How to use the tool

The interface is divided into two modes for your convenience:

  • “Search by date” mode: Select a date on the calendar or click the Today button to get an instant summary. Use the "Start of Year" or "End of Year" quick buttons to analyze period boundaries.
  • “Search by number” mode: Enter any number from 1 to 366. Be sure to specify the year, as for the 60th day onwards the result will depend on whether it is a leap year or not.
