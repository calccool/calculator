Three Point Circle Equation Calculator (Online)

How to find the equation of a circle using coordinates?

There is a rule in geometry: through any three points that do not lie on the same line, one and only one circle can be drawn. This problem is often found in cartography, computer graphics, robotics, and engineering design.

Our online calculator solves this problem instantly. You just need to enter the coordinates of points A, B and C, and the algorithm will perform all the complex algebraic transformations for you.

What does the tool calculate?

  • Center coordinates (h, k): A point equidistant from all three given vertices.
  • Radius (R): Distance from the center to any of the points.
  • Diameter (D): Double radius.
  • Equations: The result is given in two formats (canonical and general).
  • Geometry: Additionally, the circumference and area of ​​the circle are calculated.

Types of equations of a circle

The calculator provides the answer in two of the most popular mathematical formats.

1. Canonical equation

This is the most visual form, where the center and radius are immediately visible.

(x − h)² + (y − k)² = R²

Where (h, k) are the coordinates of the center, and R — radius.

2. General equation

Obtained by opening the parentheses in the canonical equation.

x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0

Calculation algorithm (Mathematical reference)

To find the parameters of a circle manually, you need to solve a system of equations. Our calculator uses the determinant method, which is the most accurate.

Step 1. Calculation of the determinant D

D = 2 · [x1(y2 − y3) + x2(y3 − y1) + x3(y1 − y2)]

Step 2. Finding the center (Ux, Uy)

Ux =
1 D
· [(x1² + y1²)(y2 − y3) + (x2² + y2²)(y3 − y1) + (x3² + y3²)(y1 − y2)]

Uy =
1 D
· [(x1² + y1²)(x3 − x2) + (x2² + y2²)(x1 − x3) + (x3² + y3²)(x2 − x1)]

Step 3. Calculate the radius

Knowing the center (Ux, Uy), the radius is calculated as the distance to any of the starting points (for example, to the first one):

R = √ (x1 − Ux)² + (y1 − Uy)²

Why might a calculator give an error?

There is only one case when it is impossible to construct a circle: if all three points lie on the same line (collinear). In this case, the “triangle” degenerates into a segment, and the radius of such a circle tends to infinity. Our tool automatically checks this condition before calculation.
