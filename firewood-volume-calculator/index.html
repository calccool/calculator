Calculator for converting firewood storage meters into dense cubic meters

Buying firewood is always a matter of trust, since wood is rarely sold in “clean” cubic meters. Most often, suppliers use the concept warehouse meter - the volume of the stacked woodpile, where air inevitably remains between the logs. In order not to overpay for emptiness and to know exactly how much energy you will receive, you need to use reducing conversion factors.

Our calculator performs a mathematically accurate conversion based on density standards and full wood ratios, even taking into account such nuances as humidity and splitting method.

Key terms for correct measurements

To use the tool correctly, it is important to distinguish between three types of volume:

  • Storage meter (cl. m): the volume of firewood tightly packed into a woodpile measuring 1x1x1 meter. Includes both wood and air gaps.
  • Dense (net) cubic meter (m³): volume of wood excluding voids. It is this indicator that is used in the forestry industry to account for commercial timber.
  • Bulk cube: the volume of firewood thrown into the body of the car without stacking (the most inaccurate measurement method).

Formula for calculating the volume of firewood

The calculator is based on the classic translation formula:

$$V_{solid} = V_{stacked} \times K_{fullness}$$

Where:

  • $V_{solid}$ - the required net volume of wood;
  • $V_{stacked}$ - volume in fold meters;
  • $K_{fullness}$ is the full wood coefficient, which depends on the length of the logs and their shape.

Additional features of the calculator

Unlike simple converters, this tool calculates the physical and energy indicators of the batch:

  • Weight calculation: The calculator takes into account the density of different species (oak, birch, pine) and their moisture level. Freshly cut firewood can be almost twice as heavy as dry firewood for the same volume.
  • Calorific value: You will receive data on how many kilowatt-hours (kWh) of energy are contained in the selected volume. This allows you to evaluate the effectiveness of the purchase.
  • Composition visualization: An interactive chart will show you the percentage of "clean wood" and "air" in your woodpile.

Instructions for use

  1. Select the mode: transfer from storage meters to cubes or vice versa.
  2. Enter the volume in the appropriate field.
  3. Specify log length. This is a critical parameter for choosing a coefficient.
  4. Select shape of firewood (split or round timber) and breed.
  5. Specify humidity (dry or freshly cut) to find out the approximate weight.
  6. Click the button "Calculate".
