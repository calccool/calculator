Physical properties of sea water: Online calculation

The density of water is not a constant value. It changes depending on how warm the water is, how many salts are dissolved in it and what pressure it is under. Our salt water density calculator allows you to instantly determine the physical parameters of a liquid, which is critical for oceanography, shipping, industrial desalination and marine aquarium farming.

What does the density of water depend on?

The final value is influenced by three main factors that our algorithm takes into account:

  1. Temperature ( $T$ ): When heated, water expands and its density decreases. The maximum density of fresh water is reached at +4°C, but for salt water the graphs look different.
  2. Salinity ( $S$ ): The more salt dissolved in water, the denser it is. Sea water is heavier than fresh water, which affects the buoyancy of ships and submarines. Salinity is usually measured in PSU (Practical Salinity Unit) or ppt (ppm, parts per thousand).
  3. Pressure (Depth): Water is considered an incompressible fluid only in simple problems. At great depths under enormous pressure, the density of water increases noticeably.

Formulas and equation of state

For accurate calculations it is used Equation of State of Sea Water (EOS-80), approved by UNESCO.

In general, the dependence is expressed by the formula:

$$\rho = \rho(T, S, P)$$

Where:

  • $\rho$ (rho) - density.
  • $T$ - temperature.
  • $S$ - salinity.
  • $P$ - pressure (depth).

Specific gravity (SG) in aquarium keeping

Owners of marine aquariums often use not absolute density (kg/m³), but Specific Gravity. This is the ratio of the density of the liquid being measured to the density of pure water.

Our calculator automatically converts values ​​so you can check your hydrometer or refractometer readings.

Freezing point

An important feature of salt water is its lower freezing point. Ordinary sea water (35 PSU) does not freeze at 0°C, but at approximately -1.9°C. Our tool calculates this parameter individually for the salinity you enter.
