Apartment sales tax calculator: personal income tax calculation online

Selling real estate is generating income, which in most cases is subject to income tax (NDFL). To avoid fines and correctly plan the transaction budget, it is necessary to take into account not only the price in the contract, but also the cadastral value of the property, as well as the tenure period.

Our calculator automatically applies the latest tax legislation of the Russian Federation, allowing you to instantly calculate the amount to be paid or confirm your right to a full tax exemption.

Basic principles of real estate taxation

When calculating tax, the tax inspectorate (FTS) relies on two key values: the sales price under the purchase and sale agreement (SPA) and 70% of the cadastral value of the property as of January 1 of the year of the transaction.

To determine the tax base, select greatest from these quantities. This rule prevents artificially lowering the value of real estate in documents.

Tax rates and statuses

The amount of the payment directly depends on your tax status in the year of the transaction:

  • Tax residents of the Russian Federation (stay in the country more than 183 days a year): rate 13%.
  • Non-residents of the Russian Federation: bid 30% (are not entitled to property deductions).

How to legally reduce your tax amount

The legislation provides two main ways to reduce the tax burden for residents. You can choose only one of them, the most profitable for a particular transaction.

Method 1: Property deduction (RUB 1,000,000)

You can reduce the income from the sale of residential real estate by a fixed amount of 1 million rubles. This method is optimal if the apartment was received as a gift, by inheritance, or its purchase price was small (for example, privatization).

Method 2: “Income minus expenses” scheme

If you still have documents confirming the costs of purchasing this apartment (checks, receipts, bank statements), you can deduct them from the current sales price. Tax will be paid only on actual profits.

Tax calculation formula

The mathematical model included in the calculator is as follows:

$$T = (Max(S, C \times 0.7) — D) \times R$$

Where:

  • $T$ — the amount of tax to be paid;
  • $S$ — sales price under the contract (SPA);
  • $C$ — cadastral value as of January 1;
  • $D$ — selected deduction (1 million or amount of expenses);
  • $R$ — tax rate (0.13 or 0.30).

When you don't need to pay tax

There is the concept of a “minimum holding period”, after which no tax is charged, regardless of the transaction amount.

The 3 year period is valid if:

  • The apartment was received as an inheritance or under a gift agreement from a close relative.
  • Ownership rights were obtained as a result of privatization.
  • The apartment was purchased under a lifelong maintenance agreement with dependents (rent).
  • The housing for sale is the only one (at the time of the transaction the seller does not own any other residential premises).

In all other cases, the standard holding period for a tax-free sale is 5 years.

Procedure for filing a 3-NDFL declaration

If, according to the calculation results, the tax amounted to more than 0 rubles, you are obliged to:

  1. Submit a declaration in form 3-NDFL by April 30 of the next year.
  2. Pay the calculated tax by July 15 of the same year.

If the income from the sale of real estate is completely covered by a property deduction of 1 million rubles and the total amount of the transaction does not exceed this limit, there is no need to file a declaration.
