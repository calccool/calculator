Ohm's Law: Basics and Formulas

Basic formulas for a DC circuit (Direct Current):

  • To find Voltage: U = I × R
  • To find the Current: I = U / R
  • To find Resistance: R = U / I

Calculation of electrical power (P)

Power is the rate at which electrical energy is transmitted or converted. It is in Watts that the consumption of household appliances (iron, kettle, lamp) is indicated.

Power formulas:

  1. Through current and voltage: P = U × I
  2. Through current and resistance: P = I² × R
  3. Through voltage and resistance: P = U² / R

Important: Our calculator automatically detects what data you have entered and selects the correct formula to calculate the missing values.

How to choose the wire cross-section?

One of the unique features of our tool is cable selection recommendation. This is critical for safety. If you pass a large current through a thin wire, it will begin to heat up, melt the insulation and can lead to a short circuit.

We use standard recommendations for copper conductors in household wiring:

  • For lighting (current up to 10A) it is usually sufficient 1.5 mm².
  • For socket groups (current up to 16A) used 2.5 mm².
  • For powerful consumers (stoves, instantaneous heaters) it is required 4.0 mm² and higher.
