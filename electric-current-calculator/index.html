Current calculator for power and voltage: Calculation of load, cable and machine

Calculating current strength is a fundamental stage in the design of any electrical network, from home wiring to an industrial workshop. Our calculator allows you to instantly convert the power consumption of equipment into Amperes, taking into account the type of network (single-phase or three-phase) and the nature of the load.

Unlike the simplest formulas, this tool takes into account power factor ( $\cos \phi$ ) and helps you immediately select the necessary components: circuit breaker and copper cable cross-section.

Basic calculation formulas (Calculation Formulas)

Depending on the type of current and the number of phases, the physics of the process changes. The calculator uses the following algorithms:

1. Direct current (DC) circuit

Used for low-voltage systems (LED strips, automotive electrics, batteries). There is no reactive component here.

$$I = \frac{P}{U}$$

Where:

  • $I$ - Current (Amps);
  • $P$ - Power (Watts);
  • $U$ - Voltage (Volts).

2. Single-phase alternating current network (AC, 1 phase)

Standard household network 220-230V. It is important to consider the power factor here if the load is not purely resistive (like a heater).

$$I = \frac{P}{U \times \cos \phi}$$

3. Three-phase alternating current network (AC, 3 phase)

Industrial networks 380-400V. The formula takes into account the phase shift (root of 3).

$$I = \frac{P}{\sqrt{3} \times U \times \cos \phi}$$

What is Cos φ (Power Factor)?

This is a parameter showing which part of the consumed energy does useful work, and which is spent on creating electromagnetic fields. Ignoring $\cos \phi$ when sizing motors or transformers may result in overheating of the wiring.

How to choose a machine and cable?

Having received the current value, you need to select protection and conductor. Our calculator does this automatically, relying on the rules of the PUE (Electrical Installation Rules) with a margin of safety.

  1. Circuit Breaker: The closest rating from the standard range is selected (6A, 10A, 16A, etc.), which exceeds the calculated operating current by approximately 15-20%. This prevents false triggering due to inrush currents.
  2. Cable cross-section: It is selected based on the permissible long-term current load for copper conductors.
