Why is it important to comply with factory settings?

Car manufacturers calculate the length of the brushes to maximize the viewing area without allowing the arms (drivers) to collide with each other.

The main risks of making the wrong choice:

  • Uncleaned areas: Brushes that are too short leave dirt in critical vision areas.
  • Hitting the racks: Excessive length leads to the rubber extending beyond the glass, which accelerates wear of the mechanism.
  • Brush conflict: If the total length of the driver's and passenger's sides exceeds the permissible length, the wipers may catch on each other during operation.

Mathematical accuracy: unit conversion and cleaning area

For international markets, sizes are often given in inches. Our algorithm uses a standard conversion formula:

$$L_{inch} = \frac{L_{mm}}{25.4}$$

Where $L_{mm}$ is the length in millimeters, and $L_{inch}$ is the result in inches (rounded to the nearest whole number for ease of selection in the store).

Also, the efficiency of windshield wipers can be assessed through the area of ​​the cleaning sector $S$ , which is calculated by the formula:

$$S = \frac{\pi \cdot R^2 \cdot \alpha}{360}$$

Here $R$ is the length of the brush (radius), and $\alpha$ is the swing angle of the leash (usually from 90° to 110° ). Our visualizer clearly demonstrates this relationship for both sides.

Understanding the types of fastenings

It is not enough to know the length - you need the brush to “sit” on the leash. Our calculator base takes into account all modern standards:

  1. Hook The oldest and most reliable standard. Found on most Asian and budget European cars. Denoted as 9x3 or 9x4.
  2. Push Button Popular mount in the VAG group (VW, Skoda, Audi) and BMW. There are two types: 16 mm (Narrow) and 19 mm (Standard). Be careful, they are not interchangeable!
  3. Pinch Tab Common on Mercedes-Benz, BMW and some Volvo models. Fixation occurs due to compression of the side clamps.
