Mathematics and formulas for diluting vinegar

The operation of the instrument is based on the classical formula for maintaining the mass of a substance in solution. To figure out how much water to add to the essence, the following equation is used:

$$V_{эссенции} = \frac{C_{жел} \cdot V_{общ}}{C_{исх}}$$

Where:

  • $V_{эссенции}$ - required volume of initial essence (ml);
  • $C_{жел}$ - desired concentration (for example, 9%);
  • $V_{общ}$ - the final volume of finished vinegar that you want to receive;
  • $C_{исх}$ - initial concentration of essence (usually 70%).

Water volume ( $V_{воды}$ ) is calculated as the difference between the total volume and the essence volume:

$$V_{воды} = V_{общ} — V_{эссенции}$$

Safety instructions and expert advice

Working with concentrated acetic acid requires caution. Essence 70% is an aggressive substance that can cause chemical burns.

Rules for preparing the solution

  1. The golden rule of chemistry: Always pour acid into water, not the other way around. This prevents sudden heat generation and accidental splashes of concentrate.
  2. Use cold water: For food purposes, it is better to take filtered or pre-boiled and cooled water.
  3. Storage: Pour the prepared solution into a glass container. Be sure to sign the new concentration on the label so as not to confuse it with pure water or essence.
  4. First aid: If the essence gets on your skin or eyes, immediately rinse the affected area with plenty of running water and consult a doctor.

Why is precision important for conservation?

When preparing for the winter, vinegar acts as a key preservative. If the concentration is below 9% (which is what the recipe calls for), bacteria may survive, causing food spoilage and jars to “explode.” Our calculator ensures that you get exactly the acidic environment needed for long-term storage.
