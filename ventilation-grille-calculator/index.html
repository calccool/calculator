Ventilation Grille Calculator: Calculation of Quantity and Open Section

Online selection of air distributors

The correct choice of ventilation grilles and diffusers is a balance between aesthetics, aerodynamics and acoustic comfort. If you choose a grille that is too small, the system will make noise and whistle. If it is too large, this means unjustified costs and design difficulties.

Our calculator helps engineers and installers instantly determine how many grilles of a given size need to be installed to pass a given volume of air without exceeding the permissible air flow rate.

How does the calculation work? (Physics of the process)

The calculations are based on the flow continuity equation. To determine the required flow area ( $F_{req}$ ), it is necessary to divide the volumetric air flow by the permissible speed.

Calculation formula:

$$F_{req} = \frac{Q}{3600 \times V}$$

Where:

  • Q — Air consumption ( $m^3/h$ ).
  • V — Design air speed in the live section ( $m/s$ ).
  • 3600 — Factor for converting hours into seconds.

What is “Living Section”?

This is one of the most important parameters that is often forgotten. The overall size of the grille (for example, $300 \times 100$ mm) is not equal to the air passage area. The slats, frame and mesh cover part of the gap.

To take this factor into account, it is used Live section coefficient (K.L.S.) or $K$ .

  • K = 0.7 — 0.8: Standard single row grilles.
  • K = 0.5 — 0.6: Decorative grilles or models with a fine mesh/filter.

The calculator calculates the actual throughput of one grid using the formula:

$$F_{one} = (W \times H) \times K$$

Calculation example

Let's say we need to submit 500 m³/h air into the office space. We chose standard gratings $300 \times 100$ mm (K.l.s = 0.7). We want the speed to not exceed 2.5 m/s (for silence).

  1. The calculator will determine the required “living” area: $0.055$ $m^2$ .
  2. Will calculate the live area of ​​one grid: $0.021$ $m^2$ .
  3. Divides the total area by the area of ​​one piece: $0.055 / 0.021 = 2.6$ .
  4. Result: We will need 3 grates.

In this case, the actual speed will decrease and be about 2.2 m/s, which is even better for acoustic comfort.
