Monolithic slab calculator: online foundation calculation

Monolithic foundation slab is a solid reinforced concrete foundation that distributes the load evenly and works confidently on difficult/weak soils, at high groundwater levels and for projects with a basement or plinth. To avoid recalculations “by eye” and overexpenditure of materials, use an online calculator: it will help you understand the volumes, budget and technological parameters in advance.

What does the calculator calculate?

  • Volume of concrete for the slab according to the entered dimensions.
  • Armature: length, approximate weight, mesh pitch and diameter (for one- or two-layer reinforcement).
  • Formwork: lateral surface area and approximate number of boards according to specified sizes.
  • (Optional) Brand of concrete - for orientation on strength in the project.

Recommendation: pawn add. margin 5–7% for concreting and ~10 % on reinforcement (overlaps, cuts, waste).

How does this work

  1. Enter width B, length A And thickness C (in mm) - the calculator will instantly give volume of concrete:
    Объём = A × B × (C/1000).
  2. For fittings, indicate cell pitch (usually 150–200 mm), diameter (often Ø12–Ø14 for residential buildings) and number of grids (usually 2 - top/bottom).
    The tool will calculate the total rod length And approximate weight (taking into account standard steel density).
  3. For formwork, set the dimensions of the boards - you will get area and assessment quantities.

Example

House 10×10 m, slab 300 mm:
10 × 10 × 0,3 = 30 м³concrete. With a margin of 5–7% - 31.5–32.1 m³.
Two-layer mesh Ø12, pitch 200×200 mm - the calculator will tell you the length and weight of the steel.

When is a stove particularly appropriate?

  • Heaving, weak, heterogeneous soils;
  • High groundwater, risk of precipitation changes;
  • Required basement/basement/heavy box;
  • Need simple geometry and high crack resistance of the base.

USHP (insulated Swedish plate)

Layers are important for USP: insulation under the slab, heated floor contours, waterproofing And drainage. Enter the geometry of the slab into the calculator, and include the thickness of thermal and waterproofing in the project (calculation of bearing capacity, distribution of reinforcement and cross-section - according to the project).

Tips:
• Insulation under the slab is rigid XPS;
• Mandatory foundation preparation (geotextiles, sand, crushed stone, tamping), slope/drainage, waterproofing joints.

Good slab design practice

  • Base: geotextile → sand cushion → crushed stone preparation → compaction in layers.
  • Protective layer of concrete to fittings: usually at least 30–40 mm (specify by project).
  • Application clamps/frogs for precise grid position.
  • Concrete - according to design stamp And mobility; ensure continuity of laying and vibration compaction.
  • Curing (humidity conditions, protection from sun/wind/frost) for the first 7 days.
  • Pawn temperature-shrinkable seams for blind areas and adjacent elements.

What the calculator does not answer (realized limitations)

  • Doesn't count bearing capacity soil/base, moment and deflection slabs are a zone constructive calculation.
  • Doesn't take into account stiffening ribs, crossbars, complex shapes and cuts, point loads (columns, stoves, heavy partitions).
  • Does not replace project documentation And geology plot.

How to use the results

  • Get volume of concrete, weight/meter of reinforcement And formwork - this is the basis for estimates and procurement.
  • Check with the project: concrete grade/class, actual thickness, reinforcement scheme, protective layers.
  • Add logistics (mixer/pump), seams, additions, fasteners, consumables and stocks.

The online monolithic slab calculator helps you quickly and transparently estimate key volumes without “by eye.” This saves budget and time on construction, and most importantly, reduces the risk of errors at the start. Use the calculated figures together with the project and the engineer’s recommendations - this way you will lay reliable foundation under the house.
