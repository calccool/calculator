Video file size calculator: accurate calculation by bitrate and duration

When working with video production - whether exporting to Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or preparing a video for uploading to YouTube - it is critical to know the final file size in advance. Our calculator, built on the basis of Gold Standard algorithms, allows you to instantly calculate the amount of data, taking into account both the video stream and the audio track.

How does the video size calculator work?

The tool supports two key operating modes that cover 100% of the tasks of a video editor or content maker:

  1. Find out the file size: you enter the bitrate (quality) and duration - the calculator displays the weight in MB and GB.
  2. Find out the bitrate (by size): you indicate how much space you have (for example, you need to “shrink” the movie to 700 MB), and the service will tell you what bitrate to set when rendering.

Mathematical calculation model

To obtain the most accurate result, we use a formula that takes into account the total data flow ( $Total Bitrate$ ).

The file size ( $S$ ) in megabytes is calculated using the following formula:

$$S = \frac{(B_v + B_a) \times T}{8 \times 1024}$$

Where:

  • $B_v$ — video stream bitrate (in kbps);
  • $B_a$ — audio bitrate (in kbps);
  • $T$ — total video duration in seconds;
  • $8 \times 1024$ (8192) - conversion factor from kilobits to megabytes.

Main factors influencing the final size

In addition to bitrate and duration, the “weight” of the video is indirectly affected by:

  • Codec (H.264, H.265/HEVC, AV1): More modern codecs allow you to maintain the same quality at a lower bitrate.
  • Compression type (VBR vs CBR): Variable bit rate (VBR) can make the file slightly smaller or larger than expected depending on the dynamics of the scenes.
  • Number of audio tracks: If the video has several language versions, add up the bitrate of each of them in the “Audio bitrate” field.
