Flight delay compensation calculator: calculation of payments according to Russian and EU rules

A flight delay is not only a spoiled mood, but also a legal basis for receiving a monetary payment. The amount of compensation directly depends on the jurisdiction in which the flight was carried out: within the Russian Federation or within the territory of the European Union.

This tool allows you to instantly calculate the amount you are entitled to, based on current legislation: Regulation (EC) No. 261/2004 And Air Code of the Russian Federation together with Consumer Rights Protection Law (CPLA).

Compensation rules in the European Union (EC 261/2004)

If your flight originated from an EU airport or was operated by a European airline, you are entitled to a flat payment in euros. The main criterion here is the flight range and the time of delay to the destination (not the departure time, but the time the plane doors open after landing).

Basic conditions for payments in the EU:

  • Arrival delay should be more than 3 hours.
  • The reason for the delay must not be due to “extraordinary circumstances” (weather, air traffic controller strikes).
  • The payment amount is fixed and does not depend on the cost of the ticket.

Compensation for flights within the Russian Federation

In Russia, the calculation system is more complex, since it combines the norms of the Air Code and civil legislation. A passenger can claim two parts of the payment at once:

  1. Penalty under the Air Code: It is a fixed 100 rubles for each hour of delay.
  2. Penalty under the PPA: It is 3% of the ticket price for each hour of waiting.

Formulas for calculating payments

To understand how the final amount is formed in Russian law, the following mathematical models are used:

Penalty formula according to the RF CC:

$$S_{vkrf} = \min(T_{delay} \times 100, P_{ticket} \times 0.5)$$

Where $T_{delay}$ is the full hours of delay, $P_{ticket}$ is the cost of the ticket.

Penalty formula under the PPA:

$$S_{zzpp} = \min(T_{delay} \times (P_{ticket} \times 0.03), P_{ticket})$$

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate calculation, follow a few simple steps:

  • Select mode: Switch to "EU Flights" or "RF Flights" at the top of the interface.
  • Specify distance (for EU): Select the distance of your route (up to 1500 km, from 1500 to 3500 km or more than 3500 km).
  • Enter the ticket price (for the Russian Federation): Enter the ticket price in rubles to calculate the penalty.
  • Specify delay time: Enter the number of full hours by which the plane was late.
  • Click "Calculate": The system will provide details for each article of the law and the total amount.

The tool also visualizes “Compensation Analysis” with a graphical indicator that shows how much your payout is relative to the maximum possible limits.

When can an airline refuse payment?

It is important to remember that compensation is paid only if the carrier is at fault (technical malfunction, lack of crew, late arrival of the plane from a previous flight). If your flight is delayed due to poor visibility, a hurricane, or at the request of security authorities, you most likely will not be able to receive monetary compensation, but you still have the right to food, water and a hotel in any case.
