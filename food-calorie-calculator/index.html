How to calculate the calorie content of a complex dish?

The biggest problem for those losing weight is counting the calories in soups, stews or salads. You cannot simply add up the calorie content of the ingredients, since the final weight of the dish changes, and the proportions are important.

Our tool works according to an algorithm weighted average, which allows you to get accurate numbers per 100 grams of the finished product.

Calculation formula

The calculator uses the following mathematical model to determine the energy value ( $E_{total}$ ) per 100g:

$$E_{100} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (M_i \times K_i)}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} M_i} \times 100$$

Where:

  • $M_i$ — mass of $i$ -th ingredient in grams.
  • $K_i$ - calorie content of $i$ -th ingredient per 1 g.
  • $n$ - number of ingredients.

Tips for use

  • Weigh dry/wet: When cooked, cereals absorb water and increase in weight by 2-3 times, but their calorie content does not change. 100 g of dry buckwheat (313 kcal) will turn into 300 g of porridge, but the total calorie content of the porridge will remain 313 kcal. Enter your weight into the calculator dry cereals
  • Consider the oil: Even one tablespoon of olive oil adds about 130 kcal to a dish. Don't forget to include frying oil in the ingredients list.
  • Watch your protein: To maintain muscle, it is recommended to consume 1.2 to 1.6 g of protein per kg of body weight. Keep an eye on the green sector on our calculator's chart.
