Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium Calculator: Calculation of Frequencies and Chi-Square

Population genetics analysis online

The Hardy-Weinberg law is the foundation of population genetics. He states that in the absence of disturbances, allele and genotype frequencies in a population remain constant from generation to generation.

Our calculator is a professional tool that allows you not only to find allele frequencies, but also to statistically check whether the population under study is in a state of genetic equilibrium.

Basic formulas (The Equations)

For a locus with two alleles (usually referred to as $A$ and $a$ ), two basic equations are used.

  1. Allele frequency: $$p + q = 1$$
    • $p$ — frequency of the dominant allele ( $A$ ).
    • $q$ — frequency of the recessive allele ( $a$ ).
  2. Genotype frequency: $$p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1$$
    • $p^2$ — frequency of homozygous dominant individuals ( $AA$ ).
    • $2pq$ — frequency of heterozygotes ( $Aa$ ).
    • $q^2$ — frequency of homozygous recessive individuals ( $aa$ ).

Calculator features

Unlike simple school calculators, our tool works in two modes and performs statistical analysis:

1. “Number of individuals” mode

Ideal for processing experimental data. You enter the observed number of individuals of each genotype ( $AA, Aa, aa$ ), and the program:

  • Calculates the frequencies $p$ and $q$ .
  • Calculates expected number of individuals (if the population were in perfect equilibrium).
  • Conducts a test Pearson chi-square ( $\chi^2$ ) to check the statistical significance of deviations.

2. “Allele frequency” mode

Useful for theoretical modeling. You specify a known frequency (for example, $p = 0.6$ ), and the calculator instantly builds the distribution of genotypes in the population.

Interpretation of the Chi-square test ( $\chi^2$ )

This is the most important part of analyzing real populations. The calculator compares Observed with Expected.

Conditions for maintaining equilibrium

If the calculator shows that the population not located in equilibrium (high Chi-square), this means that one of the 5 key conditions of the Hardy-Weinberg law is violated:

  1. No mutations: No new alleles arise.
  2. No migration (gene flow): Individuals do not leave the population and do not enter it from the outside.
  3. Random crossing (panmixia): Individuals choose partners without genotypic preferences.
  4. No natural selection: All genotypes have an equal chance of survival and reproduction.
  5. Infinitely large population: To exclude the influence of genetic drift.
