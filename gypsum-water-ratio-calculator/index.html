Plaster and water calculator: accurate calculation of proportions for casting into molds

Plaster casting is chemistry and mathematics. One of the main problems of masters is “underfilling” or, conversely, excess mixture that has to be thrown away. Our calculator solves this problem using the physical constants of materials.

The main difficulty is that the volume of the finished mixture is not equal to the volume of water. Gypsum powder has its own volume, which must be taken into account when filling the silicone mold.

Table of popular proportions (water-gypsum ratio)

Different brands of gypsum and alabaster require different volumes of liquid. The higher the grade of gypsum, the less water it usually requires to achieve high strength.

Formulas used in calculations

The calculator works on the basis of physical volume replacement. We use the average specific gravity of dry gypsum, which is approximately $2.6$ $г/см^3$ .

To calculate the required mass of powder ( $M_g$ ) for a known mold volume ( $V$ ), the formula is used:

$$M_g = \frac{V}{\frac{1}{2.6} + R}$$

Where:

  • $V$ —form volume in ml (or $см^3$ );
  • $R$ - water-gypsum ratio (for example, $0.4$ for $40\%$ );
  • $2.6$ - gypsum density.

After finding the mass of gypsum, the mass of water ( $M_w$ ) is simply calculated:

$$M_w = M_g \times R$$

How to properly prepare the mixture: step-by-step instructions

To make the product durable and free of air bubbles, follow the proven algorithm:

  1. Determine the volume of the form. The easiest way is to pour water into an empty silicone mold, and then measure its volume in a measuring cup.
  2. First water, then plaster. This is the "golden rule". Never pour water into the powder, otherwise lumps will form.
  3. Temperature regime. Use clean water at room temperature ( $20-22°C$ ). Water that is too hot will instantly speed up the setting.
  4. Introduction of additives. If you are using citric acid to retard setting, dissolve it in water to adding gypsum.
  5. Saturation. Pour the plaster in a thin stream or through a sieve, distributing it evenly over the surface of the water. Let the powder “drink” for 30-60 seconds.
  6. Degassing. Mix the mixture thoroughly (best with a vibrating motion or with a mixer on low speed) to expel excess air.

Fine tuning: additions and losses

Professionals know that part of the mixture always remains on the walls of the container. The calculator allows you to add "Provision for losses" (5-10%) so that the main composition is guaranteed to be enough to fill all the small details of the form.

To work with complex shapes, we recommend using retarders. Regular citric acid in a concentration of $0.2\%$ based on the mass of gypsum, it increases the “lifetime” of the solution from 5-7 to 15-20 minutes, which is critical for the release of air bubbles.
