APK to ZIP Converter and Online Android App Analyzer

The APK (Android Package) format is an executable file for the Android operating system, which is technically a specialized ZIP archive. However, developers, reverse engineers and IT enthusiasts often need to not only change the file extension, but also competently analyze its internal contents, estimate the size of resources and extract data in a convenient format. Our online tool performs in-depth analysis and conversion right in your browser.

Benefits of our client analyzer

  • Absolute security and privacy: Your APK file is not uploaded anywhere or transferred over the network. Decompression, bytecode reading and analysis take place in a strictly isolated environment of your browser (Sandbox).

Mathematical model of local conversion

Thanks to direct use of the JSZip library and asynchronous streamsArrayBuffer, the conversion and re-packing process is optimized to a fraction of a second. The efficiency of our engine can be described by the formula for total processing time:

$$T_{process} = \frac{S_{apk}}{R_{dec}} + \sum_{i=1}^{N} t_{parse}(file_i) + \frac{S_{raw}}{R_{enc}}$$

Where:

  • $S_{apk}$ - The initial size of the Android package download in bytes.
  • $R_{dec}$ and $R_{enc}$ - the throughput of the Deflate algorithm when decompressing and decompressing at the hardware level of your PC.
  • $N$ - the total number of internal files in the archive.
  • $t_{parse}(file_i)$ - microtiming of indexing a single file to build a visual DOM tree.

Since network ping and Upload/Download time are completely removed from the equation, the tool is able to almost instantly convert gigabyte-sized applications into convenient ZIP archives, saving you time and bandwidth.
