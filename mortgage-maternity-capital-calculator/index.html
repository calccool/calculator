Why is it important to consider a mortgage with capital capital correctly?

Maternity capital is “smart money”. Depending on how you use them (for the down payment or for early repayment), the final savings on interest may differ significantly. Our algorithm takes into account the current indexation and allows you to visualize the structure of your debt.

Basic strategies for using a certificate

When using maternity capital in a mortgage, the borrower has three options, each of which has its own financial nuances:

  1. Down payment: Allows you to enter into a mortgage with minimal personal savings. This reduces the total loan amount ( $S$ ) from day one.
  2. Reduced monthly payment: Matkapital pays off part of the principal debt, the bank recalculates the schedule, and your monthly financial burden drops. Ideal for families who value stability “here and now.”
  3. Reducing the loan term: The most profitable option from a mathematical point of view. You continue to pay the usual amount, but the mortgage is closed several years faster, cutting off the most expensive interest at the end of the term.

Mathematical calculation model

Our calculator uses the classic annuity payment formula to determine the monthly premium:

$$A = S \times \frac{r \times (1 + r)^n}{(1 + r)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $A$ - monthly annuity payment;
  • $S$ - loan amount (cost of housing minus down payment and capital);
  • $r$ - monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100);
  • $n$ — loan term in months.

When choosing a term reduction strategy, the calculator finds a new value for $n$ by solving the equation for the period at which the debt balance is reset to zero while maintaining the current payment.

Important nuances of 2026

Do not forget that when using maternity capital, you are obliged to allocate shares to children in the purchased housing. This is a legal aspect that does not affect the calculations of the calculator, but is critically important in the subsequent sale of the apartment. Also remember that the certificate is indexed annually on February 1 - if you have not yet disposed of the funds, their balance in the SFR account automatically increases.
