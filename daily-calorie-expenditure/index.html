Calorie Calculator: Calculate Daily Energy Value (TDEE)

Many people mistakenly believe that calorie burning occurs only in the gym. In fact, our body is a 24-hour “energy station”. Total daily energy expenditure ( TDEE - Total Daily Energy Expenditure) consists of four key components. Our calculator takes them all into account:

  1. BMR (Basic Metabolic Rate) - 60-70% of costs. This is the energy necessary to maintain life in a state of complete rest: breathing, blood circulation, brain function, cell growth. Even if you lie in bed all day, you will burn this amount of calories.
  2. NEAT (Household Activity) - 15-20% of costs. Energy spent on spontaneous movements: walking to the car, cleaning, gesturing, typing on the keyboard. For active people, this figure may be higher than the cost of sports.
  3. TEF (Thermal Effect of Food) - about 10% of costs. Calories that the body spends on chewing, digesting and assimilating food. Protein foods have the highest thermic effect.
  4. EAT (Training activity) - 5-10% of costs. Direct sports: running, strength training, cardio.

Calculation formulas: Mathematics of your body

To ensure maximum accuracy, our tool uses two gold standards in nutrition science.

Mifflin-St. Geor Formula

It is considered the most accurate for modern man. The American Dietetic Association has recognized it as a standard.

$$BMR_{муж} = (10 \times вес) + (6.25 \times рост) — (5 \times возраст) + 5$$

$$BMR_{жен} = (10 \times вес) + (6.25 \times рост) — (5 \times возраст) — 161$$

Harris-Benedict Formula (Revised)

The classic formula, developed back in 1919 and refined in 1984. It may be more beneficial for people with very developed muscle mass.

$$BMR_{муж} = 88.362 + (13.397 \times вес) + (4.799 \times рост) — (5.677 \times возраст)$$

$$BMR_{жен} = 447.593 + (9.247 \times вес) + (3.098 \times рост) — (4.330 \times возраст)$$

Where weight is indicated in kg, height in cm, age in years.

How to use the results to change your weight?

Having received the value TDEE (your balance point), you can adjust your nutrition depending on your goals:

  • Safe weight loss: It is recommended to create a shortage in 15-20% from TDEE. This will allow you to lose fat while maintaining muscle mass and without slowing down your metabolism.
    • Example: If TDEE = 2000 kcal, your norm is: $2000 — 20\% = 1600$ kcal.
  • Gaining muscle mass: A caloric surplus (surplus) of 10-15%.
    • Example: If TDEE = 2500 kcal, your goal is: $2500 + 10\% = 2750$ kcal.
  • Recomposition: If you're new to the sport, you can eat at the TDEE level while burning fat and building muscle through strength training and high protein intake.

Important: Never drop your calorie intake below your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) without medical supervision. This can lead to hormonal imbalances and the “yo-yo” effect (rapid weight gain).
