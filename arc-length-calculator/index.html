Online arc length calculator

The geometry of a circle is not only theoretical problems from a textbook, but also the basis of engineering calculations, architectural design and design. Our tool allows you to calculate the key parameters of a circle segment in one click, using both degree and radian measures of angles.

Unlike simple counters, this calculator implements the principle Progressive Disclosure: it doesn’t just give a number, but visualizes the segment, calculates the accompanying data (chord and area) and allows you to carry out reverse calculations - for example, finding the radius through the length of the arc.

Formulas and mathematical base calculation

To ensure maximum accuracy (up to the 4th decimal place), the calculator uses fundamental constants and trigonometric functions. The basic calculation principle depends on the central angle units $\theta$ or $\alpha$ .

Basic formula for arc length

If the angle is specified in radians ( $\theta$ ), the calculation is extremely simple:

$$L = R \times \theta$$

If you use the usual degrees ( $\alpha$ ), the formula adapts to a full revolution of the circle:

$$L = \frac{\pi \times R \times \alpha}{180}$$

Additional segment parameters

In addition to the curve length, our tool calculates the “hard” parameters of the geometry:

  • Chord length ( $c$ ): A straight line connecting the ends of an arc. $$c = 2 \times R \times \sin\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)$$
  • Sector area ( $S$ ): The space inside a circle bounded by an arc and two radii. $$S = \frac{1}{2} \times R^2 \times \theta$$
