Lens Angle of View Calculator: Crop Factor and Equivalent Focal Length

Choosing a lens isn't just a matter of focal length, but also understanding how that distance relates to the size of your camera's sensor. The same 50 mm lens will give a completely different picture on a full frame camera and on a camera with a cropped matrix (for example, APS-C).

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate three key parameters:

  • Equivalent focal length (EFL): what the focal length would look like on 35mm film.
  • Geometric viewing angle: the exact sector of space in degrees that the sensor “sees”.
  • Visual comparison: interactive simulation of cropping a frame relative to the full frame.

Why calculate viewing angle

  1. Shooting planning: You'll know for sure whether a building will fit in the frame when using a 24mm wide-angle lens.
  2. Choice of optics: Understanding whether your “fifty dollar” will become a 75 mm or 80 mm portrait lens on crop.
  3. Video surveillance and architecture: Accurate calculation of coverage areas for security systems or interior photography.

Formulas for calculating viewing angle

Mathematically, the viewing angle depends on the focal length of the lens and the physical size of the sensor. The calculation is made using the inverse trigonometric function formula:

$$\alpha = 2 \cdot \arctan \left( \frac{d}{2 \cdot f} \right)$$

Where:

  • $\alpha$ — desired viewing angle in degrees;
  • $d$ — physical size of the matrix in millimeters (width, height or diagonal);
  • $f$ is the actual focal length of the lens.

It is important to remember that the crop factor ( $K$ ) is a coefficient showing how many times the sensor diagonal is smaller than the diagonal of a standard 36x24 mm frame. The equivalent focal length is calculated as:

$$f_{equiv} = f \cdot K$$

How to use the calculator

Follow three simple steps to get accurate results:

  • Enter focal length: Enter the value written on your lens barrel (the actual focal length is always indicated, regardless of the camera type).
  • Select sensor size: Select your camera type from the list (for example, APS-C Sony) or enter a custom crop factor if your model is not in the list.
  • Explore the visualization: The calculator will plot the angle of view from above and show the cropping frame in the viewfinder simulator.

Basic terms

  • Wide Angle Lens: It has a small focus (up to 35 mm) and a huge viewing angle, which is ideal for landscapes.
  • Telephoto: It has a long focal length (from 85 mm) and a narrow viewing angle, which allows you to bring distant objects very close.
  • Vignetting: A darkening effect in the corners that can occur when using crop lenses on full-frame cameras.
