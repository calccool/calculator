Square Pyramid Numbers Calculator (Sum of Squares)

A square pyramid number is a type of figurative number that represents the number of stacked spheres in a pyramid with a square base. In simple terms, this is the sum of the squares of the first $n$ natural numbers.

If you imagine stacking oranges or cannonballs in a pyramid where the top layer consists of 1 item ( $1^2$ ), the second layer contains 4 items ( $2^2$ ), the third layer contains 9 items ( $3^2$ ), and so on, then the total number of items in such a pyramid of height $n$ will be the square pyramid number.

This calculator allows you to instantly calculate the value for any order of $n$ , showing not only the final result, but also the expansion of the series.

Calculation formula

To find the $n$ -th square pyramid number ( $P_n$ ), the following mathematical formula is used, proven by mathematical induction:

$$P_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6}$$

Where:

  • n — order of number (number of layers in the pyramid);
  • Pₙ — the final square pyramidal number.

Example calculation

Let's consider the calculation for a pyramid with a height of 5 layers ( $n = 5$ ).

Let's substitute the value into the formula:

$$P_5 = \frac{5(5 + 1)(2 \cdot 5 + 1)}{6} = \frac{5 \cdot 6 \cdot 11}{6} = 55$$

Checking by adding squares:

$$1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 = 1 + 4 + 9 + 16 + 25 = 55$$

Properties and application

Square pyramid numbers play an important role not only in number theory, but also in real-life problems:

  1. Cannonball Problem: Historically, this problem arose from the question: how many cores can be stacked into a stable pyramid on the deck of a ship? Our calculator gives an accurate answer to this question.
  2. Geometry: The number of points in a three-dimensional pyramid-shaped lattice.
  3. Combinatorics: The sum of squares is often used in calculating variance in statistics and algorithm analysis.

Relationship with tetrahedral numbers

Interesting fact: any square pyramidal number can be represented as the sum of two consecutive tetrahedral numbers.

$$P_n = Te_n + Te_{n-1}$$
