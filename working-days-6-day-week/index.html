Working days calculator for a 6-day week: online calculation using the Russian calendar

Calculation of working hours for six day work week has its own nuances that are often not taken into account in standard production calendars. Our tool is designed specifically to accurately calculate the number of working days, weekends and holidays in any given period, based on the current legislation of the Russian Federation.

This calculator is useful for:

  • Accountants: when calculating wages, vacation pay or sick leave;
  • HR employees: for drawing up vacation schedules and accounting for labor costs;
  • Workers: for planning personal time and checking the correctness of accruals.

How to use the calculator

To get instant results you need to perform just a few steps:

  1. Specify period: select the start and end date of the calculation. You can also use the quick selection buttons for the current month, next month, or until the end of the current year.
  2. Select hour rate: The default rate is 40 hours per week, but you can change it to 36 or 24 hours in accordance with your employment contract.
  3. Consider holidays: activate the switch “Take into account state. holidays of the Russian Federation" so that the system automatically excludes official non-working days from the total number of work shifts.
  4. Click "Calculate": the system will provide a detailed summary and visualize the data in the form of a chart.

Calculation method and formula

The calculator algorithm works according to a strict mathematical principle, eliminating manual calculation errors. The total number of working hours ( $H_{total}$ ) is calculated using the following formula:

$$H_{total} = D_{work} \times \left( \frac{H_{week}}{6} \right)$$

Where:

  • $D_{work}$ - number of days actually worked in the period (Monday–Saturday, excluding holidays);
  • $H_{week}$ - established weekly working hours (for example, 40 hours);
  • $6$ - number of working days per week.

Important Note: The tool takes into account all official holidays of the Russian Federation, such as New Year holidays, February 23, March 8, May holidays and others, equating them to weekends.
