Body Fat Calculator: US Navy Accurate Calculator

The number on a bathroom scale is a misleading indicator. It doesn't tell you how much muscle, water, bone tissue, and most importantly, body fat you have in your body. Two people with the same weight and height can look completely different: one will be athletic, and the second will have a flabby physique (Skinny Fat effect).

Our body composition calculator uses the US Navy Method, an internationally recognized standard for anthropometric measurements that correlates in accuracy with expensive hydrostatic weighing.

Why is body mass index (BMI) often wrong?

Classic BMI (BMI) takes into account only height and weight. This works for the average person, but fails critically for athletes.

  • Example: A bodybuilder who weighs 100 kg and is 180 cm tall would be classified by his BMI as “Obese” even though he only has 8% body fat.
  • Solution: Our tool analyzes body volumes, which allows us to separate useful (dry) mass from fat tissue.

How to take measurements correctly

The accuracy of the result depends 90% on how correctly you use the measuring tape.

  1. Height: Measured without shoes, standing with your back to the wall.
  2. Neck circumference: The tape should pass just below the Adam's apple (Adam's apple), without squeezing the skin. Shoulders should be relaxed.
  3. Waist:
    • For men: Measured horizontally at the level of the navel.
    • For women: Measured at the narrowest point of the waist (usually just above the navel).
  4. Hip circumference (women only): Measured at the widest part of the buttocks with legs together.

Mathematical calculation model

We use the current Hodgdon-Beckett formula adopted by the US Navy. It is based on the logarithmic relationship between the volume of a body and its density.

Formula for men

$$BF_{\%} = 495 / (1.0324 — 0.19077 \times \log_{10}(Waist — Neck) + 0.15456 \times \log_{10}(Height)) — 450$$

Formula for women

$$BF_{\%} = 495 / (1.29579 — 0.35004 \times \log_{10}(Waist + Hip — Neck) + 0.22100 \times \log_{10}(Height)) — 450$$

Where: Waist - waist, Neck - neck, Hip - hips, Height - height.

What is Lean Body Mass (LBM)?

Our calculator also calculates LBM (Lean Body Mass). This is your body weight minus all your fat. This indicator includes muscles, bones, internal organs, skin and water.

Knowing the LBM is critical to diet planning: protein intake is calculated per kilogram of dry mass, not total weight, to avoid overloading the kidneys. Use this metric to track your progress: if your weight drops but your LBM stays the same, you're losing weight the right way (burning fat, not muscle).
