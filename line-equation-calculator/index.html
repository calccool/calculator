Straight Line Calculator: Two Point Equation, Length and Angle

In analytical geometry, a straight line is one of the fundamental objects. Most often it is specified by two points on the plane. Our straight line calculator allows you to instantly obtain a complete mathematical description of a segment or an infinite straight line passing through points $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$ .

The tool not only displays the equation of a line in different formats, but also calculates geometric properties: length, slope and center coordinates.

How to find the equation of a line from two points?

To create an equation, you need to follow several steps. The algorithm depends on what type of equation you need: with a slope or a general one.

1. Angular coefficient (Slope)

First, the slope of the line is calculated, which is denoted by the letter $m$ (or $k$ in the Russian tradition). It shows how steeply the line rises or falls.

$$m = \frac{y_2 — y_1}{x_2 — x_1}$$

2. Equation with slope

The most popular form of notation, convenient for plotting function graphs:

$$y = mx + b$$

Where $m$ is the slope and $b$ is the Y-intercept (offset). To find $b$ , we substitute the coordinates of the first point and the found slope into the formula: $b = y_1 — m \cdot x_1$ .

3. General equation of a line

In geometric problems it is often necessary to reduce the answer to the form:

$$Ax + By + C = 0$$

Our calculator automatically performs the conversion, eliminating fractions so that the $A$ , $B$ and $C$ coefficients are integers (if possible) and easy to write.

Additional calculations

In addition to equations, the tool calculates important metric characteristics of the segment between given points.

Line Length (Distance)

Calculated using the Pythagorean theorem. This is the hypotenuse of a right triangle, the legs of which are the differences in coordinates:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 — x_1)^2 + (y_2 — y_1)^2}$$

Midpoint

The point that divides the segment $AB$ exactly in half. Its coordinates are the arithmetic mean of the coordinates of the ends:

$$x_m = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \quad y_m = \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}$$
