Half-life calculator: online calculation of radioactive decay

Radioactive decay is a process with frightening mathematical precision. The half-life ( $T_{1/2}$ ) determines the time during which the number of nuclei of an unstable isotope decreases exactly by half. This does not mean that after two periods the substance will disappear completely - it will decrease exponentially, dividing in half again and again.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate any of the parameters of this process: from the residual mass in a medical preparation to the age of an archaeological find.

Basic formula for radioactive decay

All calculations are based on the law of radioactive decay, which is mathematically described by the following formula:

$$N(t) = N_0 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$

Where:

  • $N(t)$ - final amount of substance (residue).
  • $N_0$ - the initial amount of the substance.
  • $t$ — time elapsed since the beginning of the process.
  • $T_{1/2}$ is the half-life of a particular isotope.

If you need to find the decay time or the period itself, the formula is transformed using logarithms, which our script does automatically, saving you from manual calculations.

How to use an online calculator

We have developed an interface that supports three operating modes depending on your task:

1. Calculation of the remaining substance

Used when you know the starting mass and time. For example, to understand what activity of Iodine-131 will remain in the body a week after the procedure.

2. Determination of decay time

Allows you to find out how long it will take for the amount of the isotope to decrease to a certain safe level.

3. Search for half-life

It is necessary in research tasks when it is necessary to identify an isotope or its properties based on a change in mass over a known period of time.
