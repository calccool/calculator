Calculation formulas

For the annuity payment, the classic banking formula is used: $$A = P \cdot \frac{r(1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • A - monthly payment,
  • P — loan amount (cost minus contribution),
  • r - monthly interest rate,
  • n — number of months of loan.

For the classic scheme (uniform repayment of the loan body): $$A_m = \frac{P}{n} + P_m \cdot r$$

Where Aₘ — payment in the mth month, Pₘ — the balance of the debt at the beginning of the month.

Frequently asked questions

What to choose - an annuity or a classic scheme?

An annuity is more convenient for a stable budget, since the payments are the same. The classic scheme is more profitable in terms of overpayment, but payments are reduced gradually.

Is it possible to take into account early payments?

Yes, the calculator supports one-time and monthly early repayments - with recalculation of the term or amount.

What is the optimal mortgage term?

At the same rate, it is more profitable to take out a loan for a shorter period: the overpayment falls exponentially with each shortened year.
