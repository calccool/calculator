Height Calculator (WHO): Find out Height Standards by Age for Children and Adults

Height Calculator: Comparison with WHO Data

Human height is one of the key indicators of physical development, especially important in childhood and adolescence. Parents often wonder: “Is my child’s height normal?” Adults also often compare their height with the average.

Our height calculator is a tool that allows you to instantly estimate the height of children (0 to 18 years) and adults (18+) based on authoritative data World Health Organization (WHO).

Unlike simple tables, the calculator automatically calculates your exact age (in years and months) and provides not just an “average” value, but a full normal range (15th to 85th percentile), giving a clear conclusion: “Normal”, “Below normal” or “Above normal”.

How to use the calculator?

  1. Floor: Select your gender (Male or Female) as height standards vary significantly.
  2. Date of Birth: Please indicate your exact date of birth.
  3. Current growth: Enter your actual height in centimeters.
  4. Date of measurement: Specify the date on which the measurement is taken (the default is “today”).
  5. Click "Calculate".

The calculator will instantly determine your exact age and show your status in comparison with WHO standards.

What are WHO percentiles (centile corridors)?

WHO does not use the concept of “ideal height”. Instead, the concept is applied percentiles (or “centile corridors”), which show the distribution of height among healthy children of a certain age and gender.

Our calculator uses three key metrics to estimate:

  • Average norm (50th percentile): This is the median growth. 50% of healthy people of this age are below this value, and 50% are above.
  • Normal range (15th – 85th percentile): It is considered the standard “normal corridor”. If your height falls within this range, it is considered completely normal.
  • Grade: Calculator output based on this data.

Important: “Below” or “Above” the norm is not a diagnosis, but only a statistical indicator that may be determined by genetics or individual development rates.

Height standards for adults (18+ years)

For people over 18-19 years of age (when growth plates close), the calculator uses average statistical data on the average height of the adult population. In this case, the score shows your position relative to the average height of adult men or women in the world.

Formulas for predicting growth

Although our calculator estimates current growth, there are several popular formulas for forecasting the child's final height based on the height of his parents.

Please note: These formulas are not used in our WHO calculator, but can be useful for general forecasting.

  1. Formula for boys: $$H_{boy} = \frac{(H_{father} + H_{mother}) \times 1.08}{2}$$
  2. Formula for girls: $$H_{girl} = \frac{(H_{father} \times 0.923) + H_{mother}}{2}$$

Where:

  • $H_{father}$ - father's height in cm.
  • $H_{mother}$ - mother's height in cm.

Medical Importance of Height Tracking

For children, sharp deviations from their percentile “corridor” (for example, if the child has always been in the 70th percentile and suddenly dropped to the 20th) are an important signal for contacting a doctor.
