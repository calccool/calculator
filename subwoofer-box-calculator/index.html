Subwoofer box volume calculator: calculating GV and FI online

Creating high-quality bass in a car does not start with buying an expensive amplifier, but with proper cabinet design. Even a budget speaker in a perfectly designed box will sound better than a top-of-the-line driver in a “random” box. Our calculator allows you to perform accurate engineering calculations of the two most popular types of design: Closed Box (CL) And Bass reflex (FI).

To get an accurate result, you will need the Thiel-Small parameters, which are usually indicated in the technical data sheet (Manual) of your speaker.

Thiel-Small parameters: foundation of calculation

Before you start calculating, you need to prepare three key indicators:

  • Fs (Resonant Frequency): The natural resonance frequency of the speaker in free space. The lower it is, the deeper bass the subwoofer can reproduce.
  • Vas (Equivalent Volume): A volume of air whose elasticity is comparable to the elasticity of the speaker suspension.
  • Qts (Total Q-factor): The total quality factor of the speaker, which determines its ability to effectively “brake” at the resonant frequency.

Choosing the type of registration: ZY or FI?

The choice between a closed box and a bass reflex depends on your musical preferences and the available space in the trunk.

Mathematical calculation formulas

Our calculator uses classical electroacoustics algorithms to approximate the ideal frequency response.

Sealed Box Calculation

For the SG, the main criterion is the final quality factor of the system ( $Qtc$ ). The optimal value is considered to be $Qtc = 0.707$ (Butterworth curve), which provides the best balance between bass depth and transient response.

Net volume formula ( $Vb$ ):

$$Vb = \frac{Vas}{(Qtc / Qts)^2 — 1}$$

Resonant frequency in box ( $Fc$ ):

$$Fc = Fs \cdot \frac{Qtc}{Qts}$$

Calculation of the Bass Reflex (Vented Box)

For FI, the calculation is more complicated, since the system strives for the most flat frequency response (Alignments). The optimal volume and tuning frequency ( $Fb$ ) are calculated using the formulas:

$$Vb = 15 \cdot Vas \cdot Qts^{2.87}$$

$$Fb = 0.42 \cdot Fs \cdot Qts^{-0.9}$$

Setting up the bass reflex port

If you choose FI, it is critical to correctly calculate the length of the pipe. Incorrect length will result in "buzz" or lack of bass. The length of the port ( $L$ ) depends on its cross-sectional area and the volume of the box:

$$L = \frac{23562.5 \cdot D^2}{Fb^2 \cdot Vb} — (0.823 \cdot D)$$

(Where $D$ is the pipe diameter in cm, $Vb$ is the volume in liters, $L$ is the length in cm)

How to use the calculator

  1. Select mode: Switch the tab to "ZY" for emphasis on quality or "FI" for maximum volume.
  2. Enter parameters: Specify the values ​​ $Fs$ , $Vas$ and $Qts$ from your subwoofer datasheet.
  3. Fine tuning: * In GY mode, you can change the target quality factor ( $Qtc$ ). Use the $0.5$ for audiophile dry bass or the $1.0$ for punchy punch.
    • In FI mode, enter the pipe diameter (usually 110 mm or 160 mm) to find out the required length.
  4. Result: The calculator will instantly give you the net internal volume. Remember that the volume displaced by the speaker itself and the port walls must be added to this value when designing the drawing.
