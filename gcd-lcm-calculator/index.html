What are GCD and NOC?

Before moving on to the calculations, it is important to understand the difference between these concepts:

  • GCD (GCD - Greatest Common Divisor) - this is the largest natural number by which all the numbers you choose are divisible without a remainder. For example, for the numbers 12 and 18, GCD is 6.
  • NOC (LCM - Least Common Multiple) - this is the smallest natural number, which itself is divisible without a remainder by each of the numbers you choose. For 12 and 18 the LCM is 36.

Relationship between GCD and NOC

There is a mathematical relationship between these two quantities that is often used to check the calculations of two numbers ( $a$ and $b$ ):

$$LCM(a, b) = \frac{|a \cdot b|}{GCD(a, b)}$$

How to find GCD and LCM manually?

There are several calculation methods, but the most reliable is considered prime factorization method.

Search algorithm:

  1. Break down each number into its prime factors (2, 3, 5, 7, 11, etc.).
  2. Write the factors as powers.
  3. For GCD: Select only those factors that found in all numbers, With minimum degree.
  4. For NOC: Select all occurring factors With maximum degree.

Case Study

Let's look at the numbers 24 And 36.

Calculation:

  • GCD (24, 36): Take the common factors with the smallest degree ( $2^2$ and $3^1$ ). $$2^2 \times 3 = 4 \times 3 = 12$$
  • LCM (24, 36): Take all multipliers with the highest degree ( $2^3$ and $3^2$ ). $$2^3 \times 3^2 = 8 \times 9 = 72$$
