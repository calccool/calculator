Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How to calculate sand for a foundation?

Multiply the area of ​​the base by the thickness of the layer. For example: 40 m² × 0.2 m = 8 m³ sand.

Why do we have different weights for the same volume?

Density varies. River sand is denser and heavier than quarry sand.

How to convert cubes to tons?

Multiply the volume by the density and divide by 1000. For example: 2 m³ × 1600 ÷ 1000 = 3.2 tons.

How many kg are in one m³ of sand?

From 1400 to 1900 kg depending on type.

How to convert kg to m³?

Divide the mass by the density: 3000 ÷ 1500 = 2 m³.
