Theoretical background and formulas

The distance from a point to a line is the length of the perpendicular drawn from this point to the line. In the Cartesian coordinate system, this problem is solved by the method of normals.

Basic Distance Formula

If the straight line is given by the general equation $Ax + By + C = 0$ , and the point has coordinates $M(x_0, y_0)$ , then the distance $d$ is calculated by the formula:

$$d = \frac{|A \cdot x_0 + B \cdot y_0 + C|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$$

Where:

  • $A, B$ - coefficients of variables (determine the normal vector of the line).
  • $C$ is a free member.
  • $x_0, y_0$ - coordinates of the starting point.
  • $\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}$ — normalizing factor (normal vector length).

Finding the projection point

Often in engineering problems it is necessary to find not only the length, but also the coordinates of the point itself $H(x_H, y_H)$ at which the perpendicular falls. Our calculator uses the following algorithm:

  1. Calculate the auxiliary coefficient $k$ : $$k = \frac{A \cdot x_0 + B \cdot y_0 + C}{A^2 + B^2}$$
  2. Find the projection coordinates: $$x_H = x_0 — A \cdot k$$ $$y_H = y_0 — B \cdot k$$

Calculation example

Let's look at a practical problem to check how the formula works.

Condition:

Given a point $M(3; 5)$ and a straight line $2x — y + 1 = 0$ . You need to find the distance from a point to a line.

Solution:

  1. We write out the coefficients: $A = 2$ , $B = -1$ , $C = 1$ .
  2. Point coordinates: $x_0 = 3$ , $y_0 = 5$ .
  3. Substitute the values ​​into the distance formula:

$$d = \frac{|2 \cdot 3 + (-1) \cdot 5 + 1|}{\sqrt{2^2 + (-1)^2}}$$

  1. We calculate the numerator: $|6 — 5 + 1| = |2| = 2$ .
  2. We calculate the denominator: $\sqrt{4 + 1} = \sqrt{5} \approx 2.236$ .
  3. Final result: $d = \frac{2}{2.236} \approx 0.894$ .

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the distance is zero?

If the result of the calculation is $d = 0$ , this means that the point $M$ lies directly on the line.

Why can't coefficients A and B be zero?

The coefficients $A$ and $B$ determine the direction of the straight line. If they are both equal to zero, the $C = 0$ equation no longer describes a straight line on the plane, and calculating the distance becomes impossible.

In what units is the result measured?

The calculator works with abstract coordinate units. If your coordinates are given in centimeters, then the result will be in centimeters.
