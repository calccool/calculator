Obstetric vs Embryonic term: what is the difference?

Many expectant mothers are confused about the terms. It is important to understand the difference:

  • Obstetric term (Gestational age): This is what doctors use in antenatal clinics and our calculator. The countdown is from first day of last menstruation. This is due to the fact that it is difficult to accurately determine the day of conception, but a woman knows the date of her period for sure.
  • Embryonic life: The actual age of the fetus from the moment of fertilization. It is usually 2 weeks less than obstetric.

Calculation formula: Naegele's Rule

Our algorithm is based on a modified Nägele's rule - the gold standard in obstetrics.

The basic formula looks like this:

$$ПДР = ДПМ + 280 \text{ дней}$$

Where:

  • PDR — Estimated Date of Birth.
  • DPM — Start date of Last Menstruation.

However, the standard formula is designed for an ideal cycle of 28 days. Our calculator applies the correction:

$$ПДР_{корр} = ДПМ + 280 + (L_{цикла} — 28)$$

If your cycle lasts 30 days, the calculator will automatically move your due date forward by 2 days, making the forecast more reliable.

Pregnancy Calendar: Trimesters and Important Milestones

Pregnancy is conventionally divided into three stages (trimesters). Each of them brings its own changes in the mother’s body and the development of the fetus.

Schedule of mandatory ultrasound screenings

Our calculator automatically calculates the “windows” for mandatory prenatal screenings. This is critically important, since the information content of the research depends on a specific period.

  1. First screening (11–13 weeks + 6 days): Risk assessment of chromosomal abnormalities, nuchal translucency (nuchal translucency) measurement.
  2. Second screening (18–21 weeks): Detailed examination of the anatomy of organs, exclusion of malformations, assessment of the placenta.
  3. Third screening (30–34 weeks): Assessment of fetal weight, its position (presentation) and the amount of amniotic fluid.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How accurate is this calculator?

The accuracy of the Naegele method is about 70% (birth occurs in the calculated week). Only 5% of children are born exactly on their birthday. The norm is considered to be an urgent birth between 37 and 42 weeks.

What to do if your cycle is irregular?

If your cycle varies greatly (more than 5-7 days difference between months), the OB method may be biased. In this case, the most accurate method for determining the period would be Ultrasound of the first trimester (measurement of KTP - coccygeal-parietal size).

Why is the ultrasound due date different from the monthly due date?

This is fine. If the difference is less than 5-7 days, doctors usually leave the period at monthly intervals. If the difference is greater, the period is adjusted according to ultrasound data.
