Horoscope house cusp calculator: calculation in the Placidus and Koch systems online

In professional astrology, the cusp is the “front door” of a house, the point from which the influence of a certain area of ​​life begins. Accurate calculation of the cusps is critical, since even a difference of 1-2 degrees can completely change the interpretation of the natal chart, moving the planet from one house to another.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the boundaries of all 12 houses, using two of the most popular systems in modern Western astrology: Placidus system And Koch system. This makes it possible not only to build a grid of houses, but also to conduct a comparative analysis for deeper forecasting.

What is a house cusp?

The cusp is the strongest point of the sector. It is believed that the planet, being in conjunction with the cusp (within the orb of 3-5°), has the most concentrated influence on the affairs of this house.

  • Corner houses: I (ASC), IV (IC), VII (DSC), X (MC). They determine the backbone of personality and eventfulness.
  • Subsequent houses: II, V, VIII, XI. Responsible for resources and development.
  • Falling houses: III, VI, IX, XII. Associated with adaptation and transfer of experience.

Placidus and Koch systems: what is the difference?

Choosing a house system is a matter of school and personal practice of the astrologer. Despite the fact that both systems are quadrant systems (based on the ASC and MC axes), their algorithms for calculating intermediate cusps are different.

Mathematical basis of calculation

To determine the exact position of the houses, the calculator first calculates Sidereal Time (LST) and Right Ascension of Midheaven (RAMC). The basic formulas are based on spherical trigonometry.

For calculation Medium Coeli, MC The formula used is:

$$MC = \arctan\left(\frac{\tan RAMC}{\cos \epsilon}\right)$$

Where $\epsilon$ is the inclination of the ecliptic to the equator (approximately 23.44° ).

For calculation Ascendant (ASC) the following equation applies:

$$ASC = \arctan\left(\frac{\cos RAMC}{-\sin RAMC \cdot \cos \epsilon — \tan \phi \cdot \sin \epsilon}\right)$$

Where $\phi$ is the geographic latitude of the place of birth.

Intermediate houses in the Placidus system are calculated by trisection of time arcs, which requires iterative calculations to find the intersection points of the ecliptic with the corresponding house circles.

How to use the calculator

To obtain an accurate house grid, you need to prepare three data parameters:

  1. Date of birth: Helps calculate the position of stars and planets relative to the ecliptic.
  2. Local time: It should be indicated as accurately as possible (preferably down to minutes), since the cusps of the houses shift by 1 degree every 4 minutes.
  3. Coordinates (Latitude and Longitude): The location determines the inclination of the horizon and, as a result, the position of the Ascendant.
