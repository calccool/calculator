Motorcycle customs clearance calculator: online calculation of all payments

When importing motor vehicles from abroad by individuals, a system is used total customs payment (CTP). Unlike passenger cars, where there are often flat rates per engine size, for motorcycles the calculation is based on a complex multi-step formula that includes four main components.

Our online tool allows you to calculate in seconds the total amount you will need to pay at customs. The algorithm takes into account current customs grids and exchange rates, ensuring accuracy to the ruble.

Formula for calculating the total amount

To determine the total cost of customs clearance, the following equation is used:

$$T_{total} = D + E + V + S$$

Where the components are deciphered like this:

  • $D$ (Duty): Customs duty.
  • $E$ (Excise): Excise duty (depending on power).
  • $V$ (VAT): Value added tax (VAT 20%).
  • $S$ (Service Fee): Fee for customs operations.

Main components of customs duties

Understanding the payment structure will help you correctly estimate the budget for buying a bike at auctions (e.g. BDS, JBA) or from dealers in Europe.

Customs Duty ( $D$ )

The size of the duty directly depends on the volume of the engine combustion chamber (cubic capacity). There are two main thresholds:

  1. Up to 250 $см^3$ inclusive: the rate is 10% from customs value.
  2. Over 250 $см^3$ : the rate increases to 15%.

Power Excise Tax ( $E$ )

Excise tax is a tax that depends on the “herd” under the saddle. For most motorcycles it is zero, but powerful bikes are subject to significant taxes:

  • Up to 150 hp: bet 0 rubles.
  • Over 150 hp: the calculation is for each unit of power (in 2026 the rate is about 611–640 rubles per 1 hp, depending on indexation).

Value added tax ( $V$ )

The most insidious part of the calculation. VAT in the amount 20% is calculated not on the net purchase price, but on the sum of the price of the bike and taxes already accrued:

$$V = (Cost_{rub} + D + E) \times 20\%$$

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

To get the most accurate result, follow this algorithm:

1. Assessment of customs value

Enter the price you purchased the motorcycle for. Important: Customs checks prices using their catalogs (eg NADA or Schwacke). If the price in the invoice is significantly lower than the market price, the inspector has the right to adjust it upward.

2. Specification of technical data

  • Engine size: critical for choosing the duty rate (10% or 15%).
  • Power: check the title or model manual. Even 151 hp. will result in an excise tax of more than 90,000 rubles.

3. Select currency and rate

Usually payments are made in euros or dollars. Our calculator allows you to select a currency and automatically convert everything into rubles at the current exchange rate of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, since payment at customs is made exclusively in national currency.

Professional tips for saving

  • Pass-through models: Look for motorcycles under 250cc (like the Honda CRF250 or Kawasaki Ninja 250) if you want to save 5% on duty.
  • Power limit: Try to select models with power up to 150 hp. (for example, 148-149 hp) to avoid a “penalty” excise tax.
  • Disposal collection: Currently, there is no recycling fee for motorcycles imported by individuals for personal use.
