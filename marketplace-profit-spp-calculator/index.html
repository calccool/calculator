Marketplace profit calculator: calculating seller discounts and SPP (Unit economics)

To successfully trade on marketplaces, it is not enough to simply set a price. It is important to understand how the platform’s marketing tools affect your income. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate unit economics goods, sharing your costs and marketplace bonuses for buyers.

Basic functions of the calculator

This tool automates calculations for the following parameters:

  • Retail price: The base price of an item before any discounts are applied.
  • Cost price: the amount of purchase, packaging and preparation of goods.
  • Seller discount: the percentage that you set yourself to participate in promotions.
  • SPP (Loyal Customer Discount): a bonus from the marketplace, which reduces the price for the client, but does not always reduce your payout.
  • Commission and logistics: mandatory site retention and delivery costs.

Calculation formulas

The calculator uses a mathematically verified algorithm that complies with the rules of the largest retailers:

  1. Price after seller discount ( $P_{s}$ ): $$P_{s} = P_{initial} \times (1 — \frac{D_{s}}{100})$$ Where $P_{initial}$ is the retail price, $D_{s}$ is the seller's discount.
  2. Final price for the client ( $P_{client}$ ): $$P_{client} = P_{s} \times (1 — \frac{SPP}{100})$$
  3. Commission amount ( $Sum_{comm}$ ): It is important to remember that the marketplace commission is most often calculated from the price with a seller’s discount, but to SPP deduction: $$Sum_{comm} = P_{s} \times \frac{Comm_{\%}}{100}$$
  4. Net profit ( $Profit$ ): $$Profit = (P_{s} — Sum_{comm}) — Cost — Logistics$$

Why is it important to use this calculator?

When participating in large promotions (for example, Black Friday), marketplaces often require a significant discount. If you don’t calculate the economics in advance, you can end up in the red, since the commission will be deducted from the price you set, and not from the one the buyer will pay, taking into account all the bonuses.

Step-by-step calculation instructions

  • Step 1: Enter the retail price you plan to include on the card.
  • Step 2: Indicate the cost per unit of goods (take into account not only the purchase, but also the labeling).
  • Step 3: Enter the percentage of the planned discount (usually 10–20%).
  • Step 4: Enter the current SPP percentage (it can be seen in your personal account or on the website from the buyer’s side).
  • Step 5: Click "Calculate" to get a detailed chart and total numbers.

Using this tool allows you to avoid cash gaps and accurately predict the net profit from each sale.
