Keratin, Botox and nanoplasty consumption calculator: accurate calculation of composition and cost

Accurate calculation of the composition is the key to the profitability of the master. An excess of product leads to unjustified expenses and “overload” of the client’s hair, and a deficiency leads to a violation of technology and a lack of results. Our calculator takes into account not only the length, but also the key physical parameters of the hair fiber, as well as the specifics of a particular product.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm is based on professional consumption standards, where the base value is the consumption of the composition per 1 cm of hair of normal thickness.

The formula by which the tool works:

$$V_{total} = L \cdot K_{base} \cdot D \cdot P \cdot T$$

Where:

  • $V_{total}$ - final volume of the composition in milliliters (ml).
  • $L$ - actual hair length in centimeters (cm).
  • $K_{base}$ - base flow coefficient (is 1.5 ml per 1 cm of length).
  • $D$ — density coefficient (from 0.8 to 1.5 ).
  • $P$ — porosity coefficient (up to 1.2 for damaged hair).
  • $T$ is a procedure type modifier.

Factors influencing the consumption of the composition

To obtain an ideal result, the master needs to evaluate three key parameters:

  • Hair length: Measured from crown to tip. Standard marks: 30 cm (shoulders), 45 cm (shoulder blades) and 65 cm (waist).
  • Density and structure: Thick hair has a large total surface area, which requires an increase in the volume of the composition to 50% of the norm.
  • Porosity: Damaged, bleached or “empty” hair absorbs the composition like a sponge. For such cases, the calculator provides an increasing factor of 20%.

Depending on the consistency and density of the product, the consumption will vary. Botox is usually denser, while nanoplasty is applied in a thin layer.

Financial calculation and cost

In addition to technical expense, it is extremely important to understand economics of services. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the cost based on the purchase price.

The final cost ( $C_{total}$ ) includes:

  1. Cost of the spent composition: $C_{comp} = (V_{total} / V_{bottle}) \cdot Price_{bottle}$ .
  2. Additional materials: deep cleaning shampoo (SHCH), finishing mask (step 3).
  3. Consumables: gloves, negligees, disposable towels.
  4. Depreciation: wear and tear of the iron and hair dryer.

Expert advice: Always put it in “Extras.” materials” a fixed amount (from 300 to 500 rubles) in order to cover small expenses and not work at a loss.
