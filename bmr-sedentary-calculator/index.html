BMR calculator for sedentary and bedridden people: calculating daily calorie intake

For people who are on bed rest or who lead an extremely sedentary lifestyle (for example, using a wheelchair), standard fitness calculators often produce inflated values. An error of 200–300 kcal per day can lead to a rapid gain of fat mass or, conversely, to exhaustion of the body if clinical stress factors (recovery from surgery or injury) are not taken into account.

Our tool uses a science-based approach to determine basal metabolic rate (BMR) And total energy consumption (TDEE) taking into account specific activity rates and health status.

Who needs this calculation:

  • People on long-term bed rest (recovery from injuries);
  • Elderly people with minimal physical activity;
  • Patients in rehabilitation periods;
  • Persons using wheelchairs (ward mode);
  • Medical workers to correct the diet of patients under their care.

Scientific basis: Formulas and coefficients

The calculation is based on the Mifflin-San Geor equation, which is recognized by the American Dietetic Association (ADA) as the most accurate for modern conditions.

Basal metabolic rate (BMR)

This is the amount of energy that the body spends in a state of complete rest to maintain the functioning of the brain, heart, lungs and other organs.

$$BMR_{men} = (10 \times вес, кг) + (6.25 \times рост, см) — (5 \times возраст, лет) + 5$$

$$BMR_{women} = (10 \times вес, кг) + (6.25 \times рост, см) — (5 \times возраст, лет) — 161$$

Total Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

To obtain the final value, BMR is multiplied by physical activity factors ( $AF$ ) and metabolic stress factors ( $SF$ ):

$$TDEE = BMR \times AF \times SF$$

Why is accuracy critical?

With limited mobility, metabolism slows down and muscle tissue atrophies faster. If you consume calories according to “usual” standards, excess energy will be deposited in visceral fat, which creates additional stress on the cardiovascular system.

Dietary recommendations for low BMR

  1. Protein control: Even with a calorie deficit, it is important to maintain protein levels (1.2–1.5 g per kg of body weight) to slow down muscle atrophy.
  2. Fractionality: Eat small meals 5-6 times a day to avoid heaviness in the gastrointestinal tract, which is slowed down by a sedentary lifestyle.
  3. Hydration: Water is necessary for metabolic processes. Calculate the norm as 30 ml per 1 kg of current weight.
  4. Accounting for stress factors: If the body is fighting an infection, the need for energy increases by 10–20%, even if the person does not move.
