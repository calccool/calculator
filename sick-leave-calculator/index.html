Sick leave calculator: online calculation based on length of service and minimum wage

Calculating temporary disability benefits is a multi-step process that depends on your earnings over the last two years, your accumulated insurance coverage, and current government limits. In 2025, the algorithm became more transparent thanks to the digitalization of payments through the Social Fund of Russia (SFR), but the basic calculation rules remained the same.

Basic parameters for calculation

To make the result as accurate as possible, the calculator uses the following input data:

  • Income for the previous 2 years: All official payments from which the employer paid insurance premiums are summed up.
  • Insurance experience: The total period of work during which contributions were transferred for you.
  • Duration of illness: The number of calendar days indicated in the electronic certificate of incapacity for work (ELS).
  • Minimum wage: The minimum wage, which serves as the “lower limit” for payments.

The influence of length of service on the amount of payment

The percentage of average earnings directly depends on how many years you have officially worked. This is one of the key factors that our calculator takes into account automatically.

Important exception: When caring for a sick child under 8 years of age, the benefit is always paid in the amount 100%, regardless of the parent’s length of service.

Formula for calculating sick leave

The mathematical calculation model is based on determining the average daily earnings ( $SDZ$ ). The formula looks like this:

$$SDZ = \frac{D_1 + D_2}{730} \times K_{st}$$

Where:

  • $D_1, D_2$ - income for the first and second accounting years;
  • $730$ - fixed number of days for calculation;
  • $K_{st}$ - experience coefficient (0.6, 0.8 or 1.0).

After determining $SDZ$ , the resulting value is compared with the maximum and minimum limits established by law for the current year.

Payout limits

The law limits both the minimum and maximum benefit amount per day. This protects the fund's budget and guarantees basic payments to low-paid employees.

Maximum and minimum values

In 2025, the maximum average daily earnings is limited to 5 673.97 $. This is due to the maximum bases for calculating contributions for previous periods.

On the other hand, if your income is below the minimum or your length of service is too short, the calculation is made according to the minimum wage. From January 1, 2025, the minimum wage is set at 22 440 $. In this case, the daily benefit is calculated based on the number of days in a particular month (for example, in January - 31 days, in February - 28).

Who pays the money?

The distribution of the financial burden between the employer and the state looks like this:

  1. First 3 days of illness: Paid from the employer's funds (only in case of illness or injury of the employee).
  2. From the 4th day until the closure of the ENL: Payments are made by the Social Fund of Russia (SFR) directly to the employee’s card (usually MIR).
  3. Caring for a child or family member: The entire payment period falls on the shoulders of the SFR from the first day.

All amounts calculated by the calculator are subject to personal income tax at the rate of 13%. You receive the amount in your hands minus this tax (except for maternity benefits, which are not taxed).
