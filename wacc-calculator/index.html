WACC Calculator: Weighted Average Cost of Capital Calculation

What is WACC and why calculate it?

WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital) is the weighted average cost of capital. This indicator demonstrates the minimum rate of return that a company must generate in order to satisfy the requirements of all its investors (both creditors and shareholders).

In corporate finance, WACC is used as:

  • Discount rate (Hurdle Rate) when assessing investment projects (NPV).
  • An indicator for assessing the value of a business.
  • Economic Value Added (EVA) metric.

Our calculator allows you to carry out calculations using both a simplified method (entering ready-made rates) and a professional one - using the CAPM model to estimate the cost of equity capital.

WACC Calculation Formula

The basic formula used in financial modeling is as follows:

$$WACC = \left( \frac{E}{V} \times Re \right) + \left( \frac{D}{V} \times Rd \times (1 — T) \right)$$

Where:

  • $E$ - Market Value of Equity.
  • $D$ - Market Value of Debt.
  • $V$ - Total cost of capital ( $E + D$ ).
  • $Re$ — Cost of equity capital (required return of shareholders).
  • $Rd$ — Cost of borrowed capital (interest rate on debt).
  • $T$ — Corporate tax rate.

Important: Pay attention to the $(1 — T)$ multiplier. This is the so-called "tax shield". Because interest payments on debt are tax deductible, the true cost of debt to a company is reduced by the tax amount.

Calculation of the cost of equity (Re)

Determining $Re$ is more difficult than the cost of debt because shareholders do not have a fixed interest rate. Our calculator supports the calculation of $Re$ through the model CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model):

$$Re = Rf + \beta \times (Rm — Rf)$$

  • $Rf$ (Risk-Free Rate): The risk-free rate of return (usually the yield on government bonds).
  • $\beta$ (Beta): A coefficient that reflects the volatility of an asset compared to the market.
  • $Rm$ (Expected Market Return): Expected market return.
  • $(Rm — Rf)$: Market risk premium.

How to interpret the results?

The WACC result is expressed as a percentage. This is the “bar” that any new company project must overcome in order to be profitable.

Step-by-step calculation example

Let's imagine a company with the following parameters:

  1. The shares are worth $800,000 ($E$).
  2. Debts amount to $200,000 ($D$).
  3. Investors demand 10% yield ( $Re$ ).
  4. The bank gives a loan against 5% ($Rd$).
  5. Income tax - 20% ($T$).

Calculation:

  1. Equity weight: $800 / 1000 = 0.8$ (80%).
  2. Debt weight: $200 / 1000 = 0.2$ (20%).
  3. Cost of debt including tax: $5\% \times (1 — 0.2) = 4\%$ .
  4. WACC = $(0.8 \times 10\%) + (0.2 \times 4\%) = 8\% + 0.8\% = \mathbf{8.8\%}$.
