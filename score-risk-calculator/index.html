SCORE Calculator: Estimating the Risk of Death from Cardiovascular Diseases

What is the SCORE scale?

Scale SCORE Systematic Coronary Risk Evaluation is the main tool recommended by the European Society of Cardiology (ESC) to assess the risk of death from cardiovascular disease (CVD) in the next 10 years.

Unlike simple tests, this calculator is based on large-scale research involving more than 200,000 people. It identifies asymptomatic patients who require immediate lifestyle changes or drug therapy to prevent myocardial infarction, stroke or heart failure.

How does risk assessment work?

The SCORE algorithm takes into account five key risk factors, which together provide a more accurate picture than each indicator alone:

  1. Floor: Men have a statistically higher risk of developing CVD than women of the same age.
  2. Age: The scale is applicable to people from 40 to 65 years old.
  3. Smoking status: Smoking is a powerful factor that doubles the risk of vascular accidents.
  4. Systolic blood pressure (SBP): "Upper" pressure.
  5. Total cholesterol: Blood lipid levels.

Selecting a risk region: Why is it important?

Our calculator allows you to select your region of residence, which significantly increases the accuracy of the forecast. Mortality statistics vary from country to country in Europe.

  • Low risk: France, Italy, Spain, Belgium, etc.
  • High risk: Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, Baltic countries, Eastern Europe.

Important: If you live in a country with a high mortality rate from CVD, using factors for “low risk” may lead to false positive reassurance and underestimation of the threat.

Interpretation of results

The result of the calculator is the probability (as a percentage) of the occurrence of a fatal cardiovascular event within 10 years.

Mathematical model

The calculator is based on the Weibull mathematical model, which calculates the accumulated probability of an event. A simplified dependence of risk ( $R$ ) on factors looks like this:

$$Risk = 1 — S_0(t)^{\exp(\text{General Risk Function})}$$

Where $S_0(t)$ is the basic survival rate for a given population (region), and the risk function includes the sum of weighted factors:

$$\text{General Risk Function} = \beta_1(\text{age}) + \beta_2(\text{cholesterol}) + \beta_3(\text{SBP}) + \beta_4(\text{smoking})$$

Who is NOT suitable for this calculator?

The SCORE scale is intended for primary prevention. You do not need to use this calculator if you are already automatically considered to be at very high risk for the following reasons:

  • You have already been diagnosed with a cardiovascular disease (CHD, heart attack or stroke).
  • You suffer from diabetes (type 1 or 2).
  • Have moderate or severe chronic kidney disease.
  • Total cholesterol level exceeds 8 mmol/l or blood pressure exceeds 180/110 mmHg.

In these cases, you need to follow the direct recommendations of your doctor, as your risk is already greater than 10%.
