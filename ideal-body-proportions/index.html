What is McCallum's formula?

John McCallum is a renowned author and strength training expert from the mid-20th century. He popularized a formula that has become the gold standard for natural athletes. Unlike modern "mass monster" standards, McCallum's formula aims to create a classic, athletic figure reminiscent of the Greek gods.

The calculations are based on only one parameter, which practically does not change with the gain of fat or muscle - wrist circumference. Bone thickness is thought to determine muscle growth potential and skeletal structure.

How to take measurements correctly?

To obtain an accurate result, it is important to measure the starting point correctly.

  1. Wrist: Measure just behind the bone (the styloid process of the ulna) towards the hand. The tape should fit snugly, but not tighten the skin.
  2. Current parameters (optional): If you want to compare yourself to an ideal, measure your chest as you inhale, your waist at its narrowest point, and your biceps and calves at their tightest point.

Mathematics of the ideal body

The calculation algorithm is built in a chain starting from the chest circumference. Here's what the formulas used in our calculator look like (where $Wrist$ is your wrist circumference):

  1. First, the basic chest size ( $Chest$ ) is calculated: $$Chest = 6.5 \times Wrist$$
  2. The remaining parts of the body are calculated as a percentage of the obtained chest girth:
    • Waist ( $Waist$ ): $$Waist = 0.70 \times Chest$$
    • Hips ( $Hips$ ): $$Hips = 0.85 \times Chest$$
    • Biceps ( $Arm$ ): $$Arm = 0.36 \times Chest$$
    • Forearm ( $Forearm$ ): $$Forearm = 0.29 \times Chest$$
    • Neck ( $Neck$ ): $$Neck = 0.37 \times Chest$$
    • Caviar ( $Calf$ ): $$Calf = 0.34 \times Chest$$
