Basic calculation formulas

A pyramid is a polyhedron whose base is a polygon and the remaining faces are triangles with a common vertex. For a regular pyramid (the base of which is a regular polygon, and the height falls at its center), the following formulas are used.

1. Pyramid volume ( $V$ )

The volume of any pyramid is equal to one third of the product of the area of ​​the base and the height:

$$V = \frac{1}{3} S_{осн} \cdot h$$

2. Side surface area ( $S_{бок}$ )

Equal to half the product of the perimeter of the base and the apothem (height of the side face):

$$S_{бок} = \frac{1}{2} P \cdot L$$

3. Total surface area ( $S_{полн}$ )

It is composed of the area of ​​the base and the area of ​​​​all side faces:

$$S_{полн} = S_{осн} + S_{бок}$$

4. Apothem ( $L$ )

This is the height of the side face dropped from the top. It is calculated through the Pythagorean theorem, using the height of the pyramid ( $h$ ) and the radius of the circle inscribed at the base ( $r$ ):

$$L = \sqrt{h^2 + r^2}$$

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

  • What is an apothem? Do not confuse an apothem with a lateral rib. Apothem ( $L$ ) is the height of the triangle, which is the side face. A side edge is the line where two faces meet.
  • How to find the angle of a face? The calculator calculates the angle $\alpha$ between the side face and the base plane through the tangent: $\tan(\alpha) = h / r$ , where $r$ is the radius of the inscribed circle.
