What is KDA and how is it calculated

KDA (Kill/Death/Assist) - this is a coefficient that sums up your useful activity (kills and assistance to allies) relative to the number of your deaths. Unlike simple K/D, this metric is more fair for support players, whose role is to help the team, and not to be the final blow.

Mathematical formula of KDA

The calculation uses a standard formula accepted in most eSports disciplines:

$$KDA = \frac{K + A}{D}$$

Where:

  • K (Kills) - the number of kills you have committed.
  • A (Assists) — number of assists or assists in a kill.
  • D (Deaths) - the number of your deaths.

If the death rate is zero, the system records «Perfect KDA», since mathematically division by zero is impossible, but in a gaming context it means the highest possible survivability.

Additional performance indicators

In addition to the basic KDA, professional analysts pay attention to other important metrics, which are also built into our tool.

K/D Ratio (Kill/Death Ratio)

This stat ignores assists and shows the player's pure ability to outshoot opponents. It is critical in shooters (for example, Call of Duty or CS:GO/CS2).

$$KD = \frac{K}{D}$$

Kill Participation (KP) - Participation in murders

This percentage shows how actively you contributed to the team's overall success. If a team has 50 kills and you have a hand in 40 of them, your KP will be very high, indicating your key role in the match.

Kill Participation Formula:

$$KP = \frac{(K + A)}{Team\ Kills} \times 100\%$$
