Transport tax calculator for hybrid cars: online calculation

Owners of hybrid vehicles in Russia face a special procedure for calculating tax. Unlike conventional cars with internal combustion engines, the key factor here is the type of hybrid installation and the method of fixing the power in the EVTS. Our calculator allows you to instantly determine the payment amount, based on the current methods of the Ministry of Finance of the Russian Federation.

Features of taxation of various types of hybrids

The tax base directly depends on how the electric motor interacts with the wheels and internal combustion engine. According to the latest clarifications, hybrids are divided into three main categories:

  1. Full and plug-in hybrids (HEV/PHEV): In these cars, the electric motor can drive the car independently. The tax base is sum engine power and maximum 30-minute electric motor power.
  2. Mild hybrids (MHEV): The electric motor acts as a starter-generator and helps the internal combustion engine during acceleration, but does not have a purely electric driving mode. Most often, the tax is paid only for the “horses” of a gasoline or diesel unit.
  3. Sequential hybrids (electric cars with a range extender): The internal combustion engine is used only to charge the battery. Here the rules may vary depending on the region, but most often summation also applies.

Formulas for calculating taxable capacity

For correct calculation, we use the official conversion factor from kilowatts to horsepower:

$$P_{hp} = P_{kW} \cdot 1.35962$$

The general tax base formula ( $P_{total}$ ) for full hybrids is as follows:

$$P_{total} = P_{ICE} + (P_{elec\_max30} \cdot 1.35962)$$

Where:

  • $P_{ICE}$ - internal combustion engine power (hp);
  • $P_{elec\_max30}$ - maximum 30-minute motor power (kW).

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, follow the algorithm:

  • Enter the power of the petrol or diesel engine from the registration certificate (CTC).
  • Indicate the power of the electric motor in kW (if it is indicated in the EPTS as a maximum 30-minute power).
  • Select the tax rate that is relevant for your region (for example, in Moscow for powerful cars it is 150 rubles).
  • Indicate the number of complete months of car ownership in the reporting year.
  • Activate the “Luxury Tax” switch if your car is included in the list of the Ministry of Industry and Trade (cost over 10 million rubles).

Luxury tax for hybrids

If your hybrid car costs more than 10 million rubles and is included in the annually updated list of the Ministry of Industry and Trade, an increasing factor $K_{lux} = 3$ is applied to the calculation.

The total tax amount ( $T$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$T = P_{total} \cdot Rate \cdot \frac{M}{12} \cdot K_{lux}$$

Where $Rate$ is the regional rate, and $M$ is the number of months of ownership. Our calculator automatically applies all coefficients, eliminating the need for manual calculations.
