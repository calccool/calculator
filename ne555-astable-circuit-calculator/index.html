NE555 Timer Calculator: Calculation of Frequency and Duty Cycle (Astable Mode)

Calculation of a square pulse generator on NE555

Chip NE555 (and its analogs: LM555, KR1006VI1) is a legendary integrated timer that has been used in electronics for more than 50 years. Its most popular mode of operation is astable (self-oscillating). In this mode, the timer generates a continuous sequence of rectangular pulses of a given frequency.

Our calculator allows you to instantly select component values ​​( $R_1$ , $R_2$ , $C$ ) to obtain the desired frequency or, conversely, find out the output signal parameters for existing parts.

Operating principle of the circuit

In astable mode, the $C$ capacitor is charged and discharged cyclically:

  • Charger: Current flows through resistors $R_1$ and $R_2$ . This forms the pulse duration (logical “1”).
  • Discharge: Current flows only through $R_2$ to the 7th pin of the chip (Discharge). This forms the duration of the pause (logical "0").

That is why in the classical circuit the “on” time is always longer than the “pause” time, and the duty cycle (Duty Cycle) always exceeds 50%.

Calculation formulas

To manually calculate the parameters, the following formulas are used (where $\ln(2) \approx 0.693$ ):

1. Time intervals

The high signal time ( $t_1$ ) depends on the sum of both resistors:

$$t_1 = \ln(2) \cdot (R_1 + R_2) \cdot C \approx 0.693 \cdot (R_1 + R_2) \cdot C$$

Low level time ( $t_2$ ) depends only on $R_2$ :

$$t_2 = \ln(2) \cdot R_2 \cdot C \approx 0.693 \cdot R_2 \cdot C$$

Total oscillation period ( $T$ ):

$$T = t_1 + t_2 = 0.693 \cdot (R_1 + 2R_2) \cdot C$$

2. Frequency ( $f$ )

Frequency is inversely proportional to the period and is measured in Hertz:

$$f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1.44}{(R_1 + 2R_2) \cdot C}$$

3. Duty Cycle

The duty cycle ( $D$ ) shows the ratio of the pulse duration to the full period:

$$D = \frac{t_1}{T} \cdot 100\% = \frac{R_1 + R_2}{R_1 + 2R_2} \cdot 100\%$$
