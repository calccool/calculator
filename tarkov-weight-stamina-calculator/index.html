Weight and stamina calculator in Escape from Tarkov (EFT)

In the world of Tarkov, every gram of equipment directly affects the survival of a fighter. The weight of your equipment determines how quickly your stamina bar depletes, how loud you move, and whether you can dash in a critical situation.

This calculator allows you to instantly calculate your character's critical thresholds based on your current skill level. "Strength".

Basic formulas for calculating limits

The calculation system in the game is tied to linear progression. The higher your strength level, the more weight you can carry before negative effects occur.

The following dependencies are used to calculate thresholds:

  • Lower threshold (start of overload): $$L_{low} = 28 + (Strength \times 0.3)$$
  • Upper threshold (critical overload): $$L_{high} = 75 + (Strength \times 0.5)$$

Where $Strength$ is your current Strength skill level (from 0 to 51).

Elite Strength

Upon reaching level 51 (elite) of the Strength skill, special rules come into force. The main feature is that the game no longer takes into account the weight of objects worn directly on the fighter (body armor, unloading, helmet, weapons in the hands and holster).

Important: At level 51, only the contents of your backpack and secure container count toward your “effective weight.” This allows elite players to remain mobile even while wearing heavy armor.

Consequences of overload and fines

Depending on what percentage of the maximum limit your current weight is, the character receives progressive debuffs.

Impact on gameplay

  1. Stamina regeneration: When overloaded, endurance is restored much more slowly, and in deep overload it may not be restored even when walking.
  2. "Wiggle" effect: Heavy weight increases the swing of the weapon when aiming, making it difficult to shoot at a distance.
  3. Fall injuries: The heavier your character, the higher the damage received when falling from a height. At critical weight, even jumping from a small ledge can lead to broken legs.

Tips for managing weight in a raid

To minimize negative effects and successfully exit raids with valuable loot, follow these recommendations:

  • Drop your backpack: Before starting fire contact, always remove the heavy backpack (keyZdefault). This will instantly restore your mobility and allow you to maneuver effectively.
  • Use stimulants: Injectors such as SJ6 (increases stamina reserve and regeneration) or MULE (increases maximum carrying capacity by 50%) are required for carrying heavy loot.
  • Control your insurance: If you find more expensive armor or weapons, dump your insured equipment in a safe place and put on the captured equipment. This will reduce your current weight without losing your equipment (it will be returned through insurance).
  • Pumping through walking: Remember that the strength skill is upgraded only when you have at least the minimum “yellow” overload. Walking in this state is the most reliable way of passive pumping.
