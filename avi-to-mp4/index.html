AVI to MP4 Converter online: Instant repacking without loss of quality

Most classic online converters work according to an outdated scheme: you upload a heavy video file to someone else's server, wait in line, then the computing power spends hours transcoding the pixels, and only after that you download the result. Our tool uses a fundamentally different approach - instant Fast Remuxing directly in your browser.

The media formats AVI (Audio Video Interleave) and MP4 (MPEG-4 Part 14) are not videos themselves, but only “containers” or software boxes that store independent video and audio streams. Our browser script simply takes the source streams from the old AVI box and carefully transfers them into modern MP4, without changing the data structure or re-compressing the pixels.

Mathematics of the process: Why is it so fast?

With classic transcoding (which our competitors use), the processing time of $T_{trans}$ depends on the complexity of the codec algorithm and the processor clock speed:

$$T_{trans} = \frac{V_{frames} \times C_{complexity}}{P_{cpu}}$$

Where $V_{frames}$ is the number of frames, and $C_{complexity}$ is the mathematical complexity of compression (for example, for H.264 or HEVC). In our case, direct copying of the stream (Stream Copying) is used. Computational complexity tends to zero, and the execution time of the $T_{remux}$ operation depends solely on the read speed of your file system:

$$T_{remux} = \frac{S_{file}}{V_{io}} \approx 0$$

Because $V_{io}$ (the I/O speed of modern SSDs and RAM) is enormous, repacking a multi-gigabyte video file takes milliseconds.

Technical features of playback

It is important to consider the specifics of repacking. If your original archive AVI file was compressed with a very old codec (for example, DivX 3, Xvid or MPEG-2), then after changing the container to MP4, the file may not display the image on Apple ecosystem mobile devices (iPhone/iPad). These devices mainly support modern H.264 and H.265 standards in hardware. However, in most modern media players on Windows (VLC, MPC-HC) and Android devices, playback will be flawless.
