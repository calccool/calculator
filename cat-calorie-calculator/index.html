A Scientific Approach to Calorie Calculation

The calculator is based on the standards of the World Small Animal Veterinary Association (WSAVA). The calculation takes place in two stages:

  1. RER (Resting Energy Requirement) - this is the resting energy necessary to maintain life (breathing, heart function, digestion).
  2. DER (Daily Energy Requirement) - this is the final daily norm, which adjusts the basic metabolism depending on the lifestyle and physiological status of the cat.

To calculate the basal metabolic rate (RER), the most accurate allometric formula is used:

$$RER = 70 \times (\text{Вес в кг})^{0.75}$$

The total demand (DER) is calculated by multiplying the resulting value by the activity factor:

$$DER = RER \times \text{Коэффициент}$$

Activity and status coefficients

Each cat is individual. A neutered domestic couch potato needs significantly less energy than an active young or lactating cat.

Why is it important to count food portions in grams?

Many owners make the mistake of pouring food “by eye” or relying only on the recommendations on the packaging. However, manufacturers often indicate average values. Our tool allows you to integrate the calorie content of your specific feed into the calculation.

Benefits of using a calculator:

  • Prevention of obesity: You clearly see the calorie limit.
  • Feed savings: You don’t overpay for extra grams that go to the sides.
  • Accounting for food type: The ability to calculate the norm for both high-calorie dry food and less dense wet diets.
  • Accuracy for kittens: Selection of the norm for a growing organism, which requires 2-3 times more energy than adults.

Feeding recommendations

In addition to maintaining caloric intake, experts recommend adhering to several rules:

  • Fractionality: Divide the daily intake into 2–4 feedings.
  • Access to water: When feeding dry food, the cat should drink 3 times more water than eat the food.
  • Treats: They should not make up more than 10% of the daily diet. Don't forget to subtract the calories from your treats from your main serving!
  • Weight control: Weigh your cat once a month. If your weight is steadily growing while maintaining the norm, you should reduce the activity factor in the settings.
