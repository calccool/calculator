Well volume calculator: accurate calculation of water, soil and cost of work

Calculating the volume of a well is a critical stage both when planning construction and when operating an autonomous water supply source. Our online calculator allows you to accurately determine the geometric parameters of the mine, the useful volume of water and the financial costs of excavation work.

Why do you need an accurate well calculation?

Knowing the exact parameters is necessary for:

  • Selection of pumping equipment: To prevent the pump from running dry, you need to know the static level and water reserve.
  • Flow definitions: Understanding how quickly the volume fills will help you plan water consumption in your home.
  • Earthworks: Calculating the volume of soil, taking into account its loosening, allows you to order a truck of the required tonnage for removal.
  • Procurement of materials: calculation of the number of standard concrete rings (KS 10-9, KS 15-9).

Calculation method and formulas

The calculator is based on the formula for the volume of a truncated cone (since the well shaft often has a slight narrowing towards the bottom). If the diameters of $D1$ and $D2$ are equal, the figure becomes a cylinder.

Basic volume formula:

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \pi \cdot H \cdot (R_1^2 + R_2^2 + R_1 \cdot R_2)$$

Where:

  • $V$ — shaft volume ( $м^3$ );
  • $H$ - well depth ( $м$ );
  • $R_1$ — radius of the upper hole ( $м$ );
  • $R_2$ — bottom radius ( $м$ ).

To calculate the water reserve, we use the same formula, but instead of the total depth $H$ we substitute the height of the water column $W$ .

Useful tips for calculations

  1. Opening coefficient ( $K$ ): Remember that excavated soil takes up 20-30% more space than when compacted in the soil. For sand use $K = 1.1$ , for clay $K = 1.3$ .
  2. Volume in liters: To convert the resulting cubic meters of water into liters, multiply the result by 1000 ( $1 м^3 = 1000$ liters).
  3. Safety: When calculating the cost, do not forget to include the cost of bottom filters (shungite, gravel) and sealing the seams between the rings.
