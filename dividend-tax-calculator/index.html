Investing in dividend stocks is one of the most reliable ways to create passive income. Why take taxes into account when calculating

In most jurisdictions, including the Russian Federation, dividends are income of an individual and are taxed. In 2026, a progressive scale is maintained, where the rate depends on the investor’s total annual income. A calculation error of even 2% can significantly distort long-term capital planning.

Mathematical model for calculating dividends

To deeply understand the mechanics of your portfolio, we use two basic calculation models.

Calculation by number of shares

If you have already formed a position, the total payout before taxes (Gross) is calculated using the following formula:

$$D_{total} = N \times d_{share}$$

Where:

  • $N$ - total number of shares in the portfolio;
  • $d_{share}$ - the amount of dividend declared by the board of directors per security.

Calculation of net profit (Net)

To find out the final amount in hand, you need to apply the tax rate:

$$P_{net} = D_{total} \times (1 — \frac{T}{100})$$

Where $T$ is the tax rate (in percentage) relevant for your tax residence.

The magic of reinvestment and compound interest

One of the key functions of our calculator is the ability to calculate reinvestment of dividends. When you use your profits to buy new shares, the compound interest mechanism comes into play.

In the long term (from 5 to 20 years), the income from reinvestment may exceed the amount of the initial investment. The calculator takes into account:

  1. Organic price growth: An increase in the value of the shares themselves.
  2. Dividend flow: Tax-free annual payments.
  3. Capitalization: Increasing the body of capital through the purchase of additional securities.
