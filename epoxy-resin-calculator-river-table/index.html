Epoxy resin consumption calculator for river table: exact calculation in kg and liters

Creating furniture from slabs and polymers requires pinpoint precision in calculations. An error of 100 grams can lead to the fact that there is not enough resin for the finishing layer, or, conversely, there will be an expensive surplus that will simply harden in the container. Our calculator is designed specifically for woodworkers and takes into account nuances that beginners often miss: the difference between volume and mass, as well as technological losses.

Basic calculation parameters

To achieve an ideal result, there are three key factors to consider:

  1. Fill geometry. If for a rectangular tabletop the calculation is simple, then in a “table-river” the width of the channel changes. We recommend measuring the width in 5-7 places and calculating the arithmetic average.
  2. Composition density. Epoxy resin is heavier than water. On average, 1 liter of resin weighs 1.1 kg. Ignoring this fact is the main reason for the shortage of material.
  3. Proportions of components. Manufacturers indicate the ratio of A to B by weight. Our tool automatically distributes the total mixture into resin and hardener according to your settings (eg 2:1 or 3:1).

Mathematical formula for calculation

The calculator is based on a physical formula for converting volume into mass, taking into account the loss coefficient.

The total fill volume ( $V$ ) is calculated as:

$$V = \frac{L \cdot W \cdot D}{1000}$$

Where:

  • $L$ - length (cm);
  • $W$ - average width (cm);
  • $D$ —layer thickness (cm);
  • $1000$ - conversion factor from $см^3$ to liters.

The final mass of the mixture ( $M_{total}$ ) taking into account the density ( $\rho$ ) and stock ( $K_{waste}$ ):

$$M_{total} = V \cdot \rho \cdot (1 + \frac{K_{waste}}{100})$$

Why is it important to reserve for losses?

In a professional environment, the “Gold Standard” is considered to be a reserve of 5–10%. These surpluses are spent on:

  • Absorption into wood: Even after priming, the pores of the wood can take away part of the composition.
  • Remains on the walls: About 2-3% of the mixture remains on the walls of the bucket and mixer after mixing.
  • Meniscus and shrinkage: When hardening, the resin may slightly decrease in volume, and a concave edge (meniscus) is formed at the border with the formwork, which requires grinding.

Tips for using the calculator

To get the most accurate results, follow these recommendations:

  • Use scales: Always mix ingredients by weight, not volume. The densities of resin and hardener vary, so measuring cups will give an error.
  • Consider the temperature: If the workshop is cold, the resin becomes thicker, trapping more air bubbles in it, which can visually increase volume, but not weight.
  • Table-river mode: In this mode you enter the average channel width. If you have a solid rectangular fill, switch to the appropriate mode for convenience.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How much resin is needed for 1 sq. m with a thickness of 1 cm?

With a standard density of 1.1 kg/l, approximately 11 kg of composition is consumed per square meter with a layer of 10 mm (excluding losses).

Is it possible to mix resin from different manufacturers?

Strongly not recommended. Each system has its own unique proportions and density. Always use the calculator for your specific brand, entering the data from the label.

What happens if you make a mistake with the hardener?

Violation of the proportions leads either to the stickiness of the surface (not enough hardener) or to brittleness and excessive heating (too much hardener). Our tool calculates the mass of component B down to hundredths of a gram.
