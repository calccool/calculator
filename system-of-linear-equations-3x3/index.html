General view of the 3x3 system

A system of three linear equations in three variables ( $x, y, z$ ) is written as follows:

$$\begin{cases} a_1x + b_1y + c_1z = d_1 \\ a_2x + b_2y + c_2z = d_2 \\ a_3x + b_3y + c_3z = d_3 \end{cases}$$

Where:

  • $x, y, z$ - the required variables.
  • $a, b, c$ — coefficients for unknowns (numbers).
  • $d$ - free terms (numbers on the right side of the equality).

How the calculator works: Cramer Method

To solve this system, our algorithm uses Cramer method. This is one of the most elegant and visual ways to solve quadratic systems, based on the use of determinants.

The solution process consists of the following steps:

  1. Search for the main qualifier ( $\Delta$ ): A matrix is ​​compiled from the coefficients of the variables $x, y, z$ .
  2. Search for auxiliary qualifiers ( $\Delta_x, \Delta_y, \Delta_z$ ): They are obtained by sequentially replacing the columns of coefficients for the corresponding variable with the column of free terms $d$ .
  3. Calculation of roots: If the main determinant is not zero, the roots are found by simple division:

$$x = \frac{\Delta_x}{\Delta}, \quad y = \frac{\Delta_y}{\Delta}, \quad z = \frac{\Delta_z}{\Delta}$$
