Vector Operations: Addition and Subtraction

Vector algebra is the basis not only of higher mathematics, but also of physics, mechanics and computer graphics. Unlike ordinary numbers (scalars), vectors have not only a magnitude, but also a direction, so arithmetic operations with them are performed according to special rules.

Our online vector calculator allows you to instantly find the sum or difference of two vectors specified by their coordinates, both on a plane and in three-dimensional space.

How to find the sum of vectors? (Coordinate method)

There are several geometric addition methods (triangle rule, parallelogram rule). However, for accurate calculations it is most convenient to use algebraic (coordinate) method.

If two vectors $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ are given with coordinates:

  • $\vec{a} = \{x_1; y_1; z_1\}$
  • $\vec{b} = \{x_2; y_2; z_2\}$

Then their sum will be a new vector $\vec{c}$ , the coordinates of which are equal to the sum of the corresponding coordinates of the terms:

$$\vec{c} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} = \{x_1 + x_2; \quad y_1 + y_2; \quad z_1 + z_2\}$$

Vector difference

Subtraction of vectors is performed in the same way as addition. To find the difference $\vec{a} — \vec{b}$ , you need to subtract the corresponding coordinates of the second from the coordinates of the first vector:

$$\vec{c} = \vec{a} — \vec{b} = \{x_1 — x_2; \quad y_1 — y_2; \quad z_1 — z_2\}$$

Length (Modulus) of the resulting vector

Often it is not enough just to find the coordinates of a new vector - you need to find out its length (modulus). Our calculator does this automatically.

The magnitude of the vector $|\vec{c}|$ is calculated as the square root of the sum of the squares of its coordinates:

$$|\vec{c}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$$

Calculation example

Let's say we need to add two vectors in space:

$\vec{a} = \{1; 2; 3\}$ and $\vec{b} = \{4; 5; 6\}$ .

  1. Adding X's: $1 + 4 = 5$
  2. Adding up the games: $2 + 5 = 7$
  3. Adding up the zetas: $3 + 6 = 9$

Answer: The resulting vector is $\vec{c} = \{5; 7; 9\}$ .

Length: $\sqrt{5^2 + 7^2 + 9^2} = \sqrt{25 + 49 + 81} = \sqrt{155} \approx 12.45$
