Color mathematics: basic formulas

To understand the process, we provide mathematical models that the calculator algorithm uses.

Formula for a known volume of dye

If you have a fixed amount of paint ( $V_{dye}$ ), the volume of oxide required ( $V_{ox}$ ) is calculated as follows:

$$V_{ox} = V_{dye} \times R$$

Where $R$ is the aspect ratio (for example, 1.5 for a ratio of 1:1.5).

Formula for total volume of mixture

If you need to get a specific final volume ( $V_{total}$ ), the paint mass is calculated using the following formula:

$$V_{dye} = \frac{V_{total} — V_{mixton} \times (1 + k \times R)}{1 + R}$$

Where $k$ takes the value 1 if the mixton needs oxide, and 0 if not.

How not to make mistakes in calculations

Working with mixtones and correctors

Mixtons are added to neutralize unwanted shades or enhance color. In professional coloring there is a “rule 11”, but when using a calculator it is important to remember:

  • If you add more than 5-10 grams of mixton, Necessarily you need to calculate the additional portion of the oxidizing agent.
  • In our calculator, select the "Oxide per mixton" option if the corrector makes up a significant portion of the mixture.

Why accurate weight is important

The density of oxidizer and paint varies, so professionals always use digital scales rather than measuring cups.

  1. Place the bowl on the scale and zero the container.
  2. Squeeze out the required amount of paint, focusing on the calculation of the calculator.
  3. Add oxidizing agent until the desired final mass is achieved.
  4. Mix thoroughly until the emulsion is homogeneous.

Tips for choosing proportions

  • For gray hair: Always use 1:1. Excess oxide will make the mixture too liquid, and the pigment will not be able to firmly adhere to the hair structure.
  • For lightening: A 1:2 ratio with a special blonde allows you to reduce the aggressiveness of the alkaline environment, providing a cleaner and softer lightening.
  • For porous hair: When tinting, it is better to use a little more oxide (1:2) so that the dye is distributed evenly.
