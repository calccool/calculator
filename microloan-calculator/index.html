Online microloan calculator: Accurate calculation of interest, fines and overpayments

Microfinance organizations (MFIs) often attract clients by advertising “low” rates, for example, 0.8% or 1% per day. At first glance, this may seem insignificant, but when calculated as annual interest, the amounts can be shocking.

How to use the calculator

The tool is intuitive and requires entering only three basic parameters:

  1. Loan amount ( $S$ ): How much money are you borrowing?
  2. Deadline ( $T$ ): How many days is the contract valid for?
  3. Interest rate ( $R$ ): Daily rate (usually varies from 0% for new customers to 1% maximum by law in many countries).

Additional option: If you are late, indicate the number of days of delay to see the amount of fines and penalties.

Mathematics of microcredit: Calculation formulas

Understanding the formulas protects you from hidden fees. Most microloans are based on a simple interest scheme.

Basic formula for accrued interest

The interest amount ( $I$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$I = S \times \frac{R}{100} \times T$$

Where:

  • $S$ - loan amount;
  • $R$ - interest rate per day;
  • $T$ — loan term in days.

Formula for the full amount to be refunded

To understand how much will have to be given to the MFO, the following formula is used:

$$Total = S + I + F$$

Where $F$ is the amount of fines (if there is a delay).

Calculation example

Suppose you take 15 000 $ on 20 days under 0.8% per day.

  1. We calculate interest for one day: $15\,000 \times 0.008 = 120$ $.
  2. We calculate interest for the entire term: $120 \times 20 = 2\,400$ $.
  3. Total to be returned: $15\,000 + 2\,400 = 17\,400$ $.

What is APR and why is it important?

Many borrowers overlook APR (Annual Percentage Rate) - real annual interest rate. If a rate of 1% per day seems small, then on an annualized basis it looks different:

$$APR = R_{day} \times 365$$

For a rate of 1% per day, the APR is 365% per annum. For comparison: the average rate on a credit card is 20-30% per annum. Our calculator automatically produces an APR value so you can get an objective estimate of your money's worth.

Fines and Delays: The Hidden Threat

If you do not repay the debt on time, penalties will apply. They usually consist of two parts:

  1. Continuing interest: The calculation of the base rate on the remaining debt continues.
  2. Penalty (penalty): Additional interest (often up to 20% per annum or a fixed rate of the overdue amount).

Use the “Overdue (days)” field in our calculator to see how quickly your debt grows when your payments are missed.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Does the calculator calculate insurance?

No, this calculator calculates the pure financial mathematics of the loan. Insurance and additional services (SMS information, legal assistance) are optional, and you have the right to refuse them when registering.

How does the interest-free period work?

Many microfinance organizations offer the first loan at 0%. To calculate such a loan, simply enter the value in the “Interest per day” field 0.

Does delinquency affect your credit history?

Yes, data on overdue payments is transferred to the Credit History Bureau (BKI). This could prevent you from getting a mortgage or car loan in the future. Use our tool to plan your budget and pay off your debts on time.
