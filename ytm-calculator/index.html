Why simple coupon yields are misleading

Many new investors look only at the size of the coupon. However, a bond may trade above or below its par value. If you buy paper at a discount (cheaper than face value), your real yield will be higher than the coupon. If with a premium (more expensive than face value) - lower.

Basic terms for investors

To make the calculation as accurate as possible, our tool operates on the following data:

  • Denomination: The amount that the issuer will pay you on the maturity date (usually RUB 1,000).
  • Current price: The current market value of the bond.
  • ACK (Accumulated coupon income): Fair compensation to the seller for the time he has owned the paper since the last payment.
  • Time to maturity: The number of years you plan to hold the asset.

Formula for calculating yield to maturity (YTM)

Our calculator uses a modified approximation formula that allows you to quickly evaluate the effectiveness of investments without complex iterative calculations.

Mathematically it looks like this:

$$YTM \approx \frac{C + \frac{F — P}{t}}{\frac{F + P}{2}}$$

Where:

  • $C$ - annual coupon payment;
  • $F$ — par value of the bond;
  • $P$ — purchase price (including tax accrual);
  • $t$ - number of years until maturity.
