Unified Gas Law Calculator: Calculate P, V and T Online

The unified gas law is a fundamental equation in thermodynamics and physical chemistry that relates three key parameters of an ideal gas state: pressure ( $P$ ), volume ( $V$ ) and temperature ( $T$ ).

Unlike particular gas laws, which consider the change in two parameters while fixing the third, the combined law describes processes where all three quantities change simultaneously, provided that the amount of substance (gas mass) remains unchanged.

Formula of the law

Mathematically, the law is expressed by the following relationship:

$$\frac{P_1 \times V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2 \times V_2}{T_2} = \text{const}$$

Where:

  • $P_1, V_1, T_1$ — initial pressure, volume and temperature.
  • $P_2, V_2, T_2$ — final parameters of the system.

This formula reads: the ratio of the product of pressure and volume to absolute temperature remains constant for a fixed mass of gas.

Components of the law: “Three in one”

The name “United” is not accidental. This equation is a combination of three historical discoveries. If one of the variables is fixed, the equation is transformed into one of the particular laws.

Example of problem solution

Let's consider a classic problem to understand the logic of the tool.

Task:

The gas in the cylinder under the piston occupies a volume of 2 liters at a pressure of 1 atmosphere and a temperature of 25°C. The gas is compressed to a volume of 1 liter and simultaneously heated to 100°C. What will be the final pressure of the gas?

Solution:

  1. Given:
    • $P_1 = 1$ atm
    • $V_1 = 2$ l
    • $T_1 = 25^\circ C = 298.15 K$
    • $V_2 = 1$ l
    • $T_2 = 100^\circ C = 373.15 K$
    • $P_2 = ?$
  2. Derivation of the formula: From the equation $\frac{P_1 V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2 V_2}{T_2}$ we express $P_2$ : $$P_2 = \frac{P_1 \times V_1 \times T_2}{T_1 \times V_2}$$
  3. Substitution: $$P_2 = \frac{1 \times 2 \times 373.15}{298.15 \times 1} \approx 2.503 \text{ атм}$$

Answer: The final pressure will be approximately 2.5 atmospheres.
