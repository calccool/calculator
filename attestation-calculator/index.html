Methodology and calculation formulas

According to current regulations, the grade point average (GPA) is calculated as the arithmetic mean of all final grades on the education document. In 2026, all disciplines without exception are taken into account in the calculation, including “Music”, “Art” and “Technology”, if points are given for them.

The following mathematical model is used for the calculation:

$$GPA = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} g_i}{n}$$

Where:

  • $\sum g_i$ - the sum of all received ratings;
  • $n$ - the total number of items in the certificate.

Important for 9th grade graduates: Remember that the final grade in Russian language, mathematics and two elective subjects in 2026 is formed as the arithmetic average of annual and exam (OGE) grades, rounded in favor of the student.)

9th grade certificate: calculation features

  • Are taken into account annual estimates from 5th to 9th grade.
  • Examination score affects the outcome only in 4 subjects: mathematics, Russian and two electives.
  • Total = (annual + exam) / 2 → rounded according to the rules of mathematics.
  • Some subjects, such as physical education, may be credited rather than graded.

Rounding example:
Annual “5” + exam “4” = 4.5 → total “5”

11th grade certificate: calculation features

  • Are taken into account grades for grades 10 and 11.
  • GPA is important when admission without Unified State Exam (for example, for creative specialties).
  • Unified State Exam results do not affect for grades in the certificate.
  • Without passing the Unified State Exam in Russian and mathematics certificate is not issued.

Admissions trends in 2026: what you need to know

In the current academic year, there is a clear trend towards digitalization of admissions campaigns. Most applications are submitted through State Services, where the average score is automatically calculated from the digital registry. However, manual self-monitoring remains necessary for the following reasons:

  1. Registry check: There are frequent cases of technical failures when the data in the electronic system does not match the paper certificate. Our calculator will help you notice the error in time.
  2. Passing points for “Professionality”: In 2026, the Vocational program will cover more than 70% of colleges. Study periods have been shortened and score requirements have increased due to the high demand for practical education.
  3. Item priority: If two applicants have an equal average score, the admissions committee looks at grades in core subjects (for example, physics and mathematics for IT specialties).
