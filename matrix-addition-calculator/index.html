Theoretical background: Operations on matrices

Addition and subtraction of matrices are linear operations in which actions are performed on the corresponding elements. This means that an element of the first row and first column of one matrix is ​​added to a similar element of the second matrix.

Addition formula

Let two matrices $A$ and $B$ of the same size $m \times n$ be given. The sum of these matrices is called matrix $C$ , the elements of which are calculated by the formula:

$$C_{ij} = A_{ij} + B_{ij}$$

Where:

  • $i$ — line number ( $1, 2, \dots, m$ );
  • $j$ - column number ( $1, 2, \dots, n$ ).

Subtraction formula

The difference between the matrices $A$ and $B$ is determined in a similar way, but instead of the sum, the difference of the corresponding elements is found:

$$C_{ij} = A_{ij} — B_{ij}$$

Multiply by number (Scalar)

If the resulting matrix $C$ needs to be multiplied by the number $k$ , then each element of the matrix is ​​multiplied by this number:

$$k \cdot C = \begin{pmatrix} k \cdot c_{11} & k \cdot c_{12} \\ k \cdot c_{21} & k \cdot c_{22} \end{pmatrix}$$

Operation Properties

Understanding the properties of matrix operations helps with checking manual calculations and solving complex linear algebra equations.

Calculation example

Consider the addition of two matrices of size $2 \times 2$ :

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & 5 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ 0 & 6 \end{pmatrix}$$

Solution:

$$C = A + B = \begin{pmatrix} 2+3 & 4+(-2) \\ 1+0 & 5+6 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ 1 & 11 \end{pmatrix}$$

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to add a 2x2 and a 3x3 matrix?

No. The operations of addition and subtraction are defined only for matrices of strictly the same dimension. The number of rows and columns of the $A$ matrix must match the number of rows and columns of the $B$ matrix.

How to subtract a matrix from a number?

A matrix cannot be subtracted from a regular number. A number (scalar) can only be subtracted from a matrix if we think of it as subtracting a scalar matrix (where the number is on the main diagonal), but in classical linear algebra such an operation is not directly defined.

Does the calculator support complex numbers?

This version works with real numbers (integers and fractions).
