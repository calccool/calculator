LED resistor calculator: Online calculation of resistance and power

A light emitting diode (LED) is a semiconductor device that is controlled by current rather than voltage. If you connect it directly to a power source, the current will increase like an avalanche and the crystal will instantly burn out. To avoid this, you must use current limiting resistor.

Our online calculator helps you instantly select the desired resistor value, taking into account not only the resistance, but also the power dissipation, which is critical for the reliability of the circuit.

How to calculate a resistor for an LED?

The calculation is based on Ohm's fundamental law. The resistor should absorb the “excess” voltage, leaving the LED exactly as much as it needs for its operation.

Basic formula for calculating resistance:

$$R = \frac{V_s — V_f}{I}$$

Where:

  • $R$ - resistor resistance (Ohm / $\Omega$ );
  • $V_s$ — power supply voltage (Source Voltage, V);
  • $V_f$ - forward voltage drop across the LED (Forward Voltage, V);
  • $I$ - LED operating current (Amps). Please note: Amps are substituted into the formula, so 20 mA must be written as 0.02 A.

Resistor power calculation

A common mistake for beginners is selecting the correct resistance (Ohm), but ignoring the power (Watt). If a large current is passed through a weak resistor (for example, 0.25 W), it will overheat and turn black.

Power Formula:

$$P = I^2 \cdot R$$

We recommend using a resistor with a power reserve of 1.5–2 times more than calculated. Our calculator will automatically suggest a safe value (0.25 W, 0.5 W, 1 W, etc.).

Connection diagrams

Our tool supports calculation for three scenarios:

  1. Single LED: A classic circuit where one resistor protects one diode. The most reliable option.
  2. Serial connection (Series):
    • Plus: High energy efficiency. The current in the entire circuit is the same, the voltages on the diodes are summed ( $V_{total} = V_f \cdot N$ ).
    • Minus: Requires a high voltage source. If one diode burns out, the entire chain will go out.
  3. Parallel connection:
    • Nuance: The calculator calculates the total resistor for a group of parallel diodes. However, in professional circuit design, it is recommended to install a separate resistor for each diode (or each series chain) to avoid the effect of “thermal runaway”, when, due to the variation in parameters, one diode takes on more current and burns out first.
