Volume calculator for a septic tank made of concrete rings: calculation according to SNiP for the number of people

Correct calculation of the volume of a local wastewater treatment plant (WTP) is not just a matter of convenience, but a requirement of sanitary standards. An error in calculations leads either to frequent calls for a sewer truck, or to soil contamination and fines from supervisory authorities. Our calculator uses algorithms based on the state standard SNiP 2.04.03-85, which guarantees the correct operation of your autonomous sewage system.

Basic calculation formula according to SNiP

According to building codes, the working volume of a septic tank must accommodate three times the daily influx of wastewater from everyone living in the house. This time is necessary for heavy fractions to settle to the bottom and for organic compounds to go through the stage of anaerobic decomposition.

Mathematically, the calculation looks like this:

$$V = \frac{n \cdot Q \cdot T \cdot k}{1000}$$

Where:

  • $n$ - number of permanent residents;
  • $Q$ - average rate of water consumption per person per day (liters);
  • $T$ - estimated period of settling of wastewater (according to SNiP - 3 days);
  • $k$ - safety factor (recommended 1.2 or +20% in case of guests arriving or salvo discharge).

  • 1 camera: If the daily wastewater flow does not exceed 1 $m^3$ (for about 1-3 people).

Advantages of using concrete rings

Septic tanks made from reinforced concrete rings remain the most popular solution for private homes in Ukraine due to a number of factors:

  • Durability: The service life of high-quality concrete exceeds 50 years.
  • Heaving resistance: Unlike plastic containers, heavy concrete rings do not “float” when the groundwater level is high.
  • Ease of maintenance: Smooth walls and standard dimensions make cleaning and inspection easy.
  • Affordable price: The cost of materials and installation is significantly lower than that of ready-made biological treatment stations.

Tips for using a septic tank

In order for the system to operate without odor and blockages, adhere to the following rules:

  1. Bioactivators: Add special bacteria once a month to speed up the processing of sediment.
  2. Sealing: Be sure to waterproof the seams between the rings and at the bottom junction.
  3. Ventilation: Do not forget about the vent pipe to remove the resulting gases.
  4. Pumping: Remove sludge at least once every 1-2 years.
