Social tax deduction calculator: treatment, training, sports

Social tax deduction is an opportunity to return part of the income tax (personal income tax) you paid to the budget. The state allows you to reimburse expenses for socially significant services: medicine, education, sports and even life insurance.

Our online calculator automatically takes into account changes in legislation that came into force in 2024 and helps you accurately determine the amount that the tax office will transfer to your account.

Types of expenses subject to deduction

Social deductions include the following categories of costs:

  1. Treatment: purchase of medicines, clinic services, tests, dentistry and voluntary health insurance.
  2. Education: own education (any form), education of children, brothers and sisters (full-time only).
  3. Sport: subscriptions to fitness clubs, sports sections for yourself and children (the organization must be in the register of the Ministry of Sports).
  4. Charity: donations to officially registered organizations.
  5. Insurance and pensions: contributions under voluntary life insurance contracts and pension contributions.

Limits and restrictions

Effective January 1, 2024, the expense limits for calculating the deduction have been significantly increased. It is important to distinguish between the payment period for services, since the limit applies based on the year the payment was made.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The refund amount is 13% of the actual expenses incurred, but it cannot exceed two key indicators:

  • Limit established by law.
  • The amount of personal income tax that you actually paid for the year (usually 13% of your official annual salary).

The calculation formula is as follows:

$$S_{refund} = \min(E_{eligible} \times 0.13, T_{paid})$$

Where:

  • $S_{refund}$ - total amount to be refunded.
  • $E_{eligible}$ - the amount of expenses within the limit (for regular expenses - up to 150,000 $).
  • $T_{paid}$ is the amount of tax you paid for the year.

Important nuances when using a calculator

  • Expensive treatment (Code 02): If the code “02” is indicated in the certificate of payment for medical services, you have the right to return 13% of the entire amount spent. The limit of 150,000 $ does not apply to such operations.
  • Statute of limitations: You can submit a 3-NDFL declaration to receive a social deduction within three years from the date of payment for services. In 2026, you can get your money back for 2023, 2024 and 2025.
  • Children's education: The deduction is provided for children under 24 years of age if they are full-time students (including kindergartens, schools and paid courses with a license).
  • Residue intolerance: Unlike the property deduction (when purchasing an apartment), the balance of the social deduction is not carried over to the next year. If you haven't used it completely, the "underused" part is burned.

How to get a deduction through the tax office

  1. Collect documents: checks, contracts and, most importantly, certificates of payment for services for the tax authorities (a simplified form of certificates is now in effect).
  2. Fill out the declaration: through the taxpayer’s Personal Account on the Federal Tax Service website or submit an application to the employer.
  3. Wait for verification: The desk audit takes up to 3 months, after which the money will arrive in your bank account within a month.
