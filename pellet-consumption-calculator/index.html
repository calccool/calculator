Pellet consumption calculator for home heating: calculation by area and price

Heating using wood pellets (pellets) is an environmentally friendly and automated heating method, which is often cheaper than electricity and liquefied gas. However, to plan your budget, it is important to understand in advance how much fuel you will need for the winter.

Our online calculator allows you to perform a professional calculation of fuel consumption based on the thermal parameters of your building and equipment characteristics.

Basic parameters for calculation

To obtain the most accurate result, several factors must be taken into account:

  • Room area: The total area of ​​all heated rooms.
  • Heat loss level: Depends on the quality of insulation of the walls, roof and type of double-glazed windows.
  • Boiler efficiency: Modern pellet boilers are efficient 85–95%.
  • Fuel energy efficiency: The calorific value of quality wood pellets is approx. 4.8 kWh/kg.
  • Availability of hot water supply: Additional energy consumption for heating hot water for domestic needs.

Calculation methods and formulas

The calculator is based on classic heat engineering formulas. The calculation is divided into several stages.

1. Determination of maximum thermal power ( $P_{max}$ ):

$$P_{max} = \frac{S \cdot q}{1000}$$

Where:

  • $S$ - house area ( $м^2$ );
  • $q$ - specific heat loss ( $Вт/м^2$ ).

2. Calculation of daily energy consumption ( $E_{day}$ ):

It is assumed that the average load on the boiler per season is approximately 50% from maximum power:

$$E_{day} = \frac{P_{max}}{2} \cdot 24$$

3. Calculation of fuel mass ( $M$ ):

To calculate the required mass of pellets, the useful energy released during combustion is taken into account, taking into account the efficiency:

$$M = \frac{E_{day} + E_{gvs}}{C \cdot \eta}$$

Where:

  • $E_{gvs}$ - DHW energy (approx. 1.5 kWh per person);
  • $C$ - specific heat of combustion of pellets;
  • $\eta$ - boiler efficiency (in decimal form).

How to reduce pellet consumption: expert advice

If the calculated values ​​seem high to you, consider the following optimization methods:

  1. Pellet quality: Use standard granules EN plus A1. Cheap pellets with high ash content (agropellet) have a lower calorific value and require more frequent cleaning of the boiler.
  2. Burner maintenance: Carbon deposits on the heat exchanger with a thickness of only 1 mm reduce the boiler efficiency by 5–7%. Regular cleaning is required.
  3. Installation of heat accumulator: Allows the boiler to operate in optimal mode with maximum efficiency, accumulating excess heat.
  4. Weather automatic: Reduces coolant temperature during warming periods, preventing excessive fuel consumption.
