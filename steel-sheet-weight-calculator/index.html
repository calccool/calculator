Sheet metal weight calculator: Hot rolled and cold rolled sheet (Online calculation)

When planning the purchase of rolled metal, calculating logistics or designing metal structures, it is critical to know the exact mass of the material. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the weight of a batch of rolled sheets, based on the geometric dimensions and physical properties of the alloy, eliminating the need for manual calculations using complex GOST tables.

The tool works in two directions:

  1. Direct calculation: Determination of the total mass of the batch based on the size and number of sheets.
  2. Reverse calculation: Determine the number of sheets and area to be covered based on the known total tonnage.

Formula for calculating sheet mass

The calculations are based on the fundamental physical law of the dependence of mass on the volume and density of matter. For sheet metal having the shape of a rectangular parallelepiped, the formula is as follows:

$$m = \rho \times V$$

Where $V$ (volume) is calculated as the product of linear dimensions:

$$V = L \times B \times S$$

The final formula for calculating the mass of one sheet ( $m_{sheet}$ ):

$$m_{sheet} = \rho \times L \times B \times S$$

Decoding variables:

  • $m_{sheet}$ - weight of one sheet (kg);
  • $\rho$ - material density (kg/m³);
  • $L$ - sheet length (m);
  • $B$ - sheet width (m);
  • $S$ —rolled thickness (m).

Important: When calculating manually, mistakes are often made in converting units of measurement. Thickness is usually indicated in millimeters and density in kg/m³. Our calculator automatically converts all values ​​(mm → m) to get the correct result.

Features of hot-rolled and cold-rolled steel

When choosing sheet parameters, it is important to consider the method of its production, as this affects the range of thicknesses and rolling accuracy.

  1. Hot rolled sheet (H/C):
    • Produced by rolling a hot billet.
    • GOST 19903-2015 (formerly 19903-74).
    • Thickness range: usually from 0.4 mm to 160 mm.
    • The surface may have scale, less accuracy in thickness.
  2. Cold rolled sheet (C/C):
    • It is obtained by additional processing of hot-rolled sheet under pressure.
    • GOST 19904-90.
    • Thickness range: usually from 0.35 mm to 5.0 mm.
    • High surface quality, high dimensional accuracy.

Algorithm for calculating the cost of a batch

To create an estimate, the following logic is used, implemented in the calculator:

$$Cost_{total} = m_{total} \times Price_{kg}$$

Where:

  • $Cost_{total}$ - final cost of the batch;
  • $m_{total}$ - total weight of all sheets;
  • $Price_{kg}$ - current price for 1 kg of metal.

The use of automated calculations allows you to avoid mistakes when purchasing material “with a reserve” or, conversely, to prevent a shortage of metal due to incorrect conversion of area to weight.
