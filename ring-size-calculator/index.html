Ring Size Calculator: Diameter, Girth and International Systems Converter

Choosing a piece of jewelry requires perfect precision, since the difference between adjacent sizes is a fraction of a millimeter. Our interactive tool allows you to instantly convert the parameters you know into any world system of measures. The calculator takes into account standards Russia and CIS, European standard ISO, as well as scales USA, Canada and Asia.

How to use the calculator

To get accurate results, choose one of three data entry modes:

  • By diameter: the main standard for the Russian Federation and CIS countries. This is the internal distance between the walls of the ring.
  • By girth: European standard (EU/ISO) corresponding to the circumference of the finger.
  • US Size: American system based on an inch grid converted to metric format.

After entering the value, the system will automatically visualize the ring projection and display a comparison table for all regions.

Mathematical basis and calculation formulas

The logic of the calculator is based on geometric constants and official formulas for the transition between jewelry standards. All calculations are based on finding the base diameter ( $d$ ).

Basic formulas

  1. Relationship between diameter and European size (girth): European ISO 8653 size is directly equal to the inner circumference of the ring: $$L = \pi \cdot d$$ Where $L$ is girth in mm, $d$ is diameter in mm, $\pi \approx 3.14159$ .
  2. Translation from the American system (US): The American scale has a linear dependence on the diameter: $$d = 11.63 + (Size_{US} \cdot 0.8128)$$
  3. Japanese/Chinese size calculation: The Asian size grid is calculated using the following algorithm: $$Size_{JP} = (d — 12.67) \cdot 3$$

How to measure ring size at home

If you don't have a sample ring on hand, use the Control Thread method. This is the most accurate way to determine European size (girth).

Step by step instructions

  1. Preparation: Use some non-elastic string, a thin strip of paper, or a measuring tape.
  2. Measurement: Wrap your finger at its widest point (usually the second knuckle). The ring should pass through the bone with little resistance.
  3. Fixation: Mark the point where the edges of the thread intersect with a marker or pen.
  4. Result: Unfold the thread and measure the distance between the marks with a ruler. Enter the resulting number in millimeters in the “Finger circumference” field in our calculator.

Jewelry Expert Advice

  • Consider the time of day: Fingers are prone to slight swelling in the evening. The ideal time to take measurements is midday.
  • Temperature: In the cold, blood vessels narrow, in the heat they expand. Do not measure your finger immediately after visiting the pool or active workout.
  • Tire width: If you are planning to buy a wide ring (from 6 mm and above), it is recommended to choose a size 0.25 or 0.5 larger than the calculated one.
  • Working hand: The size of the fingers on the right and left hands may differ by 0.1–0.3 mm. Always measure the exact finger on which you plan to wear the jewelry.
