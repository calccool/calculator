Rules for calculating deadlines under Articles 113 and 114 of the Arbitration Procedure Code of the Russian Federation

According to the law, procedural deadlines are determined by an exact calendar date, an indication of an event that must inevitably occur, or a period of time.

Beginning of the term

In accordance with Part 4 of Art. 113 of the Arbitration Procedure Code of the Russian Federation, the course of the procedural period, calculated in years, months or days, begins on the next day after the calendar date or occurrence of the event that determines its beginning, the day of the event is not taken into account.

Accounting for weekends and holidays

The main feature of the arbitration process is that time periods calculated in days are counted only in working days, unless otherwise provided by law.

  • Working days: All days are taken into account except official Saturdays, Sundays and non-working holidays.
  • Deadline postponement: If the last day of the period falls on a non-working day, then the end of the period is considered to be the first working day following it, Part 4 of Art. 114 Arbitration Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the final deadline date, the calculator uses an algorithm that takes into account the production calendar of the Russian Federation. The calculation formula for deadlines in working days is as follows:

$$D_{end} = D_{start} + 1 + (n + p)$$

Where:

  • $D_{end}$ — final deadline date;
  • $D_{start}$ - date of occurrence of the event, for example, the date of the decision;
  • $n$ - number of days of the procedural period;
  • $p$ - the number of weekends and holidays that fell during this period.

If the calculation is carried out in months, the system checks for the presence of the corresponding date in the ending month. If there is no corresponding date in the month in which the period ends, the period expires on the last day of this month, Part 2 of Art. 114 Arbitration Procedure Code of the Russian Federation.
