A quadratic equation is an algebraic equation of the second degree, the general form of which is described by the formula:

$$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$$

Where:

  • $a$ — the first (highest) coefficient, which should not be equal to zero ( $a \neq 0$ );
  • $b$ — second coefficient (at $x$ );
  • $c$ - free member.

Solution algorithm through discriminant

The classic solution method is based on calculating the discriminant. This is an auxiliary quantity that allows you to determine the number of roots of the equation and their values.

Discriminant formula ( $D$ ):

$$D = b^2 — 4ac$$

Vieta's theorem: a quick check

For the given quadratic equations (where the coefficient is $a = 1$ ), Vieta's theorem is often used. It allows you to find the roots orally or check the correctness of the solution obtained through the discriminant. Our calculator automatically displays these values ​​for your convenience.

According to the theorem, the sum and product of the roots are related to the coefficients as follows:

  1. Sum of roots: $$x_1 + x_2 = -\frac{b}{a}$$
  2. Product of roots: $$x_1 \cdot x_2 = \frac{c}{a}$$

This is a powerful self-assessment tool. If the sum and product of the roots you found do not correspond to the coefficients, then there was an error in the calculations.

Geometric meaning and graph

The graph of the quadratic function $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ is a parabola. Our tool generates a schematic graph so you can visually evaluate the function's behavior.

Key elements of the schedule:

  • Parabola branches: If $a > 0$ , the branches are directed upward. If $a < 0$ - down.
  • X-axis intersection points: These are the roots of the equation ( $x_1, x_2$ ).
  • Vertex of the parabola: Extremum point of the function.

Parabola vertex coordinates

Often in problems it is required to find not only the roots, but also the coordinates of the vertex ( $x_0; y_0$ ). The calculator calculates them using the following formulas:

$$x_0 = -\frac{b}{2a}$$

$$y_0 = \frac{4ac — b^2}{4a}$$
