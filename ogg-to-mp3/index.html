The Mathematics of Compression and File Size Estimation

When converting to Constant Bit Rate (CBR) MP3 format, the size of the resulting file is strictly deterministic. To calculate the occupied space on a disk or flash drive, use the following formula:

$$V \approx \frac{R \times t}{8192}$$

Where:

  • $V$ is the predicted size of the finished MP3 file in Megabytes (MB).
  • $R$ - user-selected bitrate of the audio stream (in kbit/s, for example, 192 or 320).
  • $t$ - total duration of the audio track in seconds.
  • $8192$ is a physical constant for converting kilobits to Megabytes (taking into account that 1 byte = 8 bits).

For example, background game music with a duration of 180 seconds (3 minutes), converted with a standard bitrate of 192 kbps, will take up exactly 4.2 MB. During processing, the converter algorithm automatically converts frequencies from the Float32 format (OGG original) to the Int16 format, normalizes the peaks and carefully packs the data into MP3 frames, preserving the original stereo panorama.
