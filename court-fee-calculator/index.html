State duty calculator for courts of general jurisdiction (new rates)

From September 8, 2024, radically updated state duty rates began to apply in Russia when applying to courts of general jurisdiction (COJ) and magistrates. The reform has significantly increased the cost of litigation, especially in property disputes. Our calculator automates complex calculations, allowing you to instantly get the exact amount due.

The main changes affected the following aspects:

  • Minimum size: Now the duty for the minimum claim price starts from 4,000 rubles (previously 400 rubles).
  • Maximum limit: The duty ceiling has increased to 900,000 rubles.
  • Progressive scale: 10 steps of calculation have been introduced depending on the cost of the claim.
  • Non-property claims: The cost of filing an application for individuals has increased 10 times - up to 3,000 rubles.

How to calculate the state duty for property disputes

To calculate the amount of the fee for claims subject to assessment, it is necessary to determine the “Claim Value” - this is the total value of all material claims. The total amount consists of a fixed base rate and a percentage of the amount exceeding the lower threshold of the current range.

Mathematical calculation formula

The general algorithm for calculating the final state duty ( $G$ ) can be represented as a mathematical model:

$$G = F + (CP — L) \cdot P$$

Where:

  • $G$ - final amount of state duty;
  • $F$ - fixed amount for the corresponding range;
  • $CP$ — Claim Price;
  • $L$ - lower threshold of the range (Limit);
  • $P$ - interest rate.

State duty on non-property claims

If your claim is not of a material nature (for example, divorce, protection of honor and dignity, establishment of facts of legal significance), the amount of the fee is fixed.

Rates for individuals and legal entities

  1. Divorce: 5,000 rubles.
  2. Non-property claims:
    • For individuals: 3,000 rubles.
    • For organizations: 20,000 rubles.
  3. Filing an application for special proceedings: 3,000 rubles.
  4. Issuance of copies of documents from the case: Now there is no state duty.

Who is exempt from paying state duty?

The legislation provides for a number of benefits (Article 333.36 of the Tax Code of the Russian Federation). The following are fully or partially exempt from payment:

  • Plaintiffs in claims for recovery of wages and other labor disputes.
  • Plaintiffs in claims for alimony.
  • Disabled people of groups I and II (with a claim price of up to 1,000,000 rubles).
  • Veterans of the Great Patriotic War and military operations in defense of their rights established by the law on veterans.
  • Consumers in claims related to violation of their rights (for a claim amount of up to 1,000,000 rubles).
