Calorie calculator for homemade rolls and sushi: calculating BJU online

Cooking Japanese food at home is a great way to control the quality of your food. However, classic calorie tables are often not suitable, since serving sizes and amounts of toppings vary greatly at home. Our calculator allows you to get accurate data based on the specific weight of each ingredient.

The calculation algorithm is based on summing up the energy value of all components. The basic formula the system uses is:

$$K_{total} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{W_i \cdot K_{100i}}{100}$$

Where:

  • $W_i$ - weight of a specific ingredient in grams;
  • $K_{100i}$ - calorie content of this ingredient per 100 grams;
  • $K_{total}$ is the total calorie content of the entire portion.

To determine the calorie content of one piece, the total $K_{total}$ value is divided by the number of sliced ​​pieces (usually 6 or 8 pieces).

Nutritional table for main ingredients

For the accuracy of calculations, we use average values ​​of BJU (proteins, fats and carbohydrates) of the most popular components.

Features of ingredient accounting

When using the calculator, it is important to consider the condition of the products, as this critically affects the final result:

  • Rice: Enter your weight already boiled and seasoned rice Dry cereals increase their weight by 2.5–3 times during cooking.
  • Fish: The net weight of the fillet without skin and bones is taken into account.
  • Toppings: Don't forget about sesame seeds and sauces. For example, Unagi sauce is rich in sugar, which sharply increases the carbohydrate content, and Spicy sauce based on mayonnaise increases the fat content.
  • Tempura: If you are preparing hot rolls, select the appropriate item in the toppings. Deep frying adds approx. 150-200 kcal per serving due to butter and breading.

Secrets of reducing the calorie content of sushi

If you are watching your figure, pay attention to the status indicator in our calculator. It automatically classifies your dish:

  1. Diet roll (less than 130 kcal/100g): Choose cucumber, avocado, tuna or shrimp. Use less rice and more vegetable filling.
  2. Balanced option: Classic Philadelphia with salmon and a moderate amount of cheese.
  3. Calorie bomb (more than 240 kcal/100g): Rolls with smoked eel, lots of sauce and deep-fried.

How to use the calculator

  • Indicate the mass of the finished rice and the number of nori sheets.
  • Select your main seafood item from the drop-down list and enter its weight.
  • Add vegetables or soft cheese.
  • Specify how many pieces you plan to cut the finished roll into (8 by default).
  • Click "Calculate" to see an interactive diagram of BZHU and total calorie content.

Our tool instantly visualizes the balance of nutrients, helping you understand what is more in your dish - healthy proteins from fish or fast carbohydrates from rice. This is an ideal companion for those who keep a food diary or follow an flexible diet (IIFYM).
