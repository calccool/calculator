The Sims 4 Genetics and Twin Chance Calculator

The genetic system in The Sims 4 is not just a random choice, but a complex algorithm based on a system of "weights" and probabilities. Unlike real life, where dominant and recessive genes play a key role, the game favors direct traits from the parents, leaving a small window for random mutations.

Our calculator allows players to look into the game's "code" and mathematically accurately predict what the next generation of your gaming dynasty will be like. We take into account both base parameters and hidden modifiers from various add-ons such as Spa Day or City Living.

Probability of multiple pregnancy

Under standard conditions without the use of modifiers, the chance of having more than one child is extremely small. The game uses a point system (weights) to determine the outcome of each pregnancy.

The basic event weights are distributed as follows:

  • One child: 89 points
  • Twins: 10 points
  • Triplets: 1 point

The general formula for calculating the probability ( $P$ ) for a specific event looks like this:

$$P = \frac{W_{event}}{W_{total}} \cdot 100\%$$

Where $W_{event}$ is the weight of the target outcome (for example, twins), and $W_{total}$ is the sum of all active modifiers.

To increase the chances of having twins, the game provides several mechanics. Each adds a fixed amount of points to the Twins and Triplets weights.

Inheritance of eye and hair color

In The Sims 4, the appearance inheritance system works on the 90/10 principle. If both parents have the same hair color, the child has a 90% chance of inheriting it. The remaining 10% is the chance for "genetic mutation", where the game chooses a random standard color from the available palette.

If the parents' colors are different, the probability is distributed equally:

  1. 45% — color of the first parent.
  2. 45% — the color of the second parent.
  3. 10% - random mutation.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate forecast, follow these steps:

  • Select the active mode: “Chance of multiple births” or “Genetics of offspring”.
  • In pregnancy mode, mark all the bonuses that your characters have (character traits, house properties).
  • In genetics mode, specify the phenotypes (colors) of both parents from the drop-down lists.
  • Click the button "Calculate" to see the odds visualized in interactive graphs and charts.
