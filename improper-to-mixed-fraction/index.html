Calculator: Converting an improper fraction to a mixed number

How to convert an improper fraction to a mixed fraction?

In mathematics, you often work with fractions where the numerator (top number) is greater than the denominator (bottom number). Such fractions are called wrong (eg $7/3$ ). Although they are convenient for calculations, it is common to write them in the answer as mixed number - that is, highlight the whole part and the fractional remainder.

Our online calculator automates this process: it not only finds the whole number, but also automatically reduces the fractional part to its simplest form.

Algorithm: How does it work?

To manually convert an improper fraction $\frac{A}{B}$ to a mixed number, you must perform division with a remainder.

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator. The result of the division (without the decimal point) becomes the whole part of the mixed number.
  2. Find the remainder. The remainder of the division will become the new numerator of the fraction.
  3. Keep the denominator. The denominator remains unchanged.

Conversion formula

Mathematically it looks like this:

$$\frac{Numerator}{Denominator} = Whole \frac{Remainder}{Denominator}$$

Where:

  • Whole is the result of integer division.
  • Remainder — remainder of division ( $Numerator \% Denominator$ ).

Calculation example

Let's say we need to convert a fraction $22/4$ into a mixed number.

  1. Divide 22 by 4. The closest number that is divisible by 4 is 20.
    • $20 \div 4 = 5$ (this is our whole part ).
  2. Finding the remainder:
    • $22 — 20 = 2$ (this is our new numerator ).
  3. We write down the result:
    • $5 \frac{2}{4}$.
  4. Important step: Reduction. We see that the fraction $2/4$ can be reduced by 2.
    • Final answer: $5 \frac{1}{2}$.
