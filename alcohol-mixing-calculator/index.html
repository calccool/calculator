Calculator for mixing liquids of different strengths (spirit meter)

Creating a drink of the ideal strength is not just mathematics, but chemistry. Many novice distillers make the mistake of believing that mixing 500 ml of alcohol and 500 ml of water will yield exactly 1 liter of liquid. In practice the volume will be less. This phenomenon is called contraction, and our calculator takes this physical feature into account to obtain the most accurate result.

How to use the mixing calculator

The “Gold Standard 2” interface allows you to perform calculations in two main modes, switching between tabs:

Mode 1: Mixing liquids of different strengths

Use this mode if you have two different alcohol-containing solutions (for example, the “head” fractions and the main distillate) and you need to know what the end result will be.

  • Enter the volume and strength of the first liquid.
  • Enter the volume and strength of the second liquid (for pure water, the strength is 0%).
  • Click "Calculate" to instantly see your final volume and alcohol percentage.

Mode 2: Dilution to desired strength

This is a classic scenario where you have a strong alcohol (eg 96%) and want a product at a certain strength (eg 40%).

  • Please indicate the current volume and strength of your product.
  • Set your target strength.
  • The system itself will calculate the exact amount of water that needs to be added.

Mathematical model and formulas

Simple calculations (without taking into account contraction) are based on the material balance equation. If we neglect volume compression, the dilution formula is as follows:

$$V_{water} = V_{spirit} \cdot \left( \frac{S_{initial}}{S_{target}} — 1 \right)$$

Where:

  • $V_{water}$ - required volume of water to add;
  • $V_{spirit}$ - initial volume of alcohol;
  • $S_{initial}$ - initial strength in percent;
  • $S_{target}$ - desired strength.

What is volume contraction

When mixed, water molecules occupy the free spaces between alcohol molecules due to the formation of hydrogen bonds. As a result, the final volume of the mixture is always less than the sum of the original components. Maximum compression occurs when the strength reaches about 50–54%. Our calculator uses algorithms that approximate Fertman tables, which allows you to avoid “misses” when preparing the product.

Tips for perfect results

To keep your drink clear and soft, follow the rules of the “gold standard” of distillation:

  1. Pour alcohol into water: When adding water to alcohol, a sharp reaction may occur with the release of heat, which sometimes leads to cloudiness (opalescence) of the solution.
  2. Temperature: All alcohol meter measurements should be made at liquid temperature 20°C. If the liquid is warmer or colder, the readings from the meter will be incorrect.
  3. Water quality: Use only soft, bottled or spring water. High salt content in tap water is the main cause of cloudy homemade products.
