Dishwasher salt consumption calculator based on water hardness

The efficiency of a dishwasher (DMM) directly depends on the settings of the water softening system. The central element of this system is an ion exchanger filled with a resin that attracts calcium and magnesium ions. To regenerate this resin, a special regeneration salt is required.

Using our calculator allows you to avoid two extremes: excess consumption (white residue on dishes) and lack of salt (quick failure of the heating element due to scale).

Instructions for using the calculator

To get an accurate forecast, follow these steps:

  • Enter hardness value: Enter the value obtained from the water utility protocol or using a home TDS meter.
  • Select units of measurement: Our tool supports German degrees (°dH), Russian degrees of hardness (mg-eq/l), French degrees (°fH) and ppm.
  • Please indicate the frequency of washing: Enter the number of cycles your machine performs per week.
  • Package weight: Enter the weight of the pack of salt you usually buy to calculate how long it will last.

Depending on the concentration of salts, water is usually divided into several categories. Each category requires its own mode of operation of the ion exchanger.

Formulas for calculating salt intake

The mathematical model of the calculator is based on the base consumption of the reagent for regenerating the ion exchange resin. The basic formula for calculating the mass of salt per wash cycle ( $M_{cycle}$ ) is as follows:

$$M_{cycle} = \max(0, \min(60, (dH — 6) \times 3))$$

Where:

  • $dH$ — water hardness in German degrees.
  • $6$ — the basic threshold at which the ion exchanger begins to actively use salt.
  • $3$ — consumption coefficient (grams for each degree of hardness above the threshold).

To calculate monthly consumption ( $M_{month}$ ) the formula is used:

$$M_{month} = M_{cycle} \times W \times 4.3$$

Where $W$ is the number of washes per week, and $4.3$ is the average number of weeks in a month.

Why is it important to set the salt supply correctly?

Even if you use 3-in-1 or All-in-One tablets, the built-in water softener in your car should still function if the water hardness exceeds 21°dH.

Key reasons to control flow:

  1. Heating element protection: Scale is the main cause of PMM breakdowns. A 1 mm layer of scale increases energy consumption by 10-15%.
  2. Washing quality: In soft water, detergents work more efficiently, without leaving smudges or streaks on the glass.
  3. Saving: Understanding the consumption forecast, you can buy salt in large packages, which is more profitable in terms of price per kilogram.

How to find out water hardness yourself?

If you do not have data from your water supplier, you can use the following methods:

  • Test strips: They often come complete with a new PMM. The result is shown in °dH.
  • TDS meter (salin meter): Measures the total number of dissolved particles (ppm). To convert to degrees of hardness, use our converter inside the calculator.
  • Soap test: In hard water, ordinary soap practically does not foam.
