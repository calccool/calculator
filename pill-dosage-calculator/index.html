Dosage calculator for tablets and medications: calculation by weight and purpose

Accurate drug dosing is a critical aspect of safe treatment. Often, medical prescriptions (for example, “500 mg of the active substance”) do not coincide with the dosage available in the home medicine cabinet (for example, 250 mg tablets). Our online pill dosage calculator eliminates the risk of error by helping you instantly convert grams and milligrams into exact pill quantities, including fractional parts (halves, quarters) calculations.

The tool is especially useful in pediatrics and veterinary medicine, where dosage is often tied to the patient's body weight, and for planning the purchase of long-term medications.

Basic calculation formulas

To understand how the calculator works, below are the basic pharmacological formulas used in international standards of medical mathematics.

1. Basic calculation of the number of tablets:

To determine a single dose, use a simple formula for the ratio of the required dose to the available dose:

$$\text{Tablet Count} = \frac{\text{Desired Dose (D)}}{\text{Strength on Hand (H)}}$$

  • Desired Dose (D): The dose prescribed by the doctor (for example, 500 mg).
  • Strength on Hand (H): Dosage of one tablet from the package (for example, 250 mg).

2. Calculation of dose per body weight (Pediatric):

When prescribing medications to children or animals, the dose is calculated individually:

$$\text{Target Dose} = \text{Patient Weight (kg)} \times \text{Dose Per kg}$$

Calculator functionality

Our tool takes into account many nuances that patients encounter:

  • Multi-currency units: Automatic conversion between grams (g), milligrams (mg) and micrograms (mcg). You can indicate the purpose in grams, and the availability in milligrams, the system itself will bring everything to a single denominator.
  • Calculation for the course: Enter the duration of treatment and frequency of dosing to see the total number of packages or blisters you need to purchase.
  • Crushing scheme: If the result is not a whole number, the calculator will suggest a convenient dosage regimen (for example, “1 whole + 1/4 tablet”).
  • Error protection: The system will warn if the calculated single dose exceeds reasonable limits (for example, more than 10 tablets at a time), which may indicate a data entry error.

Important Safety Note

This calculator is intended for reference purposes and to check mathematical calculations. It does not replace medical advice.

  1. Enteric coatings: Not all tablets can be divided. If the tablet is coated or is a capsule, crushing it may interfere with its effect.
  2. Rounding: When calculating dosages for potent substances (antibiotics, hormones, heart medications), always consult your pharmacist.
