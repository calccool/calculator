Theoretical basis and calculation formulas

The cutoff frequency ( $f_c$ ) is the critical point at which the output power of the signal drops to exactly half the input power. This corresponds to a voltage drop to the level 0.707 ( $1/\sqrt{2}$ ) and attenuation in -3 dB.

Calculation for RC filter

In an RC circuit, the time it takes for a capacitor to charge and discharge through a resistor determines the frequency characteristics. Basic formulas:

  1. Cutoff frequency: $$f_c = \frac{1}{2 \pi R C}$$
  2. Time constant ( $\tau$ ): $$\tau = R \times C$$
  3. Capacitance: $$X_c = \frac{1}{2 \pi f C}$$

Calculation for RL filter

In an RL circuit, the inertia of the inductor plays a key role, which prevents a rapid change in current.

  1. Cutoff frequency: $$f_c = \frac{R}{2 \pi L}$$
  2. Time constant ( $\tau$ ): $$\tau = \frac{L}{R}$$
  3. Inductive reactance: $$X_L = 2 \pi f L$$

Deep analysis of frequency response and phase response

Our tool does not just produce a number, but visualizes the amplitude-frequency and phase-frequency characteristics.

Transmission coefficient (amplitude)

The gain shows the ratio of the output voltage to the input voltage ( $V_{out} / V_{in}$ ).

  • At the cutoff frequency it is always equal to 0.707.
  • In the passband it tends to 1 (0 dB).
  • In the suppression zone, it tends to 0.

Phase shift

Filters inevitably change the phase of the signal. For first order filters the maximum shift is 90 degrees. Knowing phase shift is critical when designing audio and feedback systems to avoid self-excitation or unwanted wave interference.
