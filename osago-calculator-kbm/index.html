How does the OSAGO calculation formula work?

The final cost of the insurance premium ( $P$ ) is formed by multiplying the base rate by a number of adjustment factors. The mathematical calculation model is as follows:

$$P = TB \times KT \times KBM \times KVS \times KO \times KM \times KS$$

Where:

  • TB — Base rate. Insurance companies choose a value within the established corridor (from 1646 $ to 7535 $ for individuals).
  • KT — Territorial coefficient. Depends on the place of registration of the owner of the vehicle.
  • KBM — Bonus-malus coefficient. The main indicator of your accident-free operation.
  • KVS (KVS) — Coefficient of age and experience. The younger the driver, the higher the risk and cost.
  • KO (KO) — Limitation of the number of drivers. Multidrive (without restrictions) significantly increases the price.
  • KM (KM) — Engine power factor. Calculated based on horsepower (hp).
  • KS (KS) — Seasonality (period of use).

KBM coefficient: Your personal discount

The bonus-malus coefficient (BMR) is the only parameter that the driver can directly influence through driving style. Information about KBM is updated annually April 1 based on data from the RSA (Russian Union of Motor Insurers) database.

Main classes of KBM

  1. Class 3 (basic): Assigned to newcomers when they first take out a policy. The value is 1.17.
  2. Class 13 (maximum discount): Achieved after 10 years of accident-free driving. Meaning - 0.46 (54% discount).
  3. Class M (maximum risk): Assigned for frequent road accidents caused by the driver. Meaning - 3.92.

Accident-free driving can reduce insurance costs by more than half, while even one accident can lead to a sharp increase in the cost of the policy in the next period.

How to save on purchasing compulsory motor liability insurance in 2026

To minimize the cost of an insurance policy, experts recommend following the following strategies:

  • Base rate monitoring: Since the TB (base rate) is a flexible value, different insurance companies may offer different values ​​within the corridor. Use our calculator for comparison.
  • Careful driving: Keeping your BMR low is the most effective way to save money in the long run.
  • Driver list optimization: Try not to include young drivers with low experience in the policy unless there is an urgent need for this, since the calculation is always made based on the “worst” participant.
  • Selecting the period of use: If you use your car only in the summer (for example, for trips to the country), take out a policy for 6 or 7 months - this will reduce the coefficient of insurance coverage.
