Basic conditions of the program in 2026

For successful loan approval and correct calculation on the calculator, you must focus on the following parameters:

  • Interest rate: up to 2% per annum (fixed for the entire period).
  • Down payment: at least 20% of the cost of the property (maternity capital can be used).
  • Maximum period: up to 20 years (240 months).
  • Amount limits: * Standard - 6 000 000 $.
    • Elevated - 9 000 000 $ (for housing in single-industry towns or when purchasing a property with an area of ​​60 sq.m. or more).

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on the annuity payment formula. This is a method of repaying the loan in equal amounts over the entire period.

The monthly interest rate is calculated as:

$$i = \frac{r}{100 \cdot 12}$$

Where $r$ is the annual rate (in this case 2).

The monthly payment ( $A$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$A = P \cdot \frac{i(1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $P$ — loan amount (cost of housing minus down payment);
  • $i$ - monthly interest rate;
  • $n$ - total number of months (term in years $\cdot$ 12).

Tax deductions: how to get money back from the state

When purchasing a home through this program, you are entitled to two types of property deductions, which our calculator calculates automatically:

  1. Deduction for the purchase of real estate: 13% of the cost of the object (base limit - 2,000,000 $). Maximum return - 260 000 $.
  2. Interest deduction: 13% of the interest actually paid to the bank (base limit - 3,000,000 rubles). Maximum return - 390 000 $.

Thus, in total the family can return up to 650 000 $, which often covers a significant part of the overpayment on the loan at 2%.

Who can use the program?

The list of categories of citizens entitled to benefits in 2026 includes:

  • Young families (both spouses under 36 years old) or single-parent families with a child under 19 years old.
  • Participants in the Far Eastern Hectare and Arctic Hectare programs.
  • Teachers and medical workers with at least 5 years of experience in the Far Eastern Federal District or the Arctic.
  • Employees of the military-industrial complex (DIC).
  • Citizens who moved from the SVO zone or internally displaced persons.
