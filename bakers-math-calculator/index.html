Baker's percentage calculator: Professional calculation of bread and sourdough recipes

Baker's Math is a universal method of recording recipes in which the weight of flour is always taken as 100%, and the weight of all other ingredients is calculated as a fraction of this value. Unlike conventional gram-based cooking recipes, this system allows the baker to instantly scale production and change dough characteristics (such as moisture) without having to rewrite the entire recipe.

How Baker's Math works

The main rule of baking mathematics is that the sum of all percentages in a recipe will always be greater than 100. It seems counterintuitive, but this approach ensures perfect accuracy.

The basic formula for calculating any ingredient:

$$Weight_{ingredient} = \frac{Weight_{flour} \times Percentage_{ingredient}}{100}$$

Where:

  • Weight flour - net weight of flour in the recipe.
  • Percentage ingredient — percentage of additive (salt, water or starter).

How to use the calculator: two operating modes

Our tool supports two professional work scenarios, which can be switched in interface tabs:

  1. By weight of flour: You know how much flour you have and want to know how much water and salt to add to achieve the desired texture.
  2. By total test weight: You know what weight you need (for example, 2 loaves of 800 g), and the calculator itself will calculate how many grams of each ingredient you need to take.

Advanced Features: True Hydration and Sourdough

Conventional calculators often ignore the fact that the starter itself consists of flour and water. This leads to errors when working with highly hydrated dough. Our calculator is equipped with a function "Accounting for starter hydration".

True Hydration Formula

When you activate the professional mode, the algorithm recalculates the “fair” percentage of water by summing the moisture from the main recipe and the moisture contained inside the starter:

$$H_{true} = \frac{W_{water} + W_{starter\_water}}{W_{flour} + W_{starter\_flour}} \times 100\%$$

Why is this important?

If you use 20% liquid starter (100% moisture) at a base hydration of 70%, your actual (true) dough moisture will remain at 70%. However, if the starter is thick (50% humidity), true hydration will drop and the dough will become noticeably denser. The calculator automatically visualizes this data in a pie chart.

Tips for perfect results

  • Water temperature: For sourdough bread, try to use cool water to prevent the fermentation process from happening too quickly.
  • Salt: Standard salt content - 2%. It is not only responsible for the taste, but also strengthens the gluten (gluten window).
  • Scale accuracy: When using Baker's Math, it is recommended to use 0.1g scales, especially for salt and yeast, as small percentage deviations have a large impact on the result.
