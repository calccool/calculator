How does a down payment change the terms of a loan?

In annuity payment mechanics, the size of the loan body (the amount you borrow) is the basis for calculating interest. The relationship here is non-linear: reducing the loan principal by 20% can reduce the total overpayment by 30-40% by reducing the interest base in the first, most expensive years of debt servicing.

Annuity calculation formula

For those who want to understand the mathematics of the process, we present the annuity payment formula that is used in the banking system:

$$PMT = (S — D) \times \frac{r \times (1 + r)^n}{(1 + r)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • PMT — monthly annuity payment.
  • S — the full cost of property (real estate, cars).
  • D — the amount of the down payment (Down Payment).
  • r — monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100).
  • n — loan term in months.

Strategies for choosing an advance amount

When planning a mortgage or car loan, it is important to consider the thresholds that affect the bank's decision and interest rate.

Minimum contribution (10-15%)

  • Pros: Quick access to a deal, maintaining a financial cushion.
  • Cons: Mandatory credit insurance (often more expensive), increased interest rate, maximum overpayment.

Optimal contribution (20-30%)

The "golden mean" standard. Most banks offer base rates at this level of participation with their own funds. Risks for the lender are reduced, which often makes it possible to eliminate additional insurance.

High contribution (50% or more)

  • Pros: Minimum overpayment, possibility of registration using two documents (without proof of income).
  • Cons: Freezing a large amount of liquid funds in property.
