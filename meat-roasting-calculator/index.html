How to calculate cooking time in the oven

The main mistake novice cooks make is calculating time “by eye.” Professional cooking is based on a formula that relates weight (in kilograms) to specific heat treatment coefficients for each type of meat.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm performs calculations using the following formula:

$$T_{total} = (W \cdot t_{kg}) + t_{rest}$$

Where:

  • $T_{total}$ - total process time from placing in the oven to serving;
  • $W$ - wet weight of the product in kilograms;
  • $t_{kg}$ - standard baking time per 1 kg of weight (individually for each type);
  • $t_{rest}$ - resting time constant to stabilize meat juices.

Secrets of juicy meat: why it's important

The Role of Temperature Control

Using a food thermometer (probe) is the only way to guarantee results. For poultry (turkey, chicken) the critical point is 74°C. At this temperature, pathogenic bacteria die, but the fibers still retain cellular moisture. For beef medium rare is enough 60°C.

Mandatory "rest" of meat

Once removed from the oven, the temperature inside the piece continues to rise an additional 2 to 4 degrees during the first 10 minutes. This occurs due to the inertia of heat transfer.

  • Why can't you cut it right away? When exposed to heat, the proteins contract and push juice from the outer layers towards the center.
  • What does rest give? The fibers relax and the meat juice is evenly redistributed throughout the volume.

Recommendations for using the calculator

  1. Preparation: Remove the meat from the refrigerator 30 to 60 minutes before cooking to allow it to come to room temperature.
  2. Weight: Enter the net weight of the product without taking into account the weight of the pan or filling.
  3. Oven: Preheat the oven in advance. The calculator calculates the “net” time the product is in an already heated environment.
  4. Positioning: Place the baking tray on the middle level to ensure even distribution of convection currents.
