Why do you need to calculate the weight of corrugated sheets?

Corrugated sheets (profiled sheets) are one of the most popular materials for the construction of fences, roofing and load-bearing structures. Knowing the exact weight of a batch is necessary not only for ordering transport, but also for product quality control. Our corrugated sheet weight calculator allows you to instantly calculate the weight of an order, based on the physical parameters of the steel, and not on approximate tabular values.

The main reasons to make a calculation before purchasing:

  • Logistics: Understanding which transport to order (Gazelle up to 1.5 tons or manipulator up to 5 tons).
  • Load calculation: Designing a roof truss system requires taking into account the weight of the roof covering.
  • Thickness control (Important!): Unscrupulous sellers often pass off a sheet with a thickness of 0.4 mm as 0.5 mm. Knowing the theoretical weight of a high-quality sheet according to GOST, you can weigh the pack and identify deception.

How does the calculator work?

Unlike simple tables, our algorithm uses a roll-to-roll calculation method. Roll forming is the process of bending a flat sheet. The weight of the finished product is equal to the weight of the flat piece (strip).

Mass calculation formula:

$$Weight = L \times W_{coil} \times t \times \rho$$

Where:

  • Weight — final mass (kg);
  • L — sheet length (m);
  • W_{coil} — width of the original blank/roll (usually 1250 mm);
  • t — steel thickness (mm);
  • ρ (rho) — steel density ( $7.85 \ g/cm^3$ is used for galvanizing).

Features of corrugated sheet brands

  1. S (Wall): Brands C8, C10, C20, C21. Used for facades, fences and partitions. They have a low wave height and lower load-bearing capacity.
  2. NS (Load-bearing-wall): Brands NS35, NS44. Universal material with additional stiffening ribs. Suitable for roofing and high fences.
  3. N (Carrying): Brands H57, H60, H75, H114. Used for interfloor slabs and permanent formwork. Made from thick steel (0.7–1.2 mm).
