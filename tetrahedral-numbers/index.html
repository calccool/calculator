Tetrahedral Numbers Calculator: Online Calculation and Formulas

A tetrahedral number (or triangular pyramid number) is a type of figurative number that represents the number of balls arranged in a regular tetrahedron (triangular pyramid).

Imagine stacking oranges on a counter or cannonballs on the deck of a ship. You start with one ball on top, put three under it (in a triangle), six under them, and so on. The total number of objects in such a design will be tetrahedral number.

Our calculator allows you to instantly determine this number for any number of $n$ layers, and also shows the number of elements at the base of the pyramid.

Calculation formula

To find the $n$ -th tetrahedral number ( $Te_n$ ), it is not necessary to add the layers manually. There is a direct algebraic formula.

The tetrahedral number is equal to the sum of the first $n$ triangular numbers. The formula looks like this:

$$Te_n = \sum_{k=1}^n T_k = \frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}{6}$$

Where:

  • $Te_n$ is the desired tetrahedral number.
  • $n$ — serial number (number of layers in the pyramid).
  • $T_k$ — $k$ is the th triangular number.

Relation to binomial coefficients

Tetrahedral numbers can also be found in Pascal's triangle. They are located on the 4th diagonal (if we consider the first diagonal of ones). In combinatorics, this value corresponds to the number of combinations of $n+2$ by 3:

$$Te_n = C_{n+2}^3 = \binom{n+2}{3}$$

Properties and interesting facts

Tetrahedral numbers have a number of unique mathematical properties that are often used in number theory and geometric problems:

  1. Parity: The alternation of even and odd numbers in a sequence follows a strict pattern: odd - even - even - even.
  2. Sum of reciprocals: The sum of a series of reciprocal tetrahedral numbers tends to 3/2: $$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{Te_n} = \frac{3}{2}$$
  3. Square numbers: There are only three tetrahedral numbers that are also perfect squares:
    • $Te_1 = 1^2 = 1$
    • $Te_2 = 2^2 = 4$
    • $Te_{48} = 140^2 = 19600$
  4. Five-dimensional hypersphere: $Te_n$ determines the number of ways to distribute $n$ objects into 4 cells (partition problem).
