Coffee extraction and TDS calculator: online calculation of strength and EY

Preparing the perfect cup of coffee is not only an art, but also precise chemistry. To consistently get a tasty drink, professionals use two key parameters: TDS (total mineralization or strength) and EY (extraction level). Our online calculator allows you to instantly analyze your recipe and understand where to adjust the grind or water temperature to achieve the perfect balance.

How to use TDS calculator

The tool works in two modes, covering the needs both when analyzing a ready-made cup and when planning a new recipe.

Mode 1: Extraction Evaluation

Use this mode if you already have brewed coffee and a refractometer:

  • Coffee weight: Enter the mass of dry ground grain in grams.
  • Drink weight (Yield): indicate the mass of the finished coffee in the cup (not to be confused with the volume of water for pouring).
  • TDS indicator: enter the value obtained using the coffee refractometer.

Mode 2: Recipe calculation

This mode is useful when creating a brew profile from scratch:

  • Specify the desired coefficient ( Brew Ratio ) and targeted extraction.
  • The calculator will tell you what weight of the drink you need to get and what TDS the device should show if it fits perfectly into the recipe.

Basic formula for calculating extraction

The calculator is based on the classic extraction formula, recognized SCA (Specialty Coffee Association). It shows what percentage of soluble substances from the coffee bean has passed into water.

$$EY (\%) = \frac{\text{Вес напитка (г)} \times \text{TDS (\%)}}{\text{Вес сухого кофе (г)}}$$

Where:

  • EY (Extraction Yield): percentage of extracted substances.
  • TDS (Total Dissolved Solids): the concentration of these substances in water.

How to improve the taste of coffee using a calculator

If your result is far from the “Gold Standard”, use the following recommendations to adjust the recipe:

If extraction is too low (less than 18%)

  1. Reduce grind: smaller particles release substances faster.
  2. Increase the water temperature: this will speed up the process of dissolving substances.
  3. Increase contact time: for example, a slower infusion in a funnel.

If the extraction is too high (more than 22%)

  1. Coarse grind: The water will move through the coffee tablet faster, extracting less bitterness.
  2. Reduce water temperature: this will slow down the extraction of heavy fractions (bitterness).
  3. Reduce Yield: stop the spill a little earlier.
