BDO fail calculator: Accurate calculation of Black Desert enchant chance

Upgrading gear in Black Desert is a complex mathematical process where proper management of Failstacks directly affects your well-being in the game. This calculator is designed to allow players to instantly estimate the probability of success before pressing a button, minimizing unnecessary risks.

The tool supports three main operating modes:

  • Boss Equipment and Weapons: Calculation for levels II (DUO) to V (PEN).
  • Accessories (Jewelry): Odds rating from I (PRI) to V (PEN).
  • Black Star Weapons: Special algorithms for the most valuable items in the game.

How fail mechanics work in BDO

The item enhancement system is based on the accumulation of unsuccessful attempts. Each “fail” adds a certain percentage to the base probability of success. However, it is important to remember the existence of a “soft limit” - soft cap.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The program uses the official game odds to calculate the final value. The calculation is divided into two stages: before reaching the threshold of 70% and after it.

For standard calculation, the formula is used:

$$P = P_{base} + (FS \cdot G)$$

Where:

  • $P$ - final chance of success.
  • $P_{base}$ - base chance of the item (with 0 fails).
  • $FS$ - current number of failures (including Valks Protests).
  • $G$ - increase in chance for each failure.

If the calculated chance exceeds 70%, the attenuation coefficient (Softcap) comes into force:

$$P_{final} = 70 + (P — 70) \cdot 0.2$$

This means that after 70%, each subsequent failure gives only 20% of its initial effectiveness. This is why investing huge stacks of failures into attempts with a chance above 70-80% is not economically profitable.

Recommendations for using the calculator

To get the most accurate result and save your resources, follow these steps:

Choosing the right mode

Switch between tabs at the top of the interface. The chances for jewelry are significantly different from the chances for armor, since accessories are completely destroyed if unsuccessful.

Accounting for Valks' Protests

Use the button "+10 (Valks' Protest)" to quickly add extra points to your base stack. The calculator will instantly recalculate the visual chart.

Visual scale analysis

  • Low chance: Means that your stack of fails is too small for this purpose.
  • Optimal stack: You are in the zone of the most efficient use of resources.
  • Overcap (Softcap): The system signals that you are spending too valuable fails on an item that has already reached the limit of easy chance growth. In this case, it is better to save the stack for a more complex sharpening (for example, V Black Star).

Using a calculator allows you to avoid emotional decisions and approach sharpening as a mathematically based process.
