Online unit economics calculator: calculating CAC, LTV and margins

Unit economics is a method of determining the profitability of a business model by calculating the profitability of one unit of product or one customer (unit). In modern realities, this is the “foundation” for startups, e-commerce and service companies. If the economics at the level of one client are negative, then scaling the business will only lead to increased losses.

Our calculator allows you to instantly assess the viability of your model using key acquisition and retention metrics.

Key indicators in the calculator

For in-depth analysis we use four main parameters:

  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): The amount you spend to attract one new client.
  • AOV (Average Order Value): Average receipt per purchase.
  • LTV (Lifetime Value): The net profit that a client brings during the entire time they interact with your brand.
  • ROMI (Return on Marketing Investment): Return on marketing investment ratio, showing the effectiveness of the advertising budget.

Formulas for calculating unit economics

The tool makes calculations based on classical mathematical models adapted for the real sector and digital business.

Calculation of cost of acquisition (CAC)

If you don't know your CAC, the calculator will calculate it using the formula:

$$CAC = \frac{Marketing\_Spend}{New\_Clients}$$

Where $Marketing\_Spend$ is the total advertising costs, and $New\_Clients$ is the number of customers received.

Calculating customer lifetime value (LTV)

We calculate LTV taking into account the cost of goods or services (COGS):

$$LTV = (AOV — COGS) \times Purchase\_Frequency$$

This formula allows you to see real income minus direct expenses for fulfilling obligations.

Net profit and return on investment (ROMI)

The final marketing effectiveness is expressed by the formula:

$$ROMI = \frac{LTV — CAC}{CAC} \times 100\%$$
