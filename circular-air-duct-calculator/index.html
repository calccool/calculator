Round Duct Calculator: Area, Weight and Insulation Calculator

When designing and installing ventilation systems, the accuracy of calculations directly affects the project budget. Our round duct calculator is an engineering tool that allows you to determine the need for materials in seconds, avoid waste of metal and choose the right thermal insulation.

The tool is adapted for both spiral-wound and straight-seam air ducts, taking into account technological allowances for connections (flanges, nipples, folds).

What does the calculator calculate?

We have combined several engineering formulas in one interface. You get four key parameters:

  1. Metal development area ( $S_{met}$ ) - The clean surface area required to order steel or paint.
  2. Product weight ( $M$ ) — the mass of the air duct, which is critical for the selection of fasteners (studs, traverses, clamps) and logistics.
  3. Insulation area ( $S_{ins}$ ) — calculated by the outer diameter taking into account the thickness of the insulation (mineral wool, foam rubber).
  4. Live section ( $F$ ) — internal pipe lumen area required for aerodynamic calculation of flow velocity.

Calculation formulas

For those who are accustomed to double-checking data manually, we provide the mathematical basis on which our algorithm works.

1. Surface area (sweep):

$$S = \pi \cdot D \cdot L \cdot k$$

Where:

  • $\pi$ - Pi number (3.14159...);
  • $D$ - duct diameter (m);
  • $L$ —section length (m);
  • $k$ - safety factor (takes into account seams and cutting, usually 1.05–1.15).

2. Theoretical metal weight:

$$M = S \cdot t \cdot \rho$$

Where:

  • $t$ - steel thickness (mm);
  • $\rho$ - steel density (for galvanized steel, $7850\ кг/м^3$ is accepted).

How to use the calculator

  1. Geometry: Enter the diameter in millimeters (for example, 200 for D200) and the total length of the route. If you have several identical plots, indicate their number.
  2. Material: Select steel thickness. For most general exchange systems, 0.5 or 0.7 mm galvanization is used.
  3. Stock: The default value is 5% - this is the standard allowance for seam joints and trims. For complex shaped products, we recommend increasing this value to 10-15%.
  4. Insulation: If the air duct is transit or runs along the street, indicate the thickness of the insulation. The calculator will automatically recalculate the coverage area since the outer diameter of the insulated pipe will be larger ( $D_{ext} = D + 2 \cdot t_{ins}$ ).

Why is it important to calculate the “Living Section”?

Many calculators ignore this parameter, but it is key to aerodynamics. Knowing the open cross-section ( $F$ ) and air flow ( $L_{air}$ ), you can calculate the real flow rate ( $V$ ) in the channel:

$$V = \frac{L_{air}}{3600 \cdot F}$$

If the speed exceeds the permissible standards (for example, 4-6 m/s for offices), this will lead to increased noise. Use our calculator to check your system throughput.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Does the calculator take into account the weight of the flanges?

No, the calculation is carried out for a “bare” pipe. The weight of flanges, angles and bolts depends on the type of connection and is calculated separately in the estimate of fittings.

What steel density does the algorithm use?

We use average density carbon and galvanized steel - $7850\ кг/м^3$ . The weight of zinc coating (class 275 or other) is within the error of engineering calculations.

How to calculate paint area?

The paint area is equal to the scan area ( $S_{met}$ ) that the calculator produces. If painting is required on both sides (inside and outside), multiply the resulting value by 2.
