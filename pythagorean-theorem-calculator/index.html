Pythagorean Theorem Calculator: Calculation of Hypotenuse, Legs and Angles

Online right triangle calculator

Pythagorean theorem is the foundation of geometry that connects the sides of a right triangle. Our free online tool allows you to instantly solve any problem related to this theorem, be it a school assignment or a roof design.

How does the calculator work?

The tool operates in two modes:

  1. Finding the hypotenuse: If two legs are known ( $a$ and $b$ ).
  2. Search for leg: If the hypotenuse ( $c$ ) and one of the legs are known.

In addition to the length of the sides, the calculator automatically calculates:

  • Square triangle.
  • Perimeter figures.
  • Acute angles ( $\alpha$ and $\beta$ ) in degrees.
  • Height, lowered to the hypotenuse.

Formulas and Theory

The basic formula of the Pythagorean theorem sounds like this: “In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the legs.”

$$c^2 = a^2 + b^2$$

Where:

  • c - hypotenuse (the longest side, opposite the right angle).
  • a And b - legs (sides forming a right angle).

Derived formulas:

  1. Find hypotenuse ( $c$ ): $$c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$$
  2. Find leg ( $a$ ): $$a = \sqrt{c^2 — b^2}$$
  3. Find leg ( $b$ ): $$b = \sqrt{c^2 — a^2}$$

Fun fact: Pythagorean triplets

There are sets of three integers that perfectly satisfy the Pythagorean theorem. They are called "Pythagorean triplets". The most famous of them is (3, 4, 5).

Check it out for yourself: enter legs 3 and 4, and the calculator will show that the hypotenuse is 5.

Other popular triplets:

  • 5, 12, 13
  • 8, 15, 17
  • 7, 24, 25
