Calculator of procedural deadlines for the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation 2026: Calculation in working days

Correct calculation of procedural deadlines is a critical skill for a lawyer and the key to protecting the rights of a participant in a trial. Missing a deadline without a valid reason leads to the return of the complaint or the inability to complete a procedural action. Our calculator is designed to meet all requirements Civil Procedure Code of the Russian Federation and current judicial practice.

Key rules of calculation according to the law

According to the norms of the Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, periods are calculated in years, months or days. The main difficulty lies in determining the type of days (working or calendar) and the starting point.

  1. Beginning of the period: According to Art. 107 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation, the period begins the next day after the calendar date or the occurrence of the event that determines its beginning.
  2. Accounting for weekends: If the deadline is set in days, it is calculated in working days, unless otherwise provided by law.
  3. Deadline on weekend: If the last day of the period falls on a non-working day, the end of the period is considered to be the next working day ( Art. 108 Code of Civil Procedure of the Russian Federation ).
  4. Deadline time: The procedural action can be completed before 23:59 last day of the deadline.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm uses the following logic to determine the final date ( $D_{end}$ ), where $D_{start}$ is the event date and $T$ is the number of days of the deadline:

$$D_{term\_start} = D_{start} + 1 \text{ день}$$

For weekdays the calculation looks like this:

$$D_{end} = D_{term\_start} + (T — 1) + W_{count}$$

Where $W_{count}$ is the number of weekends and holidays falling within the interval.

How to use the calculator

The tool interface is divided into logical blocks to eliminate errors:

Mode selection

You can switch between calculation in working days (default for most terms since 2019) and calendar days (for periods calculated in months).

Data entry

  • Event date: Indicate the day on which the event occurred (for example, the date of announcement of the operative part of the decision or the date of receipt of a copy of the document).
  • Term: Enter the number of days. Pay attention to the prompts (5, 15, 30 days) - these are the most common periods in civil law.

Results and visualization

After pressing the button "Calculate", the system will generate a detailed report:

  • Visual graph: A pie chart will show the ratio of working days and time “gained” due to weekends.
  • Timeline: An interactive timeline will clearly demonstrate the date of the event, the start date of the period and the final deadline.
  • Exact time: Reminder about the possibility of submitting documents until the end of the day (23:59).

Important: When sending documents through the post office, the deadline is not considered missed if the documents were submitted to the postal service organization before twenty-four hours of the last day of the deadline. Save the postal inventory and receipt!
