3D printing cost calculator: accurate calculation of materials, wear and profit

To run a successful additive manufacturing business, or even to accurately control a home hobby, simply knowing the price of a spool of plastic is not enough. The real cost is made up of many hidden factors: from the watts consumed to the gradual wear and tear of nozzles and belts.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate costs in two modes:

  1. Net cost: to understand direct costs.
  2. Business calculation: to form a market price taking into account the risks of defects and the desired profit.

Main factors influencing cost

To get the most accurate result possible, we analyze four key components:

  • Material (Filament): Plastic consumption includes not only the weight of the part itself, but also the support, skirt (skirt) and border (brim).
  • Power consumption: Modern printers consume from 150 to 500 W, especially when printing with high-temperature plastics (ABS, Nylon) with active bed heating.
  • Equipment depreciation: Every hour of operation brings the printer closer to service or replacement. Accounting for depreciation allows you to create a fund in advance for the purchase of new equipment.
  • Risks and markup: In commercial printing, it is important to set aside a percentage for possible defects (delamination, nozzle clogging) and the actual profit of the enterprise.

Mathematical calculation model

To ensure high accuracy, the calculator uses the following formulas:

1. Material cost:

$$C_{mat} = \frac{W_{part}}{W_{spool}} \times P_{spool}$$

Where:

  • $W_{part}$ —part weight (g);
  • $W_{spool}$ — coil weight (g);
  • $P_{spool}$ - coil price.

2. Electricity costs:

$$C_{elec} = \frac{P_{watt}}{1000} \times T_{hour} \times R_{rate}$$

Where:

  • $P_{watt}$ — printer power (W);
  • $T_{hour}$ — printing time (h);
  • $R_{rate}$ - tariff per kWh.

3. Depreciation:

$$C_{wear} = \frac{P_{printer}}{L_{hours}} \times T_{hour}$$

Where $P_{printer}$ is the price of the printer, and $L_{hours}$ is its estimated resource.

Why is it important to consider depreciation?

Many beginners make the mistake of not factoring wear and tear into the price. However, a 3D printer is a complex mechanical unit. Over time, belts stretch, bearings wear out, and the table surface degrades.

Resource recommendations:

  • For Chinese budget models, we recommend installing the resource 3000 hours.
  • For reliable workhorses with rail guides - 5000-7000 hours.
  • For photopolymer printers (SLA/DLP), the service life of the LCD matrix is ​​taken into account separately, which is significantly lower (about 2000 hours).

How to optimize your printing costs

  1. Support setup: The use of Tree Supports significantly reduces filament consumption and printing time.
  2. Infill: Going from 20% to 10% infill using the Gyroid pattern maintains strength but saves up to 15% of material.
  3. Tariffs: If you're printing on a farm, using a nightly electricity rate can reduce operating costs by 5-7%.
