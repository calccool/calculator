Kt/V Calculator: Calculation of Dose and Adequacy of Hemodialysis (Daugirdas Formula)

Dialysis adequacy assessment: Kt/V and URR

The effectiveness of the hemodialysis procedure directly correlates with the well-being and survival of patients with end-stage renal disease (ESRD). To quantify how well the blood is cleared of toxins, a dimensionless index is used throughout the world Kt/V.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly calculate this indicator using the “gold standard” of nephrology - the Daugirdas equation, as well as determine the urea reduction ratio (URR).

What does the Kt/V formula mean?

This is a model of urea kinetics where:

  • K — dialyzer clearance (volume of blood completely cleared of urea per unit of time, ml/min).
  • t — time of the dialysis procedure (in minutes).
  • V - the volume of distribution of urea in the patient’s body (approximately equal to the total volume of water in the body).

Calculation formulas used

The calculator supports two calculation methods adopted in the KDOQI (National Kidney Foundation) recommendations.

1. Formula of Daugirdas II (Daugirdas Second Generation)

This is the most accurate method for estimating single dialysis (spKt/V) as it takes into account intraprocedural urea generation and fluid volume changes (ultrafiltration).

$$spKt/V = -\ln(R — 0.008 \times t) + (4 — 3.5 \times R) \times \frac{UF}{W}$$

Where:

  • R — the ratio of post-dialysis urea to pre-dialysis ( $C_{post} / C_{pre}$ ).
  • t — duration of the session in hours.
  • UF — volume of ultrafiltration (removed liquid) in liters.
  • W — patient’s weight after dialysis (“dry weight”) in kg.

2. Urea reduction ratio (URR)

A simpler, but less accurate indicator that reflects the percentage decrease in blood urea nitrogen (BUN) concentration.

$$URR = \left( 1 — \frac{C_{post}}{C_{pre}} \right) \times 100\%$$

Interpretation of results (KDOQI Standards)

The adequacy of dialysis is assessed by whether the target level of clearance is achieved. Insufficient dialysis dose leads to accumulation of toxins and worsening prognosis.

Recommendations for blood collection

To obtain an accurate result, it is critical to correctly draw blood:

  1. Test “Before” (Pre-dialysis): It is taken immediately before the start of the procedure, without mixing with saline solution.
  2. Test “After” (Post-dialysis): Should be collected after completion of dialysis, but following a “slow flow” or “pump stop” protocol to avoid recirculation (mixing of purified blood with unpurified blood in the fistula), which may falsely inflate the Kt/V result.
