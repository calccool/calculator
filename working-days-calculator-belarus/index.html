Working days calculator in Belarus: calculating the norm of hours and days according to the calendar of the Republic of Belarus

This tool is designed specifically for accountants, HR specialists and employees working in the Republic of Belarus. It allows you to instantly calculate the number of working days, weekends and holidays for any selected period, and also automatically calculates the standard working time in hours.

Main features of the calculator

The tool takes into account the specifics of the production calendar of Belarus, approved by the Ministry of Labor and Social Protection. Unlike simple counters, our application analyzes:

  • Public holidays: Automatic recording of all non-working holidays in the Republic of Belarus.
  • Operating modes: Possibility to choose between a 5-day (with two days off) and a 6-day (with one day off) work week.
  • Individual norm: Flexible adjustment of the number of hours per week Standard for correct calculation of the rate.
  • Quick presets: Switch to the current month or the entire calendar year in one click.

How is standard working time calculated?

The calculator algorithm uses the classic formula for calculating the working time fund, adopted in personnel records management in Belarus.

$$T_{norm} = D_{work} \times \left( \frac{H_{week}}{D_{week}} \right)$$

Where:

  • $T_{norm}$ - final standard working time for the period (in hours);
  • $D_{work}$ — number of calculated working days;
  • $H_{week}$ - set number of hours per week (for example, 40, 36 or 35);
  • $D_{week}$ - type of work week (5 or 6 days).

Why is it important to use an accurate calculator?

  1. Vacation planning: You can accurately calculate the end date of your vacation, taking into account that holidays are not included in the number of calendar days of vacation.
  2. Payroll calculation: Determining the exact number of days worked in case of an incomplete month (for example, when hired or fired).
  3. Charting: Building a time balance for shift schedules, taking into account the norm established by law.
  4. Accounting check: Independent control of accrued payments and bonuses.

Features of accounting for Radunitsa

Please note that Radunitsa (All Souls' Day) is a moving holiday and falls on Tuesday. In Belarus, this day is annually declared a non-working day at the state level. Our calculator takes into account fixed dates, however, when calculating periods that include the spring months, it is recommended to check the data with the current resolution of the Council of Ministers on the transfer of working days.
