Triangle median length calculator (Online calculation)

The median is one of the key elements of triangle geometry. This is a segment connecting the vertex of a triangle to the middle of the opposite side. Our interactive tool allows you to find the length of all three medians ( $m_a, m_b, m_c$ ) with high accuracy using two different input methods: through Cartesian vertex coordinates or through known side lengths.

In addition to the lengths of the medians, the calculator automatically calculates the perimeter, area of ​​the figure and the coordinates of the centroid (the intersection point of the medians).

Formulas for calculating median

Depending on the source data, different mathematical approaches are used. Our algorithm is based on Apollonius' theorem and the coordinate method.

Calculation using side lengths

If the lengths of the three sides of a triangle ( $a, b, c$ ) are known, the length of the median drawn to side $a$ ( $m_a$ ) is calculated using the following formula:

$$m_a = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2b^2 + 2c^2 — a^2}$$

Similarly for the other two sides:

  • $$m_b = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2a^2 + 2c^2 — b^2}$$
  • $$m_c = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2a^2 + 2b^2 — c^2}$$

Where:

  • $a, b, c$ - lengths of the sides of the triangle;
  • $m_a, m_b, m_c$ - medians omitted to the corresponding sides.

Calculation via vertex coordinates

If the triangle is given by the coordinates of the vertices $A(x_1, y_1)$ , $B(x_2, y_2)$ and $C(x_3, y_3)$ , the problem is solved in two stages:

  1. The coordinates of the middle of the segment (opposite side) are found. For example, for side $BC$ the point $M$ will have coordinates: $$x_M = \frac{x_2 + x_3}{2}; \quad y_M = \frac{y_2 + y_3}{2}$$
  2. The distance between the vertex $A$ and the found point $M$ is calculated using the segment length formula: $$m_a = \sqrt{(x_M — x_1)^2 + (y_M — y_1)^2}$$

Properties of the median of a triangle

Understanding the properties of the median is important for solving complex geometric problems. Here are the key features:

  • Intersection at one point: All three medians of a triangle always intersect at one point, which is called centroid (or center of gravity).
  • Division 2 to 1: The centroid divides each median in a ratio of 2:1, counting from the vertex. That is, the part of the segment from the top to the center of gravity is twice as long as from the center to the side.
  • Equal size: The median splits a triangle into two triangles of equal area (but not necessarily equal in shape).
  • Six triangles: Three medians divide the original triangle into six small triangles whose areas are equal.

How to use an online calculator

To get instant results, follow these simple steps:

  1. Select the calculation mode in the drop-down list: "By vertex coordinates" or "According to the lengths of the sides".
  2. Enter values. For coordinates, these are $(x, y)$ pairs for points A, B and C. For sides, these are numeric length values.
  3. Click the button "Calculate medians".
  4. The tool will build an interactive drawing, showing the lengths of all three medians, the coordinates of the centroid, as well as the perimeter and area of ​​the triangle.

Use this tool to review geometry homework, engineering calculations, or structural design assignments where finding the center of gravity of a shape is important.
