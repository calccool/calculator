Weight loss forecast calculator: Calculate the exact date of achieving your goal

Losing weight is pure mathematics, based on the law of conservation of energy. To get rid of fat reserves, you need to create a calorie deficit: spend more energy than you consume through food.

Our calculator uses the classic fat tissue energy density formula. It is generally accepted that for combustion 1 kg fat it is necessary to create a total deficit of approximately 7700 kcal.

Basic calculation formula

The time to reach the goal ( $T$ ) is calculated using the following formula:

$$T = \frac{W \times 7700}{D}$$

Where:

  • T - number of days.
  • W - desired weight loss (in kg).
  • D - daily calorie deficit (kcal).
  • 7700 — energy value of 1 kg of human adipose tissue.

Calculator operating modes

The tool works in two directions to solve any of your problems:

  1. “I know my deficit” — You are already on a diet (for example, you are undernourished 500 kcal per day) and want to know When the scales will show the treasured figure. The calculator will show the exact number of days and the calendar date of finish.
  2. "I know the deadline" — You have a deadline (wedding, vacation, summer). You enter the number of days and the calculator says, for how long kcal you need to cut back on your diet to make it on time.

What is considered a safe pace?

When planning to lose weight, it is important not only to achieve the goal, but also to maintain health. The World Health Organization (WHO) and nutritionists recommend the following rates:

  • Safely: 0.5 – 1.0 kg per week. This allows you to burn fat, not muscle, and minimizes the risk of the “yo-yo effect” (reverse weight gain).
  • Slowly: Less than 0.4 kg per week. The results will be permanent, but will require patience.
  • Dangerous: More than 1.2 kg per week. This pace is fraught with a slowdown in metabolism, loss of muscle mass, problems with the gallbladder and hormonal imbalance.

Important: In the first days of the diet, weight may come off faster due to the loss of excess fluid (edema). Real fat burning begins later, so focus on an average pace over 2-3 weeks.

Factors affecting speed

In addition to the calorie deficit, the actual date may be affected by:

  1. Dream: Lack of sleep increases cortisol levels, which retain water.
  2. BJU: Adequate amounts of protein help maintain muscle and burn more calories to digest food.
  3. Adaptation: Over time, metabolism may slow slightly as a smaller body requires less energy. It is recommended to recalculate your calorie intake every 5 kg lost.
