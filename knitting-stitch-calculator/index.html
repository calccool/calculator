Calculator of loops and rows for knitting using a control pattern

Accurate calculation of loops is the foundation, without which even the most expensive yarn will not turn into an ideal product. Our calculator is designed for craftsmen who value their time and want to avoid the tedious process of unraveling the canvas due to the wrong size.

The tool allows you to instantly convert the centimeters of a pattern into specific numbers for casting on the edge, taking into account the individual handwriting of the knitter and the characteristics of the selected knitting needles.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The calculator is based on classical proportions, supplemented by coefficients of freedom of fit. The following formulas are used for calculation:

$$N_{stitches} = \left( \frac{S_{swatch}}{W_{swatch}} \right) \times W_{target} \times \left( 1 + \frac{E}{100} \right)$$

$$N_{rows} = \left( \frac{R_{swatch}}{H_{swatch}} \right) \times H_{target}$$

Where:

  • $N$ : Total number (stitches or rows);
  • $S_{swatch}$ / $R_{swatch}$ : Number of stitches/rows in the pattern;
  • $W_{swatch}$ / $H_{swatch}$ : Sample width and height in cm;
  • $W_{target}$ / $H_{target}$ : Desired dimensions of the finished part;
  • $E$ : Percentage increase for ease of fit (Ease).

How to properly prepare a control sample

To ensure that the data in the calculator is as accurate as possible, follow the expert recommendations for sample preparation:

  • Knit a sample measuring at least 15x15 cm. Edge loops often have a different density, so measurements should be taken in the central part (10x10 cm square).
  • Use the same knitting needles and basic pattern, with which you plan to knit the entire product.
  • Carry out WTO (Wet heat treatment). Wash the sample in the same water and with the same detergent as the finished product. Dry it horizontally.
  • Measure in a calm state. Do not stretch or compress the canvas when applying the ruler.

Calculation of yarn consumption

Our calculator includes a unique function for estimating the weight of yarn needed. Knowing the area of ​​your control sample and its weight in grams, the algorithm compares this data with the area of ​​the future part. This allows you to predict with high accuracy whether your existing supplies will be enough or whether you should buy another skein from the same batch (lot).
