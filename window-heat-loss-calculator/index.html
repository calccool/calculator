Heat loss calculator through a plastic window: online calculation

Windows are the “energy holes” of any building. Even a modern five-chamber profile with a two-chamber double-glazed window conducts heat several times more intensely than an insulated wall. In private homes, up to 25–40% of all thermal energy can be lost through translucent structures.

Using an online calculator allows you to:

  • Justify window replacement: Understand how many years it will take for premium energy-saving packages to pay for themselves.
  • Calculate heating power: Select radiators taking into account real leaks.
  • Find weak points: Compare which is more profitable - a wider profile or glass with Low-E coating.

How the calculator works: physics and formulas

Our tool is based on the classical formula of stationary heat transfer. The calculation takes into account the total area of ​​the structure, the temperature difference (delta) and the heat transfer resistance coefficient of specific materials.

Basic calculation formula

To calculate the heat loss power ( $Q$ in Watts), the following equation is used:

$$Q = S \cdot \frac{1}{R} \cdot (T_{in} — T_{out})$$

Where:

  • $S$ — total window area ( $м^2$ );
  • $R$ — heat transfer resistance ( $м^2 \cdot ^\circ C / Вт$ );
  • $T_{in}$ — target indoor temperature;
  • $T_{out}$ — minimum temperature outside.

Since a window consists of a double-glazed window and a frame (profile), the calculator calculates their areas separately, since their $R$ indicators differ significantly. Typically the frame takes up 20% to 30% of the opening.

How to interpret the results

After clicking the “Calculate” button, the calculator displays three key parameters:

Heat loss power (W)

This is the amount of energy that the window loses every second. For example, if the result is 150 W, this means that your window works like an incandescent light bulb that is always on, only it warms the street.

Energy loss per month (kWh)

The total amount of energy that will need to be compensated by the heating system. This indicator is used for financial analysis.

Financial losses

If you specify the tariff for electricity or gas, the calculator will convert physical quantities into money. This is a “cold tax” that you pay monthly due to imperfect window designs.

Ways to reduce heat loss

If your energy saving class according to the test results is lower than “B”, we recommend paying attention to the following solutions:

  1. Installation of heat reflective film: The most budget-friendly way to increase the $R$ of an existing window by 15–20%.
  2. Argon filling: The inert gas inside the chambers reduces convection, which is especially effective when paired with i-glass.
  3. Replacing seals: Often real losses are higher than calculated due to blowing (infiltration), which is not taken into account by standard thermal conductivity formulas.
  4. Warm spacers: Replacing aluminum frames inside a double-glazed window with plastic ones removes the “cold bridge” along the edge of the glass.
