Data architecture: why CSV is more efficient for machine processing

At first glance, both formats solve similar problems, but their internal logic is radically different.

The gain in data processing speed when moving from massive Excel workbooks to raw text values ​​can be expressed through a mathematical assessment of the complexity of the parsing algorithm:

$$E_{parse} = \lim_{N \to \infty} \left( \frac{O(N \cdot M \cdot D_{xml})}{O(N \cdot M)} \right)$$

Where $N$ is the number of rows, $M$ is the number of columns, and $D_{xml}$ is the average depth of tags in the XML manifest of the source file. The flat structure of CSV completely eliminates resource-intensive tree traversal, making loading into any system almost instantaneous.

Advanced features of our converter

We designed this tool to meet stringent data preparation requirements. Instead of blind conversion, you get full control over the process.

  • UTF-8 encoding support with BOM: Automatically adding a Byte Order Mark ensures that Cyrillic characters, special characters and emoji will be read correctly the next time the file is opened in Microsoft Excel.
  • Point range export: You can specify a specific slice of cells (for example,A1:F500), programmatically cutting off unnecessary headings, explanatory notes or system calculations in the margins.
  • Bulk generation from all sheets: When you activate the appropriate option, the script will automatically parse each tab of the source book into a separate CSV document and give you a ready-made structured ZIP archive.
  • Adaptive separator: Choose a comma, semicolon, or tab character (TSV) depending on the requirements of the target platform receiving the data.
