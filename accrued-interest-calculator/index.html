Online bond cash flow calculator: calculation of accumulated coupon income

Accumulated coupon income (ACI) - this is the amount of interest on the bond that has accrued to the owner of the paper from the moment of the last coupon payment until the current day. Unlike stocks, where only those who own the paper on the cut-off date receive dividends, the bond market is fairer: income is distributed daily.

When purchasing a bond on the exchange, you will see the “clean price” (as a percentage of the face value), but the “dirty price” will be debited from your account. This difference is the ACI that you pay to the seller as compensation for the time he held the bond in the current period.

Formulas for calculating accrued income and transaction value

To understand the mechanics of the calculator, we present the basic financial formulas that are used in the algorithm.

Calculation of accumulated income

The amount of accrued income is determined in proportion to the number of days that have passed since the beginning of the period:

$$NKD = K \cdot \frac{t}{T}$$

Where:

  • $K$ — coupon value in monetary terms ($);
  • $t$ — the number of days that have passed since the date of the last payment;
  • $T$ — total duration of the current coupon period (in days).

Calculation of the final price (Dirty Price)

The actual amount that the broker will write off upon purchase:

$$P_{dirty} = (N \cdot \frac{P_{clean}}{100}) + NKD$$

Where $N$ is the par value of the bond, and $P_{clean}$ — market price as a percentage.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Do I need to pay tax on NKD?

Yes, NKD is part of the coupon income. However, when you purchase, you pay the INC to the seller, and this amount is counted as your expense, which reduces your taxable base in the future when you receive the full coupon.

Why can the NKD be equal to zero?

This happens in two cases: either today is the coupon payment day, or you are considering a “zero-coupon” (discount) bond, the income on which is generated only by the difference between the purchase price and the par value.

How can I find out the number of days since my last payment?

This information can be found on your broker's bond card under the Events or Coupons section. The date of the last payment is indicated there, from which you need to count the number of calendar days until today.
