Blood pressure calculator by age and weight: Online calculation of norms (WHO)

Blood pressure (BP) is one of the main markers of cardiovascular health. However, the concept of “norm” is not static. The famous “120 to 80” is the standard for astronauts, but for a real person the norm varies depending on age, gender and body weight.

Our engineering pressure calculator uses a combined assessment method: it compares your indicators with WHO international standards and calculates the individual physiological norm using the Volynsky formula, taking into account body composition.

How the calculation works: Formulas and Methods

Many simple services use only average tables. We went further and introduced the calculation of the adaptive norm. With age, blood vessels lose elasticity, and excess weight creates additional stress on the heart, which increases “working” pressure.

To calculate individual indicators, the modified Volynsky formula is used:

$$SYS_{norm} = 109 + (0.5 \times Age) + (0.1 \times Weight)$$

$$DIA_{norm} = 63 + (0.1 \times Age) + (0.15 \times Weight)$$

Where:

  • $SYS_{norm}$ - systolic (upper) pressure.
  • $DIA_{norm}$ - diastolic (lower) pressure.
  • $Age$ - age (years).
  • $Weight$ - weight (kg).

This formula allows you to understand whether your blood pressure is a pathology or a physiological feature of the body.

Decoding the indicators: What do the numbers mean?

When measuring, the tonometer gives two values. It is important to understand the physical meaning of each of them.

  1. Systolic (Upper): Shows the pressure in the arteries at the moment of heart contraction (systole). It directly depends on the strength of cardiac output.
  2. Diastolic (Lower): Reflects vascular tone at the moment of heart relaxation. Shows how elastic your blood vessels are.
  3. Pulse pressure (PD): The difference between the upper and lower value. A critically important parameter that our calculator calculates automatically.

What is mean arterial pressure (MAP)?

The calculator also displays the parameter MAP (Mean Arterial Pressure). This is the average pressure over a complete cardiac cycle. It determines how well the blood supplies vital organs (brain, kidneys).

MAP calculation formula:

$$MAP = DP + \frac{1}{3}(SP — DP)$$

Where $DP$ is diastolic pressure and $SP$ is systolic pressure. A normal MAP value in the range 70–100 mmHg If the reading is below 60, organs may experience oxygen starvation (ischemia).

How to measure blood pressure correctly

To ensure accurate data you enter into the calculator, follow the "Gold Standard" measurement:

  • 5 minute rule: Sit quietly before measuring.
  • Position: When sitting, your back rests on a chair, your legs are not crossed. The hand should lie on the table at heart level.
  • Exceptions: 30 minutes before the test, do not smoke, drink coffee or energy drinks.
  • Cuff: Must correspond to the volume of the hand. A cuff that is too narrow will overestimate the reading.

The influence of age on blood pressure norms

Users often search for “age norm.” It is important to understand that over the years the permissible threshold shifts slightly upward due to the natural aging processes of blood vessels.

Average guidelines by age:

  • 16–20 years: 100/70 — 120/80
  • 20–40 years: 120/70 — 130/80
  • 40–60 years: Up to 135/85
  • Over 60 years old: Up to 140/90 (without diabetes)

Note: This calculator is an informational tool. If there are regular deviations from the norm, be sure to consult a cardiologist for a diagnosis and treatment.
