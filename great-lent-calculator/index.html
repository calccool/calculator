Lent calculator: online calculation of start and end dates

Lent is a “transitional” period, since its calendar boundaries directly depend on the date of the celebration of the Holy Resurrection of Christ (Easter). Since Easter is calculated according to the lunisolar calendar, every year believers need to re-determine on what date the period of abstinence begins.

Our calculator automates this complex process using Gaussian and classical Easter algorithms. You just need to enter the year you are interested in to instantly receive:

  • The start date of the fast (including the name of the day);
  • End date (Holy Saturday or Maundy Thursday);
  • The exact number of days remaining until the beginning or end of the period;
  • A visual progress bar for the current cycle.

Differences in Calculations: Orthodoxy and Catholicism

The calculator's software algorithm takes into account the differences between the Eastern (Orthodox) and Western (Catholic) traditions. The main discrepancy lies in the calendars used: Julian and Gregorian.

Features of Orthodox fasting

In the Orthodox tradition, Lent (Lent and Holy Week) lasts exactly 48 days. It always starts at Clean Monday (7 weeks before Easter).

Features of Catholic fasting

For Catholics, Lent begins at Ash Wednesday. Its duration is calculated differently: 40 days (not counting Sundays) plus the days of Holy Week, which gives a total of 46 calendar days from Ash Wednesday to Easter.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on an algorithm for determining the date of Easter. For the Orthodox Easter (according to the old style with subsequent translation into the new one), the simplified Gauss formula is used.

Date calculation formula (fragment)

To determine the date of Easter in a specific year $Y$ , the following variables are calculated:

$$a = Y \pmod{19}$$

$$b = Y \pmod{4}$$

$$c = Y \pmod{7}$$

$$d = (19a + 15) \pmod{30}$$

$$e = (2b + 4c + 6d + 6) \pmod{7}$$

The final date according to the old style is defined as $22 + d + e$ March or $d + e — 9$ April. The calculator automatically adds 13 days to adapt to the modern civil calendar.

Key Stages of Lent

Fasting is not just a food restriction, but a deep spiritual cycle consisting of several important stages that are displayed on the interactive timeline of our service:

  1. Holy Pentecost: the first 40 days in memory of the forty-day fast of Jesus Christ in the desert.
  2. Lazarus Saturday and the Entry of the Lord into Jerusalem: transitional days before Holy Week.
  3. Holy Week: the last week before Easter, dedicated to memories of the last days of the Savior’s earthly life.
