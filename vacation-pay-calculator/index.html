Vacation pay calculator: online calculation of average earnings according to the Labor Code of the Russian Federation

Planning a vacation is not only about choosing a hotel, but also about accurately calculating your budget. The amount of vacation pay often differs from the usual salary, since the calculations are based on average daily earnings over the past 12 months. Our tool automates this process, freeing you from the routine of formulas and calendars.

How the calculation algorithm works

To obtain accurate results, the calculator uses two modes corresponding to different work scenarios:

  • Standard Mode (Full Year): Applies if you have worked the last 12 months in full, without going on sick leave or other vacations. This is the easiest way to quickly estimate the amount.
  • Exact mode (Taking into account excluded periods): If during the year you were sick or took days off at your own expense, these periods and payments for them (benefits) are excluded from the calculation. In this mode, you enter your actual total income and the number of days actually worked.

Mathematical model and formulas

According to Article 139 of the Labor Code of the Russian Federation, the calculation is based on determining the cost of one working day. We use the constant $29.3$ - this is the average number of calendar days in a month.

The basic formula for calculating vacation pay is as follows:

$$Sum = \frac{Z}{12 \cdot 29.3} \cdot D$$

Where:

  • Sum — total amount of accrued vacation pay (Gross).
  • Z — total income for the billing period (12 months).
  • D — the number of calendar days of planned vacation.
  • 29.3 — average monthly number of calendar days.

If the billing period is not fully worked out, the formula for average daily earnings ( $SDZ$ ) becomes more complicated:

$$SDZ = \frac{Z}{(FM \cdot 29.3) + \sum (\frac{29.3}{KD} \cdot OD)}$$

Here FM - number of complete months, KD - calendar days in an incomplete month, and OD - actual days worked this month.

Important nuances of taxation

Holiday pay is income on which the government taxes. Our calculator automatically takes into account Personal income tax at a rate of 13% (for residents of the Russian Federation).

Why you should use our calculator

  1. Relevance: The algorithm is configured for the production calendar.
  2. Transparency: You see not only the final figure, but also the amount of tax withheld, as well as the average daily earnings.
  3. Convenience: Interactive visual indicators (diagrams) clearly show the ratio of “net” money and tax deductions.
  4. Speed: Quick buttons for selecting standard periods (7, 14, 28 days) allow you to make calculations in a couple of clicks.

Remember that vacation pay must be paid no later than three full calendar days before the start of the vacation. If this deadline falls on a weekend, the money must arrive on the previous business day.
