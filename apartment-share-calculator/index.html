Calculator for calculating the share in an apartment in square meters

Dividing home ownership often turns into a complex mathematical and legal problem. Our tool allows you to instantly convert an abstract fraction from a title deed (such as 1/3 or 2/5) into real square footage and monetary value.

This is necessary when preparing sales and purchase agreements, donations, allocating shares to children (for example, after using maternity capital) or during legal proceedings.

How to calculate the share in an apartment

The calculator works in two modes to match your documents as accurately as possible:

  1. By fraction: if the USRN extract indicates that you own, for example, 3/10 of the apartment.
  2. By number of owners: if the apartment is in common joint ownership and is divided equally between all participants.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the area per share, use the following formula:

$$S_{share} = S_{total} \times \frac{N}{D}$$

Where:

  • $S_{share}$ —required lobe area ( $m^2$ );
  • $S_{total}$ - total area of ​​the entire apartment;
  • $N$ - numerator of the fraction (your part);
  • $D$ is the denominator of the fraction (total number of parts).

If you specify a market value, the share price ( $P_{share}$ ) is calculated in the same way:

$$P_{share} = P_{total} \times \frac{N}{D}$$

Features of owning and selling shares

When using the calculator, it is important to remember several legal factors that directly affect the disposition of property:

  • Pre-emptive right to purchase: According to the law, before selling your share to an outsider, you are required to offer in writing to buy it back to the remaining owners. They have 30 days to make a decision.
  • Living vs Total area: The calculator allows you to calculate the share of living space. This is critical for determining the order of use of rooms (for example, whether the owner can claim a separate bedroom).
  • Market discount: Remember that the price of a share as a separate property is 15-40% lower than if you simply divided the cost of the entire apartment into parts. This is due to the difficulties of living together.
