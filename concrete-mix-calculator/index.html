Concrete composition calculator: Online calculation of proportions (in kg, liters and buckets)

Preparing high-quality concrete is not just about mixing components by eye. The strength of the foundation, the durability of the paths and the reliability of the floors depend on the correct proportions. Our online concrete calculator allows you to instantly calculate the exact amount of materials needed to obtain a mixture of the desired grade (strength class).

The tool takes into account not only the volume, but also the brand of cement used (M400 or M500), and also converts dry numbers into units understandable to the builder - buckets and bags.

Why do you need an accurate calculation of proportions?

A concrete mixture consists of four main components: cement (binder), sand (fine aggregate), crushed stone (coarse aggregate) and water. An imbalance leads to critical errors:

  • Disadvantage of cement: The concrete will be loose and will not gain grade strength.
  • Excess water: Leads to cracks when drying and reduced frost resistance.
  • Sand shortage: Voids form between the crushed stones, which increases the consumption of expensive cement.

Concrete grades and their application

In our calculator you can select the required brand of concrete. If you're not sure what to choose, use this cheat sheet:

  • M100 (V7.5): Preparatory work, concrete paving, laying curbs.
  • M150 (V12.5): Floor screeds, concreting light paths, foundations for fences.
  • M200 (B15): The most popular brand. Used for strip foundations, blind areas, stairs.
  • M250 (B20): Monolithic foundations, light-loaded floor slabs.
  • M300 (V22.5): Load-bearing walls, monolithic floors, foundations of heavy houses.
  • M350 (B25): Critical structures, columns, pool bowls, crossbars.

How does the calculation formula work?

The calculation is based on the absolute volume method. The density of the concrete mixture ( $\rho_{mix}$ ) depends on the density of the aggregate grains and the water-cement ratio ( $W/C$ ).

Basic formula for mass of material ( $M$ ):

$$M = V_{total} \times \rho_{mix} \times Ratio_{component}$$

Where:

  • $V_{total}$ - required volume of concrete (m³).
  • $\rho_{mix}$ - average density of concrete (usually taken ~2300-2400 kg/m³).
  • $Ratio$ is the mass fraction of the component in the total mixture.

Calculation in buckets (Volume method)

There are no laboratory scales on the construction site, so we added a function for converting kilograms into liters and 10-liter buckets. For this, bulk densities of materials are used:

  • Cement (loose): ~1300 kg/m³
  • Sand (dry): ~1550 kg/m³
  • Crushed stone (granite): ~1450 kg/m³

$$V_{liters} = \frac{M_{kg}}{\rho_{bulk}} \times 1000$$

Advice: When mixing concrete in a concrete mixer, first add some water, then crushed stone, cement and sand. Add the rest of the water gradually, controlling the thickness of the solution.
