1RM Calculator: Calculate One Rep Maximum (1RM) Online

What is 1RM and why count it?

One rep maximum (1RM or 1RM - One Rep Max) is the maximum weight an athlete can lift in one repetition using proper form. This indicator is the “gold standard” for assessing strength in powerlifting, bodybuilding and CrossFit.

Knowing your 1RM isn't just about showing off to your friends. This is the basis for building any professional training program. Most training plans (such as strength or hypertrophy cycles) are written as a percentage of your maximum.

Why is it better to use a calculator rather than a barbell?

Trying to find out your limit experimentally (“testing”) is a huge stress for the central nervous system and a high risk of injury, especially for beginners. Our 1RM calculator allows you to calculate this indicator mathematically, based on your usual working weight, which is safe and quite accurate.

Calculation formulas

Our algorithm uses four of the most authoritative formulas in the world of strength sports and averages them out for maximum accuracy.

1. Formula Epley

It is considered one of the most accurate for the number of repetitions from 3 to 10.

$$1RM = w \cdot (1 + \frac{r}{30})$$

2. Formula Brzycki (Brzycki)

A classic formula often used in fitness applications.

$$1RM = w \cdot \frac{36}{37 — r}$$

3. Formula Lander

A more conservative formula that gives slightly lower values, which is useful for “insurance” against overtraining.

$$1RM = \frac{100 \cdot w}{101.3 — 2.67123 \cdot r}$$

(Where $w$ is the weight of the projectile, $r$ is the number of repetitions)

How to use the calculator?

  1. Select an exercise (Bench Press, Squat, Deadlift or Standing Press).
  2. Enter the weight you can handle 2 to 10 pure reps.
    • Tip: Don't use a weight you can do more than 10-12 reps with, as the formulas lose accuracy at higher reps.
  3. Click "Calculate".

The tool will show you your theoretical maximum and break it down into percentages to help you create a program.
