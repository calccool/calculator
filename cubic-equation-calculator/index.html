Solving third degree equations online

A cubic equation is a third-order algebraic equation, the general form of which is written as:

$$ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d = 0$$

where $a \ne 0$ .

Unlike quadratic equations, which are easily solved using the school discriminant, cubic equations require much more complex calculations. Our free online calculator allows you to find the exact values ​​of all three roots ( $x_1, x_2, x_3$ ) in a fraction of a second using the classical algebraic method.

Calculator features

Our tool not only provides an answer, but also performs a complete analysis of the function:

  • Finding all roots: Finds both real roots (the points where the graph intersects the X axis) and complex roots (containing the imaginary unit $i$ ).
  • Function Analysis: Calculates coordinates inflection points — places where the graph of a function changes its convexity (from upward convexity to downward convexity or vice versa).
  • Discriminant: Calculates the discriminant of a cubic polynomial to determine the nature of the roots.

Solution method (Cardano formula)

Used to find roots Cardano formula. This is historically the first method for solving cubic equations in radicals.

  1. First, the equation is reduced to the canonical (“depressive”) form $y^3 + py + q = 0$ by replacing the variable $x = y — \frac{b}{3a}$ .
  2. The coefficients $p$ and $q$ are calculated.
  3. The discriminant $\Delta = (\frac{q}{2})^2 + (\frac{p}{3})^3$ is found.
