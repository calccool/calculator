Weight management is not magic, but pure mathematics. To lose weight, gain muscle, or simply stay in shape, you need to know how much energy your body spends every day.

Our advanced online calculator uses the "Gold Standard" of nutrition - the Mifflin-San Geor and Ketch-McArdle formulas - to give you the most accurate numbers, and not approximate estimates.

What are BMR and TDEE?

Before creating a diet, it is important to understand two key indicators that our tool calculates:

  1. BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) — Level of basal metabolism. This is the number of calories your body burns when completely at rest (even if you sleep all day). Energy is spent on breathing, blood circulation, cell growth and brain function.
  2. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) — Total daily energy expenditure. This is your BMR multiplied by your activity ratio. This is the number that shows how much you need to eat to keep your weight the same.

Calculation formulas: How do we count?

Our algorithm selects the most appropriate formula based on the data you enter.

1. Mifflin-San Geor Formula

It is considered the most accurate for people with an average build. It is approved by the American Dietetic Association.

$$P = 10 \times W + 6.25 \times H — 5 \times A + S$$

  • W - weight in kg
  • H - height in cm
  • A - age in years
  • S — gender coefficient (+5 for men, -161 for women)

2. Ketch-McArdle formula

If you know your body fat percentage and entered it into the calculator, we use this formula. It is much more accurate for athletes and muscular people because it takes into account lean body mass (LBM), which consumes more energy than fat tissue.

$$BMR = 370 + (21.6 \times LBM)$$

  • LBM (Lean Body Mass) - body weight without fat.

How to use the results?

After calculation, the calculator will suggest you a nutrition strategy depending on your goal. We don't just show calories, but also calculate the balance BJU (Proteins, Fats, Carbohydrates).

  • For weight loss: A safe deficit is created (usually 15-20% of TDEE). This allows you to lose fat while maintaining muscle.
  • To gain weight: A surplus is created (10-20%). Necessary for anabolism and muscle growth.
  • Recomposition: Weight maintenance with an emphasis on high amounts of protein to gradually replace fat tissue with muscle (effective for beginners).

Advice: Recalculate your numbers every 5-7 kg of weight lost or gained, since as your body weight changes, your metabolic rate also changes.
