Online reinforcement weight calculator: Calculation of mass and length (GOST 5781-82)

When constructing a foundation, armored belt or monolithic floors, design documentation usually contains data on length required rod (in linear meters). However, metal warehouses and construction stores sell rolled metal exclusively by weight (in tons or kilograms).

Our online calculator solves the age-old problem of a private developer and foreman: how to convert meters to kilograms to order the exact amount of metal and not overpay for excess.

The tool operates in two modes:

  1. Calculation of weight by length: You know how many meters you need and want to know the total weight of the order.
  2. Calculation of length by mass: You know the weight of the pack (or the remainder) and want to understand how many meters there are.

How to use the calculator

Follow three simple steps to get instant results:

  1. Select diameter: Specify the nominal diameter of the rod (from 6 mm to 80 mm). The most popular in private housing construction are 10 mm, 12 mm and 14 mm.
  2. Specify the parameter: Enter the total length of all bars (in meters) or their mass (in kilograms) by switching the calculator tab.
  3. Get a quote: The system will automatically display the total weight, specific gravity of one meter, and number of whole whips (standard length 11.7 m).

Weight calculation formula

If you want to do the calculation manually, the basic geometric formula for the volume of a cylinder multiplied by the density of the steel is used.

$$M = L \times \rho_{st} \times \frac{\pi \cdot d^2}{4}$$

Where:

  • $M$ —reinforcement weight (kg);
  • $L$ - rod length (m);
  • $\rho_{st}$ - steel density (usually 7850 kg/m³);
  • $d$ - rod diameter (m).

A simplified formula for a quick estimate on a construction site looks like this:

$$M_{1m} = \frac{D^2}{162}$$

Where $D$ is the diameter in millimeters. For example, for the 12th fitting: $12 \times 12 / 162 \approx 0.888$ kg.

Why may actual weight vary?

It is important to understand what the calculator produces theoretical weight. In practice, the mass of metal may differ from the table within the permissible GOST errors (usually +/- 3-5%).

Actual weight is affected by:

  1. Profile type: Smooth reinforcement (A-I) and corrugated reinforcement (A-III, A500C) have different section geometry.
  2. Technological tolerances: Manufacturing plants can produce rolled products with slight deviations in diameter (“minus rolled products”).
  3. Corrosion: Rust can change the weight slightly, but this is usually neglected for construction calculations.

Advice: When purchasing large quantities (over 1 ton), always require weighing on a scale. If the seller refuses to weigh and only counts “by meter”, multiplying by a theoretical coefficient, check the actual diameter of the rod with a caliper.

Transportation Tips

Our calculator calculates not only the weight, but also the number of whips. This is critical for logistics:

  • Passenger car (roof rack): Usually withstands up to 70-100 kg. This is approximately 110 meters of d12 reinforcement.
  • Gazelle: Takes up to 1.5 tons. But keep in mind that the standard length of the whip is 11.7 m, and the Gazelle body is 3-4 meters. You will have to order metal cutting at the base.
  • Shalanda (Long length): Necessary for transporting entire 12-meter long whips without cutting, which saves metal on overlaps when tying.
