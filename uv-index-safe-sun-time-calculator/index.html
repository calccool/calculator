UV index calculator: calculating safe sun exposure time and SPF protection

Ultraviolet radiation (UV) is the invisible part of the solar spectrum that, when overexposed, causes photoaging, burns, and damage to the DNA of skin cells. Our calculator uses a scientifically proven algorithm based on the analysis of three key factors: radiation intensity, individual sensitivity of the epidermis and the effectiveness of the protection filters used.

Main influencing factors

  • UV index (UVI): International standard for measuring the strength of ultraviolet radiation. The higher it is, the faster tissue damage occurs.
  • Phototype according to Fitzpatrick: A classification dividing people into 6 types depending on the amount of melanin and reaction to the sun.
  • Ambient factor: Sand, water and snow act like mirrors, reflecting rays and increasing the total dose of radiation your skin receives.
  • SPF factor: Sunscreen extends the "safe window" by absorbing or reflecting UVB rays.

Mathematical calculation model

To determine the time before the onset of erythema (redness of the skin), a formula is used that relates the basic sensitivity of the phototype to the current environmental conditions.

$$T_{safe} = \left( \frac{T_{base}}{I_{uv} \cdot K_{env}} \right) \cdot SPF$$

Where:

  • $T_{safe}$ — final safe stay time (in minutes);
  • $T_{base}$ - base time to burn for a specific skin type with a UV index of 1;
  • $I_{uv}$ - current UV index;
  • $K_{env}$ —environmental reflectance coefficient;
  • $SPF$ is a numerical indicator of the cream’s protection factor.

Instructions for using the calculator

To get the most accurate result, follow the algorithm:

  1. Select mode: “Without cream” to estimate the natural limit or “With protection (SPF)” if you plan to use cosmetics.
  2. Determine your phototype: If in doubt, click on the question icon in the calculator to view a detailed Fitzpatrick table.
  3. Please indicate the current UV index: You can find it in any weather application. Use quick entry buttons for typical values ​​(morning, afternoon, southern regions).
  4. Select environment: Remember that on the beach, safe time is reduced by 25% due to the reflection of rays from water and sand.
  5. Get the result: The calculator will instantly show the time until the burn and give a personal recommendation for protection.

Interpreting UV Index Risk Levels

  • Low (0–2): Safe for most. No protection required, glasses recommended on bright days.
  • Moderate (3–5): Protection required. Try to stay in the shade in the middle of the day and wear hats.
  • High (6–7): SPF 30+ required. Cover your body with clothes, reduce your exposure to the open sun from 11:00 to 16:00.
  • Very high and extreme (8+): Maximum danger. The skin can burn in 10–15 minutes. It is recommended to stay indoors.

Why is it important to update your SPF?

Even if the calculator shows a long protection time at a high SPF, it is important to remember the two-hour rule. Sun filters are destroyed by exposure to light, washed off by sweat, or rubbed off on clothing. If you are actively moving or swimming, renew the protective layer every 90-120 minutes, regardless of the calculated values.
