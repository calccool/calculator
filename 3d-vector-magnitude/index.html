What is the modulus of a vector and how to find it?

Vector module (or its length/magnitude) is a scalar value that indicates the distance from the beginning of the vector (usually the origin $0,0,0$ ) to its end point. In physics, the modulus of the velocity vector is the speed of movement, the modulus of the force vector is the magnitude of the applied force.

Mathematically, the calculation of the length of the $\vec{v} = \{x, y, z\}$ vector in the Cartesian coordinate system is based on the generalized Pythagorean theorem for three-dimensional space.

Basic formulas

Below are the equations our algorithm uses:

1. Formula for length (magnitude):

$$|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$$

2. Unit vector (Normalization):

The unit vector $\hat{u}$ has the same direction as the original one, but its length is 1.

$$\hat{u} = \frac{\vec{v}}{|\vec{v}|} = \left( \frac{x}{|\vec{v}|}, \frac{y}{|\vec{v}|}, \frac{z}{|\vec{v}|} \right)$$

3. Direction cosines (Angles):

These are the angles between the vector and the positive directions of the $X, Y, Z$ axes.

$$\cos \alpha = \frac{x}{|\vec{v}|}, \quad \cos \beta = \frac{y}{|\vec{v}|}, \quad \cos \gamma = \frac{z}{|\vec{v}|}$$

Examples of use in real problems

Understanding the properties of a 3D vector is necessary in a variety of disciplines:

  • Game development (GameDev): To calculate the distance between the player and the enemy, the vector module of the difference between their positions is used. Normalization is needed to direct the character's movement so that the diagonal speed does not exceed the straight speed.
  • Physics and Mechanics: To find the resultant force in spatial structures.
  • Machine learning: Feature vectors are normalized before feeding into the neural network to speed up learning (Cosine Similarity).

Calculation example

Let's say we have a vector $\vec{a}$ with coordinates $(3, 4, 5)$ .

  1. Finding the squared coordinates: $$3^2 = 9, \quad 4^2 = 16, \quad 5^2 = 25$$
  2. Let's sum them up: $$9 + 16 + 25 = 50$$
  3. Taking the square root: $$|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{50} \approx 7.071$$
