Online scale calculator - calculation of actual dimensions and drawings

Scale is the ratio of the size of an object in an image (map, drawing, diagram) to its actual size in reality. Our online tool is designed for accurate engineering, architectural and cartographic calculations, eliminating the risk of error from manual unit conversions.

The calculator operates in three modes, providing a full cycle of work with project documentation:

  • Finding the actual size: if you know the scale and length of the line in the drawing.
  • Calculation of the size in the drawing: if you need to understand how long an object will be when transferred to paper.
  • Scale Definition: if both parameters are known and you need to calculate the reduction or increase factor.

Mathematical calculation formulas

The calculator is based on classical mathematical dependencies. When working with linear objects, direct proportion is used, but when moving to areas, it is important to take into account the quadratic dependence.

Linear scale

To calculate the actual length ( $L_{real}$ ) the formula is used:

$$L_{real} = L_{map} \cdot M$$

Where:

  • $L_{map}$ - length on the map or drawing;
  • $M$ - scaling factor (right side of the 1:N ratio).

Areal scale

A special feature of our calculator is the correct calculation of areas. When scaling areas ( $S$ ), the coefficient is squared:

$$S_{real} = S_{map} \cdot M^2$$

This is critical for surveyors and builders, since at a scale of 1:100 the area in reality will be 10,000 times larger, not 100.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, follow the algorithm:

1. Select operating mode

At the top of the interface, switch between the tabs: Actual Size, Drawing Size, or Find Scale.

2. Enter scale parameters

Specify the ratio (for example, 1 to 100). By default, the left margin is one, which corresponds to standard reduction.

3. Specify values ​​and units of measurement

Enter a numeric value. You can choose millimeters, centimeters or inches for the drawing, and meters or kilometers for real objects. The calculator will automatically perform the conversion on the fly.

4. Enable area calculation (if necessary)

If your task is to calculate the area of ​​a plot or room, activate the switch "Area calculation". All input fields and results will automatically convert to square values ​​( $см^2$ , $м^2$ , etc.).

5. Visualization and export

After clicking the “Calculate” button, the visualization block will build a projection that clearly shows the difference between objects. You can copy the result in one click for pasting into project documentation.
