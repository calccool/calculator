How is the standard working time calculated in Uzbekistan?

According to Article 257 of the Labor Code of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the calculation of working hours is carried out according to a five-day work week with two days off (Saturday and Sunday). This rule applies to all work modes, including six-day work and shift work.

The following mathematical model is used for the calculation:

$$H = (D_w \times \frac{W_h}{5}) — P_h$$

Where:

  • $H$ - the final standard of working hours for the period;
  • $D_w$ - number of working days in the period according to the 5-day schedule;
  • $W_h$ - established length of the working week (40 or 36 hours);
  • $P_h$ - the number of hours by which work is reduced on pre-holiday days.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

What are the working hours in 2026?

The norm depends on the specific month and production calendar approved by the Government of Uzbekistan. Typically the average is around 167-168 hours based on a 40 hour work week.

Is it necessary to shorten the pre-holiday day if an employee works a 6-day shift?

Yes, according to the law, the duration of daily work on the eve of holidays (non-working days) is reduced for all employees by at least one hour.

How to calculate the cost of an hour with a fixed salary?

To do this, it is necessary to divide the established monthly salary by the standard working hours in a given month, calculated according to the five-day work schedule.
