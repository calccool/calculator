Translation mathematics: formulas and coefficients

Under the hood of the converter there is a single reference base, where each value is tied to a square meter ( $м^2$ ).

The mathematical logic for converting from a $A$ unit to a $B$ unit is as follows:

$$V_{B} = V_{A} \times \frac{K_{A}}{K_{B}}$$

Where:

  • $V_{A}$ - initial area value.
  • $V_{B}$ is the desired value.
  • $K_{A}$ is the conversion factor from the original unit to square meters.
  • $K_{B}$ - conversion factor of the target unit to square meters.

Let's look at the example of converting 5 acres to hectares.

Acre factor: $K_{A} = 4046.8564$

Hectare factor: $K_{B} = 10000$

$$V_{B} = 5 \times \frac{4046.8564}{10000} = 5 \times 0.40468564 = 2.023428 \text{ га}$$
