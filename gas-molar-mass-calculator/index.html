Gas molar mass calculator (according to the Mendeleev-Clapeyron equation)

Molar mass of gas is the mass of one mole of a substance, expressed in grams per mole (g/mol). Determining this parameter is a key task in thermodynamics and chemistry, as it allows the identification of an unknown substance.

Our online calculator solves the ideal gas equation (Mendeleev-Clapeyron law) with respect to molar mass, automatically converting all entered values ​​(pressure, volume, temperature) to a consistent system of units.

Theoretical basis: Calculation formula

The calculator is based on the fundamental equation of state of an ideal gas:

$$PV = nRT$$

Where:

  • $P$ - gas pressure;
  • $V$ - volume occupied by gas;
  • $n$ —amount of substance (in moles);
  • $R$ - universal gas constant;
  • $T$ - absolute temperature (in Kelvin).

Since the amount of substance $n$ can be expressed through the ratio of the mass of gas $m$ to its molar mass $M$ ( $n = m/M$ ), the formula is transformed to find the molar mass:

$$M = \frac{mRT}{PV}$$

Example of problem solution

Let's imagine that we have a cylinder with a volume 5 liters, filled with gas mass 6.4 grams. The pressure in the cylinder is 0.98 atm, and the temperature 25°C.

  1. We substitute the values ​​into the calculator.
  2. Temperature is converted to Kelvin: $25^\circ C + 273.15 = 298.15 K$ .
  3. Formula used: $$M = \frac{6.4 \cdot 0.08206 \cdot 298.15}{0.98 \cdot 5} \approx 31.96 \text{ g/mol}$$
  4. Result: The molar mass is 31.96 g/mol. Comparing with the periodic table, we understand that this Oxygen ( $O_2$ ).

The calculator also calculates gas density ( $\rho$ ) under given conditions according to the formula:

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V} = \frac{PM}{RT}$$
