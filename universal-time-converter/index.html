Categories of supported units

For convenience, all quantities in the calculator are divided into logical groups. You can use the smart search using the drop-down list or select the desired option manually:

  • Everyday: standard calendar units (seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, months, years, centuries and millennia).
  • Metric (SI system): microscopic fractions of time from yoctoseconds ( $10^{-24}$ s) to giant exaseconds ( $10^{18}$ s).
  • Astronomical: quantities related to the movement of celestial bodies (sidereal days, synodic months, tropical, draconic and galactic years).
  • Specialized (physics): quantum and theoretical intervals such as Planck time, Shake ( $10^{-8}$ s) and chronon.
  • Historical and local: traditional measures of different cultures (Chinese ke and shi, Indian muhurtas and pranas, medieval moments and thirds, and French decimal time).

Mathematical basis and calculation formulas

The algorithm is based on the method of reduction to a base constant. Any user-entered value is first converted to the SI reference unit, second (s), and then multiplied or divided by the target value coefficient.

The general mathematical conversion model is as follows:

$$V_{target} = V_{source} \times \left( \frac{K_{source}}{K_{target}} \right)$$

Where:

  • $V_{target}$ — the desired (resulting) value;
  • $V_{source}$ - initial value entered by the user;
  • $K_{source}$ - equivalent of the original unit in seconds;
  • $K_{target}$ is the equivalent of the target unit in seconds.

For example, to convert 5 hours to astronomical sidereal minutes, the system takes the hour factor ( $3600$ ) and the sidereal minute factor ( $59.83617$ ):

$$V_{target} = 5 \times \left( \frac{3600}{59.83617} \right) \approx 300.821$$

The tool uses safe mathematics methodsMath.abs()and exponential notation for ultra-small or ultra-large numbers (e.g. $5.39 \times 10^{-44}$ for Planck time), which eliminates memory overflow errors in the browser.
