The main mistake of conventional calculators

Most online converters use the simple formula $1 \text{ мл} = 1 \text{ г}$ . This is only true for water. But in the kitchen, density is critical.

Example: 1 glass (250 ml) of water weighs 250 g, and the same glass of wheat flour - only ~150 g. The error during a normal translation will be 100 grams, which will hopelessly ruin the baked goods.

Our algorithm uses a database of densities of popular ingredients to make accurate calculations.

Calculation formulas

To convert volume to weight (and vice versa), we use the basic physical formula for mass ( $m$ ), density ( $\rho$ ) and volume ( $V$ ):

$$m = V \times \rho$$

Where the density ( $\rho$ ) is taken from the culinary reference tables. For example:

  • Granulated sugar: $\rho \approx 0.85 \text{ г/мл}$
  • Flour: $\rho \approx 0.59 \text{ г/мл}$
  • Honey: $\rho \approx 1.44 \text{ г/мл}$

Converting oven temperatures

US and UK recipes often use the Fahrenheit or Gas Mark scale. Our tool includes a separate module for translating temperature conditions.

Conversion formulas:

  • From Fahrenheit to Celsius: $C = (F — 32) \times \frac{5}{9}$
  • From Celsius to Fahrenheit: $F = (C \times \frac{9}{5}) + 32$

How to correctly measure products by volume?

Even the most accurate calculator will not help if the measurement technique is broken:

  1. Flour: Do not compact the flour in the glass. Spoon it into a measuring cup, and then remove the mound with a knife.
  2. Liquids: Use transparent measuring cups and look at the liquid level strictly horizontally (at eye level).
  3. Sticky Products: When measuring honey or molasses with a spoon, first grease it with vegetable oil - this will allow the entire product to slide off without leaving any residue.
