Pipe Calculator: Calculation of Pipe Weight and Length (Metal Calculator)

Online calculator of rolled metal (pipes)

When purchasing, transporting and installing pipe structures, it is critical to know the exact weight of the material. Suppliers often ship metal in tons, but projects specify meters. Our universal pipe calculator solves the problem of converting these quantities.

The tool works in two directions:

  1. Weight calculation: You know the length and parameters of the pipe - the calculator will show how much it weighs (one piece and the whole batch).
  2. Length calculation: You know the total weight of the batch - the calculator will tell you how many linear meters it contains.

Supported Section Types

The calculator uses geometric formulas for the three most popular types of rental:

  • Round pipe: Standard VGP, electric-welded and seamless pipes.
  • Square pipe: Popular profile for frames and racks.
  • Rectangular pipe: Profile pipe for beams and ceilings.

Formulas for calculating pipe weight

The calculations are based on determining the volume of the metal, which is then multiplied by the density of the material.

1. For round pipe

Weight is calculated through the annular cross-sectional area:

$$Mass = \pi \cdot \rho \cdot S \cdot (D — S) \cdot L$$

Where:

  • $\rho$ - material density (kg/m³).
  • $S$ - wall thickness (m).
  • $D$ - outer diameter (m).
  • $L$ - pipe length (m).

2. For profile pipe (square and rectangular)

The difference in volumes of the external and internal parallelepiped is used:

$$Mass = \rho \cdot L \cdot [A \cdot B — (A — 2S)(B — 2S)]$$

Where $A$ and $B$ are the sides of the profile, and $S$ is the wall thickness.

Note: Formulas calculate theoretical weight. The actual weight may differ depending on GOST (tolerances for wall thickness and rounding of corners for profile pipes).

Why is this calculation needed?

  1. Logistics: To avoid overloading transport. For example, knowing that 1 meter of pipe weighs 2 kg, and you need 500 meters, you will understand that you will need a machine with a lifting capacity of at least 1 ton.
  2. Supplier verification: If they sell you a “ton” of pipes, you can measure their length and wall thickness to check if you are being cheated (for example, by slipping a pipe with a thinner wall).
  3. Design: Calculation of the load on the foundation or supporting structures of the metal frame.
