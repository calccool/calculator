Week number calculator: ISO 8601 calculation online

Standard ISO 8601 is an international regulation that eliminates confusion in planning, logistics and programming. Unlike the North American system, where the week may begin on Sunday, the ISO standard strictly defines the time structure for business and government agencies.

Key rules of the standard

  • First day: The week always starts at Monday (index 1) and ends at Sunday (index 7).
  • First week rule: The first week of the year is considered to be the one that contains first thursday this year.
  • January 4: Based on the rule above, the first week is always the week containing January 4th.
  • Rolling dates: Because of these rules, December 29, 30, or 31 may be the 1st week of the following year, and January 1, 2, or 3 may be the last week of the previous year.

Mathematical algorithm and calculation formulas

The calculation is based on the definition of “control Thursday”. Mathematically, this is expressed through an adjustment to the current date.

The offset formula is used for calculation, where $d$ is the serial number of the day of the week (Mon=1, Sun=7):

$$TargetThursday = CurrentDate + (4 — d)$$

After finding the Thursday of the target week, the week number $W$ is calculated as:

$$W = \lceil \frac{Days(TargetThursday — YearStart) + 1}{7} \rceil$$

Where $YearStart$ is the beginning of the ISO year, also determined through the position of the first Thursday of January.

Why 53 weeks?

A year contains 53 ISO weeks in only two cases:

  1. Any year starting on Thursday.
  2. Leap year starting on Wednesday.
