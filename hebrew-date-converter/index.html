Hebrew Calendar Converter: Accurate Date Translation Online

The Jewish calendar is a lunisolar system, which is much more complex than the Gregorian calendar we are used to. It is used not only to determine religious holidays, but also to calculate memorials, Bar Mitzvahs and anniversaries. The main difficulty is that the Jewish day does not begin at midnight, but with the appearance of the first stars (after sunset).

Our tool solves three key problems:

  • Accuracy: Date translation taking into account the “shift” after sunset.
  • Context: Visualization of the current month in the annual cycle.
  • Planning: Instant calculation of the dates of the main holidays for the selected year.

How the conversion algorithm works

The calculator is based on the standardIntl.DateTimeFormatwith calendar supporthebrew. However, to achieve maximum accuracy, we have integrated a leap cycle check. The Jewish system uses the 19-year Metonic cycle, where 7 years are leap years (an additional month is added - Adar II).

Leap year mathematical model

To determine whether a year in the Hebrew calendar is a leap year (embolism year), use the remainder divided by 19 formula:

$$n = Year_{Hebrew} \pmod{19}$$

If the value $n$ is included in the following set, then the year contains 13 months instead of 12:

$$\{0, 3, 6, 8, 11, 14, 17\}$$

This allows the calendar to not "run away" from the solar cycle, so that holidays such as Passover always fall in the spring.

Sunset Toggle

This is a critical feature of our calculator. Since the Jewish day begins in the evening, the same Gregorian date (for example, May 15) can correspond to different Jewish dates depending on the time of day.

If you were born or the event occurred in the evening after sunset, you must activate the switch “Has the sun set yet?”. The calculator will automatically add one day to the result, ensuring religious accuracy of the calculation.

Holidays and cycle visualization

The calculator interface includes a visualization block. The ring chart shows the progress of the current month, which helps to visually assess the proximity of the end of the lunar month.

Main holidays calculated by the tool:

Rosh Hashanah (New Year)

Celebrated on the 1st of the month of Tishrei. This is the day when, according to tradition, a person’s fate for the next year is determined.

Hanukkah (Festival of Lights)

Begins on the 25th of the month of Kislev and lasts eight days. The calculator will help you find out the exact date of lighting the first candle.

Passover (Feast of Freedom)

Always falls on the 15th day of the month Nissan (full moon). Our tool automatically finds the Passover start date for any selected year using a reverse calendar database search.
