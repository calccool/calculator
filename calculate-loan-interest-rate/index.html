Real loan interest rate calculator: find out the payment rate

Banks often advertise attractive lending conditions by listing low interest rates in their brochures. However, the reality may be different: insurance, commissions and hidden fees increase the actual overpayment.

Our reverse loan calculator allows you to calculate the real interest rate based on your actual monthly payment. This is an indispensable tool for checking the honesty of banking offers and finding hidden overpayments.

Why consider the real bet?

Many borrowers look only at the size of the monthly payment, losing sight of the effective interest rate. Calculation is necessary in the following situations:

  • Checking the contract. The bank claims 10% per annum, but taking into account the life insurance included in the loan body, the real rate can reach 15-20%.
  • Comparison of offers. One bank has a lower rate, but there are commissions. The other has a higher rate, but no additional payments. The calculator will bring both sentences to a common denominator.
  • Refinancing. Understanding the current real rate will help you decide whether it is profitable to refinance elsewhere.

Formula for calculating interest rate

Most consumer loans and mortgages are issued with an annuity repayment scheme (equal payments). The classic annuity formula looks like this:

$$AP = S \cdot \frac{r \cdot (1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $AP$ - monthly annuity payment;
  • $S$ — loan amount (debt body);
  • $r$ - monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100);
  • $n$ — loan term in months.

Problem: It is not possible to algebraically express the variable $r$ (bet) from this formula. This is a transcendental equation.

Calculation method

Our algorithm uses Newton-Raphson iterative method. The calculator selects the rate at which the estimated payment matches the actual payment you entered with an accuracy of 0.00001%. This provides banking accuracy of calculations that is not available with a conventional calculator.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, you will need data from your loan agreement or payment schedule:

  1. Loan amount (in hand): Enter exactly the amount you actually received. If the bank approved 500,000 $, but immediately withheld 50,000 $ for insurance (giving you 450,000 $), indicate 450,000 $. This will show the real value of money for you.
  2. Monthly payment: The amount you deposit each month.
  3. Loan term: Specify the duration of the contract in years or months.

Factors influencing the interest rate

If the figure obtained in the calculator is higher than specified in your contract, this may be due to the following reasons:

  • Life and health insurance. Often voluntary, but affects the rate. The cost of the policy may be included in the loan amount.
  • Account servicing fees. Some banks still practice monthly fees (although this is often legally challenged).
  • SMS information services. A trifle that, on small loan amounts, can raise the effective rate by 0.5-1%.
  • Financial protection. Additional options, such as “credit holidays” or “rate reduction”, which are purchased for an additional fee.

Use the data obtained for a reasoned dialogue with bank managers or to make an informed financial decision.
