Freelancer hourly cost calculator: calculate online

Most new freelancers make a critical mistake: they divide their desired salary by 160 working hours per month. As a result, their net profit turns out to be 40–50% lower than expected. Working for yourself involves expenses that the employer incurs in the office: taxes, payment for software, sick leave and time to find clients.

Our calculator helps you avoid the “self-employed trap” and calculate a rate that will provide you with financial stability.

Freelancer rate calculation formula

To obtain an accurate figure, the calculator uses an algorithm that takes into account three variables: target load, operating expenses and tax burden. The mathematical model looks like this:

$$Rate = \frac{\frac{Net + Expenses}{1 — Tax}}{Hours_{total} \times \frac{Billable\%}{100}}$$

Where:

  • Net - desired amount in hand per month.
  • Expenses — monthly operating expenses (subscriptions, communications, depreciation of equipment).
  • Tax — tax rate (in decimal form).
  • Hours total — the total number of working hours per month, taking into account weekends and holidays.
  • Billable% — the percentage of time for which the client pays money directly.

What is Billable Time?

This is one of the most important coefficients in our tool. A freelancer cannot bill for the entire 8 hours of a workday. A significant portion of time is spent on non-productive tasks:

  1. Searching for orders and conducting negotiations.
  2. Drawing up technical specifications and contracts.
  3. Vocational training.
  4. Workplace maintenance.

Market gold standard: set the paid time ratio at the level 60%. If you spend more time on bureaucracy, your hourly rate should be higher.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

To get the most accurate results, follow these steps:

Setting up financial goals

Enter the amount you want to receive on your card after all payments. In the “Expenses” field, indicate the cost of the Internet, coworking space, paid neural networks (ChatGPT, Midjourney) and other services. Don't forget to select the tax rate - the script will automatically perform a "gross-up" calculation to calculate the required total revenue.

Detailed chart settings

Turn on the switch “Detailed chart settings”, if your mode differs from the standard 5/2.

  • Weekends and holidays: The calculator subtracts 28 days of vacation (or other amount) from the annual time fund so that your hourly rate “pays” for your vacation.
  • Hours per day: Indicate the actual time you are willing to spend on the computer.
  • Paid time: If you are a newbie and spend a lot of time searching for orders, bet 40-50%. If you have a queue of clients - 70–80%.

Why should your rate be higher than the market rate?

Freelancing is a one-person business. Your rate should cover not only food and housing, but also risks: downtime between projects, sudden illnesses and inflation. Using this calculator, you will see the “break-even point” - the minimum threshold below which it is simply unprofitable to work.
