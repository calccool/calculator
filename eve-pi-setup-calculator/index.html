Specifications and fitting structures

The effectiveness of your base directly depends on your skill level Command Center Upgrades. Every structure on the planet consumes two types of resources: computing power (CPU) and energy (Powergrid).

Table of resource consumption by structures

To calculate the total load on the system, the formula is used:

$$L_{total} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (Count_i \times Cost_i) + Links_{res}$$

Where $L_{total}$ is the total load, $Count$ is the number of buildings of a certain type, and $Links_{res}$ is the power reserve for planetary links (connecting circuits).

Production cycles and ranges of products

In EVE Online, production is divided into several stages (tiers). The right balance between raw material extraction (R0) and plant speed determines your bottom line.

Refining capacity by level

  • Tier 1 (P1): The base plant consumes 6,000 units of raw materials (R0) per hour and produces 80 units of output P1.
  • Tier 2 (P2): An advanced plant consumes 80 units of P1 (usually two types of 40 each) and produces 5 units of P2 per hour.
  • Tier 3 (P3): An advanced plant consumes P2 components and produces 3 units of P3 per hour.

To calculate daily output, the following formula is used:

$$V_{day} = Factories \times Rate_{hr} \times 24$$

Where $V_{day}$ is the volume of production for 24 hours, and $Rate_{hr}$ is the hourly production rate for the selected shooting range.

PI Optimization Tips

Maximizing link efficiency

Links between buildings also consume PG resources. To minimize costs, try to locate factories as close to each other and to the spaceport as possible. Use a star design where Launchpad is the central node.

Command Center Levels

Never skimp on the command center level. The difference between level 4 and 5 of the CC Upgrades skill can allow you to install 2 additional drills or one extra plant, which will increase the profitability of the planet by 15-20%.

Extraction balance

If the calculator shows that your plants consume 144,000 R0 units per day, adjust the extractor cycle so that the Average Yield is slightly above this value. This will create a buffer in case of temporary depletion of resources in the mining spot.
