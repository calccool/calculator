Mathematical conversion formulas

Our calculator uses standardized mathematical models to ensure accuracy.

Convert RGB to CMYK

For the calculation, an intermediate black coefficient (Key) is used, which is determined by the maximum value of one of the RGB channels:

$$K = 1 — \max\left(\frac{R}{255}, \frac{G}{255}, \frac{B}{255}\right)$$

Then the remaining components are calculated:

$$C = \frac{1 — R/255 — K}{1 — K}, \quad M = \frac{1 — G/255 — K}{1 — K}, \quad Y = \frac{1 — B/255 — K}{1 — K}$$

Convert RGB to HSL

To determine lightness ( $L$ ), the arithmetic mean of extreme values ​​is used:

$$L = \frac{\max(R, G, B) + \min(R, G, B)}{2}$$

Where the $R, G, B$ values ​​are pre-normalized to the $[0, 1]$ range.

Why is accuracy important?

An error in CMYK calculation of just 2-3% can lead to noticeable color distortion when printing on an offset press. Our algorithm performs calculations using strict logical checks, eliminating common JS interpreter errors when working with null values.
