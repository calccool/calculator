Floodlight installation height calculator: online calculation of site lighting

Correct calculation of the installation height of lighting equipment is the key to uniform illumination without “blind spots” and excessive glare. Our online calculator allows you to instantly determine design parameters for open areas, warehouses, parking lots and construction sites.

The tool takes into account not only the geometric dimensions of the site, but also specific loss coefficients, which makes it indispensable for the preliminary design of outdoor lighting systems.

Basic criteria for choosing the height of supports

The choice of height of the spotlight directly affects the luminous flux utilization factor ( $\eta$ ). The higher the light source is located, the larger the area it covers, but the lower the illumination intensity at ground level becomes.

When designing, you should rely on the following goals:

  • Uniformity (Uo): Prevents sharp changes between light and dark areas.
  • No glare: Optimal tilt angle and height minimize eye discomfort.
  • Economic efficiency: Selection of the minimum required number of supports and lamps.

Calculation methods and formulas

The calculator uses the engineering method of luminous flux utilization factor. The calculation is carried out in several stages.

Calculation of the required total luminous flux

To determine how much light the entire site “needs”, taking into account losses (dust, lamp wear, reflection), the formula is used:

$$\Phi_{total} = \frac{E \cdot S \cdot K_z}{\eta}$$

Where:

  • $E$ - target illumination (Lx);
  • $S$ - site area ( $м^2$ ), calculated as $L \cdot W$ ;
  • $K_z$ - safety factor (usually 1.5 for outdoor LEDs);
  • $\eta$ - luminous flux utilization factor (LUF).

Determining the number of spotlights

After calculating the total flux, the system divides it by the power of the selected luminaire:

$$N = \lceil \frac{\Phi_{total}}{\Phi_{lamp}} \rceil$$

The result is always rounded up to the nearest whole number.

Calculation of optimal installation height (H)

The optimal height of the support is calculated using an empirical relationship that ensures the overlap of light spots from adjacent spotlights to achieve uniformity:

$$H \approx \sqrt{\frac{S}{N}} \cdot 0.85$$

This formula allows you to find a balance: the spotlights are installed high enough so as not to dazzle, but low enough so as not to waste useful light energy.
