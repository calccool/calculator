How the calculation algorithm works

To determine the final cost, the program analyzes three independent factors that are summed together. The basic calculation formula is as follows:

$$C_{total} = P_{weight} + P_{dimensions} + P_{special}$$

Where:

  • $C_{total}$ — total amount to be paid;
  • $P_{weight}$ - surcharge for exceeding the weight limit;
  • $P_{dimensions}$ - fine for exceeding dimensions (oversized);
  • $P_{special}$ - fixed fee for transporting special categories (animals, sports, weapons).

Weight standards: standard limits

Airlines most often use the system Piece Concept (number of pieces of luggage). Each place has a strictly limited weight. If your suitcase is overweight, one of two types of fines will apply:

  1. For each kilogram of excess: Relevant for low-cost airlines, where each extra kg costs a fixed amount. $$P_{weight} = (W_{fact} — W_{limit}) \cdot Price_{unit}$$
  2. Fixed penalty: Often used by large carriers (Aeroflot, S7, Turkish Airlines) for the very fact of excess (for example, if the weight of the suitcase falls in the range of 23–32 kg).

Dimensions and rule 158 centimeters

Most airlines around the world adhere to the gold standard of measurement. The sum of the length, width and height of your suitcase should not exceed 158 cm.

Carriage of special baggage

Some things cannot be fit into a standard framework. Our calculator has a separate mode for special luggage.

  • Sports equipment: Skis, snowboards and bicycles are often transported free of charge as part of seasonal promotions, but require mandatory approval 48 hours before departure.
  • Pets: The cost depends on the method of transportation (in the cabin or luggage compartment). The weight of the container with the animal is always summed up and paid for as a separate place.
  • Musical instruments: If the instrument exceeds its dimensions, you often have to buy a separate passenger seat for it.

Expert tips: how to save money on luggage

To avoid leaving half your vacation budget at the front desk, follow these rules:

  • Weigh your luggage at home: Use hand balances. The accuracy of store scales is usually lower than that of airport scales.
  • Pay in advance: The extra charge for baggage on the airline's website or app is 20-40% cheaper than at the airport counter.
  • Use the Heavy Pocket Rule: The heaviest small items (chargers, books) can be placed in clothing pockets - their weight is not taken into account when registering.
  • Remember the 32 kg limit: This is the absolute maximum. A suitcase heavier than 32 kg is officially considered a “heavy load”, and loaders have the right to refuse to lift it due to sanitary standards. Such baggage will have to be sent through the cargo terminal.
