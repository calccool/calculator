Compensation calculator for unused vacation upon dismissal

Upon termination of an employment contract, the employer is obliged to pay the employee monetary compensation for all unused rest days (Article 127 of the Labor Code of the Russian Federation). Our online tool allows you to instantly calculate the final amount in your hands, taking into account all the nuances of the law, including tax withholdings and rules for rounding up length of service.

How does online payment work?

The calculator interface supports two operating modes to ensure maximum accuracy for the accountant and simplicity for the worker:

  1. By number of days: If you already know the rest from the HR department certificate.
  2. By dates of work: Automatic calculation of length of service from the date of admission to the day of dismissal.

To get the result, just enter your average daily earnings (ADE) and select the tax rate (NDFL). The process visualizer will instantly display the payout structure: the share you will receive on the card and the amount of tax deductions.

Calculation methods and formulas

The calculator is based on approved Rostrud algorithms. The basic compensation formula is as follows:

$$S = D_{unused} \times SDZ$$

Where:

  • $S$ — accrual amount (Gross);
  • $D_{unused}$ - number of days of unused vacation;
  • $SDZ$ - average daily earnings.

To determine number of vacation days paid for time worked, the formula is applied:

$$D_{earned} = \frac{D_{year}}{12} \times M$$

Where $D_{year}$ is the annual rate (usually 28 days ), and $M$ is the number of complete months worked.

Important rules for rounding and seniority

The calculation of compensation has a number of legal subtleties that our algorithm takes into account automatically:

  • 15 day rule: If 15 or more days were worked in the last month, it is rounded up to the full month. If less than 15 days, the month is not taken into account.
  • Full compensation for 11 months: If an employee worked for the company from 11 to 12 months in the first working year, he is entitled to compensation for a full year (28 days), provided that he does not resign of his own free will or due to reduction/liquidation.
  • Average daily earnings: To calculate the SDZ, the amount of payments for the last 12 months is taken, divided by the coefficient 29.3 (average monthly number of calendar days).

Taxation of payments

Vacation compensation is not a state benefit and is subject to personal income tax in the general manner. The calculator automatically calculates the net amount (Net) after personal income tax withholding:

$$S_{net} = S_{gross} — (S_{gross} \times Tax)$$

You can use the copy button next to each result to transfer the exact values ​​into your documents or reports.
