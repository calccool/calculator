Calculation of the Eastern (Chinese) horoscope

The Chinese zodiac is based on a 12-year lunar cycle, with each year corresponding to a specific animal. In addition, each year is governed by one of the five elements (Metal, Water, Wood, Fire, Earth), which alternate every two years, creating a complete 60-year cycle.

Mathematically, the definition of the animal year ( $A$ ) can be represented through the operation of calculating the remainder of division (modulus). If $Y$ is your birth year, then the formula looks like this:

$$A = (Y — 4) \pmod{12}$$

Where the result corresponds to the animal index:

  • 0 - Rat
  • 1 - Bull
  • 2 – Tiger
  • 3 - Rabbit
  • 4 - Dragon
  • 5 - Snake
  • 6 - Horse
  • 7 – Goat
  • 8 - Monkey
  • 9 - Rooster
  • 10 - Dog
  • 11 - Pig

Our calculator goes beyond simply determining the sign. It analyzes the last digit of the year of birth to calculate the governing element:
