PoE Chromatic Sphere Calculator: Chromatic Calculator

The coloring of nests in Path of Exile is not just an accident, but a clearly proven mathematical model. If you are trying to produce "off-colors" (colors that do not match the basic characteristics of the base), using chromatic spheres can become extremely expensive. Our tool allows you to assess risks in advance and choose the most economical crafting path.

How does the color drop algorithm work?

The likelihood of a certain color appearing in a socket directly depends on the item's Strength, Dexterity and Intelligence requirements. The higher the requirement for a specific characteristic, the higher the chance of getting the color corresponding to it:

  • Strength (Str): Red nests (Red).
  • Agility (Dex): Green nests (Green).
  • Intelligence (Int): Blue nests (Blue).

This is expressed mathematically through a system of weights. The base weight of each color is 10, to which is added the value of the corresponding requirement.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the probability of success, our calculator uses a weight formula for each of the three colors:

$$W_{color} = 10 + Requirement$$

Where:

  • $W_{color}$ - final weight of a specific color.
  • $Requirement$ - the item’s requirement for the corresponding characteristic (0 if there is no requirement).

The probability of obtaining one specific color (for example, red) is calculated as the ratio of its weight to the sum of the weights of all possible colors:

$$P_{red} = \frac{W_{str}}{W_{str} + W_{dex} + W_{int}}$$

Since when using the Chromatic Sphere ( $Chromatic \ Orb$ ) the colors of all slots change simultaneously (and the result cannot be identical to the original), the calculator iteratively calculates all combinations using Bernoulli's formula and combinatorial analysis of states.

Basic functions of the calculator

Our tool provides unique metrics that will help you save currency:

  • Chance of success: The probability of getting the desired combination with one “throw” of the sphere.
  • Average quantity: Mathematical expectation of costs. Half the time you will meet this number.
  • Confident throw (90%): The number of spheres that you need to have in your inventory to ensure a 90% chance of getting the desired result. The calculation is carried out according to the formula: $$N_{safe} = \frac{\ln(0.1)}{\ln(1 — P_{success})}$$
  • Visual dashboard: A graphical representation of your item's Bias towards certain colors based on its characteristics.

Instructions: How to use the tool

  1. Select number of slots: Indicate the current number of holes in your item (from 1 to 6).
  2. Enter the requirements (Stats): Look at the item in the game and enter the required Strength, Dexterity and Intelligence values. If an item requires only one attribute, leave the others at 0.
  3. Specify your desired colors: Enter the number of Red, Green and Blue Sockets you want to receive.
  4. Click "Calculate": The system will instantly display the probability and build a weight distribution diagram.

Tips for saving currency

If the calculator shows a too low chance of success (less than 0.1%), consider alternative methods:

  • Crafting Bench: Guaranteed painting of 1, 2 or 3 nests. It's often cheaper to get 3-4 "off-colors".
  • Vorici Method (Syndicate): Using white sockets makes picking up skill gems much easier.
  • Desecration and Tintura: In some cases, it is easier to use Desecration spheres or special divinations.
