Basic elements of a regular n-gon

To understand the calculation results, it is important to understand the terminology. A regular polygon is a convex geometric figure in which all sides are equal and all angles between adjacent sides are equal.

  • Side ( $a$ ): The length of one segment bounding the polygon.
  • Center: A point equidistant from all vertices and sides.
  • Circumradius ( $R$ ): A line segment connecting the center to any vertex of the figure.
  • Inscribed Circle Radius ( $r$ ): Also called apothem. This is a segment connecting the center with the middle of any side (perpendicular to the side).
  • Inner corner: The angle between two adjacent sides within a figure.

Calculation formulas (Formulas)

For those who want to check the calculations manually or use the data for educational purposes, we present the basic mathematical relationships. The formulas use the trigonometric function of tangent ( $tan$ ) and sine ( $sin$ ), and the angle is taken in radians ( $\pi/n$ ).

1. Perimeter

The simplest value. This is the sum of the lengths of all sides.

$$P = n \times a$$

2. Area

There are several ways to find the area ( $S$ ), depending on what you know.

Through side and apothem (the easiest way):

$$Area = \frac{1}{2} \times P \times r$$

Through the number of sides ( $n$ ) and side length ( $a$ ):

$$Area = \frac{n \times a^2}{4 \times \tan(\frac{\pi}{n})}$$

Via circumradius ( $R$ ):

$$Area = \frac{1}{2} \times n \times R^2 \times \sin(\frac{2\pi}{n})$$

3. Internal corner

The more sides a figure has, the greater the degree measure of its angle (tends to $180^\circ$ ).

$$Angle = \frac{(n — 2) \times 180}{n}$$
