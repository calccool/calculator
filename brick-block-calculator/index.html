Brick and Block Calculator: Accurate calculation of walls, mortar and construction costs

Building a house box is one of the most expensive stages. An error in calculations of just 10% can lead to either stopping construction and costly delivery of the missing batch, or overpaying for extra pallets, which then have nowhere to put them.

Our engineering calculator solves this problem. Unlike simple “length by height” formulas, it takes into account:

  • The actual volume of masonry joints (which occupy up to 25% of the wall volume).
  • Deduction of the area of ​​window and door openings.
  • Technological reserve for combat and pruning.
  • Dimensions of any materials: from classic brick (1NF) to large-format ceramic blocks and aerated concrete.

Calculation algorithm: Formulas and logic

For those who are used to trusting numbers, we reveal the mathematics of our tool. The calculation is carried out in several stages:

1. Wall geometry

First, the “net” volume of the masonry ( $V_{net}$ ) is calculated, which must be filled with material.

$$S_{total} = P \times H$$

$$S_{openings} = \sum (W_{door} \times H_{door}) + \sum (W_{window} \times H_{window})$$

$$V_{net} = (S_{total} — S_{openings}) \times T_{wall}$$

Where:

  • $P$ - wall perimeter.
  • $H$ - wall height.
  • $T_{wall}$ - wall thickness.

2. Material consumption (pieces)

To find out the number of blocks ( $N$ ), we calculate the volume of one unit, taking into account the mortar joint. This is critically important, since a 10 mm thick seam on the volume of the entire house saves cubic meters of brick.

$$V_{unit} = (L + T_{seam}) \times (H_{unit} + T_{seam}) \times W_{unit}$$

$$N = \frac{V_{net}}{V_{unit}} \times K_{waste}$$

Where $K_{waste}$ is the battle reserve coefficient (usually 1.05, that is, 5%).

3. Calculation of the solution

The volume of masonry mortar is calculated as the difference between the total volume of the wall and the net volume of all bricks without seams.

Tips before purchasing materials

  1. Consider "Combat": When transporting and unloading bricks, losses are inevitable. For facing bricks, the recommended stock is - 5%, for rough and gas block - 5-7%, for ceramic porous blocks (fragile) - up to 10%.
  2. Seam thickness:
    • For ordinary bricks, the standard seam is 10 mm.
    • For aerated concrete using foam adhesive or special adhesive, a seam is only 2-3 mm. Be sure to change this setting in the calculator for accuracy!
  3. Masonry grid: If you use reinforcing mesh every 3-4 rows, it is minor, but it does affect the height of the wall. However, when calculating the volume of material, this can be neglected.
  4. Number of pallets: Knowing the total number of pieces, check with the supplier about the capacity of one pallet (pallet). Usually this is:
    • Single brick: ≈ 400-420 pcs.
    • One and a half: ≈ 280-300 pcs.
    • Gas block: ≈ 1.44 - 1.8 m³ on a pallet.
