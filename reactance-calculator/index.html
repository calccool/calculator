Reactance (XL and XC) and Resonant Frequency Calculator

In alternating current (AC) circuits, resistors are not the only elements that prevent current from flowing. Inductors and capacitors also create resistance, but its nature is different from active. This phenomenon is called reactance (Reactance).

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate inductive and capacitive reactance, as well as find the resonance point for any LC circuit. Below we will analyze the physics of the process and the formulas underlying the calculations.

What is reactance?

Unlike active resistance ( $R$ ), which converts energy into heat, reactance ( $X$ ) stores energy in an electric or magnetic field and returns it back to the circuit. The value of this resistance directly depends on the frequency of the signal.

There are two types of reactivity:

  1. Inductive ( $X_L$ ) - created by coils, chokes, motor windings.
  2. Capacitive ( $X_C$ ) - created by capacitors and parasitic capacitance of cables.

Inductive reactance ( $X_L$ )

An inductor resists changes in the current passing through it. As the frequency of alternating current increases, the rate of change of current increases, and the coil exerts more and more resistance.

Calculation formula:

$$X_L = 2 \pi f L$$

Where:

  • $X_L$ - inductive reactance (Ohms, $\Omega$ )
  • $f$ - frequency (Hertz, Hz)
  • $L$ - inductance (Henry, H)
  • $\pi$ — mathematical constant $\approx 3.14159$

Rule: The higher the frequency or inductance, the higher the coil resistance. For direct current ( $f=0$ ), the ideal coil is a conductor with zero resistance.

Capacitance ( $X_C$ )

A capacitor, on the contrary, does not pass direct current, but conducts alternating current. As the frequency increases, the capacitor has time to charge and discharge faster, allowing more current to flow, which is equivalent to lower resistance.

Calculation formula:

$$X_C = \frac{1}{2 \pi f C}$$

Where:

  • $X_C$ - capacitance (Ohms, $\Omega$ )
  • $f$ - frequency (Hertz, Hz)
  • $C$ - capacity (Farads, F)

Rule: The higher the frequency or capacitance, the below capacitor resistance.

Resonance frequency

Of particular interest is the situation when both a coil and a capacitor (LC circuit) are present in the circuit. At a certain frequency, their reactances become equal in magnitude ( $X_L = X_C$ ). This point is called resonance. In a series circuit at resonance, the total resistance becomes minimal (tends to 0), and in a parallel circuit it becomes maximum (tends to infinity).

Resonance formula (Thomson formula):

$$f_{res} = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sqrt{LC}}$$

Where is this applied?

Calculation of reactance is critically important in radio electronics and electrical engineering:

  • Audio systems: Crossovers in speakers separate frequencies (low ones to the speaker via a coil, high ones to the tweeter via a capacitor).
  • Radio communication: Tuning the receiver to the desired wavelength is done by changing $L$ or $C$ to achieve resonance.
  • Power supply: Compensation of reactive power in factories to reduce electricity losses.
