Warm Water Floor Calculator: Calculation of Power, Temperature and Pipes

Professional calculation of water heated floors

A water heated floor is a low-temperature heating system, the effectiveness of which depends on many physical factors. An error in calculations at the design stage can lead to two extremes: either the floor will be “striped” (alternating hot and cold zones), or it will not be able to warm the room to a comfortable temperature.

Our online engineering calculator allows you to simulate the operation of the system before installation, taking into account the hydraulic and thermophysical properties of materials.

What parameters does the calculator calculate?

  1. Specific thermal power ( $q$ ): The amount of heat that each square meter of floor gives off to the room. This parameter must be compared with the heat loss of your home.
  2. Surface Temperature ( $t_{floor}$ ): According to sanitary standards (SNiP 41-01-2003), the floor temperature in residential areas should not exceed 26°C (29°C for temporary premises).
  3. Specific coolant flow: Shows how much water must pass through the loop to ensure a given heat removal.
  4. Pipe length: Material consumption (PEX-a, PERT pipes or metal-plastic) per 1 m² of area.

Physics of the process: Calculation formulas

The heat transfer of the floor depends on the temperature difference between the coolant and the air, as well as on the thermal resistance of the floor “pie”.

Basic heat flow formula:

$$q = \frac{T_{avg} — T_{room}}{R_{total}}$$

Where:

  • $T_{avg}$ - average coolant temperature ( $\frac{T_{supply} + T_{return}}{2}$ ).
  • $T_{room}$ - desired air temperature.
  • $R_{total}$ is the total thermal resistance of the layers above the pipe.

Impact of flooring

This is a critical factor. Ceramic tiles are excellent conductors of heat, while laminate, parquet or carpet act as an insulator, reducing the power of the system.

Dependence of pipe flow on laying pitch

The pipe laying pitch affects not only the uniformity of heating (“zebra effect”), but also the cost of materials. The denser the installation, the more pipe will be required and the higher the hydraulic resistance of the circuit.

How to use the tool?

  1. Set temperatures: Enter the boiler flow temperature and the desired room temperature.
  2. Please indicate the construct: Select the pipe pitch, the thickness of the screed over the pipe and the type of finishing coating.
  3. Compare with heat loss: If you know the heat loss of your home (for example, 70 W/m²), enter it to check whether the heated floor can handle it alone or whether radiators will be required.
