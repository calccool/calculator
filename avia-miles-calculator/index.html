Calculation method and formulas

The system makes calculations based on three key vectors: flight distance, fare coefficient and bonuses for the elite level of participation in the program.

Calculation of accumulated miles for a flight

The basic logic is based on converting metric distances into air miles and using multipliers. The basic formula looks like this:

$$M_{total} = (D \cdot K_{mile} \cdot F_{mult}) + (D \cdot K_{mile} \cdot S_{bonus}) + \frac{C_{spend}}{C_{rate}}$$

Where:

  • $M_{total}$ - total number of miles;
  • $D$ — flight distance in kilometers;
  • $K_{mile}$ — conversion factor from km to miles (0.621371);
  • $F_{mult}$ - tariff multiplier (from 0.25 for Economy Promo to 2.00 for Business);
  • $S_{bonus}$ - status bonus (Silver, Gold, Platinum);
  • $C_{spend}$ — amount of spending on a co-branded card;
  • $C_{rate}$ - cost of one mile at the bank rate (60 $).

Minimum Miles Rule

It is important to take into account that when flying short distances (for example, Moscow - St. Petersburg), the “minimum accrual” rule comes into force. If the calculated value of $D \cdot K_{mile} \cdot F_{mult}$ is less than 500, the system automatically rounds the base accrual to 500 miles.

Elite Level Privileges

Your status in the loyalty program directly affects the rate of accumulation. The calculator takes into account the following frequent flyer bonuses:

  • Silver status (Silver): +25% bonus miles from the distance traveled.
  • Gold status: +50% to accruals, opens access to business lounges.
  • Platinum status: maximum +75% miles per segment.

Spending planning: how to calculate an award ticket

In the “Spending and Balance” tab, you can assess the real availability of the desired flight. The system not only calculates the difference between the current balance and the cost of the ticket, but also visualizes the progress.

Accounting for taxes and fees

Remember that there are no “free” tickets. Only the fare (Airfare) is paid with miles, while fuel, airport and service fees are always paid in rubles.

$$Extra_{cash} = Taxes + Fees$$

Use the “Ticket Fees” field to see the full cost of the trip in rubles in addition to the miles written off. If the progress bar is not completely filled, the calculator will instantly show the exact number of miles that need to be accumulated.
