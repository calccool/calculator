Loan refinancing calculator: Calculation of real benefits and payback

Refinancing (or refinancing) is a powerful financial tool that allows you to pay off your current debt by obtaining a new loan on more favorable terms. However, lowering the interest rate does not always guarantee real savings.

Our Refinancing efficiency calculator designed to provide an in-depth analysis of your situation. Unlike simple banking widgets, it takes into account hidden fees, processing costs and a Cash Out feature.

How does the calculator work?

To get an accurate forecast, you will need data on the current loan and the terms offered by the new bank. The algorithm conducts a comparative analysis of two cash flows and determines the feasibility of the transaction.

Key analysis indicators:

  • Net savings: The difference between what you would pay under the old schedule and the full cost of the new loan.
  • Break-even point: The period (in months) after which savings on monthly payments will cover the costs of drawing up a new contract (appraisal, insurance, commissions).
  • Cash Out: The opportunity to receive an additional amount of cash in hand beyond the closure of the old debt.

The mathematics of refinancing

The monthly annuity payment is calculated using the classic financial formula:

$$A = S \cdot \frac{i \cdot (1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $A$ - monthly payment;
  • $S$ — amount of the loan body (debt balance + Cash Out + commissions, if they are included in the body);
  • $i$ - monthly interest rate ( $\frac{\text{годовая ставка}}{12 \cdot 100}$ );
  • $n$ — loan term in months.

Break-even point calculation

Many people forget that refinancing costs money. Real estate valuation, notary services or issuance fee amount to closing costs ( $C$ ). The breakeven point ( $T_{be}$ ) shows when you will start to really save:

$$T_{be} = \frac{C}{\Delta P}$$

Where $\Delta P$ is the difference between the old and new monthly payment. If $T_{be}$ exceeds the term of the new loan, refinancing is not financially feasible, even at a lower rate.

Factors Affecting Benefits

When using the calculator, pay attention to the following fields:

  1. Remaining debt: Enter exactly the current balance of the principal debt (can be viewed in the bank’s application), and not the original loan amount.
  2. Add. expenses: Be sure to include expenses for insurance, appraisals and certificates. If you plan to include them in the body of the loan, check the “Include expenses in the loan” checkbox - this will increase the monthly payment, but will reduce the burden “here and now”.
  3. Term: Increasing the term of a new loan always leads to an increase in the final overpayment, even if the rate is significantly lower. Try to keep the remaining term the same or reduce it.
