Theory: Law of Conservation of Mass

The calculator is based on the law of conservation of mass (Lomonosov-Lavoisier law). It states that the mass of substances that entered into a reaction is equal to the mass of substances formed as a result of the reaction.

This means that the number of atoms of each element on the left and right sides of the equation must be the same.

Balancing example

Consider the combustion reaction of methane:

$$CH_4 + O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + H_2O$$

  • Left: 1 carbon (C), 4 hydrogen (H), 2 oxygen (O).
  • Right: 1 carbon (C), 2 hydrogen (H), 3 oxygen (O).

The balance is broken. To equalize the reaction, it is necessary to add stoichiometric coefficients:

$$CH_4 + 2O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2H_2O$$

Now the number of atoms is the same (C: 1, H: 4, O: 4).
