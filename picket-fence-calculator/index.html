Picket fence calculator: calculating the quantity and cost of a fence online

Fence construction begins with precise design. Euro picket fence (metal picket fence) is a modern choice that combines the durability of metal with the aesthetics of traditional fencing. To avoid unnecessary expenses on excess material or delays due to its shortage, it is necessary to take into account not only the length of the perimeter, but also the technical parameters of a particular profile.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate material specifications based on three key factors: site geometry, the width of the selected panel and the desired degree of privacy (clearance).

Installation methods and their features

When planning a fence, it is important to decide on the type of filling of the sections. Material consumption directly depends on this:

  • One-sided filling: the classic version, where pickets are attached to one side of the log with a certain clearance. Provides excellent ventilation of the area.
  • Double-sided filling (Chess): profiles are installed on both sides in a checkerboard pattern. This creates an almost blank wall for direct views, but keeps air flowing at an angle.

Mathematical calculation model

To obtain accurate data, the calculator uses a system of equations that takes into account the conversion of units of measurement (meters to millimeters) and rounding up for multiples of whole units of production.

Basic formulas included in the algorithm:

  1. Installation step calculation ( $S$ ): $$S = w + g$$ where $w$ is the width of the picket (mm), $g$ is the selected gap (mm).
  2. Number of pickets for one-sided fence ( $N_1$ ): $$N_1 = \lceil \frac{L \cdot 1000}{S} \rceil$$ where $L$ is the length of the fence in meters.
  3. Number of pickets for “Chess” ( $N_2$ ): $$N_2 = N_1 \cdot 2$$
  4. Total footage ( $M$ ): $$M = N \cdot h$$ where $h$ is the height of one picket fence in meters.

Instructions for using the calculator

To obtain correct data, enter values ​​in the appropriate fields of the interface:

  1. Length and height: Specify the total length of the fence and the desired height of the slats.
  2. Profile width: Typically 100, 110 or 120 mm depending on the manufacturer.
  3. Gap: If you want a “blind” checker board, choose a gap slightly smaller than the width of the picket fence (for example, 70 mm with a width of 110 mm).
  4. Price: Enter the cost per 1 linear meter to see the budget for the purchase of metal.

After pressing the button "Calculate" the system will provide a detailed specification, including the total number of pieces, total footage and financial estimate. You can copy the results in one click to send to the supplier.
