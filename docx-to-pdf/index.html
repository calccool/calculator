Technical details of PDF generation

The conversion occurs in two stages: first, the DOCX archive (which is essentially a ZIP container with XML files) is unpacked and converted into semantic HTML code. This code is then rendered into a graphics format and packaged into a PDF container.

To ensure ideal print quality on a standard A4 sheet (210 × 297 mm), the converter calculates the pixel density using the formula for converting millimeters to pixels at a given DPI (Dots Per Inch):

$W_{px} = \frac{W_{mm} \times DPI}{25.4}$

At the standard resolution for high-quality documents in $96$ DPI, the base width of the rendering container is calculated as follows:

$W_{px} = \frac{210 \times 96}{25.4} \approx 794 \text{ px}$

This is why the algorithm strictly limits the width of the virtual canvas to $794$ pixels. This ensures that fonts and tables don't float, and that line breaks in the resulting PDF match exactly how the document appears on your editor's screen.

Complex formatting support

Often during conversion the borders of tables are lost or the proportions of pictures are distorted. Special CSS rules have been introduced into the core of our tool, which forcefully normalize tables (border-collapse: collapse) and scale the images so that they do not extend beyond the boundaries of the printed sheet. The result is a professionally designed PDF document, ready to print or send to partners.
