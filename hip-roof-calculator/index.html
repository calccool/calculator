Hip roof calculator: Accurate calculation of the rafter system and roof area

A hip roof is a classic hipped structure consisting of two trapezoidal slopes (facades) and two triangular slopes (hips). Unlike a gable roof, there are no gables here, which significantly reduces the wind load, but complicates the calculation of the rafter system.

Our algorithm uses trigonometric formulas to construct ideal geometry, minimizing waste of roofing materials (metal tiles, ondulin or soft roofing).

Geometric parameters and formulas

For accurate calculations, we decompose the complex 3D shape of the roof into simple planes. Basic calculations are based on the Pythagorean theorem and the properties of isosceles triangles.

1. Calculation of ridge height (H):

The height of the roof depends on the width of the house and the angle of inclination of the slopes. This is a key parameter that determines the volume of attic space.

$$H = \frac{W}{2} \times \tan(\alpha)$$

Where:

  • W — width of the building (smaller side).
  • α - angle of inclination of the rafters.

2. Ridge purlin length (L_ridge):

In a classic hip roof, the ridge is shorter than the length of the building.

$$L_{ridge} = L — W$$

If $L = W$ , the roof becomes a hipped roof (pyramid) and the ridge length is zero.

3. Calculation of roof area (S):

The total surface area is calculated as the sum of the areas of two trapezoids and two triangles, taking into account the eaves overhangs. For a quick estimate, use the projection formula:

$$S_{roof} = \frac{S_{base}}{\cos(\alpha)}$$

Where $S_{base}$ is the area of ​​the base of the house including overhangs.

The choice of inclination angle ( $\alpha$ ) is critical for snow melting and joint tightness. An incorrect angle can lead to leaks or sheets blown off by the wind.

Elements of the rafter system

When purchasing lumber, it is important to distinguish between the types of rafters, since they carry different loads. The calculator separates them for an accurate estimate:

  1. Central rafters: They rest on the mauerlat and ridge beam. Their length determines the height of the slope.
  2. Sloping (diagonal) rafters: The longest and most loaded elements are those running from the corners of the house to the ridge. Often require a double beam.
  3. Narozhniki (short rafters): They rest on diagonal legs. Their length decreases as they approach the corner.

How to use the calculator

To obtain correct data, follow the instructions:

  • House dimensions: Enter the dimensions along the outer boundaries of the walls (house boxes).
  • Overhang (cornice): The standard overhang is 50–80 cm. It protects the foundation and walls from slanting rain. Increasing the overhang significantly affects the final roof area.
  • Rafter pitch: Distance between beams. For insulation of standard width (600 mm), the recommended pitch is 58–60 cm (clean).
  • Load: The “Roof Weight” result shows only the weight of the roofing itself. To fully calculate the foundation, you need to add the snow load of your region (for the middle zone ~180 kg/m²) and the wind load.

Advice: When purchasing materials, always add a 10-15% allowance for trims and laps, especially for hip roofs where there is a lot of trimming around the ridges.
