Antiscalant dosage calculator: Calculation for reverse osmosis (RO) systems

The efficiency of a reverse osmosis (RO) system directly depends on the condition of the membrane elements. The main threat to membranes is deposits of poorly soluble salts (calcium carbonates, sulfates, silicates), which block pores and reduce productivity (Permeate flux).

To prevent this process it is used antiscalant (sedimentation inhibitor). However, it is critically important not only to choose the reagent, but also to dose it correctly. A deficiency will lead to “overgrowth” of the membranes, and an excess will lead to biofouling and unnecessary financial costs.

Our calculator allows you to accurately calculate the settings of the dosing pump, taking into account the density of the reagent and the parameters of your water treatment system.

Principle of calculation of chemical dosing

The calculation is based on mass balance: the amount of inhibitor supplied must correspond to the volume of incoming water (Feed Flow) and the target concentration (Dosage ppm).

Unlike simplified calculators, our algorithm takes into account specific gravity antiscalant. This is important, since industrial reagents are heavier than water (density 1.1–1.45 g/cm³), and ignoring this factor results in an error of up to 20-30% when preparing a solution.

Basic formulas

To understand the process, below are the formulas used in our calculation.

1. Mass flow of pure substance

Determines how many grams of reagent should enter the system every hour.

$$Q_{mass} = Q_{feed} \times D_{target}$$

Where:

  • $$Q_{mass}$$ - Mass flow (g/h).
  • $$Q_{feed}$$ — Source water flow (m³/hour).
  • $$D_{target}$$ - Target dose (ppm or g/m³).

2. Calculation of pump performance (volume flow)

Since the antiscalant is often diluted in a tank (Day Tank), the pump pumps not a pure reagent, but a solution.

$$V_{pump} = \frac{Q_{mass}}{C_{\%} \times 10 \times SG_{sol}}$$

Where:

  • $$V_{pump}$$ - Pump setting (l/hour).
  • $$C_{\%}$$ — Solution concentration in the tank (%).
  • $$SG_{sol}$$ — Density of the finished solution (g/cm³).

Recommendations for preparing the solution

  1. Concentration in tank: It is usually recommended to prepare a solution with a concentration 10-20%. The use of too weak solutions (<1%) is dangerous for bacterial contamination of the tank, since the biocidal properties of the concentrate are lost with strong dilution.
  2. Water quality: To dilute the antiscalant, use only permeate (purified water) or distillate. Using raw water will cause sediment to form directly in the dosing tank.
  3. Storage time: It is recommended to update the prepared solution in the Day Tank every 3-7 days to avoid hydrolysis of the active components and loss of effectiveness.
