Online box volume calculator: calculation in m³, liters and kg

Calculating the volume of a rectangular container or box is a basic task that occurs in logistics, construction and everyday life. Our online calculator allows you to instantly obtain data not only on physical capacity, but also on parameters critical to the transportation of goods.

To calculate the volume, you need to know three parameters: length (L), width (W) And height (H).

Calculation formulas

To obtain an accurate result, the following mathematical relationships are used:

  • Volume ( $V$ ): Calculated as the product of three sides. $$V = L \cdot W \cdot H$$
  • Surface Area ( $S$ ): The sum of the areas of all six faces of the box. This value is necessary to calculate the amount of packaging material or cardboard. $$S = 2 \cdot (L \cdot W + W \cdot H + L \cdot H)$$
  • Volumetric weight ( $VW$ ): Used by transport companies to determine the cost of transporting light but large cargo. $$VW = \frac{L \cdot W \cdot H}{k}$$ where $k$ is the divisor (coefficient) of the logistics service (usually 4000, 5000 or 6000).

Volumetric weight: what is it and why is it needed?

In the field of logistics, there is the concept of “paid weight”. Airlines and ground carriers (such as DHL, FedEx or Nova Poshta) compare actual weight parcels from her volumetric weight. Payment is made at the higher of these values.

Why is this important?

If you're shipping a large box of lightweight padding, it takes up a lot of space on your truck or plane, crowding out other cargo. That is why carriers introduced a density factor. Using our calculator will help you find out in advance:

  1. How much volume does your cargo take up in the vehicle?
  2. What area of ​​cardboard needs to be purchased to make packaging.
  3. Will a tariff for “extra dimensions” be applied to your cargo?

Divider coefficients

  • 5000 - standard for most international land and sea transport.
  • 4000 — often used in express air delivery.
  • 6000 — used by specialized services for large-size or economy transportation.
