Online ROI and ROMI calculator: calculating profitability and return on marketing

In modern business, decisions made “by eye” lead to losses. To understand whether a project is making money or simply burning the budget, profitability metrics are used. This online calculator allows you to instantly calculate three key indicators: ROI, ROMI And ROAS.

  • Budget optimization: The ability to stop unprofitable campaigns in time and scale profitable ones.
  • Forecasting: A basis for planning future investments based on solid data.
  • Transparency: A clear division between revenue, “dirty” profit and net income.

Basic formulas for calculating profitability

Our tool uses classical economic formulas, which are considered the “gold standard” of financial analysis.

ROI (Return on Investment) formula

This indicator evaluates the total return on all investments in a project or business.

$$ROI = \frac{\text{Доход} — \text{Все затраты}}{\text{Все затраты}} \times 100\%$$

ROMI (Return on Marketing Investment) formula

A metric focused exclusively on marketing activities. It takes into account the cost of the product to show the real return on advertising.

$$ROMI = \frac{\text{Доход от рекламы} — \text{Маркетинговые расходы} — \text{Себестоимость}}{\text{Маркетинговые расходы}} \times 100\%$$

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) formula

Shows the gross effectiveness of advertising expenditures without taking into account production costs and operating expenses.

$$ROAS = \frac{\text{Доход от рекламы}}{\text{Расходы на рекламу}} \times 100\%$$

Interpretation of results

What do these numbers mean for your business?

  • Above 0%: The project is paying off. You earn more than you spend.
  • Exactly 0%: Break even. You work “to zero”, investments are returned, but there is no profit.
  • Below 0%: The business is unprofitable. Each ruble invested brings in less than a ruble of revenue. The cost structure or sales strategy needs to be reconsidered.

For ROAS, a good indicator in retail is considered to be between 400% and higher, but in high-margin services this threshold can be significantly lower.
