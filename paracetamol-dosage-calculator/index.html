Paracetamol dosage calculator: Calculation of suspension and syrup by weight

When treating fever and pain, safety is key. dosing accuracy. Instructions for medications often offer average values ​​“by age,” but pediatricians around the world insist: it is necessary to strictly calculate antipyretic drugs by patient's weight. A child of the same age can weigh either 15 or 25 kg, which requires completely different amounts of the active substance.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate a safe single and daily dose of paracetamol in liquid form (syrups, suspensions, solutions), adapted to the specific weight and concentration of the drug.

Gold standard calculation: 10–15 mg per kilogram

In international medical practice, a safe therapeutic corridor for paracetamol (Acetaminophen) has been adopted. It ensures the effectiveness of the drug without the risk of toxic liver damage.

  • Single dose: 10 to 15 mg per kilogram of body weight.
  • Daily dose: no more than 60 mg per kg of weight (total over 24 hours).
  • Interval: at least 4–6 hours between doses.

Formula for calculating liquid volume

To find out how many milliliters (ml) of syrup to put into the dispenser syringe, you need to take into account the concentration of the drug. Different manufacturers produce syrups with different contents of the substance (regular or Forte).

The mathematical model of our calculator uses the following formula:

$$V_{мл} = \frac{M_{пациента} \times D_{целевая}}{C_{препарата}}$$

Where:

  • $$M$$ — Patient weight (kg).
  • $$D$$ - Recommended dose (10-15 mg/kg).
  • $$C$$ — Concentration of the selected syrup (mg in 1 ml).

Typical concentrations of drugs on the market

Before paying, be sure to check the packaging of your medicine. The most common formats are:

  1. 120 mg / 5 ml — Classic children's syrups (Panadol, Efferalgan, Kalpol).
  2. 250 mg / 5 ml — Strengthened formula (usually marked “Forte” or for children over 6 years old).
  3. 100 mg/1 ml — Concentrated suspensions and drops for the little ones.

Important Safety Warnings

Paracetamol is considered one of the safest analgesics and antipyretics, but only if the rules of administration are followed. Exceeding the dose may lead to severe hepatotoxicity (liver damage).

Observe the following rules:

  • Maximum for adults: Even if the patient's weight exceeds 70-80 kg, a single dose should never exceed 1000 mg (1 gram), and daily - 4000 mg (4 grams). Our calculator automatically takes this limitation (“ceiling effect”) into account.
  • Do not mix: Do not give your child multiple medications containing paracetamol (such as syrup and combination cold powders) at the same time to avoid overdose.
  • Measure accurately: Use only the measuring syringe or spoon included in the kit. Teaspoons come in different sizes and are not suitable for dosing medications.
