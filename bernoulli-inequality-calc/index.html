Bernoulli inequality calculator: online proof and visualization

Bernoulli's inequality is a fundamental theorem in mathematical analysis that establishes the relationship between a power function and a linear relationship. Our interactive calculator allows you not only to check the truth of an inequality for given values, but also to clearly see the difference (delta) between the left and right sides of the expression.

This tool is indispensable for students studying limits and series, as well as for engineers using methods for linearizing complex functions. Unlike standard calculators, we provide visual comparison of quantities and automatic analysis of convergence conditions.

Formula and conditions of use

In its classical form, the inequality is written as follows:

$$(1 + x)^n \ge 1 + nx$$

Where variables must satisfy the following strict conditions:

  • Base: $x \ge -1$ (with $x < -1$ and non-integer $n$ the expression may not make sense in real numbers).
  • Indicator: $n$ - any real number.

Features of inequality behavior

The sign of the inequality depends on the exponent $n$ . The calculator automatically takes into account these nuances:

  1. If $n \ge 1$ or $n \le 0$ , then the inequality is satisfied in the standard form: $(1+x)^n \ge 1+nx$ .
  2. If $0 < n < 1$ , the inequality sign is reversed: $(1+x)^n \le 1+nx$ .
  3. Equality is achieved only in two cases: if $x = 0$ or $n = 1$ .

How to use the calculator

To get instant results, follow a simple algorithm:

  1. Enter value x to the first field. Be aware of the $x \ge -1$ limitation.
  2. Please indicate your degree n. This can be either an integer (for example, 5) or a fraction (for example, 2.5 or 0.5 for roots).
  3. Click the button "Compare".
  4. Examine the result:
    • LHS (Left Hand Side): The exact value of the power function.
    • RHS (Right Hand Side): Linear approximation value.
    • Difference (Δ): Shows the approximation error.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Can you use fractional powers?

Yes, the inequality is true for any real indicators. Our calculator handles fractional powers correctly (for example, $n=0.5$ for square roots), automatically detecting the direction of the inequality sign.

Why should $x$ be greater than -1?

The $x \ge -1$ constraint is necessary because at $x < -1$ the power base of $(1+x)$ becomes negative. Raising a negative number to a fractional power (such as in $1/2$ ) in a real number field is not possible.

How does inequality relate to the number e?

Bernoulli's inequality is a key tool in proving that the sequence $(1 + 1/n)^n$ is increasing and bounded, leading to the definition of the Euler number ( $e$ ).
