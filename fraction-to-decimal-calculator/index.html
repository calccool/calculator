How to convert a fraction to a decimal?

Fractions are used in everything from culinary recipes (“1/2 cup sugar”) to construction drawings and stock quotes. However, entering data into a computer, accounting programs, or simply comparing values ​​often requires decimal format (for example, 0.5 instead of 1/2).

Our online converter performs this conversion automatically and instantly. The tool supports two types of input:

  1. Common fractions: Kind of $a/b$ (for example, $3/4$ ).
  2. Mixed numbers: Kind of $c \frac{a}{b}$ (for example, $1 \frac{5}{8}$ ).

Conversion formula

The principle of translation is very simple. In mathematics, the fraction bar literally means the division sign. To convert a fraction to a decimal number, you need divide the numerator by the denominator.

Basic formula:

$$\frac{A}{B} = A \div B$$

Example:

$$\frac{3}{4} = 3 \div 4 = 0.75$$

For mixed numbers

If you have an integer part (mixed fraction), the algorithm changes a little. First you need to convert the fractional part to decimal form, and then add (or subtract if the number is negative) the whole part.

Formula for mixed numbers:

$$W \frac{A}{B} = W + (A \div B)$$

Example:

$$2 \frac{1}{8} = 2 + (1 \div 8) = 2 + 0.125 = 2.125$$
