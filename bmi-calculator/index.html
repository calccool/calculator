Body Mass Index (BMI) – Calculation and weight standards

What is BMI and why calculate it?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a key indicator used to assess whether a person’s weight is appropriate for their height. BMI does not directly measure body fat, but is the simplest and most convenient tool for determining the potential health risks associated with being under or overweight.

BMI calculation is necessary to:

  • Determine if your weight is within the range healthy norm.
  • Assess the risks of developing diseases (diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular pathologies).
  • Monitor weight dynamics while dieting or exercising.

Formula for calculating BMI

Body Mass Index is calculated as the ratio of a person's weight in kilograms to the square of his height in meters.

The formula looks like this:

$$\text{ИМТ} = \frac{\text{Вес (кг)}}{\text{Рост (м)}^2}$$

Calculation example: If your weight is 75 kg and your height is 175 cm (1.75 m), the calculation will be as follows:

$$\text{ИМТ} = \frac{75}{1.75 \times 1.75} \approx \frac{75}{3.0625} \approx 24.5$$

This value corresponds to the Normal body weight category.

WHO BMI classification

For adults (over 18 years of age), standardized ranges are used that do not depend on gender. They help to assign the resulting value to one of the categories.

Limitations of the BMI calculator

It is important to understand that BMI is just a statistical indicator. It does not take into account body composition (the ratio of muscles, fat and bones), which is critical in the following cases:

  1. Athletes and bodybuilders: High muscle mass can result in a BMI greater than 25, incorrectly classifying a person as “Overweight” even though their body fat percentage is low.
  2. Elderly people: With age, muscle mass decreases, and a person can have a “Normal BMI”, but at the same time have an excess percentage of fat, which is also dangerous.
  3. Children and teenagers: For them, not general ranges are used, but special ones percentile curves, taking into account gender and exact age.

Ideal weight: calculation and range

Our calculator not only shows your current BMI but also calculates your range normal weight (with a BMI from 18.5 to 25), and also Ideal Weight (IW) according to Robinson's formula, which gives a more accurate estimate for adults, taking into account gender.

Ideal Weight (IW) using Robinson's formula it is calculated as follows (only for heights over 152.4 cm):

  • For men: $52 \text{ кг} + 1.9 \times (\frac{\text{Рост (см)} — 152.4}{2.54})$
  • For women: $49 \text{ кг} + 1.7 \times (\frac{\text{Рост (см)} — 152.4}{2.54})$
