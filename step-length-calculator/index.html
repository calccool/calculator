How to find out your stride length?

Knowing your own stride length is the basis for accurately tracking your physical activity. Although fitness bands and pedometers try to do this automatically, they often make the mistake of using average factory settings. Our online calculator allows you to obtain personal data based on your height, gender and type of activity.

This is necessary for:

  • Smartwatch and pedometer settings to improve accuracy.
  • Planning walking tours and tourist routes.
  • Calculation of the distance traveled if there is no GPS at hand.

Formula for calculating step length

The length of a person's step is anatomically related to his height. There are proven statistics that allow you to calculate this parameter with high accuracy without the need to take measurements with a tape measure.

The basic formula looks like this:

$$Step\ Length = Height \times k$$

Where $k$ is a coefficient depending on gender and traffic intensity:

  • For a relaxed walk: $k \approx 0.39$ (women) and $0.41$ (men).
  • For medium tempo: $k \approx 0.413$ (women) and $0.415$ (men).
  • For running: The coefficient increases significantly and can reach $1.15$ and higher.

How many steps are there in 1 kilometer?

One of the most popular questions: “How far does it take to cover a kilometer?”. The answer directly depends on your anthropometry.

For a person of average height (170-175 cm), the step length is about 70-72 cm.

To calculate the number of steps per distance, the formula is used:

$$Steps = \frac{Distance\ (m)}{Step\ Length\ (m)}$$

Calculation examples:

  • 1 km (1000 m): $\approx 1400$ steps.
  • 5 km: $\approx 7000$ steps.
  • 10 km: $\approx 14000$ steps.

The legendary “10,000 steps”

A goal of 10,000 steps per day is considered the “gold standard” for maintaining cardiovascular health. But what is this distance?

Using our calculator you will see that for a short person this is approx. 6.5 km, and for high - almost 8 kilometers. That is why it is important to focus not only on the abstract number on the pedometer screen, but also on the real distance traveled, which our tool will help calculate.
