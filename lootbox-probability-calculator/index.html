Loot box probability calculator: calculating the chance of an item dropping in N attempts

Many players fall into the "gambler's fallacy" trap of thinking that if the chance of an item dropping is 1%, then after 100 open cases, the item is guaranteed to fall out. In reality, mathematics works differently. Each box is an independent event, and the overall probability of success is calculated through the probability of “failure.”

Our calculator uses the binomial distribution formula to determine the cumulative chance. It helps you understand how “lucky” you are in Genshin Impact, CS:GO, Dota 2 or any other game with Gacha mechanics.

Basic calculation formula

To find the probability of dropping at least one item in a series of trials, we first calculate the probability that the item will never fall out, and subtract this value from unity.

$$P_{total} = 1 — (1 — p)^n$$

Where:

  • $P_{total}$ - final probability of success (from 0 to 1).
  • $p$ is the base chance of an item dropping in one attempt (in decimal form).
  • $n$ - the number of open loot boxes or completed “spins”.

Why does the chance never reach 100%?

Mathematically, while the base chance is less 100%, the possibility of failure will always exist, even if it is negligible. This is why experienced players aim for the “confidence threshold” - usually this 95% or 99%.

How to use this calculator

The Gold Standard 2 interface offers two modes of operation for an in-depth analysis of your chances:

  1. Chance in N attempts: Enter the base chance from the chest description and the amount of your savings. You will see a visual graph (donut chart) showing your position relative to other players.
  2. Finding the number of attempts: If you want to receive an item with a specific certainty (eg. 90% ), select this mode. The calculator will tell you exactly how many resources you need to accumulate.
  3. Expected Value (EV): Pay attention to the EV indicator. This is a mathematical expectation indicating the average number of attempts it takes to get 1 item in the long term.

Frequently asked questions about odds in loot boxes

  • What is “Soft Guarantor”? In some games (eg Genshin Impact), chance is not linear. After a certain attempt, $p$ 's base chance begins to increase. Our calculator does "pure" math if you don't take into account the hidden mechanics of the game.
  • Why, with a 1% chance, in 100 attempts I got only 63.4%? This is the standard distribution. Near 36.6% players in such a situation will be left with nothing - this is “bad luck statistics”.
  • Is it possible to cheat the system? No, if the random number generator (RNG) on the server side works correctly. The only way is a mathematical calculation of risks.
