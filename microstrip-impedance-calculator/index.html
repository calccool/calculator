Microstrip Line Impedance Calculator: Z₀ and Delay Calculation (Online)

Calculation of characteristic impedance of tracks on a printed circuit board (PCB)

When designing high-speed digital circuits and RF (RF) devices, impedance control is a critical factor. Microstrip Line - This is the most common type of transmission line, which is a conductor of a certain width located above the Ground Plane and separated from it by a dielectric.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly calculate the characteristic impedance ( $Z_0$ ), as well as linear capacitance, inductance and signal delay time.

Input parameters for calculation

The accuracy of the calculations depends on the correct input of the physical parameters of your board. Use data from the datasheet of your PCB manufacturer (for example, FR-4 or Rogers).

Calculation methods: IPC-2141 vs Hammerstad

Our tool supports two algorithms, covering the needs for both fast estimation and accurate modeling.

1. IPC-2141 standard

This is the classic, simplified formula recommended by the Electronics Manufacturers Association. It provides sufficient accuracy for most standard applications where the $0.1 < w/h < 2.0$ .

$$Z_0 = \frac{87}{\sqrt{\varepsilon_r + 1.41}} \ln \left( \frac{5.98 \cdot h}{0.8 \cdot w + t} \right)$$

2. Hammerstad & Jensen method

A more complex and accurate algorithm. It takes into account frequency dispersion and effective dielectric constant ( $\varepsilon_{eff}$ ). We recommend using this method for high frequency circuits and cases where the trace width ( $w$ ) is significantly greater than the dielectric height ( $h$ ).

This method also calculates Effective dielectric constant ( $\varepsilon_{eff}$ ), since part of the electric field propagates in the dielectric, and part in the air.

Additional output

In addition to impedance, the calculator provides important data for Signal Integrity:

  • Propagation Delay ( $T_{pd}$ ): The time it takes a signal to travel per unit length of conductor. Critical for length matching on DDR buses or differential pairs.
  • Linear Inductance ( $L_0$ ) and Capacitance ( $C_0$ ): Parameters required for SPICE simulation of a transmission line.

$$T_{pd} = \frac{\sqrt{\varepsilon_{eff}}}{c}$$

(where $c$ is the speed of light in vacuum)
