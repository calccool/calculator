The mathematics of code optimization

The use of preprocessors not only improves readability, but also reduces the physical amount of developer source code before building. The efficiency of converting a flat cascade into a nested SCSS structure can be expressed through the redundancy reduction function:

$$E_{scss} = 1 — \frac{V_c + \sum_{i=1}^{n} (L_i \cdot \delta_i)}{\sum_{j=1}^{m} S_j}$$

Where $E_{scss}$ is the structure optimization factor, $V_c$ is the volume of variables removed, $L_i$ is the length of the unique selector at nesting depth $\delta_i$ , and $S_j$ is the length of the original flat CSS rule. Due to the mathematical reduction of repeating parent classes, the actual volume of developer sources is reduced by 20-40%.
