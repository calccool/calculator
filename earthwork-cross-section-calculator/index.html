Cross-sectional area calculator: Calculation of excavation volumes by coordinates

When designing linear structures (roads, trenches, canals) and vertical site planning, it is critically important to accurately calculate the volume of earth masses. The basis of these calculations is the determination cross-sectional area on pickets.

Traditional methods (palettes, planimeters) are a thing of the past. Our excavation calculator uses the analytical coordinate method, allowing you to instantly obtain the exact area of ​​any complex figure - from a simple trapezoid of a roadway to a complex profile of a river or pit.

How the Coordinate Method Works (Shoelace Formula)

The calculator algorithm is based on Gauss area formula (in English literature - Shoelace formula or Surveyor’s formula). This method allows you to find the area of ​​any closed polygon if the Cartesian coordinates ( $X, Y$ ) of its vertices are known.

The principle of operation is to sum the products of the coordinates of neighboring vertices. This eliminates errors of graphic methods and the human factor.

Mathematical formula

For a polygon with $n$ vertices whose coordinates are ( $x_1, y_1$ ), ( $x_2, y_2$ ), ..., ( $x_n, y_n$ ), the area of ​​ $A$ is calculated as follows:

$$A = \frac{1}{2} | \sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i y_{i+1} — x_{i+1} y_i) |$$

Where:

  • $x_i, y_i$ — coordinates of the current point.
  • $x_{i+1}, y_{i+1}$ - coordinates of the next point (for the last point, the next is the first).

Instructions for use

Our tool is designed taking into account the specifics of the work of technical equipment engineers and surveyors.

  1. Unit selection: Switch between meters (for construction drawings) or feet (for international projects).
  2. Entering coordinates: Enter coordinate pairs $X$ (horizontal distance) and $Y$ (elevation) for each terrain breaking point.
  3. Subsequence: It is important to enter points sequentially around the section contour (clockwise or counterclockwise). Chaotic input will lead to self-crossing of lines (“butterfly effect”) and incorrect calculation.
  4. Visual control: A drawing of your section is automatically created to the right of the input table. If you make a mistake with one of the coordinates, you will immediately see it on the diagram.

Application in construction

This calculator is indispensable for solving the following problems:

  • Volume calculation: Determination of the area of ​​the excavation (cut) and embankment (fill) on the cross-sections for further calculation of the volume of soil using the average area method.
  • Hydraulics: Calculation of the living cross-section of a river bed or canal.
  • Landscape design: Determining the area of ​​lawns or paving of complex geometric shapes.
  • Cadastre: Clarification of the area of ​​the land plot according to the coordinates of boundary signs.
