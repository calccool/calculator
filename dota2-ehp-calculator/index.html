Dota 2 EHP Calculator: Effective Health Calculator (Armor & Evasion)

In Dota 2, Health (HP) is just the tip of the iceberg. Experienced players focus on Effective Health Points (EHP) - the amount of damage that must be inflicted on the enemy In fact to send your hero to the tavern.

EHP is directly related to your defensive performance. For example, with 1000 HP and high armor, you can survive 2000 physical damage. In this case, your physical EHP is 2000. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate these values ​​for any game situation.

How the mechanics of armor and resistance work

Each point of armor increases your physical EHP by 6% of your base health. This is a linear relationship that makes purchasing armor relevant at any stage of the game. Magic resistance works the same way, but it is calculated multiplicatively.

Calculation formulas

To understand how our calculator produces results, here are the basic mathematical formulas used in the current Dota 2 patch:

  1. Damage Reduction: $$DR = \frac{0.06 \times \text{Armor}}{1 + 0.06 \times |\text{Armor}|}$$
  2. Physical EHP with Evasion: $$EHP_{\text{phys}} = \frac{HP}{(1 — DR) \times (1 — \text{Evasion})}$$
  3. Magic EHP: $$EHP_{\text{mag}} = \frac{HP}{1 — \text{MagicRes}}$$

Advanced Options: Evasion

The calculator has an “Advanced mode” that allows you to include in the calculation Evasion. Dodging does not reduce the damage taken, but gives you a chance to avoid it completely.

  • Important: Dodge only works against physical attacks (right click).
  • Folding: If you have multiple sources of evasion (for example, talent and Butterfly), they add up according to the law of diminishing returns. Our calculator allows you to enter the final percentage from the game interface.
  • Counterpick: Remember that items like Monkey King Bar (MKB) or Bloodthorn can ignore your evasion, turning your EHP back to base value.

Tips for increasing survivability

To make your hero as dense as possible, follow the rule of balance between HP and resists:

  • At an early stage: If you have little health (less than 1000), it is more effective to buy items for strength and HP (Bracer, Vitality Booster).
  • In mid game: When your health exceeds 1500, each point of armor begins to provide a huge increase in EHP. At this point, Platemail or Shiva’s Guard are relevant.
  • Against mages: If the main damage comes from abilities, your priority is Glimmer Cape, Pipe of Insight or Mage Slayer, since armor does not protect against magic procasts.
