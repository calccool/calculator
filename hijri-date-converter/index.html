Hijri Date Calculator: Islamic and Gregorian Calendar Converter

Islamic calendar known as Hijra, is a lunar calendar used by more than 1.5 billion Muslims around the world to determine the dates of religious events and holidays. Unlike the solar Gregorian calendar, which is based on the period of the Earth's revolution around the Sun, the Islamic year is based on the phases of the Moon.

Key differences of the lunar calendar

  • Duration of the year: The Islamic year is shorter than the solar year by approximately 11 days. It consists of 354 or 355 days.
  • Start of counting: The Hijri era begins in 622 AD, when the Prophet Muhammad moved from Mecca to Medina.
  • Start of the month: The new month traditionally begins with the first visual observation of the young crescent moon (neomenia).

Mathematical model and calculation formulas

Our calculator uses refined algorithms to synchronize the two systems. The main difficulty is the calculation Julian Day (JD), which serves as a universal bridge between calendars.

To convert the Hijri date to the Gregorian format, an approximation based on the average lunar cycle is used. The formula for determining the Julian day for a given Hijri date is as follows:

$$JD = \lfloor \frac{11y + 3}{30} \rfloor + 354y + 30m — \lfloor \frac{m — 1}{2} \rfloor + d + 1948440 — 385$$

Where:

  • $y$ - Hijri year;
  • $m$ - month number;
  • $d$ — day of the month;
  • $\lfloor \dots \rfloor$ - function for taking an integer part (rounding down).

Main Muslim holidays

The calculator's algorithm is configured to automatically recognize key dates that are of critical importance in Islam.

  • Day of Ashura (10 Muharram): Day of Remembrance of the Prophets and an important event in the history of Islam.
  • Eid al-Fitr (1 Shawwal): The holiday of breaking the fast, which ends the fast of the month of Ramadan.
  • Eid al-Fitr (10 Dhul-Hijjah): Great holiday of sacrifice.

Important Note: Since the actual start of the month in Islam depends on the sighting of the moon, the estimated date may differ by $\pm 1$ day from the official date announced in a particular country.
