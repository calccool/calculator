Cadence calculator: Calculate your cadence and cadence

Cadence is a measure of the intensity of your movements in a given unit of time. In cyclic sports it is a key marker of performance. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate this parameter based on a simple measurement in a short period of time.

Running Cadence (SPM)

For runners, cadence is measured in steps per minute (Steps Per Minute). A low cadence often leads to “overstrider”—moving your foot too far forward, which puts shock stress on your knee joints and slows down your progress.

Cycling cadence (RPM)

Cyclists measure cadence in crank revolutions per minute (Revolutions Per Minute). The correct pedaling rhythm allows you to balance between the load on the cardiovascular system and muscle tension, preventing premature muscle acidification.

How to use the cadence calculator

We have developed the most intuitive interface with real-time visualization. To get an accurate result, follow these steps:

  1. Select mode: Switch between Running and Cycling.
  2. Specify the measurement time: You can use the quick entry buttons (30 sec, 1 min) or enter a custom time.
  3. Enter the number of movements: Count how many steps or revolutions you took during this period.
  4. Single leg option: If while running you counted the touches of only one leg (for example, only the right one), simply activate the corresponding switch. The system will automatically multiply the result by two.

Calculation formulas

The calculator uses mathematically precise algorithms to calculate the total values:

$$Cadence = \frac{Count \times k}{T_{min} + \frac{T_{sec}}{60}}$$

Where:

  • Count — entered number of movements.
  • k — correction factor (equal to 2 if the “one leg” mode is enabled).
  • T — measurement time in minutes and seconds.

To obtain the frequency per second, the formula is used:

$$Frequency = \frac{Cadence}{60}$$
