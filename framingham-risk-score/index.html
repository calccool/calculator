Framingham Risk Score: Online cardiovascular disease prognosis calculator (10 years)

The Framingham Risk Score is the most authoritative and widely used tool in global cardiology for assessing the likelihood of developing serious cardiovascular events (CHD, myocardial infarction, coronary death) over the next 10 years.

The algorithm is based on data from the Framingham Heart Study, which has been continuously conducted since 1948. This calculator is adapted for primary diagnosis in patients aged 20 to 79 years who have not previously been diagnosed with heart disease.

Key factors influencing the prognosis

The accuracy of the forecast is ensured by multifactor analysis. Our calculator takes into account the following critical biomarkers and lifestyle parameters:

  • Age and Gender: The risk naturally increases with age, and is statistically higher at an earlier age in men.
  • Cholesterol: Both total cholesterol and high-density lipoprotein (HDL or “good” cholesterol) levels are taken into account. Low HDL levels are an independent risk factor.
  • Blood pressure: Systolic (upper) pressure is a marker of stress on blood vessels.
  • Treatment of hypertension: Taking drugs that artificially lower blood pressure is taken into account as a factor indicating the presence of pathology.
  • Smoking: One of the most aggressive factors damaging the vascular endothelium.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on the Cox regression model. For those interested in medical statistics, the formula for calculating the probability of $P$ is as follows:

$$P = 1 — S_0(t)^{exp(\sum \beta_i X_i — \sum \beta_i \bar{X}_i)}$$

Where:

  • $S_0(t)$ - basic survival (probability of no events with “ideal” indicators).
  • $\beta_i$ - regression coefficients for each risk factor (age, cholesterol, etc.).
  • $X_i$ - patient-specific risk factor values.
  • $\bar{X}_i$ - average values ​​of risk factors in the population.

The calculator automatically substitutes the necessary coefficients depending on the selected gender, which eliminates manual calculation errors.

How to reduce cardiovascular risk?

If the calculator shows a medium or high risk, lifestyle modification measures must be taken. It has been proven that reducing risk factors reduces the likelihood of a heart attack by 50-80%.

  1. Quitting smoking. The risk of coronary heart disease is reduced by 50% just one year after quitting tobacco.
  2. Lipid control. Aim for HDL levels >1.0 mmol/L in men and >1.3 mmol/L in women.
  3. Normalization of pressure. The target value is below 130/80 mmHg.
  4. Physical activity. A minimum of 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week.

Important: The results of this online calculator are for reference only. To make a diagnosis and prescribe treatment, be sure to consult a cardiologist.
