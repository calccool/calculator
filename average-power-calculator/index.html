Average power, mechanical work and time calculator

In physics and engineering, understanding the relationship between power, work and time is fundamental. Our online calculator is a universal tool that allows you not only to calculate the average power of a mechanism or person, but also to solve inverse problems: find the energy expended or the time required to complete the work.

Calculator features

This tool works in three modes, automatically adapting the interface to your task. It eliminates the need to manually convert hours to seconds or horsepower to watts.

  • Power calculation (P): If the work done and the time spent are known.
  • Work/energy calculation (A): Allows you to find out how much energy a device of a certain power will consume in a given time.
  • Time calculation (t): Determines how long a machine must run to complete a certain amount of work.

Basic formulas and physical meaning

Power is a scalar physical quantity that indicates the rate at which work is performed or the rate at which energy is transferred. In the International System of Units (SI), power is measured in Watts (W).

The basic formula for calculating average power is as follows:

$$P = \frac{A}{t}$$

Where:

  • $P$ (Power) - power (Watt).
  • $A$ (Work) - work or energy (Joule).
  • $t$ (Time) — time of work execution (second).

Based on this formula, we can derive equations for two other quantities:

Work formula

To find out the amount of energy expended, multiply the power by the time:

$$A = P \times t$$

Time formula

To find out the time required to do work at constant power:

$$t = \frac{A}{P}$$

Examples of problem solving

To better understand how the calculator works, let’s consider several typical life situations.

Example 1: Lifting a load (Mechanical)

Task: A crane lifts a concrete slab doing work $25\,000 \text{ Дж}$ . The rise was taken by $50 \text{ секунд}$ . What is the average crane engine power?

Solution:

We use the power formula:

$$P = \frac{25000}{50} = 500 \text{ Вт}$$

Answer: The engine develops a power of 500 Watts (or 0.5 kW).

Example 2: Energy Consumption (Electrical)

Task: The $2 \text{ кВт}$ power heater operated continuously during $3 \text{ часов}$ . How much energy did it consume?

Solution:

First, let's convert the units to the SI system (or use a calculator).

$2 \text{ кВт} = 2000 \text{ Вт}$

$3 \text{ часа} = 3 \times 3600 = 10\,800 \text{ секунд}$

$$A = 2000 \times 10800 = 21\,600\,000 \text{ Дж}$$

In more conventional electricity bills this is:

$$A = 2 \text{ кВт} \times 3 \text{ ч} = 6 \text{ кВт}\cdot\text{ч}$$

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between work and energy?

In the context of this calculator, these concepts are interchangeable. Energy is the body's ability to do work. When work is done, energy is transferred from one form to another (for example, electrical energy is converted into heat in a kettle).

What is 1 horsepower?

This is a non-system unit of power. This calculator uses "metric horsepower", which is approximately 735.5 watts. This is the power required to uniformly lift a 75 kg load to a height of 1 meter in 1 second.

Is work time sensitive?

The amount of mechanical work itself ( $A = F \times S$ ) does not depend on time. You can lift a bag of cement to the 5th floor in a minute or an hour - the work will be the same. However power will be different: the faster you do this, the more power you will develop.
