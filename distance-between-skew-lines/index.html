Online distance calculator between crossing lines (with solution)

Finding the distance between lines in 3D space

In stereometry, two lines can be positioned relative to each other in three ways: intersect, be parallel, or cross. Crossing lines are the most interesting case: they do not have common points and do not lie in the same plane.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly find shortest distance (the length of the common perpendicular) between such lines using vector algebra methods.

Geometric meaning of the problem

Imagine two lines in space. The distance between them is the height of a parallelepiped built on three vectors:

  1. Direction vector of the first line ( $\vec{s_1}$ ).
  2. Direction vector of the second straight line ( $\vec{s_2}$ ).
  3. A vector connecting an arbitrary point on the first line with a point on the second ( $\vec{M_1M_2}$ ).

Basic formulas

For the calculation, a formula is used that connects the mixed and vector products.

The distance ( $d$ ) is calculated as:

$$d = \frac{|(\vec{M_1M_2}, \vec{s_1}, \vec{s_2})|}{|\vec{s_1} \times \vec{s_2}|}$$

Where:

  • Numerator: Mixed product of vectors module. Geometrically, this is the volume of a parallelepiped.
  • Denominator: The module of the vector product of direction vectors. Geometrically, this is the area of ​​the base of the parallelepiped.

In coordinate form:

If the lines are given by canonical equations:

$\frac{x-x_1}{p_1} = \frac{y-y_1}{q_1} = \frac{z-z_1}{r_1}$ and $\frac{x-x_2}{p_2} = \frac{y-y_2}{q_2} = \frac{z-z_2}{r_2}$

That mixed product (numerator) can be found through the determinant of the matrix:

$$\text{Mixed Product} = \begin{vmatrix} x_2 — x_1 & y_2 — y_1 & z_2 — z_1 \\ p_1 & q_1 & r_1 \\ p_2 & q_2 & r_2 \end{vmatrix}$$

Types of relative positions of lines

Our calculator automatically determines how the lines are located and selects the desired formula.

How to use the calculator?

  1. Direct L1: Enter the coordinates of any point ( $M_1$ ) belonging to the first line and the coordinates of its direction vector ( $s_1$ ).
  2. Direct L2: Enter similar data for the second line ( $M_2$ and $s_2$ ).
  3. Click the button "Calculate".

The tool will not only show the answer, but also display intermediate values: the cross product of the direction vectors and the status of the lines (are they parallel or crossed).
