Pivot Points Calculator: Classic, Fibonacci, Camarilla and Woody

In technical analysis Pivot Points is one of the most objective tools for determining market sentiment. Unlike indicators, which lag, Pivot levels are calculated based on the prices of the previous period and serve as a ready-made “road map” for the trader for the next session.

Our calculator allows you to instantly determine potential reversal zones (Pivot), profit-taking levels (Resistance) and buy zones (Support), using the five most popular calculation methods.

Why use a reference point calculator?

Traders use these levels to:

  • Trend Definitions: If the price is above the PP (Pivot Point) the mood is “bullish”, if below it is “bearish”.
  • Stop Loss and Take Profit settings: Levels S1, S2, R1, R2 serve as ideal price magnets.
  • Trading for a breakout or rebound: The price reaction to these lines is often sniping.

Calculation Methods and Formulas

Our tool supports 5 algorithms. Choose the one that suits your trading strategy.

Legend:

  • $H$ (High) — High of the previous day.
  • $L$ (Low) — Low of the previous day.
  • $C$ (Close) — Closing price.
  • $O$ (Open) — Opening price (important only for the DeMark method).
  • $PP$ - Main pivot point.

1. Classic

The most common method. Equally takes into account the high, low and close.

$$PP = \frac{H + L + C}{3}$$

$$R1 = (2 \times PP) — L$$

$$S1 = (2 \times PP) — H$$

2. Fibonacci

Combines the theory of Pivots with the “golden ratio”. Ideal for corrective movements.

$$PP = \frac{H + L + C}{3}$$

$$R1 = PP + (H — L) \times 0.382$$

$$S1 = PP — (H — L) \times 0.382$$

3. Woodie

Gives more weight to the closing price ( $2 \times C$ ), since the closing of the session is considered the most important emotional outcome of the day.

$$PP = \frac{H + L + 2 \times C}{4}$$

$$R1 = (2 \times PP) — L$$

$$S1 = (2 \times PP) — H$$

4. Camarilla

Created for intraday trading (scalping) in the flat. Generates 8 levels (4 support and resistance each), which are located very close to the closing price.

$$PP = \frac{H + L + C}{3}$$

$$R3 = C + \frac{(H — L) \times 1.1}{4}$$

$$S3 = C — \frac{(H — L) \times 1.1}{4}$$

5. DeMark

A unique method that takes into account the ratio of Opening and Closing prices. This is not really a “pivot point”, but rather a forecast of the high and low of the next candle.

$X$ base calculation:

  • If $C < O$ : $X = H + (2 \times L) + C$
  • If $C > O$ : $X = (2 \times H) + L + C$
  • If $C = O$ : $X = H + L + (2 \times C)$

$$PP = \frac{X}{4}$$

How to use the calculator

  1. Open the chart of your asset (D1 is the daily time frame most often).
  2. Write down the values High, Low And Close the previous completed candle.
  3. Enter the data in the appropriate fields of the form.
  4. Select Calculation method (default is Classic).
  5. Click "Calculate levels".
  6. Use the obtained values ​​of $S1-S3$ and $R1-R3$ to place pending orders.
