555 Timer Calculator (Monostable Mode): Delay Calculation and Component Selection

Timer 555 is perhaps the most popular integrated circuit in the history of electronics. IN monostable mode (Monostable Mode), also known as a “one-shot”, the timer generates a single output pulse of a specified duration when a trigger signal is received.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the timer delay time or select the necessary radio components to obtain the desired time interval.

How does monostable mode work?

In this mode, the 555 timer operates as a time relay. At rest, the output (Pin 3) has a low voltage level. When a momentary low level pulse is applied to the trigger input (Pin 2), the timer starts:

  1. The output switches to the high state.
  2. Charging of the capacitor $C$ begins through the resistor $R$ .
  3. As soon as the capacitor voltage reaches $2/3$ from the supply voltage ( $V_{CC}$ ), the timer is reset.

Calculation formula (The Formula)

The duration of the output pulse ( $T$ ) depends only on two external components: a resistor and a capacitor. The supply voltage does not affect the time (within reasonable limits 5V - 15V).

Basic formula for calculating time:

$$T = 1.1 \times R \times C$$

Where:

  • $T$ — pulse duration in seconds (s);
  • $R$ — resistor resistance in Ohms ( $\Omega$ );
  • $C$ is the electrical capacitance of the capacitor in Farads (F).

Note: The coefficient $1.1$ arises from the natural logarithm of $\ln(3) \approx 1.0986$ as the capacitor charges from 0V to $2/3 V_{CC}$ .

Recommendations for selecting components

To ensure that the circuit operates stably and predictably, adhere to the following rules for selecting parts:

  1. Resistor ( $R$ ):
    • Minimum value: 1 kOhm ( $1 k\Omega$ ). If you set it less, when the capacitor is discharged, too much current may flow through the 7th leg of the microcircuit, which will damage the timer.
    • Maximum value: 10 - 20 MOhm ( $M\Omega$ ). At higher values, the leakage currents of the capacitor and the microcircuit itself begin to affect (especially for bipolar versions of the NE555). For CMOS versions (eg LMC555) higher value resistors can be used.
  2. Capacitor ( $C$ ):
    • For low delays, use ceramic or film capacitors (stable, low leakage).
    • For long delays (seconds and minutes), electrolytic capacitors are used. Please note that they have a large tolerance (up to ±20%) and leakage current, which reduces the accuracy of the timer.

Application of 555 Monostable Timer

This mode is ideal for creating:

  • Automatic light turn-off timers.
  • Delay systems for acoustic protection.
  • Button debouncing suppressors.
  • Pulse formers of a certain length for logic circuits.
