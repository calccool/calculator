Blood Alcohol Calculator: When Can You Drive? (Widmark formula)

Online blood alcohol calculator (Breathalyzer)

How many ppm will a breathalyzer show after a glass of wine or a glass of beer? How many hours later is it safe to drive? Our calculator will help answer these questions using a scientific approach.

This tool is based on the internationally recognized Widmark formula, which is used by forensic experts to estimate the concentration of ethanol in human blood.

How does the calculation work?

The level of alcohol in the blood depends not only on the amount drunk, but also on physiological parameters. Our calculator takes into account key factors:

  1. Body weight: The lower the weight, the higher the alcohol concentration at the same dose.
  2. Floor: In the female body, the water content is lower (about 60%) than in the male (about 70%), therefore, with the same weight and dose, women will have a higher ppm level.
  3. Strength and volume: We automatically calculate the mass of pure ethanol entering the body.
  4. Time (Elimination): The liver processes alcohol at a constant rate (approximately 0.15‰ per hour). The calculator subtracts processed alcohol from the total.

Widmark's formula

The mathematical model looks like this:

$$c = \frac{A}{m \times r} — \beta \times T$$

Where:

  • c — blood alcohol concentration (‰).
  • A — mass of pure alcohol in grams.
  • m — body weight in kg.
  • r — distribution coefficient (0.7 for men, 0.6 for women).
  • β — alcohol elimination rate (default 0.15 ‰/h).
  • T - time elapsed after consumption.

Degrees of intoxication and driving

Remember that the effects of alcohol vary from person to person. Even at low ppm levels, the driver's response slows down.

Warning: The calculator's results are approximate and cannot be used as evidence in court or as a substitute for a real breathalyzer. Metabolic rate depends on genetics, liver health, nutrition and other factors. Never drive if you have been drinking alcohol.
