Gable Roof Calculator: Calculation of Rafters, Sheathing and Slope Angle

Professional calculation of the roof of a house online

The gable roof (gable roof) is the most popular design in private housing construction due to its reliability, ease of installation and efficient precipitation drainage. However, even a simple design requires precise engineering calculations. An error in calculating the length of the rafters or the pitch of the sheathing can lead to sagging of the roof or excessive consumption of expensive materials.

Our gable roof calculator allows you to instantly design a roofing system, taking into account the dimensions of the house, type of covering and climatic loads of your region.

What parameters does the calculator calculate?

The tool performs a complex calculation divided into three logical blocks:

  1. Geometry and Area:
    • The angle of inclination of the slopes (critical for snow melting).
    • The total surface area of ​​the roof (including eaves and gable overhangs) for the purchase of metal tiles, corrugated sheets or soft roofing.
  2. Rafter system:
    • The exact length of the rafter leg (including cuts).
    • Required number of rafters.
    • Volume of lumber (in cubic meters) for the purchase of timber.
  3. Lathing and Loads:
    • Number of rows and sheathing boards.
    • Total load per 1 m² (weight of the “roofing cake” + snow + wind).

Calculation formulas (Geometry)

The calculations are based on trigonometric functions and the Pythagorean theorem. Considering the roof in section as an isosceles triangle, we use the following formulas:

1. Rafter length ( $L$ )

To find the length of the rafter (hypotenuse), half the width of the house ( $W/2$ ) and the height of the ridge ( $H$ ) are used:

$$L = \sqrt{(W/2)^2 + H^2}$$

Note: The calculator automatically adds the length of the eaves overhang to this value.

2. Tilt angle ( $\alpha$ )

The angle of inclination of the slope is determined through the tangent:

$$\tan(\alpha) = \frac{H}{W/2}$$

3. Roof area ( $S$ )

The area is calculated for two slopes, taking into account the length of the house ( $D$ ) and all overhangs:

$$S = 2 \times (L_{full} \times D_{full})$$

Accounting for climatic loads (SNiP)

The roof must withstand not only its own weight, but also external influences.

  • Snow load: In snowy regions (for example, Siberia or the Urals), the weight of snow on the roof can reach hundreds of kilograms per square meter.
  • Wind load: Important for determining the reliability of fastening the roof to the Mauerlat.

Our algorithm summarizes the dead weight of the structure (rafters + sheathing + covering) and standard climatic loads, giving a final value in kg/m². If the resulting figure exceeds the permissible limits for the selected board section, it is recommended to reduce the rafter pitch or select a beam of a larger cross-section.
