How respawn timing works

The time the hero spends in the tavern is not random. It is strictly tied to the character's current level. In normal mode (All Pick), this value increases with each level, reaching its peak at level 30.

Key factors affecting the timer:

  • Hero level: Main parameter. The higher the level, the longer the wait.
  • Game mode: In Turbo mode, the respawn time is reduced by exactly 2 times, which speeds up the pace of the game.
  • Reaper’s Scythe (Necrophos): Necrophos's ultimate adds a fixed time penalty to the base timer.
  • Talents and bonuses: Some abilities and items can reduce the time spent in the tavern.

Formulas for calculating the mechanics of death

For those who want to gain a deeper understanding of the game's math, we use Valve's official formulas. The total time is calculated using the following algorithm:

$$T_{total} = (T_{base} + P_{scythe}) — B_{talent}$$

Where:

  • $T_{total}$ — final time in seconds;
  • $T_{base}$ — base time for a specific level (in Turbo mode, divided by 2);
  • $P_{scythe}$ - penalty from Reaper's Scythe (10, 20 or 30 sec);
  • $B_{talent}$ - bonus from talents or special effects.

Calculation of the buyback cost (Buyback)

In addition to the respawn time, it is critical to know whether you have enough gold to buy back. Our calculator automatically calculates the ransom price using the current formula, depending on the level and current game time:

$$Cost = 100 + (L^2 \cdot 1.5) + (M \cdot 15)$$

Where:

  • $L$ - current hero level;
  • $M$ - the number of full minutes of game time.

Tips for managing the time of death

  • In Turbo mode, do not forget that fast respawn allows you to force fights more often, but buyback is still expensive.
  • If Necrophos is playing against you, always allow an extra 30 seconds in the late game - this time can cost you the side or the entire game.
