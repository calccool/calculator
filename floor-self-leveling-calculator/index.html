Floor screed calculator: Calculation of mixture consumption and number of bags per 1 m²

Calculation of self-leveling floors and screeds online

Correct calculation of materials for leveling the floor is a guarantee that the work will not stop halfway due to the lack of a couple of bags, and that after repairs you will not be left with a mountain of excess cement. Our self-leveling floor calculator allows you to accurately determine the required volume of dry mixture or components for cement-sand mortar.

The tool works in two modes: for factory self-leveling floors (self-leveling mixtures) and for classic “wet” screed, which is prepared manually.

Flow calculation formula

The basic principle of calculation is based on a simple geometric formula for volume multiplied by the density (consumption) of the material.

The general formula looks like this:

$$TotalWeight = S \times H \times C$$

Where:

  • $S$ - room area ( $m^2$ ).
  • $H$ — thickness of the fill layer ( $mm$ ).
  • $C$ - average mixture consumption ( $kg/m^2$ with a layer of 1 mm).

Important: In reality, the floor is never perfectly level. It is always recommended to add reserve 5–10% to the calculated quantity to compensate for differences in height and loss of material during mixing.

Do-it-yourself screed calculation (Sand concrete)

If you plan to make a classic cement-sand screed (CSS) or use sand concrete, the calculation is a little more complicated. The calculator will help determine the proportions of the components:

  1. Cement: Binder. Typically the brand M400 or M500 is used.
  2. Sand: Filler. Must be clean, river or quarry washed.
  3. Water: To hydrate the mixture.

Standard proportions for floor screed:

  • 1 : 3 (M150) - the most popular brand of residential mortar. High strength.
  • 1 : 4 (M100) - used for leveling layers under tiles or where loads on the floor are minimal.

How to use the calculator

  1. Select material type: Ready-made store-bought mixture or homemade solution.
  2. Specify room parameters: Enter the area of ​​the room in square meters and the planned layer thickness in millimeters.
  3. Set up packaging: If you know the weight of the bags you plan to buy (25, 40 or 50 kg), indicate this to calculate the exact number of packages.
  4. Add stock: We recommend leaving the value at 5-10% for insurance.
  5. Price (optional): Enter the cost of one bag to immediately find out your budget for materials.

By pressing the button "Calculate", you will instantly receive a full report: the total weight of the dry mixture, the number of bags and (for self-mixing) the required amount of sand, cement and water.
