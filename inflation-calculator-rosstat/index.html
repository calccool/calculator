Inflation calculator in Russia (2000–2025) according to Rosstat

Understanding how the value of money changes over time is critical to personal financial planning, investment evaluation, and business management. This online tool allows you to instantly calculate how inflation processes in the Russian Federation have affected your savings or the cost of goods over the past quarter century.

How does inflation calculation work?

The calculator algorithm is based on the official Consumer Price Index (CPI), which is published monthly and annually by the Federal State Statistics Service (Rosstat). We have integrated historical data since 2000, which allows for in-depth retrospective analysis.

The tool supports two key operating modes:

  • Cost equivalent (Inflation): Allows you to find out how much money would be needed today to buy the same amount of goods and services that were available for a certain amount in the past.
  • Depreciation (Purchasing Power): Shows how much today’s money was actually “worth” in the selected year of the last century or decade.

Key Features and Benefits

  1. Current data: The database includes indicators for 2024 and forecast values ​​for 2025.
  2. Accuracy down to kopecks: The calculator takes into account the accumulated (compound) inflation rate for each year of the period.
  3. Process visualization: The interactive scale clearly demonstrates the relationship between the basic value and the “inflationary margin”.
  4. Instant result: You receive not only the total amount, but also the weighted average annual inflation rate for the selected cycle.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculations are based on the formula of the accumulated inflation index. If we consider inflation as a sequence of annual coefficients, then the final multiplier is determined as the product of these coefficients.

To calculate the total amount in equivalent mode, use the formula:

$$S_{end} = S_{start} \cdot \prod_{i=1}^{n} (1 + \frac{r_i}{100})$$

Where:

  • $S_{end}$ - total amount in the target year;
  • $S_{start}$ is the original amount in the base year;
  • $r_i$ - inflation rate in percent for a specific year $i$ ;
  • $n$ — number of years in the selected period.

To determine the average annual rate of price growth for the period, the geometric mean formula is used:

$$R_{avg} = (\sqrt[n]{K_{total}} — 1) \cdot 100\%$$

Where $K_{total}$ is the overall cumulative inflation rate for the entire period.

Why use an inflation calculator

Using this tool helps solve a number of practical problems:

Estimation of real return on deposits

If your bank deposit earns 10% per annum and inflation over the same period was 8%, your real return is only 2%. The calculator will help you not to be fooled by nominal numbers.

Comparison of historical prices

Many people remember real estate or car prices in the 2000s. The calculator allows you to convert those amounts into modern rubles in order to understand whether the product has objectively become more expensive or whether its cost has simply been adjusted for inflation.

Planning Long-Term Goals

If you plan to save for a child’s education or retirement, you need to account for inflation risks. The tool will show you what your target's purchasing power will be in 10 or 15 years.

Important: Calculations are for informational purposes and are based on average data from the consumer basket. Each person's personal inflation may differ depending on their spending structure (the ratio of costs for housing, food, equipment and services).
