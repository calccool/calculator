Mathematical model and calculation formula

The calculator is based on the difference between the length of the tropical (solar) year and the lunar Hijri year. The lunar year is shorter than the solar year by approximately 10.875 days.

For the calculation, the reference point (epoch) is used - the 1st day of the month of Ramadan 1445 Hijri, which corresponds to March 11, 2024. The formula for determining the start date for any $H$ year is as follows:

$$T_{start} = T_{base} + (H — 1445) \times L_{year}$$

Where:

  • $T_{start}$ — the desired start date of Ramadan;
  • $T_{base}$ - base date (03/11/2024);
  • $H$ - target year according to Hijri;
  • $L_{year}$ - average length of the lunar year ( $\approx 354.367$ days).

When calculating using the Gregorian calendar, the system pre-converts the year $Y$ to the corresponding Hijri year $H$ using a cyclic shift factor.

Main factors influencing the date

  1. Astronomical new moon: the moment when the Moon is on the same line between the Earth and the Sun.
  2. Geographical location: Conditions for visibility of the Moon vary in different parts of the planet.
  3. Weather conditions: cloudiness may prevent observation, which automatically extends the current month of Sha'ban to 30 days.
  4. Decision of local ulema councils: In different countries, local decisions about the beginning of fasting may be made.
