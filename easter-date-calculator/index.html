Calculation algorithm and Easter cycles

Determining the date of Easter is a complex astronomical and mathematical task. Our calculator is based on the classic Easter formulas, which are based on the solar-lunar cycle.

The basic rule established by the Council of Nicaea in 325 states: Easter is celebrated on the first Sunday after the first full moon of the spring that occurs after the vernal equinox.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the date of Orthodox Easter (according to the old style), a modified Gaussian algorithm is used. The main variables in the calculator code are calculated using the following formulas:

$$a = Y \pmod{4}$$

$$b = Y \pmod{7}$$

$$c = Y \pmod{19}$$

$$d = (19 \times c + 15) \pmod{30}$$

$$e = (2 \times a + 4 \times b — d + 34) \pmod{7}$$

Where $Y$ is the year you are looking for. The final date is obtained by adding the resulting values, taking into account the amendment between the Julian and Gregorian calendars, which in the 21st century is 13 days.

Why do different religions have different dates for Easter?

The difference in the dates of celebration is due to the use of two different chronology systems and methods of astronomical calculations.

Gregorian and Julian calendars

The Catholic Church uses Gregorian calendar (new style), which more accurately corresponds to the astronomical year. When making calculations, the Orthodox Church adheres to Julian calendar (old style). Currently, the difference between them is 13 days, which is the main reason for the discrepancy in dates.

Astronomical full moon

Apart from the 13-day difference, denominations use different methods for determining the "Easter moon".

  • The Western tradition is guided by the astronomical equinox and modern tables of the movement of the Moon.
  • The Eastern tradition uses a fixed equinox (March 21 according to the old style) and Metonic cycles, which is why Orthodox Easter can never occur earlier than April 4 and later than May 8 according to the new style.

Conditions for matching dates

Easter dates coincide approximately 30% of the time, when the full moon falls during such a period that the difference between styles does not extend the Sunday beyond one week. Our calculator has a built-in visualization block that clearly shows the difference in days for the year you have chosen.
