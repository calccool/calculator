Calculator for the current week of pregnancy and due date - find out your trimester and PDA

What does this calculator do?

This calculator allows you to calculate current obstetric week of pregnancy, estimated date of birth (ED), time remaining until delivery or how many weeks have already passed - based on data on the last menstrual period, date of conception, embryo transfer (IVF) or early ultrasound results.

You will receive:

  • gestational age (weeks + days) today;
  • expected date of birth (EDD);
  • how long is left or has passed before giving birth;
  • the number of the current week of pregnancy and the date range of this week;
  • what trimester is it now?
  • estimated date of LMP and/or conception.

How to use

  1. Select the calculation method: LMP, conception, IVF or ultrasound.
  2. Enter the relevant data (date, cycle length, embryo day, etc.).
  3. Click "Calculate" to get results.
  4. If necessary - "Reset" for new input.

What do the results mean?

  • Gestational age — how many weeks and days have passed since obstetric LMP until today’s date.
  • PDR — approximate date of birth (not a guarantee, but a forecast).
  • Remaining/passed until the traffic deadline — shows how many weeks are left or how many days have passed since the PDR.
  • Current week number And date range — clearly shows the beginning and end of the current obstetric week.
  • Trimester — I trimester: up to 14 weeks; II: 14-28; III: 28+ weeks.
  • LMP And date of conception — reference dates for calculations.

Why is this important

  • Prenatal care planning (screenings, tests)
  • Preparing for childbirth: courses, medical visits, vacations
  • Tracking the development of pregnancy and monitoring the timing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it possible to get the exact date of birth?
No - the calculated date is approximate. Only ~3-5% of women give birth exactly in the PDR.

What if the cycle is not 28 days?
Then use your cycle length or another method (conception, ultrasound) for greater accuracy.

Is the calculator suitable for IVF?
Yes - select the “IVF” method and indicate the day of the embryo. The formula is specially designed.
