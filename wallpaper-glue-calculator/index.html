Wallpaper glue consumption calculator: Accurate calculation of packs and water

Renovation is the art of precise planning. One of the most common mistakes when decorating walls is the incorrect purchase of consumables. Buy little - you'll have to run to the store in the middle of work, when the glue is already drying. Buy a lot and you'll end up with extra boxes that go in the trash.

Our wallpaper glue calculator solves this problem in seconds. Unlike primitive tables on packages, it takes into account the geometry of your room, the presence of windows and doors, as well as the need to pre-prime the walls.

What determines glue consumption?

The application rates specified by the manufacturer (for example, Quelyd, Metylan or Kleo) are often averages. In reality, three factors influence the amount of mixture:

  1. Wallpaper type. The heavier the canvas, the thicker the adhesive solution should be and the more dry mixture is used per 1 m². For example, for light paper wallpaper the glue is diluted more strongly, while for heavy vinyl or glass wallpaper a viscous, concentrated mass is required.
  2. Absorbency of walls. Porous surfaces (plaster, concrete) “drink” moisture more actively than putty and primed walls.
  3. Primer. Professionals recommend walking over the walls with a weak solution of the same glue before gluing. This binds dust and improves adhesion (grip). Our tool can take this additional expense into account.

Calculation formula

For those who want to check the calculations manually, we use the following mathematical model. First, the net pasting area ( $S_{net}$ ) is calculated:

$$S_{net} = ((L + W) \times 2 \times H) — S_{exclude}$$

Where $L$ is the length, $W$ is the width, $H$ is the height, and $S_{exclude}$ is the area of ​​windows and doors.

Then the total mass of the dry mixture ( $Mass_{total}$ ) is calculated:

$$Mass_{total} = S_{net} \times ConsumptionRate \times PrimerFactor$$

Where $ConsumptionRate$ is the application rate for the selected type of wallpaper, and $PrimerFactor$ adds a margin if the primer option is selected.

The total number of packs ( $Packs$ ) is determined by dividing the total mass by the weight of one package, rounding up:

$$Packs = \lceil \frac{Mass_{total}}{PackWeight} \rceil$$

How to use the calculator?

  1. Measure the room. Enter the length, width and height of the ceilings.
  2. Subtract the excess. Don't forget to indicate the total area of ​​windows and doorways in the "Exclude" field. There is no point in wasting glue on surfaces that will not be covered.
  3. Select the weight of the pack. Standard packages come in 200, 250, 300 or 500 grams. Look at the box you are planning to buy.
  4. Specify the type of coverage. This is the most important step as it determines the thickness of the solution.
  5. Primer. If the walls are not prepared, select “Yes” in the primer section. The calculator will add the necessary amount of glue to create the primer layer.

The result will show not only the number of boxes, but also required volume of water to obtain a solution of the correct consistency.
