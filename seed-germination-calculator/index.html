Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm is based on a modified development time equation. The basic calculation formula is as follows:

$$D_{final} = D_{base} \times K_{temp} \times K_{treat}$$

Where:

  • $D_{final}$ - total number of days before emergence;
  • $D_{base}$ —reference germination time under optimal conditions;
  • $K_{temp}$ - temperature influence coefficient (increases with cold weather);
  • $K_{treat}$ - pre-sowing treatment coefficient (equal to 0.75 when soaking, which speeds up the process by 25% ).

Key factors influencing germination

Seed germination begins with the absorption of water (imbibition), followed by the activation of enzymes. The speed of these reactions directly depends on the environment.

Ground temperature

This is a critical factor. For most nightshades (tomatoes, peppers), the optimal range is 25–28°C. If the temperature drops to 15°C, biochemical processes slow down 2–3 times. When reduced to a critical minimum (for example, 10–12°C for cucumber), the seeds may go dormant or simply rot.

Pre-sowing treatment

Soaking seeds in water or growth stimulants “turns on” the plant’s biological clock even before it enters the ground. Our calculator takes into account that pre-germinated seeds germinate at 20–30% faster than dry ones.

How to speed up the emergence of seedlings: expert advice

If the calculation shows dates that are too late, use proven agricultural techniques:

  1. Thermometer control: Don't rely on room temperature. The soil on the windowsill is always colder 3–5 degrees. Use the calculator by entering exactly the temperature of the substrate.
  2. Greenhouse effect: Cover containers with film or glass to maintain 100% humidity. This prevents the seed shell from drying out.
  3. Bottom heating: Placing seedling boxes on special heated mats allows you to keep stable 25°C, which reduces waiting to a minimum.
  4. No light needed: Before the first shoots appear, most seeds (except fine dust ones, such as petunia) do not require light - only temperature is important.
