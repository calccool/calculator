Extruder steps calculator (Steps per mm) for 3D printer

Accurate filament feeding is the foundation of high-quality 3D printing. Even if your printer is assembled perfectly, the factory settings for motor steps (E-steps) may cause errors. If the extruder extrudes less plastic than needed, you will end up with brittle parts with holes in the walls ( underextrusion ). If it is more, the parts will become covered with sagging and lose dimensional accuracy ( overextrusion ).

Calibration allows software commands to be synchronized with the physical movement of the motor. Our calculator will help you calculate the new valueM92in just a couple of measurements.

How to use the step calculator

The calculator implements two of the most popular measurement methods. Select the appropriate one in the interface tabs.

Method 1: By tag remainder (Recommended)

This is the cleanest and most accurate method as you do not need to disconnect the Bowden tube or nozzle.

  1. Place a mark with a felt-tip pen on the filament at a distance of 120 mm from the entrance to the extruder.
  2. Enter this value (120) in the “Marked distance” field.
  3. Using the printer menu or G-code, give the command to extrude 100 mm of plastic.
  4. Using a ruler or caliper, measure how many mm are left from the extruder inlet to your mark. Enter this in the Remaining field.

Method 2: By extruded rod

Suitable if you measure the filament “in the air” (for example, by disconnecting the tube after the feeder).

  1. Cut the filament flush with the exit of the extruder.
  2. Give the command to extrude 100 mm.
  3. Cut and measure the resulting piece of plastic. Enter its length in the "Actually extruded" field.

Calibration Mathematics: Basic Formulas

The calculator uses the classic proportion for calculations. The basic formula for calculating new steps is:

$$S_{new} = \frac{L_{target}}{L_{actual}} \times S_{current}$$

Where:

  • $S_{new}$ - new value of steps per mm;
  • $S_{current}$ - current value set in the firmware;
  • $L_{target}$ - the distance that the printer must was squeeze out (task);
  • $L_{actual}$ - the distance that the printer extruded In fact.

To calculate the Flow Rate, the slicer uses a formula based on wall thickness:

$$F_{new} = \frac{W_{expected}}{W_{measured}} \times F_{current}$$

  • $W_{expected}$ - expected line width (for example, 0.4 mm);
  • $W_{measured}$ is the actual wall thickness of the printed cube.

Setting Flow Rate (Extrusion Multiplier)

Once you have set up the mechanics (mm steps), you need to calibrate the flow for the specific plastic. Different types of filament (PLA, PETG, ABS) have different fluidity and coefficient of thermal expansion.

Step-by-step flow setup algorithm:

  1. Print a test cube in Vase Mode with one side.
  2. Make sure the line width in the slicer is set (eg 0.4mm or 0.45mm).
  3. Measure the wall thickness at 4-5 points using a caliper.
  4. Calculate the average and enter it into the “Fine Tuning” block of our calculator.
  5. Enter the result in the field Flow (in Cura) or Extrusion Multiplier (in PrusaSlicer/Bambu Studio).

How to save settings in the printer

After the calculator has given you a new step value, you need to store it in non-volatile memory (EEPROM).

For Marlin firmware (G-code):

Send the following commands via terminal (OctoPrint, Pronterface, Port Monitor):

  1. M92 E[ВАШЕ_ЗНАЧЕНИЕ]— setting a new value.
  2. M500- saving to memory.

For Klipper firmware:

Find in fileprinter.cfgsection[extruder]and change the parameterrotation_distance. Note that Klipper often uses rotation distance rather than steps per mm.
