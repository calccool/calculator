Online term transfer calculator - Express a variable from an equation

Transferring terms from one side of an equation to another is a fundamental skill in algebra, on which the solution of any linear and complex mathematical models is built. Our online tool automates this process, minimizing the risk of sign errors.

Main features of the calculator

  • Instant transfer: Separates the selected variable from the rest of the equation.
  • Algorithm for changing characters: Automatically applies the rule: “If you move beyond the equal sign, change the plus to the minus.”
  • Working with coefficients: Calculates the final value by dividing the right-hand side by the factor in front of the search variable.
  • Step-by-step visualization: You see not only the answer, but also the logic of each stage of the transformation.

Theoretical background: Transfer rules

When working with equations, it is important to remember the two “golden” rules of algebra:

  1. Rule of terms: Any term can be transferred from one part of the equation to another by changing its sign to the opposite. $$a + b = c \implies a = c — b$$
  2. Multiplier Rule: If a variable has a coefficient (multiplier), then after transferring all free terms, both sides of the equation are divided by this coefficient. $$ax = b \implies x = \frac{b}{a}$$

Calculator operation algorithm

To express the variable $y$ from the equation $2x + 3y = 10$ , the service performs the following steps:

  1. Finding the target term: The program finds the term in the equation containing the given letter ( $3y$ ).
  2. Insulation: All other terms ( $2x$ ) are transferred to the opposite side of the equation with the sign changing to $-2x$ . $$3y = 10 — 2x$$
  3. Normalization: The entire right side is divided by the coefficient of $y$ (by the number 3). $$y = \frac{10 — 2x}{3}$$
