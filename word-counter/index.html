Key metrics and their meaning

Our analyzer provides a detailed summary of the following parameters:

  • Words: Basic unit of measurement for translators (pay per word) and English copywriting.
  • Characters without spaces: "Gold Standard" in RuNet. Most content exchanges and customers evaluate work precisely by the number of thousands of characters without spaces (t.z.b.p.).
  • Characters with spaces: An important metric for layout designers, since a space takes up space on the page just like a letter.
  • Paragraphs and sentences: They help evaluate the structure and rhythm of the text. Paragraphs that are too long make them difficult to read (User Experience).

Timing: Reading and speaking time

One of the unique features of our service is the calculation of the time of interaction with content. We use generally accepted speech rate standards.

The following formulas are used for calculation:

1. Reading Time

The average adult reads from a computer screen at a speed of about 200 words per minute.

$$T_{read} = \frac{N_{words}}{200}$$

2. Speaking Time

The speed of the announcer or speaker is lower and averages 130 words per minute. This is critical for producing reports, podcasts, and videos.

$$T_{speak} = \frac{N_{words}}{130}$$

Frequency Analysis (SEO-Nausea)

Our algorithm automatically clears the text of “noise” (prepositions, conjunctions and words shorter than 3 letters) and builds a rating of the most used words.

This helps to identify overspam (Keyword Stuffing). If the same word appears too often, search engines (Google, Yandex) may lower the page in the search results.

  • Normal Density: The keyword takes up 2-4% of the total volume.
  • Overspam: Density above 5-7% indicates that the text is unnatural.
