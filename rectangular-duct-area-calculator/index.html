Rectangular Duct Calculator: Area, Weight and Flanges Calculator

Online calculation of direct ventilation sections

When designing and installing ventilation systems, one of the most routine tasks is calculating the specification of air ducts. Our calculator automates this process, allowing you to instantly obtain data for ordering metal and components.

This tool will be useful:

  • For HVAC designers for quick calculation of the aerodynamic section.
  • Tinsmiths to determine the development area (how many square meters of sheet are needed for production).
  • Estimators to calculate the exact weight of the metal and the cost of materials.

Calculation algorithm: Area and Section

The calculator is based on geometric formulas for the development of a parallelepiped (since a straight air duct is a hollow parallelepiped).

For duct width $A$ , height $B$ and length $L$ :

  1. Cross-sectional area ( $S_{cross}$ ): This parameter is important for calculating air speed. $$S_{cross} = A \times B$$
  2. Surface (Ream) Area ( $S_{surf}$ ): This is the area of ​​metal required to make one part (not including flanges). $$S_{surf} = 2 \cdot (A + B) \cdot L$$ Note: The calculator automatically converts millimeters to meters.

Calculation of the weight of galvanized steel

The main difference between our tool and simple geometric calculators is the calculation of the mass of the product. Weight is critical for fastener selection (studs, crossmembers) and logistics.

We use the formula:

$$Mass = S_{total} \times t \times \rho$$

Where:

  • $S_{total}$ - total area including allowance for seams (folds).
  • $t$ - steel thickness (mm).
  • $\rho$ - steel density (taken as 7850 kg/m³ ).

Consumables: Shinor and Corners

Rectangular air ducts are connected to each other using flanges that are pressed onto the ends.

  • Tire rail (profile): It goes along the perimeter of the air duct on both sides. $$Length = 2 \cdot 2 \cdot (A + B) \times N$$ Where $N$ is the number of air ducts.
  • Corners: Installed in each flange corner. Since one part has two flanges (4 corners each), one air duct always requires 8 corners.

Recommended metal thickness (SNiP)

When choosing steel thickness ( $t$ ) in the calculator, you can rely on current standards (SP 60.13330.2012).

Why do you need seam allowance?

When making an air duct, part of the metal goes into seam joints (lock) and trim when cutting the sheet.

  • For straight sections it is usually laid 10-15%.
  • For shaped products (bends, transitions) the stock can reach 20-25%.

In the “Stock” field, you can indicate your percentage to get the most accurate estimate.
