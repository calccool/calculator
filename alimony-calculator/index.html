Child support calculator: online calculation of payments for 1, 2, 3 or more children

The online child support calculator is a professional tool designed to preliminary determine the amount of monthly payments for the maintenance of minor children. The calculation is made in strict accordance with Article 81 of the Family Code of the Russian Federation.

The main feature of our service is that it takes into account tax legislation: according to the law, alimony is calculated only after personal income tax (NDFL) is withheld from the payer’s income.

Basic rules and legal framework

The amount of alimony in Russia is most often set as a share of monthly earnings. However, in some cases, courts may order payments in a lump sum (LQS) if the payer does not have a stable income or receives a salary in foreign currency.

Payment calculation formula

To obtain the correct result, a multi-stage algorithm is used. First, the tax base is determined, and then an interest rate is applied corresponding to the number of children.

Mathematically, the calculation of net income ( $I_{net}$ ) is as follows:

$$I_{net} = I_{gross} \times (1 — 0.13)$$

Where:

  • $I_{gross}$ - total income before taxes (“dirty”);
  • $0.13$ - standard personal income tax rate in the Russian Federation (13%).

The total amount of alimony ( $A$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$A = I_{net} \times P$$

Where $P$ is the percentage established by the court or by agreement of the parties.

From what income is alimony deducted?

Payments are made from almost all types of earnings, including:

  1. Basic salary and bonuses.
  2. Income from business and self-employment.
  3. Income from rental property.
  4. Royalties for intellectual activity.
  5. Pensions and temporary disability benefits.

Maximum deduction limit

It is important to remember the protection mechanisms of the law. According to the Labor Code of the Russian Federation and the Law “On Enforcement Proceedings”, the total amount of all deductions when collecting alimony for minor children cannot exceed 70% from wages. This restriction applies even in cases where the payer has arrears for past periods.
