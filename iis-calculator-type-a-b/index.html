IIS calculator: Calculation of tax deduction and comparison of benefits (Type A and Type B)

An individual investment account (IIA) is not just a tool for accessing the stock market, but a powerful legal way to increase the profitability of your investments through tax benefits from the state. Our calculator allows you to instantly compare two tax strategies so you can make an informed financial decision.

What is the difference between Type A and Type B?

The choice of deduction type depends on your life situation, the level of your “white” salary and the expected return on your portfolio.

  • Type A (Deduction for contribution): Allows you to return 13% of the amount you deposited into your account during the calendar year. This is an ideal choice for conservative investors with formal employment.
  • Type B (Income deduction): Exempts all profits received from personal income tax upon account closure. This option is beneficial for experienced traders and those who do not have official income taxed at a rate of 13%.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator uses a compound interest algorithm with annual additions at the beginning of the period. This is as close as possible to the real practice of investing, when an investor deposits funds and immediately puts them into circulation.

Final capital formula

To calculate savings without taking into account tax benefits, the formula for the accumulated annuity amount is used:

$$S = P \cdot \frac{(1 + r)^n — 1}{r} \cdot (1 + r)$$

Where:

  • $S$ - final capital;
  • $P$ - annual replenishment amount;
  • $r$ — annual interest rate (in shares);
  • $n$ - number of years.

Type A deduction calculation logic

Your Type A return is limited by two factors:

  1. Contribution limit: The state returns 13% on no more than 400,000 rubles.
  2. Personal income tax limit: You cannot get back more than you paid in taxes on your salary for the year.

The annual return formula looks like this:

$$Refund = \min(P \cdot 0.13, 52000, Salary \cdot 12 \cdot 0.13)$$

Which type of deduction should you choose?

To beat competitors in search results, we have prepared a short checklist for choosing a strategy. Our calculator automatically highlights the “Winner”, but it is important to understand the logic:

When to choose Type A

  • You work officially and pay personal income tax.
  • Your annual return is moderate (up to 12–15% per annum).
  • You plan to top up your account with up to RUB 400,000 per year.
  • Cashback from the state “here and now” is important to you.

When to choose Type B

  • You are self-employed, an individual entrepreneur on the simplified tax system, or have no official income.
  • You plan to trade actively and expect high returns (over 15–20% per annum).
  • The term of your investment significantly exceeds 3 years (over long distances, compound interest under Type B often outperforms fixed payments of Type A).

Important restrictions and rules

  • Minimum term: In order not to return the received deductions to the state, you cannot close the account for at least 3 years (for new IIS-3, the period gradually increases to 5–10 years).
  • Uniqueness of account: One individual can have only one active IRA.
  • Currency: You can only invest in rubles in IIS, although you can also buy foreign securities available on Russian exchanges with them.
