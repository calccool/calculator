Pulse Zone Calculator: Calculation using Karvonen and Tanaka Formulas

The effectiveness of training does not depend on how tired you are, but on the pulse zone in which your body worked. Whether you're running to lose weight, training for a marathon, or just keeping your heart healthy, you need an accurate target heart rate (HR) calculation.

Our heart rate zone calculator is a professional tool that uses not only basic but also advanced medical formulas to determine your ideal load.

Calculation methods: Which one to choose?

Most fitness bands use outdated standards. Our service offers three methods, from simple to professional.

1. Classic formula (Fox-Haskell)

The oldest and simplest method. It gives approximate values ​​and is suitable for beginners who do not have a sports watch or heart rate monitor.

$$MHR = 220 — Age$$

2. Tanaka Formula

A more modern standard, developed in 2001. Studies have shown that the classic formula often underestimates the maximum for people over 40 years of age. Tanaka gives a more accurate result for healthy adults.

$$MHR = 208 — (0.7 \times Age)$$

3. Karvonen method - Recommended! ?

This is the “gold standard” in sports medicine. Unlike previous methods, it takes into account your resting heart rate. This is critically important, since a trained athlete and an office worker of the same age may have a resting pulse that differs by 20-30 beats, and their training zones may not be the same.

The formula uses the concept of Heart Rate Reserve (HRR):

$$TargetHR = ((MaxHR — RestingHR) \times \%Intensity) + RestingHR$$

To use this method, take your heart rate in the morning, immediately after waking up, without getting out of bed, and enter the value in the Resting Heart Rate field.

Why is resting heart rate so important?

Resting heart rate (RHR) is an indicator of your fitness. In professional marathon runners it can drop to 40 beats per minute, while in the average person it is 60–80 beats per minute.

The lower your morning heart rate, the wider your “safety margin” (heart rate reserve) and the more intensely you can train while remaining in the aerobic zone. Our calculator automatically adapts zones to this parameter.
