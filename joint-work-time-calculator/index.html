The classic collaboration problem is found everywhere, from high school math textbooks to sprint planning in IT teams to schedules on a construction site.

“The first master paints the fence in 3 hours, and the second – in 5 hours. How long will it take them to paint the fence working together?”

Our universal calculator allows you to solve this problem instantly for any number of participants, even if their productivity and time units differ.

Mathematical formula (Theory)

The calculation is based on the concept Productivity (or operating speed). The main principle: when working together, the performance of the participants adds up.

If the scope of work is not explicitly stated (for example, “paint the fence”), it is taken as 1.

  1. We find the productivity of each: If $t_1$ is the time of the first worker, then his speed is $P_1$ : $$P_1 = \frac{1}{t_1}$$
  2. Finding the overall performance: $$P_{total} = \frac{1}{t_1} + \frac{1}{t_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{t_n}$$
  3. Finding the total time: $$T_{total} = \frac{1}{P_{total}}$$

If the amount of work differs from one unit (for example, you need to produce 100 parts ), the formula looks like this:

$$Time = \frac{Volume}{(\frac{1}{t_1} + \frac{1}{t_2} + \dots)}$$
