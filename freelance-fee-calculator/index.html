Freelance exchange commission calculator: calculation of the amount “in hand” and tax

Every freelancer is faced with a situation where, having agreed on an amount of 10,000 rubles, the actual amount on the card is significantly less. The reason is a multi-layered system of fees: from the base percentage of the site to hidden fees of payment gateways and government taxes.

Our calculator is designed specifically so that you can include all costs in the cost of the project at the stage of discussion with the customer.

Why is it important to calculate the commission in advance?

Working through intermediaries (exchanges) ensures the security of the transaction, but costs money. Main expense items:

  • Site commission: varies from 5% to 20%.
  • Withdrawal fee: fixed amount or percentage for transfer to card/wallet.
  • Tax burden: for self-employed people in the Russian Federation this is 4% when working with individuals and 6% when working with legal entities.

Formula for calculating net profit

To understand what amount ( $Gross$ ) needs to be invoiced to the client in order to receive the desired amount in hand ( $Net$ ), we use a complex formula. It takes into account that tax in most countries (including the Russian Federation) is paid from full amount of the check, and not from what came on the card.

$$Gross = \frac{Net + Fix_{wdr}}{1 — P_{plat} — P_{wdr} + (P_{plat} \cdot P_{wdr}) — Tax}$$

Where:

  • $Net$ - desired amount “net”;
  • $Fix_{wdr}$ - fixed withdrawal fee;
  • $P_{plat}$ — exchange percentage (in shares);
  • $P_{wdr}$ — payment system percentage for withdrawal;
  • $Tax$ - tax rate (for example, 0.04 or 0.06).

How to use the calculator

To get accurate results, follow three simple steps:

  1. Select mode: “I want N net” (if you are creating a price list) or “The budget is known” (if you need to understand the balance after all deductions).
  2. Specify the exchange rate: Choose from presets or enter your own value in the “Custom Percentage” field.
  3. Enable advanced settings: Add tax (e.g. 4% for self-employed) and withdrawal costs so that the donut visualization shows you the actual distribution of the money.

Tips for optimizing income

  • Lay down your taxes: Always calculate the tax from the amount the client pays ( $Gross$ ). This is a tax service requirement.
  • Consider conversion: If the exchange operates in dollars, and you withdraw in rubles, add 2-3% for losses when exchanging currencies in the “Withdrawal commission” field.
  • Long contracts: On many exchanges (for example, Upwork or Kwork), the commission falls as the total earnings with a particular client increase. Don't forget to update the data in the calculator.
