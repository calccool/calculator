Sunrise and sunset calculator by coordinates online

Knowing the exact time the sun appears and sets over the horizon is necessary not only for romantics, but also for professionals: from pilots and sailors to landscape designers and photographers (to search for that very “golden hour”). Our tool uses highly accurate NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration) algorithms to calculate astronomical data with minute-to-minute accuracy.

Unlike simple tables, this calculator takes into account:

  • Geographic latitude and longitude (entered manually or determined automatically);
  • Current equation of time, associated with the eccentricity of the earth's orbit;
  • Declination of the Sun depending on the day of the year;
  • Atmospheric refraction, which visually “raises” the sun above the horizon when it is actually still below it.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the time of sunrise and sunset, the calculation of the hour angle of the Sun is used. The basic formula for determining the moment when the upper edge of the solar disk touches the horizon (taking into account standard refraction in 0.833° ), looks like this:

$$cos(\omega_0) = \frac{cos(90.833^\circ) — sin(\phi) \cdot sin(\delta)}{cos(\phi) \cdot cos(\delta)}$$

Where:

  • $\omega_0$ — hour angle at sunrise/sunset;
  • $\phi$ - geographic latitude of the area;
  • $\delta$ - solar declination (the angle between the rays of the sun and the plane of the earth's equator).

Why does accuracy matter?

Many online services use simplified tables that do not take into account the annual fluctuations of the Earth's orbit. Our calculator updates data dynamically. True noon (the moment of zenith) rarely coincides with 12:00 local time due to the large extent of time zones and the equation of time. Our algorithm calculates this moment individually for each meridian, providing an error of no more than 30 seconds.
