Current stabilizer calculator on LM317: Resistor and power calculation

The LM317 is a legendary adjustable regulator that is most often used for voltage control. However, due to its internal architecture, it is an ideal (and extremely cheap) solution for creating stable current sources.

Our calculator will help you instantly calculate a circuit to power high-power LEDs, laser diodes, or create simple battery chargers.

How does the current stabilizer work on LM317?

The principle of operation of the circuit is ingeniously simple. The LM317 always strives to maintain a fixed reference voltage ( Vref ) size 1.25 Volt between pinsOUT(exit) andADJ(adjustment).

If we connect a resistor between these two terminals, then, according to Ohm's law, a strictly defined current will flow through it. Since the load current flows through the same resistor, it will be stabilized regardless of changes in the input voltage (within reasonable limits).

Calculation formulas (Formulas)

For manual calculation the following basic equations are used:

1. Current calculation (Current Output):

I_out = 1.25 / R

2. Resistor Value calculation:

R = 1.25 / I_out

3. Resistor Power:

P_res = 1.25 * I_out

Where:

  • I_out — desired stabilization current (Amps).
  • R — resistance of the current-setting resistor (Ohms).
  • 1.25 — reference voltage Vref (Volts).

Why do resistors burn? (Important!)

The most common mistake made by beginners is choosing a resistor of the wrong power. Even at low currents, heat is generated at the current-setting resistor.

Example: You need to get a current of 1 Ampere.

Resistor resistance: R = 1.25 / 1 = 1.25 Ohm.

Power across the resistor: P = 1.25V * 1A = 1.25 W.

If you put a standard 0.25 W resistor, it will burn out instantly. Our calculator automatically calculates the power output and tells you which resistor to choose (0.5W, 1W, 2W or a powerful 5W cement resistor).

Practical assembly tips

  1. A radiator is required. If the difference between the input voltage and the load voltage is large, the LM317 will get very hot. All “excess” energy turns into heat.
    • Heat formula: P_heat = (V_in - V_load - 1.25) * I.
  2. Input voltage. For correct operation of the microcircuit, the input voltage must be at least 3 Volts higher than the load voltage (voltage drop across LM317 + Vref).
  3. Capacitors. It is recommended to install a 0.1 µF ceramic capacitor at the input and an electrolytic capacitor (for example, 10-100 µF) at the output to suppress excitation and ripple.
  4. Maximum current. Standard LM317 in TO-220 package is rated for current up to 1.5 A. For high currents, use LM350 (up to 3A) or LM338 (up to 5A) - the formula for calculating the resistor is the same for them.
