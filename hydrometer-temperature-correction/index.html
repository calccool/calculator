Temperature correction calculator for hydrometer readings (spirit meter and sugar meter)

Any household or laboratory hydrometer - be it an alcohol meter ASP-3 or sugar meter AS-3 — calibrated to operate at a strictly defined temperature. In most cases this 20 °C (CIS standard) or 15.56 °C (international standard 60°F).

The essence of the problem lies in the change in liquid density:

  • When heated The liquid expands and its density decreases. The hydrometer dives deeper, indicating an overestimated alcohol content or an underestimated sugar content.
  • When cooling the liquid “compresses”, its density increases. The device is pushed harder, producing erroneous data.

Our calculator automatically takes into account the coefficient of thermal expansion and returns you to the point of truth.

Correction calculation formulas

For those who prefer to understand the mathematics of the process, we use refined coefficients adapted to popular household needs.

Calculation for alcohol meter (ASP-3)

For aqueous-alcoholic solutions, the average correction factor is approximately 0.33% for every degree of deviation.

$$A_{true} = A_{meas} — (T_{curr} — T_{cal}) \cdot 0.33$$

Where:

  • $A_{true}$ - true strength (% vol.);
  • $A_{meas}$ - value on the device scale;
  • $T_{curr}$ - current liquid temperature;
  • $T_{cal}$ - instrument calibration temperature (typically 20 °C).

Calculation for sugar meter (Brix / AC-3)

For sugar-containing solutions (wort), the average correction is 0.06% for every degree.

$$S_{true} = S_{meas} + (T_{curr} — T_{cal}) \cdot 0.06$$

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

  1. Select mode: Switch between "Alcohol Meter" and "Sugar Meter" at the top of the interface.
  2. Enter readings: Enter the number that you see on the hydrometer scale at the time of measurement.
  3. Specify temperature: Enter the actual temperature of the liquid (it is best to use a digital thermometer with an accuracy of 0.1 °C).
  4. Check calibration: Make sure that the calibration temperature (indicated in the device passport or on its scale) matches the one selected in the settings (usually 20 °C).
  5. Get the result: The calculator will instantly show the true value and the amount of correction made.

5 Golden Rules for Accurate Measurement

To obtain the most reliable data, follow the recommendations of professionals:

  • Cleanliness is the key to success: Any dirt on the surface of the hydrometer or residual grease on your fingers will change the weight of the device and the surface tension of the liquid.
  • No bubbles: Bubbles of carbon dioxide or air stuck to the body act like floats, “pushing” the device upward.
  • Thermal equilibrium: Wait 1-2 minutes for the hydrometer and thermometer to warm up (or cool down) to liquid temperature.
  • Meniscus measurement: Take readings along the lower edge of the meniscus (the concave line of fluid) while keeping your eyes level with the surface.
  • Mixing: Before measuring, mix the liquid thoroughly, since the temperature in the upper and lower layers of the container may differ.

Answers to popular questions

Is it possible to measure hot raw alcohol directly from the distiller?

Technically yes, using our calculator. However, in practice, at temperatures above 40 °C, alcohol begins to actively evaporate, which in itself changes its strength. It is recommended to cool the sample to 20–25 °C.

Why does the sugar meter show different values ​​at the beginning and end of fermentation?

A sugar meter measures density. At the beginning of fermentation, the density is high due to sugar. At the end of fermentation, sugar is converted into alcohol (which is lighter than water) and carbon dioxide. The presence of alcohol in finished beer or wine “underestimates” the gravity reading, so to accurately calculate alcohol, the initial and final gravity formulas (OG and FG) are used.
