How to calculate customs duty

The tax assessment process depends on the status of the recipient and the purpose of the import. Our calculator supports two main modes:

1. Parcels for individuals

For goods purchased in foreign online stores for personal use, duty-free import rules apply. The following thresholds are set for 2026:

  • Cost limit: 200 euros per parcel.
  • Weight limit: 31 kg per parcel.

If any of these parameters are exceeded, the obligation to pay a fee arises. The calculation is made according to the principle of “choosing more” between the cost and weight indicators.

Calculation formula for individuals:

$$P = \max((S — 200) \cdot 15\%, (W — 31) \cdot 2€)$$

Where:

  • $P$ - duty amount;
  • $S$ — cost of the parcel in euros;
  • $W$ — parcel weight in kilograms.

2. Commercial cargo (Legal entities and individual entrepreneurs)

For business, the calculation is much more complicated and is based on customs value, which includes not only the price of the product, but also the costs of logistics to the border.

Main types of customs payments

For commercial imports, the total amount consists of several components:

Customs duty

A fixed amount for processing the declaration, the amount of which depends on the total cost of the shipment.

Import duty

Calculated as a percentage of the customs value. The rate depends on the HS code of the product.

$$D = V_{customs} \cdot R_{duty}$$

Import VAT

Value added tax is not charged on the net value of the goods, but on the sum of the cost and the already calculated duty.

$$VAT = (V_{customs} + D) \cdot R_{vat}$$
