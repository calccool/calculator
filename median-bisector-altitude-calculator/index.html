Triangle calculator: Calculate median length, bisector and altitude

Solving geometric problems often requires finding the lengths of the main cevians of a triangle - median, bisector and height. In manual mode, these calculations are cumbersome, as they require sequential application of the cosine theorem, Heron's formula, or Stewart's theorem.

Our engineering calculator automates this process. You only need to know the lengths of three sides ( $a$ , $b$ , $c$ ) to get the exact dimensions of all the lines drawn on the selected base, as well as find out the area of ​​the figure.

Definitions and differences of main lines

To correctly interpret the calculation results, it is important to understand the geometric meaning of each segment. All three lines emerge from the same vertex and descend to the opposite side (base), but perform different functions.

1. Median (m)

A segment connecting the vertex of a triangle to the middle of the opposite side.

  • Main property: Divides the opposite side in half.
  • Sections based on: If the length of the base is $a$ , then the median divides it into two equal segments along $a/2$ .
  • Physical meaning: At the point of intersection of the medians is the center of gravity (centroid) of the triangle.

2. Bisector (l)

A line segment that divides a vertex angle into two equal angles.

  • Main property: Divides the opposite side into segments proportional to the adjacent sides (Bisector theorem).
  • Center of inscribed circle: Lies at the point of intersection of the bisectors (incenter).

3. Height (h)

A perpendicular descended from a vertex to the opposite side or its continuation.

  • Main property: Forms an angle of 90° with the base.
  • Meaning: Is the shortest distance from the vertex to the line containing the base. Used to calculate area.

Mathematical calculation formulas

The algorithm of our calculator uses classical theorems of planimetry. All calculations are made for lines dropped to the $a$ side.

Designations:

  • $a, b, c$ - sides of the triangle.
  • $p$ is the semi-perimeter, where $p = (a + b + c) / 2$ .

Median length formula ( $m_a$ )

For the calculation, Apollonius' theorem is used (a special case of Stewart's theorem):

$$m_a = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{2b^2 + 2c^2 — a^2}$$

Bisector length formula ( $l_a$ )

The most compact way to calculate is through the semi-perimeter:

$$l_a = \frac{2 \sqrt{b \cdot c \cdot p \cdot (p — a)}}{b + c}$$

The calculator also calculates into which segments ( $x$ and $y$ ) the bisector divides the side $a$ :

$$x = \frac{a \cdot c}{b + c}, \quad y = \frac{a \cdot b}{b + c}$$

Length Height Formula ( $h_a$ )

The height is calculated through the area of ​​the triangle ( $S$ ), found using Heron's formula:

$$h_a = \frac{2 \cdot S}{a}$$

Where

$$S = \sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}$$

Instructions for use

  1. Enter sides: Give the lengths of the three sides of the triangle. Make sure they are entered in the same units (cm, mm or meters).
  2. Examination: The calculator will automatically check the "Triangle Inequality" ( $a < b + c$ ). If it is not possible to construct such a figure, you will see an error message.
  3. Choice of base: Please note that the results ( $m_a, l_a, h_a$ ) are calculated for the side you entered in the field "Side a". To find the lines for the other side, simply reverse the values.
  4. Analysis: In the “Side Division” tab you will see exactly which line segments the base is divided into. This is especially useful for solving problems in strength of materials and mechanics.
