Air exchange rate calculator: calculation of room ventilation according to SNiP standards

The air exchange rate is a quantitative indicator that determines how many times within one hour the air in the room is completely replaced with fresh air. Correct calculation of ventilation directly affects the well-being of people, the durability of the finish and the absence of mold.

Insufficient air exchange leads to an increase in the concentration of carbon dioxide ( $CO_2$ ), the accumulation of excess moisture and unpleasant odors. Excessive ventilation increases the load on the heating system in winter and can create uncomfortable drafts.

Key functions of the calculator

  • Geometry calculation: determination of the volume of supply air based on the cubic capacity of the room and the recommended expansion factor.
  • Calculation according to sanitary standards: taking into account the number of people constantly present in the premises.
  • Channel flow rate: determining the air speed in the duct to prevent acoustic noise.
  • Power reserve: automatic addition of 15% reserve for network resistance.

Calculation methods and formulas

The calculator makes calculations using two main methods, after which it selects a larger value (the “maximum flow” principle) to ensure safety and comfort.

1. Calculation by multiplicity (volume)

The formula determines the volume of air based on the physical dimensions of the room:

$$L_1 = V \cdot n$$

Where:

  • $L_1$ - required air flow ( $м^3/ч$ );
  • $V$ - room volume ( $м^3$ ), calculated as Area $\times$ Height;
  • $n$ - standard multiplicity factor (see table above).

2. Calculation based on the number of people

If there are always people in the room, the calculation is carried out according to sanitary and hygienic standards:

$$L_2 = N \cdot m$$

Where:

  • $N$ - number of people;
  • $m$ - air consumption rate per person (on average 60 $м^3/ч$ for vigorous activity).

3. Calculation of air speed in the channel

To prevent the ventilation system from “humming,” the air speed in the main air ducts should not exceed certain values ​​(optimally 2.5–4 m/s).

$$v = \frac{L}{3600 \cdot S}$$

Where:

  • $v$ - air speed ( $м/с$ );
  • $L$ - air flow ( $м^3/ч$ );
  • $S$ - cross-sectional area of ​​the duct ( $м^2$ ).

How to choose the diameter of the duct

When choosing system components, it is important to maintain a balance between compactness and noise level. If the calculator shows a speed above 3–4 m/s, this is a signal that the channel diameter needs to be increased.

Basic recommendations for selection:

  • Diameter 100mm: suitable for small bathrooms and hoods with flow rates up to 80–100 $м^3/ч$ .
  • Diameter 125 mm: standard for kitchens and living rooms up to 20 $м^2$ .
  • Diameter 160 mm or more: used in main lines of air handling units.

Using this calculator allows you to avoid gross design errors and guarantees that the selected fan will cope with the task even taking into account aerodynamic losses in filters and grilles.
