Professional bathroom tile calculator: Calculation of walls and floors, taking into account openings and hidden areas

Accurate calculation of materials before starting a bathroom renovation is a guarantee of budget savings. Our bathroom tile calculator allows you to avoid two main problems: a shortage of material in the midst of work (when the required batch may no longer be in the store) and the purchase of extra boxes, which then cannot be returned.

The algorithm takes into account not only the total area, but also technological nuances: doorways, windows, hidden areas behind plumbing fixtures and the safety factor for trimming.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

To get the correct estimate, you will need a tape measure and a few minutes of time. The calculation is carried out in three stages.

Step 1: Room dimensions

Enter the length, width and height of the room.

  • Important: Measure the walls at three points (bottom, middle and top). If the values ​​differ, take greatest to calculate tiles to avoid bald spots, or average for leveling walls.
  • Trimming margin: This is a critical parameter. Tiles never lie perfectly in one piece. Some of the material will go to waste when cutting.

Step 2: Deductions and Dead Zones

You don't have to pay for tiles that won't be on the wall.

  1. Doors and windows: Specify the dimensions of the openings, and the algorithm will subtract them from the total area of ​​the walls.
  2. Area behind the bathroom: If you plan to install the bathtub against a wall and cover it with a screen, laying tiles behind and under it is not practical. Activate the option "Don't put tiles behind the bathtub" to save up to 2-3 m² of expensive material.

Step 3: Material Parameters

Tiles are sold not only by the meter, but also in packages (boxes). Indicate the footage of one package (usually indicated on the price tag), and the calculator will round the required quantity to whole packs.

Formulas for calculating area and perimeter

To understand the logic of the tool’s operation, we present the mathematical formulas underlying the calculations.

1. Calculation of floor area ( $S_{floor}$ ):

$$S_{floor} = (L \times W) — S_{exclusion}$$

Where $L$ is the length, $W$ is the width, $S_{exclusion}$ is the area of ​​the areas where the tiles do not fit (for example, under the bathroom).

2. Calculation of wall area ( $S_{walls}$ ):

$$S_{walls} = [2 \times (L + W) \times H] — S_{door} — S_{window}$$

Where $H$ is the ceiling height (or the height of the tiles).

3. Total quantity with reserve ( $S_{total}$ ):

$$S_{total} = S_{net} \times K_{waste}$$

Where $K_{waste}$ is the safety factor.

The percentage of reserve directly depends on the method of laying out the tiles and the geometry of the room. Using the wrong coefficient is the most common mistake made by beginners.

Why is it important to count in packages and not in pieces?

When purchasing ceramic tiles or porcelain tiles, sellers use the concept of “batch” or “tone.” Different batches of the same collection may differ slightly in shade or caliber (actual size).

Expert advice: Always buy tiles in multiples of boxes and make sure all boxes have the same Batch Number. Our calculator automatically converts the required square meters into the number of packages, rounding up the value.

Calculation of related materials

In addition to the tiles themselves, bathroom renovations require chemicals. Approximate consumption rates are given below:

  • Tile adhesive: Average consumption is 3-5 kg ​​per 1 m² when using a comb (notched trowel) 6-8 mm. For large-format tiles and a thick layer of adhesive, consumption can increase to 7-8 kg.
  • Grout (Fuge): Depends on the width of the seam, the length and thickness of the tile. For a standard 30x30 cm tile with a 2 mm joint, you will need approximately 0.4 kg/m².
