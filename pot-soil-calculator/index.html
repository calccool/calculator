Soil volume calculator for flower pots: round and square

Transplanting plants is a responsible process, where accuracy in calculations helps not only to save on the purchase of excess land, but also to create ideal conditions for the root system. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate how many liters of substrate you will need for your home greenhouse.

How to calculate the volume of land for replanting

Most flower pots are shaped like a truncated cone (round) or a truncated pyramid (square). This means that their upper diameter is larger than the lower. A simple calculation using the cylinder formula will not work here - the error can be up to 20-30%.

Our tool takes into account the geometry of the “correct” pot, and also allows you to subtract the volume of the drainage layer and add the proportion of raising agents such as perlite or vermiculite.

Mathematical calculation formulas

To ensure maximum accuracy, the calculator uses geometric formulas for the volume of truncated figures.

For round pots (truncated cone):

The volume of $V$ is calculated through the upper radius of $R$ , the lower radius of $r$ and the height of $h$ :

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \pi h (R^2 + R \cdot r + r^2)$$

For square pots (truncated pyramid):

The sides of the top ( $A$ ) and bottom ( $a$ ) bases are used:

$$V = \frac{1}{3} h (A^2 + A \cdot a + a^2)$$

The result is converted from cubic centimeters to liters by dividing by 1000.

Why it's important to consider drainage and additives

  1. Drainage layer: Expanded clay or pebbles at the bottom of the pot occupy useful volume. If you put 3 cm of drainage in a 15 cm high pot, you will need 20% less land than meets the eye.
  2. Leavening agents: Adding perlite makes the soil breathable. If you plan to mix soil with perlite in a ratio of 1:4, then a quarter of the volume of your pot will be taken up by baking powder.
  3. Number of pots: During a mass transplant (for example, 10–20 identical pots), even a small error in calculations of 0.5 liters will lead to a shortage of a whole package of soil.

How to use an online calculator

To get an accurate result, follow these steps:

  • Select the shape of your container (Round or Square tabs).
  • Measure the internal parameters: top and bottom width, as well as height (measure exactly the inside where the ground will be).
  • Specify the planned drainage layer in centimeters.
  • If you are using perlite or vermiculite, indicate the percentage (for example, 20%).
  • Indicate the total number of pots if they are the same.

The calculator will instantly display the final values: how many liters of clean soil, additives and drainage you need to buy in the store.
