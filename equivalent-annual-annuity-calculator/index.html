Equivalent Annuity (EAA) Calculator: Comparing Projects of Different Durations

In investment analysis and corporate finance, a problem often arises: how to choose the best project if one brings profit within 3 years, and the second within 7 years? Simple comparison NPV (Net Present Value) doesn't work here because it favors "long" projects.

To solve this problem it is used Equivalent Annual Annuity (EAA) method. Our online calculator allows you to instantly bring the cash flows of projects to a single annual denominator, making them comparable.

What is an Equivalent Annuity (EAA)?

EAA is the amount of constant annual cash flow that provides the same net present value (NPV) as the project being analyzed.

In simple words, the EAA method answers the question: “If this project were a bank deposit with annual interest payments, how much would we receive per year?”. This allows the performance of short and long investments to be compared, assuming they can be repeated (reinvested) at maturity.

Calculation formula

Mathematically, the calculation of EAA comes down to the distribution of NPV over the years of the project's life, taking into account the time value of money. The formula looks like this:

$$EAA = \frac{NPV \cdot r}{1 — (1 + r)^{-n}}$$

Where:

  • NPV — Net present value of the project.
  • r — Discount rate (WACC or required return) for the period.
  • n — Number of periods (project lifespan).

Denominator Expression

$$\frac{1 — (1 + r)^{-n}}{r}$$

also known as Annuity factor (PVIFA).
