Drug Abuse Test (DAST-20): Online Screening and Addiction Assessment

This tool is a digital version Drug Abuse Screening Test (DAST-20) - one of the most authoritative methods in the world for the initial diagnosis of problems with psychoactive substances. The test is designed for self-diagnosis and use by professionals to quickly assess a person's level of involvement in drug use (excluding alcohol and tobacco) over the past 12 months.

Our algorithm analyzes your answers and automatically calculates a dependency index, providing instant interpretation of the result.

How does the DAST-20 technique work?

The questionnaire consists of 20 items relating to various aspects of life: health, social connections, problems with the law and psychological state. Each positive answer (or negative answer in the ability to stop question) adds 1 point to the total score.

Risk calculation formula:

The algorithm is based on a simple summation of significant responses:

$$Total Score = \sum_{i=1}^{20} (Answer_i)$$

Where $Answer_i$ is 1 if the response indicates there is a problem, and 0 if not. The higher the total amount, the more serious the degree of dependence.

Why is it important to take this test?

Screening for drug abuse can identify the problem at an early stage, when it can still be easily corrected.

  • Anonymity: You answer questions in private, without outside pressure.
  • Objectivity: The test excludes emotional assessment, relying only on the facts of your behavior.
  • Impact assessment: The questions address not only the fact of use, but also its impact on family, work and health.

Important: This online calculator is a screening tool and not a definitive medical diagnosis. A high score indicates the need to contact a professional narcologist or psychotherapist to conduct a full examination.
