Date calculator for next meter verification (water, gas, electricity)

This tool allows you to instantly calculate the exact date when your meter must undergo the next metrological certification. Control calibration interval helps to avoid overpayments according to standards and penalties from resource supply organizations.

How to calculate the verification period online

To get an accurate result, you do not need to study the technical data sheet of the device. All you need to do is follow three simple steps:

  1. Select resource type: Tab “Water meters” or “Gas/electricity meters”. This will automatically load the most common intervals.
  2. Indicate the date of the last verification: This date can be found in the work completion report or in the service provider’s personal account.
  3. Set the interval: If your device is specific, select the value in years manually (from 4 to 16 years).

After pressing the button "Calculate" The calculator will not only give you the final date, but also visualize a timeline, showing what percentage of the deadline has already passed.

Formula for calculating the verification date

The mathematical calculation model is based on adding a calendar calibration interval to the date of the initial or previous certification. The formula looks like this:

$$D_{next} = D_{last} + (I_{years} \times Y_{days})$$

Where:

  • $D_{next}$ — estimated date of the next verification;
  • $D_{last}$ — date of the last recorded verification;
  • $I_{years}$ —verification interval in years;
  • $Y_{days}$ — correction for leap years in the specified period.

Why is it important to meet deadlines?

If the procedure is not carried out on time, the device is officially considered unsuitable for calculations. Consequences of missing a date:

  • Conversion to average consumption: For the first 3 months, you are charged based on your six-month average.
  • Transition to standard: After 3 months, the calculation is carried out according to the standard using an increasing factor of 1.5.
  • Loss of precision: Over time, the mechanical parts wear out, and the meter can begin to “lie” both in favor of the consumer and against him.

Answers to frequently asked questions

Where can I find the date of the last verification?

The most reliable source is the federal system FSIS "Arshin". Also, the date is always indicated in the paper verification certificate or in the device passport (if the verification was factory-made).

Do I need to change the meter instead of checking it?

Verification costs much less than replacement. However, if the instrument is more than 12 years old, the likelihood that it will fail metrological tests increases to 40%. In this case, replacement will be a more rational solution.

What does the “Verification Soon” status mean in the calculator?

Our algorithm marks the result in yellow if there is less time left until the critical date 60 days. This is the ideal time to apply to an accredited laboratory.
