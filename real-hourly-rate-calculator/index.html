Calculator of the real cost of an hour of your work: how much do you actually earn?

Most employees make a standard mistake when calculating the cost of their hour: they simply divide the monthly salary by 160 working hours. However, this figure has nothing to do with reality. To get the true value of your time, you need to take into account factors that “eat up” either your money or your free time.

Our calculator conducts an in-depth audit of your employment, separating dry numbers in the contract and actual benefits.

Hidden “taxes” on your activities

Every working day is accompanied by expenses that we are accustomed to consider as household expenses, but in fact they are an investment in the ability to work:

  • Transport costs: gasoline, car depreciation or a public transport pass.
  • Working food: difference between the cost of a home lunch and a business lunch.
  • Dress code: purchasing and maintaining clothes that you would not wear in your free time.
  • Paid services: subscriptions or services necessary solely for the performance of professional tasks.

Temporary traps

Time is a non-renewable resource. If you spend 2 hours a day on the way to the office, your working day lasts not 8, but 10 hours. The nominal rate falls in proportion to the time spent in traffic jams or the subway.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculation uses a formula that combines annual income and actual time costs. We rely on an average of 247 working days per year (for the Russian Federation) minus your paid leave.

$$\text{Реальная ставка} = \frac{(I_{month} \times 12) — (E_{month} \times 12)}{H_{nominal} + H_{commute} + H_{overtime}}$$

Where:

  • $I_{month}$ - your nominal take-home salary.
  • $E_{month}$ - monthly work-related expenses.
  • $H_{nominal}$ - working hours under the contract for the year (including vacation).
  • $H_{commute}$ - total travel time for the year.
  • $H_{overtime}$ - unpaid overtime for the year.

How to use the calculator

The tool supports two modes: Standard 5/2 for office workers and Flexible schedule for freelancers or those who work in a project system.

  1. Select mode: indicate the format of your employment.
  2. Enter basic data: your net salary and standard hours.
  3. Please indicate your holiday: the calculator will automatically subtract these days from your total working time, since they are paid but do not require attendance.
  4. Turn on "Hidden Costs": this is the most important part. Enter travel time in minutes (round trip) and average monthly expenses.
  5. Explore the visualization: the system will build a time distribution chart and show the percentage of income that is spent on “maintenance” of the work.

How to increase the value of your hour?

If the calculation shows that your actual rate is significantly lower than expected, there are three ways to optimize:

  • Transition to remote work: instantly eliminates travel costs and reduces food and clothing costs.
  • Logistics optimization: if the office is unavoidable, try staggering your schedule to avoid wasting time during peak traffic times.
  • Monetization of processing: any hours in excess must either be paid or eliminated. Every unpaid hour steals money from your base rate.

Use our calculator when looking for a new job: sometimes a vacancy with a lower salary, but 15 minutes from home, is more profitable than a highly paid position on the other side of the metropolis.
