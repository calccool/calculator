Unit conversion formulas

For those who perform calculations manually or check data in Excel, we have prepared basic conversion formulas. The SI standard unit is Watt per meter Kelvin ( $W/(m \cdot K)$ ).

Translation to SI system:

  • From kcal: $$1 \text{ kcal/(h} \cdot \text{m} \cdot ^\circ\text{C)} = 1.1622 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)}$$
  • From BTU (Imperial system): $$1 \text{ BTU/(h} \cdot \text{ft} \cdot ^\circ\text{F)} = 1.7307 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)}$$
  • From calories (CGS): $$1 \text{ cal/(s} \cdot \text{cm} \cdot ^\circ\text{C)} = 418.68 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)}$$

Translation from the SI system (reverse):

  • $$1 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)} \approx 0.8604 \text{ kcal/(h} \cdot \text{m} \cdot ^\circ\text{C)}$$
  • $$1 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)} \approx 0.5778 \text{ BTU/(h} \cdot \text{ft} \cdot ^\circ\text{F)}$$
  • $$1 \text{ W/(m} \cdot \text{K)} \approx 100 \text{ W/(cm} \cdot \text{K)}$$

Analysis of units of measurement

Depending on the industry and country, you may come across different designations:

  1. W/(m K) or W/(m K) — International Standard (SI). Used in most modern scientific works and building codes (SP, GOST, ISO).
  2. BTU/(h·ft·°F) - US and UK standard. Often found in specifications for imported air conditioners, heat exchangers and insulating materials.
  3. kcal/(h·m·°C) — Often found in old technical documentation and thermal power engineering of the CIS countries.
  4. W/(cm·K) — Used in microelectronics and semiconductor physics, where the size of objects is small (centimeters instead of meters).
