Feeding calculator for infants: Formula calculation by weight and age

How to calculate the amount of formula or milk for a baby?

Correctly calculating the amount of food is a critical issue for parents of bottle-fed or mixed-fed children. Underfeeding can lead to weight loss and dehydration, while overfeeding can lead to regurgitation, colic and metabolic disorders.

Our calculator uses official pediatric formulas accepted in neonatology to determine a child's daily nutritional needs depending on his age (in days or months) and exact weight.

Calculation methods for newborns (0–10 days)

In the first days of life, the volume of a baby's stomach changes rapidly. To calculate the daily value ( $V$ ), special formulas are used, depending on birth weight and day of life ( $N$ ).

Finkelstein formula (Tour modification)

Used for children born weighing 3200 g or more:

$$V = 80 \cdot N$$

For children weighing less than 3200 g:

$$V = 70 \cdot N$$

Zaitseva formula

A more universal method that calculates volume as a percentage of body weight ( $M$ ):

$$V = 0.02 \cdot M \cdot N$$

Calculation for children from 10 days to 1 year (Heubner-Czerny method)

This is the “gold standard” of the volumetric method in pediatrics. Nutritional requirements are calculated as a proportion of the child's actual body weight.

Important: The daily volume of formula or breast milk should not exceed 1000 ml (1 liter) in the second half of life, since the rest of the volume is replenished with complementary foods.

How to determine a single serving?

To find out how much a baby should eat at one feeding, you need to divide the daily volume by the number of feedings.

$$V_{single} = \frac{V_{daily}}{N_{feeds}}$$

Recommended feeding frequency:

  • 0–2 months: 7–8 times (including night feedings for breastfeeding).
  • 2–5 months: 6 times (every 3.5 hours).
  • 5–9 months: 5 times (every 4 hours).
  • 9–12 months: 5 times.

Energy method (calorie check)

To more accurately select nutrition, especially for low birth weight children, pediatricians use Maslov’s caloric method. It determines the energy requirement per 1 kg of weight:

  • 1st quarter (0-3 months): 115 kcal/kg
  • 2nd quarter (3-6 months): 115 kcal/kg
  • 3rd quarter (6-9 months): 110 kcal/kg
  • 4th quarter (9-12 months): 100 kcal/kg

Our calculator automatically cross-checks and provides calorie reference data based on the average energy value of the adapted mixtures (about 67 kcal/100 ml).

Note: This calculation is indicative. When breastfeeding (BF), it is recommended to feed the baby “on demand” without limiting the time spent at the breast. For bottle-fed children (IV), strict adherence to dosages is mandatory.
