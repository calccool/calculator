How does the cardiac output calculator work?

The calculations are based on fundamental hemodynamic parameters used in cardiology. The calculator takes into account two key parameters:

  1. HR (Heart Rate): Number of beats per minute. At rest in an adult this is usually 60–80 beats.
  2. SV (Stroke Volume): The amount of blood ejected by a ventricle of the heart in one contraction.

Formula for calculating heart performance

To determine Minute Volume of Blood Circulation (MCV), which in international medicine is called Cardiac Output (CO), the following formula is used:

$$МОК = ЧСС \times УО$$

Where:

  • IOC — blood volume per minute (l/min).
  • Heart rate — pulse rate (bpm).
  • UO — stroke volume (liters).

To calculate the volume over longer periods of time (day, year, life), we multiply the minute indicator by the corresponding time coefficients.

Interesting facts about how your heart works

The calculation results may be shocking. Here are a few facts to help visualize the numbers:

  • In 1 minute the heart pumps the entire volume of blood available in the body (about 5 liters).
  • Per day the order passes through your vessels 7,000 – 10,000 liters. This is enough to fill a small tanker truck.
  • For an average life (75 years) the heart makes approx. 2.5 – 3 billion cuts.
  • Energy: The heart produces enough energy every day to drive a truck 30 km. In a lifetime, this energy would be enough to fly to the moon and back.

Why is it important to know your IOC?

Minute volume of blood circulation is a measure of the efficiency of your cardiovascular system.

  • High resting heart rate low stroke volume means that the heart is working overload (“strain”) to provide oxygen to the organs.
  • Low resting heart rate (bradycardia of athletes) with a high stroke volume indicates a powerful, trained heart that works in an economical mode, conserving its resource.
