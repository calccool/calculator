Epoxy resin proportions calculator: calculation by weight and volume online

Working with epoxy resin requires pharmaceutical precision. An error in proportions of just 2-3% can result in the product remaining sticky, cloudy, or not hardening at all. This online calculator is designed to allow you to instantly obtain an accurate weighing of components based on your conditions: the total mass of the mixture or the available stock of one of the components.

Basic calculation modes

  • By weight (g): The most accurate method. The resin and hardener almost always differ in density, so using a scale is the gold standard for DIYers.
  • By volume (ml): Used less frequently, mainly for formulations where the manufacturer has directly indicated the volume ratio (for example, 2:1 in measuring cups).
  • Dye accounting: Our tool allows you to calculate the mass of pigment so that the final volume of the mixture exactly matches your shape.

Mathematical calculation formulas

To understand the process, it is important to know exactly how the parts are distributed in the mixture. If the manufacturer specifies the ratio as $A:B$ (for example, 100:35), the calculation of components for the total weight of $M$ is performed using the following formulas:

$$W_A = M \cdot \frac{A}{A + B}$$

$$W_B = M \cdot \frac{B}{A + B}$$

Where:

  • $W_A$ - required resin mass;
  • $W_B$ - required mass of hardener;
  • $M$ - target mass of the finished mixture;
  • $A, B$ - parts of the proportion (for example, 100 and 50).

If you use pigment, its mass $W_p$ is calculated from the total base:

$$W_p = (W_A + W_B) \cdot \frac{P}{100}$$

(where $P$ is the percentage of dye concentration).

Subtleties of mixing: expert advice

To ensure that your product turns out perfect, follow the proven operating algorithm.

Preparation and temperature conditions

Epoxy resin is temperature sensitive. Ideal working conditions - 22–25°C. If the resin is too thick, you can gently warm it up in warm water (no higher than 40°C), but remember: this significantly speeds up the reaction and shortens the pot life of the mixture.

Rule of "Two glasses"

This is a critical step to prevent the appearance of “no-kneads” - sticky areas on the finished product:

  1. Measure components A and B into the first glass.
  2. Mix thoroughly for 3-5 minutes, scraping the resin from the sides and bottom.
  3. Pour the mixture into a second clean glass and stir again for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Only then add pigments or pour them into the mold.

Working with air bubbles

  • Mix slowly and smoothly, avoiding agitating the resin.
  • Use a corrector (spray) or a gas torch to remove bubbles from the surface after pouring.
  • For deep pours, use only special low-viscosity resins designed for thick layers.

Safety precautions

Always work in a well-ventilated area. Use nitrile gloves and a respirator with organic vapor filters (marked A1 or A2). If resin gets on your skin, immediately remove it with a cloth and wash with soap and water.
