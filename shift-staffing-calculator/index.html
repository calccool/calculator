Shift Work Personnel Calculator: Calculate FTE and Reserve

How to calculate the exact number of personnel for shift work

Planning staff for continuous (24/7) or multi-shift work is one of the most difficult tasks in HR management. A common mistake is to use simple arithmetic: “3 x 8 hour shifts = 3 people.” This approach inevitably leads to staffing shortages, employee burnout, and schedule disruptions.

Why? This calculation does not take into account:

  • Weekends: Employees cannot work 7 days a week.
  • Standard hours: A standard work week (eg 40 hours) is always less than the total time that needs to be covered (168 hours per week for 24/7).
  • Absences: People go on vacation, get sick (sick leave) or take time off.

This calculator uses a professional HR approach to calculate FTE (Full-Time Equivalent) - full-time equivalent - to determine your real staffing needs.

1. Calculation of total requirement (Man-hours)

The first step is to determine how many total work hours your staff needs to cover in one week. The calculator does this based on the data you enter.

  • $H_{wd}$ (Weekday Hours): General hours on weekdays (5 days). $$ H_{wd} = (S_{day} + S_{eve} + S_{night})_{wd} \times L_{shift} \times 5$$
  • $H_{we}$ (Weekend Hours): General hours on weekends (2 days). $$ H_{we} = (S_{day} + S_{eve} + S_{night})_{we} \times L_{shift} \times 2$$
  • $H_{total}$ (Total Hours): Total hours required for the week. $$ H_{total} = H_{wd} + H_{we}$$

Where:

  • $S_{day}, S_{eve}, S_{night}$ - number of employees on day, evening and night shifts.
  • $L_{shift}$ - duration of one shift (for example, 8 or 12 hours).

2. Basic requirement calculation (FTE)

FTE (Full Time Equivalent) - this is not the number of people, but the number rates required to complete the job based on standard hours.

$$FTE_{base} = \frac{H_{total}}{H_{norm}}$$

  • $H_{norm}$ - Your target hours per employee per week (usually 40, but could be 36 or 32).

For example, to cover 168 hours per week (24/7) at a 40-hour rate, you would need:

$168 / 40 = 4.2$ FTE.

3. Calculation of Recommended Staff (FTE with reserve)

The base FTE shows how many bets would be needed if people never got sick or went on vacation. To make the schedule stable, you need to add "reserve percentage".

This reserve covers planned and unscheduled absences. The standard minimum reserve in the industry is 15-20%.

$$FTE_{rec} = FTE_{base} \times (1 + \frac{P_{buffer}}{100})$$

  • $P_{buffer}$ - your reserve percentage (for example, 15%).

Continuing the example: $4.2 \text{ FTE} \times (1 + 0.15) = 4.83 \text{ FTE}$ .

This means that to consistently cover the schedule 24/7 you need not 4, but 5 employees.
