Calculator for calculating the power and pressure of a pump for a well (Online selection)

Choosing a pump “by eye” or on the advice of a neighbor is the most common cause of problems with water supply in a private home. An error of just 10-15% in calculations can lead to two critical scenarios:

  1. Insufficient pressure: The water simply will not reach the second floor, or the shower will run in a thin stream when the tap is open in the kitchen.
  2. Excess power: The pump will operate in a “start-stop” mode, which will lead to its rapid wear, excessive energy consumption and the risk of draining the well (exceeding the flow rate).

Our calculator uses an engineering calculation method that takes into account not only the depth of immersion, but also the horizontal resistance of the line, the diameter of the pipes and the desired pressure at the end point.

Basic parameters for pump calculation

To select the correct equipment, it is necessary to operate on two key characteristics: Productivity (Q) And Pressure (H).

1. Calculation of productivity (Flow)

The pump capacity must cover the peak water flow in the house, but strictly not exceed the flow rate of the well. If the pump pumps more powerfully than the well is capable of producing water, the dry-running protection will work or the equipment will fail.

2. Calculation of the required pressure

This is the most difficult part where mistakes are often made. Pressure is not just the depth of the well. This is the sum of all the resistance that the pump motor must overcome in order to deliver water to the consumer at the required pressure.

Formula for calculating total head:

$$H_{total} = H_{geo} + H_{loss} + H_{pressure} + 20\%$$

Where:

  • $H_{geo}$ - Geometric height (height difference from the water surface to the highest point of water intake).
  • $H_{loss}$ - Hydraulic losses in the pipeline (depending on the material, length and diameter of the pipe).
  • $H_{pressure}$ - Required system pressure (usually 2.5 - 3 bar, equivalent to 25-30 meters of water column).

How to use the calculation results?

After entering the data, the calculator will give two values:

  1. Required head (m): Look for this value in the pump passport or on the graph of its pressure-flow characteristics (performance curve).
  2. Nominal flow (m³/h): This is the volume of water that the pump should deliver exactly at the calculated pressure.

Example: If the calculator showed a Head of 60 m and a Flow of 2.5 m³/h, a pump with the characteristics “Max. head 60 m, Max. flow rate 3 m³/h.” You need a pump that, when raised 60 meters, is still capable of delivering 2.5 cubic meters. Typically these are models with a maximum pressure of 85-90 meters.

Useful tips

  • Hydraulic accumulator: The calculation is carried out for peak consumption. To smooth out water hammer and reduce the frequency of pump starts, be sure to install a hydraulic accumulator with a volume of 50 to 100 liters.
  • Voltage stabilizer: Borehole pumps are sensitive to voltage fluctuations. A 10% drop in voltage can reduce pump head by 25%.
  • Check valve: Do not forget that installation of a check valve is required immediately after the pump (if it is not built-in), otherwise the column of water will flow back into the well.
