Rectangular greenhouse calculator: Calculation of frame, polycarbonate and foundation

Building a greenhouse with your own hands begins with a competent project. An error in calculations of even 10 centimeters can lead to overuse of expensive cellular polycarbonate or a shortage of profile pipe at the most crucial moment. Our rectangular greenhouse calculator allows you to instantly create an estimate of materials for a classic structure with a gable roof (“house”).

The tool automatically takes into account the cutting of standard sheathing sheets and helps to plan the interior space (beds).

What does this calculator calculate?

Unlike simple scripts, we analyze the design comprehensively. You receive data in four key areas:

  1. Geometry and Areas:
    • Ground area: Useful for calculating the amount of seedlings, fertilizers and soil.
    • Air volume: Necessary for choosing a heating system (stove or convector).
    • Glazing area: The total area of ​​all walls and roof slopes to be covered with film or glass.
  2. Polycarbonate:
    • The algorithm calculates the optimal number of sheets (standard 2.1x6m or 2.1x12m) taking into account the margin for trimmings (10%).
  3. Frame (Rolled metal/timber):
    • Calculation of the total length of the profile, including the lower and upper trim, wall posts, rafter system and ridge.
  4. Foundation:
    • Calculation of the volume of concrete mixture for pouring a shallow strip foundation.

Calculation Formulas

To understand how the estimate is formed, we present the main mathematical dependencies used in the algorithm.

1. Calculation of glazing area (Total Surface Area)

For a rectangular greenhouse with a gable roof, the total area is the sum of the area of ​​the walls and the area of ​​the roof. The most difficult moment is calculating the length of the roof slope ( $L_{slope}$ ) using the Pythagorean theorem:

$$L_{slope} = \sqrt{(\frac{Width}{2})^2 + H_{roof}^2}$$

Where:

  • $Width$ - greenhouse width;
  • $H_{roof}$ - ridge height (difference between total height and wall height).

The total coverage area ( $S_{total}$ ) is calculated as:

$$S_{total} = 2 \cdot (L \cdot H_{wall}) + 2 \cdot (W \cdot H_{wall}) + 2 \cdot (L \cdot L_{slope}) + (W \cdot H_{roof})$$

2. Calculation of concrete volume (Foundation Volume)

If you are planning a permanent building, you need a strip foundation. Its volume ( $V_{beton}$ ) is calculated along the perimeter ( $P$ ):

$$V_{beton} = P \cdot Width_{tape} \cdot Depth_{tape}$$

Tips for planning beds

Our calculator also suggests the optimal number of beds depending on the width of the structure:

  • Width 2 - 2.5 m: Two side beds (80-90 cm wide) and one central aisle.
  • Width 3m: Either 2 wide beds, or 3 narrow (in the shape of the letter “W”) with two aisles. This arrangement is more convenient for accessing plants near the walls.
  • Width 4+ m: Allows you to organize 3-4 full-fledged beds for industrial cultivation.
