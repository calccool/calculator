Daily protein calculator: Accurate calculation for gaining weight and losing weight

Protein is the main building material of the body. It is necessary not only for professional bodybuilders, but also for every person to maintain immunity, hormonal balance and tissue repair. However, there is no universal figure “for everyone”.

Our calculator uses adaptive coefficients recommended International Society of Sports Nutrition (ISSN) And World Health Organization (WHO) to determine your individual need.

Formula for calculating protein needs

The basic principle of calculation is based on body weight and physical activity coefficient.

The basic formula looks like this:

$$P_{daily} = W \times K_{activity}$$

Where:

  • $P_{daily}$ - daily protein requirement (g).
  • $W$ - body weight (kg). If the fat percentage is high (>25-30%), the calculation is adjusted to Lean body mass (LBM).
  • $K_{activity}$ - coefficient depending on the target and loads.

Why is fat percentage important?

One of the common mistakes of standard formulas is the calculation for general body weight.

Example: A 120 kg obese person does not need 240 g of protein (2 g/kg). This will create unnecessary stress on the gastrointestinal tract and kidneys.

Our algorithm can adjust the calculation. If you enter your fat percentage, the calculator will calculate LBM (Lean Body Mass) — weight excluding adipose tissue — and will make recommendations based on metabolically active mass.

$$LBM = Weight \times (1 — \frac{BodyFat\%}{100})$$

Protein sources: What to include in your diet

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I count protein on rest days?

Yes. Muscles grow and repair during rest (especially during sleep), and not during training. Reducing your protein intake on non-training days is a mistake that will slow down your progress.

Is excess protein dangerous?

For healthy people with normally functioning kidneys, consumption of up to 2.5–3.0 g per kg of body weight is considered safe. However, exceeding this norm usually does not provide additional benefits for muscle growth and is simply oxidized by the body for energy.

How to distribute the norm throughout the day?

It is optimal to divide the daily intake into 3-5 meals. Research shows that 20-40 grams of quality protein per meal is enough to trigger muscle protein synthesis.

Recommendations for using the calculator

  1. Weigh yourself in the morning on an empty stomach to obtain accurate data.
  2. Evaluate the activity honestly. If you go to the gym once a week, choose “Light activity” rather than “Muscle building”.
  3. Watch your calories. Protein is part of the equation. To gain weight you need a calorie surplus, to lose weight you need a deficit.
