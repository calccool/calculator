Cyberpunk 2077 Armor and Survival Calculator (EHP 2.0+)

After the global update, the defense mechanics in Night City have changed dramatically. Now armor doesn’t just give abstract numbers, but is directly tied to implants and character level. Understanding how much damage you can absorb is critical to completing on Hard difficulty.

The main indicator that our calculator calculates is Effective Health Pool (EHP). This value shows the actual amount of "pure" damage that enemies must deal to reset your health bar.

Main protection components

To accurately calculate survival, three key factors must be taken into account:

  • Health Points (HP): Your body's base safety factor.
  • Armor indicator: Reduces incoming physical damage. In the current version of the game, armor is mainly obtained through the installation of cyber implants (skeleton, skin).
  • Compensation system (Mitigation): A mechanic that allows you to block a significant portion of any incoming damage (not just physical) with a certain probability.

Protection calculation formulas

For those who want to thoroughly understand the mathematics of survival, our tool uses the following algorithms corresponding to the game code:

  1. Calculation of armor damage reduction: Armor has a non-linear effectiveness, but for most high-level builds the formula is: $$Armor_{red} = \min\left(\frac{Armor}{1500} \cdot 85, 85\right)$$ Where $Armor_{red}$ is the percentage of physical damage reduction, capped at 85%.
  2. Effective average reduction (including compensation): If you have compensation activated, the calculation becomes more complicated: $$Total_{multi} = (1 — \frac{Armor_{red}}{100}) \cdot (1 — \frac{M_{chance}}{100} \cdot \frac{M_{strength}}{100})$$ Where $M_{chance}$ is the chance of compensation, and $M_{strength}$ is its strength (basically 50%).
  3. Total EHP value: $$EHP = \frac{HP}{Total_{multi}}$$

What is a damage compensation system?

Compensation is an advanced layer of protection that is often underestimated by players. Unlike armor, it works as a “smart shield”.

Mitigation Chance

This is the probability that the defense effect will trigger when hit. This parameter can be overclocked using implants such as “Optical Camouflage” (with certain perks) or through the “Technique” ability branch.

Mitigation Strength

The percentage of damage that will be cut off when the chance is triggered. The base value is 50%, but it can be increased, making the character practically invulnerable when the effect is activated.

Instructions for using the calculator

To optimize your build, follow these steps:

  1. Enter HP: Look at the current value in the character menu.
  2. Specify Armor: The total value of all installed implants.
  3. Select mode: “Survival Analysis” will show your overall durability, and “Shock Simulation” will allow you to see how much health a specific enemy shot (for example, a sniper) will take from you.
  4. Set up compensation: If you use perks from the “Strength” or “Technique” branches for compensation, be sure to include this item for correct EHP data.

Use the findings to decide whether to replace one implant with another. Sometimes a small increase in compensation chance will provide more survivability than an additional 100 points of pure armor.
