Wilks and DOTS calculator online: calculating points for powerlifting

In powerlifting, the absolute weight lifted does not always reflect the actual strength of the athlete. To objectively compare an athlete weighing 70 kg and a heavyweight 120 kg, special mathematical coefficients are used. Our online tool allows you to instantly calculate two of the most popular indices: classic Wilks coefficient and modern DOTS standard.

DOTS Factor: Modern IPF Standard

Since 2020, the International Powerlifting Federation (IPF) has switched to the DOTS (Dynamic Objective Team Scoring) system. This formula is considered fairer because it smooths out the statistical anomalies that in the old Wilks system gave an advantage to either very light or super heavy athletes.

The DOTS formula for calculating points is as follows:

$$Score = \frac{500 \cdot Total}{Ax^4 + Bx^3 + Cx^2 + Dx + E}$$

Where:

  • $Total$ - triathlon total (squat + bench press + deadlift) in kilograms;
  • $x$ - athlete’s own weight;
  • $A, B, C, D, E$ - specific coefficients depending on the gender of the athlete.

Wilks ratio: A time-tested classic

Despite the official transition of the IPF to new systems, the Wilks coefficient remains the “gold standard” in popular powerlifting and many alternative federations. It is calculated by multiplying the amount of weight lifted by a coefficient determined by a fifth-degree polynomial equation:

$$Coeff = \frac{500}{a + bx + cx^2 + dx^3 + ex^4 + fx^5}$$
