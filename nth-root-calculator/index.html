What is the nth root?

In mathematics the root nth degree from the number $a$ the number $x$ is called which, when raised to the power $n$ , gives the number $a$ .

The mathematical formula looks like this:

$$\sqrt[n]{a} = x \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad x^n = a$$

Where:

  • a - radical number (radicand).
  • n — root indicator (degree).
  • x - the desired root.

It is also important to remember that the operation of extracting the root is equivalent to raising a number to a fractional power:

$$\sqrt[n]{a} = a^{\frac{1}{n}}$$

Our calculator shows both options in the results field, which is convenient for solving algebraic problems.

Features of working with negative numbers

Many online calculators give an error when entering a minus sign. Our algorithm works correctly within the set of real numbers:

  1. Odd degree (n = 3, 5, 7...): Extracting the root is possible from any real number. The sign of the result is preserved. Example: $\sqrt[3]{-27} = -3$ as $(-3)^3 = -27$ .
  2. Even degree (n = 2, 4, 6...): In the realm of real numbers, there is no even root of a negative number. Example: $\sqrt{-16}$ - cannot be calculated (without using complex numbers).

If you try to take the even root of a negative number, our calculator will warn you of an error instead of giving you an incorrect resultNaN.

What is an arithmetic root?

An arithmetic root of the nth power of a non-negative number $a$ is a non-negative number whose nth power is equal to $a$ . Our calculator by default finds exactly the arithmetic (positive) value for even powers.

Is it possible to use a fractional degree?

Yes. You can enter in the degree field, for example,2.5. This will be equivalent to raising the number to the power $1/2.5$ or $0.4$ . This is often used in complex engineering calculations.
