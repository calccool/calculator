What is linear interpolation

Linear interpolation is a method of finding the intermediate value of a function on a segment between two known points.
If points $(x_0, y_0)$ and $(x_1, y_1)$ are given, you can find the value $y$ with known $x$ or vice versa - $x$ with known $y$ .

Formulas used by the calculator

Example:
If $(x_0, y_0) = (1, 2)$ and $(x_1, y_1) = (4, 8)$ and $x = 2$ ,
then $k = \frac{8 — 2}{4 — 1} = 2$ , $b = 2 — 2 \cdot 1 = 0$ ,
and $y = 2 \cdot 2 + 0 = 4$ .

Where is linear interpolation used?

  • In engineering, when estimating values ​​between measurements.
  • In computer graphics - for smooth transitions and animations.
  • In science, when modeling functions between nodes.
  • In tables and calculations - for quickly determining intermediate values.

When is it important to be attentive?

  • If $x_0 = x_1$ , the formula does not work (straight vertical).
  • If the value of $x$ or $y$ is outside the range of $[x_0, x_1]$ or $[y_0, y_1]$ , this is an extrapolation—the result is less accurate.
  • For highly nonlinear functions, linear interpolation can produce a noticeable error.

Frequently asked questions

Can I enter values ​​out of range?

Yes, but this is an extrapolation - the result is less reliable.

What happens if $x_0 = x_1$ ?

Interpolation is not possible because the slope is not determined.

Is it possible to calculate $y$ and $x$ at once?

Yes, select the "Both at once" mode.

What are $k$ and $b$ for?

They allow you to obtain the equation of a line and reuse it.
