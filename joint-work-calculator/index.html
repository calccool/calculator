Collaboration Calculator: Calculate how long it takes a team to complete a task

When planning projects, renovations, or solving school problems, the question often arises: “If the first master does the job in 3 hours, and the second in 5, how long will it take them together?” Intuitively, you want to find the arithmetic mean (4 hours), but this is a blunder - in fact, they will do it faster than the fastest of them.

Our calculator uses a productivity summing algorithm, allowing you to accurately determine the completion time of a project when any number of participants are working in parallel.

How to use the tool

The tool is designed for maximum flexibility. You are not limited to two participants (A and B) - add as many performers as your task requires.

  1. Select units of measurement: Hours, days or minutes. This only affects the display, the math remains the same.
  2. Add participants: Enter the time it takes each worker to complete the task alone.
    • Example: A Senior programmer writes a module in 4 hours, a Middle programmer in 6 hours, a Junior in 12 hours.
  3. Get the result: The calculator will instantly show the total time and acceleration factor (Boost).

Mathematical formula for teamwork

The calculation is based on the concept productivity (or operating speed). If the volume of all work is taken as one ( $1$ ), then the productivity of one participant ( $P$ ) is equal to one divided by the time ( $t$ ) for which he performs this work.

$$P = \frac{1}{t}$$

When working together, the performance of all participants adds up. Total performance ( $P_{total}$ ) is calculated using the formula:

$$P_{total} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + \dots + P_n$$

Or in expanded form:

$$P_{total} = \frac{1}{t_1} + \frac{1}{t_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{t_n}$$

To find the total time ( $T_{result}$ ), you need to divide the entire amount of work by the total productivity:

$$T_{result} = \frac{1}{P_{total}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{t_1} + \frac{1}{t_2} + \dots + \frac{1}{t_n}}$$

For the classic case with two workers (A and B), the formula simplifies to:

$$T_{A+B} = \frac{t_A \times t_B}{t_A + t_B}$$

What is acceleration factor?

In the calculation results you will see the indicator Boost. This is a metric of teamwork effectiveness. It shows how many times faster the team will complete the task compared to its fastest participant.

  • If the coefficient is $< 1.5$ with two participants, perhaps the second participant is too slow and his contribution is insignificant.
  • If the coefficient grows linearly, your team is perfectly balanced.

Model limitations

It is important to understand that the calculator operates on a pure mathematical model. In real life (especially in management), “Brooks’ Law” works: “Adding labor late in a project makes it even more late.”.

Factors that the calculator does not take into account, but are worth keeping in mind:

  • Communication costs: The more people there are, the more time it takes for approval.
  • Divisibility of the task: Not every job can be done in parallel (9 women will not give birth to a child in 1 month).
  • Limited resources: Two diggers in the same hole can interfere with each other.
