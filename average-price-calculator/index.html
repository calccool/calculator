Position price averaging calculator (Shares, Crypto) - Calculate average price online

In the world of investing and trading, the Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) strategy is one of the key risk management tools. Our price averaging calculator allows you to instantly determine your new break-even point when you change the size of your position in stocks or digital assets.

The tool is designed taking into account two scenarios: when you have already completed a transaction and want to know the result, or when you are just planning the volume of purchases to achieve a specific financial goal.

Calculator operating modes

For maximum accuracy, two independent logic models are integrated into the interface:

  1. Classic averaging: You enter the data of the current position and the parameters of the new purchase. The system calculates the weighted average cost of one asset and the total amount of investments.
  2. Under target price (Target Mode): A unique function that answers the question: “How many additional coins/shares do I need to buy at the current market price so that my average price becomes equal to X?”

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on fundamental formulas of financial analysis. For the classic scenario, the weighted average cost formula is used:

$$P_{avg} = \frac{(P_1 \cdot Q_1) + (P_2 \cdot Q_2)}{Q_1 + Q_2}$$

Where:

  • $P_{avg}$ - final average price;
  • $P_1, Q_1$ — price and quantity in the current position;
  • $P_2, Q_2$ - price and quantity in the new deal.

To calculate the required volume when achieving the goal, the inverse formula is used:

$$Q_{req} = \frac{Q_1 \cdot (P_1 — P_{target})}{P_{target} — P_{market}}$$

Where $P_{target}$ is your desired price, and $P_{market}$ is the current quote on the exchange.

When should you use averaging?

The strategy is most effective when working with fundamentally strong assets. If the price falls, repurchasing allows you to reduce the average cost of ownership, which requires a smaller price rebound to make a profit. However, remember that averaging into a losing position increases your overall risk exposure. Use the calculator to calculate in advance what proportion of the portfolio the asset will occupy after the transaction is completed.
