Wildberries commission calculator: Calculation of FBO and FBS profits by product category

Trading on marketplaces is not only about finding a hit product, but also about endlessly accounting for hidden costs. Wildberries regularly updates tariffs, and what was profitable yesterday may cause losses today. Our Wildberries commission calculator designed to ensure that you see a real picture of your profits even before you send your first delivery to the warehouse.

The main feature of this tool is the visualization of unit economics. You don’t just get a “total” figure, but you see a visual diagram: what part of the price is “eaten up” by logistics, how much the site takes, and what is left to you in the end.

Main calculation indicators

The calculator uses four key variables to produce an accurate result:

  • Commission category: The percentage that WB keeps from each sale (from 10% to 25%).
  • Logistics (Delivery): Depends on the volume of goods in liters and the selected work scheme.
  • Cost price: The amount of purchasing goods, packaging and delivery to the warehouse.
  • Dimensions: The length, width and height of the package, which determine the cost of storage and transportation.

Formulas used by the calculator

So that you can trust the numbers, let's look at the mathematics of the process. First, the product volume in liters ( $V$ ) is calculated:

$$V = \frac{L \times W \times H}{1000}$$

Where $L, W, H$ is the length, width and height in centimeters. Next, the final logistics ( $L_{total}$ ) is calculated taking into account the base tariff and additional payment for each additional liter of volume:

$$L_{total} = B + ( \lceil V — 1 \rceil \times S )$$

  • $B$ - basic tariff (for the first liter).
  • $S$ - the cost of each subsequent liter.
  • $\lceil \dots \rceil$ —volume rounding up.

The final calculation of net profit ( $P$ ) looks like this:

$$P = S — (C + (S \times \frac{R}{100}) + L_{total})$$

Where $S$ is the sales price, $C$ is the cost, $R$ is the category commission rate as a percentage.

FBO or FBS: Which to choose in 2026?

The choice of work scheme directly affects your margin. The calculator allows you to instantly switch between modes to compare costs.

Advantages of the FBO scheme (from WB warehouse):

  1. Higher ranking: Wildberries algorithms give priority to goods that are already in their warehouses.
  2. Fast delivery: The buyer receives the goods as soon as possible.
  3. Less OS: You do not need to package each order separately and take it to the collection point.

Advantages of the FBS scheme (from your own warehouse):

  1. Residue control: You can sell the same product on different platforms at the same time.
  2. No storage fees: Relevant for large items or items with low turnover.
  3. Niche testing: You can put the product on display without purchasing a large batch.

Important tip: Always factor in 5-10% for unforeseen expenses: returns (reverse logistics), participation in forced actions and possible defects. If, after deducting all commissions, your margin remains below 15%, it is worth reconsidering either the sales price or the terms of purchase.
