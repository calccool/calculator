How to take measurements correctly?

The accuracy of the calculation directly depends on the correct measurements. Take a measuring tape and follow the instructions:

  1. Bust/Chest: Measured at the most protruding points of the chest. The tape should go under the armpits and be parallel to the floor.
  2. Waist: Measured at the narrowest point of the torso (usually just above the navel). Don't suck in your stomach.
  3. Hips: Measured at the most protruding points of the buttocks.
  4. Height: Important for determining the size (especially for trousers and children's clothing).

Features of calculating jeans (Jeans Size)

Jeans sizes are traditionally expressed in inches and consist of two numbers, e.g. W32 L34.

  • W (Waist) - waist circumference in inches.
  • L (Length) — inseam length (inside leg) in inches.

If you know your waist circumference in centimeters, you can check the size yourself using the conversion formula:

$$W = \frac{Waist_{cm}}{2.54}$$

Where $2.54$ is the number of centimeters in one inch. For example, with a waist of 82 cm:

$$W = \frac{82}{2.54} \approx 32.2$$

Therefore you need size W32. Our calculator performs this division automatically, rounding the result to the nearest standard size.

Children's sizes: why is height important?

Unlike adults, the size chart for children (Kids/Baby) is based not on volumes, but primarily on growth. Age is only a guideline as children grow at different rates.

  • Up to 2 years: Sizes are often given in months (3M, 6M, 12M, 18M).
  • From 2 years: Sizes correspond to height in cm (92, 98, 104, 110, etc.) or age (2T, 3T, 4T in the US system).

Use the switch "Child" in the calculator to enter your height and get the exact size that meets the standards of children's clothing manufacturers.

Tips for choosing

  • The "halves" rule: If your measurements are on the border of two sizes (for example, chest size 44 and hips 46), be guided by the type of clothing. For dresses and blouses, the top is important, for trousers - the bottom.
  • Fabric shrinkage: Cotton and natural fabrics may shrink after washing, so it is best to round up your size.
  • Outerwear: For coats and jackets, always take a size with extra for a warm sweater (usually +1 size to your standard).
