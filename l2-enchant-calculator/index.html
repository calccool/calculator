Weapon sharpening chance calculator in Lineage 2 (calculation of probability in a row)

Enchanting weapons in the world of Elmoreden is a complex mathematical process based on probability theory. Unlike simple systems, in L2 each subsequent level of enchantment requires successful completion of the previous one, which creates the effect of a “geometric progression” of difficulty.

This calculator allows players to avoid wasting adena and resources by assessing the real risks in advance. The tool takes into account the current item level, target value and the server's base chance, giving the final probability of success for the entire series of modifications in a row.

Formulas and mathematical model

The independent events formula is used to calculate the final chance of success. The probability that a series of $n$ sharpening steps will be successful is calculated as the product of the probabilities of each individual step.

$$P_{total} = (P_{single})^{n}$$

Where:

  • $P_{total}$ - final probability of success in percentage;
  • $P_{single}$ - chance of success of one scroll (in fractions of 1);
  • $n$ - number of sharpening steps (difference between target and initial level).

The calculator also calculates Mathematical expectation of the number of attempts according to the formula:

$$E = \frac{1}{P_{total}}$$

This number shows how many “guns” you will have to break on average before you get one treasured item of the required level.

Why is it important to consider “steps of degradation”

Many players make the mistake of thinking that if the chance of enchanting is 68%, then making +10 is not that difficult. However, in practice the probability decreases with each step:

  • Enchant at +4: chance 68%
  • Sharpening at +5: $68\% \times 68\% \approx 46.2\%$
  • Sharpening at +6: $68\% \times 68\% \times 68\% \approx 31.4\%$

By the time you reach level +16, the final chance of success becomes negligible, which is what makes top weapons so rare and valuable.

Tips for using the calculator

  • Use the "Safe" limit: Remember that weapons are sharpened up to +3 with 100% probability. Start the calculation with this value.
  • Consider the type of scrolls: If you are using Blessed Enchant (brilliant), the chance of success does not change, but if you fail, the item does not disappear, but is reset to 0. This does not change the probability of a “pass in a row”, but it significantly affects your costs.
  • Compare the risks: Sometimes buying a finished weapon on the market is cheaper than trying to sharpen it yourself, if the average number of attempts in the calculator exceeds 20-30 units.
