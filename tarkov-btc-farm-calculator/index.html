Bitcoin farm calculator Escape from Tarkov: calculation of profit and payback

The Bitcoin farm in Escape from Tarkov (EFT) is a key facility in the Vault, providing the player with a stable influx of in-game currency. However, due to the dynamic change in the Bitcoin rate at the Therapist and the cost of fuel at the Flea Market, the question of the payback of video cards (GPU) always remains open.

Our advanced calculator uses up-to-date mathematical models to calculate the efficiency of your farm, taking into account the number of cards installed, generator costs and the availability of solar panels.

Mathematics and calculation formulas

The rate at which coins are generated in the game is not linear. With each new video card, the efficiency gain decreases, which makes filling the farm to 50 pieces a strategic decision.

To calculate the production time of one coin, the following formula is used:

$$T = \frac{38.6}{1 + 0.038 \cdot (GPU — 1)}$$

Where:

  • T — time to generate one coin in hours.
  • GPU — the total number of video cards in the block (from 1 to 50).
  • 38.6 — base farming time on one map (may change slightly by developers in different patches).

The influence of external factors on net profit

The farm's net profit is calculated as the difference between the market value of the received bitcoins and operating expenses.

  1. Bitcoin rate: Selling coins to the Therapist is the most profitable and safest way to sell.
  2. Fuel consumption: The generator consumes resources continuously. The use of canisters (forwarding or steel) directly affects margins.
  3. Solar battery: This module in the Shelter reduces fuel consumption by 50%, which effectively doubles the efficiency of each canister and significantly accelerates the payback of the entire system.
