What is a complex number?

A complex number is written inalgebraic formHow:

$$z = a + bi$$

Where:

  • $a$ — real part (Re).
  • $b$ — imaginary part (Im).
  • $i$ — imaginary unit, having the property $i^2 = -1$ .

Formulas and calculation rules

For those studying higher mathematics, it is useful to know exactly how calculations occur.

Addition and Subtraction

The simplest operations. Real parts add to real parts, and imaginary parts add to imaginary parts.

$$(a + bi) \pm (c + di) = (a \pm c) + (b \pm d)i$$

Multiplication

Produced by expanding the parentheses, given that $i^2 = -1$ .

$$(a + bi) \cdot (c + di) = (ac — bd) + (ad + bc)i$$

Division

To divide, multiply the numerator and denominator by the numberconjugatedenominator ( $c — di$ ) to get rid of the imaginary part at the bottom of the fraction.

$$\frac{a + bi}{c + di} = \frac{(ac + bd) + (bc — ad)i}{c^2 + d^2}$$

Modulus, Argument and Trigonometric Form

Any complex number can be represented not only as a point on the plane with coordinates $(a, b)$ , but also through the length of the vector (modulus) and its angle of inclination (argument).

1. Number modulus( $|z|$ or $r$ ):

This is the distance from the origin to the point. Calculated using the Pythagorean theorem:

$$r = |z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$$

2. Argument ($\varphi$):

This is the angle between the positive semi-axis of $X$ and the number vector.

$$\varphi = \arctan \left( \frac{b}{a} \right)$$

3. Trigonometric notation:

Knowing the modulus and argument, the number can be written as:

$$z = r(\cos \varphi + i \sin \varphi)$$
