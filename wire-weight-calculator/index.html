Wire weight calculator: accurate calculation of weight and length online

When purchasing, designing or inventorying rolled metal, there is often a need to quickly convert meters to kilograms or find out how many meters of material are contained in one coil. Our online wire weight calculator allows you to perform both direct and reverse calculations with high accuracy for products of any diameter.

The tool is universal and is not tied to a specific narrow standard, which allows you to calculate parameters not only for classic steel binding wire (GOST 3282-74), but also for non-ferrous metals, microwires and specialized alloys.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The calculations are based on the standard geometric formula for determining the mass of a cylinder. To calculate the theoretical weight, you need to calculate the cross-sectional area and multiply it by the length and specific gravity of the material.

The basic formula looks like this:

$$m = \frac{\pi \cdot d^2}{4} \cdot L \cdot \rho$$

Where the following variables apply:

  • $m$ - the required wire mass (in kilograms).
  • $\pi$ - mathematical constant (Pi number, $\approx 3.14159$ ).
  • $d$ - outer diameter of the section (converted to meters for consistency of units of measurement).
  • $L$ - total rental length (in meters).
  • $\rho$ - density of the metal used (in $кг/м^3$ ).

If you are faced with the opposite task - to find out the length of the bay, knowing its mass on the scales, the formula is transformed:

$$L = \frac{4 \cdot m}{\pi \cdot d^2 \cdot \rho}$$

How to use the tool

The algorithm for working with the calculator is simplified as much as possible to save your time:

  1. Select the operating mode: switch the tab to “Find out weight” (if the length is known) or “Find out length” (if the gross/net weight is known).
  2. Specify the material from the drop-down list so that the system applies the correct density indicator $\rho$ .
  3. Enter the exact diameter of $d$ in millimeters (fractional values ​​are acceptable, for example, $1.2$ or $6.5$ ).
  4. Enter the known length of $L$ or mass of $m$ .
  5. Click the calculation button to get a detailed result, including the specific gravity of one linear meter.

Areas of application of calculations

Accurate theoretical calculations are necessary at all stages of construction and production work:

  • Budget planning: conversion of the required project footage of reinforcement ties into tonnage for ordering from the supplier.
  • Logistics and warehouse: checking the compliance of the shipped goods with the stated specifications (weighing the coil allows you to find out the actual length without unwinding).
  • Electrical engineering: calculation of the mass of a copper or aluminum conductor to determine the weight loads on cable trays and supports.
  • Spring Design: calculation of the mass of the workpiece based on the unfolded length of the turns.
