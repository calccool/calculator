Glass thickness calculator for assembling an aquarium (with stiffeners)

Assembling an aquarium with your own hands is not only creativity, but also a serious engineering calculation. The main fear of any aquarist is leakage or destruction of glass under water pressure. Our calculator uses proven physical models to determine the minimum safe wall and bottom thickness, taking into account hydrostatic pressure and design features (presence of stiffeners).

Why height is more important than volume

A common mistake made by beginners is to assume that glass thickness depends on the total displacement. In fact, the determining factor is water column height. The higher the aquarium, the greater the pressure on the bottom of the glass.

The formula for the bending moment of the plate is used for the calculation. Water pressure at a certain depth is calculated by the formula:

$$P = \rho \cdot g \cdot h$$

Where:

  • $P$ —pressure (Pa);
  • $\rho$ - water density ( $1000$ kg/m³);
  • $g$ - free fall acceleration ( $\approx 9.81$ m/s²);
  • $h$ - height of the water column (m).

The influence of stiffeners on reliability

Calculation of weight and volume

In addition to thickness, it is critical to know the weight of the future system to select a cabinet. The total weight is calculated as the sum of the mass of the glass, the volume of water and the weight of the soil:

$$M_{total} = M_{glass} + V_{water} + (V_{water} \cdot 0.15)$$

The $0.15$ coefficient takes into account the average weight of decorations and substrate relative to the volume of water.

Basic safety rules for assembly

  • Edge quality: For aquariums with a height of more than $50$ cm, polishing of the euro-edge is mandatory. This relieves internal stress in the glass.
  • Glass brand: It is recommended to use glass of a grade no lower than M1. For premium systems, choose clarified glass (Optiwhite).
  • Silicone: Use only a specialized acid-based aquarium sealant.
  • Horizon: The slightest distortion of the surface on which the aquarium stands creates critical stress in one of the corners, which can lead to a crack.
