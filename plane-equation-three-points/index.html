Three Point Plane Equation Calculator (Online)

3 Point Plane Equation Calculator

In three-dimensional space, any plane can be uniquely defined if the three points that lie on it are known, provided that they are not on the same line (not collinear).

This calculator is a tool for students, engineers and mathematicians to instantly find general (or Cartesian) plane equation according to the coordinates of three points $M_1$ , $M_2$ and $M_3$ .

General plane equation

The general equation of the plane in the Cartesian coordinate system is as follows: $Ax + By + Cz + D = 0$ .

Where:

  • $x, y, z$ - variables corresponding to the coordinates of any point on the plane.
  • $A, B, C$ - coefficients that represent coordinates normal vector ( $\vec{N}$ ). This is a vector perpendicular to the plane.
  • $D$ is a free coefficient that determines the displacement of the plane relative to the origin.

Calculation Method: How to find the equation?

The calculator uses the standard vector algebra method. The process consists of several key steps, which are also displayed in the calculation results for your convenience.

Step 1: Defining Vectors on the Plane

To find a plane, we need two vectors that lie in that plane. We can create them using our three points: $M_1(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ , $M_2(x_2, y_2, z_2)$ and $M_3(x_3, y_3, z_3)$ .

  • Vector 1 (M₁M₂): $\vec{V_1} = \{x_2 — x_1, y_2 — y_1, z_2 — z_1\}$
  • Vector 2 (M₁M₃): $\vec{V_2} = \{x_3 — x_1, y_3 — y_1, z_3 — z_1\}$

Step 2: Finding the Normal Vector ( $\vec{N}$ )

The normal vector ( $\vec{N} = \{A, B, C\}$ ) is by definition perpendicular to both vectors $\vec{V_1}$ and $\vec{V_2}$ , which lie in the plane. We find it with vector product ($\vec{V_1} \times \vec{V_2}$).

The cross product is calculated through the matrix determinant: $\vec{N} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ V_{1x} & V_{1y} & V_{1z} \\ V_{2x} & V_{2y} & V_{2z} \end{vmatrix}$ .

Expanding the determinant, we get the coefficients $A, B, C$ :

  • $A = (V_{1y} \cdot V_{2z} — V_{1z} \cdot V_{2y})$
  • $B = (V_{1z} \cdot V_{2x} — V_{1x} \cdot V_{2z})$
  • $C = (V_{1x} \cdot V_{2y} — V_{1y} \cdot V_{2x})$

Step 3: Calculate the D coefficient

Now we have $A, B$ and $C$ . To find $D$ , we take any of our starting points (for example, $M_1$ ) and plug its coordinates into the equation: $A \cdot x_1 + B \cdot y_1 + C \cdot z_1 + D = 0$ .

From here it is easy to express $D$ : $D = -(Ax_1 + By_1 + Cz_1)$ .

Step 4: Simplification (Normalization)

The calculator brings the resulting equation $Ax + By + Cz + D = 0$ to canonical form. He finds Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) for all four coefficients (A, B, C, D) and divides the equation by it.

Example:

If the calculation was given by $26x — 4y — 2z — 64 = 0$ , the calculator will find the gcd (equal to 2) and simplify the equation to:

$13x — 2y — z — 32 = 0$.

Special Cases (Collinearity)

To construct a unique plane, three points must not lie on the same line.

What happens if they lie on the same straight line?

In both of these cases, the calculator will report an error, since an infinite number of planes can be drawn through one line (or one point).
