Impermanent Loss calculator in DeFi pools

Impermanent loss ( Impermanent Loss, or IL ) is a fundamental risk that every liquidity provider faces in decentralized protocols (DEX) that use the AMM (automatic market maker).

The essence of the loss is that when the market price of assets within the pool changes, the algorithm forces a rebalance to maintain the proportion of asset values ​​(usually 50/50). As a result, if you decide to withdraw funds when prices deviate greatly from your entry point, the total dollar value of your assets will be lower than if you simply stored those tokens in your wallet ( HODL ).

Why losses are called "impermanent"

The term “impermanent” means that the loss only exists “on paper” while the funds remain in the pool. If token prices return to the values ​​at which you made the deposit, the losses will disappear. However, as soon as you confirm the liquidity withdrawal transaction, these losses become permanent and are recorded in your blockchain portfolio.

Formula for calculating non-permanent losses

For a standard pool with a 50/50 asset ratio (for example, Uniswap v2 or PancakeSwap), the calculation is based on the mathematical dependence of the price change. If we denote the price change factor as $k$ (where $k = \text{цена\_будущая} / \text{цена\_начальная}$ ), then the IL formula looks like this:

$$IL = \frac{2 \sqrt{k}}{1 + k} — 1$$

Where:

  • IL — percentage loss relative to the HODL strategy.
  • k - the relative change in the price of one asset to another.

Our calculator uses an extended version of this logic, taking into account the price changes of both tokens in the pair, allowing us to get the most accurate result for real market conditions.

Strategies for minimizing Impermanent Loss

It is impossible to completely avoid IL in volatile pairs, but the following methods can be used:

  • Pools with stablecoins: Pairs like USDT/USDC are virtually immune to IL since asset prices are pegged to the dollar.
  • Highly correlated assets: Invest in token pairs that move in the same direction (e.g. ETH/stETH staking derivatives).
  • High trading volume: Choose pools with high commission fees that can compensate for the drawdown from price changes.
  • Concentrated liquidity: With protocols like Uniswap v3, you can choose price ranges, which requires more complex calculations but allows for better risk management.
