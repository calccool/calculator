Main types of prisms and calculation formulas

A prism consists of two parallel bases (which are equal polygons) and side faces. In a straight prism, the side edges are perpendicular to the bases, and the height ( $h$ ) is equal to the length of the side edge.

We use the following basic formulas for all types:

  1. Prism volume ( $V$ ): The product of the area of ​​the base and the height. $$V = S_{base} \cdot h$$
  2. Side surface area ( $S_{lat}$ ): The product of the perimeter of the base and the height. $$S_{lat} = P_{base} \cdot h$$
  3. Total Surface Area ( $S_{total}$ ): The sum of the lateral surface and two areas of the bases. $$S_{total} = S_{lat} + 2 \cdot S_{base}$$

1. Rectangular prism (Parallelepiped)

The most common type. At the base lies a rectangle with sides $a$ and $b$ .

  • Base area: $S_{base} = a \cdot b$
  • Perimeter: $P_{base} = 2(a + b)$

2. Triangular prism

At the base is a triangle. Our calculator uses Heron's formula to calculate the area on three sides ( $a, b, c$ ), first checking the triangle inequality (the sum of two sides must always be greater than the third).

Semi-perimeter ( $p$ ):

$$p = \frac{a + b + c}{2}$$

Base area:

$$S_{base} = \sqrt{p(p-a)(p-b)(p-c)}$$

3. Regular n-gonal prism

This is a universal mode for figures based on a regular polygon with the number of sides $n$ and side length $a$ . It can be a pentagonal, hexagonal (nut, honeycomb) or even a 12-gonal prism.

The area of ​​the base is calculated through the tangent:

$$S_{base} = \frac{n \cdot a^2}{4 \cdot \tan(\frac{\pi}{n})}$$

Calculation of material mass

A unique feature of our calculator is the integration of physical parameters. Knowing the volume ( $V$ ) and density of the material ( $\rho$ ), you can find the mass ( $m$ ):

$$m = V \cdot \rho$$

The tool already has built-in reference densities of popular materials:

  • Water: $\approx 1000 \text{ кг/м}^3$
  • Concrete: $\approx 2400 \text{ кг/м}^3$ (for foundations and beams)
  • Steel: $\approx 7850 \text{ кг/м}^3$ (for rolled metal)
  • Wood: $\approx 500 \text{ кг/м}^3$ (pine average)
  • Glass: $\approx 2600 \text{ кг/м}^3$ (for optical prisms)
