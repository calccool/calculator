Universal online speed converter: Unit conversion (km/h, m/s, knots, Mach)

Speed ​​is one of the most important physical quantities that describes the speed of an object’s movement in space. In everyday life, we are accustomed to kilometers per hour on a car's speedometer, but in aviation, navigation, astrophysics and geology, completely different number systems are used.

Our multifunctional online calculator allows you to instantly convert speeds between dozens of different systems. The tool is designed for maximum convenience: it not only gives the exact result, but also shows the formula used, and also generates a visual conversion table for adjacent values.

What speed categories does the converter support?

The converter database includes both popular metric units and highly specialized physical constants. For convenience, they are divided into logical groups:

  • Metric (SI): Basic meters per second (m/s), kilometers per hour (km/h), as well as millimeters and centimeters for precise calculations.
  • British and American (Imperial): Miles per hour (mph), feet per second (fps), yards and inches, which are indispensable when working with Western documentation.
  • Marine and aviation: International and British knots, cables per minute, and Mach number (taking into account air temperature and pressure).
  • Scientific and space: The speed of light in vacuum, water and glass, the speed of sound in various media, as well as the first, second and third cosmic velocities for planet Earth.
  • Historical and exotic: Russian versts and fathoms, sea leagues, Roman miles, Chinese li and even such comic values ​​as “troubles per hour” or “furlongs in Fortnite”.
  • Ultra-slow (Geological): Measurements of the movement of tectonic plates or the growth of glaciers in millimeters, centimeters, and inches per year.

Calculation and conversion formulas

The basis of any speed calculation is the basic formula of classical kinematics, where speed ( $v$ ) equals distance ( $s$ ) divided by time ( $t$ ):

$$v = \frac{s}{t}$$

When converting from one system to another, mathematical coefficients are used. Here are some example formulas for the most popular conversions:

  • Meters per second to kilometers per hour: The value must be multiplied by 3.6. $$v_{km/h} = v_{m/s} \times 3.6$$
  • Kilometers per hour to miles per hour: The coefficient used is 0.621371. $$v_{mph} = v_{km/h} \times 0.621371$$
  • Knots to kilometers per hour: One international knot equals exactly 1.852 km/h. $$v_{km/h} = v_{knot} \times 1.852$$
