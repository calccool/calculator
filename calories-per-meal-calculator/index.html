Calorie calculator for one meal: Calculation of KBJU for breakfast, lunch and dinner

Many people know their daily calorie intake, but are faced with a problem: how to correctly distribute this amount throughout the day? Should I eat 50% of my diet for breakfast or leave a hearty dinner?

Our calculator solves this problem. He does not simply divide the total figure by the number of receptions, but uses professional nutritional protocols nutrient distribution. This helps:

  • Avoid overeating in the evening.
  • Maintain stable blood sugar levels (no insulin spikes).
  • Provide the body with energy exactly when it is needed.

Ration distribution schemes

The calculator contains the most effective nutrition algorithms used in dietetics and sports. Choose the one that suits your lifestyle:

  1. Classic fractional (5 times): Ideal for accelerating metabolism. Includes 3 main meals and 2 snacks.
  2. Classic (3 times): A standard option for those who work in an office and do not have the opportunity to be often distracted by food.
  3. Intermittent fasting (16/8): Scheme for the Leangains protocol. The entire diet is divided into 2 large meals in the “food window”.
  4. Food pyramid: Scheme: “Eat breakfast yourself, give dinner to the enemy.” The maximum calorie content is in the morning, the minimum in the evening. Suitable for early risers and those wanting to lose weight.
  5. Uniform: The diet is divided into equal parts. Convenient for athletes who are gaining mass (bodybuilding) to ensure a constant flow of amino acids.

Formulas for calculating macronutrients

The calculator performs calculations based on Atwater factors (energy value of nutrients). To check it yourself, you can use the following formulas:

1. Calculation of caloric intake:

$$E_{meal} = E_{total} \times R$$

Where $E_{meal}$ is calories per meal, $E_{total}$ is the daily intake, $R$ is the distribution coefficient (for example, 0.25 for breakfast).

2. Convert percentages to grams: To understand how much to weigh in grams, we use the following constants:

  • 1 g Protein = 4 kcal
  • 1 g Carbohydrates = 4 kcal
  • 1 g Fat = 9 kcal

$$M_{grams} = \frac{E_{meal} \times P}{C_{nutrient}}$$

Where $P$ is the percentage of the nutrient (for example, 30% protein), and $C_{nutrient}$ is the calorie content of 1 gram (4 or 9).

Menu planning tips

Having received the calculation of BZHU for one meal, you can easily assemble a plate:

  • Proteins: Chicken fillet, fish, eggs, cottage cheese. The most important building material.
  • Fats: Nuts, oils, avocados, fatty fish. Necessary for the hormonal system.
  • Carbohydrates: Cereals, durum pasta, vegetables, fruits. The main source of energy.

Important: If the sum of the BJU percentages you enter is not equal to 100%, the calculator will warn you of an error. Standard healthy proportion: 30% Protein / 30% Fat / 40% Carbohydrates.
