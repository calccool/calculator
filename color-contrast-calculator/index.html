Mathematical model for calculating contrast

Contrast is calculated based on relative brightness (Relative Luminance). This is the normalized brightness value of each point in the color space, with 0 being the darkest black and 1 being the lightest white.

Relative brightness formula

For each color component ( $R, G, B$ ) in the sRGB space, the coefficient $C_{srgb}$ is calculated:

$$C_{srgb} = \frac{C_{8bit}}{255}$$

Gamma correction is then applied to produce a linear $C$ value:

$$if (C_{srgb} \leq 0.03928) \Rightarrow C = \frac{C_{srgb}}{12.92}$$

$$else \Rightarrow C = \left(\frac{C_{srgb} + 0.055}{1.055}\right)^{2.4}$$

The final brightness of $L$ is calculated using the formula:

$$L = 0.2126 \cdot R + 0.7152 \cdot G + 0.0722 \cdot B$$

Contrast Ratio Formula

After finding the brightness of the text ( $L_1$ ) and background ( $L_2$ ), the final ratio is calculated:

$$CR = \frac{L_{light} + 0.05}{L_{dark} + 0.05}$$

Where $L_{light}$ is the larger of the two values, and $L_{dark}$ is the smaller. The result is always in the range from 1:1 (lack of contrast) up to 21:1 (black on white).

Why high contrast is important for your business

Many people mistakenly believe that accessibility is needed only by a narrow group of people. In fact, there are direct benefits to using high-contrast schemes:

  • SEO Improvement: Search engines (in particular Google through Lighthouse metrics) analyze the accessibility of pages. Low contrast can cause a site to rank lower in search results.
  • Reduced bounce rate: If the user is in pain or has difficulty reading the text, he will close the tab after 3 seconds.
  • Convenience in a mobile environment: On smartphones in bright light, only high contrast allows you to comfortably consume content.
  • Aesthetics and cleanliness: Modern UI/UX gravitates towards clarity and clear hierarchy, where color plays the role of the main navigator.

Useful tips for choosing colors

  • Avoid pure red on pure green - this is a classic problem for people with color blindness.
  • If the contrast ratio is on the edge (eg 4.48), try darkening the text color by just 2-3% to pass the AA test with confidence.
  • Use the live preview in our calculator to evaluate what a block of buttons and text paragraphs looks like before being implemented in code.
