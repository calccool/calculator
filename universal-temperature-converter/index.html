Mathematical formulas for converting temperatures

To understand the physics of processes, it is useful to know how exactly the relationships between basic quantities are calculated. The calculations take into account not only the degree scaling factor, but also the zero point offset (offset).

Conversion from degrees Celsius to Kelvin:

Absolute zero is $-273.15^\circ C$ , so the formula is linear:

$$K = ^\circ C + 273.15$$

Conversion from Fahrenheit to Celsius:

Scales have different reference points and different division values. Conversion formula:

$$^\circ C = (^\circ F — 32) \times \frac{5}{9}$$

Conversion from Celsius to Fahrenheit:

Back calculation often needed for weather reports:

$$^\circ F = \left(^\circ C \times \frac{9}{5}\right) + 32$$

Translation into Réaumur's historical scale:

This scale divides the interval between freezing and boiling of water into 80 parts:

$$^\circ R\acute{e} = ^\circ C \times 0.8$$
