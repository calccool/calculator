Kitchen backsplash tile calculator: Calculation of quantity, grout and cost

Kitchen renovations require precision, especially when it comes to finishes. A kitchen apron is not just about protecting walls from moisture and grease, but also a central design element. An error in calculations here is costly: it may be impossible to purchase additional tiles from the same batch due to differences in tone (caliber), and extra boxes are a wasted budget.

Our calculator is designed specifically for calculating kitchen splashbacks, taking into account the specifics of this type of work: the presence of corners, the need for trimming and deductions of area for hoods or windows.

How the calculation algorithm works

Unlike simple area calculators, this tool takes a comprehensive approach. It does not simply multiply the length by the height, but also takes into account the geometry of the installation.

The calculation is based on three key steps:

  1. Wall geometry: Calculation of the “net” area of ​​the working area, taking into account the configuration of the headset (straight or L-shaped).
  2. Technological stock: Adding a coefficient for pruning and fighting, which depends on the installation method.
  3. Conversion to pieces: Conversion of the resulting area into the actual number of ceramic elements and calculation of associated materials (grout).

Basic calculation formulas

To understand the calculation logic used in the calculator, we present basic mathematical dependencies.

1. Net area calculation ( $S_{net}$ ):

$$S_{net} = (L_1 \times H_1) + (L_2 \times H_2) — S_{excl}$$

Where:

  • $L, H$ - length and height of the corresponding walls.
  • $S_{excl}$ - area of ​​excluded zones (for example, a window in the apron area).

2. Calculation of area with a margin ( $S_{total}$ ):

$$S_{total} = S_{net} \times K_{waste}$$

Where $K_{waste}$ is the safety factor for trimming.

3. Calculation of the number of tiles ( $N_{tiles}$ ):

$$N_{tiles} = \lceil \frac{S_{total}}{L_{tile} \times W_{tile}} \rceil$$

Note: The result is always rounded up to the nearest whole number.

Calculation of grout (Fuges)

The calculator also provides an estimated grout mixture consumption. This is an important parameter, since for small tiles (for example, “hog” 7.5x15 cm), the area of ​​the seams can be significant.

The algorithm uses the following dependence of the flow rate ( $C_{grout}$ , kg/m²) on the parameters of the tile and seam:

$$C_{grout} = \frac{L + W}{L \times W} \times T \times J \times \rho$$

  • $L, W$ - tile length and width (mm).
  • $T$ - tile thickness (standard ~8 mm).
  • $J$ - seam width (mm).
  • $\rho$ - solution density (usually ~1.6 kg/dm³).

Kitchen Measuring Instructions

To get the most accurate result, follow this measurement algorithm before entering data:

  1. Apron length: Measure the length of the walls strictly after leveling and plastering. If the set is corner, measure both walls from the corner.
  2. Apron height: The standard height is 60 cm (600 mm). However, if you plan to install a hood without upper cabinets in this area, the height of the apron there can reach 80-100 cm. Enter the average value or calculate the hood area separately and add it to the total area.
  3. Accounting of sockets: Do not subtract the area of ​​the outlets from the total volume. On the contrary, cutouts for sockets often lead to splitting of the tiles, so a reserve is necessary here.
  4. Tile size: Please indicate the exact caliber. Often tiles are stated as 20x20 cm, but in fact have a size of 19.8x19.8 cm.
