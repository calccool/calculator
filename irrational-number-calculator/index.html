Online irrational number calculator: Root checking and analysis

This tool is designed to quickly check numbers for irrationality. Unlike standard calculators, it does not just give an approximate answer, but conducts an in-depth analysis of the structure of the number, including expansion into continued fractions and the search for rational approximations (convergents).

How does the calculator work?

Our algorithm uses the rational root theorem and the continued fraction method. If you enter the integer $x$ and the root degree $n$ , the calculator checks to see if the entire root is extracted.

  • If the root is whole - the number is rational (example: $\sqrt{4} = 2$ ).
  • If the root is not completely removed from an integer - the result is guaranteed to be irrational (example: $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414…$ ).

For square roots ( $n=2$ ) the tool additionally plots continued fraction - a unique “mathematical fingerprint” of an irrational number.

What is an irrational number?

An irrational number is a real number that cannot be represented as a fraction $\frac{p}{q}$ , where $p$ and $q$ are integers. Its decimal notation is infinite and non-periodic.

Main features:

  1. It cannot be written as a finite number.
  2. There is no repeating period in its decimal notation.
  3. It fills in the “gaps” on the number line between the rational numbers.

Continued fractions and approximations

One of the unique features of this calculator is the output continued fraction. For any irrational number $\alpha$ there is a unique decomposition of the form:

$$\alpha = a_0 + \frac{1}{a_1 + \frac{1}{a_2 + \frac{1}{a_3 + …}}}$$

This is written as $[a_0; a_1, a_2, a_3, …]$ .

It is continued fractions that allow us to find the best rational approximation. For example, for the number $\pi$ the known approximation is the fraction $22/7$ , and for $\sqrt{2}$ the calculator will show the fraction $99/70$ , which is accurate to the 4th decimal place.

Examples of irrational numbers

There are many constants in mathematics that have been proven to be irrational:

  • $\sqrt{2}$ (Root of two) — diagonal of a square with side 1. The first open irrational number.
  • $\pi$ (Pi) — the ratio of the circumference of a circle to its diameter ( $\approx 3.14159…$ ).
  • $e$ (Euler Number) — the base of the natural logarithm ( $\approx 2.71828…$ ).
  • $\phi$ (Golden Ratio) — harmonic proportion equal to $\frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}$ ( $\approx 1.618…$ ).
