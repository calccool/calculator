Blood group compatibility calculator for donation and transfusion

The issue of blood compatibility is critically important in transfusiology. An incorrect transfusion can lead to severe immunological reactions. Our calculator is designed to instantly determine the possibility of donating and receiving blood based on the system AB0 And Rh factor (Rh), taking into account the differences between whole blood (red blood cells) and plasma transfusions.

How does the antigen and antibody system work?

Biological compatibility is determined by the presence or absence of specific proteins - antigens on the surface of red blood cells and antibodies in plasma.

  • AB0 system: divides blood into four groups based on A and B antigens.
  • Rh factor: determines the presence of protein (antigen D). If it is present, it is Rh positive ( $Rh+$ ); if not, it is Rh negative ( $Rh-$ ).

The basic law of safe transfusion can be expressed by a simplified formula of immunological compliance:

$$C = (Ag_{don} \cap Ab_{rec} = \emptyset)$$

Where:

  • $C$ - compatibility status;
  • $Ag_{don}$ —donor antigens;
  • $Ab_{rec}$ —recipient antibodies;
  • $\emptyset$ is an empty set (no conflict).

Plasma Compatibility Features

Few people know that the compatibility rules for blood plasma diametrically opposed rules for red blood cells. This is due to the fact that plasma contains antibodies (agglutinins), not antigens.

  1. Universal plasma donor: Group IV (AB). Her plasma does not contain $\alpha$ and $\beta$ antibodies, so it can be transfused to everyone.
  2. Universal plasma recipient: Group I (O). People with this group can accept plasma from any other group.
  3. Rh factor: When transfusing plasma, the Rh factor is not as critical as when transfusing red blood cells, but doctors try to maintain the sameness.
