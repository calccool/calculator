PC Bottleneck Calculator: Checking CPU and GPU Compatibility

Term Bottleneck (bottleneck) describes a situation where the performance of the entire system is limited by one, weakest component. In the context of gaming and workstations, this most often concerns the bundle central processing unit (CPU) And graphics chip (GPU).

Our calculator analyzes the processing power of your components and determines how efficiently they work together. If the processor is too weak for the video card, it does not have time to prepare frames, and the expensive GPU is idle. If the video card is weak, the processor will wait for pixels to be drawn, which also leads to suboptimal use of resources.

How the calculation algorithm works

The calculator is based on a mathematical model that takes into account not only the “pure” power of the chips, but also external factors: screen resolution and amount of RAM.

Mathematical balance formula

The effective performance metrics ( $P_{eff}$ ) are used to calculate the bottleneck index:

$$P_{eff}(CPU) = Score_{CPU}$$

$$P_{eff}(GPU) = \frac{Score_{GPU}}{R_{mult}}$$

Where $R_{mult}$ is the resolution multiplier (1.0 for FullHD, 1.35 for 2K and 2.1 for 4K). The final Bottleneck percentage is calculated using the formula:

$$B\% = \left( \frac{Max(P_{eff}) — Min(P_{eff})}{Max(P_{eff})} \right) \times 100\% + Penalty_{RAM}$$

Effect of resolution on the result

Monitor resolution is a critical factor. The higher the number of pixels, the more load falls on the video card.

Factors affecting calculation accuracy

In addition to the main chips, the calculator takes into account additional parameters that can slow down the system.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

Modern games and heavy applications require a minimum of 16 GB of RAM. The algorithm imposes a performance penalty of 15%, if the system has less than 12 GB of memory installed. This is due to the occurrence of microstatters (freezes) when the system accesses the swap file on the disk.

Operating modes

The tool supports two use cases:

  • Game Mode (3D Render): Emphasis on the dynamic interaction between CPU and GPU. Screen resolution is taken into account.
  • Work tasks (CPU Render): Simulation or video editing scenarios where the video card and processor often work in parallel at their maximum capabilities, regardless of resolution.

Recommendations for eliminating the bottleneck

If the calculator shows the result above 15%, your system is considered unbalanced. Here are the main solutions:

With a weak processor (CPU Bottleneck)

  • Upgrade your processor to a model with more cores and higher clock speeds.
  • Increase the resolution in the game settings (switching from 1080p to 1440p will reduce the dependence on the CPU).
  • Close background tasks that are consuming CPU threads.

With a weak video card (GPU Bottleneck)

  • Replace the video card with a more modern model.
  • Lower your graphics settings, especially shadows, lighting, and anti-aliasing.
  • Use upscaling technologies (DLSS, FSR) to reduce the actual rendering resolution.

General Tips

  1. Check RAM: If you have 8GB, adding another stick up to 16GB is the cheapest and most effective way to remove stutters.
  2. Dual channel mode: Make sure memory is installed in pairs for double the bandwidth.
  3. Temperature: Sometimes throttling due to overheating becomes the bottleneck.

This calculator is a reference tool. Actual performance may vary depending on specific game or software optimizations.
