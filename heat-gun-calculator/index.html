Heat gun calculator: Accurate calculation of power and fuel consumption

This calculator is designed to quickly and accurately determine the required thermal power of a heater (heat gun) for any type of premises: from concrete garages and construction trailers to insulated warehouses and living rooms.

Using equipment “by eye” leads to two problems: either the gun can’t cope and the room is cold, or you overpay for excess power and unnecessary fuel consumption. Our algorithm is based on the classical formula of thermal engineering, which takes into account volume, temperature difference and heat loss coefficient.

Formula for calculating thermal power

The calculations are based on the basic formula for determining the thermal energy required to heat air in a given volume:

$$Q = V \times \Delta T \times K$$

Where:

  • Q — Thermal power (in kcal/h).
  • V — Room volume (Length × Width × Height), in m³.
  • $\Delta T$ — Temperature difference (desired inside minus actual outside), in °C.
  • K — Heat dissipation coefficient (depending on insulation).

To convert the obtained value from kilocalories to the usual kilowatts, the following ratio is used:

$$P_{кВт} = \frac{Q_{ккал/ч}}{860}$$

Fuel consumption: Gas, Diesel or Electric?

The power of the gun is only half the question. The second important aspect is operating costs. Our calculator automatically calculates the approximate fuel consumption. Below are averaged data to understand the effectiveness of different types of heaters.

  1. Electric guns (heating element/spiral)
    • Efficiency: Close to 100%.
    • Consumption: 1 kW of electricity produces 1 kW of heat.
    • Application: Residential premises, offices (do not burn oxygen).
  2. Gas guns (Propane/Butane)
    • Efficiency: About 98% (direct heating).
    • Consumption: Approximately 0.08 – 0.1 kg gas per 1 kW of power.
    • Application: Construction sites, large ventilated hangars (emit combustion products).
  3. Diesel guns
    • Efficiency: Depends on the type (direct or indirect heating).
    • Consumption: Approximately 0.1 liter per 1 kW of power.
    • Application: Manufacturing, concrete heating, large warehouses.

Calculation example for a garage

Let's say you need to heat a standard brick garage in the winter in order to comfortably repair your car.

  • Dimensions: 6m (length) × 4m (width) × 2.5m (height).
  • Temperature: It's -15°C outside, but we want a comfortable +15°C inside.
  • Insulation: Single layer brick, metal gates (poor insulation, K ≈ 2.5).

Calculation steps:

  1. Volume: $V = 6 \times 4 \times 2.5 = 60$ m³.
  2. Temperature difference: $\Delta T = 15 — (-15) = 30$ °C.
  3. Thermal energy: $Q = 60 \times 30 \times 2.5 = 4500$ kcal/h.
  4. Conversion to kW: $P = 4500 / 860 \approx 5.23$ kW.

Result: You will need a heat gun with a power of at least 5.5 - 6 kW. If you choose a 3 kW model, it will work continuously, but will not heat the air to the desired temperature.

Tips for choosing

  • Power reserve. Always choose equipment with a margin of 10-20%. Manufacturers often indicate maximum power, which is achieved only under ideal conditions (stable mains voltage, high-quality gas).
  • Ventilation. When using direct-heated gas and diesel guns, it is necessary to have fresh ventilation, as they burn oxygen and emit carbon monoxide.
  • Air mixing. For high rooms (more than 3 meters), it is recommended to use guns with a powerful fan so that warm air does not accumulate under the ceiling, but circulates in the work area.
