How instant repacking (Zero-RAM) works

Most standard online converters use a resource-intensive transcoding process that completely recalculates every pixel in the video. This not only takes a huge amount of time, but also inevitably reduces the quality of the picture.

Our converter works on the “Fast Remux” principle. A video file is simply a container containing video (eg H.264) and audio (AAC, MP3) streams. The tool neatly extracts these streams from the MKV container and instantly transfers them to a compatible MP4 container.

Since the bitrate of the original streams does not change, the final file size is calculated using the standard formula:

$$Size_{MP4} = \left( \frac{Bitrate_{video} + Bitrate_{audio}}{8} \right) \times Time_{seconds}$$

Where $Size_{MP4}$ is the size of the final file in bytes, and the bitrate is measured in bits per second. Due to the absence of recoding, the size of the finished MP4 file will be almost identical to the original MKV, and the quality will be preserved at 100%.

Technical nuances and compatibility

Although the MP4 container is supported by 99% of devices in the world (including the Apple ecosystem, PlayStation/Xbox game consoles and Smart TV), it is important to understand the features of the internal codec.

If your source MKV file contains a video stream in an exotic format or an audio track in AC3 format, then after changing the container to MP4, the built-in player of the operating system may refuse to play audio or video. In such rare cases, it is recommended to use universal players for viewing, such as VLC, which can read any codecs inside a standard MP4 container.
