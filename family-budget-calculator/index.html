How does the 50/30/20 rule work?

Our calculator's algorithm is based on a technique popularized by Senator Elizabeth Warren. She suggests dividing net family income into three categories:

  1. 50% - Necessary needs (Needs): These are expenses without which it is impossible to live. This includes rent or mortgage, utilities, food, transportation, and basic medical care.
  2. 30% - Wants: Expenses that improve the quality of life, but are not vital. These include restaurants, subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify), shopping, hobbies and vacations.
  3. 20% - Savings and Debts: Money that should be used to pay off loans, invest, or create an emergency fund.

Basic calculation formulas

The calculator automatically distributes the entered data and calculates key indicators.

1. Balance calculation

A basic indicator that determines whether you are living within your means or on credit.

$$Balance = I — (E_{needs} + E_{wants})$$

Where:

  • $I$ (Income) - total family income;
  • $E_{needs}$ - amount of mandatory expenses;
  • $E_{wants}$ - the amount of variable expenses.

2. Financial airbag (Safety Net)

Experts recommend having a reserve of money equal to 6 months of expenses. The calculator predicts how long it will take to accumulate this amount given the current balance:

$$T = \frac{E_{total} \times 6}{Balance}$$

Where:

  • $T$ — time in months;
  • $E_{total}$ - average monthly expenses.
