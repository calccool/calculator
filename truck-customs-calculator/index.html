Customs clearance calculator for trucks and tractors 2026

The process of importing commercial vehicles requires detailed financial planning. Unlike passenger cars, customs clearance of trucks and tractors directly depends on their purpose, gross weight, engine size and environmental standard. Our calculator automates complex government formulas, allowing you to instantly find out the final cost of a car, taking into account all taxes.

Main components of customs duties

When importing trucks, the total amount of taxes is formed from three basic components:

  1. Import customs duty: Percentage of the invoice (purchase) cost of the car.
  2. Excise duty: A tax that is calculated based on the engine specifications and the age of the vehicle.
  3. VAT (Value Added Tax): The final tax, which is calculated on the total cost of the car and already accrued duties.

Formulas used in calculations

For a deep understanding of the process, we present the mathematical models by which the calculator algorithm works:

  • Duty calculation ( $D$ ): $$D = P \times R$$ where $P$ is the customs value of the car, $R$ is the duty rate (in shares).
  • Excise tax calculation ( $E$ ): $$E = V \times K$$ where $V$ is the engine size in $см^3$ , $K$ is the age coefficient.
  • VAT calculation ( $Vat$ ): $$Vat = (P + D + E) \times 0.20$$
  • Total tax amount ( $T$ ): $$T = D + E + Vat$$

Comparison of rates depending on the age of the vehicle

The age of the truck is a key factor influencing the amount of excise tax. The older the equipment, the higher the environmental tax.

Features of customs clearance of truck tractors

Truck tractors often fall under preferential categories. Our calculator has a mode switch that automatically adjusts the duty rate:

  • Trucks (flatbed, vans): The standard rate is 10%.
  • Tractor units: In many cases a zero rate applies ( 0% ), which significantly reduces the financial burden of importing.
