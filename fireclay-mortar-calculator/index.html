Fireclay clay consumption calculator for furnace masonry: online mortar calculation

The construction of a stove or fireplace requires a special approach to the selection of materials. Refractory brick (fireclay) expands when heated, so ordinary cement mortar cannot be used to bind it. Applicable mortar - a ready-made mixture of fireclay powder and refractory clay.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly calculate the volume of the finished solution, the required mass of the dry mixture, and even the amount of water for mixing. This eliminates the risk of stopping work due to a lack of material or unnecessary costs for purchasing excess material.

Basic calculation parameters

To obtain an accurate result, three key factors must be taken into account:

  • Brick quantity: the total number of firebrick units that will be laid in the mortar.
  • Brick size: standard brands (ShB-5, ShB-8, ShB-9) have different dimensions, which directly affects the contact area and the volume of seams.
  • Seam thickness: In refractory masonry, it is critical to maintain a minimum joint thickness (2–5 mm). The thinner the seam, the higher the heat resistance of the entire structure.

Formula for calculating mortar volume

The calculation algorithm is based on the difference in volumes of a “conditional block” (brick + joint) and pure brick. Mathematically it looks like this:

  1. First, the volume of mortar for one brick is calculated ( $V_m$ ): $$V_m = ((L + j) \cdot (W + j) \cdot (H + j)) — (L \cdot W \cdot H)$$ Where:
  • $L, W, H$ - length, width and height of the brick in meters;
  • $j$ - seam thickness in meters.
  1. The resulting value is then multiplied by the total number of bricks ( $N$ ) and the safety factor ( $k = 1.1$ to account for 10% trimming and loss): $$V_{total} = V_m \cdot N \cdot k$$
  2. To convert volume into mass, the average density of the dry fireclay mixture (mortar) is used, which is approximately 1300 kg/ $м^3$ .

Recommendations for preparing the solution

In order for the masonry to be durable, it is important not only to correctly calculate the quantity, but also to properly prepare the mixture.

  • Water proportions: On average, 1 kg of dry mixture requires 0.28 liters clean water. A standard 20 kg bag takes about 5.5–5.6 liters.
  • Consistency: The finished solution should resemble sour cream in thickness - not run off the trowel, but at the same time be easily distributed over the surface.
  • Soak: If you are using pure fire clay (not a pre-mixed clay), it will need to be soaked 12-24 hours before you begin. Ready-made mortars do not need this.
  • Seam thickness: Try to maintain the seam 3 mm. This is the “gold standard”, providing maximum strength during thermal cycles of heating and cooling.
