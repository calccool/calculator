Body surface area (BSA) calculator - formula selection + dose per m²

Body surface area ( Body Surface Area — BSA ) is an important physiological parameter that serves as the basis for many medical calculations: drug dosage, assessment of transfusion volumes, calculation of hemodynamic parameters, etc.
Today we offer a simple but accurate online tool: the BSA calculator, which takes into account height and weight, allows you to select units of measurement (cm or inches, kg or pounds), select a calculation formula and optionally indicate the dose per m² for indicative medical calculations.

What is BSA and why is it needed?

  • BSA is the area of ​​human skin (approximately) in square meters (m²).
  • Used in tasks such as:
    • dosage of chemotherapy and other drugs with a narrow therapeutic index;
    • normalization of physiological parameters (for example, cardiac output index, blood volume) to body size;
  • Why not just weight? Weight does not reflect body surface area and active metabolic mass as clearly as BSA.
  • Average values: adult men ~1.9 m², women ~1.6 m².
  • Important: different formulas give different BSA values ​​- changes can be up to ±0.4 m².

How to use the calculator

  1. Please enter your height and weight.
  2. Select units: cm or inches for height; kg or pounds for weight.
  3. Select the BSA calculation formula (Mosteller, Du Bois or Haycock).
  4. If necessary, enter “Dose per m² (mg/m²)” - the tool will calculate the estimated dose.
  5. Click “Calculate” - you will get:
    • BSA value in m²;
    • formula used;
    • normalized input data;
    • (optional) results for dosage selection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Can the formula be used for children?
A - Yes, but it is important to note that some formulas are specifically validated for children. For example, Haycock and Mosteller show better accuracy in such groups.

Q2. Why are different formulas chosen?
A - Different formulas are developed on different groups (adults, children, different races). Some overestimate or underestimate in special groups.

Q3. What to do if the dose per m² exceeds the recommended one?
A - This is a signal to review the dosage with a medical specialist. The tool provides guidance but does not replace clinical supervision.
