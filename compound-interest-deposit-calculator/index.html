What is Capitalization (Compound Interest)?

The key advantage of a profitable deposit is capitalization. It means that the accrued interest is not paid to you, but is added to the principal amount of the deposit (the “body” of the deposit). In the next period, interest will be accrued on this increased amount.

  • Simple interest:Interest is always calculated only on the original deposit amount.
  • Compound Interest (Capitalization):Interest is calculated on the original amountplusall previously accrued interest. This creates an “interest on interest” effect that causes your savings to grow exponentially, especially over the long term.

Formulas for Calculating Deposit Profitability

To understand the magic of compound interest, the following formulas are used:

1. Simple Interest Formula

Used if you are withdrawing interest (capitalization "at the end of the term").

$$A = P \cdot (1 + r \cdot t)$$

  • $A$ – Total amount
  • $P$ – Initial amount (Principal)
  • $r$ – Annual interest rate (Rate, expressed as 0.05 for 5%)
  • $t$ – Term in years (Term)

2. Compound Interest Formula

Used if there is capitalization.

$$A = P \cdot (1 + \frac{r}{n})^{nt}$$

  • $A$ – Total amount
  • $P$ – Initial amount (Principal)
  • $r$ – Annual rate (Rate)
  • $n$ – Number of capitalization periods per year (for example, 12 for monthly)
  • $t$ – Term in years (Term)

3. Formula with Replenishments (Future Value of a Series)

When you regularly top up your account, the calculation becomes more complicated. Our calculator uses an iterative (monthly) method, which is equivalent to a combination of the compound interest formula for the initial amount and the future annuity value formula for top-ups:

$$A = P(1 + \frac{r}{n})^{nt} + M \cdot \frac{(1 + \frac{r}{n})^{nt} — 1}{\frac{r}{n}}$$

  • $M$ – Regular replenishment amount (Monthly Payment)

What is the Effective Rate (EIR)?

Effective Interest Rateshows the real return on the deposit if interest were accrued once at the end of the year.

For example, the nominal rate is 12% per annum withmonthlycapitalization will actually give12.68%annual income. This happens because each month interest is added to the base, and the next month interest is accrued on a larger amount.

EIR calculation formula:

$$EIR = (1 + \frac{r}{n})^{n} — 1$$

This indicator is the best way to compare deposits with different capitalization frequencies (for example, 12% monthly versus 12.5% ​​annual). Our calculator will calculate the EIR for you automatically.
