Calculation formulas

To ensure transparency of calculations, we present the mathematical basis on which our algorithm operates.

1. Calculation for strip foundation

For the tape, the total length of the longitudinal rods is calculated. Cross braces and vertical posts are usually made of the same material or thinner (structurally); allowance for overlap is taken into account here.

$$L_{total} = (P \cdot N_{rods}) \cdot K_{waste}$$

Where:

  • $P$ - foundation perimeter (m).
  • $N_{rods}$ - the number of longitudinal bars in the reinforcement pattern.
  • $K_{waste}$ - safety factor (overlaps and trims, usually 1.05–1.10).

2. Calculation for slab foundation

The monolithic slab is reinforced with two meshes (lower and upper). The calculation is carried out by the number of rods along the length and width.

$$N_{X} = \frac{W}{S} + 1; \quad N_{Y} = \frac{L}{S} + 1$$

Overall Length:

$$L_{total} = (N_{X} \cdot L + N_{Y} \cdot W) \cdot N_{layers} \cdot K_{waste}$$

Where:

  • $L, W$ - length and width of the slab.
  • $S$ - grid spacing (usually 0.2 m).
  • $N_{layers}$ - number of reinforcement layers (standard 2).

Advantages and disadvantages of fiberglass reinforcement

When choosing a material, it is important to take into account the physicochemical properties of ASP.

Pros:

  • Corrosion resistance: Absolutely does not rust, ideal for wet soils, foundations of bathhouses and piers.
  • Radio transparency: Does not interfere with Wi-Fi and cellular communications inside the house (unlike a steel circuit).
  • Absence of cold bridges: Thermal conductivity is 100 times lower than that of steel.
  • Ease: The weight of 100 meters of reinforcement with a diameter of 8 mm is only about 8 kg.

Cons (limitations):

  • Low modulus of elasticity: ASP stretches stronger than steel under load. Not recommended for floors and long spans where deflection is important.
  • Inability to bend on an object: The corners of the foundation cannot be reinforced with bent composite reinforcement (it will break). For corners, it is necessary to use special factory elements or bent steel rods.
  • Heat resistance: Loses load-bearing capacity when heated above 600°C (not critical for private housing construction, since the reinforcement is inside the concrete).

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to weld composite reinforcement?

No, fiberglass does not conduct current and burns at high temperatures. The connection is made only viscous (plastic clamps or annealed wire).

How to knit reinforcement: with wire or ties?

For TSA, plastic ties (clamps) are most often used. It is 30-50% faster than wire knitting and does not require a special hook. The strength of the unit is sufficient to fix the frame before pouring.

How to cut fiberglass?

The material is easily cut with a conventional grinder (angle grinder) with a cutting wheel for metal or stone, as well as a hacksaw for metal (for small diameters).
