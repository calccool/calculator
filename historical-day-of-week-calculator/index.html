Zeller's Algorithm: Mathematical Basis

Accurate calculations are based on Zeller algorithm - a formula developed by the German mathematician Christian Zeller in the 19th century. It allows you to calculate the day of the week for any date in the Gregorian or Julian calendar.

For the Gregorian calendar the formula is as follows:

$$h = (q + \lfloor \frac{13(m+1)}{5} \rfloor + K + \lfloor \frac{K}{4} \rfloor + \lfloor \frac{J}{4} \rfloor — 2J) \pmod 7$$

Where variables mean:

  • $h$ - result (0 = Saturday, 1 = Sunday, 2 = Monday, etc.);
  • $q$ — day of the month;
  • $m$ - month number (in the algorithm March is 3, and January and February are considered the 13th and 14th months of the previous year);
  • $K$ - centenary year ( $year \pmod{100}$ );
  • $J$ - century number ( $\lfloor year / 100 \rfloor$ ).

Gregorian vs Julian calendars

When working with historical dates, it is important to remember the calendar reform of Pope Gregory XIII. In October 1582, the accumulated error of the Julian calendar led to the fact that after October 4, October 15 immediately arrived.
