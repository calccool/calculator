Online linoleum calculator: calculation of area, price and laying schemes

Buying flooring is a balance between savings and quality of joints. Linoleum is supplied in rolls of a fixed width, so the main task of the calculator is to determine the optimal number of strips and the direction of their laying to minimize waste.

Our tool uses an advanced algorithm that compares two scenarios: laying along and across the room. This allows you to choose the option with the least amount of scraps and the most favorable final price.

Mathematical calculation model

To calculate the total amount of material, we use a formula that takes into account the technological stock ( $M$ ) and the pattern pitch ( $R$ ).

If the room width ( $W_{room}$ ) is greater than the roll width ( $W_{roll}$ ), the number of strips ( $N$ ) is calculated as:

$$N = \lceil \frac{W_{room} + M}{W_{roll}} \rceil$$

The total length of the roll ( $L_{total}$ ) in the presence of a pattern (rapport) is calculated as follows: the first strip only requires margin for trimming, and each subsequent strip requires additional margin for matching the pattern:

$$L_{total} = (L_{room} + M) + (N — 1) \cdot (L_{room} + M + R)$$

Where:

  • $L_{room}$ - room length in meters;
  • $M$ - trimming margin (standard 0.1 m);
  • $R$ - repeat (pattern step).

Key parameters when choosing

When working with a calculator, it is important to consider three critical factors that affect the accuracy of the result:

  • Roll width: Linoleum is available in standard sizes. Choosing a width that is as close as possible to the width of your room avoids unnecessary seams.
  • Trimming margin: Walls in modern apartments are rarely perfectly parallel. We recommend leaving a minimum of 10 cm (5 cm on each side) to compensate for the curvature of the walls.
  • Rapport (drawing step): If you choose linoleum “like parquet” or with a geometric pattern, you will have to move the strips relative to each other for a perfect fit. This increases material consumption.
