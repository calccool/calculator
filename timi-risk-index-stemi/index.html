TIMI Risk Index (TRI) Calculator: Prediction of Mortality in Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)

TIMI Risk Index (TRI) is a validated clinical tool designed to rapidly assess the risk of in-hospital mortality in patients with acute ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI).

Unlike the more complex TIMI risk scores (which requires 7 parameters), this index is based on only three vital signs that are known to the doctor already in the first minutes of contact with the patient. This makes it an indispensable tool for emergency physicians, cardiologists and resuscitators during the initial triage of patients.

Calculation formula

The TRI was developed by the research group Thrombolysis in Myocardial Infarction (InTIME-II study, Morrow et al., 2000). The model is based on the pathophysiological relationship between hemodynamic instability and survival prognosis.

The formula looks like this:

$$\text{TRI} = \frac{\text{ЧСС} \times (\text{Возраст} / 10)^2}{\text{Систолическое АД}}$$

Where:

  • Heart rate — Heart rate (bpm).
  • Age - Full years.
  • Systolic blood pressure — Upper blood pressure (mm Hg).

Interpretation of results: Risk groups

According to a study of more than 13,000 patients, there is a direct correlation between the index value and 30-day mortality. The higher the score, the higher the risk of cardiogenic shock and death.

Influence of parameters on forecast

This calculator takes into account the “triad” of factors that determine the patient’s hemodynamic status:

  1. Age: It is the strongest independent predictor of mortality. In the formula, age is squared, which reflects the exponential increase in risks in older patients.
  2. Heart rate (HR): Tachycardia during a heart attack often indicates sympathetic activation in response to pain or, worse, a decrease in stroke volume and incipient heart failure.
  3. Systolic blood pressure: In the context of acute infarction, low blood pressure (hypotension) is an ominous sign of left ventricular dysfunction and impending cardiogenic shock. That is why blood pressure is in the denominator of the formula: the lower it is, the higher the risk index.

Clinical Application

The TIMI risk index is used for:

  • Triage: Quickly identify patients who require more aggressive therapy or transport to centers with PCI (percutaneous coronary intervention) capabilities.
  • Forecasting: Estimates of the likelihood of developing cardiogenic shock in the first 24 hours of hospitalization.
  • Monitoring: Assessment of the effectiveness of the therapy (a decrease in the index over time indicates stabilization of the condition).

Important: This tool is auxiliary. The final decision on treatment tactics is made by the attending physician based on a comprehensive assessment of the patient’s condition, ECG and biomarkers of myocardial necrosis.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between TIMI Risk Score and TIMI Risk Index?

The TIMI Risk Score uses 7-8 variables (including medical history, angina, ST elevation, weight, etc.) and gives a score from 0 to 14. The TIMI Risk Index (this calculator) uses continuous variables (BP, HR, Age) and is calculated using a mathematical formula. The index is considered more convenient for quick assessment at the patient's bedside.

For which patients is this calculator applicable?

It has been validated primarily for patients with STEMI (ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction) for whom thrombolysis or primary PCI is planned.

What value is considered critical?

Index value is higher 30 (Group V) is associated with an almost 20-fold increase in the risk of death compared with group I. Such patients require immediate attention from the resuscitation team.
