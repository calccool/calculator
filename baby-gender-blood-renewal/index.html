Gender of the child based on blood renewal: Online calculator (Boy or Girl?)

Baby gender calculator using the blood renewal method

One of the most popular “folk” methods of planning pregnancy is the theory of blood renewal. Despite the fact that official medicine is skeptical about this method, statistics show a surprisingly high percentage of coincidences.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly determine whose blood - the father's or the mother's - is dominant at the time of conception, and predict the gender of the unborn baby.

The essence of the method: Update cycles

The theory is based on the assertion that blood cells in the human body are renewed with a certain cyclicity, which depends on gender. It is believed that at the moment of renewal, the blood reaches maximum strength, and then gradually “ages.”

The gender of the child depends on whose genetic material is stronger at the moment of conception. And it is stronger in the parent whose blood is “younger” (that is, less time has passed since the date of the last update).

Cycle frequency:

  • For men: Blood is renewed every 4 years.
  • For women: Blood is renewed every 3 years.

How is the calculation made?

To determine sex, it is necessary to calculate the coefficient of “freshness” of the blood. This is done by dividing the parent's age by its cycle length. We are not interested in the whole number (how many times the blood was renewed in total), but remainder.

The calculation formula is as follows:

$$R = Age \pmod{Cycle}$$

Where:

  • $R$ (Remainder) is a residue indicating the “age” of the blood in the current cycle.
  • $Age$ - the exact age of the parent at the time of conception.
  • $Cycle$ - cycle duration (3 for women, 4 for men).

Example:

Let's say a man is 29 years old and a woman is 25 years old.

  1. Father: $29 / 4 = 7$ (whole cycles) and remainder 1 year.
  2. Mother: $25 / 3 = 8$ (whole cycles) and remainder 1 year.

In this case, the residuals are equal, the probability of having children of different sexes (twins) or the error of the method increases.

If the man was 30 years old:

  1. Father: $30 / 4 = 7.5$ (remainder 2 years ).
  2. Mother: (remainder 1 year ).
  3. Result: The mother's blood (1 year) is younger than the father's blood (2 years). Forecast - Girl.

Factors affecting the calculation

It is important to remember that “updating” is not only a calendar process. There are factors that can forcefully restart the blood renewal cycle ahead of schedule. For the most accurate forecast, instead of the date of birth, you should use the date of the last major event from the list below (if there was one):

  • Major blood loss: Accidents, injuries with loss of more than 1 liter of blood.
  • Operations: Surgical interventions.
  • Donation: Regular blood donation.
  • Childbirth: Childbirth itself is a process of renewal for a woman.
