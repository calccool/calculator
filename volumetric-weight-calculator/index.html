Parcel volumetric weight calculator (SDEK, Russian Post, PEK)

In the field of logistics and cargo transportation, there are two concepts of mass: actual (scale readings) and volumetric (overall). Shipping companies use the concept of volumetric weight to estimate the density of cargo. Since light but large packages take up a lot of usable space in the cargo hold of an airplane or truck, carriers charge for “air” if the volumetric weight exceeds the actual weight.

This online tool allows you to instantly determine the estimated weight of your parcel, taking into account the current coefficients (divisors) of the most popular logistics operators in Russia and the world.

Formula for calculating volumetric weight

The calculation is based on the external dimensions of the package. The standard mathematical model used in SDEK, PEC and other services is as follows:

$$V_w = \frac{L \times W \times H}{K} \times N$$

Where:

  • $V_w$ - final volumetric weight (kg);
  • $L$ - package length (cm);
  • $W$ — package width (cm);
  • $H$ —package height (cm);
  • $K$ — transport company divisor;
  • $N$ - the number of identical packages in the batch.

Coefficients (divisors) of popular carriers

Each company sets its own divisor depending on the transport used and internal tariffs. The lower the divisor, the more expensive the cargo volume is for the sender.

How to use an online calculator

To get an accurate result, follow the algorithm:

  1. Enter dimensions: indicate the length, width and height of one box in centimeters. You can use the quick selection buttons for standard sizes (XS to L).
  2. Please indicate your physical weight: Enter the actual weight of the parcel in kilograms.
  3. Select delivery service: Select the desired divisor from the list or enter your own value (Custom) if your carrier uses a non-standard factor.
  4. Specify quantity: If you are sending a batch of several identical boxes, activate the "Multiple identical locations" switch.
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will automatically determine the volume in cubic meters ( $м^3$ ), compare the weight and highlight the value that will be used for invoicing.

Why might the final payable weight be higher?

Logistics companies always choose greatest of two values:

  • If $Физический\,вес > Объемный\,вес$ , payment is based on actual weight (beneficial for heavy and compact cargo, for example, spare parts).
  • If $Объемный\,вес > Физический\,вес$ , payment is based on dimensions (typical for clothes, pillows, electronics).

Calculation example

Let's say you send a box of soft toys weighing 3 kg and dimensions $50 \times 40 \times 40$ cm via SDEK (divisor 5000).

  1. Find the volumetric weight: $(50 \times 40 \times 40) / 5000 = 16$ kg.
  2. Compare: $16\,кг (объем) > 3\,кг (физика)$ .
  3. Result: You will pay for 16 kg, despite the fact that the parcel weighs only 3 kg.
