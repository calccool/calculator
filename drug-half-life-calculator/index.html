What is the half-life ( $T_{1/2}$ )?

Half-life (Half-Life) - this is the time required for the concentration of a substance (drug, alcohol, radioisotope) in the blood plasma to decrease by exactly 50% of the peak value.

This indicator is an individual characteristic for each drug, but may vary depending on the condition of the patient’s liver and kidneys, his age and metabolism.

Calculation formulas

The calculator is based on the exponential decay model (first order kinetics) that describes the elimination of most drugs.

1. Basic concentration formula

To find out the concentration ( $C_t$ ) after time $t$ :

$$C_t = C_0 \times (0.5)^{\frac{t}{T_{1/2}}}$$

Where:

  • $C_0$ - initial dose or concentration.
  • $t$ - past tense.
  • $T_{1/2}$ - half-life.

2. Half-life formula

If two concentrations are known at different times:

$$T_{1/2} = \frac{t \times \ln(2)}{\ln(C_0 / C_t)}$$

5 half-life rule

In clinical pharmacology, there is a “Golden Rule”: it is believed that the drug is completely eliminated from the body within 5 half-lives.

Reference: Half-lives of popular substances

Below are the average values ​​for a healthy adult (for reference):

  • Caffeine: 5–6 hours
  • Ibuprofen: 2–4 hours
  • Paracetamol: 1–4 hours
  • Aspirin: 0.25 hours (15 minutes)
  • Diazepam: 20–100 hours (very long lasting effect)
  • Alcohol (Ethanol): Has zero order kinetics (derived linearly, not exponentially), so for an accurate calculation use our Alcohol calculator (Vidmarka).
