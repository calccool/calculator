How does a soap calculator work?

The algorithm is based on saponification coefficients (SAP values) for each specific type of fat. We've included the most popular base oils that provide the perfect balance of firmness, cleansing and foam.

Main stages of calculation:

  • Selecting the type of alkali: NaOH for classic bars or KOH for shampoos and beldi.
  • Entering oil weights: Enter the mass of each component in grams.
  • Superfat setting: Select the percentage of free oils that will remain in the skin care soap.
  • Determination of aqueous phase: Adjust the concentration of the solution (standard - 33%).

Mathematical model of saponification

To calculate the final weight of alkali, a formula is used that sums the product of the mass of each oil by its specific saponification index, adjusted for insurance (overfat):

$$Lye_{mass} = \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} (Oil_{i} \times SAP_{i}) \right) \times (1 — \frac{Superfat}{100})$$

If you are making liquid soap, the calculator additionally takes into account the purity of the KOH alkali, since it is most often found on sale in a concentration of 90%. The formula is adjusted as follows:

$$KOH_{final} = \frac{Lye_{discounted}}{Purity / 100}$$

Safety Tips

Working with alkali requires strict adherence to safety precautions. Always remember the main rule: lye is poured into water, and not vice versa. Use protective equipment (gloves, goggles, respirator) and work in a well-ventilated area.
