Future annuity value calculator: Calculation of savings and deposits

Planning for your financial future requires precision. Future Annuity Value Calculator is a powerful tool for investors, savers and anyone who wants to create capital. Unlike simple deposit calculators, this tool takes into account regular deposits (annuity payments) and the effect of compound interest, allowing you to see a true picture of how your money is growing over time.

How to use the calculator

The tool is designed for maximum flexibility. To get an accurate forecast, fill out the following fields:

  1. Payment amount (PMT): The amount of your regular contribution (for example, how much you save from your salary each month).
  2. Contribution frequency: Choose how often you deposit money - monthly, quarterly or annually. The calculator will automatically adjust the rate for the selected period.
  3. Annual rate (r): The expected return on your deposit or investment portfolio as a percentage per annum.
  4. Accumulation period: The length of time in years over which you plan to make contributions.
  5. Annuity type: A critical parameter that is often overlooked:
    • Postnumerando (End): The contribution is made at the end of the period (standard for most bank deposits).
    • Prenumerando (Begin): The contribution is made at the beginning of the period (typical for insurance savings programs or rent).

What is the difference between an ordinary annuity and a prenumerando annuity?

Many online calculators only consider a “regular” annuity (payment at the end of the month). However, if you invest at the beginning of the month (“pay yourself first”), your money lasts exactly one period longer.

Over long distances the difference can be significant. Our algorithm allows you to choose the type Timing, to take this nuance into account and show you the most accurate amount.

Calculation Formulas

For those who want to understand the mathematics of the process, we provide the formulas used in the calculations.

1. For an ordinary annuity (payment at the end of the period):

FV = P * ((1 + r)^n — 1) / r

2. For an annuity prenumerando (payment at the beginning of the period):

FV_due = FV * (1 + r)

Where:

  • FV — Future Value.
  • P — Amount of regular payment.
  • r — Interest rate for the period (annual rate / number of payments per year).
  • n — Total number of payments (years * payments per year).
