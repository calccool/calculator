Golden Ratio Facial Calculator: Test for Symmetry and Ideal Proportions

Beauty is subjective, but mathematics says otherwise. For centuries, artists, sculptors and plastic surgeons have used Golden Ratio (Fibonacci number, $\phi \approx 1.618$ ) as a universal standard of aesthetic harmony.

Our face symmetry calculator uses algorithms based on the Marquardt Beauty Mask to analyze your features. It measures the distances between key points (anthropometric landmarks) and calculates how close they are to the mathematical ideal.

How to measure your face correctly?

To get an accurate result, you will need a mirror and a ruler (or a full-face photo and a graphic editor). To make the beauty test objective, keep your face relaxed, without smiling.

The calculator requests the following parameters (see diagram in the calculator):

  1. Vertical measurements:
    • Face height: The distance from the hairline (trichion) to the lower point of the chin (mentone).
    • Eye position: From the top of the head to the line of the pupils.
    • Nose and lips: Distances from the pupils to the tip of the nose and to the closure of the lips.
  2. Horizontal measurements:
    • Nose width: In the widest part of the wings of the nose.
    • Face Width: In the widest part (usually along the cheekbones).

Mathematics of beauty: Formulas for the ideal face

The ideal face obeys a strict geometric progression. The main idea is that the ratio of the larger segment to the smaller one should tend to the number Phi.

Here are the main relationships that our algorithm checks:

1. Rule of the “Cross”

The width of the face should be related to the width of the nose, just as the height of the face is related to the width of the face. But a more accurate formula for the nose area looks like this:

$$Ratio = \frac{\text{Width of Face}}{\text{Outer Distance Between Eyes}} \approx 1.618$$

2. Nose and Lips

This is the “golden triangle” of the face. In ideal proportion, the width of the mouth should be $\phi$ times the width of the nose:

$$Lips Width = Nose Width \times 1.618$$

3. Vertical harmony

The distance from nose to chin should be related to the distance from lips to chin as 1.618:

$$\frac{\text{Nose to Chin}}{\text{Lips to Chin}} \approx 1.618$$

What does your result mean?

After entering the data, the calculator will display «Symmetry Score» (Symmetry Score) as a percentage.

  • 90-100%: Your proportions are phenomenally close to the “Marquardt mask.” Such faces are often found on top models and actors.
  • 80-89%: Great harmony. The face is perceived by others as very attractive.
  • 60-79%: Normal, natural symmetry. Most people are in this range. Small deviations give the face uniqueness and character.
  • < 60%: Significant deviations from the golden ratio. However, remember that mathematics does not take into account charm, skin condition and sparkle in the eyes.
