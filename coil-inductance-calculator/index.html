Inductance coil calculator: Online calculation of turns and parameters

This tool is designed for radio amateurs, engineers and DIY enthusiasts. Our reel calculator allows you to quickly determine the physical and electrical properties of the solenoid without complex manual calculations. The script automatically determines the type of winding (single-layer or multi-layer) and warns if the permissible current density is exceeded.

How to use the calculator

To obtain accurate data, fill in the following fields:

  1. Wire diameter (d): Specify the thickness of the core without insulation. This affects the resistance and laying of the turns.
  2. Number of turns (N): The planned number of turns of the wire.
  3. Frame geometry: Enter the diameter (D) and winding length (L).
  4. Operating current (I): Necessary for calculating voltage drop, power and heating.

Main calculated parameters

Our algorithm uses adapted formulas for the metric system, providing high accuracy for air coils (without ferromagnetic core).

1. Inductance (L)

The main parameter showing the ability of the coil to accumulate magnetic field energy. For single-layer coils, the approximate Wheeler formula is used:

L (uH) = (D^2 * N^2) / (45 * D + 100 * Length)

Where:

  • L (uH) — inductance in microhenry.
  • D — frame diameter in cm.
  • N - number of turns.
  • Length — winding length in cm.

2. Active resistance (R)

Depends on the conductor material. We have added the ability to select materials, as this is critical for accurate calculations.

R = rho * l / S

  • rho (ρ) — electrical resistivity of the material.
  • l - total length of the wire.
  • S - cross-sectional area of ​​the wire.

3. Current density (J)

Critical security parameter. If the current density exceeds 4-6 A/mm², the coil will begin to get very hot, which can lead to melting of the insulation (varnish) and an interturn short circuit. Our calculator will highlight this parameter in red if the value is outside safe limits.

Multilayer winding

If the wire diameter and number of turns are such that they do not fit in one layer on a given frame length, the calculator automatically switches to calculation mode multilayer coil. This takes into account the increase in the average diameter of the turn, which affects both the length of the required wire and the final inductance.

Where is this calculation applied?

  • Acoustics: Calculation of coils for crossovers (frequency filters) of speakers.
  • Radio engineering: Creation of oscillatory circuits, antennas and matching devices.
  • Electromagnets: Calculation of solenoids to create mechanical traction.
  • Power supplies: Design of chokes for noise suppression.
