State duty calculator for the arbitration court: calculation according to the new rules

Since September 9, 2024, the cost of justice in arbitration courts has increased significantly. Old limits and minimum payments are a thing of the past. Our tool allows you to instantly calculate the exact amount of payment, based on the current version of Article 333.21 of the Tax Code of the Russian Federation.

The calculator takes into account:

  • Cost of claim (for property disputes);
  • Category of the plaintiff (individual, individual entrepreneur or organization);
  • Type of appeal (statement of claim or application for a court order).

Table of new state duty rates for property claims

Disputes subject to assessment are now subject to a progressive scale. The higher the claim price, the larger the fixed part and the lower the interest rate for exceeding the threshold.

Calculation formulas

To understand the mathematics of the process, we present the basic formulas that our algorithm uses.

If the claim price ( $S$ ) is in the range from 1 million to 10 million rubles, the total amount ( $P$ ) is calculated as follows:

$$P = 55\,000 + (S — 1\,000\,000) \times 0.03$$

When submitting an application for extradition court order, the state duty is 50% from the standard amount:

$$P_{order} = \frac{P_{standard}}{2}$$

Features of non-property claims

If your dispute is not subject to assessment (for example, declaring a transaction invalid or appealing decisions of government agencies), the amount of the fee is fixed and depends on the legal status of the plaintiff:

For individuals and individual entrepreneurs

For citizens and individual entrepreneurs for most non-property disputes, the rate is 15,000 rubles.

For legal entities

Organizations for filing similar claims are required to pay to the budget 50,000 rubles.

Payment procedure and important nuances

  1. Rounding: According to the rules, the amount of state duty is rounded to whole rubles (kopecks less 50 are discarded and 50 and more are rounded to the nearest ruble).
  2. Validity: The payment receipt is valid for a long time, but the amount must correspond to the rates in effect on the day the claim is filed in court.
  3. Benefits: A number of plaintiffs (for example, disabled people of groups I and II, veterans) may be entitled to an exemption from payment or a reduction in the amount if the cost of the claim does not exceed the established limits.
