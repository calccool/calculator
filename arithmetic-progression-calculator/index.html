Arithmetic progression is one of the fundamental number sequences in algebra. It is a series of numbers in which each subsequent term is obtained from the previous one by adding the same number to it. This number is called difference of arithmetic progression and is designated by the letter $d$ .

Understanding the principles of how progression works is necessary not only for solving school problems, but also in financial calculations (for example, when calculating simple interest), physics (uniformly accelerated motion) and construction. Basic formulas for arithmetic progression

For manual calculations or checking results, it is useful to know the basic mathematical relationships.

nth term formula

To find any term of a progression without calculating all the previous ones, use the general term formula:

$$a_n = a_1 + d(n — 1)$$

Where:

  • $a_n$ —the required nth term;
  • $a_1$ is the first member of the sequence;
  • $d$ — progression difference;
  • $n$ - member serial number.

Formula for the sum of the first n terms

The sum of an arithmetic progression ( $S_n$ ) shows the result of adding all numbers from the first to the nth inclusive. There are two ways to find it.

Through the first and last term:

$$S_n = \frac{a_1 + a_n}{2} \cdot n$$

Through the first term and the difference:

$$S_n = \frac{2a_1 + d(n — 1)}{2} \cdot n$$

Example of problem solution

Let's consider a classic problem to understand the logic of the calculations.

Condition: An arithmetic progression is given, where the first term is $a_1 = 3$ and the difference is $d = 4$ . You need to find the 10th term of this sequence and the sum of the first 10 terms.

Solution:

  1. Find the 10th term ( $a_{10}$ ): We use the formula $a_n = a_1 + d(n — 1)$ . $$a_{10} = 3 + 4 \cdot (10 — 1) = 3 + 4 \cdot 9 = 3 + 36 = 39$$
  2. Find the sum ( $S_{10}$ ): We use the sum formula through the first and last terms. $$S_{10} = \frac{3 + 39}{2} \cdot 10 = \frac{42}{2} \cdot 10 = 21 \cdot 10 = 210$$

Answer: The 10th term is 39, the sum of the first ten terms is 210.

Characteristic property

This is an important theorem that is often used to test whether a sequence is an arithmetic progression.

Each term of an arithmetic progression (starting from the second) is equal to the arithmetic mean of its neighboring terms.

Mathematical notation for the property:

$$a_n = \frac{a_{n-1} + a_{n+1}}{2}$$

If this equality holds for any three consecutive numbers in a series, then you have an arithmetic progression.
