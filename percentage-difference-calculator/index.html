Percentage difference calculator: online calculation of the change between numbers

In business, analytics, and everyday life, there is often a need to understand how one value differs from another, not in absolute terms, but in relative terms. Our percentage difference calculator is a multifunctional tool that allows you to instantly calculate the dynamics of indicators, be it revenue growth, weight changes or measurement errors.

Using percentages helps standardize data, making it comparable. For example, an increase of 100 rubles may be significant for the price of bread, but unnoticeable for the cost of a car. The percentage difference immediately puts everything in its place.

Calculation methods: which one to choose?

Our calculator supports two fundamental mathematical approaches. The choice of method depends on your goals:

Relative Change Method

This method is used when you have a clear chronology: “was” (number A) and “became” (number B). It shows by what percentage the final value increased or decreased relative to the initial one.

Calculation formula:

$$\Delta\% = \frac{B — A}{A} \times 100\%$$

  • Positive result: means percentage growth.
  • Negative result: means percentage drop.

Symmetric Difference

Used when there is no cause-and-effect relationship or chronology between the numbers (for example, comparing two different stores). In this case, the difference is calculated relative to the arithmetic mean of these numbers.

Calculation formula:

$$\Delta\%_{sym} = \frac{|B — A|}{(|A| + |B|) / 2} \times 100\%$$

Frequently asked questions

How do you find out by what percentage the number has increased?

Enter the initial value and the final value - the calculator will show a positive percentage if there is an increase.

How to determine percentage reduction?

If the second number is less than the first, the result will be negative, for example -15%, which means a decrease of 15%.

How to calculate what percentage a number is of another?

Use the second part of the calculator - enter both numbers and get the share of each as a percentage of their total.
