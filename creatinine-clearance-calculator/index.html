Creatinine Clearance Calculator: Assessing Kidney Function

Welcome to our advanced medical calculator designed to help you accurately estimate creatinine clearance (CC). This indicator is a key indicator Glomerular filtration rate (GFR) - the most important parameter that reflects how effectively the kidneys clear the blood of waste products, such as creatinine.

Our tool is indispensable for:

  • Medical workers: For precise dosage of medications that are excreted by the kidneys.
  • Patients: For monitoring the condition of the kidneys in chronic diseases (CKD).
  • Medical students: To study and verify calculations using various methodologies.

Unlike simple calculators, our tool provides calculations using three key formulas at once and automatically determines the stage of chronic kidney disease (CKD).

How to use the calculator?

To calculate, simply enter your data:

  1. Formula: Select a method (CKD-EPI is recommended as the most modern).
  2. Age and Gender: These parameters are critical because muscle mass (and therefore creatinine production) varies.
  3. Serum creatinine: Enter the result of your analysis.
  4. Units: Indicate what the laboratory measured your creatinine - in mg/dl (mg/dL) or µmol/l (μmol/L). The calculator will automatically perform the conversion.
  5. Weight: Required only for the Cockcroft-Gault formula.

Formulas for Calculating Creatinine Clearance (GFR)

The calculator uses three internationally recognized formulas to provide the most comprehensive analysis.

1. Cockcroft-Gault formula

This is the "classical" method, developed in 1973. It is easy to calculate and is still widely used to adjust drug doses. Its main feature is accounting body weight patient.

Formula:

$$CrCl = \frac{(140 — \text{Age}) \times \text{Weight (kg)}}{(72 \times \text{SCr (mg/dL)})} \times \text{SexFactor}$$

  • Where SexFactor = $1$ for men and $0.85$ for women.

2. Formula CKD-EPI 2021 (Recommended)

It is the most modern and most accurate method for estimating GFR in adults, recommended by international nephrology societies. Formula 2021 does not use race coefficient, which makes it universal. It does not require weight, as it normalizes the result to a standard body surface area (1.73 m²).

Formula:

$$GFR = 142 \times \min(\frac{\text{SCr}}{\kappa}, 1)^{\alpha} \times \max(\frac{\text{SCr}}{\kappa}, 1)^{-1.200} \times 0.9938^{\text{Age}} \times \text{SexFactor}$$

  • For women: $\kappa = 0.7$, $\alpha = -0.241$, $\text{SexFactor} = 1.012$
  • For men: $\kappa = 0.9$, $\alpha = -0.302$, $\text{SexFactor} = 1$
  • (SCr - serum creatinine in mg/dl)

3. Jelliffe Formula

An older method that also does not require weight. Sometimes used in specific clinical situations.

Formula:

$$\text{CrCl (Male)} = \frac{98 — (0.8 \times (\text{Age} — 20))}{\text{SCr (mg/dL)}}$$

  • For women, the result is multiplied by 0.9.

Importance of Units: mg/dL vs µmol/L

Different laboratories report results in different units. Entering units incorrectly is the most common error in calculations.

  • mg/dl (mg/dL) - milligrams per deciliter.
  • µmol/l (μmol/L) - micromoles per liter.

Our calculator automatically converts values ​​using the standard ratio: $$1 \text{ mg/dL} = 88.4 \text{ \mu mol/L}$$

Interpretation of Results: Stages of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

The resulting GFR result (usually according to the formula CKD-EPI ) allows you to determine the stage of chronic kidney disease (CKD).
