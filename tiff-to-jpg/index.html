Technical aspects and calculation formulas

When converting, it is important to understand how the image parameters change. Our algorithm allows you to change the Scale of the image. The final resolution is calculated using the formula:

$$W_{new} = \text{round}(W_{orig} \cdot \frac{P_{scale}}{100})$$

$$H_{new} = \text{round}(H_{orig} \cdot \frac{P_{scale}}{100})$$

Where:

  • $W, H$ - width and height in pixels;
  • $P_{scale}$ - the zoom percentage you select (from 10% to 200%).

Since JPG does not support transparency, if there is an alpha channel in the original TIFF, the resulting $C_{res}$ pixel is calculated by overlaying the color of the original $C_{src}$ pixel onto your chosen $C_{bg}$ background:

$$C_{res} = C_{src} \cdot \alpha + C_{bg} \cdot (1 — \alpha)$$
