Floor tile calculator: Accurate calculation taking into account layout and margin

An error in calculations when purchasing tiles or porcelain stoneware is one of the most common and costly problems during renovations. If there is not enough material, it may be impossible to purchase “exactly the same” box: different batches (tones) of the same collection may differ significantly in shade. If you buy too much, you will simply freeze your budget in unnecessary leftovers that are difficult to return.

Our floor tile calculator takes into account the geometry of the room, the installation method and even the multiplicity of packaging, so that you receive a ready-made estimate in seconds.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

To get the most accurate result, follow the algorithm:

  1. Room measurements. Enter the length and width of the room in meters. If the room has a complex shape (L-shaped, with niches), use the “Add area” or “Exclude area” fields.
    • Advice: Measure the floor at three points (at two walls and in the center) and take the maximum value if the geometry is broken.
  2. Selecting a layout. This is a critical parameter affecting the safety factor (see table below).
  3. Tile options. Enter the dimensions of one element (in cm). For example, for standard porcelain tiles it is 60×60 or 20×120 (wood-look).
  4. Packaging and price. If you know how many meters there are in one pack (usually indicated on the price tag, for example, 1.44 m²), enter this value. The calculator will round up your order to whole boxes.

Calculation formulas

To understand the logic of the tool’s operation, we present a mathematical calculation model.

1. Calculation of base area ( $S_{base}$ ):

$$S_{base} = (L_{room} \times W_{room}) + S_{add} — S_{sub}$$

Where $L$ and $W$ are the length and width of the room.

2. Area including stock ( $S_{total}$ ):

$$S_{total} = S_{base} \times K_{waste}$$

3. Number of tiles ( $N_{tiles}$ ):

$$N_{tiles} = \left\lceil \frac{S_{total}}{L_{tile} \times W_{tile}} \right\rceil$$

Note: The symbol $\lceil x \rceil$ means round up to the nearest whole number.

4. Calculation of the number of packages ( $N_{packs}$ ):

If the footage of one pack is known ( $S_{pack}$ ):

$$N_{packs} = \left\lceil \frac{S_{total}}{S_{pack}} \right\rceil$$

3 expert tips before purchasing

  1. Check the caliber and tone. Each box is marked with a tone code (Tone) and caliber (Caliber). All boxes must have the same markings. Different batches will differ in color and size by 1-2mm.
  2. Consider the seams. The calculator calculates the “net” area of ​​the tile. The presence of seams (usually 1.5–2 mm for porcelain stoneware) slightly reduces consumption, but this is neglected to create a safety margin.
  3. Don't throw away leftovers. After repair, save 1-2 tiles. If something falls and cracks the floor in the future, you can replace the element. It will be impossible to find the same collection in 5 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to subtract the area under the furniture?

If you are planning a built-in kitchen or a huge wardrobe that will never move, the area under them can be subtracted (the “Exclude area” field). However, for bathrooms and free-standing furniture, it is recommended to tile the entire floor surface for waterproofing and aesthetics.

How to calculate the plinth?

Our calculator automatically displays the perimeter of the room. Subtract the width of the doorway from this number to find out the required plinth molding. The margin for the baseboard is usually +5%.
