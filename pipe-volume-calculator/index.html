Pipe volume calculator: online calculation in liters and m³

Accurate calculation of pipe volume is a critical task in the design of heating, water supply and process piping systems. An error in calculations can lead to a lack of coolant, incorrect choice of expansion tank, or excessive costs for antifreeze.

Our calculator allows you to instantly get data on the internal volume (capacity) and external area of ​​the pipe for painting or insulation.

When is volume calculation necessary?

The most common scenarios that engineers and homeowners face when calculating are:

  • Filling the heating system: determining the exact number of liters of water or expensive antifreeze.
  • Purchase of thermal insulation: calculation of the external surface area of ​​the pipe.
  • Hydraulic calculations: determination of system inertia and flow speed.
  • Construction: using pipes as piles or hollow columns that need to be filled with concrete.

Formulas and calculation methods

The calculator is based on geometric formulas for the volume of a cylinder. To obtain the result, we need to know the internal radius and the total length of the highway.

Calculation of cavity volume

The basic formula for calculating volume is as follows:

$$V = \pi \cdot R^2 \cdot L$$

Where:

  • $V$ — pipe volume ( $м^3$ );
  • $\pi$ — mathematical constant (~3.14159);
  • $R$ — inner radius of the pipe ( $м$ );
  • $L$ - pipe length ( $м$ ).

If you know the diameter ( $D$ ), the formula transforms into:

$$V = \frac{\pi \cdot D^2}{4} \cdot L$$

Conversion to liters

Since liters are more often used in everyday tasks, the calculator automatically converts cubic meters according to the ratio: 1 $м^3$ = 1000 liters.

Coolant mass calculation

In addition to the geometric volume, it is important to consider the mass of the contents, since it creates a load on the supports and fastenings. Mass is calculated by multiplying the volume by the density of the liquid:

$$M = V \cdot \rho$$

The calculator has built-in density presets for the main types of liquids:

  1. Water: $\rho \approx 1000$ $кг/м^3$ .
  2. Ethylene glycol (Antifreeze): $\rho \approx 1070$ $кг/м^3$ .
  3. Machine oil: $\rho \approx 850$ $кг/м^3$ .

Features of calculation by outer diameter

If you do not know the internal diameter, use the “By external and wall” calculation mode. In this case, the program itself will calculate the internal clearance:

$$D_{inner} = D_{outer} — 2 \cdot S$$

Where $S$ is the wall thickness. This is the most accurate method for modern polypropylene (PN20, PN25) and steel water and gas pipes.
