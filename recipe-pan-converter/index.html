Calculator for converting recipe ingredients to a different mold size

Every pastry chef has encountered a situation where the recipe specifies a mold with a diameter of 18 cm, and only available 24 cm. Using the original amount of products will result in the sponge cake being too thin or, conversely, “running away” from the mold.

Our online calculator solves this problem by calculating the exact scaling factor. Unlike simple tools, this algorithm supports:

  • Changing geometry (for example, from a round shape to a rectangular one);
  • Volumetric conversion taking into account the height of the sides;
  • Automatic scaling of the product list.

Mathematical precision: how the algorithm works

Recalculation is based on comparison of areas or volumes of geometric shapes. To get the desired result, the calculator calculates the ratio of the parameters of your form to the parameters from the recipe.

Formulas used

To calculate the area of ​​round shapes, use the circle formula:

$$S = \pi \cdot r^2 = \frac{\pi \cdot d^2}{4}$$

Where $d$ is the diameter and $r$ is the radius.

For rectangular and square shapes:

$$S = a \cdot b$$

Where $a$ and $b$ are the width and length of the sides.

The main indicator is Conversion factor ( $K$ ). It is calculated as follows:

  1. By area: $K = \frac{S_{new}}{S_{old}}$ (if the height of the products is the same).
  2. By volume: $K = \frac{V_{new}}{V_{old}}$ , where $V = S \cdot h$ ( $h$ is the side height).

All ingredients from the original list are simply multiplied by the resulting number $K$ .

Advanced mode: volume and height accounting

Standard conversion by diameter is not always sufficient. If you plan to bake a tall cake in a wide but low pan, you need to activate the option “Consider the height of the forms”.

This is critical for:

  • Mousse cakes;
  • Cheesecakes, where the height of the cheese layer is important;
  • Baking soufflés and biscuits with varying degrees of rise.

In this mode, the calculator switches from plane analysis to three-dimensional analysis, ensuring that the dough fills the mold to exactly the desired level.

Instructions for using the calculator

  1. Select source form type: Round or rectangular.
  2. Enter dimensions: For a circle - diameter, for a rectangle - width and length in centimeters.
  3. Specify your form parameters: Those sizes for which you need to adapt the recipe.
  4. Add ingredients (optional): Enter the name and quantity of products from the original recipe (in grams, pieces or milliliters).
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will instantly display the coefficient and a ready list of products with new values.

Baking Tips for Changing Sizes

  • Baking time: As the diameter of the mold increases, the time in the oven usually increases, but not proportionally. Check doneness with a skewer.
  • Temperature: For wider pans, it is sometimes worth lowering the temperature by 5-10°C to prevent the edges from burning while the middle bakes.
  • Eggs: If the calculation turns out to be, for example, 2.4 eggs, use 2 large eggs (category C0) or beat 3 eggs and measure the required mass on the scale.
