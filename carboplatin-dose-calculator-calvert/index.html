Carboplatin dose calculator (AUC) using Calvert's formula

Calculating the correct dosage of chemotherapy drugs is critical to the effectiveness of treatment and minimization of toxicity. This online calculator allows you to quickly and accurately determine your individual dose of Carboplatin using the internationally recognized Calvert formula. The tool automatically calculates the patient's glomerular filtration rate (GFR), eliminating the need for the physician to perform additional manual calculations.

Calvert formula: calculation principle

Unlike most cytotoxic drugs, which are dosed based on body surface area (BSA), the dose of Carboplatin is determined based on the patient's renal function. This is due to the fact that the drug is excreted primarily by the kidneys.

The basic equation looks like this:

$$\text{Доза (мг)} = \text{Target AUC} \times (\text{GFR} + 25)$$

Where:

  • Target AUC (Area Under the Curve) - the desired area under the concentration-time curve (mg/ml/min).
  • GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate) - glomerular filtration rate (ml/min).
  • 25 — constant correction taking into account extrarenal clearance of the drug.

Calculation of GFR (Cockcroft-Gault)

Because direct measurement of GFR is labor-intensive, the standard in clinical practice for the Calvert formula is to use the Cockcroft-Gault equation to estimate creatinine clearance. Our calculator performs this calculation automatically.

Formula for men:

$$\text{GFR} = \frac{(140 — \text{Возраст}) \times \text{Вес (кг)}}{72 \times \text{Креатинин (мг/дл)}}$$

Formula for women:

$$\text{GFR} = \left( \frac{(140 — \text{Возраст}) \times \text{Вес (кг)}}{72 \times \text{Креатинин (мг/дл)}} \right) \times 0.85$$

If the baseline creatinine level is entered in µmol/L, the calculator pre-converts it using the ratio:

$$1 \text{ мг/дл} = 88.4 \text{ мкмоль/л}$$

How to use the calculator

To obtain an accurate result, it is necessary to enter current clinical data of the patient. The algorithm supports automatic conversion of units of measurement.

  1. Gender and Age: Necessary for assessing muscle mass and basal metabolism, which affect creatinine levels.
  2. Body weight: Enter the patient's current weight. For obesity, the doctor may decide to use corrected body weight, but the classic Calvert formula often uses actual weight.
  3. Serum creatinine: A key indicator for assessing renal function.
  4. Target AUC: Value determined by treatment protocol.

Limitations of the method

Despite the high accuracy, the calculation method has its limitations:

  • Low muscle mass (Cachexia): In malnourished patients, creatinine levels may be artificially low, leading to overestimation of GFR and risk of overdose. In such cases, it may be necessary to limit the maximum clearance (CAP GFR), for example, to 125 ml/min.
  • Acute renal failure: The formula is not valid if creatinine levels are unstable.
