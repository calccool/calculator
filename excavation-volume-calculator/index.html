Excavation volume calculator: Calculation of Pit and Trench with removal

Planning excavation work is the first and most expensive stage of any construction. An error in calculations here is costly: ordering extra dump trucks hits the budget, and the lack of space for dumping soil stops construction.

Our excavation calculator solves the main problem of estimators and superintendents: he calculates not only the “geometric hole” in the ground, but also the real volume of loose soil that will have to be removed.

How is the calculation made?

Many people mistakenly believe that the volume of a pit is simply the length times the width and depth ( $V = L \times W \times H$ ). However, in reality, the walls of the pit are never strictly vertical (except in cases with sheet piling). Technologically, they are carried out with slopes to avoid shedding.

Our algorithm uses the professional Simpson formula (prismatoid formula) to calculate the volume of figures with complex geometry and slopes as accurately as possible:

$$V = \frac{H}{6} (S_{bottom} + S_{top} + 4 S_{mid})$$

  • $V$ - Pit volume;
  • $H$ - Recess depth;
  • $S_{bottom}$ - Bottom area;
  • $S_{top}$ - Top area (including slopes);
  • $S_{mid}$ - Sectional area at mid-height.

Why is there always more excavated soil?

This is a key point that newbies miss. Soil in its natural occurrence (in the ground) is in a compacted state. When the excavator takes it out, the bonds between the particles are destroyed, the earth is saturated with air and increases in volume. It's called initial loosening.

To calculate the actual volume to be exported, we use the formula:

$$V_{loose} = V_{geo} \times K_{bulking}$$

  • $V_{loose}$ - Volume of loosened soil (what we load into the dump truck);
  • $V_{geo}$ - Geometric volume (what is in the ground);
  • $K_{bulking}$ — Loosening coefficient.

Tool capabilities

  1. Three types of output:
    • Rectangular pit - for house foundations, slabs, basements.
    • Trench — for strip foundations and laying communications (pipes, cables).
    • Round well - for septic tanks, cesspools and caissons.
  2. Mass calculation: Knowing the volume and type of soil, we calculate the total weight in tons. This is necessary to select the lifting capacity of the equipment.
  3. Logistics (Dump trucks): The script divides the loosened volume by the volume of your truck body (for example, KAMAZ - 10 m³, Scania - 20 m³), ​​rounding the number of trips up.

Useful tips

  • Consider slopes: The weaker the soil (sand), the flatter the walls of the pit should be, otherwise it will crumble. This significantly increases the amount of work on top.
  • Humus cutting: If you are building a house, do not take the top fertile layer (chernozem, 20-30 cm) to a landfill. Store it separately, it will be useful for landscaping.
  • Stock: When ordering cars, always add +5-10% to the estimated number of trips, since the soil in the body never lies perfectly tightly (“headed” or with voids).
