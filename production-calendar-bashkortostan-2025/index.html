Production calendar of Bashkortostan 2025 holidays, transfers and hours standards

Production calendar of Bashkortostan 2025 takes into account federal And regional non-working days, as well as official weekend transfers. It helps HR officers, accountants and managers plan schedules, vacation campaigns, payroll and working hours in a 40/36/24-hour work week.

Why is it needed?

  • Exact hours by month and quarter.
  • Vacation and shift planning no manual errors.
  • Correct charges (salary, sick leave, holidays).
  • Reduced overheads: less double-checking and editing.
  • Transparency for employees - all dates are “in sight”.

What's special about the calendar of Bashkortostan

For organizations operating in the Republic of Bashkortostan, in addition to federal holidays, regional:

  • Eid al-Adha — March 30, 2025;
  • Eid al-Adha — June 6, 2025;
  • Republic Day of Bashkortostan — October 11, 2025.

Holidays that fall on the weekend are transferred on weekdays - due to this, the number of non-working days per year increases.

Non-working holidays (Bashkortostan, 2025)

Federal

  • January 1–8 - New Year holidays (January 7 - Christmas).
  • February 23 — Defender of the Fatherland Day.
  • March 8 — International Women's Day.
  • May 1 — Spring and Labor Holiday.
  • May 9 — Victory Day.
  • June 12 — Russia Day.
  • November 4 — Day of National Unity.

Regional (Republic of Bashkortostan)

  • March 30 - Eid al-Fitr.
  • June 6 - Eid al-Fitr.
  • October 11 — Day of the Republic of Bashkortostan.

Transfers of holidays (also valid in the Republic of Belarus)

  • With Saturday 4 January - on Friday 2 May;
  • With Sunday January 5 - on Wednesday 31 December;
  • With Sunday 23 February - on Thursday 8 May;
  • With Saturday March 8 - on Friday 13 June;
  • With Saturday November 1st - on Monday November 3.

Note: marked separately on the calendar shortened days (minus 1 hour) on the eve of a number of holidays.

Standard working hours (40/36/24 hours)

Time standards are shown on the calendar page by month, quarter, half-year and year-end taking into account regional holidays. The table allows you to:

  • quickly reconcile accruals;
  • create shift schedules;
  • plan vacations taking into account actual working days.

How to use a calendar at work

  • HR: vacations, maternity leave, shift schedules, transfers.
  • Accounting and accounting: standard hours, increased rates for holidays/weekends.
  • Heads of departments: project deadlines, resource planning, coordination of overtime.
  • Employees: planning personal affairs and vacations without conflicts with the production schedule.

FAQ

Can this calendar be used by all organizations in the Republic of Belarus?

Yes, if the organization operates in Bashkortostan and has not established a different operating mode by local acts (subject to compliance with the Labor Code of the Russian Federation).

What to do if the company has a shift/continuous schedule?

Use the calendar as a basis for standard hours, and build shift schedules through internal schedules (taking into account transfers and shortened days).

Are regional holidays obligatory?

Yes, for organizations operating in the Republic of Belarus, regional holidays are counted as non-working holidays.
