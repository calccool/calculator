Calculator for calculating the power of a heater (air heater) for ventilation

Calculating the heater power is a key stage in the design of any supply ventilation system. An error at this stage leads either to “defrosting” the system and supplying icy air into the room, or to unjustified costs for electricity and equipment.

Our algorithm is based on the fundamental formula of thermodynamics for heating a moving air stream. It is based on the heat capacity of air and its density.

Basic calculation formula

To determine the required thermal power, the following equation is used:

$$P = L \times \Delta T \times C \times \rho / 3600$$

Where:

  • P — Heater power (kW).
  • L — Air flow (m³/h).
  • ΔT — Temperature difference (T_output — T_input) in °C.
  • C — Specific heat capacity of air (taken as 1.006 kJ/kg °C).
  • ρ — Air density (taken as 1.2 kg/m³).

In engineering practice, a summary coefficient is often used to simplify 0.336. So the working formula looks like this:

$$P (Вт) = L \times \Delta T \times 0.336$$

How to use the calculator

To obtain correct data, you must accurately fill in the following fields:

  1. Air consumption (L): It is taken from the passport data of your fan or calculated based on the air exchange rate of the room.
  2. Inlet temperature: This is the estimated winter temperature for your region (the coldest five-day period). For example, for Moscow it is -25°C or -28°C.
  3. Outlet temperature: Comfortable temperature that you want to supply to the room (usually +18...+22°C).
  4. Power reserve: It is recommended to reserve 10-15% in case of extreme frosts, heat loss in air ducts and voltage drops in the network.

Features of the selection of electric heaters

When choosing an electric heater (TEH), it is critical to consider the load on the electrical network. The calculator automatically calculates the current (Amperes) for two cases:

  • Single-phase network (220V): Suitable for household heaters with power up to 5-7 kW. Above this power, the load on the wiring becomes critical.
  • Three-phase network (380V): Used for industrial and semi-industrial installations of any capacity. The load is distributed evenly across three phases, which allows the use of cables with a smaller cross-section.

Water heaters

If the calculated power exceeds the capabilities of your electrical network (for example, more than 15 kW is required, and the input circuit breaker is only 25A), it is advisable to use a water heater. It is connected to the heating system of the cottage or central heating system.

Calculation result in kcal/h (kilocalories) will help you select a heat exchanger in manufacturers’ catalogs, since the power of water equipment is often indicated in these units or requires recalculation of coolant flow.
