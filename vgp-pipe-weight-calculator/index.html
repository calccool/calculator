Water and gas pipe (WGP) weight calculator according to GOST 3262-75

Water-gas pipes (WGP) are the basis of engineering communications. They are used for installation of heating systems, water supply and gas pipelines. When purchasing and logistics of rolled metal, the main problem arises: factories ship pipe in tons, but in design estimates and during installation it is measured in linear meters.

Formula for calculating the weight of a steel pipe

If you don’t have a calculator at hand, the theoretical weight of one linear meter of steel round pipe is calculated using the standard metallurgical formula (with a steel density of 7850 kg/m³):

$$M = 0.02466 \cdot s \cdot (D_n — s)$$

Where:

  • $M$ - weight of 1 linear meter of pipe, kg;
  • $D_n$ — pipe outer diameter, mm;
  • $s$ — wall thickness, mm;
  • $0.02466$ is a coefficient that takes into account the density of carbon steel and the conversion of units of measurement.

Accounting for galvanized coating

VGP pipes are available in two versions: black (without coating) and galvanized. The zinc layer, which protects the metal from corrosion, adds additional weight to the product. According to GOST 3262-75, galvanized pipes are exactly 3% heavier than non-galvanized pipes.

The mass formula for a galvanized pipe looks like this:

$$M_{zn} = M \cdot 1.03$$

Classification of VGP pipes by wall thickness

GOST 3262-75 divides water and gas pipes into three series depending on wall thickness. The choice of series directly affects weight, throughput and ability to withstand operating pressure:

  1. Lungs: Used for low pressure gas pipelines and systems with minimal load. They are characterized by the smallest weight and wall thickness.
  2. Ordinary: Golden mean. The most popular type for indoor plumbing and heating risers.
  3. Reinforced: They have maximum wall thickness. They are used in pipelines with high water or gas pressure, as well as where increased corrosion resistance and reliability of the weld are required.
