Figure Grace Calculator: Calculation of Proportions and Body Type

Body harmony in numbers: how to evaluate your figure?

Beauty is subjective, but anthropometry - the study of the proportions of the human body - relies on precise numbers. Our figure grace calculator allows you not just to find out dry measurements, but to evaluate how harmoniously developed your body is relative to medical and aesthetic standards.

The tool conducts a comprehensive analysis in three key areas: overall grace index, body type (waist-to-hip ratio) and leg symmetry.

1. Grace Index: Formula

The calculation is based on the classic formula for assessing body constitution, which takes into account the ratio of the chest, waist and lower leg volume. This allows you to determine your somatotype: from fragile (asthenic) to strong (hypersthenic).

The calculation formula is as follows:

$$I = C — (W + S)$$

Where:

  • $I$ — Grace index;
  • $C$ — Chest circumference (Chest);
  • $W$ — Waist circumference (Waist);
  • $S$ - Shin circumference.

Interpretation of results

Note: For men, the norms are shifted towards lower values ​​(usually less than 1 is considered normal due to an anatomically wider waist and smaller hips compared to women).

2. Body type (WHR)

In addition to grace, the calculator calculates WHR index (Waist-to-Hip Ratio) - waist to hip ratio. This is a key marker not only of aesthetics, but also of health.

$$WHR = \frac{\text{Обхват талии}}{\text{Обхват бедер}}$$

  • Hourglass (X-type): Considered the standard of femininity. The waist is significantly narrower than the hips ( $WHR \approx 0.7$ ).
  • "Pear" (A-type): Hips wider than shoulders and chest. Fat deposits accumulate in the lower part of the body.
  • "Apple" (O-type): The waist is slightly defined or wider than the hips ( $WHR > 0.85$ in women). This may indicate the presence of visceral fat.

3. Perfect legs

Many people strive for perfect legs, but what does this mean mathematically? The canonical ratio is considered to be in which the thigh volume (in the upper part) is approximately 1.5 - 1.6 times more volume of the lower leg.

Our algorithm compares your measurements and tells you which part of your leg is more developed or if they are in perfect balance.

How to take measurements correctly?

To get an accurate result, follow the instructions:

  1. Breast: Measure with a full inhalation at the most protruding points of the chest and shoulder blades.
  2. Waist: The tape should pass through the narrowest part of the body (usually just above the navel), without pulling in the stomach.
  3. Hips (buttocks): At the most prominent points of the buttocks.
  4. Thigh (leg): Measured at the widest part of the leg, just below the gluteal fold.
  5. Shin: In the widest part of the calf muscle.
