Basic human life cycles

The theory of biorhythms identifies three basic rhythms, which have different periodicities and affect specific areas of life:

  • Physical cycle (23 days): responsible for energy, endurance, coordination of movements and resistance to disease.
  • Emotional cycle (28 days): regulates mood, sensitivity, creativity and psychological stability.
  • Intellectual cycle (33 days): affects memory, logical thinking, speed of information processing and learning ability.
  • Intuitive cycle (38 days): (additional) helps to assess the level of subconscious perception and “sixth sense”.

Mathematical calculation model

Our calculator is based on the trigonometric sine function. Each cycle begins at the moment of birth from the zero point, rises (positive phase), and then falls (negative phase).

To calculate the biorhythm percentage, the following formula is used:

$$B = \sin\left(\frac{2\pi \cdot t}{P}\right) \cdot 100\%$$

Where:

  • $B$ - current biorhythm value as a percentage;
  • $t$ - number of days lived from the moment of birth to the forecast date;
  • $P$ - period of a specific cycle (23, 28, 33 or 38 days);
  • $\pi$ is a mathematical constant (Pi number).
