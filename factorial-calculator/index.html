What is factorial?

Factorial of a natural number n (denoted as n! ) is the product of all natural numbers from 1 to n inclusive.

Basic formulas

Classic definition:

$$n! = 1 \times 2 \times 3 \times \dots \times (n-1) \times n$$

Recurrence formula (often used in programming):

$$n! = n \times (n-1)!$$

Important: By definition, it is assumed that the factorial of zero is equal to one:

$$0! = 1$$

Advanced Mathematics

For advanced math users, our tool provides data based on complex approximations.

Stirling formula

When it is necessary to estimate the factorial of a very large number without calculating it exactly, use the approximate formula of James Stirling. It gives a small relative error at large $n$ :

$$n! \approx \sqrt{2\pi n} \left( \frac{n}{e} \right)^n$$

Where:

  • $\pi \approx 3.14159…$
  • $e \approx 2.71828…$ (Euler number)

Zeroes at the end of the factorial

In programming olympiad problems, you often need to find out how many zeros the number $n!$ ends with. This is determined by the number of factors of 5 in the expansion of the number. We use Legendre's formula:

$$Zeros = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \left\lfloor \frac{n}{5^k} \right\rfloor$$

Where is factorial used?

  1. Permutations: In how many ways can 10 books be arranged on a shelf? Answer: $10! = 3,628,800$ .
  2. Combinations and Placements: To calculate odds in lotteries, binomial odds formulas, including factorials, are used.
  3. Taylor series: In higher mathematics, factorials are in the denominators when expanding functions (sine, cosine, exponent) into series.
