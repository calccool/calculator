Calculator of the beginning and end of twilight: civil, navigational and astronomical

Twilight is a unique period of time when the Sun is already below the horizon, but its rays, scattered in the upper atmosphere, still provide a certain level of illumination. Our calculator allows you to instantly determine the boundaries of these periods based on your geographic coordinates and the selected date.

Unlike simple tools, this algorithm takes into account the equation of time and the declination of the Sun, providing the accuracy needed for both everyday planning and the professional needs of photographers, sailors and astronomers.

Mathematical model and calculation formulas

The calculation is based on determining the hour angle of the Sun for a specific height above the horizon. The basic formula used in the calculator code to calculate the hour angle ( $H$ ) is as follows:

$$H = \arccos\left(\frac{\sin(h) — \sin(\phi) \cdot \sin(\delta)}{\cos(\phi) \cdot \cos(\delta)}\right)$$

Where:

  • $h$ — height of the Sun (for example, $-6^\circ$ for civil twilight);
  • $\phi$ — observation point latitude;
  • $\delta$ - declination of the Sun (depending on the day of the year).

To find the solar declination ( $\delta$ ) the following approximation is used:

$$\delta = -23.44^\circ \cdot \cos\left(\frac{360^\circ}{365} \cdot (N + 10)\right)$$

here $N$ is the serial number of the day of the year.

The calculator correctly handles scenarios of the Far North and Antarctica. If in the selected location there is a Polar day or Polar night, the system automatically adapts the interface, notifying the user that the Sun does not set below the horizon or rise above it.

Who is this calculation useful for?

  • For photographers and videographers: To find the ideal Golden Hour and prepare for night shooting.
  • For sailors and tourists: Navigational twilight is critical to positioning and navigating safely at dusk.
  • For amateur astronomers: The end of astronomical twilight marks the beginning of “true night,” when atmospheric light ceases to interfere with the observation of deep space objects.
  • Housing and communal services specialists: For setting up automatic street lighting control systems.
