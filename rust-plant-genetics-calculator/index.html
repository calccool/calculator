Mechanics of crossing: how genes work

In Rust, each plant has a chain of 6 genetic slots. When a seedling is planted next to other mature plants (in the “Crossbreeding” stage), it begins to absorb their genetic code. The winning gene in each slot is determined based on mathematical weight.

Mathematical model for calculating weights

The game algorithm assigns different “strengths” to green and red genes. To predict which gene will end up in the slot after a cross, the following formula is used:

$$W_{total} = \sum (V_{gene})$$

Where the $V$ weight value for each type is:

  • Green genes (G, Y, H): $V = 0.6$
  • Red genes (W, X): $V = 1.0$

The winner is the gene whose total mass in a particular slot from all neighboring plants is higher. If the weights are equal, the result is determined randomly (50/50), which is displayed in our calculator as a risk warning.

Tips for the perfect farm

  • Get rid of red genes: The $W$ and $X$ genes have a higher weight ( $1.0$ ), so they are more difficult to overlap. You may need two green genes ( $0.6 + 0.6 = 1.2$ ) to defeat one red one.
  • Use dummies: Sometimes it is beneficial to use a clone with mediocre genes just for the sake of one necessary slot, in order to outweigh the total mass.
  • Cross pollination stage: Remember that crossing occurs only when the central plant enters the "Crossbreeding" stage and the surrounding clones are at least in the "Sapling" stage.
