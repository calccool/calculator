Fabric consumption calculator for bed linen: accurate calculation taking into account shrinkage

Sewing bed linen yourself is not only saving money, but also the opportunity to create a set for individual sizes of a mattress or blanket. However, the main difficulty lies in the correct calculation of footage. An error of 10–15 centimeters can lead to the fact that after the first wash the sheet will become small and the duvet cover will be skewed.

Our calculator uses a professional thread cutting algorithm that takes into account three critical factors:

  1. Roll width: The 150 cm, 220 cm and 240 cm standards require different arrangement of parts.
  2. Technological allowances: For hemming edges and connecting seams.
  3. Shrinkage rate: The property of natural fabrics (cotton, linen, satin) to decrease in size after wet-heat treatment.

Mathematical calculation model

To obtain a perfectly accurate result, the calculator uses a system of equations that takes into account all entered parameters.

Calculation of fabric for sheets

The sheet is calculated as a single sheet. If the width of the product, including allowances, exceeds the width of the roll, the system will issue a warning about the need for a stitch seam.

$$L_{sheet} = (L_{ready} + S) \times (1 + \frac{K_{shrink}}{100})$$

Where:

  • $L_{ready}$ - desired sheet length;
  • $S$ - seam allowances (standard 5 cm);
  • $K_{shrink}$ - shrinkage percentage.

Duvet cover calculation

A duvet cover is most often made from a single piece of fabric folded in half. The formula takes into account two product lengths:

$$L_{duvet} = (L_{blanket} \times 2 + S) \times (1 + \frac{K_{shrink}}{100})$$

Optimizing pillowcases

This is the most difficult block. The program checks how many pillowcases can be “fitted” into the width of your roll ( $W_{roll}$ ).

  1. The width of the pillowcase cut is calculated: $W_{cut} = (W_{pill} + S) \times K_{shrink\_mult}$ .
  2. The number of products in one row is determined: $N = \lfloor W_{roll} / W_{cut} \rfloor$ .
  3. The final footage depends on the number of rows required to sew the required number of pillowcases.

Instructions for using the calculator

To get the correct result, follow these guidelines for filling out the fields:

  • Roll width: Most often, fabric with a width of 220 cm is purchased for bed linen. This allows you to sew without unnecessary seams in the middle of the product.
  • Bed sheet sizes: Do not enter a mattress size. To the width of the mattress you need to add the height of the mattress on both sides and another 10–15 cm for tucking under the mattress.
  • Duvet cover dimensions: Please indicate the net size of your quilt. The calculator itself will add 5 cm to the fit and seams.
  • Pillowcase scent: The algorithm already includes a standard “valve” (smell) of 25 cm, which holds the pillow inside.

Standard sizes for quick checks

If you are not sure about the sizes, refer to state standards (GOST) and Euro standards:

  • 1.5 bedroom: Bed sheet 150x215, duvet cover 145x210.
  • 2 bedroom: Bed sheet 180x220, duvet cover 175x210.
  • Euro: Bed sheet 240x260, duvet cover 200x220.
  • Family (Duet): 1 sheet, 2 duvet covers 145x210.

Useful tips when buying fabric

  • Buy with stock: Even the most accurate calculator will not protect you from a crooked cut of fabric by a store clerk. We recommend rounding the total up to the nearest 10 cm.
  • Decatation: Before cutting, be sure to iron the fabric with steam or soak it in warm water and dry it. This is a forced shrink that ensures that the finished product will not shrink after the first wash.
  • Pattern direction: If the fabric has a large directional print, the cost may increase, since the parts cannot be “flipped” to save space on the canvas.
