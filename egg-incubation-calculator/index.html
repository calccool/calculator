Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm is based on the addition of time intervals, where the astronomical day ( $24$ hours) is taken as a unit of time. The formula for determining the completion date of incubation is as follows:

$$D_{end} = D_{start} + T_{total}$$

Where:

  • $D_{end}$ —date of mass hatching of poultry;
  • $D_{start}$ - date and time of actual laying of eggs into the chamber;
  • $T_{total}$ is the incubation period constant for a specific species.

To determine the critical stopping point of the turning mechanism (to avoid injury to the formed embryo), the formula is used:

$$D_{stop} = D_{start} + (T_{total} — 3)$$

Main phases of the incubation process

To achieve a high percentage of hatchability, it is necessary to strictly adhere to the schedule of technological operations that our calculator generates:

  • Period of active development: At this time, the formation of all body systems occurs. The main requirement is a stable temperature and regular turning of eggs (up to 6-12 times a day).
  • Stopping a coup: 2-3 days before hatching, the eggs are transferred to a horizontal position. This is necessary so that the chick can take the correct position for pecking.
  • Beginning of biting: The first cracks appear on the shell. At this moment, the humidity in the incubator should be maximum to soften the shell membranes.

Recommended microclimate modes

In addition to the time frame, maintaining temperature conditions is critical. Our calculator takes into account phase changes in the necessary conditions:

Temperature and humidity

  • For land birds (chickens, turkeys): In the first phase, the temperature $37.8°C$ is maintained at the humidity $50-55\%$ . During the hatching period, the temperature drops to $37.3°C$ and the humidity rises to $70\%$ .
  • For waterfowl (ducks, geese): They require higher humidity (up to $60\%$ at the beginning and up to $75-80\%$ at the end) and mandatory cooling/spraying of eggs from the second half of the period.
