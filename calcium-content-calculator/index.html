Calculator (Ca) and oxide (CaO) content calculator

Determination of calcium content is one of the key tasks in analytical chemistry, construction inspection (analysis of cement and limestone), as well as in medicine and water quality control. Our calculator allows you to automate routine calculations using the two most common methods of titrimetric analysis: complexometry and permanganatometry.

Principles of calculation

The result of the analysis depends on the chosen chemical method, since the stoichiometry of the reactions differs. The calculator uses the following basic constants and algorithms.

1. Complexometry method (EDTA / Trilon B)

This is the most modern and accurate method. It is based on the formation of a strong complex compound of calcium ions with ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid (EDTA) in an alkaline environment (usually at pH 12-13).

Reaction equation in simplified form:

$$Ca^{2+} + H_2Y^{2-} \rightarrow CaY^{2-} + 2H^+$$

Since the reaction occurs in a 1:1 ratio, the formula for calculating the mass of calcium looks like this:

$$m(Ca) = \frac{V \cdot M \cdot A_r(Ca)}{1000}$$

Where:

  • $V$ —volume of EDTA solution used for titration (ml);
  • $M$ —molarity of EDTA solution (mol/l);
  • $A_r(Ca)$ - atomic mass of calcium (40.078 g/mol).

2. Permanganatometry method

The classical method is based on the precipitation of calcium in the form of oxalate ( $CaC_2O_4$ ), followed by dissolving the precipitate in sulfuric acid and titrating the liberated oxalic acid with a solution of potassium permanganate ( $KMnO_4$ ).

The law of equivalents is used here. The equivalent mass of calcium is equal to half its atomic mass (since the valence is 2):

$$E(Ca) = \frac{40.078}{2} \approx 20.04 \text{ г/экв}$$

Calculation formula:

$$m(Ca) = \frac{V \cdot N \cdot E(Ca)}{1000}$$

Where $N$ is the normality of the $KMnO_4$ solution.

Conversion of Calcium (Ca) to Calcium Oxide (CaO)

In geology and the building materials industry (cement, concrete, gypsum), it is customary to express the calcium content in the form of its oxide ( $CaO$ ).

The conversion factor is calculated through the ratio of molar masses:

$$K = \frac{M(CaO)}{M(Ca)} = \frac{40.08 + 16.00}{40.08} \approx 1.3992$$

Thus, to obtain the result for the oxide:

$$CaO (\%) = Ca (\%) \cdot 1.3992$$

Analysis algorithm

To obtain accurate data to enter into the calculator, you must follow the laboratory procedure:

  1. Sample preparation. An accurate portion of the analyte (usually 0.1–0.5 g) is dissolved in acid and transferred to a volumetric flask.
  2. Aliquot. For titration, take part of the solution (an aliquot) or titrate the entire volume if the sample was small.
  3. Titration.
    • For EDTA: Add an indicator (murexide or eriochrome black T) and a buffer solution. Titrate until the color changes from cherry to blue-violet.
    • For $KMnO_4$ : Precipitate calcium with ammonium oxalate, filter, wash the precipitate, dissolve it in $H_2SO_4$ and titrate the hot solution with permanganate until a faint pink color appears.
  4. Calculation. Enter the resulting volume ( $V$ ) and titrant concentration into the form above for instant results.

Frequent errors in calculations

  • Confusion with units. Make sure the concentration is correct: for EDTA it is usually Molarity (M), for permanganate it is Normality (N).
  • Accounting for dilution. If you took an aliquot (for example, 20 ml from a 200 ml flask), the calculator result must be multiplied by the dilution factor (in this example, 10).
  • Ignoring blank samples. For particularly accurate analyses, from the volume used for titrating the sample, it is necessary to subtract the volume used for titration of the blank sample (distilled water with reagents).
