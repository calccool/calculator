Online square triangular number calculator

In number theory, there is a unique set of numbers that have a dual nature. A square triangular number is a natural number that can be represented geometrically as both a square (square number) and a triangle (triangular number).

In simple terms, if you have $N$ pebbles, you can form them into a perfect square or equilateral triangle without leaving any residue.

Mathematical definition

The number $N$ is a square triangular if there are integers $s$ and $n$ such that the equality holds:

$$N = s^2 = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$$

Where:

  • $N$ is the required number.
  • $s$ - side of the square (square root).
  • $n$ - side of the triangle (triangular root).

Calculation formula

The problem of finding such numbers comes down to solving Pell's Diophantine equation: $x^2 — 8y^2 = 1$ . The great mathematician Leonhard Euler proved the existence of formulas that allow you to find these numbers by their serial number $k$ .

To calculate the $k$ th square triangular number ( $N_k$ ), the following recurrence relations are used:

  1. Through previous values: $$N_k = 34 \cdot N_{k-1} — N_{k-2} + 2$$ (with $N_0 = 0, N_1 = 1$ )
  2. Direct formula (via Binet's formula): $$N_k = \left( \frac{(3 + 2\sqrt{2})^k — (3 — 2\sqrt{2})^k}{4\sqrt{2}} \right)^2$$

Features and Properties

The study of these numbers reveals interesting patterns in number theory:

  • Rarity: There are an infinite number of such numbers, but they are extremely rare. There are only three of them in the first million natural numbers.
  • Relation to the root of 2: The ratio of the side of the triangle to the side of the square ( $n/s$ ) as $k$ grows tends to $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4142$ .
  • Alternation: The square roots of these numbers ( $s_k$ ) also form a sequence associated with solutions to Pell's equation.
