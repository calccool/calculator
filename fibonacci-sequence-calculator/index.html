Fibonacci Sequence Calculator: Number Generator and Golden Ratio Finder

The Fibonacci sequence is perhaps the most famous number series in mathematics, describing the architecture of nature itself: from the spirals of shells and the arrangement of sunflower seeds to the galactic arms.

Our engineering calculator allows you not only to get a list of numbers, but also to deeply explore the properties of this series. You can find a specific term in a sequence (even a very large one, thanks to BigInt algorithms), calculate the sum of a series, or check whether an arbitrary number is a “Fibonacci number.”

Mathematical model

The series is based on a simple recurrence relation: each next number is equal to the sum of the previous two.

Recursive formula

The classic definition looks like this:

$$F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}$$

Under initial conditions:

$$F_0 = 0, \quad F_1 = 1$$

Binet's formula

To calculate the $n$ th term without going through all the previous ones, our calculator uses Binet’s formula based on irrational numbers:

$$F_n = \frac{\varphi^n — (1-\varphi)^n}{\sqrt{5}}$$

Where $\varphi$ (phi) is the famous “Golden Ratio”.

Golden ratio and convergence

An amazing property of the Fibonacci series is that the ratio of two adjacent numbers ( $F_n / F_{n-1}$ ) approaches the golden ratio more and more accurately with each step.

Practical Application

Why do you need to calculate these numbers?

  1. Programming: Optimization of search algorithms, Fibonacci heaps, performance testing of recursive functions.
  2. Trading (Forex, Crypto): Fibonacci retracement levels are used to predict support and resistance on price charts.
  3. Scrum and Agile: Planning Poker uses numbers in the series (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13) to estimate the difficulty of tasks to avoid false precision.
  4. Botany and Biology: Modeling phyllotaxis (the arrangement of leaves on the stem) and population growth.
