Basic formulas and notations

A rectangle is a quadrilateral in which all the angles are right ( $90^\circ$ ) and the opposite sides are parallel and equal in pairs. The following basic equations are used to carry out calculations:

1. Area of ​​a rectangle ( $S$ )

Area is the space bounded by the sides of a figure. It is equal to the product of length and width.

$$S = a \cdot b$$

2. Perimeter of a rectangle ( $P$ )

The perimeter is the total length of the shape's border (the sum of the lengths of all sides).

$$P = 2 \cdot (a + b)$$

3. Rectangle diagonal ( $d$ )

A diagonal is a line segment connecting two opposite vertices. It divides a rectangle into two equal right triangles, so it is calculated using the Pythagorean theorem.

$$d = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$$

Solving inverse problems

Often in construction or geodesy it is not the length of the sides that is known, but the final area (for example, 50 m² of tiles were purchased) or the perimeter (the length of the fence). Our calculator can find unknown sides using algebraic transformations of basic formulas.

Finding side via Area ( $S$ ) and known side ( $a$ )

If you know the total area and one of the sides, the other side can be found by dividing:

$$b = \frac{S}{a}$$

Finding side via Perimeter ( $P$ ) and known side ( $a$ )

If the total perimeter of the fence and the length of one wall are known, the second wall is found as follows:

$$b = \frac{P}{2} — a$$

Practical application: cost calculation

A unique feature of our tool is the “Estimate” block. When renovating, it is important not only to know the square footage of the floor or ceiling, but also to understand the budget.

Calculation algorithm:

  1. Select Two Sides mode.
  2. Enter the length and width of the room.
  3. In the field “Price per unit. area" indicate the cost of 1 square meter of material (for example, parquet or suspended ceiling).
  4. The calculator will automatically give the total amount using the formula:

$$Cost = S \cdot Price$$

Important: When purchasing building materials (wallpaper, tiles, laminate), always add 10-15% stock to the estimated area for trimming and scrap.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

In what units should I enter data?

The calculator works with pure numbers. If you enter the sides in meters, the area will be in square meters ( $m^2$ ). If in centimeters - in square centimeters ( $cm^2$ ). The main thing is to make sure that the length and width are in the same units.

How to find the diagonal if only the area is known?

It is impossible to find the diagonal knowing only area, since a rectangle can have an infinite number of proportions (for example, $2 \times 50$ or $10 \times 10$ ). You need to know at least one side.

Does the square calculator work?

Yes, a square is a special case of a rectangle, where $a = b$ . You can use this tool or go to our specialized Square calculator.
