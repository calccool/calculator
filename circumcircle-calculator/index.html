Triangle Circumference Calculator (Center and Radius)

Calculator for inscribed triangle

In geometry circumcircle is a unique circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle. Its center is called circumcenter (circumcenter), and it is equidistant from all vertices.

This online tool allows you to instantly find the parameters of such a circle, knowing only the coordinates of the triangle vertices: $A(x_1, y_1)$ , $B(x_2, y_2)$ and $C(x_3, y_3)$ .

What does the calculator calculate?

  1. $(U_x, U_y)$ Center Coordinates: The point where the perpendicular bisectors of the sides of a triangle intersect.
  2. Radius $(R)$ : The distance from the center to any of the vertices.
  3. Circle Equation: Canonical formula of the form $(x — h)^2 + (y — k)^2 = R^2$ .
  4. Areas: Additionally, the area of ​​the triangle itself and the area of ​​the circle circumscribed around it are calculated.

Mathematical method

The calculation is based on solving a system of equations of perpendicular bisectors. However, for calculations on a computer, the formula through the determinant $D$ is more often used:

$$D = 2 \cdot [x_1(y_2 — y_3) + x_2(y_3 — y_1) + x_3(y_1 — y_2)]$$

If $D = 0$ , this means that the points lie on the same line (collinear), and it is impossible to construct a triangle (and therefore a circle).

Center coordinate formulas

$$U_x = \frac{1}{D} \cdot [(x_1^2 + y_1^2)(y_2 — y_3) + (x_2^2 + y_2^2)(y_3 — y_1) + (x_3^2 + y_3^2)(y_1 — y_2)]$$

$$U_y = \frac{1}{D} \cdot [(x_1^2 + y_1^2)(x_3 — x_2) + (x_2^2 + y_2^2)(x_1 — x_3) + (x_3^2 + y_3^2)(x_2 — x_1)]$$

Center position

Interesting fact: the position of the center of the circumcircle depends on the type of triangle:

  • Acute angled: The center lies inside the triangle.
  • Rectangular: The center lies exactly in the middle of the hypotenuse.
  • Obtuse: The center lies outside the triangle.

Our calculator can handle any of these cases.
