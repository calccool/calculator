Ping calculator: calculating latency by physical distance to the server

Ping (latency) is a key indicator of the quality of an Internet connection, especially for online gamers, traders and system administrators. Unlike standard speed tests, this calculator allows you to predict the theoretical latency limit before you even connect to the server.

The basis of the calculation is a fundamental physical limitation - the speed of signal propagation in the medium. Even if you have a gigabit Internet, you will not be able to get a ping below a certain value if the server is on another continent. Our tool helps you understand whether your current latency is an anomaly or due to the laws of physics.

How latency is calculated: physics and algorithms

The calculator's mathematical model is based on the round trip time (RTT) of the signal. It is important to understand that the speed of light in a vacuum $c \approx 300,000$ km/s is unattainable for cable networks. In quartz glass optical fibers, the signal propagation speed is approximately 30% lower.

Theoretical ping formula

The following formula is used to calculate the delay:

$$T_{total} = T_{phys} + T_{equip} + T_{last\_mile}$$

Where:

  • $T_{phys}$ - delay of signal passage along the highway;
  • $T_{equip}$ - time for packet processing by routers;
  • $T_{last\_mile}$ - the base latency of your connection type.

The calculation of the physical part of $T_{phys}$ looks like this:

$$T_{phys} = \frac{D \cdot 2 \cdot K_{route}}{V_{fiber}}$$

In this formula:

  • $D$ — distance to the server in kilometers;
  • $2$ - coefficient for taking into account the round trip path;
  • $K_{route}$ — route curvature coefficient (usually from 1.3 to 1.8);
  • $V_{fiber}$ is the speed of light in the cable, taken as $200$ km/ms.

Why is the real ping in games always higher?

When using the calculator, you get a “clean” forecast, but in reality the values ​​may differ. Main reasons for discrepancies:

  1. Complex routing: Packets rarely travel in a straight line. The $K_{route}$ coefficient takes into account that the cable can loop between cities and countries.
  2. Loading nodes (Hops): Each intermediate router spends time parsing the packet header and choosing a direction.
  3. Game engine: Often the "game ping" you see in the CS2, Dota 2 or Valorant interface includes the frame processing time of the server itself.

Assessing connection quality for gaming

To understand how comfortable the gameplay will be based on the results obtained, use the following scale:

  • 0 – 40 ms: Perfect. Suitable for professional eSports and jet shooters.
  • 40 – 90 ms: Fine. A comfortable level for most players, the delay is almost not felt.
  • 90 – 160 ms: Acceptable. You can play MMOs or strategy games, but in fast games, "teleports" are possible.
  • Over 160 ms: Badly. High risk of desynchronization and discomfort.
