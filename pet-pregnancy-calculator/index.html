Pregnancy calculator for cats and dogs: online breeding calendar and birth dates

Waiting for offspring from a four-legged friend is a crucial period that requires the owner to have precise knowledge of physiological processes. Our online calculator helps you instantly determine your expected date of birth (EDD) and create a detailed schedule of examinations.

The tool takes into account species differences between felines and canines, since the average gestation period differs between them.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The calculator is based on the standard method of adding the average gestational age to the date of the first successful mating.

The following formula is used for calculation:

$$PDR = D_{v} + G_{avg}$$

Where:

  • $PDR$ - estimated due date.
  • $D_{v}$ - date of first mating.
  • $G_{avg}$ - average duration of pregnancy (in days).

Pregnancy in dogs: timing and stages

In dogs, the average gestation period for puppies is 63 days, however, the normal range is considered to be from 58 to 68 days. Such a wide range is explained by the individual characteristics of the bitch, her weight and the number of fruits in the litter.

Key indicators for dogs

  • Minimum term: 58 days (with a large litter, labor may begin earlier).
  • Maximum period: 68 days (common in small breeds or with 1-2 puppies).
  • Optimal time for ultrasound: 25th day after mating.
  • X-ray diagnostics: after the 45th day (when the fruit skeletons begin to mineralize).

Pregnancy in cats: what you need to know

Cats have a more stable cycle. The average period is 65 days, with a physiological norm of 63 to 67 days. If birth occurs before the 60th day, the kittens are often not viable, and a delay of more than 70 days requires immediate veterinary intervention.

Instructions for using the calculator

To get an accurate forecast, follow three simple steps:

  1. Select pet type: Switch the tab to "Cat" or "Dog".
  2. Please enter the date: Enter the day of the first contact (mating) in the appropriate field.
  3. Examine the result: the system will instantly build a visual dashboard with the percentage of pregnancy completion and a list of important dates.

Why is it important to know the exact date?

  • Pathology control: you will be able to notice post-maturity or premature birth in time.
  • Scheduling doctor visits: The schedule will tell you when it’s time to sign up for a follow-up ultrasound.
  • Preparing the “nest”: a week before the PDR, you need to prepare a quiet place and all the necessary medications.

Using our calculator, you get not just a dry date, but a full-fledged roadmap for the development of future babies. This helps reduce stress levels for both owner and pet, ensuring the safe birth of the offspring.
