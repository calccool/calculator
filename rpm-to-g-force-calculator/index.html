What is the difference between RPM and RCF

Understanding the differences between these terms is critical to reproducible experiments:

  • RPM (Revolutions Per Minute) is the rotor speed. It shows how many full circles the centrifuge nest makes in one minute. However, RPM does not take into account the physical force exerted on the sample.
  • RCF (Relative Centrifugal Force) is the relative centrifugal acceleration, measured in units g (acceleration of gravity). It is this force that is responsible for the deposition of particles.

The main problem is that at the same speed (for example, 3000 RPM) on different centrifuges, the force g will be different. This is due to the difference in rotor radius (r). The longer the rotor arm, the higher the load on the test tube at the same rotation speed.

Formulas for calculating centrifugal force

For independent calculations, physical constants relating angular velocity and radius are used.

G-force calculation via RPM

To find out the acceleration, knowing the revolutions and radius in centimeters, use the formula:

$$RCF = 1.118 \cdot 10^{-5} \cdot r \cdot RPM^2$$

RPM calculation via G-force

If the protocol specifies a value in g, and you need to configure the device:

$$RPM = \sqrt{\frac{RCF}{1.118 \cdot 10^{-5} \cdot r}}$$

Where:

  • r — rotor radius in centimeters (distance from the center of the shaft to the bottom of the test tube in a horizontal position).
  • 1.118 \cdot 10^{-5} — coefficient that takes into account the conversion of units of measurement.

How to correctly measure the rotor radius

In order for the calculation to be as accurate as possible, it is necessary to correctly determine the parameter r:

  1. Find the center of rotation (rotor axis).
  2. Measure the distance to the outermost point of the nest (bottom of the test tube). This value is called Rmax.
  3. Exactly Rmax used to calculate the maximum load acting on the sediment.

Always check the maximum RCF values ​​that your tubes are rated for. Exceeding permissible force g may cause the plastic to break down and damage the centrifuge. If you use adapters, be sure to recalculate the radius as they shorten the distance from the center of rotation to the sample.
