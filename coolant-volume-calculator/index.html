Coolant and antifreeze volume calculator for heating system

Correct calculation of coolant volume is a critical stage in the design or maintenance of a private home. An error in the calculations can lead to ineffective pump operation, insufficient heating of the premises, or incorrect selection of the expansion tank.

Our calculator supports two calculation methods:

  1. According to boiler power: Express method based on averaged statistical data.
  2. By system elements: Accurate engineering calculation summing up the capacity of radiators, pipes and boiler heat exchanger.

Basic calculation formulas

To calculate the volume of pipes in detail, the geometric formula of a cylinder is used. To get the result in liters, we convert millimeters to meters and calculate the internal cross-section:

$$V_{pipe} = \frac{\pi \cdot d^2 \cdot L}{4000}$$

Where:

  • $V_{pipe}$ - volume of coolant in pipes (l);
  • $d$ - internal diameter of the pipe (mm);
  • $L$ - total length of the highway (m).

The total system volume ( $V_{total}$ ) is calculated taking into account the safety factor for the expansion tank (usually 10%):

$$V_{total} = (V_{rad} + V_{pipe} + V_{boiler}) \cdot 1.1$$

Why dilute antifreeze for heating?

Using pure ethylene glycol concentrate (freezing point -65°C ) is not recommended due to its high viscosity. This puts excess load on the circulation pump. It is considered optimal to dilute the composition with distilled water until the crystallization temperature is reached. -20°C or -30°C.

Advantages of choosing the right concentration:

  • Viscosity reduction: Improves fluidity and heat transfer.
  • Corrosion protection: Impurities in regular water can neutralize antifreeze additives, so it is important to use distillate.
  • Saving: Proper dilution can significantly reduce the cost of purchasing expensive concentrate.

Recommendations for filling the system

To ensure that the heating system works for a long time and without failures, adhere to the following rules:

  • Expansion tank accounting: Always add 10% to the resulting calculated volume, since part of the liquid will be in the expansion tank.
  • Refill reserve: Recommended to buy at 5-10% more coolant than the calculation showed, to compensate for possible leaks when the system is de-aerated.
  • Mixing: Never mix different brands or formulations of antifreeze (for example, ethylene glycol and propylene glycol) without first checking for compatibility, as this may cause sedimentation.
