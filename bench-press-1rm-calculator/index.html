Bench Press 1RM Calculator: Accurate calculation of maximum weight for strength

1RM (One Rep Max), or maximum weight for one repetition, is a key indicator of strength training in the bench press. It's not just a number; it is the basis for creating an effective training program that guarantees progress and minimizes the risk of injury.

Trying to test your 1RM can actually be dangerous and requires perfect technique. Luckily, there are proven formulas that can estimate your maximum lift based on lower-weight, higher-rep work sets. Our calculator uses the most popular ones to give you the most accurate average estimate.

Why do you need to know your 1RM on the bench press?

Knowing your maximum bench press weight is critical for anyone involved in strength sports.

Main advantages:

  • Load planning: 1RM is used to determine percentage ranges for all training sets (for example, 70% of 1RM for hypertrophy or 90% for maximal strength development).
  • Progress assessment: This is the most objective indicator of your strength growth over time.
  • Safety: A calculated 1RM allows you to lift submaximal weights while avoiding injury-prone "one-rep" lifts.
  • Motivation: A clear, measurable goal for future training cycles.

How to calculate 1RM: Popular formulas

Formulas for estimating 1RM are based on the relationship between the weight lifted ( $W$ ) and the number of repetitions to failure ( $R$ ). The fewer repetitions you do with the working weight, the more accurate your 1RM calculation will be.

Our calculator uses the three most authoritative formulas:

Epley Formula

The Epley formula is considered one of the most convenient and is often used in powerlifting: $$1\text{ПМ} = W \times (1 + \frac{R}{30})$$

Brzycki formula

The Britski formula has proven itself in hypertrophy and strength training, especially at higher reps: $$1\text{ПМ} = W \times \frac{36}{37 — R}$$

McDonagh formula

This formula, although more complex, is often used to check calculations: $$1\text{ПМ} = \frac{W}{1.0278 — 0.0278 \times R}$$

Where:

  • $1\text{ПМ}$ - maximum weight for one repetition.
  • $W$ - lifted working weight in kilograms.
  • $R$ - the number of repetitions completed until failure.
