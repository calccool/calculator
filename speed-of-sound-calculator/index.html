Sound speed calculator: online calculation for various environments and temperatures

The speed of sound is the distance that a sound wave travels in an elastic medium per unit time. Unlike the speed of light, this value is not a constant and directly depends on the physical properties of matter: density, temperature and elastic modulus.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the speed of sound vibrations using two modes:

  • Gas environment: Calculation based on thermodynamic parameters (adiabatic constant, molar mass).
  • Liquids and solids: Use of temperature coefficient corrected reference values.

Additionally, the tool performs supersonic analysis, calculating the Mach number for moving objects, which is critical for aerodynamics and aircraft construction.

Influence of the environment on the propagation of sound waves

The physics of the process is explained by the rate of transfer of kinetic energy between molecules. The denser the particles are packed and the stronger the connections between them, the faster the disturbance propagates.

Speed ​​of sound in gases

In ideal gases, the key factor is temperature. As it increases, the average speed of movement of molecules increases, which accelerates the transfer of momentum. For air under standard conditions ( $0$ °C) the speed is approximately $331$ m/s.

Speed ​​of sound in solids and liquids

In solids such as steel or aluminum, the speed of sound is much higher due to the high Young's modulus (resistance to deformation). Sound in metals moves almost $15$ - $20$ times faster than in air.

Formulas and mathematical calculation model

The calculator uses fundamental equations of thermodynamics and continuum mechanics to obtain accurate results.

Calculation for ideal gases

The mathematical model is based on the Laplace equation:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma \cdot R \cdot T}{M}}$$

Where:

  • $\gamma$ - adiabatic index (for diatomic gases, including air, $\gamma \approx 1.4$ );
  • $R$ —universal gas constant ( $8.314$ J/(mol K));
  • $T$ - absolute temperature in Kelvin ( $T_K = T_C + 273.15$ );
  • $M$ is the molar mass of the gas (kg/mol).

Mach number calculation

The Mach number ( $M_a$ ) is a dimensionless quantity that determines the ratio of the speed of an object to the local speed of sound:

$$M_a = \frac{V_{object}}{v_{sound}}$$

This parameter is used to classify the flight modes: subsonic ( $M_a < 1$ ), transonic, supersonic and hypersonic.

Instructions for using the calculator

To get the result, follow these steps:

  1. Select environment type: Switch between the Gases or Liquids/Solids tabs.
  2. Specify material: Select from the list (eg air, helium, steel) or leave the default value.
