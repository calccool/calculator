Swimming speed calculator: calculating 100m pace and swim time

For a swimmer, “pace” is more important than “speed” in usual km/h. Pace shows how long it takes you to cover a standard distance of 100 meters. This is the main indicator on which the training process in the pool and open water is based. Our calculator allows you to instantly switch between calculating your current pace and predicting your finishing time.

Main features of the calculator

  • Tempo calculation: Enter your distance and total time to see your 100m average.

Mathematical basis: calculation formulas

For accurate training planning, the following dependencies are used.

1. Calculation of pace ( $P$ ) for 100 meters:

If you know the total time ( $T$ ) in seconds and the distance ( $D$ ) in meters, the formula looks like this:

$$P = \frac{T}{D} \times 100$$

The result is converted from seconds back to MM:SS format.

2. Calculation of linear speed ( $V$ ) in km/h:

To compare swimming speed to running or cycling speed:

$$V = \frac{D}{T} \times 3.6$$

3. Calculation of the number of pools ( $L$ ):

$$L = \lceil \frac{D}{L_{pool}} \rceil$$

Where $L_{pool}$ is the length of the pool bowl (25 or 50 m).

How to improve your pace?

To progress, it is not enough to simply swim from side to side. Experts recommend:

  1. Interval training: Swim segments of 100 or 200 meters with a fixed rest, trying to maintain the target pace.
  2. Equipment control: Water resistance increases in proportion to the square of the speed. Improving the body's streamlining has a greater effect than brute force.
  3. Using splits: Our calculator calculates control points (25%, 50%, 75%). Try to complete the second half of the distance no slower than the first (Negative Splits).
