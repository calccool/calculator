How does the psychomatrix calculation work?

The algorithm for calculating the psychomatrix is ​​based on the determination of four “working numbers”. So that you understand the accuracy of our tool, we present the mathematical model by which your personal square is constructed.

Mathematical algorithm:

Let the date of birth be represented as a sequence of numbers. For example: 25.04.1985.

  1. First number ( $W_1$ ): The sum of all digits of the date of birth. $$W_1 = 2+5+0+4+1+9+8+5 = 34$$
  2. Second number ( $W_2$ ): The sum of the digits of the first number. $$W_2 = 3+4 = 7$$
  3. Third number ( $W_3$ ): The difference between the first number and double the first (non-zero) digit of the birthday. $$W_3 = W_1 — (2 \times d_1) = 34 — (2 \times 2) = 30$$
  4. Fourth number ( $W_4$ ): Sum of digits of the third number. $$W_4 = 3+0 = 3$$

The final psychomatrix is ​​formed by distributing all the numbers from the date of birth and the resulting working numbers ( $W_1, W_2, W_3, W_4$ ) across nine sectors of the square.

The meaning of the sectors of the Pythagorean Square

Each cell in the table is responsible for a specific area of ​​life and characteristics of temperament. The number of digits in a cell determines the intensity of manifestation of a particular quality.
