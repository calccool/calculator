Income tax calculator 2025–2026: calculation of new rates online

Income tax is a direct federal tax paid by legal entities operating under the General Taxation System (OSNO). The object of taxation is the profit received by the organization, that is, the difference between its income and expenses.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly calculate the tax amount, net profit and see the visual distribution of the company's financial flows. The tool is fully adapted to changes in tax legislation that came into force on January 1, 2025.

Main features of the calculator

The tool supports two operating modes, making it useful for both quick assessments and detailed planning:

  • Simplified input: Enter only the total amounts of income and expenses.
  • Detailed calculation: division into sales revenue and non-operating income/expenses.
  • Accounting for losses: automatic application of restrictions on reducing the tax base (no more than 50% according to Article 283 of the Tax Code of the Russian Federation).
  • Visualization: A dynamic chart shows the share of taxes and operating expenses in your income structure.

Formula for calculating income tax

Mathematically, tax calculation can be represented as sequential actions. First, the tax base is determined:

$$B = (I_{rev} + I_{non}) — (E_{prod} + E_{non}) — L_{prev}$$

Where:

  • $B$ - tax base;
  • $I_{rev}$ - sales revenue;
  • $I_{non}$ - non-operating income;
  • $E_{prod}$ - production and sales costs;
  • $E_{non}$ - non-operating expenses;
  • $L_{prev}$ - the amount of the transferred loss of previous years.

The final tax amount ( $T$ ) is calculated by multiplying the base by the effective rate ( $R$ ):

$$T = B \times R$$

Step-by-step instructions for use

  1. Select operating mode. Use “Simplified” if you have ready-made totals from the balance sheet, or “Detailed” for a more accurate analysis.
  2. Enter financial indicators. Indicate amounts excluding VAT, since income tax is calculated from “net” income.
  3. Set up your tax rate. The default is 25%. If your company is eligible for incentives (such as educational or healthcare organizations), select a 0% rate or enter your own value.
  4. Consider the losses. If in previous tax periods the company worked in the red, activate the “Take into account losses of previous years” switch. The calculator itself will limit the deduction according to the law so that you do not make mistakes in the calculations.
  5. Get the result. Click “Calculate” and the system will instantly generate a report indicating the tax base, tax amount and net profit available for distribution.

Using this tool minimizes the risk of arithmetic errors and helps to quickly predict the tax burden when planning transactions for 2026.
