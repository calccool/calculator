Theoretical note: Vector product

The cross product of two vectors $\vec{a} = \{a_x; a_y; a_z\}$ and $\vec{b} = \{b_x; b_y; b_z\}$ is a vector $\vec{c}$ , which satisfies the following conditions:

  • The length of the vector $\vec{c}$ is numerically equal to the area of ​​the parallelogram built on the vectors $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ .
  • Vector $\vec{c}$ is perpendicular to the plane in which the original vectors lie.
  • The direction is determined by the right hand rule (or right screw).

Calculation formula using coordinates

To find the coordinates of the resulting vector, the method of calculating the determinant of a third-order matrix is ​​used, where the first line is the unit vectors (unit vectors $i, j, k$ ):

$$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ a_x & a_y & a_z \\ b_x & b_y & b_z \end{vmatrix}$$

Expanding this determinant, we obtain formulas for each coordinate of the new vector $\vec{c} = \{c_x; c_y; c_z\}$ :

$$c_x = a_y \cdot b_z — a_z \cdot b_y$$

$$c_y = a_z \cdot b_x — a_x \cdot b_z$$

$$c_z = a_x \cdot b_y — a_y \cdot b_x$$

Theoretical background: Dot product

A dot product is an operation whose result is a number (scalar) rather than a vector. It is extremely important for determining angles and work done by a force.

Algebraic formula for three-dimensional space:

$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_x \cdot b_x + a_y \cdot b_y + a_z \cdot b_z$$

Geometric meaning and angle

The scalar product can also be expressed in terms of the lengths of the vectors and the cosine of the angle between them:

$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}| \cdot |\vec{b}| \cdot \cos \alpha$$

This gives the formula for finding the angle $\alpha$ :

$$\alpha = \arccos \left( \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}| \cdot |\vec{b}|} \right)$$

Where the modulus (length) of the vector is calculated as the root of the sum of the squares of its coordinates:

$$|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{a_x^2 + a_y^2 + a_z^2}$$

Properties of the relative position of vectors

Our calculator automatically checks two key properties:

  1. Collinearity: Vectors lie on one line or on parallel lines. Condition: their vector product is equal to the zero vector ( $\vec{0}$ ), or the coordinates are proportional: $$\frac{a_x}{b_x} = \frac{a_y}{b_y} = \frac{a_z}{b_z}$$
  2. Orthogonality: The vectors are perpendicular to each other (the angle between them is 90°). Condition: their scalar product is equal to zero. $$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0$$
