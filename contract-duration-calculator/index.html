Contract duration calculator: add years, months and days to a date online

In legal and business practice, accuracy in calculating deadlines is a critical factor. A mistake on one day can result in an automatic extension of an unwanted contract or a missed statute of limitations. Our calculator is designed to eliminate the human factor when working with calendar dates.

The tool supports two main operating modes:

  1. Adding a deadline to a date: used to determine the expiration date of a contract when the validity period is known (for example, 11 months or 3 years).
  2. Difference between dates: allows you to instantly find out how much time has passed from the moment the document was signed to today or any other date.

How does the mathematical calculation logic work?

The calculator operates not just with the number of days, but with the calendar structure of the year. This is important since the length of the months varies from 28 to 31 days, and February in a leap year makes its own adjustments.

The formula for determining the end date of $D_{end}$ is as follows:

$$D_{end} = D_{start} + \Delta Y + \Delta M + \Delta D$$

Where:

  • $D_{start}$ —commencement date of the obligations;
  • $\Delta Y$ - number of full calendar years;
  • $\Delta M$ - number of full calendar months;
  • $\Delta D$ - additional calendar days.

When calculating the notice period, the reverse algorithm is used:

$$D_{notice} = D_{end} — T_{notice}$$

Here $T_{notice}$ is the period within which the party is obliged to notify of termination.

Instructions for use

To get an accurate result, follow these steps:

  1. Select the operating mode: “Calculate end date” or “Difference between dates”.
  2. Specify Start date (usually this is the date of signature or entry into force).
  3. Enter the period in the appropriate fields (years, months, days).
  4. If the contract requires notice of termination, enter the number of days in the Notice of Termination field.
  5. Click the button "Calculate".

After clicking the button, the system will generate a detailed report, including the status of the contract (active, completed or not yet started) and the exact number of days in the period.

Why can’t you count “in your head”?

Many people mistakenly believe that “adding a month” means simply adding 30 days. However, if the agreement is signed on August 31, then a month later (in September) such a date does not exist. The system will automatically adjust the value to September 30, which corresponds to the rules of the civil code of most countries.
