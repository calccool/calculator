Calculator for screw piles for a frame house: online calculation of quantity and load

The construction of a frame house begins with a solid foundation. Screw piles are the best choice for prefabricated structures, but their number cannot be determined “by eye”. An error in the calculations can lead to uneven shrinkage or deformation of the grillage.

Our calculator allows you to perform a comprehensive engineering analysis that combines the geometric design of the pile field and testing for actual loads.

How the calculation algorithm works

The program uses a two-stage calculation system, which makes it stand out among its simplified analogues.

1. Geometric method

First, the minimum required number of supports is calculated based on the perimeter of the building and the length of the internal load-bearing walls. Basic rule: piles must be installed in the corners of the house and at the intersections of walls.

Formula for determining the number of piles along the length of the grillage:

$$N_{geo} = \left( \lceil \frac{L}{S} \rceil + 1 \right) + \left( \lceil \frac{W}{S} \rceil — 1 \right) \times 2 + \lceil \frac{I}{S} \rceil$$

Where:

  • $L$ - length of the house (m);
  • $W$ - house width (m);
  • $I$ - total length of internal walls (m);
  • $S$ - selected installation step (m).

2. Calculation based on bearing capacity

If you activate the "Advanced mode", the calculator calculates the weight of the house, including payload and snow load, and compares it with the capabilities of the soil.

Technical parameters and loads

For accuracy, we use weighted averages of frame pie weights and regulatory data for climate zones.

Weighting factors used in the calculator

Load-bearing capacity of one pile

The capabilities of the foundation directly depend on the diameter of the steel pipe and the type of soil:

  • 89 mm (Light loads): Suitable for light terraces, gazebos or extensions. Denomination - 1.5 tons.
  • 108 mm (Standard): An ideal choice for a residential frame house. Denomination - 2.5 tons.
  • 133 mm (Reinforced): For heavy two-story structures or difficult soils. Denomination - 4.5 tons.

Influence of soil type on the foundation

One of the unique functions of our tool is taking into account the coefficient of soil resistance. When calculating, the formula is used:

$$P_{final} = P_{base} \times K_{soil}$$

Where $K_{soil}$ is the correction factor:

  1. Dense sand ( $K=1.2$ ): Allows piles to be loaded above their nominal value.
  2. Clay/Loam ( $K=1.0$ ): Standard design conditions.
  3. Peat/Swamp ( $K=0.6$ ): Requires a significant increase in the number of supports due to weak fixation of the blade.

Why is it important to consider the installation step?

The optimal step for a frame house is 2–2.5 meters. Increasing the pitch to 3 meters is permissible only for one-story light buildings on dense soils. If the calculator shows that “The quantity has been increased due to the weight of the house,” this is a signal that the selected step geometrically cannot handle the weight of the structure, and supports need to be installed more often to ensure a safety margin.
