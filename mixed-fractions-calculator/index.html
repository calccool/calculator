Mixed Fractions Calculator: Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication and Division

Actions with mixed numbers online

Mixed fractions (numbers consisting of an integer part and a fraction, such as $2 \frac{3}{4}$ ) often cause difficulties in calculations. They cannot simply be added or multiplied “head-on”—preliminary processing is required first.

Our smart fraction calculator automates this process. It instantly converts numbers to improper fractions, performs the chosen mathematical operation, reduces the result and returns it in convenient form (mixed, improper and decimal).

How to use the calculator?

  1. Enter The whole part, Numerator And Denominator for the first fraction.
    • If the fraction is a common fraction (without a whole part), leave the “Integer” field equal to 0.
  2. Select action: Addition (+), Subtraction (-), Multiplication (×) or Division (÷).
  3. Enter data for the second fraction.
  4. Click "Calculate" to see the answer and step-by-step solution.

Solution algorithm (How does it work?)

The calculator follows standard mathematical rules that are taught in school:

1. Convert to an improper fraction

First, any mixed fraction $A \frac{B}{C}$ is converted to an improper fraction using the formula:

$$\frac{A \times C + B}{C}$$

Example: $1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1 \times 2 + 1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}$.

2. Perform the operation

  • Addition and Subtraction: Fractions are reduced to Lowest Common Denominator (LCD).
  • Multiplication: Numerators and denominators are multiplied: $\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d}$ .
  • Division: The first fraction is multiplied by the “inverted” second: $\frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c}$ .

3. Reducing and highlighting the whole part

The resulting result is reduced by the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD). If the numerator is greater than the denominator, the calculator will highlight the whole part again to present the answer as a nice mixed fraction.
