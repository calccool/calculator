Aspect Ratio Calculator: Calculate screen and image proportions without distortion

Aspect Ratio is the proportional ratio of the width of an image or screen to its height. When resizing media content, it is critical to maintain this ratio to avoid stretching or shrinking the image, which visually distorts the objects.

Our tool is not just a calculator, but a full-fledged simulator that shows in real time what your “canvas” will look like and calculates the decimal multiplier for fine-tuning your design or video editing.

How does the proportion calculator work?

To work with the tool, it is enough to know two parameters of the source image and at least one parameter of the desired size. The system will automatically determine the missing value.

Mathematical formula for calculation

The calculations are based on the rule of proportion. If we have the original values ​​ $W_1$ (width) and $H_1$ (height), and we want to get a new value $W_2$ with a known $H_2$ (or vice versa), the following formulas are used:

  • To find the width: $$W_2 = H_2 \times \left(\frac{W_1}{H_1}\right)$$
  • To find altitude: $$H_2 = W_2 \times \left(\frac{H_1}{W_1}\right)$$
  • Coefficient (multiplier): $$K = \frac{W}{H}$$

Instructions for using the tool

  1. Specify the basic proportion: Enter the width and height of the original in the $W_1$ and $H_1$ fields. You can also click on one of the preset buttons (eg 16:9) to fill in the fields instantly.
  2. Specify the required size: Enter the desired width value ( $W_2$ ) or height ( $H_2$ ). The calculator will substitute the second number itself, ensuring there are no distortions.
  3. Explore the results:
    • Ideal proportion: A mathematically simplified aspect ratio (for example, 1920x1080 makes 16:9).
    • Calculated size: The final resolution in pixels.
    • Decimal multiplier: The number by which to multiply the height to get the width.
