Mathematical formulas

Depending on the problem, the sum of squares can be calculated in different ways. Our calculator works with arbitrary data set.

Formula for an arbitrary series of numbers

Formula for the first n natural numbers

A special case often encountered in algebra problems is the sum of squares of the sequence from 1 to $n$ (for example, $1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2$ ). The formula used for this is:

$$S_n = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$$

Calculation example

Let's look at how the calculator works using a simple example. Let's say we have a data set: 2, -3, 4.

  1. Square each number:
    • $2^2 = 4$
    • $(-3)^2 = 9$ (note that the square of a negative number is always positive)
    • $4^2 = 16$
  2. Let's sum up the obtained values: $$4 + 9 + 16 = 29$$
  3. Result: The sum of the squares is 29.

Areas of application

This metric is rarely used on its own, but it is a building block for many important metrics.

  • Statistics and Variance: The sum of squared deviations from the mean is the basis for calculating variance and standard deviation. Without this, it is impossible to assess risks in finance or errors in physics.
  • Least squares method (LSM): Used in regression analysis to plot trend lines. The smaller the sum of squares of the residuals, the more accurately the model describes the data.
  • Signal processing: The energy of a signal is often proportional to the sum of the squares of its amplitudes.
  • Geometry: The Pythagorean Theorem ( $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ ) is a special case of the sum of squares for calculating distances (hypotenuse).

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to enter negative numbers?

Yes, sure. When squaring ( $(-x)^2 = x^2$ ), the minus sign disappears, so the contribution of a negative number to the total will always be positive.

How is RMS (Root Mean Square) calculated?

Our calculator automatically outputs RMS. This is the root of the arithmetic mean of squares. The formula looks like this:

$$RMS = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i^2}$$
