Brew Ratio Calculator: Ideal coffee and water ratios for V60, espresso and funnel

Brew Ratio (brew ratio) is the fundamental ratio of the mass of ground coffee to the mass of water. In the coffee industry, it is the main extraction control tool. Changing this coefficient by just one can turn a dense, sweet drink into a watery one or, conversely, too sharp.

Understanding proportions allows you to:

  • Stabilize the result: get the same delicious cup every morning.
  • Manage your body: make coffee lighter or denser.
  • Save grain: accurately calculate the dosage according to the volume of your favorite cup or server.

How to use the Brew Ratio calculator

Our tool supports three advanced calculation modes that cover any need - from home brewing in a funnel to professional espresso bar setup.

Three brewing modes

  1. By grain (g): If you only have a limited amount of coffee left, enter its weight and the calculator will tell you how much water to add.
  2. By water (ml): if you brew a standard volume (for example, a 400 ml server), the system will calculate the ideal dosage of ground grain.
  3. By drink (Yield): the most accurate method for those who want to get exactly one cup (for example, 250 ml) of finished coffee, taking into account the loss of water that will remain in the coffee grounds.

Basic formulas of coffee mathematics

For those who want to understand the mechanics of the process, we use the following formulas. The main difficulty is that ground coffee retains part of the water (absorption coefficient), which on average is equal to 2.0 (for 1 gram of coffee there is 2 ml of retained water).

Calculation of required water ( $W$ ):

$$W = C \times R$$

Where:

  • $C$ - mass of coffee in grams;
  • $R$ - selected proportion (Ratio).

Calculation of the finished drink ( $Y$ - Yield):

$$Y = W — (C \times A)$$

Where:

  • $A$ - absorption coefficient (in our calculator it is taken as 2.0 for filter methods).

Secrets of perfect extraction

In addition to proportion, the final result is influenced by three more factors that work in conjunction with Brew Ratio:

  • Grind: small proportions (1:12) usually require a coarser grind to avoid over-extraction.
  • Temperature: The lighter the roast, the higher the water temperature should be (92-96°C).
  • Contact time: If your coffee is brewing too long (more than 4 minutes for a funnel), try increasing the grind or decreasing the number of infusions.

Why is the volume of the drink less than the volume of poured water?

A common mistake newbies make is pouring 250 ml of water in the hope of getting 250 ml of coffee. A coffee tablet (cake) works like a sponge. On average, 15 grams of coffee will “steal” 30 ml of water from you. Our calculator automatically subtracts this amount in Per Drink mode to ensure you always get a full cup.

Want to experiment? Start with the classic ratio 1:16 (for 1 gram of coffee 16 grams of water). This is a starting point from which you can move towards decreasing (for greater density) or increasing (for greater lightness) the proportion.
