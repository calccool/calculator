EHP to PoE calculator: calculating effective health, armor and evasion

In the world of Path of Exile (PoE), Life or Energy Shield is just the tip of the iceberg. Your actual survivability is determined by the parameter EHP (Effective Health Pool) — the amount of “raw” incoming damage that a character can absorb before his health bar drops to zero.

Understanding your EHP is critical for completing end-game content such as Uber bosses, deep Delve, or maps affected by 100% Delirium. Our calculator allows you to break down your defense into atoms and see where your build falls short.

How the mechanics of protection work in the calculator

The calculator divides incoming threats into two main types: physical attacks and elemental spells. This allows you to fine-tune calculations for specific game situations.

Physical EHP and armor specifics

Armor in PoE works non-linearly. Unlike resistances, the stronger the blow dealt to you, the weaker it is. That is why our tool has an “Advanced Mode”, where you can specify Raw Damage (the strength of the enemy's blow).

Main components of physical protection:

  • Mitigation: Direct damage reduction through Armor.
  • Avoidance: Chance of no damage being dealt at all due to Evasion and Attack Block.

Elemental EHP and Spell Defense

For magical attacks, the key factors are your resistances and the new layers of defense introduced in the latest patches:

  1. Resistances: Every 1% above the base 75% gives an exponential increase in survivability.
  2. Spell Suppression: A unique mechanism that is guaranteed to reduce incoming damage from spells by 50% (or more if you have the appropriate talents).
  3. Spell Block: Mechanics of completely ignoring spell damage.

Calculation formulas

For those who want to further understand the mathematics of the game, the calculator uses the actual formulas of the current version of Path of Exile.

Calculation of armor damage reduction

The physical softening formula ( $DR$ ) is as follows:

$$DR = \frac{Armor}{Armor + 5 \cdot Damage}$$

Where:

  • $Armor$ is your armor rating.
  • $Damage$ - the magnitude of the incoming blow.
  • $DR$ cannot exceed 90%.

Final EHP formula

Total effective health is calculated using a formula that combines your resource pool and all layers of protection:

$$EHP = \frac{Life + ES}{(1 — Mitigation) \cdot (1 — Avoidance)}$$

Instructions: how to use the calculator

To get the most accurate result, follow this algorithm:

  1. Select mode: Switch between "Physical" and "Elemental" EHP depending on what threat you are analyzing.
  2. Enter basic values: Indicate your current amount of Health (Life) and Energy Shield (ES) from the character window (keyCin the game).
  3. Fill in the protection parameters: Enter the Armor, Evasion Chance and Block values.
  4. Adjust the impact force (for pros): Turn on the checkbox “Indicate the strength of the enemy’s impact.”
    • 2 500 - average mob hit on maps.
    • 5 000 - a strong blow from a rare monster.
    • 10 000 — level of Shaper or Sirus strikes.
  5. Analyze the graph: An interactive chart will show you how much of your survivability comes from pure meat (Life/ES) and how much from your defensive mechanics.
