Vaccination calendar: Vaccination schedule calculator by date of birth

Timely vaccination is the foundation of a child’s health and the only reliable way to protect against dangerous infectious diseases. However, it is extremely difficult to keep in mind complex drug administration regimens, revaccinations and intervals between doses.

Our interactive vaccination calculator developed on the basis of the approved National Preventive Vaccination Calendar. It automatically generates a personalized immunization schedule, counting the required intervals from your baby's date of birth.

How to use the calculator

The tool takes into account standard medical protocols for healthy children. To get the graph:

  1. Please indicate the exact child's date of birth in the appropriate field.
  2. Click the button "Show graph".
  3. The system will instantly build a timeline with past and upcoming vaccinations.
  4. Use the print feature to take the plan with you to your pediatrician appointment.

Important: This calculation is for reference only and assumes that the child has no medical exemptions. The individual schedule can be adjusted by your attending physician depending on the baby’s health condition, the presence of allergic reactions or past illnesses.

The principle of calculating the timing of immunization

The calculator is based on a mathematical model of adding regulated time intervals to the date of birth ( $D_{birth}$ ).

The formula for calculating the date of a specific vaccination ( $D_{vac}$ ) is as follows:

$$D_{vac} = D_{birth} + \Delta t$$

Where $\Delta t$ is the recommended vaccination age. Depending on the type of vaccine, $\Delta t$ can be measured in hours, days, months or years.

Examples of calculating intervals

  • For BCG: $\Delta t = 3 \dots 7$ days. Vaccination is carried out in the maternity hospital before discharge.
  • For the first DPT: $\Delta t = 3$ of the month. If the child was born on January 1, the planned vaccination date is April 1.
  • For measles booster vaccination: $\Delta t = 6$ years. This is an important stage before entering school.

Decoding the main vaccines

For parents who are encountering abbreviations in a vaccination certificate for the first time, we have prepared a brief reference:

  • BCG - protects against tuberculosis. Leaves a characteristic scar on the shoulder.
  • DTP (DTP) - a combined vaccine against whooping cough, diphtheria and tetanus. Often causes fever, which is a normal immune reaction. Modern analogues (for example, Pentaxim or Infanrix) may be easier to tolerate.
  • PDA (MMR) — comprehensive protection against measles, mumps (“mumps”) and rubella.
  • Hepatitis B (HepB) - a viral disease that affects the liver. The course consists of three doses to form lasting immunity.

Why can't you miss deadlines?

Keeping a calendar is critical to building herd immunity and personal protection.

  1. Immune window: Vaccines are administered precisely at the age when the child’s body is most vulnerable to specific infections, but is already able to produce antibodies.
  2. Efficiency of revaccination: Violation of the intervals between doses (for example, with DTP or Hepatitis B) can lead to the fact that immunity is not fully formed, and the course will have to be started again or adjusted.

Use our calculator as a guide for planning visits to the clinic, but always confirm the final dates with your pediatrician, especially if the child had an acute respiratory viral infection or had a medical emergency.
