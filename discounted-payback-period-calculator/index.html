Discounted Payback Period (DPP) Calculator Online

What is discounted payback period?

When assessing the effectiveness of investment projects, it is not enough to simply add up future income. Money tends to depreciate over time due to inflation and lost profits. $1,000 today is worth more than $1,000 a year from now.

Discounted Payback Period (DPP) - this is the time required for the sum of discounted (reduced to present value) cash flows to cover the amount of the initial investment.

Unlike the simple payback period (PP), this method gives a more realistic picture, since it takes into account time value of money.

How does the calculator work?

Our tool automates complex financial analysis. The following data is used for calculation:

  1. Initial investment ( $I_0$ ): The amount invested at the start (zero period).
  2. Discount rate ( $r$ ): An interest rate that reflects the cost of capital, risk, or expected return (such as the deposit rate or WACC).
  3. Cash Flow: The net profit that the project generates each year.

Calculation formulas

To find the DPP, you first need to calculate the discounted cash flow (DCF) for each period $t$ using the formula:

$$DCF_t = \frac{CF_t}{(1 + r)^t}$$

Where:

  • $CF_t$ is the nominal cash flow per year $t$ .
  • $r$ — discount rate (in fractions of a unit, for example, 0.1 for 10%).
  • $t$ - year number.

Then it is calculated NPV (Net Present Value) on a cumulative basis. The payback period occurs at the moment when the cumulative NPV changes sign from minus to plus.

The exact formula for a fractional year is:

$$DPP = Y + \frac{|NPV_Y|}{DCF_{Y+1}}$$

Where:

  • $Y$ - number of full years until payback.
  • $|NPV_Y|$ — balance of outstanding debt at the end of the year $Y$ (absolute value).
  • $DCF_{Y+1}$ - discounted flow next year.

Calculation example

Let's assume you invest RUB 100,000. at a rate of 10%.

Income by year: 50,000, 50,000, 50,000.

Result: The project will pay off in the 3rd year.

A simple payback period would be exactly 2 years ( $50+50=100$ ), but taking into account discounting, the period increased to 2.35 years.

Advantages and disadvantages of the DPP method

When making business decisions, it is important to consider the features of the indicator:

  • Plus: Shows real liquidity risk. If the DPP is longer than the life of the project, you cannot invest.
  • Plus: Takes into account alternative returns (through the discount rate).
  • Minus: Ignores cash flows that will come in after payback moment.
