Tuning mathematics and resale formula

If you decide to sell your car through LSC, it is important to understand that you will never get your full investment back. The game applies strict depreciation rules.

Modification cost formula

The total cost of tuning is calculated as the sum of all selected components multiplied by the class coefficient ( $K_{tier}$ ):

$$C_{total} = (C_{engine} + C_{brakes} + C_{trans} + C_{susp} + C_{turbo} + C_{armor} + C_{visuals}) \times K_{tier}$$

Sales revenue formula

When selling a car at Los Santos Customs, the final amount ( $V_{resale}$ ) is formed according to the following principle: you receive 60% of the state price of the car and 50% of the cost of all installed modifications.

$$V_{resale} = (P_{base} \times 0.6) + (C_{total} \times 0.5)$$

Where:

  • $P_{base}$ - the base price of the car in the showroom.
  • $C_{total}$ - the full cost of the installed tuning.

Main tuning categories in LSC

To achieve maximum performance (“Full Tune”), it is necessary to upgrade key components. In our calculator you can select levels for each of them:

Technical part (Performance)

  • Engine: Increases acceleration. Level 4 is required for any competition car.
  • Brakes: Reduce braking distance. Affects handling in sharp turns.
  • Transmission: Improves gear shifting and gives a significant boost to acceleration.
  • Turbocharger (Turbo): The most expensive and effective upgrade to improve overclocking dynamics.

Safety and aesthetics

  • Armor: Increases body strength in collisions. With 100% armor, the vehicle can withstand much more damage before smoke appears from under the hood.
  • Visual tuning: Includes paint, wheel replacement, body kits and neon. These elements have little effect on speed (with the exception of some spoilers, which increase downforce), but represent a significant portion of the costs.
