Penalty calculator under Art. 395 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation: accurate calculation of interest online

Article 395 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes the measure of liability for failure to fulfill a monetary obligation. If the debtor evades repayment of funds or is late in paying for goods or services, the creditor has the right to collect interest for the use of someone else’s money.

The amount of this interest is by default tied to the key rate of the Bank of Russia that was in effect during the relevant periods of delay, unless a different amount is established by law or your agreement.

Formula for calculating interest for using someone else's money

Our calculator uses a precise mathematical algorithm that breaks the entire overdue period into periods of validity of different rates of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation. The basic formula for each individual period is as follows:

$$P = S \times \frac{R}{100} \times \frac{d}{Y}$$

Where:

  • $P$ - the total amount of interest (penalties) for a specific period;
  • $S$ - principal amount;
  • $R$ — applied interest rate (key rate of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation or fixed contractual rate);
  • $d$ — number of days overdue in a given period;
  • $Y$ - number of days in a year ( $365$ or $366$ if the year is a leap year).

If a debt moves from a regular year to a leap year, the algorithm automatically splits the accrual by applying the correct divisor for each time period.

How to use an online calculator

We have developed this tool for lawyers, accountants and individuals to simplify routine calculations for preparing pre-trial claims or statements of claim as much as possible.

Step-by-step calculation algorithm

  1. Select calculation mode. Calculation is available at the current rate of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (changes automatically) or at a fixed percentage, if it is specified in your agreement.
  2. Enter the amount of debt. You can use quick add buttons (eg +10,000) for convenience.
  3. Specify the period. Specify the start date of the delinquency and the date of actual payment (or the date of filing the claim).
  4. Set up last day accounting. According to judicial practice (Resolution of the Plenum of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation dated March 24, 2016 No. 7), the day of payment of the debt is included in the period of delay. This option is enabled by default in the calculator, but you can disable it.

After clicking the “Calculate” button, you will receive a clear visualization of the ratio of the principal debt and accrued sanctions, as well as a detailed table for each period of rate change.

Features of calculation at the negotiated rate

If your contract provides for a non-standard rate (for example, $0.1\%$ for each day of delay), switch the calculator to the “Fixed%” tab. The tool will allow you to select the accrual period - per day or per year, and instantly recalculate the result without reference to the floating rates of the Central Bank. In this case, the formula simplifies:

$$P = S \times \frac{R_{\text{day}}}{100} \times d$$

Where $R_{\text{day}}$ is the daily interest rate.
