Rust scrap calculator: Calculation of studying blueprints and farming time

In the world of Rust, scrap metal is the main currency of progress. Without it, it is impossible to study powerful weapons, strong armor or high-tech mechanisms. Our scrap calculator created for those who want to know exactly how many resources and time it will take to achieve the next technological stage.

The tool supports two modes: planning a specific goal (for example, buying a workbench) and mass study of found drawings of different shooting ranges.

Main features of the calculator

The tool takes into account all the variables that players encounter on official and modified servers:

  • Calculation by shooting range: Quickly enter the number of drawings worth 20, 75, 125 and 500 scrap metal units.
  • Inventory accounting: Subtracts the scrap you already have from the total.
  • Teamwork: The function of dividing the farm norm among all members of a clan or group.
  • Time forecast: Mathematical calculation of farming time based on your current efficiency (per hour).
  • Progress visualization: Interactive chart showing study completion percentage.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator uses precise formulas to calculate resource shortages and required time. If you play in a group, the calculation is made according to the following algorithm:

$$D = (T_{total} — C_{inv})$$

$$P_{norm} = \frac{D}{N_{players}}$$

Where:

  • $D$ - Shortage (how much is left to collect);
  • $T_{total}$ — Total target amount of scrap;
  • $C_{inv}$ - Scrap that is already in stock;
  • $N_{players}$ — Number of active players in the group;
  • $P_{norm}$ - Individual rate for one person.

To calculate time, use the formula:

$$Time = \frac{D}{Rate}$$

where $Rate$ is the amount of scrap mined per hour.

Farming efficiency: where is the fastest way to collect scrap?

The speed of resource accumulation directly depends on the chosen route. Our calculator offers presets based on the average performance of experienced players:

  • Roads and water barrels (~200-300 skr/hour): Suitable for a safe start. The main income comes from processing components.
  • Metro and Monuments (~500-800 skr/hour): Medium risk level. Clearing underground tunnels using blue and red access cards.
  • Oil rigs and cargo (>1000 skr/hour): High risk, maximum return. Requires good equipment, but allows you to complete the Tier 3 research branch in one evening.
