Midpoint coordinates and segment length (3D) calculator: Online calculation

In analytical geometry and 3D modeling, you often need to find a point equidistant from two given coordinates, or divide a line segment in a certain proportion. Our online midpoint calculator solves these problems instantly, working both in three-dimensional space (3D) and on a two-dimensional plane (2D).

In addition to finding the center, the tool automatically calculates length of the segment (distance between points) and vector components, making it an indispensable tool for students, engineers and game developers.

Main features of the calculator

Unlike primitive analogues, this tool performs a comprehensive analysis of two points $A(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2, z_2)$ :

  1. Finding the middle: Finds the exact coordinates of the center of a segment.
  2. Euclidean distance: Calculates the exact length of a straight line between points.
  3. Division in proportion: Allows you to find a point dividing a segment in the ratio $m:n$ (for example, 1:2 or 2:3).
  4. Vector analysis: Shows the offset along the $\Delta x, \Delta y, \Delta z$ axes.

Calculation formulas

To understand the process, below are the mathematical formulas that the algorithm uses.

1. Midpoint Formula

The midpoint $M$ is the arithmetic mean of the corresponding coordinates of the ends of the segment.

$$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \; \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}, \; \frac{z_1 + z_2}{2} \right)$$

2. Distance between points (Distance Formula)

The length of a segment in 3D space is calculated as the root of the sum of squared coordinate differences:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 — x_1)^2 + (y_2 — y_1)^2 + (z_2 — z_1)^2}$$

3. Division of a segment in a given ratio

If you need to find the point $P$ dividing the segment $AB$ in relation to $m : n$ (where $m$ is parts from point $A$ and $n$ is parts from point $B$ ), the following formula is applied:

$$P_x = \frac{n \cdot x_1 + m \cdot x_2}{m + n}, \quad P_y = \frac{n \cdot y_1 + m \cdot y_2}{m + n}, \dots$$

Where is this calculation applied?

  • Computer Graphics and GameDev: To find the center of an object, calculate the camera (“LookAt”), interpolate movement and determine the distance to the target.

Instructions

  1. Enter coordinates Points A ( $x, y, z$ ). For 2D calculations, leave the $z$ field equal to 0.
  2. Enter coordinates Point B.
  3. If you just want the middle, leave the ratio fields m And n equal to 1 (default).
  4. Click "Calculate".
  5. The tool will build a projection graph and display the coordinates of the desired point, as well as the length of the segment.
