Hot rolled channel weight calculator (GOST 8240-97) online

Hot-rolled channel is one of the most popular types of shaped metal products, used in load-bearing structures, mechanical engineering and floors. Accurate calculation of its weight is critically important at the design stage (to assess the load on the foundation) and during procurement (to control the shipment of metal and order transport).

Our calculator is based on GOST 8240-97, which regulates the assortment of hot-rolled steel channels. The algorithm takes into account the section geometry, steel density ( $7850 \text{ кг/м}^3$ ) and the specifics of various rolled series.

How to use the calculator

The tool supports two operating modes, meeting the needs of both engineers and suppliers:

  1. Calculation of weight by length: You enter the total footage (for example, for an estimate), and the calculator displays the total theoretical weight of the lot.
  2. Calculation of length by mass: You indicate the purchased weight (for example, 2 tons), and the system calculates how many linear meters of rental you should receive.

Classification of channels: Series U, P, E, L

The weight of a linear meter of a channel directly depends on the shape of its cross section. According to the standard, there are several key series that can be selected in the calculator:

  • Series U (With slanted internal edges): Classic profile, where the internal edges of the shelves have a slope of 4% to 10%. This increases rigidity, but complicates bolted connections.
  • Series P (with parallel edges): A modern and easy-to-install option. The walls of the shelves are smooth, which simplifies the structural components.
  • Series E (Economy): They have a thinner wall compared to the P series, which reduces the weight of the meter and the cost of the structure, but reduces the load-bearing capacity.
  • Series L (Lung): Lightweight version with parallel edges for unloaded structures.

Weight calculation formula

The theoretical mass of a linear meter of rolled metal is calculated from its cross-sectional area and the average density of steel. The basic formula looks like this:

$$m = \rho \times S \times L$$

Where:

  • $m$ —required mass (kg);
  • $\rho$ - steel density (taken as $7850 \text{ кг/м}^3$ );
  • $S$ - cross-sectional area of ​​the profile ( $\text{м}^2$ );
  • $L$ - channel length (m).

The cross-sectional area of ​​ $S$ for a channel is a complex value, since the profile has roundings in the internal corners. Our calculator contains the exact values ​​of the $S$ area from the standard tables, which guarantees minimal error compared to a simplified geometric calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the weight in the calculator differ from the weight on the metal scrap scale?

The calculator counts theoretical weight according to GOST. Real rolled products often have a “minus tolerance” (the wall is slightly thinner than the allowed minimum), so the actual weight may be slightly less than the calculated one.

What is the difference between channel 16 and 16a?

The index “a” denotes a reinforced profile. At the channel 16a the shelves are wider and the wall is thicker than the usual No. 16, so its linear meter weighs more.

Can the calculator data be used for stainless steel channels?

With caution. The density of stainless steel ( $7900-7950 \text{ кг/м}^3$ ) is slightly higher than that of black steel, so the actual weight of stainless steel will be 1-1.5% more than the value shown.
