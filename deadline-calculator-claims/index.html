Claim response time calculator: online calculation of the deadline under the Civil Code of the Russian Federation

Meeting deadlines for reviewing official requests is a critical aspect in legal practice. An untimely response to a pre-trial claim may lead to the accrual of penalties, fines under the Consumer Rights Protection Law (CPL) or to the loss of a case in court due to non-compliance with the claim procedure.

Our online tool automates the calculation, based on the norms of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, and helps to accurately determine the date by which the response must be sent by 23:59.

Rules for calculating deadlines by law

According to the law, the calculation of the period begins not on the day the document is received, but from the next day. The calculator contains key principles:

  • Beginning of the period: Art. 191 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation establishes that the countdown begins the next day after the calendar date or the occurrence of the event that determines its beginning.
  • Deadline on a weekend: If the last day of the period falls on a non-working day, the end of the period is considered to be the next working day following it. 193 Civil Code of the Russian Federation.
  • Procedure: If the response is sent via mail, the deadline is considered met if the documents are submitted to the post office before midnight of the last day of Art. 194 Civil Code of the Russian Federation.

Deadline calculation formula

Mathematically, the calculation of the deadline can be represented as follows:

$$T_{deadline} = T_{start} + D_{period} + S_{shift}$$

Where:

  • $T_{deadline}$ - final date (deadline);
  • $T_{start}$ - date of actual receipt of the claim;
  • $D_{period}$ — number of days to respond (calendar or working);
  • $S_{shift}$ - the number of days of transfer if the end of the period falls on a weekend or holiday.

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

To obtain an accurate legal result, follow these steps:

  1. Select the date of receipt: Indicate the day when the claim was delivered to you personally (against your signature) or arrived at your post office.
  2. Set category: Select a suitable template (for example, 10 days for consumer protection) or enter your own period from the contract.
  3. Select day type: Clarify whether days are considered calendar days (by default) or working days (if this is expressly stated in the law or contract).
  4. Check the transfer checkbox: Make sure that the “Carry over from weekend” option is active. 193 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation, so that the calculator automatically moves the deadline if it falls on a Sunday.
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will give the exact date and visualize the timeline.

Important nuances when sending a response

When preparing a response, it is important not only to calculate the date, but also to correctly record the fact of sending:

  • When sending by Russian Post, use registered letter with a list of attachments.
  • Keep the cashier's receipt - it is the date on the receipt that confirms compliance with the deadline, even if the letter reaches the addressee in a week.
  • If the claim is served in person, request a receipt stamp (receipt stamp, date, signature) on your copy.
