Real cost of ownership (TCO) and cost per km calculator

Many car owners make the classic mistake when assessing their car budget: they only consider gas costs. However, the real cost of owning a car ( TCO — Total Cost of Ownership ) often turns out to be 2–3 times higher than gas station receipts.

Our car expense calculator helps you see the full financial picture. It takes into account not only obvious expenses, but also “invisible” factors such as loss of market value (depreciation) and periodic maintenance.

What is the real cost of a car?

To understand where the money goes, we divided expenses into three key groups. Our algorithm summarizes them and leads to a clear indicator - the cost of one kilometer.

1. Fuel costs (Variable)

This is money that burns in the engine in the literal sense of the word. They depend on your mileage and riding style.

  • Calculation formula: $$Cost_{fuel} = \frac{Distance}{100} \times Consumption \times Price$$ Where $Distance$ is mileage, $Consumption$ is consumption (l/100 km), $Price$ is the price per liter.

2. Shock absorption (Hidden)

The biggest expense item for new and fresh cars. As soon as you leave the dealership, the car loses value.

  • On average, a car becomes cheaper by 10–20% per year.
  • Even if you don't drive a car, it still loses value every month. Our calculator allows you to take this into account (enter the difference between the purchase price and the current sale price, divided by the tenure).

3. Fixed and recurring costs

Expenses that are inevitable, even if the mileage is minimal:

  • Insurance: OSAGO and CASCO.
  • Taxes: Transport tax (depending on horsepower).
  • Maintenance and repair: Changing oil, filters, pads, buying tires (winter/summer) and seasonal tire fitting.
  • Little things: Paid parking, car wash, fines, anti-freeze.

Why know the cost of 1 km of travel?

Indicator Cost per km (Price per kilometer) is the best indicator of efficiency.

  1. For work: If you use your personal vehicle for business purposes, you need to understand whether your employer's compensation covers actual wear and tear on the vehicle, not just gas.
  2. For comparison with a taxi: It often turns out that for low mileage (up to 5-7 thousand km per year), driving a taxi or car sharing is cheaper than maintaining your own car, especially taking into account depreciation.
  3. To plan your budget: Realizing that every kilometer costs a nominal 15 rubles, you begin to plan your trips more rationally.
