Fasteners and hardware calculator: Calculation of the weight and number of screws, nails, dowels

During construction and renovation, an eternal dilemma arises: design documentation requires accurate quantities fasteners (in pieces), and hardware stores sell them by weight (in kilograms or packs). An error in calculations leads either to crew downtime due to a lack of a couple of dozen screws, or to overpayment for extra kilograms of metal.

Our calculator solves this problem instantly. It uses current regulatory weight data for 1000 pieces. various hardware, allowing you to convert the purchase volume into piece quantities and vice versa.

What types of fasteners do we consider?

The tool supports 5 main categories of construction fasteners:

  1. Black wood screws (GD): Large thread, countersunk head. The most popular type for drywall and wooden frames.
  2. Metal screws (GM/Klopy): Fine thread, sharp tip or drill. Used for profiles and sheet metal.
  3. Roofing screws: Hex head with EPDM washer. They are heavy due to the seal.
  4. Dowel nails: Polypropylene dowel + nail-screw. Basic fasteners for concrete and brick.
  5. Construction nails: Classic fasteners for rough work.

Calculation formulas

To understand how the conversion is performed, we provide a mathematical basis.

1. Converting weight to quantity

If you are buying fasteners by weight and want to know if it is enough for your scope of work:

$$N = \frac{M_{total}}{m_{1}} \times 1000$$

Where:

  • $N$ - total quantity (pcs).
  • $M_{total}$ - total purchase weight (kg).
  • $m_{1}$ - theoretical weight of 1000 pieces of the selected standard size (kg).

2. Convert quantity to weight

If you know that you need to tighten 5000 screws and want to know how many kilograms to buy:

$$M_{total} = \frac{N}{1000} \times m_{1}$$

Purchasing Tips

  • Safety factor. Always add 5-7% to the estimated quantity. Hardware tends to get lost and bend during installation, and screwdriver bits can tear off splines.
  • Packing. Roofing screws are often sold in packs of 250 or 500 pieces. Use our calculator to find out the weight of such a package for transportation.
  • The price of a mistake. When working with dowel-nails (installing a profile to a wall), the lack of fasteners is critical. It’s better to buy an extra pack than to stop hammer drilling.
