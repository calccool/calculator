Karvonen Calculator: Calculate target heart rate zones and heart rate for training

The Karvonen method is the “gold standard” in determining the intensity of physical activity. Unlike simplified formulas that only take age into account, this method takes your resting heart rate (RHR). This allows you to get personalized data that's perfect for your current fitness level.

Our calculator will help you accurately determine your heart rate zones so you can train effectively - whether your goal is to burn fat, increase endurance, or strengthen your cardiovascular system.

Karvonen Formula: How does it work?

The calculation is based on the concept Heart rate reserve (HRR). This is the difference between your maximum heart rate and your resting heart rate. The higher your fitness, the lower your resting heart rate and the greater your “functional reserve”.

Mathematically, the formula looks like this:

$$Target\ HR = ((MHR — RHR) \times \%Intensity) + RHR$$

Where:

  • Target HR — Target heart rate (desired value).
  • MHR (Maximum Heart Rate) — Maximum heart rate.
  • RHR (Resting Heart Rate) - Pulse at rest.
  • % Intensity — Desired load percentage (from 0% to 100%).

Important: To calculate Maximum Heart Rate (MHR), our calculator allows you to choose the classic formula ( $220 — Age$ ) or the more modern Tanaka formula ( $208 — 0.7 \times Age$ ), which is considered more accurate for people over 40 years old.

How to measure resting heart rate (RHR) correctly?

The accuracy of the Karvonen method directly depends on how accurately you indicate your resting heart rate.

  1. Take measurements in the morning, immediately after waking up, without getting out of bed.
  2. Feel for a pulse in your neck or wrist.
  3. Count the number of beats in 60 seconds.
  4. For maximum accuracy, repeat the procedure 3-4 days in a row and calculate the average.
