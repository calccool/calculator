What is the Future Value of Money?

Future value (FV) is the amount to which current assets will grow by a certain date at a given interest rate. This calculation is the foundation for planning a pension, saving for children’s education or purchasing real estate.

The key growth factor is compound interest - a process when profit is accrued not only on the starting capital, but also on the already accumulated interest.

Calculation formulas

Depending on the terms of the deposit, the calculation mathematics changes. Our tool uses advanced financial algorithms.

1. Basic formula (Compound interest)

If interest is calculated once a year:

$$FV = PV \times (1 + r)^n$$

2. Formula taking into account capitalization frequency

If the bank charges interest more frequently (for example, monthly or daily), income grows faster. The formula takes the form:

$$FV = PV \times \left(1 + \frac{r}{k}\right)^{n \times k}$$

Where:

  • PV (Present Value) - Current (initial) investment amount.
  • r — Annual interest rate (decimal, 8% = 0.08).
  • n — Investment period in years.
  • k — Number of accrual periods per year (12 for monthly, 365 for daily).

3. Calculation of real cost (taking into account inflation)

To understand the purchasing power of the future amount, it is necessary to adjust the result for the inflation rate ( $i$ ):

$$Real FV = \frac{FV_{nominal}}{(1 + i)^n}$$

How to use the calculator

Our tool provides flexible settings for an accurate forecast:

  1. Present Value (PV): The amount you are willing to invest today.
  2. Interest rate: Expected annual return (for example, deposit rate or average growth of the S&P 500).
  3. Capitalization: Choose how often profits are reinvested. For bank deposits, this is most often “Monthly” or “At the end of the term” (once a year).
