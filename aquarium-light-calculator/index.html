Aquarium light calculator: calculating lumens per liter (lm/l)

Creating a stable ecosystem in an aquarium begins not with a filter, but with proper photosynthesis. Light is the main lever for controlling plant growth and biological balance. Our calculator will help you move away from the outdated watts per liter rule and move to the modern standard - calculating luminous flux in lumens.

Why count lumens and not watts?

Previously, when aquarists only had fluorescent lamps (T5 and T8), calculating by watt made sense, since their light output was approximately the same. With the advent of LED technology, everything has changed: one 10 W LED can produce 800 lm, and another - 1300 lm.

Lumen (lm) - This is a real indicator of how much light the lamp emits. It is this parameter that modern aquarscapers focus on so as not to turn their aquarium into either a dark swamp or an algae farm.

Calculation formulas

The calculator works based on two key parameters: net water volume and required intensity.

1. Calculation of net water volume ( $V_{net}$ ):

Most aquarists forget that soil, stones and driftwood displace a significant part of the volume. We use the $0.85$ coefficient to get a realistic value:

$$V_{net} = V_{total} \cdot 0.85$$

2. Calculation of the required luminous flux ( $L_{total}$ ):

$$L_{total} = V_{net} \cdot L_{req}$$

Where $L_{req}$ is the lumen rate per liter depending on the complexity of your plants.

Efficiency of different types of lamps

When choosing equipment, remember the coefficient of performance (efficiency). Different light sources convert electricity into lumens with different efficiency:

  1. Light Emitting Diodes (LED): On average they issue 100–120 lm/W. This is the most economical and durable option.
  2. Fluorescent lamps (T5/T8): They give out about 60–80 lm/W. They require regular replacement (every 6-10 months), as their spectrum “burns out” over time.
  3. Spotlights (SOV): Powerful sources that give out 80–90 lm/W, perfectly penetrate deep aquariums (from 50 cm and above).

Use the obtained data to select equipment. If your calculation showed 3000 lm, you will need either a 30 W LED lamp or a bunch of fluorescent lamps with a total power of about 50 W.
