Hot-rolled (steel) angle weight calculator: online calculation according to GOST

Hot-rolled steel angle is one of the most popular types of shaped metal products in construction and mechanical engineering. When drawing up estimates, ordering transport for transportation or calculating loads on metal structures, it is critically important to know the exact weight of the shipment.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly convert meters to kilograms (and vice versa) without having to manually look up values ​​in cumbersome charts.

Key features of the calculator

The tool is designed to solve real logistics and engineering problems:

Formulas and algorithm for calculating mass

The calculations are based on strict geometric parameters of the profile and physical properties of the metal. Although GOSTs indicate exact tabular values ​​taking into account the radii of internal rounding and blunting of flanges, for operational calculations a basic geometric formula is used, which gives an error of less than 1.5%.

First, the cross-sectional area of ​​the $S$ profile is calculated:

$$S = t \cdot (A + B — t)$$

Where:

  • $A$ — width of the first shelf (mm);
  • $B$ - width of the second shelf (mm);
  • $t$ - shelf thickness (mm).

Then the theoretical mass of one linear meter $m$ is determined:

$$m = \frac{S}{10^6} \cdot \rho$$

Where:

  • $S$ - cross-sectional area in square millimeters;
  • $\rho$ - steel density (the standard value for black rolled steel is $7850$ $кг/м^3$ ).

The final weight of the $M$ batch is calculated by multiplying the weight of one meter by the total length of the $L$ :

$$M = m \cdot L$$

Standards and GOSTs for steel angles

In the CIS countries, the production of hot-rolled angles is strictly regulated by two main state standards. This guarantees compliance of the geometric dimensions and strength characteristics with the declared values.

Basic regulatory framework

  1. GOST 8509-93 (Equal-flange hot-rolled steel angles): The most common type of rental. Used to create rigid frames, connections, trusses and other structures where the load is distributed evenly.
  2. GOST 8510-86 (Hot-rolled unequal steel angles): They are used in specific architectural and engineering solutions, when it is necessary to direct the load-bearing capacity of rolled products in a certain direction while maintaining a minimum total weight of the structure.
