Calculator of signal attenuation in twisted pair cable along the cable length

Attenuation is the gradual loss of signal intensity as it passes through a transmission medium. In the case of twisted pair, this process is inevitable and is determined by the physical properties of copper, dielectric losses and skin effect. If the attenuation exceeds the permissible threshold (usually 24 dB for gigabit networks), equipment on the receiver side ceases to recognize logical zeros and ones, which leads to an increase in errors (CRC) and a decrease in speed.

How the physics of the process works

Main factors influencing power loss:

  • Signal frequency: The higher the operating frequency (MHz), the stronger the skin effect (current is forced onto the surface of the conductor), which increases the resistance.
  • Segment length: Losses increase linearly with distance.
  • Temperature: When heated, the resistance of copper increases. ISO/IEC 11801 recommends taking into account the attenuation coefficient 0.4% for every degree Celsius above 20°C.

Mathematical calculation model

Our calculator uses the standard approximation formula for calculating insertion loss per 100 meters ( $IL_{100}$ ):

$$IL_{100} = a \cdot \sqrt{f} + b \cdot f + \frac{c}{\sqrt{f}}$$

Where:

  • $f$ — signal frequency in MHz.
  • $a, b, c$ - coefficients specific to each cable category (Cat 5e, 6, etc.).

To obtain the final result, taking into account the temperature ( $T$ ) and the actual length ( $L$ ), the formula is used:

$$Total\,Loss = IL_{100} \cdot \frac{L}{100} \cdot (1 + (T — 20) \cdot 0.004)$$

Practical recommendations for use

To get the most accurate results, follow these rules:

  1. Determine the operating frequency: For 100BASE-TX networks (100 Mbit/s), the calculation should be carried out at the frequency 31.25 MHz. For Gigabit Ethernet (1000BASE-T), the critical frequency is 100 MHz.
  2. Consider the headroom: In real conditions, it is recommended to leave a reserve of 2-3 dB for losses in connectors and patch panels.
  3. Temperature: If the cable is laid corrugated on the sunny side of the building, the temperature inside can reach 50-60°C. In this case, the effective segment length can be reduced from 100 meters to 85-90 meters.

Typical design mistakes

  • Excess length: The use of “passive” extension cords without repeaters at distances over 105 meters.
  • Too sharp bends: Clamping the cable changes the coil geometry, which locally increases attenuation and Return Loss.
  • CCA cable (Al-Cu): The calculator is designed for pure copper. Copper Clad Aluminum (CCA) has much higher losses and does not meet TIA/EIA standards.
