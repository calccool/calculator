How to Solve Linear Inequality? (Step by Step Theory)

Solving a linear inequality is very similar to solving a linear equation, but with one critical exception. The goal is to isolate the $x$ variable.

Step 1: Grouping

Collect all terms containing $x$ on the left side of the inequality, and all free numbers (constants) on the right. When a term is moved through the inequality sign, its own sign changes to the opposite (as in equations).

  • Original: $ax + b > cx + d$
  • Grouping: $(a — c)x > (d — b)$

Step 2: Isolate the $x$

At this point you have a simplified inequality of the form $Ax > B$ . To find $x$ , you need to divide both sides by the coefficient $A$ . And here lies the main rule.

The Most Important Rule: Reversal of the Sign

This is the only, but most important difference from the equations.

Rule: If you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by negative number, inequality sign needs to be turned over to the opposite.

Examples of sign reversals:

  • $-2x < 10 \quad \rightarrow \quad x > -5$ (Sign flipped from $<$ to $>$ )
  • $-5x \ge 15 \quad \rightarrow \quad x \le -3$ (Sign flipped from $\ge$ to $\le$ )

If you divide by a positive number, the sign remains the same.
