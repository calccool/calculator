Caliper Fat Percentage Calculator (Jackson-Pollock 3-Fold Method)

Determining body composition is a key step in fitness and medicine. Unlike body mass index (BMI), which takes into account only total weight and height, caliperometry allows you to divide body weight into fatty And fat-free (dry).

This online calculator uses the method Jackson-Pollock (3-site Jackson-Pollock skinfold method). This is the gold standard in the fitness industry, providing high accuracy of calculations (error less than 3-4%) provided that the measurements are taken correctly. The algorithm takes into account gender differences in the accumulation of adipose tissue, age-related changes in body density and the thickness of skin folds.

Measurement instructions: where and how to make a tuck?

To get an accurate result, you will need a caliper (a special tool for measuring the thickness of the fold). The accuracy of the calculation is 90% dependent on the correctness of the measurements.

General rules for using a caliper

  1. Side: All measurements are carried out strictly with right side bodies.
  2. Tuck: Grasp the skin and subcutaneous fat with your thumb and forefinger (distance between fingers is about 2-3 cm). Pull the fold away from the muscle.
  3. Dimension: Place the caliper jaws perpendicular to the fold, 1 cm from the fingers.
  4. Fixation: Wait 1-2 seconds for the caliper spring to compress the fold and record the reading.

Measuring points for men

In men, fat accumulates mainly in the abdominal area (apple type). The Jackson-Pollock method uses the following three points:

  • Chest: Diagonal fold. Take midway between the anterior axillary line and the nipple.
  • Abdominal: Vertical fold. Taken at a distance of 2 cm to the right of the navel.
  • Thigh: Vertical fold. It is taken on the front surface of the thigh, exactly in the middle between the inguinal fold and the top of the kneecap.

Measuring points for women

In women, adipose tissue is distributed according to the gynoid type (“pear”). The measuring points are different:

  • Triceps: Vertical fold. It is taken on the back of the shoulder, exactly halfway between the shoulder (acromion) and elbow joint. The hand should be relaxed and lowered.
  • Iliac region (Suprailiac): Diagonal fold. Taken directly above the iliac crest (side of the pelvic bone) along the natural waistline.
  • Thigh: Vertical fold. Similar to the male measurement - the middle of the front thigh.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator is based on regression equations derived by researchers A.S. Jackson and M.L. Pollock. The calculation takes place in two stages: first, body density is calculated, then it is converted into fat percentage.

1. Calculation of body density (Body Density)

For calculations, the sum of the three folds $S$ (in millimeters) and the age of $A$ (in years) are used.

For men:

$$D_m = 1.10938 — (0.0008267 \times S) + (0.0000016 \times S^2) — (0.0002574 \times A)$$

For women:

$$D_f = 1.0994921 — (0.0009929 \times S) + (0.0000023 \times S^2) — (0.0001392 \times A)$$

2. Siri Equation

The resulting body density is converted to body fat percentage:

$$\%Fat = \left(\frac{495}{Density}\right) — 450$$

The calculator also automatically calculates lean body mass (LBM) - the weight of muscles, bones, internal organs and water, excluding adipose tissue. This is a critical indicator for athletes gaining muscle mass.

Common errors in measurements

To get reliable results, avoid the following mistakes:

  1. Measurement after training. Physical activity causes blood flow to the skin and tissue swelling, which increases the thickness of the fold. Measure your body fat before your workout.
  2. Using cheap plastic calipers. They may produce errors due to a weak spring. For professional purposes, it is better to use models with constant pressure.
  3. Muscle capture. If you strain the muscle when measuring or grab it with your fingers along with fat, the result will be overestimated.
  4. Wet skin. If your skin is wet or sweaty, your fingers may slip, making it difficult to pull the fold back properly.

Use this tool every 2-4 weeks to track your progress. Remember that a mirror and measuring tape are excellent additions to caliperometry data.
