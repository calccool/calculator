How does fraction sorting work? (Mathematical algorithm)

In order for a computer (or a person) to compare fractions, they must be reduced to a “general form.” There are two main methods that our algorithm uses.

Method 1: Convert to Decimal

This is the fastest way to compare. Each fraction is divided (numerator by denominator).

Example: Need to compare 1/2, 3/8 And 0.6.

  1. 1/2 = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5
  2. 3/8 = 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375
  3. 0.6 = 0.6

Result in ascending order: 3/8 (0.375) < 1/2 (0.5) < 0.6.

Method 2: Reduction to a common denominator

This method is more often required in schools. To compare fractions, you need to make their “parts” the same.

Let's look at fractions: 2/3 And 3/5.

  1. Find the LCD (Least Common Denominator) for 3 and 5. This is 15.
  2. Multiplying the numerators:
    • For 2/3: (15 ÷ 3) × 2 = 10. New fraction: 10/15.
    • For 3/5: (15 ÷ 5) × 3 = 9. New fraction: 9/15.
  3. Let's compare the numerators: 10 > 9, which means 2/3 > 3/5.
