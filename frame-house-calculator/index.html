Online frame house calculator: Accurate calculation of materials and construction estimates

Why do you need an accurate calculation of a frame house?

Building a frame house is mathematics, where every mistake costs money. A shortage of just 5% of lumber can stop construction for a week, and excess insulation is “frozen” funds that could have been spent on finishing.

Our interactive frame house calculator designed by professional engineers. Unlike simple estimate tables, it uses geometric algorithms to calculate the “net” volume of materials, taking into account openings, rack spacing and roof design features.

What does this tool count?

We have divided the calculation into 4 key stages so that you get a complete picture of the upcoming costs:

  1. 🌲 Power frame: Volume of wood for external walls, internal partitions, floor/ceiling joists and rafter system.
  2. 🔨 Covering: Area of ​​board materials (OSB, plywood) for external wall cladding and continuous roof sheathing.
  3. ❄️ Thermal insulation: Calculation of the cubic capacity of insulation (mineral wool, basalt) minus the volume of wooden studs.
  4. 💰 Financial estimate: The total cost of all materials with visualization of costs on the chart.

Calculation formulas: How does it work?

For transparency of calculations, we publish the basic formulas included in the algorithm of our calculator. This will help you check your calculations manually.

1. Calculation of the number of racks

The number of racks ( $N_{st}$ ) depends on the perimeter of the building ( $P$ ) and the pitch of the racks ( $S_{step}$ ):

$$N_{st} = \lceil \frac{P}{S_{step}} \rceil + N_{corners} + N_{openings}$$

Where $N_{corners}$ are additional racks in the corners, and $N_{openings}$ are reinforcement of the openings.

2. Lumber volume (Walls)

Knowing the number of racks, the height of the walls ( $H$ ) and the section of the board ( $A \times B$ ), we obtain the volume:

$$V_{wall} = (N_{st} \times H \times A \times B) + V_{plates}$$

Where $V_{plates}$ is the volume of the upper and lower trim (usually 3 perimeters).

3. Calculation of insulation (“Net” volume)

Many calculators make the mistake of counting insulation simply by the volume of the walls. We subtract the volume of wood so that you do not overpay:

$$V_{insulation} = (S_{wall} \times T_{wall}) — V_{frame}$$

Where $S_{wall}$ is the area of ​​the walls minus windows, $T_{wall}$ is the thickness of the wall, $V_{frame}$ is the volume of the wooden frame.

How to use the calculator

  1. Select preset: If you have a standard project (for example, “6x6 country house”), select it from the list for a quick start.
  2. Please indicate dimensions: Enter the length, width and height of the walls. Don't forget to indicate the total length of the internal partitions - they take up to 30% of the budget for wood.
  3. Set up the construct: Specify the section of the board (usually 150x50 mm) and the pitch of the racks (the standard for insulation is 600 mm).
  4. Specify prices: Enter current prices per cube of timber and insulation in your region to accurately calculate the estimate.
  5. Get the result: Click "Calculate". A detailed estimate with tabs and graphs will open below. You can print it or save it as PDF by clicking the printer button.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Does the calculator take into account jibs and crossbars?

The basic calculation includes a safety factor for strapping and structural elements. To accurately account for the jib, we recommend setting a margin of 10%.

How to calculate a roof?

The calculator automatically calculates a simple gable roof based on the dimensions of the house and the angle of inclination that you set with the slider.
