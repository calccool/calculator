Chinese horoscope: Animal and element calculator by date of birth

Eastern astrology is a complex mathematical system that combines the 12-year cycle of the earthly branches (animals) and the 10-year cycle of the celestial trunks (elements). Unlike Western tradition, Chinese New Year does not have a fixed date, making simple calculations by year often erroneous.

Our calculator implements an algorithm based on the solar calendar Bazi (Four Pillars of Destiny). This ensures that users born in January or early February will receive their true sign and not the previous or subsequent year's symbol.

Mathematical calculation model

To determine your sign and element, the following formulas are used:

  1. Animal Definition (Earthly Branch): The animal index $I_a$ is calculated by the remainder of the year divided by 12: $$I_a = Year \pmod{12}$$ Where the remainder 4 corresponds to the Rat, 5 to the Ox, and so on.
  2. Element definition (Heavenly trunk): The element is determined by the last digit of the year of birth: $$E = Year \pmod{10}$$ The resulting number corresponds to a pair of elements (Yang/Yin).

  1. Tree: Gives the sign goodwill and a desire for expansion.
