Fagerström Test: Nicotine Addiction Calculator (Online Assessment)

The Fagerström Test for Nicotine Dependence (FTND) is the international gold standard in addiction medicine and pulmonology, developed by Dr. Carl Fagerström in 1978 (and modified in 1991). This tool allows you to determine the degree of physical attachment of the body to nicotine, which is a key factor when choosing a smoking cessation strategy.

Our calculator automates scoring, eliminating the possibility of error, and provides instant interpretation of the results.

Calculation algorithm and formula

The methodology is based on a score of six key behavioral factors of a smoker. The final nicotine dependence index ( $ND$ ) is calculated as the sum of answers to the questionnaire:

$$ND = \sum_{i=1}^{6} Q_i$$

Where $Q_i$ is the weight of the answer to the $i$ th question. Questions are weighted differently depending on their clinical significance. The greatest contribution to the final score (up to 3 points) is made by the time of smoking the first cigarette after waking up, as this is a direct indicator of the intensity of morning withdrawal syndrome.

Key assessment parameters:

  • Morning cravings: Shows the rate at which the nicotine concentration in the blood drops overnight.
  • Number of cigarettes: Characterizes the total toxic load.
  • Behavioral control: The ability to abstain from smoking in socially unacceptable situations or during illness.

Interpretation of results

The test result varies from 0 to 10 points. The higher the score, the stronger the physiological need for nicotine and the more pronounced the withdrawal syndrome will be.

Why determine the level of dependence?

Understanding your Fagerström score is essential to choosing the right treatment for tobacco smoking. Errors in judgment often lead to failures:

  1. At low dependency (0-3 points) Using potent nicotine patches or tablets can cause nicotine overdose and increase addiction. Cognitive behavioral therapy and trigger removal are effective here.
  2. With high dependence (6+ points) attempts to quit “by willpower” have low statistical effectiveness (less than 5% success). In this case, according to clinical recommendations, the prescription of drugs (nicotine replacement therapy, nicotinic receptor agonists) is required to relieve physical withdrawal.

Factors Affecting Test Accuracy

Although the Fagerström test has high validity, for a more complete picture you should consider:

  • Smoking experience: The longer a person smokes, the stronger the neural connections of the habit become stronger.
  • Type of tobacco products: Electronic nicotine delivery systems (vapes, heated tobacco systems) can change behavior patterns, making the classic test less accurate in terms of “number of cigarettes.”
  • Genetic factors: The rate of nicotine metabolism (the work of the CYP2A6 enzyme) affects the frequency of smoking.

Use this calculator as a first step towards diagnosis. For high scores, consultation with a specialist is strongly recommended to create an individual smoking cessation plan.
