Format comparison: ZIP vs TAR.GZ

To better understand why the second option is preferable for servers, let's look at the basic architectural differences between these formats.

Mathematics of compression and algorithm efficiency

The conversion process involves translating data through the DEFLATE algorithm, which is used by the GZIP library. DEFLATE is an elegant combination of the LZ77 algorithm (search for repeat sequences) and Huffman coding (entropy coding).

The compression efficiency directly depends on the Shannon information entropy of the source data, which is calculated by the formula:

$$H(X) = — \sum_{i=1}^{n} P(x_i) \log_2 P(x_i)$$

Where $P(x_i)$ is the probability of a certain byte or sequence appearing in the archive. Because TAR first "glues" all files together into one continuous bitstream, the GZIP algorithm has a longer context (sliding window) to find duplicates. This allows you to significantly reduce the overall entropy and achieve a compression level that is physically unattainable for a regular ZIP archive when working with many small text files.
