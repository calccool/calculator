Personal property tax calculator: calculation based on cadastral value

Property tax for individuals in most regions is calculated based on cadastral value object. This is the closest approximation to the market value of real estate established by the state. Our online tool allows you to instantly calculate the payment amount, taking into account all legal benefits and reduction factors.

For an accurate calculation you will need two main indicators:

  • Cadastral value (can be found in the extract from the Unified State Register or on the Rosreestr website).
  • Total area of ​​the object according to the technical data sheet.

Tax deductions by type of object

The state provides an automatic tax deduction that reduces the taxable area. The tax is not paid for all meters, but only for the difference between the total area and the preferential one.

Formula for calculating property tax

The calculation is performed in several stages. First, the tax base is determined, and then the tax rate is applied taking into account the share of ownership.

1. Determination of the tax base

The tax base is the cadastral value of the part of the area that is subject to taxation after applying the deduction.

$$Base = C_{cad} \cdot \frac{S_{total} — S_{deduction}}{S_{total}}$$

Where:

  • $C_{cad}$ - full cadastral value;
  • $S_{total}$ - total area of ​​the object;
  • $S_{deduction}$ - tax deduction area (for example, 20 $м^2$ for an apartment).

2. Total tax amount

Once the base is determined, the tax rate is applied and individual ownership conditions are taken into account.

$$Tax = Base \cdot \frac{Rate}{100} \cdot Share \cdot \frac{M}{12}$$

Where:

  • $Rate$ - tax rate (usually 0.1% for residential premises);
  • $Share$ - share in ownership (for example, 0.5 when owning half);
  • $M$ - the number of complete months of ownership of the object per year.

Tax rates

Local authorities have the power to change base rates, but in most cases the following values ​​apply:

Standard rate 0.1%

Applicable to most residential properties:

  • Residential buildings and residential premises (apartments, rooms).
  • Garages and parking spaces.
  • Objects of unfinished construction (if it is a residential building).
  • Outbuildings with an area of ​​up to 50 square meters. meters.

Increased rate 2.0%

Suitable for expensive or commercial real estate:

  • Objects whose cadastral value exceeds 300 million rubles.
  • Administrative, business and shopping centers.
  • Non-residential premises used for offices, retail outlets, catering.

Other real estate 0.5%

Rate for all other types of objects not included in the lists above.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Where can I find the cadastral value?

The easiest way is to use the public cadastral map on the official website of Rosreestr or order an extract from the Unified State Register of Real Estate. The value is updated every few years during the government revaluation.

Do you need to calculate your taxes yourself?

The Tax Service (FTS) independently calculates the tax and sends a notification to the taxpayer’s personal account or by mail. However, the use of a calculator is necessary for:

  • Checking the correctness of Federal Tax Service accruals.
  • Planning a budget when purchasing a new property.
  • Estimates of the tax burden when dividing shares.

What if I owned the apartment for less than a year?

In this case, the ownership coefficient is applied. For example, if you bought an apartment in August, tax will be charged only for 5 full months of ownership. Our calculator supports calculations for an arbitrary holding period.
