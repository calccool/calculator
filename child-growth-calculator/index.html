Child Height and Weight Calculator: Estimation according to WHO Standards (0-17 years)

Child Height and Weight Calculator according to WHO standards

Every parent asks the question: “Is my child developing correctly?” Physical parameters such as height and weight are key indicators of health. Our calculator is a comprehensive tool that not only gives a forecast, but also conducts in-depth analysis of your child's development based on official data from the World Health Organization (WHO).

This tool combines two approaches:

  1. Prognostic method: Calculates expected height and weight based on birth data and pediatric formulas (useful for younger children).
  2. Estimation method (WHO): Compares actual (current) height, weight and BMI of the child with norms for his exact age and gender, showing in which range he falls.

How to use the calculator?

To get the most accurate result, please fill out all fields:

  • Floor: The standards for boys and girls are significantly different.
  • Date of Birth and Date of Measurement: The calculator will automatically calculate the exact age of the child (in years, months and days), which is critical for correct estimation.
  • Data at Birth: Used to calculate forecast values.
  • Current Height and Weight: Used to calculate BMI and compare with WHO standards.

1. Predictive calculation: What to expect?

Pediatricians often use simple formulas to quickly assess a child's expected development. Our calculator uses them to calculate the "Expected Weight (Forecast)" field.

Important: These formulas are very approximate and do not take into account individual characteristics. They are most relevant for children in the first years of life.

  • Weight gain (younger age):
    • 0-6 months: The average increase is about 600-800 grams per month.
    • 6-12 months: The average increase slows down to 400-500 grams per month.
  • Formulas for children over 1 year:
    • Expected weight (kg): $W (kg) \approx 10.5 + (2 \times Y)$ (where $Y$ is age in years).
    • Expected height (cm): $H (cm) \approx 75 + (6 \times Y)$ (where $Y$ is age in years).

2. WHO assessment: Comparison with the norm

The most accurate assessment method is comparison with WHO centile tables (or percentiles). These tables are based on statistics from thousands of healthy children around the world.

What are percentiles?

If your child's height is in the "50th percentile," that means that 50% of children his age and gender are shorter than him, and 50% are taller. This is the ideal “golden mean”.

Our calculator uses a standard range 15th to 85th percentile as "Norma".

3. Child’s BMI: An important indicator

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a ratio of weight to height that helps determine whether a child is underweight, normal, or overweight for his/her age. current growth.

BMI formula:

$$BMI = \frac{Weight (kg)}{Height (m)^2}$$

Important: BMI is interpreted differently in children than in adults. A low BMI in a tall child may be normal, while the same BMI in a short child may indicate overweight. Therefore, the calculator also compares BMI with WHO BMI-for-age percentiles (for children over 2 years old).

Conclusion

Our baby development calculator is your first step to understanding your baby's physical condition. It provides a comprehensive picture by comparing forecast data with actual WHO statistical norms.

Remember that all children develop individually. This calculator does not provide a diagnosis. If you have any concerns about your child's height, weight, or BMI, be sure to consult with your pediatrician.
