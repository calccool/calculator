Solution dilution calculator (C1V1 = C2V2): Calculate concentration and volume

Dilution of solutions is a routine but critical procedure in any chemical or biological laboratory. An error in calculations at this stage can lead to failure of the experiment or damage to expensive reagents.

Our online calculator uses the equation C₁V₁ = C₂V₂ to help you quickly determine how much concentrated (“stock”) substance to take and how much solvent to add to obtain a solution with the desired concentration.

Basic dilution formula (The Dilution Equation)

The calculations are based on the law of conservation of the amount of matter. The number of moles (or mass) of solute before dilution is equal to the amount after dilution.

The formula looks like this:

$$C_1 V_1 = C_2 V_2$$

Where:

  • $C_1$ — Initial concentration (source or stock solution).
  • $V_1$ — Initial volume (how much stock solution needs to be taken).
  • $C_2$ — Final concentration (desired).
  • $V_2$ — Final volume (total volume of the finished solution).

To find the required volume of stock solution ( $V_1$ ), the formula is converted as follows:

$$V_1 = \frac{C_2 \times V_2}{C_1}$$

How to use the calculator

The tool automatically takes into account units of measurement, so you don't have to manually convert millimoles to moles.

  1. Enter the drain parameters ( $C_1$ ): For example, you have a 5M (molar) NaCl solution.
  2. Specify the target ( $C_2$ and $V_2$ ): For example, you want to get 100 ml of a solution with a concentration of 10 mM.
  3. Get the result: The calculator will show the volume of aliquot ( $V_1$ ) that needs to be taken and the volume of solvent (water, buffer) that needs to be added.

Calculation example (Case Study)

Let's imagine that you need to prepare a buffer for electrophoresis.

  • Available: Stock solution 10X TBE ( $C_1$ ).
  • Required: 500 ml working solution 0.5X ( $C_2$ , $V_2$ ).

Calculation: $V_1 = (0.5 \times 500) / 10 = 25$ ml.

To obtain the final volume, we add water to an aliquot: $500 — 25 = 475$ ml.

Supported units of measure

Our calculator is universal and supports various concentration measurement systems:

  • Molarity: M (mol/l), mM (millimole), µM (micromole). Used in biochemistry.
  • Mass concentration: g/l (g/L), mg/ml (mg/mL).
  • Percentages and shares: % (percent), ppm (parts per million), X (multiplicity, for example 50X).

Important: When working with percentages, make sure you use the same type of percentage (weight w/w or volume v/v) for both values ​​so that the calculation is correct.
