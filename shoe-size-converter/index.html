How to measure your foot length correctly?

To get an accurate result, do not rely on the numbers on the soles of old shoes. The foot can change over time and brands use different lasts.

  1. Preparation: Wear a sock of the thickness you plan to wear with your new shoes.
  2. Fixation: Stand on a piece of paper with your heel against the wall.
  3. Measurement: Mark with a pencil the most prominent point of your longest finger (usually the thumb, but not always).
  4. Result: Measure the distance from the edge of the sheet (wall) to the mark with a ruler.

Important: Measure both feet! Often one foot is slightly longer than the other. Focus on the greater value.

Reference Formula

Most European manufacturers use the Paris point system. If you want to check the calculation manually, use the following formula:

EU Size = 1.5 * (Foot Length in cm + 1.5 cm)

Where1.5 cm- this is a functional allowance (for the displacement of the foot when walking). For sports shoes, this allowance can be increased to 0.5–1 cm, depending on the type of activity.

Common mistakes when choosing a size

  1. Ignore Width. If you have wide feet, add +0.5 to your US/UK size or look for models marked Wide (W).
  2. Buying "back to back". There should always be 0.5–1 cm of free space between the toes and the toe of the shoe.
  3. Different brands. Nike, Adidas and New Balance may have a discrepancy of 0.5 sizes with the same markings. Our calculator shows the "golden mean" accepted in international trade (ISO 9407).
