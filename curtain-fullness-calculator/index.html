Curtain pleat calculator: calculating the gather factor and fabric footage online

Correct calculation of fabric footage is the foundation of an ideal interior. An error of 20–30 centimeters can turn a luxurious curtain into a stretched fabric that looks cheap and does not perform its functions. Our calculator is designed to help you get professional results without being a sewing expert.

What is the assembly factor (K)

Build factor - this is a numerical value that shows how many times the width of the fabric when straightened should exceed the length of the cornice. The higher this indicator, the thicker and more magnificent the folds will be.

Mathematical calculation model

For those who prefer to check the algorithms manually, our calculator uses the following formula to calculate directly (from curtain rod to fabric):

$$W_{total} = (L_{cornice} \cdot k + N_{curtains} \cdot 2 \cdot S_{hem}) \cdot (1 + \frac{U_{shrink}}{100})$$

Where:

  • $W_{total}$ - final fabric width for purchase;
  • $L_{cornice}$ - working length of the cornice;
  • $k$ - selected assembly coefficient;
  • $N_{curtains}$ - number of curtains (one or two);
  • $S_{hem}$ - extra for side seams (on each side);
  • $U_{shrink}$ - percentage of fabric shrinkage after decaling or washing.

Why is it important to consider shrinkage and seams?

Many people make the mistake of multiplying the length of the cornice by a factor and forgetting about technical allowances.

  1. Side seams: It takes from 2 to 5 cm to process one edge. If you have two sliding curtains, you will have 4 side edges - this is an additional 10-20 cm of fabric.
  2. Shrinkage: Natural fabrics (cotton, linen, viscose) “shrink” by 3–7% after the first wash. If you do not include this percentage in the calculation of the width, the curtains may no longer close after cleaning.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

We have introduced two modes of operation into the tool to make it as useful as possible both for planning a purchase and for working with leftover fabric.

Mode 1: Flow along the eaves (Direct calculation)

This mode is ideal if you already have a curtain rod hanging and are just going to the store to buy fabric.

  • Enter the length of the curtain rod.
  • Choose a shirring type (from light wave to French thickness).
  • Turn on “Seam Consideration” if you plan to hem the edges yourself.
  • Get the exact footage to purchase.

Mode 2: Selection of cornice by fabric (Reverse calculation)

It happens that you fell in love with the rest of the fabric in the store or you already have a piece.

  • Enter the width of the existing canvas.
  • The calculator will show what maximum curtain rod length this fabric will be enough for at the selected coefficient, so that the curtains do not look “poor.”

Professional measuring tips

  • Measure only the working part: If the curtain rod has decorative caps that are not used to move the hooks, do not include them in the calculation.
  • Drawing connection: If the fabric has a large repeat (repeating pattern), it is worth adding the length of one repeat to the final result in order to combine the pattern on the two canvases.
  • Height matters: Please remember that this calculator calculates width. When calculating the height, do not forget to add 10–15 cm for folding the bottom and processing the top with curtain tape.
