Why count cable weight?

Cable and wire products (CPP) have a high density due to the copper and lead content. Even a small coil of power cable can weigh more than 100 kg. Preliminary calculation is required for:

  • Logistics: Selection of vehicles with appropriate carrying capacity.
  • Installation: Calculation of loads on cable trays, overpasses and load-bearing cables.
  • Warehouse accounting: Quick inventory of residues without unwinding drums (through weighing).

Formula for calculating cable mass

For theoretical calculations, a basic formula is used based on the specific gravity of the materials. However, in reality, a cable is a complex composite structure (core, insulation, armor, shell).

A simplified mathematical model looks like this:

$$M = L \times m_{ud}$$

Where:

  • $M$ - total cable weight (kg).
  • $L$ - line length (m).
  • $m_{ud}$ - specific weight of one meter of cable (kg/m).

If it is necessary to take into account the weight of the container (drum), the formula takes the form:

$$M_{total} = (L \times m_{ud}) + M_{tare}$$

Where $M_{tare}$ is the weight of the wooden drum according to GOST 5151-79.

Calculation of cross-section and volume of copper

Sometimes it is necessary to calculate the mass of pure metal (for example, to hand over scrap or calculate resistance). The mass of copper is calculated through density ( $\rho \approx 8.92$ g/cm³) and volume:

$$M_{Cu} = N \times S \times L \times \rho$$

Where:

  • $N$ - number of cores.
  • $S$ - cross-section of one core (mm²).

Types of cables and features of their weight

Different brands of cable have radically different weights with the same cross-section of current-carrying conductors. This is due to the type of insulation and the presence of armor.

  1. VVGng / VVGng-LS: The most popular power cable with PVC insulation. Relatively lightweight, used for internal laying.
  2. NYM: Analogous to VVG, but with chalk-filled filler between the cores, which adds about 10-15% to the weight and makes the cable round in cross-section.
  3. KG (Flexible Cable): Has heavy rubber insulation that is UV and frost resistant. Significantly heavier than PVC analogues.
  4. VBBShv (Armored): The heaviest type. Contains two steel strips under the outer shell to protect against mechanical damage (trenching).

How to find out the cable length by weight?

This problem is often solved by storekeepers when taking inventory. To avoid unwinding the drum, it is weighed as a whole.

  1. The weight of the empty drum is subtracted from the resulting weight (usually indicated on the “cheek” of the drum or taken from the table above).
  2. The resulting “net” weight is divided by the specific weight of one meter of a given cable brand.

$$L = \frac{M_{total} — M_{tare}}{m_{ud}}$$

Our calculator supports this mode of operation: simply switch the tab to “Calculate length” and enter the known weight.
