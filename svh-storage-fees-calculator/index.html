Storage cost calculator for temporary storage warehouse: online calculation of customs duties and PRR

A temporary storage warehouse (TSW) is a mandatory stage for most imported goods awaiting customs clearance. The costs of terminal processing and placement often become an unpleasant surprise for foreign trade participants due to the complex tariff system. Our calculator automates this process, taking into account the specifics of warehouse logistics.

Basic principles of tariffing at terminals

Most temporary storage warehouses use a system of “units of account”. Unlike conventional warehouses, here rounding is always upward, which significantly affects the final amount.

  • Weight tariffication: Usually the calculation is carried out for every full or incomplete 100 kg of gross weight.
  • Volume tariff: It is used for large but light loads, where the unit of account is 1 $m^3$ .
  • Technological storage: This is a “free window” (usually 2-3 days) during which the terminal does not charge storage fees, but loading and unloading operations are charged in any case.

Formulas for calculating warehouse fees

To accurately forecast the budget, we use a mathematical model that takes into account the type of cargo and the period under customs control.

1. Definition of units of account ( $U$ ):

If the calculation is based on weight ( $M$ in kg):

$$U = \lceil \frac{M}{100} \rceil$$

If the calculation is by volume ( $V$ to $m^3$ ):

$$U = \lceil V \rceil$$

2. Calculation of the total amount ( $S_{total}$ ):

The amount consists of the cost of storage and a fixed fee for cargo handling:

$$S_{total} = (U \cdot D_{paid} \cdot T \cdot K) + (U \cdot P)$$

Where:

  • $D_{paid}$ — number of paid days (Total term minus free period);
  • $T$ - base rate per unit per day;
  • $K$ — load type coefficient (standard, temperature or ADR);
  • $P$ - cost of PRR per one unit of account.

Comparison of coefficients for different types of cargo

Tariffs for temporary storage warehouses directly depend on the conditions of detention. Dangerous goods (ADR) or goods that require temperature control are more expensive due to the need for special zones and energy costs.

How to minimize costs for temporary storage warehouses

Prolonged stay of cargo in a warehouse is often associated with delays in filing or issuing a declaration. In order not to overpay the terminal, we recommend adhering to the following rules:

  1. Preliminary information: Submit documents to customs before the vehicle arrives at the temporary storage warehouse.
  2. Document verification: Errors in invoices or packing slips are the leading cause of downtime beyond the free period.
  3. Taking into account the specifics of the warehouse: Some warehouses include the day of arrival and day of departure in the paid period as a full day. Our calculator allows you to flexibly configure these parameters in the “Customize tariffs” block.

Terminal processing and PRR

Loading and unloading operations (LOW) are an inevitable expense item. Even if you managed to release the goods during the free period, the warehouse will issue an invoice for moving the goods by forklift when unloading from the truck/container and subsequent loading into your vehicle. On average in the market, this fee is about 350 rubles for every 100 kg of weight.
