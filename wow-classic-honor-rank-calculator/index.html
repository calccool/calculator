WoW Classic Honor to Rank Calculator: Accurate RP Calculation

The PvP ranking system in the classic version of World of Warcraft is not just an accumulation of points, but a complex mathematical race. Unlike modern additions, here your progress depends on how effectively you fought relative to other players on the server and how much the “write-off” of points (Decay) affected you.

Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate how much effort (CP) you need to put in this week in order to see the long-awaited rank upgrade next Wednesday.

Basic terms of the PvP system

  • RP (Rank Points): Points that determine your current rank. These are the ones we count to understand progress.
  • CP (Contribution Points): “Honour”, which you farm in BGs and in the open world during the week.
  • Decay: Weekly write-off of 20% of your total RP. This is an "aging" mechanic that forces players to stay active.

Calculation formulas: Mathematics of your victory

The calculator uses the standard formulas of the original 1.12 honor system. To determine the final result, we carry out the calculation in three stages.

1. Determination of current RPs

Your progress within the rank as a percentage (which you see in the character window) is converted into points. If your rank is $R$ and your progress is $P$ , then:

$$RP_{current} = RP_{min}(R) + (RP_{max}(R) — RP_{min}(R)) \times \frac{P}{100}$$

2. Calculation of weekly write-off (Decay)

Every Wednesday the server takes a portion of your progress. This is a necessary evil.

$$Decay = RP_{current} \times 0.20$$

3. Required number of points for the goal

To reach the next rank ( $RP_{target}$ ), you need to not only gain the difference, but also completely cover the Decay.

$$CP_{needed} = RP_{target} — RP_{current} + Decay$$
