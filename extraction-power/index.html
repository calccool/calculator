Hood power calculator

Online kitchen hood power calculator

This calculator helps calculate hood performance depending on the parameters of the room and the air duct.
The result shows how many cubic meters of air (m³/h) the hood must pass through in order to effectively purify the air in the room, and also recommends optimal duct diameter And air flow speed.

Why is it important to correctly calculate the hood power:

  • Insufficient power - steam, odors and grease will remain in the room.
  • Excessive - excess noise and excessive energy consumption.
  • Incorrect duct diameter causes performance losses of up to 30%.
  • Every bend or long duct reduces the efficiency of the hood.

Formula for calculating hood power

The basic formula used in the calculator: Q = V × n

Where:

  • Q — required exhaust capacity (m³/h),
  • V — room volume (m³),
  • n — frequency of air exchange (number of times per hour when the air is completely renewed).

For the kitchen it is usually accepted n = 10–15, for bathroom n = 6–8, for toilet n = 8–10.

Example:
Kitchen area 12 m², ceiling height 2.7 m.
Volume: 12 × 2.7 = 32.4 m³.
Power: 32.4 × 12 = 388.8 m³/h.
Taking into account losses and reserve ≈ 430–460 m³/h.

Formula taking into account losses and reserves

Q = V × n × k₁ × k₂ × (1 + Z)

Where:

  • k₁ — duct resistance coefficient (turns, length),
  • k₂ — coefficient of installation type (wall-mounted/island),
  • Z — reserve for losses and production noise (usually 10–20%).

Tips for choosing a hood

  • For the kitchen up to 10 m² a model with performance is enough 350–400 m³/h.
  • For indoors 12–18 m² - from 450 to 650 m³/h.
  • If you often cook fried foods, choose stock +20–30%.
  • For long air ducts, use smooth plastic pipes instead of corrugation.
  • Avoid more three turns — each reduces power by 5–10%.
  • The hood noise increases in proportion to the pressure loss - do not reduce the diameter of the channel.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What power is needed for a 10 m² kitchen?
At a height of 2.7 m and normal cooking - approx. 400 m³/h.

2. Is it possible to take the hood “with a reserve”?
Yes, it is better if the rated power is 20% higher. This will reduce noise and improve efficiency.

3. Does the bend of the duct have an effect?
Yes, every turn adds resistance. With three turns, up to 25% of the flow is lost.

4. What does “air speed in the channel” mean?
This is a parameter in m/s that shows the movement of air through the pipe. Optimum - 3–7 m/s.
