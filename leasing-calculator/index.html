Online Leasing Calculator: Calculation of Payments and Real Increase in Price

Online calculation of leasing payments

Leasing (financial lease) is a popular tool for businesses and individuals that allows them to purchase transport or special equipment while distributing the financial burden. However, hidden fees and complex interest calculation schemes often make it difficult to understand the real cost of a transaction.

Our leasing calculator is a professional tool that will help you independently check the leasing company’s commercial offer, calculate the monthly payment and see the overpayment.

How to use the calculator?

To get an accurate graph, enter the following parameters:

  1. Property value: The full price of the vehicle or equipment from the supplier.
  2. Advance (Down payment): Typically ranges from 10% to 49%. The larger the advance, the less the overpayment.
  3. Leasing term: The period for which the contract is concluded (in months).
  4. Price increase rate: The annual percentage at which the leasing company finances the transaction.
  5. Redemption payment: The residual value that must be paid at the end of the term for the property to become yours. Having a buyout payment reduces the monthly burden.

Leasing calculation formula

Calculation of leasing payments is based on compound interest formulas. The following formula is used for the annuity schedule:

$$PMT = A \cdot \frac{i \cdot (1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $PMT$ - Monthly payment.
  • $A$ - Amount of financing (Cost - Advance).
  • $i$ - Monthly interest rate (Annual / 12).
  • $n$ — Leasing term in months.

Note: If the contract provides for a redemption payment, the formula becomes more complicated, since interest is also charged on the residual value.

Why take into account VAT?

For legal entities on the OSNO (General Taxation System), leasing is beneficial due to the possibility of VAT reimbursement. Our calculator separately allocates the amount of VAT (usually 20%) as part of the total payments. Your company will be able to deduct this amount, which actually reduces the cost of purchasing property by 20%.
