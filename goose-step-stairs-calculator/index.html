Goose Step staircase calculator: online design of steps and materials

When the area of ​​the house dictates its conditions, and a standard flight of stairs turns into an impregnable wall, an engineering trick comes to the rescue - the “Goose Step” staircase (also known as the “butterfly” or “sambo”). This is an ideal solution for openings where the length of the platform is limited and the angle of inclination exceeds the critical 45°.

Our calculator allows you not just to “estimate” the dimensions, but to carry out a full-fledged engineering calculation taking into account the ergonomics of the foot and the consumption of materials.

Advantages of the Goose Step design

The staircase got its name because of the specific gait of a person: due to the cutouts on the steps, the step turns out to be a waddle. Despite the apparent complexity, it has undeniable advantages:

  • Extreme compactness: Possibility of installation at an inclination angle of up to 65°.
  • Safety: due to the wide part of the step (tread), reliable support for the foot is provided.
  • Saving: 1.5–2 times less lumber is required than for a classic staircase.
  • Aesthetics: the unusual zigzag look becomes an accent in a modern interior.

Technical parameters and calculation formulas

Our calculator is based on the golden rule of design - Blondel's formula. It determines the ratio of the height of the riser and the depth of the tread for the most natural movement.

For a sambo-type ladder, the calculation is carried out according to the following relationship:

$$2 \cdot h + s \approx 620 \text{ мм}$$

Where:

  • $h$ - step height (riser);
  • $s$ - tread depth in the wide part;
  • $620$ is the average human step length.

How to use an online calculator

To obtain an accurate estimate and geometry parameters, follow three simple steps:

  1. Measure the height: Enter the distance from the finished floor of the first floor to the floor of the second floor in millimeters.
  2. Determine the length of the opening: Indicate how much horizontal space you are willing to allocate for the stairs.
  3. Select material: If you activate the “Materials and Estimate” mode, indicate the thickness of the step and the cost of a cubic meter of wood (oak, pine, ash) to receive a volume and price calculation.

Convenience index and safety

Our tool implements a unique visualization algorithm “Convenience Index”. He analyzes the result for compliance with GOST and SNiP.

Important tip: When installing a goose step ladder, always start the installation so that the first step is under the “leading” foot. Usually this is the right leg. In the drawing in our visualizer you will see a checkerboard arrangement of the wide parts - this is what guarantees that you will not lose your rhythm when lifting.

If the calculation shows an angle greater than 60°, we recommend increasing the length of the opening or considering a screw design for increased safety.
