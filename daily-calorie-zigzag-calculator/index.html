Calorie calculator by day: Calculation of the Zig-Zag strategy and BMR norms

Many who begin the journey to an ideal body face the same problem: in the first weeks the weight comes off quickly, but then the scale arrow freezes (plateau effect). This happens due to metabolic adaptation. The body, getting used to a constant low amount of energy, slows down the metabolism, reducing the level of the hormone leptin and thyroid hormones.

Our Calorie calculator by day uses an advanced strategy "Zig-Zag" (Calorie Cycling), which “deceives” the body by alternating days with low and high calorie intake.

How does the Zig-Zag strategy work?

Instead of monotonous eating 1500 kcal every day, you get a wave-like schedule. For example:

  • Low Days: They create an aggressive deficiency of adipose tissue.
  • High Days (Refeeds): Days with increased caloric intake (usually to maintenance levels). They restore leptin levels, provide psychological relief and a signal to the body: “There is no hunger, you can continue burning fat.”

The average weekly deficit remains the same, but the metabolism remains fast.

Mathematical basis of calculation

Our algorithm is based on two golden formulas of nutrition. You can select any of them in the settings.

1. Mifflin-St Jeor Formula

It is considered the most accurate for modern people (developed in 2005).

$$BMR_{муж} = (10 \times Вес) + (6.25 \times Рост) — (5 \times Возраст) + 5$$

$$BMR_{жен} = (10 \times Вес) + (6.25 \times Рост) — (5 \times Возраст) — 161$$

2. Harris-Benedict Formula (Revised)

Classic formula (1984), often used in clinical settings.

$$BMR_{муж} = 88.36 + (13.4 \times Вес) + (4.8 \times Рост) — (5.7 \times Возраст)$$

$$BMR_{жен} = 447.6 + (9.2 \times Вес) + (3.1 \times Рост) — (4.3 \times Возраст)$$

Where:

  • Weight — in kilograms (kg)
  • Height — in centimeters (cm)
  • Age - in years

Physical activity quotients (TDEE)

Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the calories you need to simply live at rest. To find out your True Daily Value (TDEE), the calculator multiplies your BMR by your activity factor.

How to use the results?

After entering the data, you will receive a table with a weekly schedule.

  1. “Low” days: On these days, try to eat more protein and vegetables to help keep you full when you're deficient.
  2. “High” days (High/Refeed): Use these days for intense training. Add complex carbohydrates (cereals, pasta) to your diet to replenish muscle glycogen stores.
  3. Safety: The calculator automatically limits your caloric intake to ensure you don't fall below your basal metabolic rate (BMR) for too long, which is critical for women's health and hormonal health.

Use this tool at least once a month to adjust your plan as your weight changes.
