Interest loss calculator for early termination of a deposit

Early termination of a bank deposit is a procedure in which the depositor withdraws funds before the date specified in the agreement. In most cases, this leads to a significant loss of profitability, as banks recalculate accumulated interest at the rate "Poste restante" (usually this is 0.01% per annum).

Our calculator allows you to instantly estimate your financial losses by comparing the expected profit at the end of the term with the actual payout if you exit early.

Main features of the calculator

  • Two accrual modes: support for deposits with payment at the end of the term (simple interest) and deposits with monthly capitalization (compound interest).
  • Flexible setting of termination rate: you can indicate your bank's specific rate for cases of violation of the terms of the contract.
  • Instant visualization: An interactive chart will show what percentage of profit will be “burned” when closing the account today.
  • Background information: built-in tips will help you understand what the demand rate is and how it affects the body of the deposit.

Mathematical calculation formulas

To understand how the numbers in the results are formed, we use standard banking algorithms.

1. Calculation of simple interest

If your contribution does not require capitalization, income is calculated using the formula:

$$I = P \times \frac{r}{100} \times \frac{t}{12}$$

Where:

  • $I$ — accrued interest;
  • $P$ — deposit amount;
  • $r$ — annual interest rate;
  • $t$ — placement period in months.

2. Calculation of compound interest (capitalization)

When adding interest to the deposit amount monthly, the compound interest formula is used:

$$A = P \times (1 + \frac{r}{100 \times 12})^t — P$$

3. Determination of lost profits

The final loss is the difference between the potential income under the contract and the actual income at the penalty rate:

$$L = I_{expected} — I_{actual}$$

Instructions: how to use the calculator

Step 1: Contract parameters

Select the type of interest calculation at the top of the calculator (with or without capitalization). Enter the initial deposit amount, the term in months and your annual rate.

Step 2: Closing Conditions

Indicate how long the deposit actually lay in the bank. The default “Termination rate” field is 0.01%, but you can change it if your agreement provides for preferential termination (for example, 1/2 of the base rate).

Step 3: Analyze the results

Click the "Calculate" button. The system will display the “Financial results” block, where the amount you are losing will be highlighted in red. Pay attention to the graph - it clearly demonstrates the scale of lost profits.

Tips: how to minimize losses

  1. Splitting of deposits. Instead of one large deposit, open several small ones. If you need money, you can close only one of them, maintaining the profitability of the rest.
  2. Deposits with preferential termination. Look for offers where after a certain period (for example, after six months) the closing rate does not drop to zero, but remains at 50–70% of the original one.
  3. Using expense transactions. If you only need part of the amount, consider deposits with the possibility of partial withdrawal without losing interest up to the minimum balance.
