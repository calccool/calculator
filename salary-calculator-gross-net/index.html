Salary calculator: transfer from Gross to Net and payroll calculation online

When discussing remuneration, there is often confusion between what is written in the employment contract and what actually comes to the bank card. To avoid falling into the trap of expectations, it is important to understand the difference between two key terms:

  1. Gross - This is the amount before taxes. This is what is indicated in vacancies and employment contracts.
  2. Net — net salary “in hand” after withholding income tax.

Our calculator performs an instant conversion between these values, and also allows the employer to estimate total personnel costs (TPC).

Basic calculation formulas

For a standard personal income tax rate of 13%, the calculation of net income is as follows:

$$Net = Gross \times (1 — 0.13)$$

If you need to find out the salary amount based on the desired take-home income, use the inverse formula:

$$Gross = \frac{Net}{1 — 0.13}$$

In 2026, with income over 5 million rubles per year, the personal income tax rate increases to 15% for the excess amount, which is also taken into account in the advanced settings of our tool.

Employer expenses and the Payroll Fund (WF)

For a company, the cost of an employee is not limited to the Gross amount. On top of the salary, the employer is obliged to pay insurance contributions. In 2026, the structure of these payments looks like this:

  • Mandatory pension, medical and social insurance: standard rate is 30%.
  • Contributions for injuries: from 0.2% to 8.5% depending on the class of professional risk.
  • Benefits for SMEs: for small and medium-sized businesses there is a reduced rate of 15% on the part of the salary exceeding the minimum wage.
