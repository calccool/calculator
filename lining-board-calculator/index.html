Lining calculator: Accurate calculation of the number of boards and packages (m²)

The main mistake when buying lining is calculating the material based on the total width of the board. Manufacturers indicate the size of the entire lamella (for example, 96 mm), but during installation the tenon fits into the groove, and the actual covered area decreases.

Our calculator uses an algorithm usable area, excluding the mounting tenon from the calculations. This ensures that you do not have to purchase additional material in the midst of repairs.

Calculation formulas

For transparency of calculations, we use classical engineering formulas. The algorithm works in three stages:

1. Calculation of net cladding area ( $S_{net}$ )

First, the area of ​​all walls (and ceiling, if the option is selected) is calculated, from which the area of ​​windows and doors is subtracted:

$$S_{walls} = (L_{room} + W_{room}) \times 2 \times H_{room}$$

$$S_{net} = S_{walls} + S_{ceiling} — S_{openings}$$

Where:

  • $L, W, H$ - length, width and height of the room.
  • $S_{openings}$ - total area of ​​openings.

2. Calculation of the number of boards ( $N_{boards}$ )

Here it is critical to use the working width of the lamella ( $W_{work}$ ):

$$S_{one\_board} = L_{board} \times W_{work}$$

$$N_{boards} = \frac{S_{net}}{S_{one\_board}} \times K_{waste}$$

Where $K_{waste}$ is the safety factor for trimming (usually 1.05 - 1.10).

3. Calculation of packages ( $N_{packs}$ )

Since the material is shipped in multiples of packages, the final value is rounded up:

$$N_{packs} = \lceil \frac{N_{boards}}{Q_{in\_pack}} \rceil$$

How to use the calculator

  1. Room measurements. Measure the length, width and height of the walls. If you plan to sheathe the ceiling, check the appropriate box - the program will automatically add its area ( $L \times W$ ) to the calculation.
  2. Accounting for openings. Add up the area of ​​all windows and doors. This will save your budget and avoid buying unnecessary material.
  3. Board parameters. Check with the seller working width. If the data is unknown, use the standard value for Eurolining - 88 mm.
  4. Stock Enough for simple installation 5-10%. If you plan to lay diagonally or have complex room geometry, increase the margin to 15%.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

How much lining is in one cube (m³)?

It depends on the thickness of the board. Volume formula:

$$V = N_{boards} \times L_{board} \times W_{total} \times H_{thickness}$$

Please note: when calculating cubic capacity (volume for transportation), it is used general, not the working width.

What are clamps and how many of them are needed?

Claymers are hidden fasteners. Consumption depends on the pitch of the sheathing (usually 40-50 cm). On average, about 20 clamps are used per 1 m² of lining.

How does variety affect the calculation?

The calculator calculates volume, but not quality. When purchasing grades “C” or “B”, it is recommended to increase the stock to 15-20%, since some of the boards may be rejected (falling out knots, cracks). For the “Extra” and “A” grades, a standard reserve of 5-10% is sufficient.
