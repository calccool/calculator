Schengen 90/180 calculator: Online calculation of days of stay

Planning trips to Europe requires strict compliance with visa laws. The main difficulty for travelers is the so-called "floating window". Unlike the old rules, where days were counted within a half-year, the current system means that for each new day of stay, the previous 180 days are taken into account.

Our calculator automates this process, eliminating the need to manually calculate dates using stamps in your passport.

Basic principles of stay control

For your visit to be legal, the following conditions must be met:

  • Total term: No more than 90 days within Schengen.
  • Control period: The window is 180 days, which is constantly shifting.
  • Accounting for boundaries: The day of entry (even at 23:59) and the day of departure (even at 00:01) are always counted as a full day of stay.

Mathematical calculation model

The calculator algorithm is based on checking each day of the planned stay of $d$ for compliance with the condition:

$$S = \sum_{i=d-179}^{d} t_i \le 90$$

Where:

  • $S$ - the sum of days of stay in the calculation window;
  • $d$ - current day being checked;
  • $t_i$ is a binary indicator of presence in Schengen on the day $i$ (1 - present, 0 - absent).

The check is performed iteratively for each subsequent day from the moment of entry until the sum $S$ reaches a critical value of 90.

Consequences of breaking a rule (Overstay)

Exceeding the limit even by one day can lead to serious administrative consequences:

  1. Monetary fine: The amount varies from 100 to 4000 euros depending on the country and the duration of the violation.
  2. Deportation: Forced expulsion with a ban on entry into EU countries for a period of 1 to 5 years.
  3. Future failures: Information about the violation is entered into the SIS (Schengen Information System), which makes obtaining visas almost impossible.

Answers to frequently asked questions

Does the day of departure count?

Yes, according to EU Regulation, any partial day is considered as full. If you checked in on Friday and checked out on Saturday, that’s a 2-day stay.

Is it possible to reset days by traveling outside Schengen for one day?

No. The 180-day window rule operates continuously. Departure “resets” only the continuity of stay, but not the total number of accumulated days in the system.

How to calculate if I had a national visa (D), and then switched to visa-free?

Days spent on a category D visa or residence permit, do not count within a limit of 90 days for short-term stays. Calculation under the 90/180 rule only starts from the moment you start using the visa waiver or C visa.
