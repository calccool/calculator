Universal biological age converter: Accurate conversion of animal years to human years

For a long time it was believed that to convert the age of a dog or cat into human equivalent, it was enough to multiply their years by 7. This is a deep misconception. Zoologists and veterinarians have proven that the aging process in different species is nonlinear. For example, a one-year-old cat’s physiological development corresponds to a 15-year-old teenager, and not to a 7-year-old child.

Our calculator uses updated allometric coefficients, which take into account the metabolism, heart rate and average lifespan of a particular species.

How does biological aging work?

The rate of aging of the body directly depends on the metabolic rate and size of the animal. In biology, this is described by Kleiber's law. Small mammals (mice, hamsters) burn life resources faster, while large ones (elephants, turtles) live in “slow” time.

For an accurate calculation, the following stages of growing up are used:

  1. Childhood (Rapid Growth): Extremely rapid development in the first 1-2 years.
  2. Maturity (Plateau): Slowing down the aging process.
  3. Geriatric period (Aging): Acceleration of degenerative processes towards the end of life.

Calculation formulas and coefficients

In the scientific community, to determine epigenetic age (using the example of dogs), a logarithmic formula derived by geneticists from the University of California is often used:

$$Age_{\text{human}} = 16 \ln(Age_{\text{dog}}) + 31$$

However, for the universal converter we use an adapted system of linear coefficients $k$ , which allows us to compare human age ( $A_h$ ) and animal age ( $A_a$ ):

$$A_h = A_a \times k$$

Factors influencing a pet's longevity

The calculated age is a biological reference, but the actual state of the body depends on many variables. If you want your pet to live longer than the estimated maximum, pay attention to the following aspects:

  • Genetics and breed: In dogs, small breeds (Chihuahuas, toy terriers) live much longer than giant breeds (Mastiffs, Mastiffs). The difference in the rate of aging can reach 30%.
  • Nutrition (Nutriciology): A balanced diet prevents obesity, the leading cause of premature aging in pets.
  • Habitat: Indoor cats live on average 15–18 years, while outdoor cats rarely survive the 5-year mark due to stress and infections.
  • Veterinary control: Regular check-ups (especially blood tests and ultrasound) can detect age-related diseases at an early stage.
