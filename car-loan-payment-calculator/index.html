How to calculate a car loan yourself?

Buying a car on credit is a serious financial decision. Bank promotional offers often indicate a “minimum payment” but are silent about the actual amount of overpayment and hidden fees.

Our car loan calculator is an independent tool that will help you see the complete financial picture before going to a car dealership. It allows you to simulate any conditions: from a classic bank loan to installment plans with a balance payment.

Annuity or Differentiated: What to choose?

This is the most common question from borrowers. Our calculator supports both repayment plans so you can compare them at a glance.

1. Annuity scheme (Equal payments)

You pay the bank the same amount every month for the entire term. This is convenient for the family budget, but in the end you will pay more interest. At the beginning of the term, most of the payment goes to pay off interest, and only a small part goes to the principal of the debt.

Formula for calculating annuity payment:

$$A = K \cdot \frac{i \cdot (1 + i)^n}{(1 + i)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $A$ - monthly payment.
  • $K$ — loan amount (car price minus down payment).
  • $i$ - monthly interest rate (annual / 12 / 100).
  • $n$ - number of months.

2. Differentiated scheme (Decreasing payments)

The principal amount is divided into equal parts, and interest is charged on the balance. In the first months, payments will be maximum (hard on the budget), but with each month the load decreases. The final overpayment with this scheme is always less.

Hidden commissions and real rate

Banks often lower the advertised interest rate, but add additional fees that significantly increase the cost of the loan. Our tool allows you to take them into account:

  • One-time commissions: Fee for issuing a loan, issuing an account, or compulsory life insurance during a transaction.
  • Monthly commissions: Fee for account maintenance or SMS notification. Even 100 rubles a month over 5 years turns into 6,000 rubles of additional expenses.

How to use the calculator?

  1. Enter the cost of the car: The full price of the car in the showroom or from the seller.
  2. Enter your down payment: The amount you are willing to deposit immediately (in cash or via Trade-In). The higher it is, the lower the overpayment.
  3. Loan parameters: Specify the bank's rate and term (usually from 12 to 84 months).
  4. Add commissions: If the contract has CASCO insurance included in the loan body, or other fees, indicate them in the appropriate block.
