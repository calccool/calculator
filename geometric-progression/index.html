Online geometric progression calculator: Sum, nth term and Graph

A geometric progression is a sequence of numbers in which each subsequent number is obtained by multiplying the previous one by a constant, non-zero number called denominator of progression ( $q$ ).

Our online calculator allows you not only to find any number in a sequence, but also to plot changes in values, as well as calculate the sum of a series, which is often required in financial calculations (compound interest), physics and biology.

How to use the calculator

To get instant results, fill out three main fields:

  1. First Member ( $a_1$ ) — the number from which the sequence begins.
  2. Denominator ( $q$ ) - the number by which each term is multiplied.
    • If $q > 1$ , the progression increases.
    • If $0 < q < 1$ , the progression is decreasing.
    • If $q < 0$ , the progression is alternating.
  3. Member number ( $n$ ) — the serial number of the number you want to find or to which you need to calculate the sum.

Basic formulas

Our tool uses classical algebraic formulas for calculations.

1. Formula of the nth term

To find any number in a sequence, knowing the first number and denominator:

$$b_n = b_1 \cdot q^{n-1}$$

2. Formula for the sum of the first n terms

If the denominator is $q \neq 1$ , the sum is calculated as follows:

$$S_n = \frac{b_1 (q^n — 1)}{q — 1}$$

3. Sum of infinitely decreasing progression

If the modulus of the denominator is $|q| < 1$ , then the sequence infinitely approaches zero, and we can find the sum of all its terms to infinity:

$$S_{\infty} = \frac{b_1}{1 — q}$$

(The calculator automatically detects this condition and displays the result in the $S_\infty$ field).

Sample problems

Example 1: Investment

You invested 1000 rubles ( $a_1$ ) at 10% per annum. This means that every year the amount is multiplied by 1.1 ( $q$ ). How much money will there be in 5 years ( $n=6$ , since the first year is the start)?

  • Calculation: $1000 \cdot 1.1^5 = 1610.51$ rub.

Example 2: Bacterial division

There is 1 bacterium ( $a_1$ ) in a Petri dish. Every 20 minutes they divide in two ( $q=2$ ). How many bacteria will there be after the 10th division?

  • Calculation: $1 \cdot 2^{10-1} = 512$.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Can the denominator be negative?

Yes. In this case, the signs of the numbers in the sequence will alternate (plus, minus, plus...). Our calculator correctly handles such situations and marks the graph in red for negative values.

What does the graph show?

The graph visualizes the first 10 terms of your progression. This helps to visually assess the rate of growth (exponential) or decay of the sequence.

How to find the denominator of $q$ ?

If you know two adjacent terms of the progression ( $b_n$ and $b_{n+1}$ ), simply divide the subsequent one by the previous one: $q = \frac{b_{n+1}}{b_n}$ .
