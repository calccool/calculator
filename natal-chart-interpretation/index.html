Astronomical accuracy and calculation formulas

Calculating a natal chart is a complex mathematical task that requires calculating the ecliptic longitude of celestial bodies. The basic formula for determining the position of the planet ( $\lambda$ ) includes taking into account the average longitude of the epoch and correction for anomaly:

$$\lambda = L + 1.915^\circ \sin(M) + 0.020^\circ \sin(2M)$$

Where:

  • $L$ - average longitude;
  • $M$ - average solar anomaly.

To calculate Ascendant (ASC) - ascending sign - the sidereal time formula and the geographical latitude of the place of birth are used:

$$ASC = \arctan\left(\frac{-\cos(RAMC)}{\sin(RAMC) \cdot \cos(\epsilon) — \tan(\phi) \cdot \sin(\epsilon)}\right)$$

Where:

  • $RAMC$ - right ascension of the midheaven;
  • $\epsilon$ —ecliptic inclination;
  • $\phi$ - geographic latitude.
