Accelerated loan repayment calculator: Payments every 2 weeks (Bi-weekly)

Many borrowers are unaware that simply changing the frequency of payments can save hundreds of thousands of rubles (or dollars) and reduce the term of the mortgage by several years. Our biweekly payment calculator clearly demonstrates the magic of compound interest and the effect of the “thirteenth payment”.

What is the essence of the “Bi-weekly” method?

The standard banking scheme involves 12 payments per year (once a month).

The Bi-weekly strategy works differently: you split your regular monthly payment in half and pay that half every two weeks.

There are 52 weeks in a year. This means that you will make 26 semi-payments:

$$26 \times 0.5 = 13$$

Thus, unnoticed by your budget, you contribute one full additional payment per year. This amount goes to repay the principal debt (the body of the loan), which dramatically reduces the overpayment.

Calculation formulas

To calculate the standard annuity payment ( $A$ ), the classic formula is used:

$$A = P \cdot \frac{r \cdot (1+r)^n}{(1+r)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • $P$ - loan amount.
  • $r$ - monthly interest rate ( $Годовая / 12$ ).
  • $n$ - number of months.

In the scheme Bi-weekly we are modifying the approach. The payment is $A / 2$ , but the accrued interest is recalculated every 14 days, and the loan body decreases faster.

Main advantages of the scheme

  1. Tremendous savings: You do not pay interest to the bank for the “extra” years of using the money.
  2. Psychological comfort: Paying half the amount every two weeks is often easier than parting with a large amount once a month (especially if your paycheck comes in twice a month).
  3. Fast Freedom: A 30-year mortgage can turn into a 24-25 year mortgage without refinancing.

How to put this into practice?

Most banks offer a once-a-month schedule by default. To switch to this diagram:

  • Option A: Find out from your bank if they officially support the biweekly payment schedule.
  • Option B (Universal): If the bank does not change the schedule, you can simply set up automatic payment. Divide the monthly installment by 12 (for example, 5,000 / 12 = 416) and add this amount to each monthly prepayment payment. The effect will be identical to the Bi-weekly method.
