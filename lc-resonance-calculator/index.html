LC Circuit Resonant Frequency Calculator: Calculation of L, C, F and Quality Factor

Oscillatory LC circuit is the simplest electrical circuit consisting of an inductor ( $L$ ) and a capacitor ( $C$ ). The main feature of such a circuit is the phenomenon of resonance, in which inductive and capacitive reactances compensate each other, allowing signals of a certain frequency to be isolated or suppressed.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly solve three classic amateur radio problems:

  1. Find frequency ( $F$ ): If the part values ​​are known.
  2. Select inductance ( $L$ ): To tune to the desired frequency with an existing capacitor.
  3. Select capacity ( $C$ ): To tune to the frequency when the coil is ready.

Thomson's formula and operating principle

The calculations are based on the classical Thomson's formula, describing the dependence of the resonant frequency on the circuit parameters.

To calculate the frequency, the equation is used:

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}$$

Where:

  • $f$ - resonant frequency (Hertz);
  • $L$ - inductance (Henry);
  • $C$ - capacitance (Farads).

If you need to find the parameters of components for a given frequency, the formula is converted as follows:

$$L = \frac{1}{4\pi^2 f^2 C} \quad \text{и} \quad C = \frac{1}{4\pi^2 f^2 L}$$

Additional parameters: Impedance and Quality factor

In addition to the base frequency, our tool calculates critical antenna and filter matching characteristics that are often missed in simple calculators.

1. Characteristic (wave) impedance ( $Z_0$ )

This parameter shows the reactance of the elements at the resonant frequency. It is important for matching the circuit to the load (for example, 50 or 75 ohm coaxial cable).

$$Z_0 = \sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}$$

2. Circuit quality factor ( $Q$ -factor)

The quality factor characterizes the quality of the oscillatory system: the higher it is, the narrower the bandwidth and the lower the energy loss. To calculate the quality factor, it is necessary to indicate the active loss resistance ( $R$ ) in the circuit.

$$Q = \frac{1}{R} \sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}$$

Practical Application

Where is this calculation used?

  • Radios: To tune the input circuit to the frequency of a radio station.
  • Transmitters: To match the output stage with the antenna (P-circuit).
  • Audio crossovers: To calculate speaker filters (although RC or RL circuits are more often used there, LC gives a steeper cutoff).
  • Metal detectors: The search coil is part of an LC circuit whose frequency changes as it approaches metal.
