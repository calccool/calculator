Superfat calculator for soap from scratch: accurate calculation of cholesterol and HS

Superfat (or superfat) is a certain amount of free oils in soap that have not reacted with alkali. During the saponification process, alkali interacts with fatty acids, turning them into salts (soap itself). If there is just enough lye to saponify all the oils, the soap will be “hard” and can dry out the skin.

The presence of excess fat solves two critical problems:

  1. Safety: Creates a “safety reserve” in case of scale errors, ensuring that there is no free alkali left in the finished product.
  2. Care: Unsaponified oils remain in the soap structure, nourishing, moisturizing and protecting the skin during washing.

Difference between HS and GS

There are two fundamental approaches to calculating and entering care oils, which our calculator supports:

  • Cold method (CS): The entire volume of oils is mixed with alkali immediately. We cannot know exactly what kind of oil will remain in the superfat, so we simply make an “alkali discount.”
  • Hot method (HS): The soap is brought to the stage of readiness (complete completion of the reaction), after which a specific “valuable” oil is added to it. This allows you to accurately control the composition of the care fraction.

How to use the overfat calculator

To obtain the exact recipe, follow these steps:

Calculation for the cold method

  1. Select a tab "Cold way".
  2. Enter total mass of oils in grams.
  3. Specify calculated alkali (its value is taken from the main soap calculator for 0% superfat).
  4. Select the one you want percentage of excess fat (from 1% to 15%).
  5. Click "Calculate". The system will show the final mass of alkali minus the discount.

Calculation for the hot method

  1. Switch to tab "Hot way".
  2. Enter total mass of oils, which are already in the pan.
  3. Specify percentage of excess fat, which you plan to add after the bath.
  4. The calculator will instantly calculate the exact weight of oil to be added to the finished mass.

Mathematical calculation model

For those who prefer to check data manually, our algorithm uses the following formulas:

Alkali discount formula (CS)

To find the final weight of alkali ( $L_{final}$ ), you need to know the base amount of alkali ( $L_{base}$ ) for complete saponification and the percentage of superfat ( $SF$ ):

$$L_{final} = L_{base} \times \left(1 — \frac{SF}{100}\right)$$

Formula for added oil mass (AO)

The calculation of the weight of the care oil ( $M_{sf}$ ) is based on the total weight of the base oils ( $M_{oil}$ ) in the recipe:

$$M_{sf} = M_{oil} \times \frac{SF}{100}$$

Expert advice on working with excess fat

  • Don't overdo it: Too high a percentage of superfat (more than 15%) can cause the soap to lather poorly, become too soft, or quickly go rancid (especially when using oils with a short shelf life).
  • Best before date: If you add high fat, use stable oils (coconut, palm, shea, cocoa). Avoid high percentages of sunflower or corn oil, as the free fatty acids in them oxidize quickly.
  • Scale accuracy: Always use a scale in 0.1 g or 0.01 g increments. Even a small measurement error when working with lye can change the final percentage of superfat.
