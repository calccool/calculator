Calculation algorithm: how we count your calories

The calculations are based on the gold standard of dietetics - the Mifflin-St. Geor formula, which is recognized by the American Dietetic Association as the most accurate for modern people.

1. Calculation of basal metabolic rate (BMR)

This is the amount of energy that your body spends in a state of complete rest (for breathing, blood circulation, cell growth).

$$BMR_{муж} = (10 \times W) + (6.25 \times H) — (5 \times A) + 5$$

$$BMR_{жен} = (10 \times W) + (6.25 \times H) — (5 \times A) — 161$$

Where:

  • W — weight in kilograms;
  • H — height in centimeters;
  • A — age in years.

2. Activity coefficient (TDEE)

To find out the actual daily requirement (TDEE), the basal metabolic rate is multiplied by the exercise factor:

  • 1.2 — Sedentary lifestyle (office work, lack of sports).
  • 1.375 - Light activity (light workouts 1-3 times a week).
  • 1.55 — Moderate activity (intensive exercise 3–5 times a week).
  • 1.725 — High activity (heavy loads 6-7 times a week).
  • 1.9 — Extreme activity (physical work + professional sports).

Surplus strategy: Dry mass vs Dirty mass

The main rule of weight gain is calorie surplus. You must consume more energy than you expend. Our calculator offers three strategies:

  1. Careful set (+10% to normal). Minimizes fat accumulation. Ideal for endomorphs (people who tend to be overweight). Muscle growth will be slow, but high quality.
  2. Standard (+15% to normal). Golden mean. Optimal balance between muscle growth rate and fat control.
  3. Quick dial (+20% to normal). Suitable for ectomorphs (thin people with fast metabolisms) who find it difficult to lose weight.

Why is drinking regime important?

Our calculator also calculates the water norm. When gaining weight, the need for fluid increases for two reasons:

  1. Absorbing large amounts of protein puts a strain on the kidneys, and water helps remove waste products.
  2. Glycogen (the storage of carbohydrates in muscles) binds water. 1 gram of glycogen holds 3-4 grams of water. Dehydrated muscle does not grow.

Common mistakes when gaining weight

If the calculator shows the norm, but the weight does not increase, check these points:

  • Eating too infrequently. Eating 3500 kcal in two meals is physically difficult and harmful to the gastrointestinal tract. Divide your diet into 5-6 meals (as our portion calculation suggests).
  • Ignoring sleep. Muscles grow not in the gym, but in bed. Sleep at least 7-8 hours.
  • Lack of fiber. On a high-calorie diet, be sure to eat vegetables to improve digestion.
