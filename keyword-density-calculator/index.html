Academic nausea is an indicator that determines the degree to which the text is spammed with the most frequently occurring words. If this ratio is too high, search robots may impose a filter on the page for over-optimization.

Key indicators of SEO analysis

For in-depth text checking, we use three basic metrics that are recognized as the gold standard in copywriting:

Academic text nausea

It is calculated as the ratio of the number of repetitions of the most frequent word to the total number of words in the document. Unlike classic nausea, this indicator is always expressed as a percentage.

Mathematically it looks like this:

$$N_{acad} = \frac{f_{max}}{W_{total}} \times 100\%$$

Where:

  • $f_{max}$ - frequency of the most repeated word;
  • $W_{total}$ - the total number of words in the text.

Classic nausea

This parameter is more “hard” and focuses on the absolute number of repetitions of the same root or word. It does not depend directly on text length as much as the academic indicator.

Calculation formula:

$$N_{class} = \sqrt{f_{max}}$$

Wateriness of the text

The “water” indicator determines the percentage of stop words (prepositions, conjunctions, interjections, particles) that do not carry a direct semantic load. High water content dilutes the usefulness of the content, making it less valuable to the user.

How to reduce nausea and improve rankings?

If the calculator detects “searching” for keywords, use the following tips:

  • Use LSI words: Replace direct occurrences of keys with synonyms, thematic words and associations.
  • Rebuild the structure: Often high nausea occurs due to redundant listings in lists.
  • Remove verbal garbage: Get rid of introductory constructions (“as is known,” “thus,” “certainly”) if they do not make sense.
  • Break up sentences: Long and complex constructions often force the writer to repeat the subject.
