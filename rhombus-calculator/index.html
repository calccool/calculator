Rhombus calculator: Calculate area, perimeter and angles online

A rhombus is a unique geometric figure, a special case of a parallelogram in which all four sides are equal. Calculator functionality

Unlike simple scripts, this tool supports 4 calculation modes, covering 99% of school and engineering tasks:

  1. Along two diagonals ( $d_1, d_2$ ): The classic method, if the lengths of the segments connecting opposite vertices are known.
  2. By side and height ( $a, h$ ): Convenient when a rhombus is viewed as a parallelogram.
  3. By side and corner ( $a, \alpha$ ): Ideal for trigonometric problems.
  4. Side and diagonal ( $a, d_1$ ): Allows you to find the second diagonal and area using the Pythagorean theorem.

Basic calculation formulas

To understand the logic of the calculator, below are the mathematical dependencies used in the calculations.

1. Area of ​​a rhombus ( $S$ )

Area is the size of the plane bounded by the sides of the figure. Depending on the source data, it is calculated in three ways:

  • Through diagonals: $$S = \frac{1}{2} \cdot d_1 \cdot d_2$$
  • Through side and height: $$S = a \cdot h$$
  • Through the side and sine of the angle: $$S = a^2 \cdot \sin(\alpha)$$

2. Perimeter ( $P$ )

Since all sides of a rhombus are equal, the perimeter formula is extremely simple:

$$P = 4 \cdot a$$

3. Relationship between diagonals and sides

The diagonals of a rhombus intersect at right angles and are divided in half at the point of intersection. This creates four right triangles within the figure, allowing the Pythagorean theorem to be used:

$$(\frac{d_1}{2})^2 + (\frac{d_2}{2})^2 = a^2$$

From here we can express the length of the side in terms of diagonals:

$$a = \frac{\sqrt{d_1^2 + d_2^2}}{2}$$

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to fit a circle into a rhombus?

Yes, you can fit a circle into any rhombus. The center of this circle lies at the point of intersection of the diagonals. The radius of such a circle is calculated by the formula:

$$r = \frac{d_1 \cdot d_2}{4a} = \frac{h}{2}$$

Our calculator calculates this parameter automatically.

Can the height be greater than the side?

No. Height ( $h$ ) is perpendicular, the shortest distance between sides. In a right triangle, the leg is always less than the hypotenuse, so the strict inequality $h \le a$ holds. The equality $h = a$ is possible only in one case - if the rhombus is a square.

How to find the angles of a rhombus?

If the diagonals are known, the angles can be found using the tangent of the half angle. The diagonals bisect the angle, forming a right triangle with legs $d_1/2$ and $d_2/2$ :

$$\tan(\frac{\alpha}{2}) = \frac{d_2}{d_1}$$

Where:

$$\alpha = 2 \cdot \arctan(\frac{d_2}{d_1})$$
