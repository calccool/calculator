Roll lawn calculator: Calculation of the number of rolls, weight and turnkey cost

This tool is designed for landscape designers and owners of suburban areas. Unlike simple calculators, our algorithm takes into account not only the “square”, but also logistic parameters: multiplicity of pallets, weight for choosing transport and agrotechnical norms for applying fertilizers during laying.

How to calculate the required amount of lawn?

An error in calculations when ordering rolled turf is costly. If you buy less, the new batch may differ in the shade of the grass, and delivery of the additional order will eat up the budget. Buy too much and you'll end up paying extra for extra pallets and heavy sod disposal.

Our calculator works according to the professional formula of landscape architects.

Calculation algorithm

To obtain accurate data, the following formulas are used:

  1. Net area calculation ( $S_{net}$ ): For rectangular sections: $$S_{net} = L \times W$$ Where $L$ is the length, $W$ is the width of the section.
  2. Taking into account the trimming coefficient ( $K_{waste}$ ): It is impossible to lay a lawn without waste.
    • For simple rectangular shapes: $5\%$ ( $K = 1.05$ )
    • For areas with flowerbeds, paths and radii: $10\%$ ( $K = 1.10$ )
  3. Total number of rolls ( $N_{rolls}$ ): $$N_{rolls} = \lceil \frac{S_{net} \times K_{waste}}{S_{roll}} \rceil$$ Where $S_{roll}$ is the area of ​​one roll (standard is $0.8 м^2$ ).

Reference information: Weight and Dimensions

When ordering transport, it is critical to know the weight of the cargo. Freshly cut lawn contains water, so its weight varies.

Important: Lawn is a perishable product. When rolled up (in a roll), it begins to “burn” (smolder) after 24-48 hours, especially in hot weather. Calculate delivery strictly at the time of installation.

Tips for laying and fertilizing

The calculator automatically calculates the required quantity starting fertilizer. This is a mandatory installation step.

  • Application rate: For rooting (Pre-Seeding), an NPK complex with a predominance of phosphorus is usually used. The average consumption rate is 3.5 kg per 100 m² (one hundred square meters).
  • Soil preparation: The soil must be compacted with a garden roller so that an adult’s foot does not fall through, but leaves a mark.
  • Styling: The rolls are laid in a checkerboard pattern (like brickwork), the seams are tightly joined, but do not overlap each other.

Logistics and transport

Use the "Total Weight" field to order the correct vehicle:

  • Up to 1.5 tons: Gazelle (holds ~1 pallet / 50 m²).
  • Up to 5 tons: Valdai/Manipulator (holds ~3-4 pallets / 150-200 m²).
  • Up to 10 tons: KAMAZ (holds ~8-10 pallets / 400-500 m²).
  • Up to 20 tons: Truck (holds ~16-20 pallets / 800-1000 m²).
