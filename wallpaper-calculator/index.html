Online wallpaper calculator: Accurate calculation of the number of rolls, taking into account rapport

Repairs require precision. The most common mistake when buying wallpaper is using the simple formula “divide the area of ​​the walls by the area of ​​the roll.” This method ignores trimming, pattern adjustment (rapport) and technological allowances, which leads either to a shortage of one roll at the most crucial moment, or to the purchase of excess material that cannot be returned.

Our calculator uses "Strip method" — an algorithm used by professional estimators and foremen. It virtually “sticks” wallpaper around the perimeter of your room, taking into account each canvas as a whole, and not abstract square meters.

Basic calculation parameters

To get the correct result, it is important to understand the key terms used in the calculator:

  1. Perimeter and exceptions: We subtract the area of ​​windows and doors, but we do it smartly: if the opening occupies a significant part of the wall, the strips of wallpaper above and below it are calculated separately.
  2. Rapport (drawing step): This is the distance through which the pattern on the wallpaper is repeated. If you choose wallpaper with a large pattern, the material consumption increases, since part of the canvas is cut to match the pattern.
  3. Technological stock (trimming): It is recommended to lay 5–10 cm on each strip to compensate for uneven floors and ceilings.

Calculation formulas

So that you can be sure of the result, we provide the mathematical basis of our algorithm. The calculation is carried out in several stages.

1. Determination of the number of stripes (solid)

First, the perimeter of the $P$ room is calculated and divided by the width of the roll $W_{roll}$ . The result is always rounded up to the nearest whole number.

$$N_{strips} = \lceil \frac{P_{room}}{W_{roll}} \rceil$$

2. Calculation of the working length of the strip

This is a critical stage. The length of one strip $L_{strip}$ consists of the ceiling height $H$ , the trim margin $T$ and the amount of repeat $R$ .

$$L_{strip} = H + T + R$$

3. Number of strips from one roll

We determine how many solid strips can be obtained from one roll of length $L_{roll}$ . The rounding is downwards (the scraps are not glued together).

$$K_{per\_roll} = \lfloor \frac{L_{roll}}{L_{strip}} \rfloor$$

4. Total number of rolls

The total number of strips required is divided by the number of strips from one roll.

$$N_{rolls} = \lceil \frac{N_{strips}}{K_{per\_roll}} \rceil$$

Consumption of related materials

Our tool is not limited to just wallpaper. We also calculate chemistry:

  • Wallpaper glue: Consumption depends on the type of base (paper, vinyl, non-woven fabric). On average, the formula is: $S_{total} \times 0.2$ kg (at a consumption of 200 g/m²).
  • Primer: Mandatory stage of dust removal of walls before gluing. Average consumption: $S_{total} \times 0.15$ kg.

Expert purchasing advice

  • Batch Number: Always check the lot number on the labels. Rolls from different batches may have a subtle difference in shade, which will become disastrously visible on the wall.
  • Wallpaper with rapport: If the pattern pitch is greater than 0 (for example, 64 cm), always take +1 roll to the calculated value for difficult areas (corners, areas above windows).
  • Glue: For non-woven wallpaper, the glue is applied only to the wall, which saves up to 20% of the composition. For paper ones - both on the wall and on the canvas.
