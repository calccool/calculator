Voltage Divider Calculator: Online Resistor and Power Calculator

What is a voltage divider?

A resistive voltage divider is one of the most fundamental circuits in electronics. It is used to step down the input voltage ( $V_{in}$ ) to a certain level ( $V_{out}$ ) using two series resistors.

Our online calculator allows you not only to find the output voltage, but also to solve the inverse problem: select resistance values ​​to obtain the desired voltage.

Calculation formula

The operation of the divider is described by Ohm's law. The basic formula for finding the output voltage looks like this:

$$V_{out} = V_{in} \times \frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2}$$

Where:

  • $V_{in}$ — Input voltage (source voltage).
  • $V_{out}$ — Output voltage (voltage across the lower resistor $R_2$ ).
  • $R_1$ — Resistance of the upper arm (closer to plus).
  • $R_2$ — Resistance of the lower arm (closer to ground/minus).

Calculator operating modes

The tool supports three usage scenarios, making it a universal assistant for circuit design:

  1. Find $V_{out}$ : Classic problem. You know what resistors you have and want to check what voltage you get at the midpoint.
  2. Find $R_1$ : You know the input voltage and the required output. You have already selected one resistor ( $R_2$ ) and are looking for a pair for it.
  3. Find $R_2$ : Similar to the previous one, but select the lower resistor.

Importance of Power Calculation

One of the most common mistakes made by beginners is to ignore the current flowing through the divider. If you choose resistances that are too low (for example, 10 ohms), a large current will flow through them.

Our calculator automatically calculates power dissipation ( $P$ ) on each resistor according to the formula:

$$P = I^2 \times R$$

This will help you choose the right component type:

  • <0.125 W - standard SMD resistors (0805, 1206) or output 1/8 W are suitable.
  • 0.25 W – 0.5 W - larger output resistors are required.
  • > 1 W - powerful ceramic or wirewound resistors are needed (the divider will get hot).
