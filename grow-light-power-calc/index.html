Phytolamp power calculator: calculation of PPFD and DLI for seedlings

Effectively growing plants indoors or on a windowsill is impossible without understanding the physics of light. The usual lumens (Lm) and luxes (Lx) that we are used to in everyday life show brightness to the human eye, but are almost useless for photosynthesis. Plants need photons of a certain spectrum - PAR (Photosynthetically Active Radiation).

Basic terms and units of measurement

To ensure your calculation is as accurate as possible, the calculator uses three key parameters:

  1. PPFD (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density): The number of micromoles of light falling on 1 m² of surface per second ( $\mu mol/m^2/s$ ). This is an "instant serving" of leaf food.
  2. DLI (Daily Light Integral): The total amount of light received by the plant during the entire daylight hours ( $mol/m^2/day$ ). This is the most important indicator for productivity.
  3. PPE (Photosynthetic Flux Efficiency): An indicator of how efficiently the lamp converts electricity into photons useful for plants ( $\mu mol/J$ ).

Mathematical calculation model

Our calculator works based on fundamental lighting engineering formulas. You can check the calculations manually using the following dependencies:

1. Calculation of DLI (Daily Light Integral)

To determine the daily norm, the formula is used:

$$DLI = \frac{PPFD \times 3600 \times T_{hours}}{1,000,000}$$

Where $T_{hours}$ is the duration of daylight hours in hours.

2. Calculation of required power (W)

To understand how many watts your LED board should draw from an outlet, we take into account area ( $S$ ), reflection loss, and diode efficiency ( $PPE$ ):

$$P_{watts} = \frac{S \times PPFD}{PPE \times (1 — L_{loss})}$$

Where $L_{loss}$ is the loss coefficient (for example, 0.2 for a grow box with high-quality Mylar coating).

Why is accurate power calculation important?

Using phytolamps “by eye” often leads to two problems:

  • Light burn: Too high an intensity destroys chlorophyll, the edges of the leaves turn yellow and dry.
  • Pulling seedlings: With a deficiency of photons, the stem becomes thin and weak, as the plant spends all its energy searching for a light source.

How to use the calculator

  1. Select mode: You can specify the length and width of the shelf or directly enter the total area in square meters.
  2. Specify growth stage: Select a plant type from the list or enter a custom PPFD value.
  3. Set up photoperiod: Indicate how many hours a day the lamp will work.
  4. Fine tuning (Advanced): If you know the characteristics of your diodes (for example, Samsung LM301B have a high PPE ~2.7), indicate this to get the most accurate result on power consumption.

Using a professional calculation will allow you not only to get strong seedlings, but also to optimize energy costs, avoiding the purchase of overly powerful equipment.
