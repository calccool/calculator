Cardiac Ejection Fraction (EF) Calculator: Calculation by Teicholtz and Volumes

What is ejection fraction and why calculate it?

Ejection Fraction (EF) is one of the most important indicators of heart health. It shows how efficiently the left ventricle pumps blood with each contraction. This parameter is measured as a percentage and is the gold standard for diagnosing heart failure.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly interpret echocardiography (cardiac ultrasound) or MRI data using two main calculation methods.

Calculation methods

In cardiology, different approaches are used to calculate EF depending on the available data.

1. Calculation by volume (Classic)

This method is the most accurate. It is used if the doctor's report indicates ventricular volumes.

Formula:

$$EF = \frac{EDV — ESV}{EDV} \times 100\%$$

Where:

  • EDV (KDO) - End-diastolic volume (the amount of blood in the ventricle before the beat).
  • ESV (CSR) - End systolic volume (the amount of blood remaining after a stroke).

The difference $(EDV — ESV)$ is called Stroke Volume (SV).

2. Teichholz method

Often, ultrasound reports indicate only the linear dimensions (diameters) of the ventricle. The Teicholtz method allows one to approximately convert these diameters into volumes and calculate the ejection fraction.

Teicholz volume formula:

$$V = \frac{7 \times D^3}{2.4 + D}$$

Where $D$ is the ventricular diameter (VDR or VSD).

How to use the calculator?

  1. Select method:
    • If you have values ​​in milliliters (ml), select "By volume".
    • If you have values ​​in millimeters (mm) from the ultrasound report, select "According to Teicholz".
  2. Enter details:
    • For volumes: EDV (End-Diastolic Volume) and CSO (End-Systolic Volume).
    • For Teicholz: CDR (LVIDd) and CSR (LVIDs).
  3. Get the result: The script will calculate the ejection percentage, stroke volume and show the status (Normal or Pathological).

Important: A low ejection fraction does not always mean a death sentence. Modern therapy can significantly improve this indicator. The results of the calculator are for informational purposes only; To make a diagnosis, consult a cardiologist.
