VO₂Max calculator: Calculation based on the results of the Beep test (Leger test)

This tool allows you to instantly determine your VO₂Max (maximum oxygen consumption) based on the results of a popular field endurance test - Beep Test (also known as the Legere test, Multi-Stage Fitness Test or 20 meter shuttle run).

What is VO₂Max and why should you know it?

VO₂Max (MPC) is the “gold standard” of aerobic training. The indicator reflects the maximum volume of oxygen (in milliliters) that your body can consume and use in one minute per kilogram of body weight ( $мл/кг/мин$ ).

The higher this indicator, the more efficiently your heart pumps blood and your muscles absorb oxygen. Knowing your IPC is necessary for:

  • 🏃 Assessments of current physical fitness.
  • 📈 Track your training progress.
  • ❤️ Monitoring the health of the cardiovascular system.
  • 👮 Preparation for standards in law enforcement agencies and sports sections.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, you will need data obtained after the test is completed (when you were no longer able to reach the line before the beep).

  1. Gender and Age: Necessary for a correct assessment of your level (standards for 20-year-olds and 40-year-olds are different).
  2. Level: The last level you have completely completed (announced by an audio recording of the test).
  3. Shuttles: The number of runs (shuttles) completed in the last unfinished level.

Calculation formula

Our calculator uses the classic formula developed by Professor Luc Léger in 1988. The calculation is carried out in two stages:

  1. Definition of final speed ( $V$ ): $$V = 8.5 + 0.5 \times (Level — 1)$$ Where speed is measured in km/h.
  2. VO₂Max calculation: $$VO_2Max = -23.4 + 5.8 \times V$$

Note: This formula is standard for adults (over 18 years of age). For children and adolescents, more complex coefficients are used that take into account biological age.

How to improve your score in Beep Test?

To improve your shuttle running performance and increase your VO₂Max, include in your training plan:

  • Interval running: Alternating between high and low intensity (e.g. fartlek or Tabata protocol).
  • Long crosses: Run at a slow pace (zone 2) for at least 40-60 minutes to develop basic endurance.
  • U-turn training: In the Beep Test, a lot of energy is wasted on braking and accelerating every 20 meters. Practice your quick turnaround technique.
