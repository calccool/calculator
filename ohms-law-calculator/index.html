Physical meaning and basic formulas

Ohm's law for a circuit section is a fundamental principle of electrical engineering that states that current is directly proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to the resistance of the conductor. In conjunction with the electric power formula, a closed mathematical system of four interconnected variables is formed.

Basic calculation formulas embedded in the core of the calculator:

$$I = \frac{U}{R}$$

$$U = I \cdot R$$

$$R = \frac{U}{I}$$

To calculate electrical power, the following derivative equations are used:

$$P = U \cdot I$$

$$P = I^2 \cdot R$$

$$P = \frac{U^2}{R}$$

Where $I$ is the current, $U$ is the electrical voltage, $R$ is the electrical resistance, and $P$ is the power consumption.

Areas of application of calculations

Accurate electrical circuit calculations are critical to ensuring equipment safety and performance. Our calculator helps solve the following practical problems:

  • Selection of cable cross-section: Knowing the power of the connected device and the network voltage, you can calculate the current strength and select a wire of the required diameter to avoid overheating.
  • Selection of power supplies: calculation of the required adapter power for LED strips, microcontrollers or radio equipment.
  • Electronics repair: calculating the value of a burnt resistor based on the voltage drop and operating current in the circuit.
  • Circuit design: matching load resistances with power supplies to prevent short circuits.

It is important to remember that the classical Ohm's law in its presented form is valid for direct current circuits, as well as for alternating current circuits containing exclusively active resistance (for example, incandescent lamps or heating elements). When working with reactive loads (motors, transformers, capacitors), impedance and phase shift must be taken into account.
