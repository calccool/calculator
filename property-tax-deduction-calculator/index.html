Property deduction calculator: calculating personal income tax refund when buying a home and mortgage

Property tax deduction is a form of government support that allows citizens of the Russian Federation to return part of the paid income tax (NDFL) after purchasing residential real estate. Essentially, the state returns you 13% of the cost of housing and interest paid on the loan, but within strictly established limits.

Only tax residents of the Russian Federation who have official income from which personal income tax is withheld (for example, wages, income from renting out property, etc.) have the right to deduction.

Main components of the deduction

  • Deduction for the purchase of housing: 13% of the cost of the object (apartment, house, room or share) is returned.
  • Interest deduction: 13% of the amount actually paid to the bank on interest on the mortgage loan is returned.
  • Deduction for finishing: if the housing was purchased in a “rough finish” state, the costs of materials and work can be included in the total cost of the property.

Limits and refund amounts

The law limits the maximum amount from which a refund can be made. Even if the apartment costs 20 million rubles, the calculation will be made based on the established “ceiling”.

Maximum amounts per person

Property deduction for spouses

If real estate was acquired during marriage after January 1, 2014, both spouses are entitled to receive the deduction in full, regardless of who the documents are registered to. This allows you to actually double family limits:

  • For the purchase of housing: up to 520 000 $ for two.
  • For mortgage interest: up to 780 000 $ for two.
  • Total family benefit: up to 1 300 000 $.

Formulas for calculating deductions

The calculation of the refund amount is based on comparing the cost of the object with the established limit.

1. Formula for the main deduction

The refund amount for the purchase ( $R_{base}$ ) is calculated as follows:

$$R_{base} = \min(S, L) \times 0.13$$

Where:

  • $S$ - cost of housing under contract;
  • $L$ - legal limit (RUB 2,000,000 per person).

2. Interest deduction formula

For mortgage interest ( $R_{int}$ ) a similar approach applies:

$$R_{int} = \min(I, L_{int}) \times 0.13$$

Where:

  • $I$ - amount of interest actually paid;
  • $L_{int}$ - interest limit (RUB 3,000,000).

3. Tax refund deadline

It is important to understand that you will not be able to receive the entire amount at once if your annual personal income tax is less than the deduction amount. The balance is carried forward to subsequent years until paid in full.

$$T = \frac{R_{total}}{W \times 12 \times 0.13}$$

Where $T$ is the number of years, $W$ is your official monthly salary.

Required documents for submission to the Federal Tax Service

After making the calculations, you can start processing documents. You will need:

  • Passport and TIN;
  • Sales and purchase agreement or equity participation agreement (DPA);
  • Extract from the Unified State Register or acceptance certificate;
  • Payment documents (checks, receipts, receipts);
  • Certificate of income (formerly 2-NDFL) - if you submit through a declaration;
  • Marriage certificate (when distributing deductions between spouses).

You can apply for a deduction through the taxpayer’s personal account on the Federal Tax Service website, which is much faster and simpler than traditional paper filing.
