How to find the equation of a line given a point and slope?

In analytical geometry, there are several ways to define a straight line. One of the most common and convenient is to use slope (slope, denoted as $m$ ) and the coordinates of one known points ( $x_1, y_1$ ) through which this line passes.

Our calculator performs this task automatically, converting the data into a slope equation ( $y = mx + b$ ) and a general line equation ( $Ax + By + C = 0$ ).

Basic formula

To create an equation, use the formula of a straight line passing through a point with a given slope:

$$y — y_1 = m(x — x_1)$$

Where:

  • $x, y$ — variable coordinates of any point on a line.
  • $x_1, y_1$ — coordinates of a known point.
  • $m$ — angular coefficient (tangent of the angle of inclination of the straight line to the $X$ axis).

Step-by-step solution algorithm

Let's say we have a point $A(4, 2)$ and a slope $m = 3$ . How to find the equation of a line manually?

  1. Substitute the values ​​into the formula: Replace $x_1$ , $y_1$ and $m$ with our numbers: $$y — 2 = 3(x — 4)$$
  2. Expand the brackets: Multiply the slope by each term in the bracket on the right: $$y — 2 = 3x — 12$$
  3. Express $y$ (reduce to $y = mx + b$ ):Move $-2$ to the right side of the equation, changing the sign: $$y = 3x — 12 + 2$$ $$y = 3x — 10$$

Now we know that the equation of our line is: $y = 3x — 10$, where is the free term $b = -10$ (the point of intersection with the axis $Y$ ).

Forms of writing the equation of a line

Our tool outputs the result in the two most popular formats. Understanding the difference between them is important for solving geometric problems.

1. Equation with slope (Slope-Intercept Form)

The most convenient view for plotting charts.

$$y = mx + b$$

  • $m$ — slope (steepness). If $m > 0$ , the graph is growing; if $m < 0$ , decreases.
  • $b$ — point of intersection with the $Y$ axis (Y-intercept).

2. General equation of a line (General Form)

Often used in algebra and computer graphics, as it allows you to describe even vertical lines.

$$Ax + By + C = 0$$

To get this view from the example above ( $y = 3x — 10$ ), you need to move all the terms to one side:

$$3x — y — 10 = 0$$

Frequently Asked Questions

How to find $m$ if only two points are given?

If you don't have a slope, but have two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ , first calculate $m$ using the formula:

$$m = \frac{y_2 — y_1}{x_2 — x_1}$$

Then use the resulting $m$ and any of the points in our calculator.

What is a free member of $b$ ?

This is the value of $y$ when $x = 0$ . Geometrically, this is the point at which the graph intersects the vertical coordinate axis. Calculated as:

$$b = y_1 — m \cdot x_1$$
