Calculator for calculating the number of diffusers and grilles: Selection of air distributors

Correct calculation of air distribution devices (ADUs) is a balance between comfort and budget. If too few diffusers are installed, the flow rate will increase, resulting in noise and drafts. If there are too many, the cost of the system and the complexity of installation will increase unjustifiably.

Our online calculator helps engineers and installers quickly determine the optimal number of air supply or exhaust points, taking into account the geometry of the grille and the effective area factor.

How does the calculation work?

The calculation algorithm is based on the flow continuity equation. To find out the number of devices ( $N$ ), it is necessary to divide the total air flow by the throughput of one diffuser at a given permissible speed.

Basic formulas used in the calculator:

1. Effective cross-sectional area ( $A_{eff}$ )

Unlike geometric area, effective area takes into account only the space through which air actually passes (minus frames, slats and valves).

$$A_{eff} = A_{geo} \times K$$

Where:

  • $A_{geo}$ - geometric area (for round: $\pi r^2$ , for rectangular: $W \times H$ );
  • $K$ - open section coefficient (usually 0.7–0.8).

2. Bandwidth of one device ( $Q_1$ )

$$Q_1 = V_{max} \times A_{eff} \times 3600$$

Where:

  • $V_{max}$ - maximum permissible air speed (m/s);
  • $3600$ - conversion factor from m³/s to m³/h.

3. Total quantity ( $N$ )

$$N = \lceil \frac{Q_{total}}{Q_1} \rceil$$

The result is always rounded up to the nearest whole number.

Recommended air speeds (V)

The speed of air exiting the diffuser is a critical parameter. Exceeding these values ​​will lead to aerodynamic noise, which cannot be drowned out by noise suppressors (since it occurs directly at the exit to the room).

Why is it important to consider the open section coefficient (K)?

Many simple calculators calculate the diffuser area as the area of ​​a circle ( $S = \pi R^2$ ), ignoring the design. However, any anemostat or grille has a frame and blinds that cover from 20% to 40% of the clearance.

  • Conventional diffusers (DPU): $K \approx 0.75$
  • Perforated gratings: $K \approx 0.5 — 0.6$
  • Slot diffusers: $K \approx 0.65$
  • Mesh grilles: $K \approx 0.85$

In our calculator you can manually set the parameter "Living section", in order to obtain a result as close as possible to the actual aerodynamic tests of the manufacturers.
