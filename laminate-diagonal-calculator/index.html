Calculator for calculating laminate flooring when laying diagonally

Diagonal laying of flooring is a popular design technique that allows you to visually expand the space and hide the geometric imperfections of the walls. However, this installation method requires a more complex mathematical approach than the classic direct layout, since the number of trims increases significantly.

Our online calculator automates this process, eliminating manual calculations and the risk of running out of material in the middle of a renovation.

Why is the consumption higher when diagonal?

When installing at a 45 degree angle, each board adjacent to the wall is cut at an angle. Unlike straight laying, where a cut piece can often become the beginning of the next row, with a diagonal pattern, most of the triangular trim goes to waste.

Mathematical formula for calculation

To determine the final volume of purchases, we use an algorithm that takes into account the net area and the technological stock ratio:

$$S_{total} = S_{room} \times (1 + \frac{K_{waste}}{100})$$

Where:

  • $S_{total}$ - final purchase area;
  • $S_{room}$ - actual floor area;
  • $K_{waste}$ - percentage of margin for trimming (from 10% to 20%).

After receiving the total area, the system calculates the number of packages:

$$Packs = \lceil \frac{S_{total}}{L \times W \times N} \rceil$$

where $L$ is the length of the board, $W$ is the width, $N$ is the number of pieces in the pack.

Recommended margin for trimming

The choice of reserve percentage depends on the complexity of the room configuration and your experience as a specialist.

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

  1. Select input mode: If you know the exact floor area, switch to Room Area mode. If not, enter the length and width of the walls in meters.
  2. Specify laminate parameters: Enter the data from the packaging (length and width of the strip in mm). You can also use the quick selection buttons for standard sizes.
  3. Set the quantity in the pack: Usually it is 7, 8 or 10 pieces. This is critical for the correct formation of estimates.
  4. Set the stock percentage: For the diagonal, we recommend leaving it at 15%.
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will instantly display the number of packages, the actual area purchased, and even show how many whole boards will remain in stock.

Expert installation advice

  • Acclimatization: Before installation, the laminate must be left in the room for at least 48 hours to equalize the humidity.
  • Direction: The diagonal is usually drawn from the far corner of the room to the exit or from the window so that the light falls along long joints.
  • Party: Always check the lot number on the packages. The same item from different batches may have micro-differences in shade or locking system.
