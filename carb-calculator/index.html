Carbohydrate calculator: Online calculation of daily requirements

Carbohydrates are the main “fuel” for the human body. Not only your weight, but also your energy level, brain function and hormonal levels depend on the correct calculation of their consumption. Our carbohydrate calculator uses scientifically proven formulas to determine your individual needs, taking into account body parameters, physical activity and chosen nutrition strategy.

Why count carbs?

Many people mistakenly believe that to lose weight you need to completely eliminate carbohydrates. This is a myth. Carbohydrates are needed for:

  • Energy: Glucose is the main source of nutrition for the brain and central nervous system.
  • Muscle preservation: When there is a carbohydrate deficiency, the body begins to break down muscle tissue for energy (gluconeogenesis).
  • Metabolism: Carbohydrates are involved in the metabolism of fats and proteins.

The main secret is the balance and quality of the nutrients consumed.

How the calculation algorithm works

Our tool is based on a two-step calculation. First, your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) are determined, and then the weight of your chosen diet is applied.

Basic Metabolism Formula (Mifflin-St. George)

This is the most accurate formula to date for calculating calories:

$$BMR_{муж} = (10 \times Вес) + (6.25 \times Рост) — (5 \times Возраст) + 5$$

$$BMR_{жен} = (10 \times Вес) + (6.25 \times Рост) — (5 \times Возраст) — 161$$

Where:

  • Weight in kg
  • Height in cm
  • Age in years

The resulting value is multiplied by activity factor (from 1.2 to 1.9) and is adjusted depending on your goal (weight loss, maintenance or gain).

Types of Carbohydrate Strategies

In the calculator you can select the type of diet that directly affects the percentage of carbohydrates in the diet:

  1. Keto/Low Carb (10-15%): Strict limitation. The body enters a state of ketosis by using fats as energy. Suitable for quick “drying”, but requires medical supervision.
  2. Moderate LowCarb (25%): A mild option for weight loss. Allows you to control insulin levels without completely giving up side dishes.
  3. Balanced (45-50%): WHO gold standard. Optimal for most healthy people maintaining weight.
  4. High carb (60%+): Necessary for professional athletes (marathon runners, swimmers, crossfit) to replenish glycogen reserves.

Complex and simple carbohydrates: the 80/20 rule

The calculator not only gives the total number, but also divides it into complex and simple carbohydrates.

  • Complex (slow) carbohydrates: Should form the basis of the diet (about 80%). They take a long time to digest, give you satiety and do not cause sudden spikes in sugar.
  • Simple (fast) carbohydrates: Acceptable in amounts up to 20% (fruits, honey, sweets). They are best consumed after training for quick recovery.

Frequent errors in calculations

  1. Ignoring fiber. Vegetables contain carbohydrates, but most of them are indigestible fiber, which can not be counted in the total caloric intake.
  2. Fear of the evening. Carbohydrates eaten after 18:00 do not automatically turn into fat. Only the overall daily balance is important.
  3. Calculation for the finished product. Remember that cereals and pasta absorb water when cooked and increase in weight by 2-3 times. 100 g of dry buckwheat = ~300 g of boiled buckwheat, but the amount of carbohydrates (72 g) remains unchanged.
