Many trophy hunters are faced with the fact that the time indicated in the guides on Stratege or PowerPyx does not coincide with reality. Guides are usually written by experts who know the shortest paths, while the average player spends time exploring the world, mistakes and random events.

Our smart calculator allows you to adapt the dry numbers from the “basic forecasts” to your personal playing style and the specific features of the project.

How the forecasting algorithm works

The calculator is based on a multifactor model that adjusts the base time from the guides through a system of coefficients. We take into account not only the mechanical complexity, but also the architecture of the achievements themselves.

Mathematical calculation model

The following formula is used to calculate the total time:

$$T_{total} = (T_{guide} \times K_{skill}) + \sum T_{modifiers}$$

Where:

  • $T_{guide}$ - base time to complete the guide in hours.
  • $K_{skill}$ - skill coefficient (from 0.75 for pros to 1.3 for beginners).
  • $T_{modifiers}$ - additional time costs for specific conditions.

The forecast number of days $D$ is calculated as:

$$D = \frac{T_{total}}{H_{play}}$$

Where $H_{play}$ is your average gaming session per day.

Missable Trophies

Having missable achievements +15% of the time is one of the most insidious factors. It forces the player to constantly check the guide, which slows down natural progression. In some cases, missing one trophy may require a full second playthrough (New Game+).

Tips for optimizing your path to Platinum

  1. Examine the trophy map: Check for missable prizes first so you don't have to replay the 50-hour campaign.
  2. Combine tasks: If the game has an online component, start with it while the servers are active and there is “live” online.
  3. Use cloud saves: In games with multiple endings, this can save up to 40% of time by eliminating replays.
  4. Evaluate RNG soberly: If platinum requires a rare drop (for example, in the Souls or MMORPG series), put the maximum “Strong Grind” factor into the calculator.
