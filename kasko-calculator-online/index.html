Mathematical model for calculating insurance premiums

The cost of the policy is not determined randomly. It is based on a strict mathematical formula that takes into account the likelihood of an insured event occurring.

The basic calculation formula is as follows:

$$P = V \cdot T_b \cdot K_s \cdot K_c \cdot K_f$$

Where:

  • $P$ — final insurance premium (policy cost);
  • $V$ - the market value of the car at the time of insurance;
  • $T_b$ - the basic tariff of the insurance company (depending on the year of manufacture of the car);
  • $K_s$ - coefficient of age and experience of drivers;
  • $K_c$ — coverage type coefficient (full CASCO or only “Damage”);
  • $K_f$ - deductible coefficient (discount for the insurer's participation in the loss).

Key factors influencing price

  1. Market value of the car: Main parameter. The more expensive the car, the higher the potential payout in case of total or theft.
  2. Vehicle age: For new cars (0–1 year) rates are minimal. For cars older than 5 years, tariffs are rising, as the cost of spare parts for repairs remains high, and the market price of the car is decreasing.
  3. Drivers experience: Young drivers (up to 22 years old) with up to 3 years of experience are considered the most risky category, which can increase the cost of the policy by 1.5–1.8 times.
  4. Franchise size: The most effective legal way to save.

How to reduce the cost of CASCO without losing the quality of protection

If the final figure in the calculator seems high to you, use the following expert recommendations:

  • Apply franchise: Setting a franchise in the amount of 15–30 thousand rubles reduces the price of the policy more significantly than any seasonal promotion.
  • Limit the list of drivers: The “Multidrive” option (without restrictions) is the most expensive. Enter only those who will actually be driving.
  • Select only “Damage”: If your car is stored in a secure parking lot and is not among the top 10 most stolen models, eliminating the Theft risk will save you 10-15%.
  • Install telematics: Many insurance companies offer discounts of up to 20% when installing equipment that monitors careful driving.
