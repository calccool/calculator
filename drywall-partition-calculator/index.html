Plasterboard partition calculator: Calculation of profile and plasterboard (Knauf)

Installing an interior partition made of plasterboard (GCR) is the fastest way to zone a space. However, the main mistake of beginners and even experienced craftsmen is the incorrect purchase of materials. An error in calculations leads either to work downtime due to a lack of a couple of profiles, or to unnecessary spending on leftovers, which then have nowhere to go.

Our calculator is developed based on system technological maps Knauf (S111, S112). It takes into account not only the “clean” wall area, but also technological reserves for trimming, the configuration of doorways and the multiplicity of materials in factory packaging.

How the calculation algorithm works

The calculations are based on the geometry of frame-sheathing structures. The calculation is carried out in three stages: determining the sheathing area, the configuration of the metal frame and counting consumables.

1. Calculation of drywall (gypsum board)

The number of sheets depends on the area of ​​the partition minus the doorway and the number of layers of cladding. The professional standard involves 2 layers of sheathing for better sound insulation and rigidity.

Formula for calculating the net cladding area ( $S_{gkl}$ ):

$$S_{gkl} = ((L \times H) — (W_{door} \times H_{door})) \times 2 \times N_{layers}$$

Where:

  • $L$ - partition length.
  • $H$ - ceiling height.
  • $W_{door}, H_{door}$ - dimensions of the doorway.
  • $N_{layers}$ - number of layers (1 or 2).

2. Calculation of metal frame

The frame consists of two types of profiles:

  • Mon (UW) — Guide profile. Attaches to the floor and ceiling.
  • PS (CW) — Rack profile. Installed vertically in increments of 600 mm (or 400 mm under the tile).

Formula for calculating the number of rack profiles ( $N_{ps}$ ):

$$N_{ps} = \lceil \frac{L}{0.6} \rceil + 1 + K_{door}$$

Where $K_{door}$ are additional reinforced posts for framing the doorway (usually 2 pieces).

Features of the selection of components

When using the calculator, pay attention to the following parameters that affect the final cost and strength of the structure:

Profile thickness

  • 50mm: Economy option. Suitable only for decorative partitions of small height. Sound insulation is minimal.
  • 75 mm: Golden mean. Allows you to lay a layer of mineral wool 50 mm with an air gap or 75 mm tightly.
  • 100 mm: Capital partition. Used for high walls (more than 3 meters) or when sewer pipes (50-90 mm in diameter) need to be hidden inside.

Number of skin layers

  • 1 layer: Allowed only in low traffic areas. When hit, the wall makes a booming sound (“drum”), making it difficult to hang heavy shelves on it.
  • 2 layers: Standard for quality repairs. The overlap of the joints of the second layer overlaps the joints of the first, which eliminates the appearance of cracks. Sound insulation and load-bearing capacity are significantly increased (you can hang TVs and cabinets on special dowels).

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Do I need to subtract windows and doors?

Yes, our calculator has the “There is a doorway” function. When activated, the program will automatically subtract the area of ​​the opening from drywall and insulation, but will add a margin of profile to strengthen the door frame.

How much margin does the calculator include?

The algorithm includes the following safety factors for waste (trimming):

  • Drywall: 5%
  • Profiles: calculation is carried out in multiples of whole logs (3 meters), the remains are considered waste.
  • Hardware: 10% (for loss and defects).

What kind of cotton wool should I use for the partition?

For partitions it is recommended to use specialized acoustic mineral wool. Unlike facade thermal insulation, it has a special fiber structure that effectively dampens sound waves. The calculator calculates the area of ​​insulation equal to the area of ​​the wall with a small margin for dense installation.
