Oil and gasoline proportion calculator for 2-stroke engines (2T)

Two-stroke engines (trimmers, chainsaws, lawn mowers) do not have a separate lubrication system. The oil is mixed directly with gasoline, and it is this mixture that is responsible for the life of the cylinder-piston group. A mistake “by eye” is the shortest path to engine jamming or the formation of a thick layer of carbon deposits.

Our tool allows you to instantly calculate the amount of additive using both standard factory settings (1:50, 1:40) and individual parameters for running-in equipment.

Basic proportions and their purpose

Manufacturers of equipment (Stihl, Husqvarna, Makita) and oils indicate different data. To avoid confusion, be guided by the following standards:

  • 1:50 (2%) - the gold standard for modern branded equipment when using high-quality synthetics or semi-synthetics.
  • 1:40 (2.5%) - often found in recommendations for Chinese trimmers and chainsaws, as well as when working in difficult conditions.
  • 1:25 (4%) — “Run-in” mode. An increased oil content is necessary during the first 2–3 tanks of operation of a new engine for grinding in parts.

Mathematical formula for calculation

For those who are used to checking everything on their own, the calculator performs calculations using the following formula:

$$V_{\text{oil}} = \frac{V_{\text{gas}} \cdot 1000}{R}$$

Where:

  • $V_{\text{oil}}$ - oil volume in milliliters (ml);
  • $V_{\text{gas}}$ - volume of gasoline in liters (l);
  • $R$ - proportion value (the second number in the ratio, for example, 40 or 50).

Expert tips for preparing the mixture

For equipment to serve for years, it is not enough just to know the numbers. Please pay attention to the important nuances of operating 2T motors:

Choice of gasoline and oil

Use only fresh gasoline with an octane rating of at least AI-92. The oil must be labeled 2T (usually JASO FD or ISO-L-EGD standards). Never fill in oil intended for 4-stroke engines (automotive) - it does not burn completely and clogs the outlet port.

Mixing order

  1. Pour half the required amount of gasoline into a clean canister.
  2. Add the full portion of oil calculated by our calculator.
  3. Shake the mixture.
  4. Add the remaining gasoline and stir again.

Shelf life of the finished mixture

Important: Do not store diluted gasoline for longer than 2-3 weeks. Over time, the oil in gasoline begins to oxidize and lose its lubricating properties. Running on the “old” mixture often leads to engine overheating.

Visual process control

Pay attention to the visualization block in our calculator. It clearly demonstrates how a drop of oil dissolves into the fuel base, helping you better understand the physics of the process. If you see that the mixture is too dark, check the proportion settings again.
