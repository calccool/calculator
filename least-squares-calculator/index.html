Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Calculator: Linear Regression with Graph and Solution

This tool is a professional linear regression calculator, which allows you to find the equation of a straight line that best describes the relationship between a set of points $(x, y)$ . The least squares method (LSM) minimizes the sum of the squares of the vertical deviations of points from the desired line.

This tool is indispensable for:

  • Statistical data analysis and search for trends.
  • Solutions to problems in econometrics and higher mathematics.
  • Forecasting future values ​​based on historical sampling.

Calculator features

Unlike simple analogues, this service conducts a complete statistical analysis of the sample:

  4. Forecasting: Allows you to enter an arbitrary value of $X$ and get the predicted value of $Y$ based on the constructed model.

Calculation formulas

To find the equation of the line $y = mx + b$ , the following mathematical formulas are used. Let $n$ be the number of data pairs.

1. Slope (m)

Determines the slope of the line. Calculated through the covariance of variables:

$$m = \frac{n \sum (xy) — \sum x \sum y}{n \sum x^2 — (\sum x)^2}$$

2. Free member (b)

Point of intersection of the straight line with the Y axis (Y-intercept):

$$b = \frac{\sum y — m \sum x}{n}$$

3. Correlation coefficient (r)

Shows the strength and direction of a linear relationship (from -1 to 1):

$$r = \frac{n \sum (xy) — \sum x \sum y}{\sqrt{[n \sum x^2 — (\sum x)^2][n \sum y^2 — (\sum y)^2]}}$$

4. Coefficient of determination ( $R^2$ )

Shows how well the model fits the data (the closer to 1, the more accurate the model):

$$R^2 = r^2$$
