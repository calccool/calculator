Child development calculator: WHO standards for height, weight and skills (Online tracker)

Why track your child's physical development?

Every parent asks the question: “Are we growing normally?” Physical development is one of the main indicators of a child’s health. Our interactive development tracker allows you to check your child's parameters in seconds against the reference data of the World Health Organization (WHO Child Growth Standards).

Unlike outdated tables, this tool takes into account not just the "average" value, but centile corridors. This helps to understand whether the child is in the golden mean, or whether there is a tendency towards under/overweight.

How does the calculator work and what are centiles?

The algorithm uses a database of anthropometric indicators for boys and girls. The result is given not just as a number, but as a status relative to the norm.

  • Median (50%) - ideal average value.
  • Normal corridor - the range of values ​​in which most healthy children fall.
  • Deviations — signals that you should pay attention to (for example, high BMI or low height).

Main assessment parameters

Our tool analyzes three key indicators:

  1. Weight estimation. Allows you to eliminate malnutrition (underweight) or the risk of obesity.
  2. Growth assessment. An important marker of hormonal health and proper nutrition.
  3. BMI (Body Mass Index). For children it is calculated differently than for adults, and strictly depends on age.

Formula for calculating BMI (BMI)

Body mass index is a basic indicator of harmonious development. To calculate it, the standard international formula is used:

$$BMI = \frac{m}{h^2}$$

Where:

  • $m$ - body weight (weight) in kilograms.
  • $h$ - height (height) in meters.

However, the resulting number for a child cannot be compared with adult norms (where 25 is already an excess). Our calculator automatically interprets the resulting $BMI$ according to pediatric age tables.

Skills calendar: What should a child be able to do?

In addition to physical parameters, our tracker tells psychomotor norms (Milestones). These are control points for the development of the nervous system.

  • 0–3 months: Holding the head, “revitalization complex”, following with the gaze.
  • 4–6 months: Roll onto your stomach, lean on straight arms, first syllables.
  • 7–9 months: Crawling (on the belly or on all fours), sitting without support, pincer grip.
  • 10–12 months: Standing at the support, the first steps, conscious words (“mother”, “give”).

Important: All children develop individually. A deviation in mastering a skill of 1-2 months is often normal, but a significant delay is a reason to consult a neurologist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are WHO standards better than domestic tables?

WHO standards are based on a study of healthy children who are predominantly breastfed, which eliminates the excessive norms of weight gain characteristic of children on artificial formula of the previous generation.

What to do if the calculator shows “Below normal”?

A one-time measurement is not always indicative. Dynamics are important. If the child is active, eats and sleeps well, a slight deviation may be a constitutional feature. However, if weight or height is significantly outside the normal range, consultation with a pediatrician or endocrinologist is recommended.
