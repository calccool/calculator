Sleep calculator: How much sleep do you need to get enough sleep? Sleep phases

Briefly about the main thing

  • The dream passes cycles of ~90 minutes (allowable range 90–110 min).
  • It's better to wake up at the end of the cycle, and not in the middle.
  • Adults usually have enough 4–6 cycles per night (≈6–9 hours, taking into account the time to fall asleep).
  • Our calculator selects several convenient options time of going to bed/getting up, taking into account your delay in falling asleep.

Why is this even necessary?

Sleep is not a “pause”, but a service to the body: the brain processes information, strengthens the immune system, restores muscles, stabilizes mood and attention.
Both lack of sleep and excess make you feel worse. It is important not only How many, but also When.

(1 cycle ≈ 90 minutes; range - overnight, including night awakenings in children)

These are guidelines. Some people feel energetic on 4-5 cycles, others need “six”.

Important nuances

  • Delay in falling asleep. Allow an honest 10–20 minutes (or more if you take a long time to “unwind”).
  • Chronotype (lark/night owl) affects “comfortable” windows - choose options where it’s easier for you to wake up.
  • Stable schedule (going to bed and getting up at the same time) is more important than single “perfect” nights.
  • Caffeine, screens, late workouts interfere with falling asleep quickly - this breaks the calculation.
  • Daytime nap: better 20–30 minutes (short “power nap”) or ~90 minutes (full cycle), but not “in between”.

Mini-reminder for taking a nap (daytime)

  • 20–30 min — quick recovery without “sleepy inertia.”
  • ~90 min — a full cycle if there is a window.
  • Avoid the “intermediate” 40-60 minutes - there is a high risk of waking up in deep sleep.

FAQ

Why am I tired after the “classic” 8 hours?
Most likely the alarm clock is ringing mid cycle (in deep sleep). Try shifting the time by 10-20 minutes to get to the end of the cycle, or use a calculator - it will select options.

Didn't sleep all night - when to go to bed in the morning?
It's better to close full cycles: ~1.5 hours (1 cycle) or ~3 hours (2 cycles) than 2–2.5 hours. This way there is less “sleepy inertia”.

How many stages of sleep?
Simplified, there are 4: falling asleep, light, deep, REM. They are repeated several times during the night.

If you get up at 05:00, what time do you go to bed?
Taking into account 15 minutes to fall asleep, the following are often suitable:

  • 21:45 (≈5 cycles) or
  • 23:15 (≈4 cycles).
