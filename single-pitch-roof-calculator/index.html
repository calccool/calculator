Shed roof calculator: Calculation of the angle of inclination, length of rafters and cross-section of beams

A shed roof is the most economical and easy-to-install roofing structure, widely used in the construction of garages, verandas, sheds, as well as modern high-tech residential buildings. Despite its apparent simplicity, it requires strict engineering calculations. An error in calculating the angle of inclination or cross-section of the rafters can lead to the roof collapsing under the weight of snow or the roof being torn off by the wind.

Our online engineering calculator allows you to perform a complete thermal and structural calculation of a pitched roof, taking into account climatic standards (SNiP) and the characteristics of the selected material.

Operating principle and calculation formulas

The calculator is based on trigonometric functions and the laws of strength. The program builds a mathematical model of a rafter leg as an inclined beam on two supports.

1. Geometry and angle of inclination

The main parameter is the slope angle ( $\alpha$ ). It depends on the difference in wall heights and the width of the building. The calculation is made through the tangent of the angle:

$$\tan(\alpha) = \frac{H_{rise}}{W_{base}}$$

Where:

  • $H_{rise}$ - lifting height (height difference between supporting walls).
  • $W_{base}$ - base width (span).

The length of the rafter leg (without overhangs) is calculated using the Pythagorean theorem:

$$L_{rafter} = \sqrt{W_{base}^2 + H_{rise}^2}$$

2. Calculation of loads

The calculator sums up permanent and live loads to check the strength of the system:

  • Permanent: Weight of the roofing pie (material + lathing + insulation + rafters).
  • Temporary: Snow load (depending on the region of the Russian Federation) and wind pressure.

Formula for full load per linear meter of rafters ( $Q$ ):

$$Q = (S \cdot \mu + W + G_{mat}) \cdot K_{step}$$

Where:

  • $S$ - standard snow load.
  • $\mu$ - slope coefficient (if the angle is > 60°, snow is not taken into account).
  • $W$ - wind load.
  • $G_{mat}$ - weight of roofing material.
  • $K_{step}$ - rafter installation step.

How to use the calculator

To obtain an accurate estimate and drawing, follow the algorithm:

  1. Dimensions of the building: Specify the span width and the length of the building wall. Don't forget to indicate the desired overhangs (cornice and gable) - they protect the walls from rain.
  2. Lifting height: Set the height by which one wall is higher than the other. If you do not know the height, select it based on the resulting angle of inclination (it is displayed in the results).
  3. Rafter system: Specify the pitch of the rafters (standard - 58-60 cm for insulation) and the type of wood.
  4. Loads: Select your snow and wind region using the SP 20.13330.2016 map. This is critical for calculating the beam section.

Calculation results

After clicking the “Calculate roof” button you will receive a detailed summary:

  • Angle rating: The program will tell you whether the selected angle is suitable for your type of roof (for example, you cannot lay the tiles at 10 degrees - they will leak).
  • Material Specification: The exact number of sheets/packs of covering, volume of lumber for rafters and sheathing, footage of waterproofing.
  • Deflection check: A unique feature of our calculator. We check whether the timber (for example, 50x150 mm) can withstand the snow cap in your region. If the safety margin is small, the system will mark it in red and recommend increasing the section.
  • Drainage system: Calculation of the length of gutters for collecting sediment from the lower slope.

Use the data obtained to purchase materials and control construction crews. The calculations are advisory in nature and are based on GOST and SNiP “Loads and Impacts”.
