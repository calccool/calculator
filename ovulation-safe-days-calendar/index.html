Ovulation and Safe Days Calculator: Conception Calendar (Ogino-Knaus Method)

Calendar method of contraception and pregnancy planning

Understanding your menstrual cycle is key to effective family planning. Our online calendar helps women identify the two most important periods:

  1. Fertile window: Days when the chance of getting pregnant is highest.
  2. Safe days: Periods when conception is theoretically impossible.

The instrument is based on the proven physiological Ogino-Knaus method, which takes into account sperm viability and egg lifespan.

How does ovulation calculation work?

The calculator is based on a standard biological algorithm. The menstrual cycle is divided into phases, the duration of which depends on the individual characteristics of the body, but the phase after ovulation (luteal phase) in most women is stable and lasts 14 days.

Formula for calculating ovulation date

To find the day the egg is released, use the following formula:

$$Day_{ovulation} = Length_{cycle} — 14$$

What is the Fertile Window?

Many people mistakenly believe that you can only get pregnant on the day of ovulation. This is wrong. Fertile window is a period that includes several days before and one day after ovulation.

  • Sperm can live in the female body for up to 5 days while waiting for the egg to be released.
  • Egg only lives 12–24 hours after ovulation.

Thus, the “dangerous” period begins 5 days before ovulation and ends one day after it.

Reliability of the method

The calendar method is effective for women with regular cycle. If your period comes on the same day, the accuracy of the forecast is high.

However, it is worth remembering the factors that shift ovulation:

  • Stress and overwork.
  • Change of climate or time zones.
  • Hormonal imbalances or colds.

Important: This calculator is an informational tool. For 100% protection against unwanted pregnancy, use the methods recommended by your gynecologist, and to accurately determine ovulation - ultrasound monitoring or pharmacy tests.
