Physical meaning and basic formula of pressure

In classical physics, pressure is defined as the ratio of the force acting perpendicular to a surface to the area of ​​that surface.

In the International System of Units (SI), the basic mathematical model is the following formula:

$$P = \frac{F}{S}$$

Where:

  • $P$ — pressure (in Pascals, Pa);
  • $F$ - force applied to the surface (in Newtons, N);
  • $S$ - surface area (in square meters, m²).

Accordingly, $1 \text{ Па} = 1 \text{ Н} / 1 \text{ м}^2$ . However, in practice, using only Pascals is inconvenient due to the huge spread of values ​​in different industries: from ultra-high vacuum to pressure inside stars. For this, multiple prefixes (kPa, MPa) and historically established non-systemic units are used.

Conversion formulas between common systems

If you need to perform calculations manually, without using the interface, you can rely on the following basic transformation equations.

Converting pressure from pounds per square inch (psi) to bars (bar):

$$P_{\text{bar}} = P_{\text{psi}} \times 0.0689476$$

Conversion from standard atmospheres (atm) to kilopascals (kPa):

$$P_{\text{kPa}} = P_{\text{atm}} \times 101.325$$

Convert from millimeters of mercury (mmHg) to Pascals (Pa):

$$P_{\text{Pa}} = P_{\text{mmHg}} \times 133.322387$$
