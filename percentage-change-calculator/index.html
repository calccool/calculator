Percentage Increase and Change Calculator: Online Calculator

Universal interest calculator

In the world of finance, statistics and everyday shopping, knowing how to work with percentages is a key skill. Our online calculator solves the three most popular problems: finds the percentage difference between numbers, adds a given percentage to the amount, or calculates the final discounted price.

Below we will analyze formulas and examples for each calculation mode.

1. How to calculate percentage increase or decrease?

This mode is used when you have two numbers (start and end) and you want to understand how the percentage has changed. This is a classic problem for estimating profit growth, inflation or weight changes.

Formula:

$$\text{Percentage Change} = \frac{V_{final} — V_{initial}}{|V_{initial}|} \times 100\%$$

  • $V_{initial}$ - initial value.
  • $V_{final}$ is the final value.

Calculation examples:

Important: If the result is positive, this is growth. If negative - Decrease.

2. How to add a percentage to a number?

This function is indispensable for business and accounting, for example, when calculating the markup on a product or adding tax (VAT). Instead of calculating the percentage separately and then adding it up, we use a coefficient (multiplier).

Formula:

$$\text{Result} = \text{Value} \times (1 + \frac{P}{100})$$

Example:

You need to add 20% (for example VAT) to the amount of 1000.

$$1000 \times (1 + 0.20) = 1000 \times 1.2 = 1200$$

3. How to subtract a percentage from a number?

An ideal tool for shoppers during sales. Allows you to quickly find out the final price of the product taking into account the discount.

Formula:

$$\text{Result} = \text{Value} \times (1 — \frac{P}{100})$$

Example:

The product costs 5000, the discount is 15%.

$$5000 \times (1 — 0.15) = 5000 \times 0.85 = 4250$$
