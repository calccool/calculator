How does baby gender prediction work?

There are many theories linking the blood types of parents with the gender of the unborn child. One of the most popular is that certain combinations of mother's and father's blood groups create a predisposition to conceiving a child of a certain sex.

Although official medicine claims that the sex of a child depends solely on the sperm (carrier of the X or Y chromosome), statistics from the “folk method” often show surprising coincidences. Our algorithm analyzes the combination of blood groups (I, II, III, IV) and produces a forecast: who is more likely to be expected.

Scientific Calculation: Blood Type Inheritance

Unlike gender, a child's blood type is inherited according to the strict laws of genetics (Mendel's laws). System AB0 determined by the set of antigens that the baby receives from mom and dad.

Inheritance formulas (Genetics)

Blood type is determined by two alleles (one from the mother, one from the father). The $A$ and $B$ alleles are dominant, while the $0$ allele is recessive.

Mathematically, genotypes look like this:

  • Group I (0): Genotype is always $$i^0 i^0$$
  • Group II (A): Genotype $$I^A I^A$$ or $$I^A i^0$$
  • Group III (B): Genotype $$I^B I^B$$ or $$I^B i^0$$
  • Group IV (AB): Genotype is always $$I^A I^B$$

For example, if the mother has group II (heterozygote

$$I^A i^0$$

), and dad has group III (heterozygote

$$I^B i^0$$

), then the probability calculation looks like this:

$$P(Child) = \frac{1}{4} (I^A I^B) + \frac{1}{4} (I^A i^0) + \frac{1}{4} (I^B i^0) + \frac{1}{4} (i^0 i^0)$$

This means that such a couple has a 25% chance of having a child with any from four blood groups.

Rh factor and the risk of conflict

An important part of our calculator is the compatibility assessment by Rh factor ( $Rh$ ). Rh factor is a protein on the surface of red blood cells.

  • Rh+ (positive): there is protein.
  • Rh- (negative): no protein.

Rhesus conflict possible only in one situation:

If the future mother has Rh-negative blood (Rh-), A dad - Rh positive (Rh+), and the child inherits the “plus” from the father.

In this case, the mother's immune system may perceive the fetal red blood cells as foreign. In other cases (if the mother has “+”, or both parents have “-”), there is no risk of conflict.
