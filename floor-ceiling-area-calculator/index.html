How to use the calculator

The tool works in three modes, covering 99% of standard apartment and house layouts.

Step 1: Select Geometry

Select the shape of your room from the drop-down list:

  • Rectangular (Standard) - a classic room with four corners.
  • L-shaped (Complex) - suitable for corridors, kitchens with niches or combined loggias. In this mode, the room is divided into two rectangular sectors.

Step 2: Enter Dimensions

Use a laser tape measure or regular measuring tape. All values ​​are entered in meters (For example,5.4meters).

  • For L-shaped Enter the length and width for each of the two sections separately.

Step 3: Adjustments and estimates

  • Area deduction: If the room has a column, vent, or furnace, enter its area in the Subtract Area box. This will allow you to avoid purchasing material for areas where installation is impossible.
  • Material price: Indicate the cost of one square meter of the selected coating (laminate, tiles, stretch ceiling) to get a final estimate.

Calculation formulas

For transparency of calculations, we present the mathematical algorithm that our tool uses.

Area calculation ( $S$ )

For a standard rectangular room, the basic geometric formula is:

$$S = A \times B$$

Where:

  • $S$ - area ( $м^2$ );
  • $A$ - room length (m);
  • $B$ - room width (m).

For rooms of complex shape (L-shaped), the decomposition method is used (dividing into simple shapes):

$$S_{total} = (A_1 \times B_1) + (A_2 \times B_2) — S_{sub}$$

Where $S_{sub}$ is the area of ​​technical obstacles (deductions).

Perimeter calculation ( $P$ )

The perimeter is necessary to purchase skirting boards, ceiling moldings or damper tape when pouring screed.

$$P = (A + B) \times 2$$

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Are the floor and ceiling areas the same?

In 95% of cases - yes. In typical houses, the walls are vertical, so the floor area is equal to the ceiling area. The exceptions are:

  1. Attic floors: where the ceiling is sloped and its area is larger than the floor area.
  2. Multi-level ceilings: complex plasterboard structures increase the actual surface area for painting.
  3. Curved walls: in the old fund the discrepancy can reach 3-5%.

Why subtract the area of ​​obstacles?

This is critical for expensive materials. For example, if you are laying parquet at a cost of 5,000 rubles/m², and the room has a built-in wardrobe with an area of ​​1.5 m², which you do not plan to dismantle, the deduction will save you 7,500 rubles.

How to count an L-shaped room?

Mentally divide the room into two rectangles. For example, a long corridor (Section 1) and a turn (Section 2). Measure the length and width of each rectangle separately and enter the data in the appropriate fields of the calculator in L-shape mode.
