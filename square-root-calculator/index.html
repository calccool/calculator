What is a square root?

The arithmetic square root of a non-negative number $a$ is a non-negative number $b$ whose square is equal to $a$ . Mathematically it is written like this:

$$\sqrt{a} = b \iff b^2 = a, \quad \text{где } a \ge 0, b \ge 0$$

The operation of extracting the root is the inverse of squaring. The sign of the root is called a radical, and the number $a$ is called a radical expression.

Geometric meaning

The name "square" root has a direct connection with geometry. If we represent the number $a$ as the area of ​​a square ( $S$ ), then $\sqrt{a}$ will be equal to the length of the side of this square. That is why our calculator has visualization through a square - this helps to intuitively understand the essence of the calculations.

Basic properties of square roots

To successfully solve problems, it is important to remember the basic properties of radicals. These identities are used by our algorithm to simplify the answers.

1. Root from the work:

The root of the product of non-negative factors is equal to the product of the roots of these factors:

$$\sqrt{a \cdot b} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$$

2. Root of a fraction:

The root of a fraction is equal to the root of the numerator divided by the root of the denominator:

$$\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}, \quad (b \ne 0)$$

3. Raising the root to a power:

$$(\sqrt{a})^2 = a$$

4. Root of the degree:

$$\sqrt{a^{2n}} = |a^n|$$

Common calculation errors

When working with roots, mistakes are often made related to the domain of definition and signs.

Taking the root of a negative number

In the set of real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ , it is impossible to extract the square root of a negative number, since the square of any real number is non-negative. However, in the complex number set $\mathbb{C}$ it is possible (the result will contain the imaginary unit $i$ ), but our calculator works in the classical real domain used in school and most engineering problems.

Confusion with the sign

The equation $x^2 = 16$ has two roots: $4$ and $-4$ . However, the radical symbol $\sqrt{16}$ only denotes arithmetic (non-negative) root, that is, only $4$ . If both options need to be taken into account, $\pm$ is placed before the root sign.

Estimation method without calculator

If you don’t have a tool at hand, you can estimate the value of the root using the “boundaries” method.

Let's say we need to find $\sqrt{50}$ :

  1. We find the nearest exact squares: $49 (7^2)$ and $64 (8^2)$ .
  2. Since $49 < 50 < 64$ , so is $7 < \sqrt{50} < 8$ .
  3. Since 50 is very close to 49, the answer will be slightly more than 7 (about 7.07).
