Mathematical justification of the cycle

Why is the number of Friday the 13th limited to three? It's all about the structure of the Gregorian calendar. The distribution of days of the week depends on whether the year is a leap year and what day of the week it begins on.

Probability formula

To determine the day of the week of any number, an algorithm similar to Ziller's method is used. If we designate the day of the week as $W$ , then the formula for the 13th of the month is as follows:

$$W = (13 + \lfloor \frac{13(M+1)}{5} \rfloor + K + \lfloor \frac{K}{4} \rfloor + \lfloor \frac{J}{4} \rfloor — 2J) \pmod 7$$

Where:

  • $M$ - month number (March = 3, ..., February = 14);
  • $K$ - last two digits of the year;
  • $J$ - century (first two digits of the year).

If the result is $W = 6$ (in some variations 5, depending on the starting point), then the 13th is Friday.

Interesting facts about the calendar

  1. Cyclicity: Every 400 years the calendar repeats itself completely. Within this cycle, Friday the 13th occurs slightly more often than any other day of the week on the 13th.
  2. Minimum and maximum: Over a 28-year cycle, the number of Friday the 13th is distributed evenly, but never drops to zero.
  3. Long breaks: The maximum period between two Friday the 13th can be 14 months.
