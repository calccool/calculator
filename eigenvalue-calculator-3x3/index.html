What are the eigenvalues ​​of a matrix?

In linear algebra, the eigenvector of a matrix

$$A$$

is called a non-zero vector $$\mathbf{v}$$ which, when multiplied by this matrix, does not change its direction, but is only scaled by a factor

$$\lambda$$

. This coefficient

$$\lambda$$

and is called eigenvalue (or eigenvalue).

Mathematically, this definition is written by the equation:

$$A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$$

Where:

  • $$A$$ is a square matrix (in our case 3x3).
  • $$\mathbf{v}$$ is an eigenvector.
  • $$\lambda$$ is an eigenvalue (scalar).

Calculation algorithm: how it works

To find the eigenvalues, you need to solve the characteristic equation. It is derived from the condition that the system of equations

$$(A — \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = 0$$

has a non-zero solution only when the determinant of the coefficient matrix is ​​equal to zero.

Characteristic equation

For a 3x3 matrix the equation looks like this:

$$\det(A — \lambda I) = 0$$

Having expanded the determinant, we obtain a cubic equation for

$$\lambda$$

:

$$-\lambda^3 + \text{tr}(A)\lambda^2 — C_2\lambda + \det(A) = 0$$

Where the polynomial coefficients have the following meaning:

  • tr(A) (Trace of matrix) - sum of diagonal elements: $$a_{11} + a_{22} + a_{33}$$ .
  • C₂ is the sum of second-order principal minors.
  • det(A) is the determinant of the matrix.

Solving the cubic equation

Our calculator uses Cardano's formula and Vieta's trigonometric method to find the roots of this equation. This allows you to accurately determine how:

  1. Real roots (simple and multiples).
  2. Complex roots (type $$a \pm bi$$ ), which are often found in rotation matrices.

Practical Application

Finding eigenvectors and values ​​is used in a variety of areas:

  • Quantum mechanics: To find energy levels of systems.
  • Data Analysis (PCA): Principal component analysis uses the eigenvectors of the covariance matrix to reduce the dimensionality of the data.
  • Mechanics: Calculation of the main axes of inertia of a solid body and analysis of vibrations of structures.
  • Computer graphics: Transformations, rotations and scaling of 3D objects.
