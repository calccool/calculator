Calculation methods and formulas

Our calculator is based on proven algorithms that are used in the forestry industry. To determine the volume of one log, a formula for the volume of a cylinder is used, adapted to the natural shape of the tree trunk.

Basic volume formula

For an ideal cylinder the calculation is as follows:

$$V = \pi \cdot \left(\frac{D}{2}\right)^2 \cdot L$$

Where:

  • $V$ - log volume in cubic meters ( $m^3$ );
  • $\pi$ — mathematical constant (~3.14159);
  • $D$ - average log diameter in meters;
  • $L$ - log length in meters.

Accounting for wood curability

In reality, the tree trunk has a cone shape. The difference between the diameter of the butt (lower part) and the top is called run away. When calculating “at the top”, the calculator automatically applies a convergence coefficient (on average 1 cm per 1 meter of length) so that the result corresponds to the actual cubic capacity, and not to a pure cylinder according to the minimum diameter.

Recommendations for taking measurements

To ensure that your calculation in the cubeturner is as fair and accurate as possible, follow the rules of expert measurement:

  • Diameter measurement: Always measure the diameter of the bare trunk, excluding the bark. If the log has an oval cross-section, measure the minimum and maximum diameters and enter the arithmetic average into the calculator.
  • Length measurement: take into account the allowance for trimming (usually 3-5 cm), which is not included in the paid cubic capacity.
  • Curvature: If there is a strong curvature of the log, its useful volume is reduced, which should be taken into account when agreeing on the price.
