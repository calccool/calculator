CPC calculator: Cost per click calculation, formulas and break-even analysis

Cost Per Click (CPC) is one of the key performance indicators in online marketing. Understanding how much you pay for each visit of a potential client to your website allows you to competently manage your advertising budget in Google Ads, Yandex.Direct, Facebook and other networks.

How is the cost per click calculated?

Depending on the advertising purchasing model and available data, one of three formulas is used. Our tool automates these calculations.

1. Classic formula (CPM to CPC conversion)

If you buy ads using a cost-per-impression (CPM) model, but want to know how much it costs you per visitor, use click-through rate (CTR).

Formula:

$$CPC = \frac{CPM}{1000 \times (CTR / 100)}$$

Where:

  • CPM — cost of 1000 impressions.
  • CTR — ratio of clicks to impressions as a percentage.

Example: You pay $5 for 1000 impressions, and 2% of users click your banner.

$CPC = 5 / (1000 \times 0.02) = \$ $0.25 per click.

2. Calculation after the fact (Average price)

Used to analyze already completed campaigns or reporting. Shows the average cost of transition.

Formula:

$$CPC = \frac{\text{Общий бюджет}}{\text{Количество кликов}}$$

3. Break-even CPC (Based on ROAS)

This is a strategic calculation. It shows what maximum You can afford to place a bid at an auction so that advertising does not work at a disadvantage, based on the conversion of your site and the average check.

Formula:

$$MaxCPC = \text{Средний чек} \times \text{Конверсия сайта}$$

What determines the cost of a click at an auction?

Even if you use automatic strategies, several factors influence the final price:

  1. Competition in the niche: The more advertisers compete for the audience, the hotter the auction becomes.
  2. Quality Score: Search engines reduce the cost per click for ads that are relevant to the user's query and lead to a high-quality landing page.
  3. Time and geotargeting: Clicks during working hours in big cities are usually more expensive than on weekends in the regions.
  4. Ad format: Video ads, banners and text blocks have different average costs.
