What measurement systems does the converter support?

For maximum convenience, all units of measurement are divided into logical categories. You can easily translate values ​​both within one system and between completely different historical eras.

Modern and historical systems

  • Metric system (SI): Basic modern units. From the smallest picograms ( $pg$ ) to gigatons ( $Gt$ ).
  • British and American (Avoirdupois): Standard measures of English-speaking countries, including ounces, pounds, groans and long tons.
  • Troy and jewelry measures: The most precise units for measuring precious metals and stones (carats, troy ounces, pennyweights).
  • Pharmaceutical measures: Historical units (scrupules, pharmaceutical drachms) used in medicine and pharmacology before the transition to the metric system.
  • Culinary (household) measures: A unique category for converting volumes into mass (teaspoons, glasses, pinches), calculated based on the reference density of water, where $1 мл = 1 г$ .
  • Old Russian measures: Ideal for historians and readers of classical literature (poods, zolotniks, doli, berkivtsi).
  • Asian units: Traditional measures of China (liang, jin), Japan (momme, kin) and India (tola, sir).
  • Antique measures: Rare units of Ancient Greece, Ancient Rome and Ancient Egypt (talents, libras, obols).
  • Astronomical masses: To solve astrophysics problems (electron mass, Earth mass, Sun mass).

Difference between mass and weight

In everyday speech, the terms "weight" and "mass" are often used interchangeably, but in physics they are two fundamentally different concepts. Our converter translates exactly mass — a scalar quantity that determines the amount of matter in an object.

The mass of a body ( $m$ ) remains the same whether the object is on Earth, on the Moon, or in outer space. Weight ( $P$ ) is a vector quantity, the force with which a body acts on a support or suspension in a gravitational field. Weight is calculated using the formula:

$$P = m \times g$$

Where $m$ is the mass (in kilograms), and $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity (on Earth approximately $9.81 m/s^2$ ).

Conversion formulas for popular units

For those who want to understand the mathematical logic of the calculations or program their own formulas, below are the exact conversion factors (based on international agreements, where 1 pound of Avoirdupois is legally equivalent to 0.45359237 kg).

Converting British measures to metric:

  • Pound to kilograms: $$m_{kg} = m_{lb} \times 0.45359237$$
  • Ounce to grams: $$m_{g} = m_{oz} \times 28.349523125$$
  • Moan in kilograms: $$m_{kg} = m_{st} \times 6.35029318$$

Translation of jewelry measures:

  • Carats to grams: $$m_{g} = m_{ct} \times 0.2$$
  • Troy ounce to grams: $$m_{g} = m_{ozt} \times 31.1034768$$
