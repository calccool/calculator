The mathematics of text rendering in PDF

When transferring text from the web (where the size is specified in screen pixels) to the physical vector format of PDF, an internal algorithm performs the conversion of the base values. The mathematical dependence of the physical size of an element on pixels and screen resolution (DPI) is expressed by the following formula:

$$L_{mm}=\left(\frac{L_{px}}{\text{DPI}}\right)\times25.4$$

Where $L_{mm}$ is the final length in millimeters, $L_{px}$ is the original size in pixels, and $25.4$ is the exact conversion factor from inches to millimeters.

This ensures that the resulting PDF will have crystal clear fonts at any magnification level.
