Working hours calculator: calculation for 36, 24 and shortened weeks

Accurate planning of work schedules requires strict compliance with labor laws. Our tool is designed to automate calculations of reduced working hours. It is ideal for employees working in the Far North, medical workers, teachers and those engaged in work with hazardous working conditions.

The calculator instantly calculates the monthly norm in hours, based on the procedure approved by Order of the Ministry of Health and Social Development of the Russian Federation No. 588n.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, you just need to follow three simple steps:

  • Select mode: 36 hours (standard for women in the RKS), 24 hours (for certain categories of doctors and industries) or enter your own unique standard.
  • Specify the number of working days: Enter the total number of working days in the billing month based on a five-day workweek calendar.
  • Add pre-holidays: Indicate the number of days in the month that immediately precede holidays (according to the Labor Code of the Russian Federation, such shifts are reduced by 1 hour).

Formula for calculating time standard

The calculation algorithm is based on a unified state standard. To determine the norm, the following mathematical model is used:

$$N_{month} = \left( \frac{T_{week}}{5} \right) \times D_{work} — H_{short}$$

Where:

  • $N_{month}$ - final standard working hours for the month;
  • $T_{week}$ - established length of the working week (36, 24 or other);
  • $D_{work}$ - number of working days in a month according to a five-day schedule;
  • $H_{short}$ - the number of hours by which working hours are reduced on the eve of the holidays.

Features of accounting for pre-holiday days

One of the most common mistakes when calculating manually is ignoring shortened days. According to Art. 95 of the Labor Code of the Russian Federation, the duration of the working day or shift immediately preceding a non-working holiday is reduced by one hour.

It is important to remember that this rule applies to all employees, regardless of their established norm (be it 40, 36 or 24 hours). Our calculator automatically subtracts these hours from your total balance, eliminating the risk of overtime or timesheet errors.

Load visualization
