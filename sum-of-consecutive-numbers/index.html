  1. Analysis (Decomposition): Finds all possible representations of the number $N$ as a sum of consecutive natural numbers.
  2. Synthesis (Sum Range): Instantly calculates the sum of all integers in a given range.

How does number expansion work?

Not every number can be represented as a sum of consecutive terms. For example, powers of two ( $2, 4, 8, 16 \dots$ ) cannot be expanded - this is a fundamental property of number theory.

For all other numbers, our algorithm finds options using the properties of an arithmetic progression. This is useful for:

  • Solutions to school Olympiad problems.
  • Search for "triangular numbers".
  • Programming and algorithmic tests.

Range Sum (Arithmetic Progression)

If you need to add numbers from $A$ to $B$ (for example, from 50 to 150), you don't need to do it manually. The calculator uses the formula for the sum of terms of an arithmetic progression.

The Formula:

$$S = \frac{(A + B) \times N}{2}$$

Where:

  • $A$ - the beginning of the range.
  • $B$ - end of the range.
  • $N$ - the number of numbers in the range (calculated as $B — A + 1$ ).

How to use the calculator?

  1. Select Operating mode:
    • "Find Sequences" - to find out what a number consists of.
    • "Find the sum of a range" - to add many numbers in a row.
  2. Enter data (number $N$ or boundaries $Start$ and $End$ ).
  3. Click "Calculate".

The tool will instantly show the result, the number of terms and check whether your number is “triangular” (that is, the sum of the numbers from 1 to $k$ ).
