What is an orthocenter?

The orthocenter is the point of intersection of the triangle's three altitudes (or their extensions).

Recall that the altitude is the perpendicular drawn from the vertex of the triangle to the opposite side (or to the line containing this side).

Algorithm: How to find the coordinates of the orthocenter?

Our calculator uses analytical geometry methods. To find the height intersection point manually, you need to follow these steps:

  1. Find the angle coefficients of the sides. Calculate the slope ( $m$ ) for the side $BC$ , using the coordinates of the points $B$ and $C$ : $$m_{BC} = \frac{y_C — y_B}{x_C — x_B}$$
  2. Determine the slope of the height ( $h_a$ ). Since the height from point $A$ is perpendicular to side $BC$ , their slopes are related by the perpendicularity relation (the product is -1). The height slope will be: $$m_{h_a} = -\frac{1}{m_{BC}}$$
  3. Create an equation for height. Using the equation of a straight line passing through a point $A(x_A, y_A)$ with a known slope $m_{h_a}$ : $$y — y_A = m_{h_a} \cdot (x — x_A)$$
  4. Repeat for the second height. Similarly, we find the equation for the height $h_b$ drawn from the point $B$ to the side $AC$ .
  5. Find the intersection point. Solve the resulting system of two linear equations. The solution of the system ( $x, y$ ) will be the coordinates of the orthocenter $H$ .

Calculation example

Let's say we have a triangle with vertices:

  • $A(0, 0)$
  • $B(4, 0)$
  • $C(0, 3)$

This is a right triangle (the legs lie on the coordinate axes).

  1. The height from $C$ to $AB$ is the same as the axis of $Y$ ( $x=0$ ).
  2. The height from $B$ to $AC$ is the same as the axis of $X$ ( $y=0$ ).
  3. The point of their intersection is obvious: $(0, 0)$ .

In this case, the orthocenter coincides with the vertex of $A$ , where the right angle is located. Our calculator will automatically detect this case and give the result Right triangle.
