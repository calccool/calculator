Triangle circumcircle calculator: Online calculation of radius and center coordinates

This online calculator solves the classic problem of analytical geometry: constructing a circle passing through three given points (vertices of a triangle). The tool is indispensable for engineers, architects, game developers (GameDev) and students of technical universities.

Unlike simple analogues, our algorithm calculates the full range of geometric characteristics:

  • Center coordinates $(x_0; y_0)$ - the point of intersection of the bisector perpendiculars.
  • Circumcision radius $(R)$ — the exact distance from the center to any vertex.
  • Canonical equation of a circle — a ready-made formula for inserting into drawings or code.
  • Metric parameters - circumference and area of ​​a limited circle.

How to use the calculator

To get an accurate result, follow these steps:

  1. Set the coordinates of the first vertex to $A (x_a, y_a)$ .
  2. Enter the coordinates of the second vertex $B (x_b, y_b)$ .
  3. Specify the coordinates of the third vertex $C (x_c, y_c)$ .
  4. Click the button "Calculate".

Important: If three points lie on the same line (collinear), it is impossible to construct a triangle and draw a circle around it. The system will automatically detect such a case and issue a corresponding notification.

Theoretical basis and formulas

The circumcircle is the circle containing all three vertices of the triangle. Its center (Circumcenter) is located at the intersection point of the perpendicular bisectors drawn to the sides of the triangle.

Algorithm for calculating center coordinates

To calculate the coordinates of the $(U_x, U_y)$ center, we use the determinant method, which eliminates errors when solving a system of linear equations.

First the auxiliary determinant $D$ is found:

$$D = 2 \cdot [x_a(y_b — y_c) + x_b(y_c — y_a) + x_c(y_a — y_b)]$$

If $D = 0$ , the points lie on the same line. Otherwise, the center coordinates are calculated as follows:

$$U_x = \frac{1}{D} \cdot [(x_a^2 + y_a^2)(y_b — y_c) + (x_b^2 + y_b^2)(y_c — y_a) + (x_c^2 + y_c^2)(y_a — y_b)]$$

$$U_y = \frac{1}{D} \cdot [(x_a^2 + y_a^2)(x_c — x_b) + (x_b^2 + y_b^2)(x_a — x_c) + (x_c^2 + y_c^2)(x_b — x_a)]$$

Radius Calculation and Equations

Knowing the coordinates of the center of $(U_x, U_y)$ , the radius of $R$ is calculated as the distance from the center to any of the vertices (for example, to point A):

$$R = \sqrt{(x_a — U_x)^2 + (y_a — U_y)^2}$$

The final equation of the circle is reduced to canonical form:

$$(x — U_x)^2 + (y — U_y)^2 = R^2$$

Circle Center Position Properties

The position of the center of the circumscribed circle directly depends on the type of triangle. This is a great way to check the correctness of geometric constructions.

Practical Application

The calculation of the circumscribed circle is used not only in school geometry, but also in real problems:

  • Cartography and GPS: Triangulation of an object's position using satellite signals.
  • Robotics: Construction of routes to avoid obstacles (the Voronoi diagram is constructed based on the centers of circumscribed circles).
  • Construction: Design of arched structures and domes supported by three load-bearing points.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Is it possible to describe a circle around any triangle?

Yes, around any triangle you can describe a circle, and only one. The exception is “degenerate” triangles, where all three vertices lie on the same line.

In what units is the result given?

The calculator works with universal coordinate units. If you entered coordinates in millimeters, then the radius, area and circumference will also be calculated in millimeters (and square millimeters, respectively).

How to find the diameter?

Diameter is twice the radius. The formula is simple: $D = 2R$ . Our calculator displays this value automatically in the results block.
