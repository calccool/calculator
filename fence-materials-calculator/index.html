How the materials calculator works

The algorithm takes into account the geometry of your site and the specifics of the selected material. The calculation is carried out in three stages:

  1. Geometry: Calculation of the net perimeter of the fence minus the entrance group (gates and wickets).
  2. Frame: Calculation of load-bearing elements - pillars and transverse joists (profile pipes).
  3. Sheathing: Determining the amount of facing material taking into account installation tolerances (overlap, gaps).

Basic calculation formulas

To understand how the calculator works, we present the basic formulas used in construction estimates.

Calculation of the perimeter and length of the fence

If the plot is rectangular, the perimeter ( $P$ ) is calculated through the length ( $L$ ) and width ( $W$ ) of the plot:

$$P = (L + W) \times 2$$

The net length of the fence ( $L_{fence}$ ), required for the purchase of materials, is calculated by subtracting the width of the gate ( $W_{gate}$ ):

$$L_{fence} = P — W_{gate}$$

Calculation of the number of pillars

The number of support posts ( $N_{post}$ ) depends on the installation pitch ( $S$ ). Typically the pitch is from 2.5 to 3 meters.

$$N_{post} = \lceil \frac{L_{fence}}{S} \rceil + 1$$

Note: $+1$ is added to complete the loop.

Sheathing calculation (Corrugated sheeting)

The key point when calculating profiled sheets is the use useful (working) width, not overall size.

$$N_{sheets} = \lceil \frac{L_{fence}}{W_{useful}} \rceil$$

Where $W_{useful}$ is the working width of the sheet (usually 30–50 mm less than the total due to wave overlap).

Detailed analysis of input parameters

To make the calculation as accurate as possible, pay attention to the following fields of the calculator:

1. Dimensions input method

  • On the sides: Ideal for rectangular plots (6, 10, 15 acres). You enter the length and width, the system calculates the perimeter.
  • Overall Length: Use this mode if your plot has a complex shape (trapezoid, polygon) and you know the total length of the boundaries from the cadastral plan.

2. Logs (cross members)

Joists are horizontal guides to which the sheathing is attached.

  • 2 rows: Recommended for fences up to 2 meters high.
  • 3 rows: Mandatory for fences higher than 2 meters to ensure rigidity.
  • Without lag: Select this item only for the Chainlink mesh if it is mounted by tension without a frame.

3. Fasteners (Screws)

The calculator automatically predicts the consumption of roofing screws or rivets:

  • For corrugated sheets: The calculation is based on fastening through a wave of 2 or 3 logs.
  • For picket fence: 2 attachment points are taken into account for each joist for each plank.

Tips for saving money when building a fence

  1. Exact pillar pitch: Increasing the pitch from 2.5 to 3 meters on a plot of 10 acres (perimeter ~120 m) allows you to save about 8-10 pillars and the corresponding volume of concrete.
  2. Choice of corrugated sheets: For the fence, wall corrugated sheeting grade S-8 or S-10 with a thickness of 0.4–0.45 mm is sufficient. The use of roofing sheets (NS) is unreasonably expensive.
  3. Waste accounting: When calculating a picket fence or board, always include a 5-10% margin for defects during cutting and installation (our calculator has an option to select a margin).
