Distance calculator between two points (2D and 3D)

Finding the distance between two objects is a fundamental problem in analytical geometry, which is used everywhere: from navigation and architecture to computer game development. Our calculator allows you to instantly calculate the length of a segment, knowing only the coordinates of its ends.

Mathematical calculation formulas

The calculations are based on the Pythagorean theorem. Depending on the dimension of space (two-dimensional or three-dimensional), the formulas take the following form:

1. Plane distance (2D)

For two points $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$ , the distance $d$ is calculated by the formula:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 — x_1)^2 + (y_2 — y_1)^2}$$

2. Distance in space (3D)

For points in the three-dimensional coordinate system $A(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2, z_2)$ , the formula is expanded:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 — x_1)^2 + (y_2 — y_1)^2 + (z_2 — z_1)^2}$$

Coordinates of the midpoint of the segment

The midpoint of segment $M$ is the point equidistant from $A$ and $B$ . Its coordinates are the arithmetic mean of the corresponding coordinates of the ends:

  • $x_m = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}$
  • $y_m = \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}$
  • $z_m = \frac{z_1 + z_2}{2}$
  • Tilt angle (for 2D)

The angle $\alpha$ between the segment and the positive direction of the OX axis is calculated using the arctangent:

$$\alpha = arctan\left(\frac{y_2 — y_1}{x_2 — x_1}\right)$$
