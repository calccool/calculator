How Calculation Works: Science and Mathematics

Our calculator is based on World Health Organization (WHO) recommendations and physiological standards for fluid distribution. We use two modes:

  1. Manual input: if you already know your daily volume.
  2. Automatic calculation: based on your weight, gender and physical activity.

To determine the individual consumption rate, the following mathematical model is used:

$$V_{daily} = (W \cdot C_{gender}) + \left(\frac{A}{60} \cdot 500\right)$$

Where:

  • $V_{daily}$ - final daily value in milliliters;
  • $W$ - user weight in kilograms;
  • $C_{gender}$ - metabolic rate (35 for men, 31 for women);
  • $A$ - physical activity time in minutes (adds 500 ml for each hour of exercise).

Why is it important to know your water allowance?

Water is the main solvent in the human body. Maintaining proper balance (hydration) directly affects:

  • Energy and concentration: The brain is 75% water, and even slight dehydration causes brain fog.
  • Metabolism: A sufficient volume of liquid accelerates lipolysis (the breakdown of fats).
  • Skin health: Elasticity and complexion depend on the level of moisture in the tissues.

How to use the calculator

The interface is designed according to the “Progressive Disclosure” principle, which makes it as user-friendly as possible:

  • Select mode: “I know my volume” for a quick check or “Calculate my volume” for a personal analysis.
  • Use quick buttons: We've added presets (1.5L, 2.0L, etc.) so you don't have to enter numbers manually.
  • See the visualization: A special block with an animated drop and a filling tank will show your hydration level in real time.
  • Copy the results: With one click on the icon, you can save your data for a habit tracker or food diary.
