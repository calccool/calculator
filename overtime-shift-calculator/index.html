Overtime calculator for shift work: calculation of overtime according to the Labor Code of the Russian Federation

Working on a shift schedule (for example, 2/2, 3/3 or every other day) is often accompanied by summarized accounting of working hours (Article 104 of the Labor Code of the Russian Federation). Unlike the standard “five-day week”, here overtime is counted not at the end of the day, but at the end of the accounting period: month, quarter or year.

Our calculator automatically calculates your entitlement payments, dividing the basic part, excess hours and specific allowances for special working conditions.

Legislative basis for calculations

According to Articles 152, 153 and 154 of the Labor Code of the Russian Federation, remuneration for labor in conditions deviating from normal conditions is made according to the following rules:

  • Overtime: The first two hours of overtime during the accounting period are paid at least one and a half times (coefficient 1.5), the subsequent ones - at least double (coefficient 2.0).
  • Night time: Period from 22:00 to 06:00. The minimum premium is 20% of the hourly rate.
  • Weekends and holidays: Work on an official holiday is legally paid at double the rate, even if it is included in your schedule.

Formulas used in the calculator

To obtain an accurate result, the calculator uses algorithms that comply with the methodology of the Ministry of Labor.

  1. Calculation of the hourly rate ( $R_h$ ) at the established salary: $$R_h = \frac{S}{N}$$ Where $S$ is the salary for the period, and $N$ is the standard hours according to the production calendar.
  2. Overtime pay calculation ( $P_{ot}$ ): $$P_{ot} = (H_{1.5} \cdot 1.5 \cdot R_h) + (H_{2.0} \cdot 2.0 \cdot R_h)$$ Where $H_{1.5}$ is the first 2 hours of processing, and $H_{2.0}$ is all subsequent hours.
  3. Total accrual: $$Total = (H_{act} \cdot R_h) + P_{ot\_extra} + P_{night} + P_{hol}$$

How to use the overtime calculator

To get an accurate breakdown of your salary (before personal income tax), follow a few simple steps:

  1. Select mode: “Hourly rate” if you know the cost of your hour, or “Salary for the period” if you have a fixed salary.
  2. Specify the norm: Enter the number of hours you were supposed to work according to the production calendar (for example, 160 hours).
  3. Enter fact: Indicate how many hours were actually worked.
  4. Additional options: If you worked at night or on public holidays, enable the "Take into account night and holiday hours" switch.
  5. Click "Calculate": The system will issue a detailed diagram and a detailed receipt with all charges.

Why is this important

With a shift schedule, employers often make mistakes in summary accounting. For example, they forget that holiday hours already paid at double rate should not be taken into account when calculating overtime at the end of the period, so that double payment for the same hour does not occur. Our algorithm takes these nuances into account, helping you protect your labor rights.
