Heads and tails selection calculator: fractional distillation calculation online

The second distillation (fractional distillation) is a critical step in home distillation, the purpose of which is to separate ethyl alcohol from dangerous impurities. Qualitative division into "heads", "body" And "tails" allows you to get a clean drink without a hangover or unpleasant odor.

Our online calculator automates complex calculations using two methods: based on the parameters of the raw alcohol and the amount of sugar in the original mash.

Basic concepts and calculation formulas

All calculations are based on the concept Absolute Alcohol (AS). This is the theoretical volume of 100% ethanol in your solution.

To calculate the volume of fractions, the following mathematical models are used:

  1. Calculation of absolute alcohol: $$V_{AC} = V_{CC} \cdot \frac{K_{CC}}{100}$$ where $V_{CC}$ is the volume of raw alcohol (l), and $K_{CC}$ is its strength (%).
  2. Volume of the head fraction (heads): $$V_{heads} = V_{AC} \cdot \frac{P_{heads}}{100}$$ where $P_{heads}$ is the percentage of goals selected (usually 10%).
  3. Expected body volume (drinkable fraction): $$V_{body} = \frac{V_{AC} \cdot P_{body}}{K_{exp}}$$ where $K_{exp}$ is the target output strength of the product.

Step-by-step instructions for using the calculator

To get an accurate result, follow the algorithm:

  • Select mode: “By raw alcohol” (if you have already done the first distillation) or “By sugar” (for preliminary planning).
  • Enter details: Indicate the volume of raw material and its strength, measured with a hydrometer at a temperature of 20°C.
  • Fine tuning: If you are working on a distillation column, activate “Fine tuning” to change the percentage of head selection (for example, up to 15% for grain washes) and set the planned selection rate (ml/h).
  • Analysis of results: The visualization unit will show the distribution of alcohol in the system and the estimated time that will be required for dropwise selection of heads.

Why is it important to select heads drop by drop?

Heads are low-boiling fractions. To prevent them from being “smeared” all over the body, they must be removed from the cube extremely slowly. Optimal speed - 100–150 ml per hour (about 1-2 drops per second). Our calculator automatically calculates the time for this step, helping you plan the process correctly.

Advice from professionals

  • Focus on the smell: The calculator gives accurate numbers, but make the final decision on the end of the selection of goals “on the nose”. Rub a drop of distillate on your palms - the strong chemical smell should disappear.
  • Terms: The calculator uses the concept of “Absolute alcohol”, since it is from this that all proportions in the professional community are calculated.
  • Safety: Never leave a running machine unattended, even if the calculator has calculated a long sampling time.
