Laminate calculator: Accurate calculation of the number of packages and the cost of the floor

When purchasing floor coverings (laminate, parquet boards, quartz-vinyl), the builder’s golden rule applies: “It’s better to buy one pack more than to look for the missing bar in a month”.

The problem is that different batches of the same laminate may differ in shade (tone). If there is not enough material, the purchased pack may visually stand out on the floor. On the other hand, overpaying for an extra 3-4 packages is an unnecessary expense. Our calculator solves this problem by calculating the ideal balance taking into account the trim margin.

Calculation formulas

The algorithm of our calculator is based on geometric formulas using flow coefficients.

1. Net area of ​​the room ( $S_{room}$ ):

$$S_{room} = L \times W$$

where $L$ is the length of the room, $W$ is the width of the room.

2. Calculated area with margin ( $S_{total}$ ):

To compensate for the trim that will inevitably occur when installing against walls, we add a safety factor ( $K$ ):

$$S_{total} = S_{room} \times (1 + \frac{K}{100})$$

3. Number of packages ( $N_{packs}$ ):

Since laminate is sold only in whole packages, the final value is rounded up:

$$N_{packs} = \lceil \frac{S_{total}}{S_{pack}} \rceil$$

where $S_{pack}$ is the area of ​​the laminate in one package (specified by the manufacturer).

Influence of installation method on consumption

The choice of installation scheme directly affects the amount of waste.

  • Direct laying (Deck): The panels are laid parallel to one of the walls. Scraps from the last row can often be used as the beginning of the next row.
    • Recommended stock: 5%
    • Economical: High.
  • Diagonal laying (At an angle of 45°): Visually expands the space, but requires trimming each board adjacent to the wall at an angle.
    • Recommended stock: 10-15%
    • Economical: Low (more cuttings).

Step by step instructions

  1. Measure the room: Measure the maximum length and width of the room with a tape measure (taking into account niches and doorways).
  2. Find out the parameters of the pack: Look at the price tag or box for the “m² per package” parameter.
  3. Select method: Decide how the boards will lie - straight or diagonal.
  4. Specify price (optional): Enter your cost per square meter to get your final estimate.

Pro tip: Always keep your receipt. Most large hardware stores have extra unopened packs can be returned within 14-100 days (depending on the store’s rules). So rounding up is your insurance and costs nothing on return.
