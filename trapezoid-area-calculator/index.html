Online Trapezoid Calculator: Area, Perimeter and Height

Online calculation of trapezoid parameters

A trapezoid is a quadrilateral in which two sides are parallel (the bases) and the other two are not (the sides). Calculation of its geometric properties is necessary not only in geometry lessons, but also in construction, landscape design and cutting materials.

Our universal calculator allows you to find all unknown parameters of a figure, even if the initial data is minimal. The tool works with both isosceles, rectangular and scalene trapezoids.

Calculator features

Unlike simple scripts that only calculate area, our tool performs a full geometric analysis:

  1. Calculation of Area ( $S$ ) and Perimeter ( $P$ ): Basic calculations based on bases and heights or sides.
  2. Height Search ( $h$ ): A unique function - if the height is unknown, we will calculate it by knowing the lengths of all four sides.
  3. Midline ( $m$ ): Automatic calculation of the line connecting the midpoints of the sides.
  4. Diagonal Lengths ( $d_1, d_2$ ): Calculation of segments connecting opposite vertices.

Basic formulas and notations

To understand the calculations we use the following notation:

  • $a, b$ - the base of the trapezoid.
  • $c, d$ - sides.
  • $h$ - height (perpendicular between the bases).

1. Area of ​​a trapezoid

The most popular formula. The area is equal to the product of half the sum of the bases and the height:

$$S = \frac{a + b}{2} \times h$$

2. Perimeter

The sum of the lengths of all sides of the figure:

$$P = a + b + c + d$$

3. Middle line

The middle line ( $m$ ) is parallel to the bases and equal to their half-sum. By the way, the area can also be found as the product of the midline and the height ( $S = m \times h$ ).

$$m = \frac{a + b}{2}$$

How to find the height if only the sides are known?

This is a complex problem that our calculator solves automatically. If there is no height in the conditions, but all sides are given ( $a, b, c, d$ ), an algorithm is used based on Heron's formula for a triangle formed by displacement of the lateral sides.

The formula looks like this:

$$h = \frac{2}{|a — b|} \sqrt{p(p-c)(p-d)(p-|a-b|)}$$

(Where $p$ is the semi-perimeter of the auxiliary triangle).
