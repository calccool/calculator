How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

To receive an accurate calculation, fill in the following fields:

  1. Purchase price and advance: Enter the full cost of the item or property. If you have your own funds (down payment), indicate them - the calculator will automatically calculate the “body” of the loan.
  2. Term and Rate: Specify the interest rate (annual) offered by the bank and the loan term in years or months.
  3. Payment type: This is the most important parameter. Select Annuity (equal payments every month) or Differentiated (payment decreases towards the end of the term).
  4. Hidden fees: Banks often include one-time loan fees or monthly insurance fees. Specify them to see full cost of the loan.

After clicking the “Calculate” button, you will receive not only the final figure, but also a detailed diagram showing how much of your money is “eaten up” by interest.

Calculation formulas

For those who want to check the calculations manually, we provide the mathematical formulas that our algorithm uses.

1. Calculation of the Annuity payment:

The payment is calculated so that the debt is repaid in full in equal installments.

$$A = K \cdot \frac{i \cdot (1 + i)^n}{(1 + i)^n — 1}$$

Where:

  • A — monthly annuity payment.
  • K — loan amount (body).
  • i — monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100).
  • n — number of months (loan term).

2. Calculation of differentiated payment:

Here the payment changes every month and consists of two parts: a fixed part of the debt and interest on the balance.

$$P_m = \frac{K}{n} + (O_m \cdot i)$$

Where:

  • Pm - payment in a specific month.
  • K/n - a fixed part of the repayment of the principal debt.
  • Om — the balance of the debt at the beginning of the month.
