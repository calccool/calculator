How does Fast Remux technology work?

Most users do not know that MOV and MP4 are not the videos themselves, but only “media containers” (packaging) containing identical video and audio streams (most often encoded with the H.264 or HEVC codec).

Our tool does not perform resource-intensive pixel transcoding. Instead, the algorithm carefully extracts the original streams from the MOV container and instantly transfers them to the universal MP4 container.

Due to the absence of recoding, the mathematical model of the final file size remains constant. The basic physics of digital video is described by the following bitrate time integral:

$S = \int_{0}^{T} (R_v(t) + R_a(t)) dt$

Where $S$ is the file size, $T$ is the duration, $R_v(t)$ and $R_a(t)$ are variable video and audio bitrates. Since our algorithm leaves $R_v$ and $R_a$ intact, the conversion occurs without losing even one bit of the original quality. The resulting MP4 file will weigh exactly the same as the original MOV.
