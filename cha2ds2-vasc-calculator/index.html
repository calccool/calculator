CHA₂DS₂-VASc scale: Online Stroke Risk Calculator for AF

Atrial fibrillation (atrial fibrillation) is the most common cause of cardioembolic strokes. Blood stagnates in the left atrial appendage, forming blood clots that can break off and block the blood vessels of the brain.

Scale CHA₂DS₂-VASc is the international gold standard for stroke risk stratification in patients with atrial fibrillation (AF). It is included in the clinical guidelines of the European Society of Cardiology (ESC) and the American Heart Association (AHA/ACC).

Our tool allows doctors and patients to instantly calculate a score and determine the need for oral anticoagulants (blood thinners).

Explanation of the abbreviation CHA₂DS₂-VASc

The name of the scale is an acronym, where each letter represents a clinical risk factor, and the number (if any) represents its “weight” in points.

The calculation formula looks like the sum of the following components:

  • C (Congestive heart failure) — Heart failure or left ventricular dysfunction (+1 point).
  • H (Hypertension) — Arterial hypertension (including controlled by drugs) (+1 point).
  • A₂ (Age ≥ 75) — Age 75 years and older. This is the most powerful risk factor (+2 points).
  • D (Diabetes mellitus) — Diabetes mellitus (+1 point).
  • S₂ (Stroke) - History of stroke, transient ischemic attack (TIA) or thromboembolism (+2 points).
  • V (Vascular disease) — Vascular diseases (myocardial infarction, peripheral atherosclerosis, plaques in the aorta) (+1 point).
  • A (Age 65–74) — Age from 65 to 74 years (+1 point).
  • Sc (Sex category) — Female (+1 point).

Balancing benefits and risks: HAS-BLED scale

Prescribing anticoagulants always carries a risk of bleeding. Therefore, our calculator has an additional module built into it - a scale HAS-BLED.

If the patient scores on this scale 3 or more points, this does not mean canceling treatment, but requires:

  1. More frequent doctor monitoring.
  2. Correction of modifiable factors (normalization of blood pressure, cessation of alcohol, withdrawal of NSAIDs).

Note: This calculator is a reference tool. The final decision on drug prescription is made by the attending physician based on the complete clinical picture.
