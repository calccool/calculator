Truck capacity calculator: online pallet arrangement diagram

Optimizing vehicle loading is a key stage in logistics, allowing to reduce the cost of transporting a unit of goods. Our calculator is designed to instantly calculate the maximum number of pallets that can be placed in the body, taking into account their dimensions, weight and stackability.

How the calculation algorithm works

The program uses a mathematical modeling method to find the most effective location option. Unlike simple calculators, our tool compares two pallet orientation options (longitudinal and transverse) and selects the one that minimizes dead space.

To determine the final capacity, the following formulas are used:

  1. Calculation of quantity by floor area: $$N_{floor} = \max \left( \left\lfloor \frac{L_{truck}}{L_{pal}} \right\rfloor \cdot \left\lfloor \frac{W_{truck}}{W_{pal}} \right\rfloor, \left\lfloor \frac{L_{truck}}{W_{pal}} \right\rfloor \cdot \left\lfloor \frac{W_{truck}}{L_{pal}} \right\rfloor \right)$$ where $L$ is the length, $W$ is the width of the body and pallet, respectively.
  2. Accounting for tiers (stacking): If the load allows stacking, the number of levels ( $T$ ) is calculated as: $$T = \left\lfloor \frac{H_{truck}}{H_{pal}} \right\rfloor$$ Total theoretical capacity: $N_{total} = N_{floor} \cdot T$ .
  3. Load limit: The final quantity ( $N_{final}$ ) is adjusted depending on the maximum load weight ( $M_{max}$ ): $$N_{final} = \min \left( N_{total}, \left\lfloor \frac{M_{max}}{M_{pal\_brutto}} \right\rfloor \right)$$

Recommendations for cargo placement

When planning loading, you should adhere to safety rules and efficient logistics:

  • Center of gravity: Try to distribute the heaviest pallets over the axles or closer to the center of the body to avoid axial overload.
  • Fragile cargo: If the product is pressure sensitive, be sure to disable the “Allow stacking” feature in the calculator settings.
  • Fastening: Remember that the design diagram assumes a close arrangement, but in reality it is necessary to leave technological gaps (2–5 cm) and use fastening belts.
