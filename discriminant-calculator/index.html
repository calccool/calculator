What is Discriminant?

Discriminant (from lat. discriminans - “discriminating”) is a number that determines the nature of the solution to the quadratic equation. Knowing only the discriminant, you can immediately tell how many roots the equation has and what type they belong to.

Calculation formula

For the standard equation ax2+bx+c=0, where:

  • a is the leading coefficient (at x2);
  • b is the second coefficient (at x);
  • c is the free term.

The discriminant is calculated using the formula:

D=b2−4ac

Analysis of roots depending on D

The D value serves as an indicator of the number and type of roots.

Formulas for finding roots

Our calculator automatically selects the desired solution algorithm:

1. If D>0 (Two real roots): The general formula used is:

x1,2​=2a−b±D​​

2. If D=0 (One root): Since 0​=0, the formula simplifies:

x=2a−b​

3. If D<0 (Complex roots): There are no solutions in real numbers, but there are roots in the field of complex numbers. The imaginary unit i is used (where i2=−1):

x1,2​=2a−b±i∣D∣​​

Example output: 2+3i and 2−3i.

Additional Features: Parabola Analysis

Any quadratic equation describes the parabola y=ax2+bx+c. The calculator calculates its key parameters.

1. Vertex of the Parabola (xv​,yv​)

This is the extremum point (minimum or maximum of the function).

  • Vertex x coordinate: xv​=2a−b​
  • Vertex y coordinate: yv​=a(xv​)2+b(xv​)+c

2. Direction of branches

  • If a>0 - branches are directed up (the function has a minimum).
  • If a<0 - branches are directed down (the function has a maximum).
