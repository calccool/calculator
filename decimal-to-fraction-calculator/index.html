In mathematics, engineering, and everyday calculations, there is often a need to represent a decimal fraction (for example, 0.75 ) in the form of an ordinary ( 3/4 ). Translation algorithm (Step by step guide)

The conversion is carried out according to a strict mathematical rule. Let's look at it using the example of a number 2.75.

Step 1: Write as a Fraction

Any number can be represented as a fraction with a denominator of 1.

$$\frac{2.75}{1}$$

Step 2: Getting rid of the comma

We multiply the numerator and denominator by 10, 100 or 1000 - depending on the number of decimal places. There are two digits in the number 2.75, which means we multiply by 100.

$$\frac{2.75 \times 100}{1 \times 100} = \frac{275}{100}$$

Step 3: Reducing a Fraction

Now we need to simplify the resulting fraction. To do this, we find GCD (the largest number that divides both 275 and 100). In this case it is 25.

$$\frac{275 \div 25}{100 \div 25} = \frac{11}{4}$$

Step 4: Convert to Mixed Number

Since the numerator (11) is greater than the denominator (4), we can isolate the whole part.

$$11 \div 4 = 2 \text{ (остаток 3)}$$

Result: $2 \frac{3}{4}$
