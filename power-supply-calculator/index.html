Power supply unit (PSU) power calculator for PC: online calculation

The choice of power supply unit is the foundation for the stability of the entire system. An error in calculations can lead to sudden reboots under load, component degradation, or failure of an expensive video card. Our tool allows you to high-precision online power supply calculation, based on real TDP indicators and the specifics of modern components.

Why is it important to consider power reserve?

Modern power supplies have an efficiency curve (COP). Peak efficiency is usually achieved at a load of 50% to 70% from face value. If your computer draws 400 watts, buying a 400 watt unit will force it to operate at its limit, which will increase the noise level and shorten the life of the capacitors.

The “gold standard” is included in the algorithm of our calculator - reserve of 30-35%, which guarantees:

  • Quiet operation of the power supply cooling system.
  • Stable nutrition during short-term spikes in consumption (Spikes).
  • Possibility of a small system upgrade in the future.

Power calculation formula

The calculator uses a complex algorithm that takes into account both TDP rating data and overclocking efficiency factors.

$$P_{total} = (TDP_{cpu} + TDP_{gpu}) \cdot k_{oc} + \sum P_{periphery} + P_{base}$$

Where:

  • $P_{total}$ - peak power consumption.
  • $k_{oc}$ - acceleration factor (equal to 1.2 when the Overclocking option is enabled).
  • $\sum P_{periphery}$ is the sum of the powers of all drives and fans.
  • $P_{base}$ - basic consumption of the motherboard and chipset (taken as 45 W ).

The final recommendation is calculated using the formula, rounded to the nearest denomination (50 W increments):

$$P_{rec} = \lceil (P_{total} \cdot 1.35) / 50 \rceil \cdot 50$$

80 PLUS Certification Guidelines

When choosing a power supply, it is not enough to know only the watts. Pay attention to the result of our calculator in the “Energy Efficiency Certificate” field.

1. 80 PLUS Standard / Bronze

The optimal choice for budget and mid-range gaming builds with a power of up to 500-600 W. Provides efficiency of about 82-85%.

2. 80 PLUS Gold

The "gold standard" for modern gaming PCs. We recommend this certification for any system with rated capacity over 650 W. This guarantees high-quality components and energy savings.

3. 80 PLUS Platinum / Titanium

Required for workstations and Hi-End systems operating 24/7. Maximum voltage stability on 12V, 5V and 3.3V lines.

How to use a calculator: step-by-step instructions

  1. Enter CPU and GPU TDP: This data can be found in the specifications of your components. We've also added quick entry buttons for popular models (RTX 4060, 4090, etc.).
  2. Specify the number of drives: HDDs consume significantly more than SSDs, especially at system startup.
  3. Consider cooling: The pump of a liquid cooling system (dropsy) requires a separate power supply, unlike simple air coolers.
  4. Overclocking option: If you plan to remove Power Limits in the BIOS, be sure to activate this switch - consumption will increase by 20%.
  5. Get the result: The calculator will instantly build a load distribution diagram and give you a specific power supply rating that is worth buying.

Expert advice: Never buy a power supply close to peak power. Working at the limit causes the electrolytic capacitors to overheat, which over time causes voltage ripple that can kill your SSD or motherboard.
