Unit economics calculator for sellers: calculation of profit and margin

Unit economics is the foundation of profitable trading. Many novice sellers make the mistake of taking into account only the purchase price and the site commission. However, the devil is in the details: round-trip logistics, packaging costs and, most importantly, the redemption rate.

Our online calculator allows you to instantly decompose the price of a product and understand how much you earn from each “net” unit sold.

Key performance indicators

To prevent your business from becoming a self-employed hobby, you need to track three key metrics:

  • Net profit: the amount that remains in your pocket after paying absolutely all operating and commodity expenses.
  • Margin: ratio of profit to revenue. It shows what percentage of each ruble you earn is your benefit.
  • ROI (Return on Investment): return on investment. If your ROI is 100%, this means that for every ruble invested in the purchase you receive one ruble of net profit.

Calculation formulas in the calculator

Our tool uses an advanced algorithm that takes into account circular logistics. This is especially important for low redemption categories (such as clothing or shoes).

  1. Average logistics cost per 1 sale: $$L_{avg} = \frac{(L_{client} \cdot 100) + (L_{return} \cdot (100 — B))}{B}$$ Where:
    • $L_{client}$ - logistics to the client;
    • $L_{return}$ — cost of return to warehouse;
    • $B$ — percentage of product repurchase (%).
  2. Net profit: $$P = S — (C + Com + L_{avg} + Pack + T)$$ Where:
    • $S$ - sale price;
    • $C$ - cost (purchase);
    • $Com$ - marketplace commission;
    • $Pack$ - fulfillment and packaging;
    • $T$ - taxes.

Why is it important to consider fulfillment and redemption rates?

Fulfillment (packaging, labeling, delivery to the warehouse) often constitutes a significant part of the costs that sellers forget to include in unit economics. Even more insidious redemption percentage. If a product is redeemed only 50% of the time, this means that for every successful sale there are two trips for the product to the customer and one trip back to the warehouse.

Using our calculator helps:

  • Accurately calculate the price to participate in promotions and sales.
  • Determine the maximum allowable purchase cost.
  • Understand whether taxes and commissions are eating up all your profits.

How to use the tool

The calculator interface is divided into two blocks: “Product and Marketplace” and “Operating Expenses”.

  • Enter the sales price and cost.
  • Specify the site's commission (usually from 5% to 25% depending on the category).
  • Important: Be sure to indicate the buyback percentage if you trade using the FBO or FBS system.
  • Add packaging costs and taxes (for the simplified tax system “Income” this is usually 6-7%).

After clicking the "Calculate" button, you will see an interactive price structure chart. If the profit sector is colored red, your business plan requires an urgent revision of prices or searching for another supplier.
