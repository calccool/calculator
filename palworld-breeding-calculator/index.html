Hidden Mechanics: What is Breeding Power?

Each crossing is based on a hidden numerical coefficient - Breeding Power. In Palworld, the result does not depend on chance, but on a strict mathematical dependence. The lower the power value, the “rare” and stronger the Pal is considered.

To calculate the final creature, the following formula is used:

$$P_{child} = \lfloor \frac{P_{parent\_A} + P_{parent\_B} + 1}{2} \rfloor$$

Where:

  • $P_{child}$ - estimated power of the future descendant.
  • $P_{parent\_A}$ - power rank of the first parent.
  • $P_{parent\_B}$ - power rank of the second parent.
  • $\lfloor ... \rfloor$ - rounds the result down to the nearest whole number.

After calculating the final value, the game searches its database for the Pal whose own power rank is closest to the resulting number.

Quick Breeding Tips

  • Cake: Don't forget that without the cake in the box at the date's pasture, the process will not begin.
  • Transfer of skills: Remember that the offspring can inherit the passive skills of the parents. Use the calculator to find a pair with the desired characteristics (for example, “Hard Worker” or “Sprinter”).
  • Legendary genes: Crossing two legendary Pals (Power 10) always produces strong results, but to produce top workers (like Anubis) it often requires combinations of mid-tier Pals.
